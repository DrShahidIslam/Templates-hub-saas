# Standard Operating Procedure: New Client Onboarding Excellence

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the systematic process for transitioning a new client from the sales pipeline to active operations. The goal of this onboarding protocol is to establish professional rapport, define clear project scope, align on communication expectations, and ensure all administrative requirements are met to facilitate a seamless launch. Adherence to this process reduces churn, eliminates ambiguity, and establishes the foundation for a long-term, high-value partnership.

## Phase 1: Administrative Setup and CRM Integration
*   Verify receipt of signed Master Service Agreement (MSA) and initial deposit.
*   Create a dedicated digital folder in the secure company file management system (e.g., SharePoint/Google Drive).
*   Add the client profile to the CRM with all primary contacts, billing details, and contract end dates.
*   Generate an invoice for the first billing cycle or onboarding fee.
*   Assign an Internal Account Manager (AM) and Project Lead to the account.

## Phase 2: The Welcome Experience
*   Send the "Official Welcome Email" within 24 hours of contract execution, introducing the account team.
*   Distribute the "Client Onboarding Packet," including the Project Roadmap, Communication Policy, and Escalation Matrix.
*   Schedule the Kickoff Meeting using the company calendar tool (e.g., Calendly), ensuring all key stakeholders are invited.
*   Request necessary access credentials (e.g., CMS, analytics, CRM) via a secure password management system (e.g., LastPass/Bitwarden).

## Phase 3: The Kickoff Meeting Execution
*   Review the project scope, deliverables, and timeline to ensure alignment.
*   Identify key performance indicators (KPIs) and client-specific success metrics.
*   Establish recurring meeting cadences (weekly/bi-weekly status updates).
*   Confirm the preferred communication channels (Slack, Email, Project Management software).
*   Document all action items and meeting minutes; share with the client within 2 hours of meeting conclusion.

## Phase 4: Operational Launch
*   Configure the client’s project dashboard in the internal Project Management tool (e.g., Asana, Jira, ClickUp).
*   Upload all initial project assets and strategy documents.
*   Perform a "Sanity Check" audit of all platforms/accounts to ensure access levels are correct.
*   Send an internal "Onboarding Complete" notification to the leadership team.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips:
*   **The "Zero-Latency" Rule:** Respond to all client inquiries within 4 business hours during the first 30 days. High responsiveness builds trust during the vulnerable transition phase.
*   **Over-Communicate:** In the beginning, share more information than requested. It is better to provide a detailed update that wasn't strictly necessary than to leave the client wondering about project progress.
*   **The "Win" Early:** Aim to deliver a small, quick win within the first two weeks of onboarding to demonstrate immediate value.

### Pitfalls to Avoid:
*   **Incomplete Access:** Do not wait until the day of a deadline to test administrative access. Verify all logins during the first 48 hours.
*   **Scope Creep:** Failing to explicitly define what is *out of scope* during the kickoff leads to uncompensated labor. Always reference the MSA.
*   **Assuming Shared Language:** Clients may use different terminology for the same concepts. During the kickoff, clarify definitions of key terms to ensure internal and external stakeholders are aligned.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if a client fails to provide the requested access credentials on time?**
A: Send a polite but firm "Blocker Notification" email. Highlight that project timelines are dependent on these assets and provide a new estimated completion date based on the delay.

**Q: How do we handle client contacts who were not part of the initial sales process?**
A: Ensure you have an introductory discovery call with new stakeholders to explain the project history and goals. Never assume the "new" contact has been briefed by their internal team.

**Q: Should I change the communication cadence if the client is slow to provide feedback?**
A: Do not unilaterally change the cadence. If a client is unresponsive, switch to a "Summary Update" format where you document work completed, pending items, and the impact of the delay on the final deadline.

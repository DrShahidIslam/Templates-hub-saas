# Standard Operating Procedure: Professional Facility Fumigation

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the preparation, execution, and post-fumigation clearance of facility spaces. Fumigation is a high-risk operation involving toxic gases; therefore, strict adherence to this document is required to ensure regulatory compliance, protect personnel, and maintain the structural and operational integrity of the facility. All activities must be performed by certified pest control professionals in accordance with local environmental and occupational safety regulations.

## Phase 1: Pre-Fumigation Preparation

*   **Hazard Notification:** Notify all onsite personnel and neighboring facilities at least 48 hours prior to fumigation. 
*   **Site Inspection:** Conduct a thorough perimeter walk to identify potential gas leakage points (e.g., vents, floor drains, unsealed gaps).
*   **Asset Protection:** Ensure all electronic equipment, moisture-sensitive materials, and food products are either removed, sealed in gas-tight containers, or covered with impermeable polyethylene sheeting.
*   **Power and HVAC:** Shut down all HVAC systems and electrical circuits that could trigger a spark or provide a pathway for gas migration.
*   **Clearance:** Perform a final headcount and physical sweep of the facility to ensure no unauthorized persons or pets are present.

## Phase 2: Execution and Sealing

*   **Gas-Tight Sealing:** Utilize heavy-duty polyethylene (min. 4-mil thickness) to encapsulate the building or targeted area. Seal all seams with high-tack adhesive tape.
*   **Warning Signage:** Place standardized “DANGER: FUMIGATION IN PROGRESS” signage at all entrances and egress points. 
*   **Introduction of Fumigant:** Introduce the fumigant by a licensed technician using specialized monitoring equipment to ensure the concentration levels remain within the target range.
*   **Perimeter Monitoring:** Maintain a continuous watch on the exterior perimeter for any evidence of tent ballooning or gas odor. 
*   **Log Keeping:** Maintain an active log of gas concentration readings every two hours throughout the exposure duration.

## Phase 3: Ventilation and Clearance

*   **Gas Dissipation:** Initiate the aeration process by removing seals from windows and doors in accordance with the fumigant manufacturer’s ventilation requirements.
*   **Air Quality Testing:** Use calibrated detection equipment to verify that gas concentrations are below the Threshold Limit Value (TLV) before allowing human entry.
*   **Documentation:** Issue a formal Certificate of Clearance to the facility manager once the air quality is certified safe.
*   **Cleanup:** Remove all plastic sheeting, signage, and equipment utilized during the process.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Always double-check the “clearance time” listed on the fumigant label. Aeration can take significantly longer during high-humidity or cold-weather conditions.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "dead-bolt" log. The person responsible for locking the facility should be the only one with the physical key during the process to prevent accidental entry.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "hiding spots." Pests often relocate to sub-flooring or wall voids; ensure the fumigant concentration accounts for these hard-to-reach areas.
*   **Pitfall:** Improper sealing. Even a microscopic tear in the polyethylene tenting can lead to a failed treatment and significant safety liabilities.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How long does the facility need to remain closed after fumigation?**
The duration is dictated by the specific fumigant used and the volume of the space. Generally, a minimum of 24–48 hours is required for exposure and aeration combined, but your certified technician will provide an exact timeline based on your facility's air exchange rate.

**2. Is it necessary to remove all perishable items?**
Yes. Even if items are stored in cupboards, gas can permeate standard food packaging. All food, beverages, and medications must be removed or sealed in specialized gas-tight containers to prevent contamination.

**3. What should I do if I smell gas after the clearance is issued?**
Immediately evacuate the building, secure the perimeter, and call the fumigation contractor and emergency services. Do not re-enter the facility until the contractor has re-tested the air and confirmed it is safe.

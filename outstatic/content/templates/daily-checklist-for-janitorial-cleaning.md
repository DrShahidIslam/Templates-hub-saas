# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Janitorial Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the definitive guide for maintaining optimal cleanliness, hygiene, and facility aesthetics. Adherence to this checklist ensures a consistent environment for employees and visitors while extending the lifespan of facility assets. All custodial staff are expected to execute these tasks with attention to detail, prioritizing safety, chemical efficacy, and high-touch surface sanitation.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Supply Management
*   **Inventory Check:** Verify that all cleaning carts are stocked with microfiber cloths, EPA-approved disinfectants, glass cleaner, floor neutralizer, and trash liners.
*   **PPE Compliance:** Ensure all staff are equipped with appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (gloves, goggles if necessary, and non-slip footwear).
*   **Equipment Audit:** Inspect vacuum cleaners, floor scrubbers, and mop buckets for functionality. Report any mechanical failures to the facility manager immediately.
*   **Signage:** Place "Caution: Wet Floor" signage at all entry points prior to commencing wet cleaning operations.

## Phase 2: Restroom Sanitation (High-Priority)
*   **Refill Consumables:** Replenish toilet paper, paper towels, hand soap, and seat covers.
*   **Surface Disinfection:** Spray and wipe down sinks, faucets, counters, and mirrors.
*   **Toilet/Urinal Care:** Scrub the interior of bowls and urinals using an acid-based cleaner; wipe the exterior, seat, and handle with disinfectant.
*   **Waste Removal:** Empty sanitary napkin bins and general waste baskets; replace liners.
*   **Floor Maintenance:** Sweep and mop floors using a germicidal cleaner, paying special attention to the areas around the base of the toilets.

## Phase 3: Office and Common Area Maintenance
*   **Waste Management:** Empty all trash bins, recycling containers, and compost bins. Replace liners if soiled.
*   **Surface Cleaning:** Dust and wipe down desks, tabletops, and conference room surfaces. 
*   **High-Touch Point Sanitization:** Wipe down light switches, door handles, elevator buttons, and handrails using disinfectant.
*   **Flooring:** Perform a thorough vacuuming of all carpeted areas; damp-mop hard surface flooring.
*   **Glass and Mirrors:** Use glass cleaner to remove fingerprints and smudges from glass doors and partitions.

## Phase 4: Final Inspection and Securing
*   **Walk-through:** Conduct a final pass to ensure no cleaning equipment or chemicals have been left in public view.
*   **Securing the Facility:** Ensure all windows are closed, and doors are locked in accordance with building security protocols.
*   **Log Completion:** Sign off on the cleaning log posted in the maintenance closet or designated area.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The Microfiber Rule:** Use color-coded microfiber cloths (e.g., Red for restrooms, Blue for offices) to prevent cross-contamination. Replace cloths frequently as they become soiled.
*   **Pro Tip: Dwell Time:** Always allow disinfectant chemicals to sit on the surface for the manufacturer’s recommended "dwell time" (usually 3–10 minutes) before wiping; otherwise, sanitization is ineffective.
*   **Pitfall: Over-wetting:** Avoid using excessive water on wood surfaces or electrical outlets. Over-wetting can cause structural damage or electrical hazards.
*   **Pitfall: Chemical Mixing:** Never mix ammonia and bleach. This combination creates toxic chloramine gas. Always follow the manufacturer’s dilution ratios strictly.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should microfiber cloths be laundered?**
Microfiber cloths should be treated as single-use per area or shift. They must be laundered in hot water with a mild detergent and tumble dried on low heat to preserve the synthetic fibers.

**2. What should I do if I encounter a spill I cannot identify?**
Safety first. Do not attempt to touch or clean unknown substances. Cordon off the area, contact the facility manager or security, and refer to the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) binder if an MSDS label is visible on the container.

**3. Is it necessary to dust before vacuuming?**
Yes. Always follow a "Top-Down" approach. Dusting surfaces first allows particles to fall to the floor, which are then collected during the final vacuuming/mopping phase. Doing it in reverse creates double work.

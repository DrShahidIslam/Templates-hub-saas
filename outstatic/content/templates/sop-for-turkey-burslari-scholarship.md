# Standard Operating Procedure: Turkey Bursları Scholarship Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for prospective international students aiming to navigate the Türkiye Scholarships (Türkiye Bursları) application process. Given the highly competitive nature of this government-funded program, applicants must adhere to a disciplined, structured approach to ensure eligibility, documentation accuracy, and timely submission. This document outlines the lifecycle of the application, from initial profile creation to final submission.

## Phase 1: Pre-Application Eligibility & Preparation
*   [ ] **Verify Nationality:** Confirm your country is included in the current year's eligible list.
*   [ ] **Age Requirement Check:** Confirm you meet the age threshold (typically under 21 for undergraduate, 30 for Master’s, 35 for PhD).
*   [ ] **Academic Performance:** Ensure your cumulative GPA meets the minimum requirement (typically 70% for undergraduate, 75% for graduate/medical).
*   [ ] **Document Digitization:** Prepare high-resolution scans of the following in PDF format:
    *   Valid Passport/National ID.
    *   Recent passport-sized photograph.
    *   National Exam results (e.g., SAT, IB, GCE) if applicable.
    *   Academic transcripts and degree certificates.
    *   Language certificates (TOEFL, IELTS) if applicable.
*   [ ] **Language Proficiency:** Decide whether to apply for Turkish-medium or English-medium programs; note that some English programs may require an international test certificate.

## Phase 2: Drafting the Motivation Letter
*   [ ] **Identify Objectives:** Clearly articulate why you want to study in Turkey and why you chose your specific department.
*   [ ] **Alignment:** Connect your past experiences (volunteering, internships) to your future career goals.
*   [ ] **Professional Tone:** Ensure the letter is written in a professional academic style, avoiding generic templates.
*   [ ] **Review:** Proofread for grammar, flow, and strict adherence to word count limitations within the portal.

## Phase 3: Reference Letters & Extracurriculars
*   [ ] **Secure Recommendations:** Request letters from professors or employers who can speak specifically to your academic potential and leadership qualities.
*   [ ] **Upload Documentation:** Ensure all extracurricular certificates, internships, and awards are categorized and uploaded into the "Social Activities" section of the portal.
*   [ ] **Letter Format:** Ensure letters are signed, dated, and printed on official institutional letterhead.

## Phase 4: Final Review & Submission
*   [ ] **Profile Completeness:** Audit the portal to ensure every mandatory field has a green checkmark or "Completed" status.
*   [ ] **Selection Preferences:** Select your top university choices with careful consideration of location, department reputation, and your own academic profile.
*   [ ] **Submission:** Submit before the final deadline; avoid last-minute submission to prevent server-side traffic delays.
*   [ ] **Confirmation:** Save the submission confirmation receipt sent to your registered email address.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (The "Why Turkey" Factor):** Emphasize your cultural interest in Turkey. Show that you have researched the country beyond just the scholarship benefits—mention specific aspects of Turkey’s history, geography, or diplomatic standing that interest you.
*   **Pro Tip (Accuracy is King):** If your transcript is not in English or Turkish, it must be officially translated and uploaded alongside the original document. Failure to do so will result in immediate disqualification.
*   **Pitfall (The "Generic Template" Trap):** Do not copy-paste motivational letters found online. The review committee uses plagiarism detection software. An authentic, personalized letter has a significantly higher success rate.
*   **Pitfall (Over-claiming):** Only upload extracurricular activities that you can prove. If you claim to have won an international award but cannot provide a certificate upon request, your credibility is compromised.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: If I do not have an English proficiency certificate, can I still apply?**
A: Yes, you can apply; however, for English-medium programs, you will be required to sit for a language proficiency test in Turkey. If you fail to meet the required level, you will be required to take a one-year intensive English preparatory course.

**Q: Can I apply for more than one level of study?**
A: No, you can only apply for one level of study (e.g., either Undergraduate OR Master’s, not both simultaneously). 

**Q: Is it mandatory to speak Turkish to apply for the scholarship?**
A: No, it is not mandatory. If you are accepted into a Turkish-medium program, the scholarship includes a one-year mandatory Turkish language course to ensure you are fluent enough for your academic studies.

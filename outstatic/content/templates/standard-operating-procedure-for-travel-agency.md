# Standard Operating Procedure: Travel Agency Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional workflow for managing end-to-end travel agency services. It is designed to ensure consistency, mitigate risk, and maintain a high standard of client satisfaction from initial inquiry to post-trip follow-up. Adherence to these procedures is mandatory for all travel consultants to ensure accurate documentation, financial compliance, and exceptional service delivery.

## Phase 1: Inquiry Management and Qualification
*   **Acknowledge Inquiry:** Respond to all client inquiries via the preferred channel (email, CRM, or phone) within 24 business hours.
*   **Qualify Lead:** Conduct a discovery call to determine budget, travel dates, passenger demographics, and specific preferences (e.g., accessibility requirements, dietary needs).
*   **Document Needs:** Create a client profile in the Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system, logging all preferences and historical travel data.

## Phase 2: Itinerary Planning and Quotation
*   **Research Options:** Utilize Global Distribution Systems (GDS) or preferred supplier portals to curate a minimum of two itinerary options tailored to the client’s request.
*   **Verify Availability:** Confirm real-time availability for flights, accommodations, and transfers before presenting the proposal.
*   **Transparent Pricing:** Provide a detailed quote inclusive of taxes, service fees, and optional travel insurance. Clearly highlight what is excluded (e.g., resort fees, city taxes).
*   **Review Proposal:** Double-check all dates, spelling of passenger names against passports, and destination entry requirements.

## Phase 3: Booking and Financial Processing
*   **Confirm Intent:** Secure written authorization (email or digital contract) from the client to proceed with bookings.
*   **Execute Bookings:** Secure reservations immediately following authorization.
*   **Process Payments:** Collect payments securely via PCI-compliant gateways. Ensure all supplier confirmation numbers are recorded in the client’s file.
*   **Issuance:** Issue electronic tickets and vouchers. Send a formal confirmation summary to the client within one business day of booking.

## Phase 4: Pre-Departure and Documentation
*   **Visa Check:** Verify visa requirements for all destinations using tools like IATA Timatic. Provide clear instructions and links to the necessary application portals.
*   **Final Documentation:** Send the “Travel Packet” (e-tickets, itineraries, transfer instructions, and contact list) 7–14 days prior to departure.
*   **Final Briefing:** Conduct a final pre-trip check-in to ensure the client is aware of any last-minute schedule changes or health/safety protocols.

## Phase 5: Post-Trip Follow-Up
*   **Client Debrief:** Send a personalized follow-up email 48 hours after the client’s return.
*   **Feedback Collection:** Request a review or testimonial to build brand social proof.
*   **Archive File:** Ensure all financial records are finalized and move the client file to the “Completed” status for future marketing re-engagement.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always recommend comprehensive travel insurance at the time of the initial deposit. It protects both the client and your agency from non-refundable losses.
*   **Pro Tip:** Build a "Preferred Supplier" list. Negotiating exclusive perks (e.g., room upgrades or early check-in) builds immense client loyalty.
*   **Pitfall:** Overlooking "Passport Validity." Always remind clients that most international destinations require at least 6 months of passport validity beyond the return date.
*   **Pitfall:** Failing to document verbal changes. If a client requests a change via phone, always follow up with an email confirmation to create a digital paper trail.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the agency’s policy on cancellations?**
A: All cancellations are subject to the specific supplier’s terms and conditions in addition to the agency’s non-refundable service fee. These are clearly outlined in the initial booking contract.

**Q: How do we handle emergency situations while the client is abroad?**
A: We provide a 24/7 emergency contact number on the final itinerary. Our consultants are trained to prioritize client safety and will interface directly with local vendors to resolve issues immediately.

**Q: Can I modify an itinerary once the booking is confirmed?**
A: Modifications are permitted but may incur supplier change fees and a secondary agency administrative fee. All modification requests must be submitted in writing.

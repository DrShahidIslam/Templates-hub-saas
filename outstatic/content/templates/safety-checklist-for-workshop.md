# Standard Operating Procedure: Workshop Safety Inspection & Compliance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety protocols and inspection requirements for maintaining a secure workshop environment. The objective of this document is to minimize the risk of workplace accidents, ensure compliance with OSHA (or local equivalent) regulations, and extend the lifespan of workshop equipment. All personnel are expected to adhere to these guidelines during pre-shift, weekly, and monthly assessments to foster a culture of safety and operational excellence.

## 1. Daily Pre-Shift Environment Check
*   **Walkways & Exits:** Ensure all aisles are clear of debris, scrap materials, or tripping hazards. Verify that emergency exit doors are unlocked and unobstructed.
*   **Lighting & Visibility:** Confirm all overhead lighting is functional. Replace flickering or burnt-out bulbs immediately to prevent shadows on workspaces.
*   **Air Quality & Ventilation:** Verify that dust collection systems and exhaust fans are operational and not clogged.
*   **Fire Suppression:** Inspect fire extinguishers to ensure the pressure gauge is in the "green" zone and the safety pin is intact.

## 2. Equipment & Machinery Inspection
*   **Guarding:** Confirm all machine guards, shields, and interlocks are in place and functioning correctly. Never bypass a safety guard.
*   **Emergency Stops (E-Stops):** Visually inspect E-stop buttons for damage. Ensure they are easily accessible and not obscured by workpieces.
*   **Power Integrity:** Inspect all power cords for fraying, exposed wiring, or damaged plugs. Remove any faulty equipment from service immediately.
*   **Calibration:** Ensure blades, bits, and cutting surfaces are sharp and securely mounted. Dull tools increase the risk of "kickback" and injury.

## 3. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Compliance
*   **Eye Protection:** Verify that ANSI-rated safety glasses are available and stored in a clean, dust-free environment.
*   **Respiratory Protection:** Ensure N95 or particulate respirators are available for tasks involving sanding, grinding, or chemical application.
*   **Hearing Protection:** Confirm that earplugs or earmuffs are accessible near equipment exceeding 85 decibels.
*   **Attire:** Check that all personnel are wearing appropriate footwear (steel-toe/non-slip) and that loose clothing, jewelry, or long hair is properly contained.

## 4. Housekeeping & Hazardous Material Storage
*   **Chemical Containment:** Ensure all solvents, lubricants, and chemicals are labeled clearly and stored in fire-rated cabinets.
*   **Waste Management:** Empty oily rag bins daily to prevent spontaneous combustion. Ensure metal shavings and wood dust are disposed of in designated, fire-resistant containers.
*   **Spill Kits:** Verify that spill kits are fully stocked and located in close proximity to chemical storage areas.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The "Shadow Board" Method:** Implement shadow boards for all hand tools. It provides an immediate visual cue if a tool is missing or left in an unsafe area.
*   **Pro Tip: Start-of-Shift Briefing:** Dedicate two minutes before the shift begins to discuss potential hazards for the day's specific tasks.
*   **Pitfall: Complacency:** The most frequent accidents happen to experienced workers who become too comfortable. Never skip the inspection, even if you have used a machine for years.
*   **Pitfall: Improper Storage:** Never store heavy items on high shelves. If an item must be stored high, ensure it is secured against seismic or accidental movement.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should the comprehensive safety audit be conducted?**
A: While daily spot checks are mandatory, a comprehensive, documented safety audit should be performed at least once per month by the designated Safety Officer or Floor Manager.

**Q: What is the correct procedure if a piece of equipment fails a safety check?**
A: "Lock-out/Tag-out" (LOTO) procedures must be initiated immediately. Place a "DO NOT USE" tag on the machine, remove the power source or key, and report the issue to maintenance/management immediately.

**Q: Are there specific requirements for storing flammable liquids?**
A: Yes. All flammable liquids must be stored in specialized, yellow, fire-rated safety cabinets. Never store flammable materials near heat sources or high-traffic pedestrian areas.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Kitchen Equipment Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory protocol for the systematic inspection, cleaning, and preventive maintenance of commercial kitchen equipment. The primary objective is to maximize equipment longevity, ensure compliance with health and safety regulations, mitigate the risk of catastrophic mechanical failure during peak service hours, and maintain optimal energy efficiency. All kitchen staff and maintenance personnel are responsible for adhering to these guidelines to ensure a safe and productive culinary environment.

## Phase 1: Refrigeration & Cold Storage
*   **Condenser Coils:** Inspect and vacuum dust/debris buildup to prevent compressor overheating.
*   **Door Gaskets:** Check for cracks, tears, or loose seals. Test with the "dollar bill test" (if the bill slides out easily, the gasket requires replacement).
*   **Drain Lines:** Flush evaporator drain pans to prevent bacterial growth and water backup.
*   **Temperature Calibration:** Verify digital or analog display accuracy against a calibrated handheld thermometer.

## Phase 2: Cooking Equipment (Ranges, Ovens, Fryers)
*   **Burner Orifices:** Inspect gas ranges for uneven flame patterns; clean ports to remove carbon buildup.
*   **Fryer Thermostat:** Perform a boil-out procedure; check the high-limit thermostat for proper calibration.
*   **Oven Seals:** Inspect door ropes and hinges for heat loss.
*   **Ventilation Filters:** Remove and degrease hood filters; inspect the interior of the hood plenum for grease accumulation.

## Phase 3: Dishwashing & Sanitation Systems
*   **Spray Arms:** Remove and inspect nozzles for lime scale or debris blockage.
*   **Chemical Lines:** Check for leaks in detergent, sanitizer, and rinse aid tubing.
*   **Waste Screens:** Clean and inspect scrap baskets to ensure no debris enters the pump assembly.
*   **Water Softeners:** Check salt levels and verify the regeneration cycle is functioning correctly.

## Phase 4: Electrical & Safety Components
*   **Cords and Plugs:** Examine power cables for fraying, exposed wiring, or heat-related discoloration.
*   **Emergency Shut-offs:** Ensure gas and electrical emergency cut-off switches are unobstructed and clearly labeled.
*   **Fire Suppression Systems:** Verify that the ANSUL system link is taut and that the service tag is current (annual inspection must be logged).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The Maintenance Logbook.** Always keep a physical or digital logbook attached to each piece of equipment. Documenting the date, technician name, and specific task performed creates a valuable asset history for warranty claims and resale value.
*   **Pro Tip: Proactive Parts Stocking.** Keep a small inventory of high-turnover "wear items" (e.g., door gaskets, pilot igniters, and light bulbs) on-site to reduce equipment downtime.
*   **Pitfall: Over-Cleaning.** Avoid using abrasive steel wool or caustic industrial chemicals on stainless steel surfaces, as this can degrade the protective chromium-oxide layer, leading to rust.
*   **Pitfall: Neglecting Water Quality.** Hard water is the silent killer of dishwashers and steamers. If you notice white scaling, consult a water filtration specialist immediately.

## FAQ
**Q: How often should a deep clean and preventive maintenance check be performed?**
A: Daily inspections are the responsibility of the line staff. A comprehensive preventive maintenance check should be conducted by a qualified technician or facilities manager at least every 90 days.

**Q: Should I attempt to fix gas-related issues myself?**
A: Absolutely not. Any gas-related maintenance, including burner adjustments or pilot light repairs, should be performed by a licensed technician to ensure safety and code compliance.

**Q: Why is my refrigeration unit freezing over despite being set to the right temperature?**
A: This is usually caused by restricted airflow (dirty condenser coils) or a faulty door gasket letting in ambient moisture. If both look clean, check the evaporator fan motor for proper rotation.

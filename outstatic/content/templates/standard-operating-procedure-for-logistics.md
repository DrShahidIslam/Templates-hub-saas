# Standard Operating Procedure: Logistics and Supply Chain Execution

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the systematic workflow for managing inbound and outbound logistics, ensuring operational efficiency, inventory accuracy, and timely delivery. This document serves as the primary reference for warehouse supervisors, logistics coordinators, and inventory management teams to maintain high service levels while minimizing overhead costs.

## 1. Pre-Shipment and Order Processing
*   **Order Verification:** Confirm all customer orders against inventory availability and credit terms in the ERP system.
*   **Documentation Preparation:** Generate necessary shipping documentation, including Bills of Lading (BOL), commercial invoices, and packing lists.
*   **Carrier Selection:** Select the optimal carrier based on lead time, cost-efficiency, and service level agreement (SLA) requirements.
*   **Pick-Ticket Generation:** Release pick-tickets to the warehouse floor via the Warehouse Management System (WMS).

## 2. Warehouse Operations (Picking and Packing)
*   **Picking Protocol:** Execute order picking using FIFO (First-In, First-Out) or FEFO (First-Expired, First-Out) methodologies to ensure inventory freshness.
*   **Quality Inspection:** Verify product integrity, labels, and expiration dates before moving items to the packing station.
*   **Packaging Standards:** Apply protective packaging according to product fragility and dimensions to prevent transit damage.
*   **Labeling Compliance:** Apply shipping labels, tracking barcodes, and hazard warnings (if applicable) in accordance with carrier specifications.

## 3. Dispatch and Transport Execution
*   **Consolidation:** Group orders by destination or carrier route to maximize shipping efficiency and reduce "less-than-truckload" (LTL) costs.
*   **Carrier Handover:** Perform a final scan of all outbound shipments to update the tracking status in the WMS.
*   **Manifesting:** Cross-check the physical load against the digital manifest to ensure 100% load accuracy.
*   **Proof of Dispatch:** Secure the BOL signature from the carrier representative and store in the digital archive.

## 4. Post-Shipment Tracking and Reconciliation
*   **Real-time Tracking:** Monitor tracking status for all shipments, proactively flagging delays to customer support.
*   **Exception Handling:** Immediately initiate trace protocols for lost, damaged, or delayed shipments.
*   **Performance Reporting:** Log shipping transit times and carrier performance metrics for monthly QBR (Quarterly Business Review) analysis.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a "double-scan" verification process at the pack station to eliminate shipping errors and reduce returns.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a 10% safety stock buffer for high-velocity SKUs to prevent backorders during peak seasons.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-relying on a single carrier. Always maintain a backup logistics partner to mitigate supply chain disruptions.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "dim-weight" (dimensional weight) shipping costs. Ensure your packaging is as compact as possible to avoid unnecessary surcharges.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should we conduct inventory cycle counts?**
A: High-velocity SKUs should be counted weekly, while low-velocity items can be counted on a monthly or quarterly basis to ensure 98%+ inventory accuracy.

**Q: What is the recommended procedure for reporting damaged goods?**
A: Document damage with timestamped photographs immediately upon arrival, notify the carrier within 24 hours, and file a formal claim using the original BOL and supporting evidence.

**Q: How do we determine if we should switch carriers?**
A: Review your carrier performance monthly. If a specific provider falls below a 95% on-time delivery rate or shows a high frequency of damage claims, begin evaluating alternative providers.

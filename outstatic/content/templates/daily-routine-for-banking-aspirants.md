# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Routine for Banking Aspirants

This document outlines a high-performance daily regimen designed for individuals preparing for competitive banking examinations (IBPS, SBI, RBI, etc.). Banking exams demand a unique blend of speed, accuracy, and endurance across Quantitative Aptitude, Reasoning, English, and General Awareness. This SOP serves as a blueprint to cultivate discipline, maximize cognitive retention, and ensure consistent progress toward the goal of clearing the selection process.

## Section 1: Morning Foundation (06:00 – 09:00)

The early hours are reserved for high-intensity cognitive tasks where focus is at its peak.

*   **06:00 – 06:30:** Wake up, hydration, and light physical movement to prime the brain.
*   **06:30 – 07:30:** Current Affairs (CA) ingestion. Use a reputable source (e.g., editorial analysis, daily news videos, or GA apps). Do not just read; maintain a summarized notebook.
*   **07:30 – 09:00:** English Proficiency. Focus on Reading Comprehension (RC), vocabulary building, and grammar rules. Read one editorial article to improve reading speed and analytical ability.

## Section 2: Core Concept Mastery (09:30 – 13:00)

This block is dedicated to building the "Quant and Reasoning" foundation.

*   **09:30 – 11:00:** Quantitative Aptitude. Pick one topic (e.g., Profit & Loss, Time & Work, Data Interpretation). Practice 30–50 questions of varying difficulty levels.
*   **11:00 – 13:00:** Logical Reasoning. Focus on Puzzles and Seating Arrangement. Solve at least 5–7 complex sets daily to build the requisite pattern-recognition speed.

## Section 3: Consolidation & Simulation (14:30 – 17:30)

Transition from theory to application. This stage mimics the pressure of an actual examination environment.

*   **14:30 – 16:00:** Sectional Mocks. Choose one subject per day to test your speed under a timer. Analyze the errors immediately after the timer stops.
*   **16:00 – 17:30:** Revision of Weak Links. Revisit concepts from the morning session that proved difficult during your practice questions.

## Section 4: Final Review & Planning (19:00 – 21:00)

The evening session is for reflection and preparation for the following day.

*   **19:00 – 20:00:** General Awareness Revision. Review the notes taken during the morning session.
*   **20:00 – 20:30:** Daily Performance Audit. Did you meet your target for today? Identify distractions and plan the next day’s specific targets.
*   **20:30 – 21:00:** Light review of formulas, tables, squares, and cubes.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **The 80/20 Rule:** Dedicate 80% of your time to practicing questions and 20% to learning concepts.
*   **Analytical Log:** Maintain an 'Error Log.' Note down every mistake made in mocks. Analyze if the error was due to conceptual failure or silly calculation errors.
*   **Mock Timing:** Always attempt mocks at the same time you anticipate your real exam shift to align your biological clock.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **Over-reliance on Video Lectures:** Watching videos is passive learning. Transition to self-study as soon as possible.
*   **The "Notification" Trap:** Keep your phone in another room or on "Do Not Disturb" during core study hours.
*   **Ignoring GA:** Many aspirants focus only on Math/Reasoning and fail the Mains because they neglected General Awareness until the last minute.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Should I take a full-length mock test every single day?**
A: No. In the early stages, focus on sectional mocks. Transition to full-length mocks 2–3 times a week as the exam approaches to build endurance.

**Q: How do I improve my calculation speed for Data Interpretation?**
A: Memorize tables up to 25, squares up to 40, and cubes up to 20. Spend 10 minutes daily practicing mental arithmetic (addition/subtraction of large numbers).

**Q: What if I have a bad day and miss my study targets?**
A: Do not spiral. Acknowledge the gap, adjust your plan for the next day, and compensate by adding an extra hour to your weak subject. Consistency beats perfection.

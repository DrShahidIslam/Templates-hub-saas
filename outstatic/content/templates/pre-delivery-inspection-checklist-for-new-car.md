# Standard Operating Procedure: New Vehicle Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI)

### Introduction
The Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) is the final and most critical quality control gate in the automotive sales process. An exhaustive PDI ensures that the vehicle meets the manufacturer’s rigorous safety, performance, and aesthetic standards before it is handed over to the customer. This SOP is designed to standardize the inspection process, mitigate liability, ensure maximum customer satisfaction, and protect the dealership’s reputation by identifying and rectifying potential defects before the client takes possession.

## 1. Exterior Inspection
*   **Paintwork:** Examine every body panel under bright, neutral lighting for scratches, swirl marks, dents, "orange peel," or mismatched paint touch-ups.
*   **Panel Gaps:** Verify that all gaps between panels (hood, trunk, doors, fenders) are uniform and consistent.
*   **Glass and Mirrors:** Inspect all windows, windshields, and side mirrors for chips, cracks, or distortion. Verify all electric mirror adjustment functions.
*   **Tires and Wheels:** Check tire pressure against the manufacturer’s specification (B-pillar sticker). Inspect rims for curb rash or finish defects. Verify tire tread depth and alignment.
*   **Lighting:** Test all exterior lights (high/low beams, turn signals, brake lights, reverse lights, and fog lights) for functionality and condensation within the lenses.

## 2. Interior Inspection
*   **Upholstery:** Inspect all leather, fabric, and stitching for tears, stains, or loose threads. Check headliner for any markings.
*   **Electronics/Infotainment:** Verify the operation of the central touchscreen, Bluetooth/Apple CarPlay/Android Auto connectivity, navigation, and audio system quality.
*   **Climate Control:** Test HVAC system in all modes: hot, cold, defrost, and fan speed variations. Ensure A/C compressor engages and cools effectively.
*   **Safety Features:** Ensure all seatbelts retract and lock properly. Verify functionality of backup cameras, parking sensors, and blind-spot monitoring systems.
*   **Functionality:** Test all power windows, door locks, seat adjustments (including memory settings), sunroof operation, and steering wheel controls.

## 3. Under the Hood & Undercarriage
*   **Fluid Levels:** Check engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, windshield washer fluid, and power steering fluid (if applicable) for correct levels.
*   **Leaks:** Inspect the engine bay and the area directly beneath the vehicle for any signs of leaking oil, coolant, or hydraulic fluid.
*   **Battery:** Perform a load test on the battery to ensure it holds the correct charge. Ensure terminals are clean and tightened.
*   **Belts and Hoses:** Visually inspect for any signs of premature cracking, fraying, or improper routing.

## 4. Documentation & Final Setup
*   **Odometer Verification:** Ensure mileage is consistent with transportation logs and dealer documentation.
*   **Key Sets:** Verify two complete sets of keys/fobs are present and both function correctly (including remote start).
*   **Safety Kit:** Ensure the owner’s manual, jack, lug wrench, and emergency inflator kit (or spare tire) are present.
*   **Detailing:** Confirm the vehicle has undergone a final "delivery detail," ensuring the interior is odor-free and the exterior is clean.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always perform the inspection in a designated, well-lit "delivery bay." Natural, diffused light is better than harsh showroom spotlighting for revealing paint defects.
*   **Pro Tip:** Document everything. If a minor defect is found that cannot be repaired immediately, take a photograph and have the client sign an acknowledgment of a "Due Bill" to fix it at a later date.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not skip the "test drive" portion of the PDI. Even new cars can have alignment issues or electrical gremlins that only present themselves under load or at speed.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid rushing. A rushed PDI is the primary cause of "comebacks" and customer complaints post-delivery.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if I find a significant mechanical defect during the PDI?**
A: Immediately halt the delivery process. Notify the Service Manager and the Sales Manager. Do not release the vehicle until a qualified technician has performed a formal repair and the vehicle has been re-inspected.

**Q: Should the customer be present for the PDI?**
A: The PDI is an internal dealership process. However, a "demonstration" of these systems should be performed with the customer present during the final vehicle delivery walk-through to ensure they understand how their car operates.

**Q: How long should a standard PDI take?**
A: A comprehensive PDI should typically take between 45 and 90 minutes, depending on the complexity of the vehicle’s technology package and the thoroughness of the cleaning/prep team.

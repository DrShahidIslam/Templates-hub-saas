# Standard Operating Procedure: Australian Tourist Visa (Subclass 600) Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for applicants preparing to submit an application for an Australian Visitor Visa (Subclass 600). The Australian Department of Home Affairs maintains a rigorous vetting process; therefore, precision, document authenticity, and clear articulation of the purpose of travel are critical to securing approval. Following this checklist will ensure all regulatory requirements are met, minimizing the risk of application processing delays or refusal.

## Phase 1: Eligibility and Documentation Gathering

*   **Create an ImmiAccount:** Register an account on the official Department of Home Affairs website. This is the central portal for all electronic visa applications.
*   **Passport Verification:** Ensure your passport has at least six months of validity remaining from the date of your intended arrival in Australia. Scan the bio-data page in high resolution.
*   **Proof of Identity:** Prepare a digital copy of your National Identity Card and, if applicable, a birth certificate or marriage certificate to establish family ties.
*   **Recent Photographs:** Secure a recent passport-sized photograph (taken within the last six months) that meets Australian government specifications (white background, neutral expression).

## Phase 2: Financial and Intent Justification

*   **Evidence of Funds:** Collate recent bank statements (covering the last 3–6 months) showing consistent transactions. Ensure the balance is sufficient to cover airfare, accommodation, and daily expenses.
*   **Proof of Employment/Study:** Obtain an original letter from your employer or educational institution stating your leave dates, salary, and confirmation that you are expected to return to your home country.
*   **Itinerary Planning:** Draft a clear, day-by-day itinerary of your intended activities, including flight bookings (or reservations) and confirmed or tentative accommodation arrangements.
*   **Evidence of Incentives to Return:** Collate documents showing ties to your home country, such as property deeds, vehicle registrations, or family enrollment records, to satisfy the "Genuine Temporary Entrant" (GTE) requirement.

## Phase 3: Final Review and Submission

*   **Form 1419 Completion:** Complete Form 1419 (Application for a Visitor Visa) electronically within the ImmiAccount portal. Ensure all information is consistent with your supporting documents.
*   **Health and Character Declarations:** Honestly answer all questions regarding health history and criminal records. Disclose any past visa refusals or travel history discrepancies.
*   **Payment Execution:** Process the non-refundable visa application fee via the secure payment portal. Keep a record of the transaction receipt.
*   **Final Verification:** Perform a final audit of all uploaded files to ensure they are clear, legible, and properly categorized in PDF or JPEG format.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The GTE Requirement:** The "Genuine Temporary Entrant" clause is the most common reason for refusal. Do not just show money in an account; prove you have a stable life at home that you intend to return to after your vacation.
*   **Certified Translations:** If your documents (e.g., birth certificates, bank statements) are not in English, they must be translated by an accredited translator. Submitting non-English documents without translation is a common cause for immediate rejection.
*   **Avoid "Over-Documenting":** While completeness is essential, do not upload irrelevant files. Focus on the core requirements; excessive, unorganized paperwork can overwhelm the Case Officer and delay processing.
*   **Be Truthful:** Never attempt to hide previous visa denials from other countries. The Australian Department of Home Affairs shares intelligence with other nations; providing false information will likely result in a permanent ban.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How long does it usually take to process a Subclass 600 visa?**
Processing times vary significantly based on your citizenship and current global demand. While many are processed within 15–30 days, it is highly recommended to apply at least two months prior to your intended travel date.

**2. Can I work while in Australia on a tourist visa?**
No. The Subclass 600 visa is strictly for tourism or business visitor activities. Undertaking any form of paid employment while on this visa is a violation of your visa conditions and will lead to cancellation and potential deportation.

**3. Do I need to book my flights before my visa is approved?**
The Department of Home Affairs advises that you should not finalize non-refundable travel bookings until your visa has been granted. Providing a detailed, tentative itinerary is sufficient for the application process.

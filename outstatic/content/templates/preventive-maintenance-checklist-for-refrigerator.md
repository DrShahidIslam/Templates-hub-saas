# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance for Refrigeration Units

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory preventive maintenance protocols for commercial and residential refrigeration units. The primary objective is to maximize equipment longevity, ensure food safety compliance, minimize energy consumption, and prevent catastrophic mechanical failure. Routine maintenance, performed at least bi-annually, is essential for maintaining optimal compressor health and thermal consistency. Failure to adhere to these procedures may result in voided warranties, increased utility costs, and equipment downtime.

## Section 1: Safety & Preparation
*   **De-energize Equipment:** Disconnect the unit from the power source or lock out the breaker to prevent electrical shock.
*   **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Wear cut-resistant gloves and safety glasses when handling coils or sharp internal components.
*   **Clearance:** Ensure the workspace is clear of obstructions and the area around the unit is ventilated.
*   **Gather Tools:** Prepare a soft-bristled brush, a vacuum with a brush attachment, a coil cleaning spray (non-corrosive), a microfiber cloth, and a spirit level.

## Section 2: Condenser Coil & Exterior Maintenance
*   **Condenser Cleaning:** Locate the condenser coils (typically at the rear or bottom). Use a vacuum to remove heavy dust and a soft brush to dislodge debris. 
*   **Airflow Check:** Inspect the condenser fan blade for obstructions. Ensure the fan spins freely and is not vibrating.
*   **Ventilation Clearance:** Verify there is at least 3–4 inches of clearance between the unit and surrounding walls to allow for heat dissipation.
*   **Exterior Surfaces:** Wipe down the exterior cabinet with a food-safe stainless steel cleaner to prevent oxidation.

## Section 3: Interior & Door Integrity
*   **Gasket Inspection:** Examine door seals for cracks, gaps, or brittleness. Perform the "Dollar Bill Test": close the door on a dollar bill; if it slides out easily, the gasket requires replacement.
*   **Hinge Calibration:** Check door hinges for tension. Ensure the door closes and seals autonomously from a 45-degree angle.
*   **Internal Lighting:** Replace burnt-out bulbs with manufacturer-specified LED equivalents to reduce heat gain.
*   **Drainage Check:** Clear the evaporator drain pan and line to prevent water pooling or ice accumulation within the unit.

## Section 4: Performance & Calibration
*   **Temperature Verification:** Use a calibrated digital thermometer to cross-reference the internal display with the actual ambient temperature of the cabinet.
*   **Thermostat Calibration:** Adjust the temperature control settings to ensure they correspond with the manufacturer’s recommended holding temperatures (typically 37°F to 40°F for refrigerators).
*   **Compressor Noise Check:** Listen for abnormal clicking or grinding sounds. If noted, log the anomaly for immediate inspection by a certified technician.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pitfall - The Over-Cleaning Mistake:** Avoid using high-pressure water or compressed air on fragile aluminum condenser fins; this can bend the fins and restrict airflow. Always brush in the direction of the fins.
*   **Pro Tip - The Logbook:** Maintain a maintenance log attached to the side of the unit. Documenting the date of service and the technician’s initials ensures accountability and tracks the performance history of the unit.
*   **Pitfall - Chemical Sensitivity:** Never use harsh industrial degreasers on interior plastic liners, as they can cause micro-fractures (crazing) which harbor bacteria. Use a mild soap and water solution.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I clean the condenser coils?**
A: In a high-traffic commercial environment, clean the coils every 90 days. For residential use, bi-annual cleaning is usually sufficient. 

**Q: Why is my refrigerator leaking water inside?**
A: This is most often caused by a clogged defrost drain line. Dust or food debris can block the line, causing water to back up and overflow into the cabinet. 

**Q: Is it safe to leave the unit running while I clean the coils?**
A: It is highly recommended to disconnect power. Working near electrical fans and exposed motor components while the unit is energized poses a significant safety risk.

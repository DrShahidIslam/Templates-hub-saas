# Standard Operating Procedure: PET Bottle Blowing Machine Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety, preparation, and operational protocols for the semi-automatic or automatic PET bottle blowing process. Adherence to these guidelines ensures optimal machine performance, consistent bottle quality, and the prevention of workplace accidents. This document is intended for trained operators only; any deviation must be authorized by the shift supervisor.

## 1. Pre-Operational Safety & Preparation

*   **PPE Verification:** Don required safety gear, including heat-resistant gloves, safety goggles, ear protection, and steel-toed boots.
*   **Safety Interlocks:** Inspect all emergency stop buttons and light curtains to ensure they are functional and unobstructed.
*   **Preform Inspection:** Verify that preforms are dry, clean, and stored at the required ambient temperature.
*   **Tool Check:** Ensure blow pins, molds, and heater banks are free of debris, dust, or residual plastic buildup.
*   **Air Supply:** Confirm that the high-pressure air compressor (typically 30–40 bar) is set to the specified operating pressure.

## 2. Machine Startup & Parameter Setup

*   **System Activation:** Power on the control panel and initiate the machine warm-up cycle.
*   **Heater Calibration:** Adjust the heater bank temperatures based on the specific grammage and color of the preforms.
*   **Cycle Timing:** Input the precise timing for heating, blowing (pre-blow and high-pressure blow), and mold cooling via the HMI (Human Machine Interface).
*   **Chiller Verification:** Ensure the mold cooling chiller is set to the specified temperature (typically 8–12°C) to prevent mold overheating.
*   **Dry Cycle:** Run the machine for 3–5 cycles without preforms to verify mechanical synchronization.

## 3. Production & Quality Monitoring

*   **Feeding:** Load preforms into the hopper and ensure the elevator/orienter is feeding consistently without jams.
*   **First-Article Inspection:** Perform a visual check on the first ten bottles; inspect for flash, short shots, thick/thin wall distribution, or neck deformity.
*   **Continuous Monitoring:** Monitor the HMI for pressure fluctuations, heating errors, or mold sticking alarms.
*   **Sample Testing:** Every hour, perform a "Top Load" test and weight verification to ensure structural integrity and material usage compliance.

## 4. Shutdown & Maintenance Procedures

*   **Clearing the Line:** Empty the preform hopper and allow the machine to complete the cycle until all remaining preforms are converted.
*   **Controlled Power Down:** Initiate the cooling cycle to prevent rapid temperature changes in the mold.
*   **Cleaning:** Perform a thorough cleaning of the heater tunnels and mold surfaces using an approved non-abrasive cleaner.
*   **Lock-Out/Tag-Out (LOTO):** For any mechanical adjustment or internal maintenance, ensure the machine is powered off and locked according to site LOTO protocols.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a strictly controlled environment (humidity and temperature). Fluctuations in ambient air temperature can drastically affect blow-molding consistency.
*   **Pro Tip:** Keep a log of "Ideal Parameters" for every preform type/bottle design to minimize setup time during product changeovers.
*   **Pitfall - Moisture:** Never use damp preforms; moisture trapped in the PET material causes "blushing" or cloudiness in the finished bottle.
*   **Pitfall - Over-heating:** Watch for "white stress marks" on the bottle base—this is a primary indicator of heater banks being set too high, which degrades plastic quality.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Why are my bottles coming out with deformed necks or finishes?**
A: This is usually caused by excessive heat reaching the neck area of the preform. Ensure the neck cooling system is operational and that the heating zone parameters are correctly isolated.

**Q: What should I do if the bottle is stuck in the mold?**
A: Immediately initiate an emergency stop. Once the machine is safe, manually vent the high-pressure air and use a non-marring tool to carefully remove the plastic. Do not use metal screwdrivers as they will scratch the mold surface.

**Q: How often should the blow nozzles be cleaned?**
A: Blow nozzles should be wiped down at the start of every shift and inspected for air-flow restrictions. A clogged nozzle is the most common cause of "short shots" or uneven wall thickness.

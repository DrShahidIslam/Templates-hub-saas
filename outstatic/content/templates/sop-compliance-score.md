# Standard Operating Procedure: SOP Compliance Scoring

The SOP Compliance Score serves as a critical operational metric designed to quantify the adherence of departmental processes to established organizational standards. By systematically auditing documentation, execution workflows, and output quality, this procedure ensures operational consistency, identifies training gaps, and mitigates regulatory or performance risks. This SOP outlines the methodology for calculating, reviewing, and remediating compliance scores to drive continuous improvement across all operational units.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Scope Definition
*   Identify the specific SOP or process family to be audited.
*   Define the audit period (e.g., monthly, quarterly, or ad-hoc).
*   Select the sample size of tasks or documentation logs to be reviewed.
*   Ensure the auditor has access to the most recent version of the SOP in the Document Management System (DMS).
*   Notify the department lead of the impending audit schedule to ensure transparency.

## Phase 2: Execution of Compliance Audit
*   Review objective evidence (e.g., logs, digital signatures, time-stamps) against each step defined in the SOP.
*   Assign a binary score (1 for compliant, 0 for non-compliant) to each process step.
*   Evaluate "Critical Control Points"—steps that directly impact safety, security, or legal compliance—with a weighted multiplier.
*   Document specific reasons for any non-compliance (e.g., human error, equipment failure, outdated SOP instructions).
*   Capture photographic or digital evidence of the process execution where applicable.

## Phase 3: Calculation and Reporting
*   Apply the formula: (Total Points Scored / Total Possible Points) x 100 = Final Compliance Percentage.
*   Categorize scores: 95-100% (Exemplary), 85-94% (Satisfactory), 70-84% (Needs Improvement), <70% (Critical).
*   Draft a summary report detailing the overall score, key findings, and identified trends.
*   Distribute the report to stakeholders within 48 hours of audit completion.

## Phase 4: Remediation and Feedback Loop
*   Schedule a debriefing session with the process owners to review low-scoring areas.
*   Develop a Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) plan for any item scoring below threshold.
*   Update the SOP if non-compliance is caused by the process being outdated or inefficient.
*   Implement targeted retraining for staff if the root cause is identified as knowledge deficiency.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use weighted scoring for critical steps. A process may be 90% compliant, but if the 10% failure rate involves a safety protocol, the overall score should be flagged as "At Risk."
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate data collection. Use digital forms or CRM audit logs rather than manual paper-based checklists to reduce administrative burden and bias.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "Checklist Fatigue." If an SOP has more than 20 steps, consider breaking it into sub-processes to ensure auditors remain focused.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use the score as a punitive tool. If staff feel threatened by low scores, they are likely to fabricate compliance data, rendering your metrics useless.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should we conduct compliance audits?**
A: High-risk operational areas should be audited monthly, while standard administrative processes can be audited quarterly or biannually based on performance stability.

**Q: What should I do if the SOP itself is the reason for low compliance?**
A: If the audit reveals that staff are ignoring steps because they are inefficient or obsolete, initiate an immediate SOP Change Request (SCR) to align the documentation with best-practice reality.

**Q: Can we use automated tools to monitor compliance?**
A: Yes. Many modern ERP and BPM systems offer real-time compliance dashboards that trigger alerts when a process step is skipped or performed outside of defined parameters.

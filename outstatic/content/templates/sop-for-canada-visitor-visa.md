# Standard Operating Procedure: Canada Visitor Visa (TRV) Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional administrative workflow for preparing and submitting a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) application for Canada. Adhering to these protocols ensures that all documentation meets the strict requirements of Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC), thereby minimizing the risk of application rejection due to procedural errors or missing evidence. This guide is intended for applicants aiming to demonstrate genuine intent and financial sufficiency.

## Phase 1: Eligibility and Strategy
*   **Confirm Purpose of Visit:** Clearly define if the visit is for tourism, visiting family, or business.
*   **Check Visa Requirements:** Verify if you require a TRV or an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) based on your nationality.
*   **Assessment of Ties:** Evaluate your ties to your home country (employment, property, family) to demonstrate your intent to return.
*   **Biometrics Check:** Confirm if you have provided biometrics in the last 10 years.

## Phase 2: Documentation Preparation
*   **Passport:** Ensure at least six months of validity beyond the intended departure date from Canada.
*   **Proof of Funds:**
    *   Last 4–6 months of personal bank statements (stamped by the bank).
    *   Employment letter (stating salary, tenure, and approved leave dates).
    *   Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 3 years.
*   **Invitation (If applicable):** Signed invitation letter from the Canadian host, including their status (PR or Citizen card copy) and proof of relationship.
*   **Travel Itinerary:** A detailed daily plan showing where you will stay and what you intend to do.
*   **Digital Requirements:** High-quality color scans of all documents in PDF format; passport-sized digital photos meeting IRCC specifications.

## Phase 3: Application Submission
*   **GCKey Account:** Create or log into your IRCC portal account.
*   **Questionnaire Completion:** Answer the screening questionnaire accurately to generate your specific document checklist.
*   **Document Upload:** Upload files ensuring they are legible and within the file size limit (max 4MB per file).
*   **Fee Payment:** Pay the application processing fee and biometrics fee via credit/debit card.
*   **Submission:** Finalize the application and download the confirmation of submission.

## Phase 4: Biometrics and Processing
*   **Biometrics Appointment:** Schedule and attend the VFS Global or Visa Application Centre (VAC) appointment within the requested timeframe.
*   **Passport Request:** Monitor the GCKey portal for the "Original Passport Request" (OPR) letter.
*   **Passport Submission:** Submit your physical passport to the nearest VAC for visa stamping.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Write a "Letter of Explanation" (LoE). Even if not explicitly mandatory, a one-page summary connecting your documents and explaining your ties to your home country significantly strengthens your case.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain consistent balances. Avoid large, unexplained cash deposits into your bank account immediately before applying, as this raises red flags for visa officers.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-documentation. Do not include irrelevant documents (e.g., high school certificates). Focus strictly on proof of finances and proof of intent to return.
*   **Pitfall:** Misrepresentation. Any discrepancy between your application details and your physical documents can result in a five-year ban from entering Canada.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is the most common reason for a Canada visitor visa refusal?**
The most common reason is "insufficient proof of ties to the home country." The visa officer must be convinced that you have enough personal, professional, and financial motivation to return home after your visit.

**2. Does having a high bank balance guarantee approval?**
No. While financial sufficiency is required, a high balance alone does not overcome a lack of social or professional roots in your home country. You must show the source of your funds and a stable income history.

**3. How long does the visa processing take?**
Processing times vary significantly based on your country of residence and current IRCC backlogs. Always check the official [IRCC Processing Times tool](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/application/check-processing-times.html) for the most current data for your specific region.

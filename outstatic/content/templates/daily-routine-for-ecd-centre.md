# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Routine for Early Childhood Development (ECD) Centres

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the primary operational framework for daily activities within our ECD centre. Adherence to this routine ensures a consistent, safe, and stimulating environment that supports the holistic development of every child. By standardizing opening procedures, instructional transitions, hygiene protocols, and dismissal processes, staff can focus on high-quality engagement while maintaining rigorous safety and compliance standards.

## 1. Morning Arrival and Health Screening
*   **Facility Inspection:** Conduct a perimeter and interior safety walk-through to ensure all areas are clear of hazards and that emergency exits are unobstructed.
*   **Sanitization Check:** Ensure all high-touch surfaces are wiped down and that handwashing stations are stocked with soap, paper towels, and sanitizer.
*   **Health Screening:** Execute mandatory health checks upon arrival (visual assessment, temperature checks where applicable) per current health department mandates.
*   **Logbook Entry:** Document daily attendance in the electronic or physical ledger, noting the arrival time and the name of the authorized adult dropping off the child.
*   **Transition Ritual:** Facilitate a structured hand-off from parents to staff to minimize separation anxiety.

## 2. Classroom Management and Routine Care
*   **Nutrition Management:** Verify all dietary restriction records before serving snacks or meals. Ensure all children wash hands thoroughly before and after eating.
*   **Hygiene Cycles:** Execute a scheduled diapering and toileting rotation. Maintain a visual log of bathroom breaks to ensure hygiene standards are met.
*   **Active Supervision:** Implement "counting heads" at every transition point (moving from indoor to outdoor, room to room).
*   **Learning Station Prep:** Set up age-appropriate provocations and learning materials 15 minutes before activity blocks commence.

## 3. Outdoor Play and Physical Activity
*   **Equipment Audit:** Verify that all outdoor climbing equipment and toys are stable and free of debris before letting children access the yard.
*   **Sun/Weather Safety:** Apply sunscreen or ensure protective clothing is worn based on the daily weather forecast.
*   **Supervision Ratios:** Maintain mandated staff-to-child ratios at all times during outdoor play. Position staff to ensure "line of sight" coverage of the entire play area.

## 4. Afternoon Transition and Dismissal
*   **Clean-up Ritual:** Incorporate "tidy-up" songs to encourage children to assist in organizing toys and supplies.
*   **Communication Prep:** Ensure daily reports (digital or physical) are completed, detailing the child’s mood, intake, and developmental highlights.
*   **ID Verification:** Strictly enforce the Authorized Pick-up List. Staff must request a photo ID if the individual is not recognized as a primary guardian.
*   **Final Facility Check:** Perform a sweep of all rooms to ensure no child is left behind, verify that all appliances are off, and lock all external doors.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

**Pro Tips:**
*   **Visual Schedules:** Post a pictorial daily schedule at the children's eye level. This helps children predict transitions and reduces behavioral outbursts.
*   **The "Two-Minute" Warning:** Give children a two-minute verbal warning before every transition (e.g., "In two minutes, we will clean up our blocks"). This allows them time to wrap up their play, reducing frustration.
*   **Proactive Hydration:** Schedule water breaks every 60–90 minutes, even if children do not explicitly ask, to prevent fatigue and irritability.

**Pitfalls to Avoid:**
*   **Relying on Memory:** Never rely on verbal hand-offs. Always document transitions, incidents, and supply counts in the master logbook.
*   **Staff Silos:** Avoid "zoning out" during supervision. Engage with the children during play rather than focusing solely on adult-to-adult conversation.
*   **Ignoring Subtle Changes:** Do not ignore a change in a child's demeanor or physical appearance. Report any red flags (lethargy, unexplained marks, etc.) to the center supervisor immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if an unauthorized person attempts to pick up a child?**
A: Do not release the child. Keep the child in a safe, supervised area and immediately contact the primary guardian on file. If the individual persists, alert your supervisor or center management to involve security or local law enforcement.

**Q: How often should the diaper changing or toilet areas be deep-cleaned?**
A: These areas must be spot-cleaned after every use and undergo a full, rigorous disinfection (using a food-safe, approved sanitizing agent) at least twice per day, as well as during the closing shift.

**Q: If a child has a minor scrape during outdoor play, what is the protocol?**
A: Administer first aid according to our First Aid Policy, fill out an incident report, and inform the parent or guardian immediately upon pick-up. Both the staff member and the parent/guardian must sign the incident report.

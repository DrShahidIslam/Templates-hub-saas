# Standard Operating Procedure: Fleet Vehicle Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the systematic protocol for the inspection, servicing, and preventative maintenance of all company-owned vehicles. The objective is to ensure maximum fleet uptime, operator safety, and compliance with manufacturer specifications and regulatory standards. Adherence to this procedure is mandatory for all fleet managers, drivers, and maintenance technicians to mitigate the risk of mechanical failure and prolong the operational lifespan of company assets.

## 1. Daily Pre-Trip Inspections (Driver Responsibility)
*   **Visual Exterior Check:** Inspect tires for adequate pressure and tread depth. Check for body damage, leaking fluids, or broken glass.
*   **Fluid Levels:** Verify engine oil, coolant, and windshield washer fluid levels.
*   **Lighting Systems:** Test headlights, high beams, brake lights, turn signals, and hazard lights.
*   **Internal Systems:** Confirm the functionality of the horn, seatbelts, windshield wipers, and dashboard warning indicators.
*   **Documentation:** Log the inspection in the digital fleet management system or the physical vehicle logbook.

## 2. Routine Preventative Maintenance (Technical Team)
*   **Engine Service:** Perform oil changes and filter replacements at designated mileage intervals (e.g., every 5,000–7,500 miles).
*   **Braking System:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and lines for wear; replace components as needed to meet safety thresholds.
*   **Tire Management:** Perform tire rotations, re-balancing, and alignment checks to prevent uneven wear.
*   **Cooling and Heating:** Check radiator hoses, coolant concentration, and HVAC efficiency to prevent overheating.
*   **Electrical System:** Test battery voltage and charging system performance to ensure reliable starting.

## 3. Scheduled Periodic Inspections
*   **Quarterly Audit:** Conduct an in-depth review of chassis components, suspension, steering linkages, and exhaust systems.
*   **Annual Compliance Check:** Verify state-required emissions testing, safety certifications, and registration validity.
*   **System Diagnostics:** Run OBD-II diagnostic scans to identify any "silent" error codes that have not yet triggered a dashboard warning light.

## 4. Emergency Repairs and Unscheduled Maintenance
*   **Incident Reporting:** Immediately report all mechanical failures or accidents to the Fleet Manager.
*   **Out-of-Service Protocol:** If a vehicle is deemed unsafe, place a "Do Not Operate" tag on the steering wheel and lock the keys in the secure drop box until cleared by a mechanic.
*   **Third-Party Coordination:** If repairs require specialized expertise, authorize work only through pre-approved vendors within the company network.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Predictive Scheduling:** Use fleet management software to set automated alerts for upcoming service intervals based on telematics data rather than just odometer readings.
*   **Standardized Checklists:** Use laminated, physical checklists for drivers to ensure consistency during daily inspections.
*   **Fluid Analysis:** For high-mileage vehicles, perform oil analysis to detect internal engine wear before catastrophic failure occurs.

### Pitfalls
*   **Ignoring Minor Warnings:** Treating "minor" issues (like a squeaky belt or a soft brake pedal) as non-urgent often leads to expensive, complex repairs.
*   **Inconsistent Logging:** Failing to record maintenance history lowers the vehicle's resale value and complicates warranty claims.
*   **Neglecting Tires:** Many fleet accidents are caused by under-inflated tires; treat tire pressure as a critical safety metric, not a secondary check.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How do we handle maintenance for vehicles currently in the field?**
A: Drivers are responsible for using pre-approved network service centers. All invoices must be uploaded to the central portal within 24 hours of service completion.

**Q: What should I do if a maintenance light comes on mid-trip?**
A: If the light is yellow, proceed to a safe location and call the Fleet Manager for instructions. If the light is red, pull over immediately, turn off the engine, and request roadside assistance.

**Q: Can a driver perform their own repairs?**
A: No. All maintenance must be performed by certified technicians to ensure warranty compliance and liability coverage. Unauthorized repairs are strictly prohibited.

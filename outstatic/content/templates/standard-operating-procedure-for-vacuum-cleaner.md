# Standard Operating Procedure: Vacuum Cleaner Maintenance and Operation

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the safe and efficient operation and maintenance of industrial and commercial-grade vacuum cleaners. Adherence to these guidelines ensures optimal suction performance, extends the lifespan of the equipment, and minimizes health and safety risks to the operator. All personnel responsible for floor care must be trained on this procedure before operating the equipment.

## Section 1: Pre-Operational Inspection
Before connecting the device to a power source, perform the following safety and readiness checks:
*   **Cord Integrity:** Inspect the power cord for fraying, exposed wiring, or cracked insulation. Do not operate if damage is detected.
*   **Hose/Attachment Check:** Ensure all hoses are free of clogs and securely attached to the intake port.
*   **Filter Verification:** Confirm the filter is properly seated. Never operate the unit without a functional, clean filter installed.
*   **Bag/Canister Status:** Check the dust bag or canister. If the bag is more than 3/4 full, replace it. If using a canister, ensure it is emptied and rinsed if necessary.
*   **Environment Scan:** Clear the floor of large, hard debris (e.g., coins, heavy plastic shards, or metal fasteners) that could damage the internal impeller.

## Section 2: Operational Procedures
Follow these steps to ensure effective cleaning while protecting the motor:
*   **Power Connection:** Plug the unit into a grounded outlet. Ensure the cord is positioned behind the operator to avoid tripping hazards or accidental vacuuming of the cord.
*   **Surface Adjustment:** Adjust the height/brush roll setting based on the floor type (Hardwood vs. Carpet). 
*   **Systematic Movement:** Use slow, overlapping passes. Moving too quickly prevents the vacuum from pulling dust from the base of carpet fibers.
*   **Airflow Monitoring:** Listen for changes in motor pitch. A high-pitched whine often indicates a blockage, while a "choked" sound suggests a full bag or severely clogged filter.
*   **Shutdown Protocol:** Turn the unit off before unplugging from the wall. Always pull the plug by the head, not the cord.

## Section 3: Post-Operational Maintenance
Regular maintenance prevents performance degradation and motor burnout:
*   **Emptying:** Empty the canister or replace the dust bag immediately after every use.
*   **Filter Cleaning:** Shake or brush off filters as required by the manufacturer’s specifications. Replace HEPA filters according to the established maintenance schedule.
*   **Brush Roll Clearance:** Inspect the brush roll for hair, string, or fibers. Use a seam ripper or scissors to cut away tangles carefully.
*   **Storage:** Coil the cord loosely (do not wrap it tightly around the unit) and store in a cool, dry area.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** For maximum efficiency, vacuum in two directions (e.g., North-South, then East-West) in high-traffic areas.
*   **Pro Tip:** If the vacuum has a washable filter, ensure it is 100% bone-dry before re-installing; a damp filter creates a breeding ground for mold and can damage the motor.
*   **Pitfall:** Never use a dry vacuum to clean up wet spills unless it is specifically rated as a "Wet/Dry Vac." Liquid intake will cause immediate electrical failure and shock risk.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid running the vacuum over its own cord; this is the number one cause of cord failure and terminal damage.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: My vacuum has lost suction, but the bag is empty. What should I check first?**
A: Check for a blockage in the hose. Detach the hose from the vacuum and look through it; if you can't see light at the other end, use a long, blunt tool to gently push the clog out.

**Q: How often should I replace the HEPA filter?**
A: Under standard operational conditions, HEPA filters should be replaced every 6 months or after every 100 hours of use, whichever comes first. Refer to the specific manufacturer’s manual for your unit.

**Q: Is it safe to vacuum up construction dust (drywall/plaster)?**
A: No. Fine construction dust will pass through standard bags and filters, potentially clogging the motor and circulating dust back into the air. Use an industrial vacuum with a dedicated fine-dust filtration system for such tasks.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Vehicle Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for conducting a comprehensive vehicle inspection for Uber operation. Maintaining high vehicle standards is critical to ensuring passenger safety, maintaining your driver rating, and adhering to local regulatory requirements. Drivers are expected to complete this inspection at the start of every shift to identify potential safety hazards and aesthetic issues before entering service.

## 1. Exterior Inspection
*   **Lights & Signals:** Test headlights (high and low beam), taillights, brake lights, reverse lights, and all four turn signals. Ensure lenses are free of cracks or moisture buildup.
*   **Tires:** Inspect tire tread depth (must exceed 2/32") and ensure proper inflation. Check for sidewall bulges, embedded debris, or exposed wire.
*   **Body Integrity:** Scan for significant dents, scratches, or missing trim that could result in a failed inspection by Uber or negative rider feedback.
*   **Glass & Visibility:** Verify that the windshield is free of major cracks or stars that obstruct the driver’s line of sight. Ensure all mirrors are secure and adjusted correctly.
*   **Windshield Wipers:** Ensure wiper blades are not streaking and that the fluid reservoir is full.

## 2. Interior & Cabin Cleanliness
*   **Upholstery:** Check seats for tears, stains, or lingering odors. Ensure seat belts are fully functional and click securely.
*   **Floorboards:** Remove all debris, trash, and excessive dust. Ensure floor mats are properly secured and do not obstruct the pedals.
*   **Climate Control:** Verify that both heating and air conditioning units are functioning properly to maintain passenger comfort.
*   **Door Mechanics:** Test all four interior and exterior door handles. Ensure child safety locks are disengaged unless specifically required.
*   **Driver Identification:** Ensure Uber trade dress (stickers/emblems) is clearly visible and placed according to local legal requirements.

## 3. Mechanical & Safety Systems
*   **Braking System:** Perform a stationary brake test to ensure the pedal feels firm and does not sink to the floor. Check for any grinding or squealing noises during initial movement.
*   **Dashboard Warnings:** Ensure no "Check Engine," "TPMS" (Tire Pressure), or "ABS" warning lights are illuminated.
*   **Fluids:** Verify engine oil, coolant, and brake fluid levels. Ensure there are no active leaks under the vehicle.
*   **Emergency Kit:** Confirm the presence of a functional jack, lug wrench, and a spare tire (or tire inflation kit).
*   **Tech Integration:** Verify that your smartphone mount is secure and that your charging cables are in good working order.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Carry a small "driver’s kit" in the trunk containing glass cleaner, a microfiber towel, and air freshener. A 60-second spot clean between rides significantly boosts tip potential.
*   **Pro Tip:** Document your vehicle’s condition with timestamped photos at the start of the week. This acts as a safeguard against false damage claims filed by passengers.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not ignore minor "nuisance" sounds (e.g., clicking or humming). These are often precursor indicators of mechanical failure that can leave you stranded during a trip.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid over-scenting the vehicle. Strong artificial fragrances are a common cause of low ratings; use neutral odor eliminators instead.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I conduct this full inspection?**
A: While a quick walk-around should happen daily, a full mechanical and interior deep-dive should be conducted weekly or every 500 miles, whichever comes first.

**Q: What should I do if my dashboard light turns on during a trip?**
A: Safely pull over at the first opportunity. If the light is yellow, check your manual for urgency. If the light is red (or if there is engine smoke/heat), end the trip, prioritize passenger safety, and contact Uber Support immediately to report an emergency.

**Q: Am I required to keep paper records of these inspections?**
A: While Uber may not require you to submit daily paper logs, keeping a digital or physical maintenance log is highly recommended for tax purposes and to prove vehicle upkeep in the event of a platform dispute.

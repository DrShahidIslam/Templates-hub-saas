# Standard Operating Procedure: COVID-19 Health & Safety Protocols (Pakistan)

This document outlines the mandatory operating procedures for maintaining a safe work environment in compliance with guidelines issued by the National Command and Operation Center (NCOC) and the Ministry of National Health Services, Regulations and Coordination (MoNHSRC), Pakistan. The objective of this SOP is to minimize transmission risks, ensure business continuity, and protect the workforce through rigorous hygiene, social distancing, and reactive management strategies.

## 1. Facility & Hygiene Management
*   **Entrance Screening:** Implement mandatory thermal scanning at all entry points. Deny entry to any individual displaying a temperature above 99°F (37.2°C).
*   **Sanitization Stations:** Place alcohol-based hand sanitizers (minimum 70% alcohol) at every entrance, near elevators, in meeting rooms, and at workstations.
*   **High-Touch Point Disinfection:** Sanitize door handles, light switches, elevator buttons, and common area surfaces at least four times daily using EPA-approved disinfectants.
*   **Ventilation:** Keep windows and doors open where possible to ensure cross-ventilation. If using central air conditioning, ensure regular filter cleaning and maximize fresh air intake.
*   **Signage:** Display NCOC-approved COVID-19 awareness posters in both Urdu and English in high-traffic areas, detailing handwashing techniques and respiratory etiquette.

## 2. Workforce Safety & Social Distancing
*   **Face Mask Policy:** Strictly enforce the wearing of surgical or cloth face masks for all employees and visitors while on the premises.
*   **Workspace Configuration:** Arrange desks to maintain a minimum distance of 6 feet between employees. Utilize physical barriers (plexiglass) where 6 feet is not feasible.
*   **Meeting Protocol:** Prioritize virtual meetings over face-to-face interactions. If in-person meetings are essential, restrict attendance to the minimum required and ensure the room capacity allows for distancing.
*   **Common Area Limitations:** Limit the number of people in break rooms and prayer areas (Masallah) to prevent crowding. Stagger lunch breaks to avoid peak congestion.
*   **Work-from-Home (WFH):** Encourage a hybrid work model for non-essential staff to reduce on-site density.

## 3. Incident Response & Reporting
*   **Isolation Area:** Designate a specific room or area as an isolation zone for employees showing symptoms during work hours.
*   **Contact Tracing:** Maintain a daily log of all visitors and non-standard staff movements to facilitate rapid contact tracing if a positive case occurs.
*   **Notification Protocol:** In the event of a confirmed positive case, the employee must notify the HR/Operations department immediately. The affected area must be deep-cleaned and disinfected before re-entry.
*   **Quarantine Compliance:** Adhere strictly to the current isolation period stipulated by the MoNHSRC for confirmed positive cases before allowing a return to the office.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Conduct "Spot Checks" on hygiene compliance. A surprise inspection by a designated Safety Officer often yields higher adherence to mask-wearing and sanitization protocols.
*   **Pro Tip:** Leverage the "Pass Track" app or other digital tools for tracking vaccination status and travel history of employees.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-reliance on "Fogging" or "Misting" as a primary cleaning method. These methods are ineffective against surface-bound pathogens; manual wiping of surfaces with disinfectant is far more reliable.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring mental health. Long-term adherence to strict protocols can lead to employee burnout. Acknowledge the stress caused by these protocols to maintain morale.

## FAQ
**Q: If an employee tests positive, is the whole office required to shut down?**
A: Not necessarily. Following the deep cleaning of the infected individual's workspace and tracing their immediate contacts for quarantine, the business can typically continue operations. Total shutdown is only required if there is evidence of wide-scale transmission within the facility.

**Q: Are surgical masks mandatory, or are cloth masks sufficient?**
A: While cloth masks are permitted for general use, surgical masks (or N95/KN95 in high-risk areas) are highly recommended for better protection against newer variants. Ensure masks cover both the nose and mouth entirely.

**Q: Should we demand proof of vaccination from all visitors?**
A: Yes. In alignment with Pakistani government policies for public/commercial spaces, it is advised to check vaccination certificates (or the NADRA-issued digital QR code) for all external visitors to ensure a fully vaccinated environment.

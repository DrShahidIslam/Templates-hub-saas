# Standard Operating Procedure: Residential Property Leasing

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional requirements and systematic approach for leasing a residential property. The objective is to mitigate financial and legal risks, ensure tenant suitability, and maintain the long-term value of the asset. By following this protocol, property managers and owners can ensure a seamless transition between occupancies, uphold compliance with local landlord-tenant laws, and foster professional tenant relationships.

## Phase 1: Property Preparation and Marketing
*   [ ] **Deep Clean and Maintenance:** Conduct a professional cleaning and address all minor repairs (leaky faucets, burned-out bulbs, paint touch-ups).
*   [ ] **Staging:** Ensure the property is presented in its best light. Use high-quality photography and, if applicable, virtual tours.
*   [ ] **Pricing Strategy:** Conduct a comparative market analysis (CMA) to set a competitive rental rate.
*   [ ] **Marketing Deployment:** List the property on primary real estate platforms, social media, and local community boards.
*   [ ] **Safety Certification:** Verify all smoke detectors, carbon monoxide detectors, and fire extinguishers are compliant with local codes.

## Phase 2: Tenant Screening and Due Diligence
*   [ ] **Showing Protocol:** Conduct pre-screening calls to qualify prospective tenants before scheduling physical walk-throughs.
*   [ ] **Application Collection:** Require a formal application including government-issued ID, proof of income (last 3 pay stubs), and bank statements.
*   [ ] **Background Check:** Verify credit reports (targeting a minimum score), criminal history, and eviction records.
*   [ ] **Reference Verification:** Contact previous landlords to verify payment history and property care.
*   [ ] **Employment Verification:** Confirm current employment status and tenure with the applicant's HR department or supervisor.

## Phase 3: Lease Documentation and Execution
*   [ ] **Lease Agreement Drafting:** Utilize a legally vetted lease agreement that conforms to state and municipal landlord-tenant regulations.
*   [ ] **Disclosure Integration:** Include mandatory disclosures (e.g., lead-based paint, flood zone status, or building rules).
*   [ ] **Security Deposit Policy:** Outline the security deposit amount, terms of holding, and specific conditions for deductions.
*   [ ] **Signature Execution:** Use a digital signature platform for transparency and audit trails. Ensure all adult occupants are listed on the lease.

## Phase 4: Move-In Procedures
*   [ ] **Condition Report:** Conduct a comprehensive move-in inspection with the tenant. Document existing conditions via photos or video.
*   [ ] **Utility Transfer:** Confirm that the tenant has successfully transferred necessary utilities (gas, electric, water) into their name.
*   [ ] **Handover:** Issue keys, garage remotes, and access fobs. Provide a digital "Welcome Packet" containing emergency contacts and maintenance request procedures.
*   [ ] **First Month Payment:** Ensure the full first month's rent and security deposit are received via cleared funds (bank draft or electronic transfer) before handing over possession.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always require renters' insurance. It protects the tenant’s personal property and provides liability coverage, which reduces your own legal exposure.
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate rent collection. Using a dedicated portal prevents "check-in-the-mail" delays and streamlines accounting.
*   **Pitfall:** Never skip the move-in inspection. Without a signed condition report, it is legally difficult to deduct damage costs from the security deposit later.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "handshake" agreements. Always memorialize every change to the lease—no matter how minor—in writing.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I require a specific credit score for potential tenants?**
A: Yes, establishing a minimum credit score is a standard business practice. However, ensure this criterion is applied consistently to every applicant to comply with Fair Housing laws.

**Q: What should I do if a tenant asks to modify the property (e.g., painting walls)?**
A: All requested modifications should be submitted in writing. If approved, outline in a formal addendum whether the tenant is responsible for reverting the property to its original state upon move-out.

**Q: How often should I conduct mid-lease inspections?**
A: It is generally recommended to perform a "drive-by" or scheduled interior inspection every 6 months to ensure the property is being maintained properly, provided you give the tenant appropriate notice as required by local law.

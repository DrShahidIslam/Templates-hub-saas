# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance (PM) Management via Mobile Application

This SOP outlines the standardized procedure for managing, tracking, and executing preventive maintenance tasks using the designated facility management application. The objective of this protocol is to maximize equipment uptime, ensure regulatory compliance, and maintain a rigorous audit trail of all maintenance activities. Adherence to these steps ensures that every asset is accounted for, maintenance intervals are strictly observed, and technician accountability is maintained through real-time digital verification.

## Phase 1: Scheduling and Asset Configuration
*   **Asset Registration:** Input all equipment serial numbers, installation dates, and manufacturer specifications into the app to establish the digital asset registry.
*   **Defining Intervals:** Set recurring PM triggers based on time (e.g., monthly) or usage metrics (e.g., run-hours/odometer) as recommended by the manufacturer.
*   **Task Template Creation:** Build standardized digital checklists for each asset type, ensuring all mandatory safety checks and lubrication/calibration steps are included.
*   **Notification Setup:** Configure automated push notifications and email alerts to dispatch technicians 48 hours prior to the task due date.

## Phase 2: Execution and Data Entry
*   **Task Assignment:** Review the dashboard daily; technicians must "Claim" tasks before beginning work to ensure accurate labor tracking.
*   **On-Site Verification:** Scan the asset’s unique QR code/Barcode via the app to ensure the correct machine is being serviced.
*   **Step-by-Step Compliance:** Execute all mandatory fields in the digital checklist. Do not bypass any required safety verification steps.
*   **Documentation:** Upload high-resolution photos of the equipment state (before and after maintenance) and any findings (e.g., worn belts, fluid leaks).
*   **Part Consumption:** Log all parts used (e.g., filters, lubricants) to update the inventory module automatically.

## Phase 3: Review and Quality Assurance
*   **Supervisor Validation:** Review completed work orders within 24 hours of submission for accuracy and completeness.
*   **Close-Out:** Approve the work order in the system to finalize the record and reset the clock for the next PM cycle.
*   **Trend Analysis:** Monthly, export maintenance reports to identify repeat failures, high-cost assets, or bottlenecks in technician performance.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Voice-to-Text" feature in the app when your hands are occupied or you are in a tight space to record observations without removing PPE.
*   **Pro Tip:** Set up a "High Priority" flag for assets that are critical to the production line; these should be prioritized over standard facility upkeep in the daily view.
*   **Pitfall:** "Checklist Fatigue." Avoid creating overly granular checklists that require 50+ clicks; focus on mission-critical inspection points to keep data entry accurate.
*   **Pitfall:** Relying on mobile offline mode for too long. Ensure technicians sync their device at the end of every shift to prevent data loss or server synchronization errors.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if the app fails to sync while I am in an offline area?**
A: Continue your work and finish the checklist. The app will cache the data locally. Once you return to an area with connectivity, open the app dashboard and manually trigger a "Sync" or "Push Data" command to ensure the server updates.

**Q: How do I handle emergency/unscheduled repairs in the app?**
A: Use the "Create Ad-Hoc Work Order" feature. Never record an emergency repair under a scheduled PM work order, as this will skew your data and distort your preventive maintenance metrics.

**Q: Can we customize the checklists for specific seasonal requirements?**
A: Yes. Use the "Template Versioning" feature in the settings menu to create seasonal variations (e.g., winterizing cooling towers) while keeping the core inspection steps intact.

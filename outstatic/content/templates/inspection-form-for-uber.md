# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Vehicle Safety and Quality Inspection

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory inspection protocols for all vehicles operating under the Uber platform. Maintaining a high standard of vehicle safety and cleanliness is critical to ensuring passenger comfort, regulatory compliance, and driver platform longevity. This guide serves as a comprehensive framework to standardize the inspection process, ensuring every vehicle meets both Uber’s minimum requirements and local legislative standards. Drivers are expected to perform these inspections systematically to mitigate operational risks and maintain high star ratings.

## Pre-Inspection Checklist

## H2: Exterior Condition and Safety
*   **Lights and Indicators:** Verify that high/low beams, turn signals (front/rear), brake lights, hazard lights, and reverse lights are fully functional.
*   **Body Integrity:** Inspect for significant dents, deep scratches, or sharp edges that could pose a safety risk or negatively impact brand perception.
*   **Tires:** Check for adequate tread depth (minimum 4/32 inch recommended) and inspect for any bulges, sidewall cuts, or uneven wear patterns.
*   **Glass and Visibility:** Ensure the windshield is free of cracks or chips that obstruct the driver’s view. Verify that all side mirrors and the rearview mirror are securely mounted and adjustable.
*   **Wipers:** Test windshield wipers for streak-free performance and ensure washer fluid reservoirs are topped off.

## H2: Interior Cleanliness and Amenities
*   **Upholstery:** Inspect seats for tears, stains, or excessive wear. Ensure seat covers (if used) are clean and properly fitted.
*   **Floor Condition:** Remove debris, dirt, or mud from floor mats. Ensure mats are not obstructing the accelerator or brake pedals.
*   **Climate Control:** Verify that the A/C and heating systems function effectively across all vents.
*   **Seatbelts:** Confirm that all seatbelts (front and rear) extend and retract smoothly and that buckles click securely into place.
*   **Door Mechanics:** Test all four interior and exterior door handles to ensure they open and close reliably for passengers.

## H2: Mechanical and Operational Systems
*   **Braking System:** Ensure the pedal feels firm and the vehicle comes to a stop without grinding noises or pulling to one side.
*   **Dashboard Warnings:** Confirm there are no active "Check Engine," "ABS," "Airbag," or other critical warning lights illuminated on the instrument cluster.
*   **Fluid Levels:** Check oil, coolant, and brake fluid levels. Ensure there are no visible leaks under the vehicle.
*   **Horn:** Verify the horn is audible and operational as per local safety regulations.
*   **Emergency Kit:** Ensure the vehicle is equipped with a functional spare tire, jack, lug wrench, and a basic first-aid kit.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Document Everything:** Keep a digital or physical log of your inspections. If you encounter a minor issue, photograph it and document when you intend to have it repaired.
*   **Pro Tip: The "Sniff Test":** Vehicles retain odors. Use high-quality, unscented upholstery cleaners rather than heavy perfumes, which can trigger allergies or discomfort in passengers.
*   **Pitfall: The "Check Engine" Light:** Never ignore a warning light. Uber’s automated systems and passenger reports can lead to temporary deactivation if a vehicle is flagged for safety concerns.
*   **Pitfall: Obstructed Views:** Hanging items from the rearview mirror or placing non-essential stickers on the windshield can lead to a failed inspection and potential traffic citations.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should I perform this inspection?**
While Uber requires formal inspections annually (or as mandated by local laws), you should perform a "quick-look" inspection of the exterior and interior before every shift to ensure passenger satisfaction.

**2. What happens if my vehicle fails a formal Uber-required inspection?**
If your vehicle fails, you will generally be given a specific window of time to address the identified issues. You must have the repairs completed and provide proof or pass a re-inspection to remain active on the platform.

**3. Do I need to be a mechanic to perform these checks?**
No, these checks are designed for the driver. However, if you notice mechanical issues such as unusual engine noises, fluid leaks, or braking concerns, you must consult a certified professional mechanic immediately. Do not attempt "DIY" repairs on safety-critical systems.

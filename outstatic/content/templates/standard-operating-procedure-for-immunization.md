# Standard Operating Procedure: Immunization Administration

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory clinical protocols for the safe, effective, and efficient administration of vaccines. Adherence to these guidelines is essential to maintain the cold chain, ensure patient safety, minimize adverse reactions, and uphold accurate clinical documentation. All staff members authorized to administer immunizations must be trained annually on these procedures and remain current with regional and national health authority guidelines.

## 1. Pre-Administration Preparation
*   **Cold Chain Verification:** Confirm that the vaccine refrigerator temperature has been logged within the required range (typically 2°C to 8°C) for the preceding 24 hours.
*   **Verification of Patient Identity:** Utilize two patient identifiers (Full Name and Date of Birth) to confirm identity before vaccine selection.
*   **Screening for Contraindications:** Review the patient’s health history for allergies to vaccine components (e.g., egg proteins, neomycin), history of Guillain-Barré syndrome, or current febrile illnesses.
*   **Consent:** Provide the Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) or equivalent educational material and obtain informed, documented consent.
*   **Workspace Sanitation:** Ensure the administration area is clean and that all necessary emergency supplies (e.g., epinephrine kit, CPR mask) are readily accessible.

## 2. Vaccine Preparation
*   **Visual Inspection:** Check the vaccine vial for cloudiness, discoloration, or foreign particulates. Do not administer if the product appears compromised.
*   **Expiry Check:** Verify the expiration date. If the vaccine has been reconstituted, ensure it is used within the manufacturer-specified time window.
*   **Syringe Labeling:** If drawing up multiple vaccines, label each syringe clearly with the vaccine name, dosage, and expiration date/time.
*   **Aseptic Technique:** Sanitize hands according to WHO/CDC guidelines. Clean the vial stopper with a sterile alcohol swab and allow it to dry before needle insertion.

## 3. Administration Protocol
*   **Positioning:** Ensure the patient is in a seated or lying position to prevent injury in the event of vasovagal syncope.
*   **Site Selection:** Confirm the appropriate injection site based on age and muscle mass (typically the deltoid for adults; the vastus lateralis for infants).
*   **Needle Insertion:** Perform the injection using the correct angle (90 degrees for IM; 45 degrees for SQ) using the appropriate needle length based on patient body mass.
*   **Injection Technique:** Inject the medication steadily, then withdraw the needle and immediately engage the safety mechanism. **Never recap needles.**
*   **Post-Injection Care:** Apply pressure with sterile gauze; use an adhesive bandage if bleeding persists.

## 4. Documentation and Monitoring
*   **Medical Record Entry:** Record the vaccine name, manufacturer, lot number, expiration date, site of administration, route, and the date/time of administration in the Electronic Health Record (EHR).
*   **Observation:** Monitor the patient for a minimum of 15 minutes post-administration for signs of an immediate allergic reaction (anaphylaxis).
*   **Reporting:** Ensure the administration is reported to the relevant immunization registry if required by local law.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always utilize the "Look-Alike, Sound-Alike" (LASA) check. Before drawing up the vaccine, read the label aloud to ensure it matches the order exactly.
*   **Pro Tip:** If a patient is anxious, have them focus on a specific task or deep breathing to mitigate the risk of fainting.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not store vaccines in the door of the refrigerator; temperature fluctuations are too extreme. Always keep vaccines in the central body of the unit.
*   **Pitfall:** Never pre-fill syringes unless administration is immediate. Vaccine efficacy can degrade, and the risk of medication error increases significantly with pre-filled, unlabeled syringes.

## FAQ
**Q: What should I do if the refrigerator temperature falls outside the acceptable range?**
A: Immediately mark the vaccines "Do Not Use," isolate them in a temperature-controlled environment, and notify the immunization lead or facility manager. Do not discard vaccines until you have received confirmation from the manufacturer or public health officials regarding their viability.

**Q: Is it necessary to aspirate before injecting the vaccine?**
A: No. Current global clinical guidelines indicate that aspiration is not necessary for standard vaccinations, as there are no large blood vessels in the recommended injection sites.

**Q: How long should a patient remain in the clinic after receiving a vaccine?**
A: A 15-minute observation period is the industry standard for most immunizations to monitor for syncope or immediate allergic reactions. For patients with a history of severe allergies, consider extending this to 30 minutes.

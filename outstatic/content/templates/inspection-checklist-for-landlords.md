# Standard Operating Procedure: Rental Property Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides a structured framework for landlords and property managers to conduct comprehensive move-in, routine, and move-out inspections. Consistent and meticulous documentation is essential for maintaining asset value, ensuring tenant compliance with lease terms, and protecting legal interests during security deposit reconciliations. Adherence to this checklist minimizes liability and fosters a professional landlord-tenant relationship.

## 1. Exterior & Perimeter Inspection
*   **Roof and Gutters:** Inspect for loose shingles, debris, or sagging gutters.
*   **Siding and Trim:** Check for rot, peeling paint, or physical damage.
*   **Foundation:** Scan for visible cracks or shifting.
*   **Landscaping:** Confirm trees are trimmed back from the structure; verify lawn maintenance compliance.
*   **Entryways:** Test all exterior door locks, deadbolts, and screen doors for proper operation.
*   **Exterior Lighting:** Test porch lights and motion sensors; replace burnt-out bulbs.

## 2. Interior Common Areas & Living Spaces
*   **Walls and Ceilings:** Check for water stains, holes, or unauthorized paint/wallpaper.
*   **Flooring:** Inspect carpet for stains/tears and hardwood/tile for scratches, gouges, or warping.
*   **Windows:** Ensure all windows lock securely and screens are intact.
*   **Electrical:** Test all outlets with a circuit tester; ensure light switches function.
*   **HVAC:** Check air filters for cleanliness; verify thermostat responsiveness.

## 3. Kitchen & Utility Areas
*   **Appliances:** Run the dishwasher, test oven burners, confirm refrigerator cooling, and verify microwave operation.
*   **Plumbing:** Check under all sinks for active leaks or signs of water damage; test faucet pressure and drainage.
*   **Cabinetry:** Ensure drawers slide smoothly and hinges are not loose or broken.
*   **Countertops:** Look for heat damage, deep scratches, or staining.

## 4. Bathrooms
*   **Toilets:** Flush to check for proper cycling, handle stability, and leaks at the base.
*   **Shower/Tub:** Inspect caulking/grout for mold or separation; test water pressure and drain speed.
*   **Ventilation:** Confirm exhaust fans are functional and clear of heavy dust.
*   **Fixtures:** Inspect vanity and mirrors for chips or silvering.

## 5. Safety & Compliance
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test every unit; ensure they are properly mounted and have fresh batteries.
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Check expiration dates and ensure they are mounted in accessible locations.
*   **Exits:** Verify that all egress windows and doors are unobstructed.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Photo Log" Rule:** Always take date-stamped, high-resolution photos of every room, specifically focusing on pre-existing damage. Digital documentation is your primary evidence in a dispute.
*   **Tenant Participation:** Always encourage the tenant to be present during move-in and move-out inspections. This promotes transparency and reduces "surprise" deductions from security deposits.
*   **Pitfall - The "Quick Glance":** Never rush an inspection. Skipping a look under the kitchen sink or behind a door can result in thousands of dollars in hidden water damage or structural neglect.
*   **Pitfall - Vague Language:** Avoid descriptors like "good condition." Instead, be specific: "Two-inch gouge on the northwest corner of the living room laminate flooring."

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should I conduct routine inspections?**
It is standard practice to perform interior inspections once every six months. Always check local municipal laws, as some jurisdictions limit the frequency of inspections.

**2. Can I inspect the property without the tenant present?**
Generally, no. You must provide proper written notice (usually 24–48 hours, depending on state law) before entering. Always attempt to schedule the inspection during a time that is convenient for the tenant to maintain a positive relationship.

**3. What should I do if I find lease violations during an inspection?**
Document the violation with photos and notes. Provide the tenant with a written "Notice to Cure," outlining the specific issue and a reasonable timeframe for them to correct it. Follow up with a second inspection to verify the correction.

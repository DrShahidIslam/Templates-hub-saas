# Standard Operating Procedure: Facility and Equipment Decontamination

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the decontamination of surfaces, equipment, and environment within this facility. Adherence to these procedures is critical to ensuring a safe working environment, mitigating cross-contamination risks, and complying with health and safety regulatory standards. This document applies to all personnel, contractors, and visitors tasked with cleaning or sanitizing sensitive areas.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Safety
*   **Verify PPE Requirements:** Consult the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for the specific chemical agent in use. Don appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including gloves, eye protection, and respiratory protection if required.
*   **Area Assessment:** Ensure the area to be decontaminated is clear of unnecessary personnel. Post signage indicating "Decontamination in Progress—Authorized Personnel Only."
*   **Supply Readiness:** Gather all necessary materials, including hospital-grade disinfectants, microfiber cloths, waste disposal bags, and cleaning tools. 
*   **Ventilation Check:** Confirm that the ventilation system is operational or that windows are open to ensure adequate airflow, unless the protocol requires a sealed environment.

## Phase 2: Execution of Decontamination
*   **Pre-Cleaning (Removal of Gross Contamination):** Remove visible debris, dirt, or organic matter using a detergent-based solution. Disinfectants are often inactivated by organic matter; surface must be physically clean before disinfection.
*   **Application of Disinfectant:** Apply the chemical agent according to the manufacturer’s instructions (spraying, wiping, or fogging).
*   **Adhere to Dwell Time:** Ensure the surface remains visibly wet for the full "contact time" specified on the disinfectant label (e.g., 3–10 minutes). Do not allow the surface to air dry before the contact time has elapsed.
*   **Mechanical Action:** Use a directional wiping technique (e.g., "S" pattern or top-to-bottom) to prevent re-depositing contaminants. Never use a circular motion, as this re-spreads pathogens.
*   **Waste Management:** Place all used cloths and disposable PPE into a designated biohazard or hazardous waste bag immediately after use.

## Phase 3: Post-Decontamination Verification
*   **Visual Inspection:** Conduct a thorough visual sweep to ensure no residues or skipped spots remain.
*   **Equipment Reset:** Return all cleaning equipment to the designated storage area. Ensure spray bottles are clearly labeled and stored safely.
*   **PPE Removal:** Remove PPE in the correct order (gloves first, then eye protection, then gown/coveralls) to avoid self-contamination. Perform hand hygiene immediately after PPE removal.
*   **Documentation:** Log the decontamination event in the facility master log, noting the date, time, agent used, and the initials of the personnel involved.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always dilute concentrated chemicals using distilled water if specified; tap water minerals can occasionally interfere with the chemical stability of the disinfectant.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Clean to Dirty" approach. Always start your decontamination process in the cleanest area of the room and progress toward the dirtiest area to avoid spreading contaminants.
*   **Pitfall (The "Double Dipping" Error):** Never dip a dirty cloth back into the disinfectant solution. Use a "one-wipe, one-side" rule, folding the cloth to a fresh side for each new surface area.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Expiration):** Always check the expiration date of chemical agents. Expired disinfectants lose efficacy, creating a false sense of security while leaving surfaces contaminated.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I mix different cleaning chemicals to make a stronger solution?**
A: Absolutely not. Mixing chemicals (such as bleach and ammonia) can create toxic, potentially lethal gases. Always use chemicals exactly as prescribed by the manufacturer.

**Q: How do I determine the required dwell time for a specific product?**
A: The required contact/dwell time is always printed on the manufacturer’s label or listed on the product’s specific Safety Data Sheet (SDS). If you cannot locate this information, do not use the product.

**Q: What should I do if a chemical splash occurs during the decontamination process?**
A: Immediately consult the SDS for first-aid procedures specific to that chemical. Flush the affected area with water for at least 15 minutes and report the incident to the Safety Officer or your direct supervisor immediately.

# Standard Operating Procedure: UTEP Audit Form Execution

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for completing the University/Utility Technical Evaluation and Performance (UTEP) audit form. The objective of this procedure is to ensure systematic data collection, maintain compliance with operational standards, and guarantee that all technical assets are evaluated against verified performance benchmarks. Adherence to this process is critical for identifying potential systemic risks and ensuring accurate reporting for administrative review.

## Phase 1: Pre-Audit Preparation
*   **Verify Access:** Ensure active credentials for the UTEP portal and offline data capture tools.
*   **Calibration Check:** Confirm that all measurement instruments (if physical hardware is involved) have been calibrated within the last 12 months.
*   **Document Retrieval:** Gather previous audit reports, maintenance logs, and current asset specifications to serve as a baseline for comparison.
*   **Stakeholder Notification:** Notify department heads 48 hours in advance regarding expected downtime or access requirements during the audit.

## Phase 2: Field Data Collection
*   **Visual Inspection:** Conduct a perimeter and interior walk-through of the target asset. Record any signs of wear, corrosion, or unauthorized modifications.
*   **Parameter Verification:** Input real-time operating metrics (e.g., temperature, throughput, pressure) into the UTEP form.
*   **Photo Evidence:** Capture high-resolution images of asset nameplates, serial numbers, and any detected anomalies as required by the form’s "Evidence" section.
*   **Error Logging:** Cross-reference any reading that falls outside the standard operating range with the manufacturer’s technical manual.

## Phase 3: Form Completion and Validation
*   **Data Input:** Populate all mandatory fields in the digital UTEP form. Use the provided drop-down menus to ensure data standardization.
*   **Narrative Justification:** Provide concise, objective summaries for any "Non-Compliant" or "Needs Attention" flags. Avoid subjective language.
*   **Cross-Validation:** Review all numerical entries for decimal accuracy and proper unit formatting (e.g., PSI vs. BAR).
*   **Digital Sign-off:** Attach digital signatures for both the primary auditor and the department supervisor before final submission.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Use Dictation Tools:** If performing an audit in a high-noise environment, use mobile speech-to-text features to record observations immediately, then transcribe them into the form during the review phase.
*   **Batching:** When auditing multiple assets, save your progress frequently in the offline portal to prevent data loss due to session timeouts.
*   **Baseline Referencing:** Keep a PDF copy of the most recent audit on your tablet for immediate "side-by-side" comparison during the current walkthrough.

### Pitfalls
*   **"Rubber Stamping":** Do not auto-populate all fields based on previous audits. Each audit must represent a fresh verification of current state.
*   **Vague Descriptions:** Avoid using terms like "okay" or "fine." Use descriptive terminology like "within operating tolerance" or "minor surface oxidation observed."
*   **Skipping Mandatory Photos:** Missing a required photo will trigger a system validation error, potentially delaying your submission by 24 hours while you return to the site.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if I discover an emergency safety issue during the audit?**
A: Stop the audit immediately. Secure the area, notify your direct supervisor and the Safety/Compliance office, and follow the emergency lockout/tagout (LOTO) protocols before returning to complete the form.

**Q: Can I edit the UTEP form after I have pressed "Submit"?**
A: No. Once the form is submitted, it is locked for administrative review. If you discover a critical error, you must file a "Correction Request" form via the IT Help Desk portal referencing the original submission ID.

**Q: How long should I retain my notes after the form is submitted?**
A: Per operational policy, you must retain physical or digital field notes for a minimum of 90 days following the submission of the audit to allow for any post-audit inquiries.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Organizational Vehicle Use

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory protocols for the safe, efficient, and professional use of company-owned or leased vehicles. The objective of this policy is to minimize operational risk, ensure compliance with local traffic regulations, maintain vehicle longevity, and uphold the organization’s professional reputation. All employees authorized to operate company vehicles are expected to adhere to these guidelines without exception.

## 1. Pre-Departure Requirements
*   **Documentation Check:** Verify that the glove compartment contains current registration, proof of insurance, and the company’s accident report kit.
*   **Visual Inspection:** Conduct a "walk-around" to check for new dents, scratches, or broken glass. Report any discrepancies to the Fleet Manager before departure.
*   **Fluid & Tire Check:** Ensure tires are properly inflated and verify that fuel levels are sufficient for the intended trip (minimum 1/4 tank). 
*   **Safety Equipment:** Confirm the presence and viability of the fire extinguisher, first aid kit, and emergency roadside triangles.
*   **Driver Authentication:** Ensure your personal driver’s license is valid and on your person.

## 2. Operational Protocols
*   **Compliance:** Strictly adhere to all local traffic laws, including speed limits, traffic signals, and stop signs. The company will not reimburse fines resulting from driver negligence.
*   **Distracted Driving:** Use of hand-held mobile devices while the vehicle is in motion is strictly prohibited. Use hands-free systems only for essential professional communication.
*   **Passenger Safety:** Ensure all passengers are wearing seatbelts. Do not exceed the vehicle’s rated passenger capacity.
*   **Alcohol & Substance Policy:** Zero-tolerance policy. Operating a company vehicle under the influence of alcohol or any substance that impairs judgment is grounds for immediate termination.
*   **Cargo Management:** Ensure all equipment and cargo are properly secured to prevent movement during transit.

## 3. Post-Trip & Maintenance Procedures
*   **Refueling:** Return the vehicle with a full tank of fuel if the journey exceeds 50 miles, or as directed by department policy.
*   **Interior Sanitization:** Remove all trash, personal items, and debris. Ensure the cabin is left in a professional condition for the next user.
*   **Damage Reporting:** Immediately log any new incidents, mechanical noises, or warning lights that appeared during your shift in the Fleet Maintenance Log.
*   **Key Security:** Return keys to the designated secure lockbox or fleet office immediately upon completion of the shift.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always take photos of the vehicle from all four corners before and after your trip. This protects you from being held liable for pre-existing damage.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a dedicated GPS/navigation app to plan routes in advance. This reduces the urge to handle your phone while driving and minimizes fuel consumption through efficient routing.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "Check Engine" or other dashboard warning lights. Even if the vehicle seems to be driving fine, continue operation can lead to catastrophic failure and significant repair costs.
*   **Pitfall:** Failing to report minor scrapes. Concealing minor damage often leads to bigger issues regarding insurance claims and accountability. Always be transparent.

## FAQ
**Q: What should I do if I am involved in an accident?**
A: Prioritize safety. Move to a safe area, call emergency services if there are injuries, contact the police for an official report, and immediately notify your supervisor. Do not admit fault at the scene.

**Q: Can I use the company vehicle for personal errands?**
A: Company vehicles are strictly for business-related travel. Any personal use must be pre-authorized in writing by your department manager.

**Q: What if the vehicle breaks down during a shift?**
A: Pull over safely, engage hazard lights, and contact the fleet department or the roadside assistance number located on the insurance card provided in the vehicle. Do not attempt major roadside repairs yourself.

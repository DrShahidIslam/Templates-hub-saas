# Standard Operating Procedure: Bakery Operations & Quality Control

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for daily bakery operations, encompassing food safety, production consistency, and inventory management. Adherence to these guidelines ensures product uniformity, minimizes waste, and maintains full compliance with local health department regulations. All staff are expected to follow these procedures to uphold the bakery’s commitment to quality and safety.

## Phase 1: Pre-Opening & Facility Preparation
*   **Hygiene Check:** Ensure all staff are in clean uniforms, with hair restrained and hands sanitized.
*   **Temperature Logging:** Record the internal temperature of all reach-in refrigerators and walk-in freezers (Target: <40°F/4°C for refrigeration; <0°F/-18°C for freezers).
*   **Calibration:** Verify that digital scales are calibrated and ovens are preheated to the designated specifications for the day’s production schedule.
*   **Sanitization:** Sanitize all food-contact surfaces using an approved food-grade sanitizer before any ingredient preparation begins.

## Phase 2: Ingredient Preparation & Mixing
*   **Mise en Place:** Assemble all ingredients according to the production sheet before beginning mixing to prevent errors.
*   **Measurement:** Use weight (grams) rather than volume (cups) for all dry ingredients to ensure maximum consistency.
*   **Allergen Control:** Clearly label containers to prevent cross-contamination, especially when handling nuts, dairy, or gluten-free alternatives.
*   **Batch Tracking:** Record lot numbers of flour, eggs, and dairy to ensure traceability in the event of an ingredient recall.

## Phase 3: Baking & Cooling
*   **Oven Loading:** Follow the "first-in, first-out" rule for tray placement; ensure even spacing for optimal airflow.
*   **Quality Monitoring:** Use internal thermometers for large loaves to confirm full doneness before removing from the oven.
*   **Cooling Protocol:** Transfer baked goods to cooling racks immediately. Do not stack hot products, as this causes trapped steam and "soggy bottoms."

## Phase 4: Packaging & Display
*   **Visual Inspection:** Discard any items that do not meet the aesthetic "Gold Standard" (e.g., uneven browning, structural collapse).
*   **Labeling:** Apply labels including the product name, date of manufacture, allergen warning, and "Best Before" date.
*   **FIFO Display:** Rearrange display cases to ensure the oldest stock is at the front and freshest stock is at the back.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use an ice-water bath for dough mixing during summer months to prevent premature yeast activity and maintain gluten structure.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "Waste Log" book. Analyzing why items were discarded (e.g., overbaking vs. low demand) is critical for profit optimization.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not crowd the oven. Overloading causes temperature drops and inconsistent bake cycles, leading to wasted batches.
*   **Pitfall:** Neglecting the "dry-to-wet" ratio. Always incorporate dry ingredients gradually to prevent clumping and over-mixing, which leads to tough textures.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should the ovens be cleaned?**
A: A deep clean of the oven interiors should be performed weekly, with daily spot-cleaning of debris to prevent smoke and burnt flavor carry-over.

**Q: What is the procedure if a thermometer fails during a shift?**
A: Immediately stop production using that piece of equipment. Notify the manager, use a backup calibrated thermometer, and submit a maintenance request for the faulty unit.

**Q: How do we handle customer allergen inquiries?**
A: Staff must never guess. Always refer to the master ingredient list kept in the binder at the front counter. If the information is not clear, advise the customer that the product is "not allergen-certified."

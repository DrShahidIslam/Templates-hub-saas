# Standard Operating Procedure: New Homeowner Preventive Maintenance

Owning a home is a significant investment that requires proactive care to preserve equity, ensure safety, and avoid costly emergency repairs. This SOP provides a structured maintenance framework designed to transition a new homeowner from reactive crisis management to a professional-grade, preventive maintenance schedule. By adhering to these cyclical inspections, you will extend the lifespan of your mechanical systems and maintain the structural integrity of your property.

## Monthly Maintenance Checklist
*   **HVAC Filters:** Inspect and replace air filters every 30–90 days, depending on pet ownership and environmental air quality.
*   **Kitchen Drains:** Pour a mixture of baking soda and hot water down kitchen drains to clear minor grease buildup.
*   **Smoke/Carbon Monoxide Detectors:** Perform the "test" function on all units to ensure audible alarm operation.
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Check gauges to ensure the needle is in the green "charged" zone and verify the pin is intact.
*   **Interior Plumbing:** Visually inspect under all sinks for signs of moisture, pipe sweating, or slow leaks.

## Quarterly Maintenance Checklist
*   **GFCI Outlets:** Press the "Test" button on all Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter outlets (kitchen, bath, garage) to ensure they trip and reset correctly.
*   **Range Hood Filters:** Degrease metal mesh filters using a dishwasher cycle or hot, soapy water.
*   **Water Softener/Filtration:** If applicable, verify salt levels and replenish as necessary.
*   **Garage Door Sensors:** Test the auto-reverse feature by placing a solid object (e.g., a cardboard box) in the door's path.
*   **HVAC Vents:** Vacuum dust and debris from supply and return registers to maintain airflow efficiency.

## Bi-Annual Maintenance Checklist
*   **Water Heater:** Flush the sediment from the tank to improve heating efficiency and extend the life of the heating element/burner.
*   **HVAC Service:** Schedule a professional seasonal tune-up (Spring for A/C, Fall for heating).
*   **Windows and Doors:** Inspect weather stripping and caulking for signs of dry rot or cracking to prevent drafts and water intrusion.
*   **Sump Pump:** Pour a bucket of water into the pit to ensure the float switch activates the pump and the discharge line is clear.
*   **Attic/Crawlspace:** Inspect for signs of pests, water stains, or insulation gaps.

## Annual Maintenance Checklist
*   **Gutters and Downspouts:** Clean debris from gutters and ensure downspouts divert water at least 5 feet away from the foundation.
*   **Exterior Siding/Masonry:** Inspect for cracks, peeling paint, or vegetation growth that could lead to moisture trapped against the structure.
*   **Landscaping:** Trim trees and bushes back at least 3 feet from the home’s exterior to prevent moisture damage and pest access.
*   **Roof Inspection:** From the ground (or via drone), look for missing, curled, or damaged shingles.
*   **Dryer Vent:** Clean the entire exhaust duct from the dryer to the exterior exit point to mitigate fire risk.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (The "Main" Rule):** Label your main water shut-off valve and your electrical panel breakers with a permanent marker. In a leak or fire emergency, you do not want to be searching for these.
*   **Pro Tip (Digital Records):** Create a cloud-based folder for your home. Store all appliance manuals, receipts for major work, and warranty documents here. This adds significant value when you eventually sell the home.
*   **Pitfall (The "Out of Sight" Trap):** Many homeowners neglect crawlspaces and attics because they aren't living space. These areas are ground zero for mold and pest issues; inspect them at least twice a year.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Small Leaks):** A "tiny drip" under a sink is a precursor to mold and cabinet rot. Address moisture immediately; it never improves on its own.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should I perform my own HVAC maintenance?**
A: You should handle simple tasks like changing filters and cleaning vents. However, mechanical maintenance involving refrigerant or complex wiring must be performed by a licensed HVAC professional to maintain manufacturer warranties.

**Q: How do I know if my water heater needs flushing?**
A: If your hot water supply seems shorter than usual, or if you hear "popping" or "rumbling" sounds coming from the tank, it is likely sediment buildup requiring a flush.

**Q: What is the most common cause of home water damage?**
A: Aside from major plumbing failures, the most common cause is poor exterior drainage. If your gutters are clogged or your downspouts are dumping water against your foundation, you are at a high risk for basement leaks and foundation shifting.

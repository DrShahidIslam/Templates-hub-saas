# Standard Operating Procedure: Transport & Logistics Operations

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the primary framework for managing daily transport operations, ensuring consistency, safety, and regulatory compliance. The objective is to optimize delivery timelines, maintain asset health, and uphold superior service standards for all logistics stakeholders. Adherence to these protocols is mandatory for all personnel involved in dispatch, fleet management, and driver coordination.

## 1. Pre-Departure & Fleet Readiness
*   **Vehicle Inspection (DVIR):** Conduct a mandatory 360-degree walk-around check, including tires, brakes, lights, fluid levels, and emergency safety equipment.
*   **Documentation Verification:** Confirm the driver possesses a valid commercial driver's license (CDL), medical card, and proof of insurance.
*   **Load Securement:** Inspect cargo straps, load bars, and bracing to ensure they meet Department of Transportation (DOT) standards for weight distribution and stability.
*   **Electronic Logging Device (ELD) Sync:** Verify that the ELD is active and correctly configured to record Hours of Service (HOS) before the ignition starts.

## 2. Dispatch & Route Execution
*   **Route Optimization:** Utilize GPS fleet software to map the most efficient route, considering live traffic, weather patterns, and low-bridge clearance warnings.
*   **Communication Protocol:** Establish check-in intervals (e.g., every 4 hours or upon reaching specific transit hubs).
*   **Real-Time Tracking:** Operations staff must maintain a live dashboard view of all active units, tracking geofence breaches or unexpected delays.
*   **Proof of Delivery (POD):** Ensure drivers use the digital scanning application to capture signatures and timestamps immediately upon unloading to trigger the invoicing process.

## 3. Incident Management & Safety
*   **Breakdown Protocol:** In the event of a mechanical failure, drivers must move to a safe shoulder, set reflective triangles, and contact the 24/7 Operations Support line immediately.
*   **Accident Response:** Follow the "Accident Kit" procedure: secure the scene, contact local authorities, document damage with photos, and notify the Safety Manager within 30 minutes.
*   **Weather Alerts:** Dispatch must issue real-time "Stop or Reroute" orders if severe weather conditions threaten safe transit.

## 4. Maintenance & End-of-Day Reporting
*   **Post-Trip Inspection:** Drivers must complete a secondary vehicle inspection upon return to the depot, noting any new damages or performance issues.
*   **Fueling & Cleaning:** Refuel vehicles to the designated level and remove all trash and debris from the cab.
*   **Digital Data Sync:** Upload all digital manifests, logbooks, and fuel receipts to the company cloud storage by the end of the shift.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement "Predictive Maintenance" instead of "Reactive Maintenance." Schedule servicing based on engine hours or mileage milestones before a breakdown occurs to minimize downtime.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain an open communication channel with drivers; they are your eyes on the road. A positive driver experience correlates directly with lower turnover.
*   **Pitfall (Compliance):** Failing to audit ELD logs weekly can lead to massive regulatory fines and degraded safety ratings.
*   **Pitfall (Cargo):** Underestimating the time required for loading/unloading frequently causes a "domino effect" of delays throughout the week. Always build a 15% time-buffer into arrival windows.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should the vehicle inspection checklist be updated?**
A: The checklist should be reviewed every quarter to incorporate new safety standards, vehicle technology upgrades, or recurring mechanical issues identified in the fleet.

**Q: What is the procedure if a driver exceeds their HOS (Hours of Service)?**
A: If a driver is approaching their limit, dispatch must immediately locate the nearest safe parking location. Operating beyond legal limits is a strict policy violation that risks the company’s operating license.

**Q: What is the best way to handle a customer dispute regarding damaged cargo?**
A: Always refer to the timestamped digital POD and the load-securement photos taken at the point of origin. Proper documentation is the only valid defense against liability claims.

# Standard Operating Procedure: New Hire Onboarding Template Form

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for utilizing the New Hire Onboarding Template Form to ensure a seamless transition for incoming employees. By utilizing this template, the organization ensures data integrity, legal compliance, and a high-touch cultural integration. All hiring managers and HR personnel are required to follow this workflow to guarantee that every new hire receives consistent provisioning, administrative setup, and role-based training documentation.

## Phase 1: Pre-Submission Preparation
*   Verify the candidate has officially signed the offer letter.
*   Confirm the employee’s start date with the Department Head.
*   Identify the hardware and software access requirements specific to the employee’s role.
*   Ensure all necessary departmental budget codes for equipment are ready.

## Phase 2: Form Completion and Submission
*   Open the master Onboarding Template Form in the HRIS portal.
*   Input primary candidate data (Legal Name, Personal Email, Phone).
*   Select the "Role Profile" from the dropdown menu to auto-populate the standard checklist for that department.
*   Specify hardware requests (Laptop model, monitor requirements, peripherals).
*   Define system access requirements (Slack, CRM, ERP, Email, Proprietary tools).
*   Review all entries for accuracy, specifically focusing on the spelling of names and the correct start date.
*   Submit the form to initiate the automated workflow to IT and Facilities.

## Phase 3: Validation and Distribution
*   Monitor the HRIS dashboard for "Submission Received" confirmation.
*   Verify receipt of the automated confirmation email (sent to IT and the Hiring Manager).
*   Schedule the "First Day Welcome" meeting in the new hire's calendar.
*   Archive a digital copy of the submitted form in the employee’s secure personnel folder.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Complete the form at least 10 business days before the start date. IT lead times for hardware provisioning can vary, and early submission avoids first-day technical delays.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Internal Notes" field to specify any unique accommodations or specific desk location preferences to ensure the employee feels welcomed immediately.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "batching" form submissions. Always submit a separate form for each individual hire to prevent data contamination or provisioning errors in the HRIS system.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use temporary or placeholder email addresses; always use the candidate’s primary contact method to ensure they receive critical pre-boarding documentation.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if I need to change information after the form is submitted?**
A: You cannot edit a submitted form. Please contact the IT/HR support desk immediately to request a formal revision or cancellation of the original submission, and then submit a corrected form.

**Q: Can I request special software not listed on the standard template?**
A: Yes. Use the "Additional Requirements" text box at the bottom of the form to specify specialized software. Note that these may require additional manager approval for licensing costs.

**Q: Who is notified once I hit "Submit"?**
A: The form triggers automated notifications to the IT Provisioning Team, Facilities Management, Payroll, and the designated Departmental Onboarding Coordinator.

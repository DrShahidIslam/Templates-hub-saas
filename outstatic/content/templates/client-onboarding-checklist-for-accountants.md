# Standard Operating Procedure: Client Onboarding for Accounting Firms

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for onboarding new clients into our accounting practice. The objective of this procedure is to ensure a seamless transition, establish clear professional boundaries, mitigate compliance risks, and provide a world-class client experience from day one. By following this protocol, we ensure that all necessary documentation is secured, access rights are properly managed, and the client’s financial ecosystem is understood before active engagement begins.

## Phase 1: Qualification & Engagement
*   **Initial Discovery Call:** Complete the standardized client discovery form to assess service needs, business complexity, and firm capacity.
*   **Conflict Check:** Verify the prospective client against current databases to ensure no conflicts of interest exist.
*   **Fee Proposal:** Issue a formal engagement letter/proposal outlining scope of work, service tiers, billing frequency, and payment terms via digital signature software.
*   **Payment Setup:** Secure a signed ACH/Credit Card authorization form to ensure automated billing.
*   **Counter-Signature:** Ensure the engagement letter is countersigned by a Partner or Principal before any work commences.

## Phase 2: Compliance & Data Collection
*   **KYC/AML Verification:** Collect and verify government-issued IDs and perform necessary Anti-Money Laundering checks.
*   **Client Portal Setup:** Send an invitation to the secure client portal (e.g., ShareFile, Liscio) for document exchange.
*   **Information Request List:** Dispatch a comprehensive request list for prior-year tax returns, financial statements, bank/loan statements, and incorporation documents.
*   **Professional Ethics:** Request and obtain a signed "Letter of Authorization" to contact the client's previous accounting firm for transition files (as applicable).

## Phase 3: Technical Setup & Access
*   **Software Provisioning:** Add the client to internal Practice Management (PM) software and time-tracking systems.
*   **Cloud Access:** Establish "Accountant Access" permissions for QuickBooks Online, Xero, or other accounting platforms.
*   **Third-Party Integration:** Connect bank feeds, payroll processing systems, and receipt management tools (e.g., Dext, Fyle).
*   **Internal Notification:** Notify the production team via Slack/Teams channel that the client file is ready for processing.

## Phase 4: The Kick-Off Meeting
*   **Welcome Package:** Send a digital welcome kit including the firm's communication policy, office hours, and contact escalation matrix.
*   **Onboarding Meeting:** Hold a 30-minute virtual kick-off call to review the project roadmap, key deadlines, and expectations for data delivery.
*   **Confirm Workflow:** Reiterate the preferred communication channel and established turnaround times for financial deliverables.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate your onboarding via a workflow tool (e.g., Zapier, Karbon, or Jetpack Workflow) to ensure no task is missed and the client receives consistent automated updates.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a video walkthrough (Loom) to explain the client portal to clients who are not tech-savvy; this drastically reduces support tickets later.
*   **Pitfall:** Never start substantive accounting work before the engagement letter is signed and the initial deposit/payment method is secured.
*   **Pitfall:** Scope Creep—Document the initial scope meticulously. If a client requests "extra" services during onboarding, pause to issue a Change Order rather than performing the work for free.

## FAQ
**Q: How long should the onboarding process typically take?**
A: Depending on the complexity of the entity, a standard onboarding should take between 5 to 10 business days, assuming the client is responsive in providing the requested documentation.

**Q: What if the client refuses to provide access to their current bank feeds?**
A: This is a red flag. If they refuse necessary access, clearly state that you cannot guarantee the accuracy of the financial reporting and reserve the right to pause the engagement until access is granted.

**Q: Should I perform a "cleanup" of past books during onboarding?**
A: Never assume cleanup is included in standard onboarding. If the books are in poor condition, conduct a "Diagnostic Review" as a standalone, paid project before beginning ongoing accounting services.

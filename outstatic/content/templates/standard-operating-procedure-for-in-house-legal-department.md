# Standard Operating Procedure: In-House Legal Department Operations

This document outlines the standardized protocols for the in-house legal department to ensure regulatory compliance, risk mitigation, and operational efficiency across the organization. By following these procedures, the department will maintain consistency in document handling, contract lifecycles, and internal advisory functions, ultimately safeguarding the company’s legal interests while supporting business velocity.

## 1. Intake and Matter Management
*   **Centralized Intake:** All legal requests must be submitted via the designated Legal Request Portal or centralized ticketing system to ensure tracking and auditability.
*   **Initial Triage:** Within one business day of submission, an attorney or legal operations specialist must assess the request for complexity, urgency, and priority.
*   **Categorization:** Assign each matter a unique tracking ID and category (e.g., Commercial Contract, Employment, IP/Trademark, Litigation, or General Advisory).
*   **Conflict Check:** Perform a preliminary internal review to ensure no conflict of interest exists regarding the parties involved.

## 2. Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM)
*   **Standard Template Use:** Utilize the pre-approved company templates for all standard agreements (NDAs, MSAs, SOWs).
*   **Redlining Standards:** All modifications must be tracked using "Track Changes." Do not accept redlines in PDF format; request the source file (Word).
*   **Approval Workflow:** Ensure the document undergoes mandatory review by the department head and relevant business stakeholders before moving to final signature.
*   **Electronic Signature:** Execute all contracts via the company’s authorized e-signature platform.
*   **Repository Archiving:** Once fully executed, upload the final version to the centralized Legal Document Management System (LDMS) with appropriate metadata tags.

## 3. Advisory and Internal Communication
*   **Record of Advice:** All significant legal guidance provided to business units must be documented in writing (via email or memo) to establish a paper trail.
*   **Privilege Preservation:** Clearly mark sensitive legal advice as "Privileged & Confidential – Attorney-Client Communication" to assist in potential future discovery efforts.
*   **Cross-Functional Sync:** Schedule bi-weekly meetings with HR, Finance, and Security teams to identify cross-departmental risks and compliance gaps.

## 4. Compliance and Training
*   **Regulatory Monitoring:** Maintain an updated "Compliance Calendar" tracking filing deadlines, license renewals, and mandatory reporting dates.
*   **Annual Training:** Conduct mandatory training for employees on core legal topics, including Data Privacy (GDPR/CCPA), Anti-Bribery/FCPA, and Insider Trading.
*   **Policy Review:** Review and update company policies (e.g., Code of Conduct, Employee Handbook) on an annual basis to reflect evolving legal standards.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Establish a Self-Service Center.** Create a "Legal Playbook" on the company intranet with FAQs and approved templates to reduce the volume of low-value, repetitive requests.
*   **Pro Tip: Use Automated Alerts.** Set automated reminders for contract renewals 90 days before the expiration date to prevent auto-renewal liabilities.
*   **Pitfall: Scope Creep.** Avoid acting as a business consultant. Clearly define where "legal advice" ends and "business strategy" begins to maintain the integrity of the attorney-client privilege.
*   **Pitfall: Failure to Document.** An oral agreement is a dangerous agreement. If it isn't documented in the files, for all legal intents and purposes, it didn't happen.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How should I handle an urgent request that bypasses the formal portal?**
A: Acknowledge receipt of the request, but immediately redirect the stakeholder to the formal portal. Explain that for audit and tracking purposes, all legal matters must be logged into the central system to guarantee timely processing.

**Q: What is the procedure if a business unit wants to use an external law firm?**
A: All external counsel engagements must be vetted and approved by the General Counsel. The legal department must establish engagement letters, set budget caps, and manage the billing process to control costs.

**Q: How long should we keep signed contracts and legal correspondence?**
A: Under current records retention policy, all core legal agreements should be kept for the life of the contract plus seven years. Always consult the specific company "Records Retention Schedule" before purging any files.

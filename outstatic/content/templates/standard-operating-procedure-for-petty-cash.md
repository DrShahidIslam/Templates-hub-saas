# Standard Operating Procedure: Petty Cash Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the formal guidelines for the administration, utilization, and reconciliation of petty cash funds. The purpose of this document is to ensure fiscal accountability, prevent misappropriation, and maintain accurate financial records for small-value operational expenses. Adherence to these procedures is mandatory for all employees authorized to handle or request petty cash.

## 1. Fund Establishment and Security
*   **Custodian Assignment:** Designate a single "Petty Cash Custodian" responsible for the physical security and record-keeping of the fund.
*   **Secure Storage:** Maintain the petty cash fund in a locked, fireproof lockbox stored within a secure, access-controlled area.
*   **Access Control:** Access to the key or security code must be restricted solely to the Custodian and one secondary backup manager.
*   **Fixed Amount:** Maintain the fund at a pre-set "Imprest" balance (e.g., $500). The sum of cash on hand plus processed receipts must always equal this total.

## 2. Disbursement Procedures
*   **Authorization:** All requests for cash must be accompanied by a "Petty Cash Voucher" signed by the requester’s department head.
*   **Documentation:** No funds shall be released without an original receipt, invoice, or a signed voucher explaining the business purpose.
*   **Verification:** The Custodian must verify that the expenditure is within policy limits before handing over any cash.
*   **Recording:** Log the transaction immediately in the Petty Cash Ledger, noting the date, amount, vendor, and general ledger (GL) account code.

## 3. Reconciliation and Replenishment
*   **Periodic Count:** Perform a surprise physical count of the cash box at least once per month.
*   **Preparation for Replenishment:** When the fund drops below 20% of its total value, compile all vouchers and receipts.
*   **Requesting Funds:** Submit a "Petty Cash Replenishment Request" to the Finance Department, attaching the summary report and all original receipts.
*   **Fund Reset:** Upon receipt of the reimbursement check, cash the check and place the funds into the lockbox to restore the full Imprest balance.

## 4. Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a dedicated "Petty Cash Log" spreadsheet that automatically calculates the expected remaining balance versus the physical count to spot errors immediately.
*   **Pro Tip:** Prohibit the use of petty cash for personal loans, salary advances, or high-value equipment purchases; mandate a formal procurement process for these items.
*   **Pitfall - The "Missing Receipt" Trap:** Allowing employees to take cash without a receipt creates a lack of accountability. If a receipt is lost, require the employee to complete a "Lost Receipt Affidavit" before releasing funds.
*   **Pitfall - Commingling:** Never store personal cash or non-business related items in the petty cash box. This is the fastest way to invite audit failures.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if the cash count does not match the ledger?**
A: Record the discrepancy immediately in the "Over/Short" column of your ledger. If the amount is significant (greater than $5), report it to the Finance Manager immediately for an investigation into potential security breaches.

**Q: Can I use petty cash to pay a contractor or service provider for work done?**
A: No. Petty cash is strictly for minor out-of-pocket operational expenses (e.g., office supplies, courier fees). Payments for services must be processed through Accounts Payable to ensure proper tax reporting and vendor management.

**Q: How do I handle a request for an expense that exceeds the pre-set limit?**
A: Any expense exceeding the petty cash limit (e.g., $50) should be denied and redirected to the standard company procurement or expense reimbursement policy, requiring a corporate credit card or a formal Purchase Order.

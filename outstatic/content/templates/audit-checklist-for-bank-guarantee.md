# Standard Operating Procedure: Bank Guarantee Audit

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory audit requirements for validating, monitoring, and managing Bank Guarantees (BGs). As a critical financial instrument, a Bank Guarantee represents a significant liability for the organization; therefore, it is imperative to ensure that every instrument is authentic, legally binding, and aligned with internal risk management policies. This audit process is designed to mitigate operational risk, ensure compliance with treasury mandates, and prevent fraudulent activity.

## I. Validation of Authenticity
*   **Direct Confirmation:** Contact the issuing bank’s official branch representative via verified channels (SWIFT, authenticated email, or registered post) to confirm the existence and validity of the BG.
*   **Cross-Reference Swift Details:** For international guarantees, ensure the MT760 (or relevant SWIFT message) has been received and authenticated by the beneficiary’s bank.
*   **Signatory Verification:** Cross-check the names and titles of the bank signatories against the bank’s authorized signatory list or public regulatory filings.
*   **Physical Security:** If the BG is a hard-copy original, inspect it for security features such as watermarks, micro-printing, and embossed seals.

## II. Terms and Conditions Review
*   **Guarantee Amount:** Verify the face value matches the contractual obligation.
*   **Expiry Clause:** Confirm the "Expiry Date" and the "Claim Period." Ensure the claim period provides sufficient lead time (typically 30–90 days post-expiry) to process a claim if needed.
*   **Governing Law:** Ensure the BG is subject to a jurisdiction that is favorable or acceptable to the organization’s legal department.
*   **Irrevocability:** Confirm the wording explicitly states the guarantee is "irrevocable" and "unconditional."
*   **Automatic Extension:** Check for an "Evergreen Clause" or automatic extension triggers to ensure the guarantee does not lapse inadvertently during a project.

## III. Operational Compliance
*   **Accounting Entry:** Confirm the BG has been recorded in the Treasury Management System (TMS) or General Ledger with the correct maturity date and counterparty risk profile.
*   **Fee Reconciliation:** Audit the fees paid for the issuance or amendment of the BG to ensure they align with the agreed-upon bank charges.
*   **Document Retention:** Ensure a high-resolution digital copy is stored in the central document repository, with the physical original secured in a fireproof vault.
*   **Review Cycle:** Verify that the BG is subject to a quarterly or bi-annual review to determine if the underlying contract still requires the guarantee.

## IV. Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Use Automated Alerts:** Set up automated calendar reminders for 90, 60, and 30 days prior to expiry to allow sufficient time for extensions or releases.
*   **Pro Tip: Standardize Wording:** Use a pre-approved template for BGs whenever possible to avoid "weak" language that may hinder a future claim.
*   **Pitfall: The "Draft" Trap:** Never accept a photocopy or scan as a final instrument. Always wait for the authenticated original or the SWIFT confirmation.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Jurisdiction:** Avoid "soft law" jurisdictions where enforcing a claim against a foreign bank may be legally complex or time-consuming.
*   **Pitfall: Expiry Date Overlap:** Always ensure the expiry date of the BG extends beyond the final delivery or performance date of the contract by at least 6 months.

## V. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What should I do if the bank claims they cannot verify a BG via SWIFT?**
If a bank is unwilling to provide verification via SWIFT, demand a formal letter signed by a senior bank official on bank letterhead. If they refuse to provide written confirmation, treat the BG as high-risk and escalate the matter to the Legal and Treasury departments immediately.

**2. How often should we audit our existing portfolio of Bank Guarantees?**
You should conduct a formal audit of all active guarantees on a quarterly basis. This ensures that expiring guarantees are addressed, fees are accurate, and the guarantees remain relevant to current business operations.

**3. What is an 'Evergreen Clause' and why is it important?**
An Evergreen Clause allows for the automatic renewal of a Bank Guarantee for successive periods unless the issuer provides notice of non-renewal by a specific date. This prevents the guarantee from accidentally expiring if a project timeline is extended.

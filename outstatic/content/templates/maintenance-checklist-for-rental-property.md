# Standard Operating Procedure: Rental Property Maintenance

Effective property maintenance is the cornerstone of protecting asset value, ensuring tenant retention, and minimizing long-term capital expenditure. This SOP establishes a standardized protocol for conducting biannual comprehensive property inspections and routine maintenance. By adhering to this systematic approach, property managers can proactively identify structural or mechanical failures before they escalate into costly emergency repairs, while simultaneously maintaining a high standard of living for tenants.

## 1. Exterior & Structural Integrity
*   **Roof and Gutters:** Inspect shingles for missing or damaged sections; clear gutters and downspouts of debris to ensure proper drainage.
*   **Foundation:** Check for cracks, settling, or water pooling near the base of the building.
*   **Siding/Paint:** Look for peeling paint, wood rot, or damaged siding that may allow moisture penetration.
*   **Exterior Lighting:** Verify all porch, driveway, and security lights are functional.
*   **Landscaping/Hardscaping:** Trim tree limbs overhanging the roof; inspect walkways for trip hazards.

## 2. Plumbing & HVAC Systems
*   **HVAC Filters:** Replace filters (Standard practice: every 90 days).
*   **HVAC Unit:** Inspect the exterior condenser unit for debris; test system cycle for heating and cooling efficiency.
*   **Water Heaters:** Check for signs of corrosion or leaks around the base and pressure relief valve; flush tank if sediment buildup is suspected.
*   **Faucets & Drains:** Test all faucets for proper pressure and check under sinks for moisture or slow leaks.
*   **Toilets:** Ensure secure mounting to the floor and check that seals (flappers) are functioning correctly.

## 3. Interior & Life Safety
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test every unit; replace batteries as a standard policy regardless of age.
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Confirm the gauge is in the "green" zone and the unit is mounted in an accessible location.
*   **Windows & Doors:** Test all locks for security; ensure weather stripping is intact and windows operate smoothly.
*   **Walls & Ceilings:** Inspect for signs of water stains, mold, or structural cracking.
*   **Appliances:** Run a full cycle on the dishwasher, verify oven temperature accuracy, and check refrigerator seals.

## 4. Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **The "One-Touch" Rule:** Whenever a technician is inside for one repair, mandate a 5-minute "look-around" for other potential issues (e.g., changing a lightbulb while fixing a leaky faucet).
*   **Photo Documentation:** Maintain a digital file for every property. Photo-document the condition of major systems during every visit to establish a timeline of wear and tear.
*   **Preventative Scheduling:** Use a recurring calendar system to automate maintenance requests rather than relying on reactive reporting.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **Ignoring Small Leaks:** A minor drip under a sink can lead to massive sub-floor rot and mold remediation costs within months.
*   **DIY Liability:** Avoid performing electrical or complex plumbing work in-house unless you hold the appropriate licensure. The cost savings are rarely worth the insurance and safety risks.
*   **Tenant Friction:** Always provide legally mandated notice (typically 24–48 hours) before entry, even for routine maintenance, to maintain a professional landlord-tenant relationship.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**How often should a comprehensive maintenance inspection be performed?**
At minimum, once every six months. If the property is older or in a climate with extreme weather, quarterly inspections are recommended to catch structural fatigue early.

**Should I provide tenants with a list of maintenance responsibilities?**
Yes. Clearly outline in the lease agreement what is the tenant’s responsibility (e.g., changing lightbulbs, keeping vents clean, reporting leaks immediately) to prevent disputes regarding negligence.

**What is the best way to track maintenance history?**
Utilize Property Management Software (PMS) such as AppFolio or Buildium. If managing a smaller portfolio, a centralized spreadsheet or cloud-based document tracking system is essential for maintaining accurate records for tax purposes and future sale disclosures.

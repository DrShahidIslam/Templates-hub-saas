# Standard Operating Procedure: New Apartment Move-In Inspection & Setup

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocol for conducting a comprehensive pre-move-in inspection and the subsequent initial setup of a new residential unit. Adhering to this systematic approach ensures that all physical defects are documented for security deposit protection, essential utilities are functional, and the living environment is sanitized and prepared for immediate occupancy. 

## Phase 1: Pre-Occupancy Inspection
Before moving any furniture into the unit, perform a thorough physical walkthrough.

*   **Documentation:** Take high-resolution photos and videos of every room, wall, floor, and appliance, specifically noting any pre-existing scratches, dents, or stains.
*   **Walls & Ceilings:** Inspect for water damage, mold, peeling paint, or holes.
*   **Flooring:** Check for carpet stains, loose floorboards, or tile cracks.
*   **Windows & Doors:** Verify all locks function, screens are intact, and windows open/close smoothly.
*   **Electrical Outlets:** Test each outlet with a simple plug-in device (e.g., phone charger) to ensure power connectivity.
*   **Plumbing:** Turn on all faucets, flush toilets, and inspect under sinks for signs of leaks or moisture.

## Phase 2: Functional Testing & Safety
Verify that the critical infrastructure is fully operational and compliant with safety standards.

*   **HVAC System:** Run both heat and air conditioning to ensure the thermostat communicates correctly with the unit.
*   **Appliances:** Check the refrigerator cooling, stove/oven heat, dishwasher cycles, and microwave functionality.
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test every alarm; replace batteries immediately if the unit does not beep upon pressing the 'test' button.
*   **Lighting:** Ensure every light fixture has working bulbs and switches respond instantly.
*   **Water Heater:** Confirm hot water is available at all taps after allowing the system to cycle.

## Phase 3: Administrative & Utility Setup
Ensure the legal and logistical framework of the apartment is fully established.

*   **Utility Transfer:** Confirm electricity, gas, and internet accounts have been officially transferred into your name.
*   **Key Inventory:** Verify that all issued keys (front door, mailbox, storage unit, laundry room) work as intended.
*   **Lease Review:** Store a digital copy of the signed lease in a secure cloud folder for quick reference regarding maintenance policies.
*   **Emergency Contacts:** Locate the property manager’s 24/7 maintenance emergency number and save it to your phone.

## Phase 4: Hygiene & Deep Cleaning
Even in "professionally cleaned" units, perform a baseline sanitization.

*   **Surfaces:** Wipe down all high-touch areas, including light switches, cabinet handles, and countertops.
*   **Appliances:** Clean the interior of the refrigerator, oven, and dishwasher.
*   **Pest Mitigation:** Inspect cabinets and baseboards for signs of pest activity; place traps in out-of-sight areas as a preventative measure.
*   **Air Quality:** Replace air filters if the unit uses a forced-air system, as these are frequently neglected during turnover.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The 'Move-In Checklist' Form:** Most landlords provide a formal inspection sheet. Always fill this out and submit a copy to the landlord via email within 48 hours of possession. This creates a time-stamped digital trail.
*   **Pitfall - Ignoring Noises:** If you hear odd clicks or hums in the walls, report them immediately. What seems like a minor annoyance can lead to electrical hazards or pest infestations.
*   **Pro Tip - The Water Pressure Test:** Don't just check the temperature. Ensure the water pressure is consistent across all fixtures; weak pressure may indicate an issue with the pipes that you don't want to be blamed for later.
*   **Pitfall - Skipping the "Before" Photos:** Never assume your landlord is keeping honest records. Your photos are your only defense against wrongful security deposit deductions.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How long do I have to report damages found after move-in?**
Typically, you should document all findings within 48 to 72 hours of receiving your keys. Check your lease agreement, as some management companies specify a strict "Move-In Condition Report" deadline.

**2. Should I change the locks?**
Check your lease first. Many apartment complexes forbid changing locks due to fire safety and emergency access requirements. If allowed, you must provide the landlord with a copy of the new key immediately.

**3. What should I do if the apartment is not clean upon arrival?**
Do not move your belongings in immediately. Take photos of the mess and contact the property manager in writing to request a professional re-clean. Having a "cleanliness dispute" in writing is essential if you decide to withhold a portion of rent or request a credit.

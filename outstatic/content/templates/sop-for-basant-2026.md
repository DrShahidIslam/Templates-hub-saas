# Standard Operating Procedure: Basant 2026 Event Execution

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the strategic framework, logistical requirements, and safety protocols for the successful execution of Basant 2026. As Basant involves high-stakes public interaction and complex regulatory environments, this document serves as the primary manual for operations managers, site supervisors, and safety coordinators to ensure a seamless, compliant, and high-impact event experience.

## 1. Pre-Event Planning & Regulatory Compliance (T-Minus 90 Days)
*   **Permit Acquisition:** Secure all municipal, environmental, and public safety permits. Ensure documentation for site usage, noise ordinance exemptions, and street closures is filed.
*   **Safety Audit:** Conduct a comprehensive risk assessment, focusing on kite-string safety (glass/chemical coating restrictions), crowd control, and emergency egress routes.
*   **Stakeholder Alignment:** Host briefings with local law enforcement, fire departments, and emergency medical services (EMS).
*   **Vendor Procurement:** Finalize contracts for event infrastructure (stages, barricades, sanitation units) and food/beverage concessions.

## 2. Infrastructure & Logistics Setup (T-Minus 48 Hours)
*   **Site Mapping:** Mark high-traffic zones, designated kite-flying areas, and "No-Fly" zones (e.g., near power lines and major transit paths).
*   **Emergency Infrastructure:** Position mobile medical stations and clear fire lanes. Verify that fire suppression equipment is staged at every major station.
*   **Signage Installation:** Deploy high-visibility signage for safety regulations, event schedules, and exits.
*   **Communication Network:** Test dedicated radio channels for internal security, operations, and external emergency services.

## 3. Operational Execution (Event Day)
*   **Morning Sweep:** Perform a final "go/no-go" inspection of all physical structures and aerial safety zones.
*   **Access Control:** Implement perimeter fencing and security checkpoints to manage attendee density and monitor for prohibited materials (e.g., banned kite strings).
*   **Real-Time Monitoring:** Deploy observers to monitor wind conditions, crowd density, and any sign of infrastructure failure.
*   **Sanitation & Waste:** Execute the scheduled waste-management rotation to prevent litter-related hazards.

## 4. Post-Event Demobilization (T-Plus 24 Hours)
*   **Site Restoration:** Complete a full site cleanup, ensuring all kite debris (especially metallic or chemical-coated string) is cleared from trees and power lines.
*   **Post-Mortem Review:** Debrief the operations team to analyze incident logs and efficiency metrics.
*   **Final Report:** Consolidate all financial records, permit compliance documents, and safety reports for archival.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a "Red-Flag" system where a single command can halt all aerial activities immediately if weather or safety conditions deteriorate.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use geofencing or digital signage to notify attendees of sudden changes in wind or safety status.
*   **Pitfall:** Underestimating the volume of waste generated by street food vendors; always over-provision waste disposal units by at least 20%.
*   **Pitfall:** Failure to account for power lines in "kite-dense" zones; prioritize aerial exclusion zones over optimal visibility.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: How do we handle unauthorized kite-string usage?**
Security teams must be empowered to confiscate prohibited strings at entrance checkpoints. If found inside the venue, the user should be issued a warning and the materials safely disposed of in hazardous waste containers.

**Q2: What is the procedure if a kite makes contact with a power line?**
Operations must immediately alert the local utility company via a dedicated priority line. The immediate area around the pole/transformer must be cordoned off by security until the utility crew confirms safety.

**Q3: How are noise and environmental ordinances managed?**
All sound equipment must have decibel-limiting hardware installed. An independent sound engineer should monitor levels throughout the event to ensure compliance with local municipal codes.

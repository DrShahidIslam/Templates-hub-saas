# Standard Operating Procedure: Construction Project Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory framework for managing construction projects from the pre-construction phase through final project closeout. Its purpose is to ensure consistent delivery, minimize financial risk, uphold rigorous safety standards, and maintain quality control across all project lifecycles. By adhering to these protocols, project managers and site superintendents will ensure operational alignment with company standards, regulatory requirements, and client expectations.

## Phase 1: Pre-Construction and Mobilization
*   **Permit Verification:** Confirm all municipal permits, zoning approvals, and utility connections are finalized and physically available on-site.
*   **Site Survey & Safety Audit:** Conduct a comprehensive site walk-through to document existing conditions and establish perimeter fencing, signage, and restricted zones.
*   **Subcontractor Onboarding:** Execute scope-of-work contracts, obtain Certificates of Insurance (COI), and conduct a mandatory safety induction for all subcontractors.
*   **Logistics Planning:** Finalize the layout for site offices, material storage areas, waste management, and heavy equipment access points.

## Phase 2: Execution and Quality Control
*   **Daily Reporting:** Document labor hours, material deliveries, equipment utilization, and site observations in the centralized project management software.
*   **Progress Meetings:** Hold mandatory weekly "Owner-Architect-Contractor" (OAC) meetings to review schedule variance and upcoming critical milestones.
*   **Quality Inspections:** Perform documented checks at major structural milestones (e.g., foundation pouring, framing completion, MEP rough-ins) before proceeding to the next trade.
*   **Change Order Management:** Issue formal Change Order Requests (COR) for any work falling outside the original scope, requiring written client approval prior to execution.

## Phase 3: Project Closeout and Handover
*   **Punch List Execution:** Conduct a final walk-through with the client to generate a comprehensive list of outstanding items; clear all items within 14 business days.
*   **As-Built Documentation:** Compile and submit all red-lined drawings, equipment manuals, maintenance schedules, and warranty certificates.
*   **Financial Reconciliation:** Ensure all subcontractor invoices are settled, lien waivers are collected, and final payments from the client are processed.
*   **Post-Mortem Review:** Conduct an internal team debrief to evaluate project performance, identify budget variances, and document "lessons learned" for future tenders.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use Building Information Modeling (BIM) or cloud-based document control systems to ensure the field crew is always working off the most current drawing revision.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain an open line of communication with local inspectors; proactive consultation often resolves code compliance issues before they result in a "Stop Work" order.
*   **Pitfall - Scope Creep:** Failing to document verbal requests from the client is the fastest way to erode project margins. Never perform "extra" work without a signed change order.
*   **Pitfall - Safety Complacency:** Treat safety as a production metric, not an administrative burden. Regular "Toolbox Talks" are essential to keep safety top-of-mind for site laborers.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is the standard protocol for handling an on-site safety incident?**
Immediate action must prioritize medical attention. Once the area is secure, the Project Manager must file an internal Incident Report within 4 hours, notify the insurance carrier, and conduct a root-cause analysis to prevent recurrence.

**2. How often should the project schedule be updated?**
The project schedule is a living document and must be updated every Friday afternoon to reflect the current status, incorporating any impacts from weather or supply chain delays.

**3. What constitutes a complete "As-Built" package?**
A complete package includes final signed-off inspection reports, manufacturer warranty documentation, final red-lined CAD drawings reflecting actual field conditions, and a full contact directory of all primary subcontractors and vendors.

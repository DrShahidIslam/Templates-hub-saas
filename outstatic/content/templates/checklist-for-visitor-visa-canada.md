# Standard Operating Procedure: Canada Visitor Visa (TRV) Application

## Introduction
The Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) application process for Canada is a rigorous administrative procedure that requires strict adherence to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) guidelines. As an operations manager, I have developed this SOP to ensure all documentation is accurate, compliant, and optimized for a positive adjudication outcome. Success hinges on demonstrating "home ties"—proof that the applicant has sufficient incentives to return to their country of origin upon the expiration of their visa.

## Phase 1: Eligibility and Document Gathering
*   [ ] **Determine Eligibility:** Verify if the applicant requires a TRV or an eTA based on country of citizenship.
*   [ ] **Gather Proof of Financial Support:** Collect bank statements for the last 4–6 months, pay stubs, and proof of assets (property, investments).
*   [ ] **Establish Purpose of Travel:** Draft a formal cover letter detailing the itinerary, duration of stay, and specific purpose (e.g., tourism, visiting family).
*   [ ] **Compile Identity Documents:** Ensure passports are valid for at least six months beyond the intended date of departure from Canada.
*   [ ] **Biometrics:** Confirm if you have provided biometrics in the last 10 years; if not, prepare to schedule an appointment at a Visa Application Centre (VAC).

## Phase 2: Form Preparation and Digital Submission
*   [ ] **Create IRCC Portal Account:** Use the official IRCC secure account portal to initiate the application.
*   [ ] **Complete IMM 5257 (Application for Visitor Visa):** Ensure all gaps in employment or travel history are explained.
*   [ ] **Family Information Form (IMM 5645):** Provide exhaustive details for parents, siblings, and dependents.
*   [ ] **Document Digitization:** Scan all documents in high resolution (PDF format). Ensure files do not exceed IRCC size limits (typically 4MB).
*   [ ] **Translation:** Ensure any document not in English or French is accompanied by a certified translation and an affidavit from the translator.

## Phase 3: Final Review and Submission
*   [ ] **Quality Assurance Check:** Cross-reference information on the forms with the supporting documents (e.g., travel dates must match the bank statement activity and itinerary).
*   [ ] **Fee Payment:** Process the visa application fee and biometric fee via credit card through the portal.
*   [ ] **Submission Confirmation:** Download and save the submission confirmation page and the application number for tracking purposes.
*   [ ] **Biometric Appointment:** Once the Biometric Instruction Letter (BIL) is received, book the appointment within 30 days.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Home Ties" Factor:** The most common reason for refusal is "insufficient evidence of ties to the home country." Always include a letter from an employer granting leave, property deeds, or proof of family responsibilities.
*   **Consistency is Key:** Ensure the details on your application form are identical to the information on your supporting documents. Discrepancies are flagged by the system as potential misrepresentation.
*   **Avoid Generic Cover Letters:** Customize the cover letter to specifically address the visa officer. Explain exactly why you are visiting, who you are meeting, and why you must return home by a specific date.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not purchase non-refundable flight tickets until the visa is approved. IRCC explicitly advises against this.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Can I work in Canada while on a visitor visa?**
No. A visitor visa authorizes temporary entry for tourism or family visits only. Engaging in unauthorized employment is a violation of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act and will lead to deportation.

**2. How long does the processing usually take?**
Processing times vary significantly based on the applicant's country of residence and the current volume of applications. You can check the "Check processing times" tool on the official IRCC website for the most up-to-date estimates.

**3. What happens if my application is refused?**
If refused, you will receive a letter outlining the reasons. You have the right to reapply at any time, provided you address the concerns raised in the refusal letter (e.g., providing more evidence of funds or clarifying travel intentions). There is no formal appeal process for a temporary resident visa refusal.

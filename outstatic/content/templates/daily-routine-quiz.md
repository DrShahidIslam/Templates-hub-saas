# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Routine Quiz Administration

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for administering, monitoring, and analyzing the "Daily Routine Quiz." The objective of this procedure is to ensure consistent performance tracking, maintain data integrity, and provide immediate feedback loops to participants. By adhering to this workflow, we minimize administrative friction and ensure that every quiz cycle serves as a high-value touchpoint for organizational alignment and skill reinforcement.

## Phase 1: Pre-Administration & System Readiness
*   **Verify Platform Connectivity:** Ensure the quiz interface (LMS or digital form) is live and accessible.
*   **Sync Question Banks:** Confirm that the daily question set corresponds to the current training module or focus area.
*   **Time-Lock Verification:** Set the automated release and cut-off timestamps to ensure the quiz is available during the designated operational window.
*   **Notification Dispatch:** Send an automated reminder to all eligible participants 15 minutes prior to the quiz release.

## Phase 2: Live Execution & Monitoring
*   **Real-time Oversight:** Monitor the dashboard for "In-Progress" submissions to identify any technical bottlenecks or connectivity issues reported by users.
*   **Latency Management:** Maintain a help-desk standby to address "Access Denied" or "Submission Error" tickets within 5 minutes of receipt.
*   **Integrity Enforcement:** Ensure anti-cheating protocols (browser locking or randomized question ordering) are actively engaged throughout the live session.

## Phase 3: Post-Quiz Analysis & Reporting
*   **Auto-Grading Audit:** Verify that the system has correctly tallied scores; manually review any flagged open-ended responses.
*   **Performance Benchmarking:** Export the raw data and compare the daily average against the established Key Performance Indicator (KPI) baseline.
*   **Insight Distribution:** Generate an automated summary report for management, highlighting both the highest-scoring departments and areas of common misconception.
*   **Feedback Loop:** Push the "Correct Answer Key" and brief explanations to the participants within 60 minutes of the closing window to reinforce learning.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use "Spaced Repetition" by incorporating 10% of questions from the previous week’s quiz to ensure long-term retention.
*   **Pro Tip:** Keep the quiz duration under 7 minutes; shorter, frequent touchpoints lead to significantly higher completion rates than long, weekly assessments.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "question fatigue" by varying the format (multiple choice, drag-and-drop, and true/false). Stagnant formats lead to mechanical answering rather than thoughtful engagement.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not release feedback before the final cutoff time, as this invites collusion and compromises the integrity of the performance data.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if a participant experiences a system crash mid-quiz?**
A: Immediately verify the participant's status in the backend. If a crash is confirmed, manually reset their "Attempt Counter" to allow a one-time retake, and log the incident in the system error report.

**Q: How do we handle participants who consistently miss the daily window?**
A: Compile a "Non-Compliance Report" at the end of the week. If a pattern persists, escalate to the direct supervisor to determine if the issue is a lack of priority or a scheduling conflict.

**Q: Can we customize the quiz for different departments?**
A: Yes. Use a "Branching Logic" feature in your platform to serve different question banks based on the user's role profile upon login. Ensure all role-specific banks are updated during the Phase 1 setup.

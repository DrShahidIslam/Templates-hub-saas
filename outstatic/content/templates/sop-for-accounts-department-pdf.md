# Standard Operating Procedure: Accounts Department Documentation & PDF Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized methodology for creating, organizing, securing, and archiving financial documentation within the Accounts Department. By converting physical and digital financial records into structured PDF formats, the department ensures audit readiness, data integrity, and compliance with institutional record-keeping policies. This document serves as the master guide for all accounting personnel to maintain consistency in document naming, storage hierarchy, and digital security protocols.

## 1. Document Preparation and Digitization
*   **Verification:** Review all source documents (invoices, receipts, vouchers) for physical signatures, stamps, and authorization prior to scanning.
*   **Cleaning:** Remove staples, paperclips, and adhesive notes to prevent scanner damage and document obstruction.
*   **Scanning Configuration:** Set scanner to 300 DPI for standard documents. Use "Greyscale" for text-heavy documents and "Color" for bank statements or complex contracts.
*   **PDF Conversion:** Utilize enterprise-grade software to convert scans directly into Searchable PDF (PDF/A) format to ensure long-term readability.

## 2. Standardized Naming Convention
*   **Structure:** Adhere strictly to the `YYYY-MM-DD_Category_Vendor/Client_ReferenceID` format.
    *   *Example:* `2023-10-15_INV_AcmeCorp_PO-9921.pdf`
*   **Consistency:** Avoid special characters (!, @, #, *) and spaces; use underscores (_) for separators.
*   **Version Control:** If a document is updated, append `_v[Number]` at the end of the filename (e.g., `_v2.pdf`).

## 3. Storage and Access Control
*   **Folder Hierarchy:** Organize digital filing systems by Financial Year > Month > Department/Category.
*   **Access Rights:** Apply "Read-Only" permissions for junior staff; restrict "Modify/Delete" permissions to the Finance Manager or Lead Accountant.
*   **Cloud Sync:** Ensure all PDFs are saved to the designated secure company server or encrypted cloud drive with automated daily backups.

## 4. Security and Compliance
*   **Encryption:** Password-protect sensitive files (e.g., payroll data, tax filings) using AES-256 encryption.
*   **Data Masking:** Redact PII (Personally Identifiable Information) on PDFs before sharing with external third parties.
*   **Retention Policy:** Flag documents with an "Expiry Date" based on statutory requirements (e.g., 7 years for tax records) to trigger automated deletion/archiving.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use OCR (Optical Character Recognition) software to make scanned PDFs searchable. This significantly reduces time spent during internal and external audits.
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate the filing process using "Watch Folders" if your document management system supports it; this moves files into the correct directory based on keywords.
*   **Pitfall:** Never use personal cloud storage (Google Drive/Dropbox personal accounts) for company financial documents; this is a critical security breach.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not store files on the local desktop. Local files are excluded from enterprise backup solutions and are easily lost during hardware failure.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How do I handle large files that exceed email attachment limits?**
Do not compress files to the point of unreadability. Instead, upload the PDF to the company’s secure internal file portal and share a view-only link with the recipient.

**2. What should I do if a PDF file becomes corrupted?**
Immediately notify the IT Department and the Finance Manager. Restore the document from the most recent system backup folder rather than attempting to "repair" the file, which may result in data discrepancies.

**3. Is it mandatory to keep the physical paper copy after digitizing?**
Refer to your local statutory tax laws. In many jurisdictions, digitizing is sufficient, but for specific legal contracts or tax-deductible receipts, you may be required to archive the physical copy in a secure, fire-proof cabinet for a set duration.

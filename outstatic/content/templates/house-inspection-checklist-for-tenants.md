# Standard Operating Procedure: Tenant Move-In Property Inspection

This SOP provides a systematic framework for conducting a comprehensive property inspection before or upon move-in. The primary objective is to document the existing condition of the premises to protect your security deposit, identify potential maintenance issues, and ensure the property is move-in ready. As an operations professional, I recommend approaching this with a "risk mitigation" mindset: if it isn't documented, it never happened.

## 1. Preparation & Documentation
*   **Documentation Kit:** Bring a smartphone with a high-resolution camera, a notepad, a pen, a flashlight (even during the day), and your signed lease agreement.
*   **Review:** Have the landlord’s move-in checklist (if provided) ready to compare notes.
*   **Charging:** Ensure your phone is at 100% battery and has sufficient storage for high-quality photos/videos.
*   **Time Allocation:** Dedicate at least 60–90 minutes. Do not rush this process.

## 2. Exterior & Entryway Inspection
*   **Doors:** Test all locks for smooth operation. Inspect the door frame for signs of forced entry or rotting wood.
*   **Exterior Walls/Foundation:** Look for significant cracks, water damage, or mold.
*   **Windows:** Ensure all exterior windows lock securely and screens are intact.
*   **Common Areas:** Check entryways, porches, or hallways for cleanliness and structural safety.

## 3. Interior Walls, Ceilings, & Floors
*   **Walls:** Note every scuff, hole, stain, or peeling paint. Use a coin to gauge the size of holes if necessary.
*   **Ceilings:** Check for water stains (which indicate past or present leaks) and sagging areas.
*   **Flooring:** Look for carpet stains, tears, warped floorboards, or tile cracks. Document wear patterns in high-traffic areas.
*   **Baseboards:** Check for gaps, water damage, or animal-related damage.

## 4. Kitchen & Bathroom Systems
*   **Appliances:** Turn on the stove burners, check oven heating, test the refrigerator/freezer temperature, and run the dishwasher cycle.
*   **Plumbing:** Fill sinks and tubs to check drainage speed. Check under the cabinets for active leaks or signs of water damage.
*   **Faucets:** Turn on all hot/cold taps to check water pressure and leak points.
*   **Fixtures:** Ensure all lights are functional and cabinet doors/drawers operate smoothly.

## 5. Electrical & HVAC
*   **Outlets:** Use a phone charger to test every wall outlet.
*   **Switches:** Toggle every light switch to ensure they control the intended fixtures.
*   **HVAC:** Turn on the heat and the AC. Ensure air blows consistently and notice any strange odors.
*   **Safety Devices:** Test every smoke detector and carbon monoxide detector by pressing the "test" button.

## 6. Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Date-Stamped Evidence:** Use a timestamped photo app or email your photos to your landlord immediately after the inspection. This creates an indisputable digital record of the move-in date.
*   **Pro Tip: The "Video Walkthrough":** In addition to photos, record a slow, narrated video walkthrough. Mention any discrepancies aloud: "I am noting a scratch on the hardwood floor in the living room corner."
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring "Minor" Issues:** Tenants often skip documenting small issues (e.g., a loose towel rack or minor scuff). Always report these. If they aren't documented, you may be held responsible for them upon move-out.
*   **Pitfall: Verbal Agreements:** Never rely on a landlord saying, "I’ll fix that later." Ensure every repair request resulting from this inspection is documented in writing via email.

## 7. FAQ

**Q: What if I find a major issue during move-in?**
A: Document it immediately with high-quality photos and contact the property manager in writing. If the property is uninhabitable (e.g., no water, active electrical hazard), notify the landlord immediately and keep copies of all correspondence.

**Q: Do I need to be present for the inspection with the landlord?**
A: It is highly recommended. Conducting the inspection together allows you to discuss concerns in real-time and reach an agreement on the property’s current condition.

**Q: How long do I have to submit my inspection report?**
A: Refer to your lease agreement. Most states and landlords allow a 48-to-72-hour window after move-in to submit a formal move-in condition report. Do not exceed this window.

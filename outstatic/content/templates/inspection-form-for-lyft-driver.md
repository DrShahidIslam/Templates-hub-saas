# Standard Operating Procedure: Lyft Vehicle Safety & Compliance Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety inspection protocol for all vehicles operating on the Lyft platform. Maintaining a high vehicle standard is critical for passenger safety, driver compliance, and the preservation of a high star rating. Adherence to these guidelines ensures your vehicle meets both state regulatory requirements and Lyft’s specific safety benchmarks. Conduct this inspection at the start of every shift and maintain a monthly record for insurance and compliance documentation.

## Phase 1: Exterior & Visibility Inspection

*   **Exterior Body Condition:** Check for significant body damage, large dents, or sharp edges that could pose a safety risk to passengers.
*   **Lights & Signaling:** Test headlights (high and low beam), brake lights, reverse lights, and turn signals (front and back). 
*   **Tires:** Inspect tire pressure using a gauge and check tread depth. Ensure tires are not bald (minimum 2/32" depth required) and show no visible dry rot or bulges.
*   **Windshield & Glass:** Check for cracks, chips, or obstructions in the driver’s line of sight. Ensure all mirrors (side and rearview) are firmly attached and adjustable.
*   **Wipers:** Ensure blades clear the windshield effectively without streaking or skipping.

## Phase 2: Interior Safety & Comfort

*   **Seatbelts:** Verify that all passenger seatbelts latch securely and retract properly.
*   **Door Functionality:** Ensure all interior and exterior door handles function correctly. Check that child safety locks are disengaged unless specifically requested.
*   **Climate Control:** Verify that the A/C and heating systems are functional and capable of regulating the cabin temperature.
*   **Upholstery & Cleanliness:** Ensure seats are free of stains, rips, or debris. Clear the floor mats of loose items that could impede pedals.
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Confirm there are no active warning lights (e.g., Check Engine, ABS, Airbag, Oil Pressure) present on the instrument cluster.

## Phase 3: Fluid & Mechanical Systems

*   **Engine Fluids:** Check oil, coolant, and brake fluid levels. Ensure there are no active leaks visible under the vehicle.
*   **Braking Performance:** Conduct a low-speed "brake test" to ensure the vehicle stops without pulling to one side or producing grinding noises.
*   **Steering:** Ensure there is no excessive play in the steering wheel and that the power steering system operates smoothly.
*   **Horn:** Verify the horn is audible and responsive.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Keep a "Driver’s Go-Bag" in the trunk containing a small bottle of glass cleaner, microfiber cloths, and a portable vacuum. A quick 60-second spot-clean between rides prevents poor cleanliness ratings.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a tire tread depth gauge. Do not rely on visual estimation, as it is often inaccurate and can lead to dangerous blowouts.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "minor" warning lights. A check engine light is often a precursor to a total vehicle failure; addressing it early prevents being deactivated from the platform for maintenance issues.
*   **Pitfall:** Failing to document repairs. Always keep digital or physical receipts for professional mechanic visits to provide as proof of vehicle upkeep if Lyft audits your account.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform this inspection?**
A: While a full mechanical check should be done monthly, you should perform a "walk-around" safety check (lights, tires, and interior cleanliness) at the start of every driving session.

**Q: What do I do if my vehicle fails a specific part of the checklist?**
A: If the failure involves safety components (brakes, lights, seatbelts, or tires), do not go online. Remove the vehicle from service immediately and have it repaired by a certified technician.

**Q: Does Lyft require a formal inspection document?**
A: Depending on your city or state regulations, you may be required to upload a formal inspection form signed by a licensed mechanic. Always check the "Documents" tab in your Lyft Driver Dashboard to confirm your local requirements.

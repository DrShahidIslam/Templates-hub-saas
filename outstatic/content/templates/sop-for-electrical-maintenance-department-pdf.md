# Standard Operating Procedure: Electrical Maintenance Department

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the Electrical Maintenance Department to ensure the safe, efficient, and reliable operation of all facility electrical systems. The objective of this document is to standardize maintenance practices, mitigate the risk of electrical hazards, ensure compliance with OSHA and NFPA 70E standards, and maximize the operational lifespan of critical infrastructure. All personnel are required to adhere to these procedures strictly, prioritizing lockout/tagout (LOTO) safety and documentation integrity.

## 1. Pre-Maintenance Preparation and Safety
*   **Permit Verification:** Confirm that a valid Work Order and, if applicable, a Hot Work Permit have been issued and approved.
*   **Lockout/Tagout (LOTO):** Verify that the energy isolation procedure is followed. Apply locks and tags to all energy sources.
*   **PPE Audit:** Ensure all technicians are equipped with the appropriate Arc Flash PPE commensurate with the incident energy levels of the equipment being serviced.
*   **Site Inspection:** Clear the workspace of debris, verify adequate lighting, and ensure a secondary technician is present if working on circuits exceeding 50V.

## 2. Maintenance Execution Checklist
*   **Visual Inspection:** Conduct a thorough visual sweep for charred insulation, signs of water ingress, pests, or physical damage to enclosures.
*   **Thermographic Survey:** Utilize infrared (IR) cameras to identify "hot spots" at busbar connections, terminals, and circuit breakers.
*   **Cleaning:** Remove dust and contaminants using approved non-conductive, vacuum-based methods; avoid compressed air unless it is filtered and moisture-free.
*   **Tightening:** Use a calibrated torque wrench to tighten all electrical connections to manufacturer-specified foot-pounds.
*   **Component Testing:** Perform insulation resistance (Megger) testing and contact resistance testing where required by the equipment’s preventive maintenance schedule.

## 3. Post-Maintenance and Commissioning
*   **Functional Testing:** Verify that all control circuits, interlocks, and safety relays are functioning correctly before closing the main breaker.
*   **Cleanup:** Remove all tools, rags, and temporary debris from the electrical cabinet.
*   **LOTO Removal:** Formally remove locks and tags only after verifying that all personnel are clear of the equipment.
*   **Energization:** Re-energize the system following the facility’s specific startup sequence.
*   **Verification:** Measure voltage and current outputs to ensure they are within the operating parameters.

## 4. Documentation and Reporting
*   **Log Completion:** Update the Asset Management System (CMMS) with the date of service, findings, and parts consumed.
*   **Calibration Records:** Attach photos of terminal readings or test results (IR imagery) to the digital work order.
*   **Inventory Reconciliation:** Note any depletion of critical spares and initiate a requisition request if stock falls below par levels.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always treat a circuit as "live" until a calibrated voltmeter proves otherwise; never rely solely on a circuit breaker's physical position.
*   **Pro Tip:** Keep a "clean as you go" policy. Stray metallic shavings or debris left in a panel are the leading causes of secondary arc faults.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-torquing terminals is as dangerous as under-torquing. It can lead to cracked lugs and compromised structural integrity of the connection.
*   **Pitfall:** Failure to document "near misses" prevents the team from learning patterns in equipment failure. Treat every minor shock or surprise event as a reportable incident.

## FAQ
**Q: How often should our IR thermography be performed?**
A: High-voltage critical infrastructure should undergo IR thermography at least once every six months, while standard distribution panels can be checked annually.

**Q: What is the mandatory response if an electrical component looks physically degraded?**
A: Immediately escalate the finding to the Electrical Supervisor. Do not attempt a "quick fix" or bypass; replace the component with an OEM-spec part to maintain the equipment's UL/CSA rating.

**Q: Who is authorized to remove a LOTO lock?**
A: Only the individual who applied the lock is permitted to remove it. In cases where the employee is unavailable, a strict "Lost Key/Lock Removal" protocol involving the Department Manager and a secondary safety verification must be executed.

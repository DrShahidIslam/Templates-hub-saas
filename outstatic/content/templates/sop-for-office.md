# Standard Operating Procedure: General Office Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the foundational guide for maintaining a professional, efficient, and safe office environment. By standardizing daily opening, maintenance, and closing routines, this document ensures that all staff members contribute to a high-functioning workplace, minimizes administrative friction, and upholds company standards for facility management and operational excellence.

## 1. Opening Procedures
*   **Access Control:** Unlock all primary entry doors and deactivate the security alarm system according to the security protocol.
*   **Climate & Lighting:** Adjust the HVAC system to the designated comfortable range and ensure all common area lighting is fully functional.
*   **Workspace Readiness:** Power on common area printers, copiers, and conference room equipment to ensure system readiness before business hours.
*   **Beverage Stations:** Restock the kitchen coffee service, tea supplies, and ensure the water cooler is operational and clean.
*   **Mail/Deliveries:** Collect and distribute incoming mail from the lobby or mailbox to the appropriate departments or mail slots.

## 2. Mid-Day Operational Maintenance
*   **Common Area Hygiene:** Conduct a "walk-through" every four hours to clear any debris from conference rooms, breakrooms, and reception areas.
*   **Supply Replenishment:** Monitor paper levels in printers and kitchen stock (paper towels, soap, sanitizer); refill as needed.
*   **Meeting Turnaround:** Immediately following a meeting, clear whiteboards, organize chairs, and ensure that AV equipment is turned off or reset for the next user.
*   **Communication:** Review shared digital calendars to ensure upcoming meetings are accounted for and resources are reserved.

## 3. Closing Procedures
*   **Security Sweep:** Verify that all windows are closed and locked. Perform a final patrol of the floor to ensure no unauthorized individuals remain.
*   **Equipment Power-Down:** Ensure all non-essential electronics (copiers, coffee machines, specialized lab equipment) are powered down to save energy and mitigate fire risks.
*   **Clean Desk Policy:** Encourage staff to clear sensitive documents from workstations and secure them in locked pedestals or filing cabinets.
*   **Trash Disposal:** Ensure all waste and recycling bins are moved to the designated pick-up location if applicable, or cleared from common desks.
*   **Final Alarm:** Set the office security system, turn off all interior lighting, and lock the main exit door.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Communication):** Use a shared digital dashboard (like Trello or Slack) to report low supplies in real-time, preventing "out-of-stock" emergencies.
*   **Pro Tip (Organization):** Implement a color-coded bin system for recycling and waste to ensure high compliance with environmental standards.
*   **Pitfall (Neglect):** Do not ignore minor maintenance issues (e.g., a flickering light or a loose handle); report them immediately, as small issues often lead to costly major repairs.
*   **Pitfall (Security):** Never share your physical alarm code or digital access keycard with visitors; always utilize the official visitor sign-in log.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Who is responsible for restocking the breakroom supplies?**
A: Supplies are monitored by the Office Manager; however, all employees are encouraged to notify the office team via the designated request channel when supplies reach the 20% threshold.

**Q: What should I do if the security alarm fails to set at the end of the day?**
A: Do not leave the premises unattended. Immediately contact the security provider listed on the emergency contact sheet posted near the main exit and notify your direct supervisor.

**Q: Are employees allowed to leave personal items in the office overnight?**
A: Personal belongings may be left in personal storage lockers. However, the company is not liable for items left on desks, and desks must be kept clear per the company’s "Clean Desk Policy" for security and hygiene reasons.

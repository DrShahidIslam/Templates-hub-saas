# Standard Operating Procedure: Compliance Audit Readiness for Bangladesh Garment Industry

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive framework for garment manufacturing facilities operating in Bangladesh to ensure alignment with national labor laws (Bangladesh Labour Act 2006/2015), international buyer codes of conduct, and Accord/RSC safety standards. Maintaining proactive compliance not only mitigates the risk of order cancellations and legal sanctions but also fosters a sustainable, ethical work environment. This guide is designed to prepare management and floor supervisors for both scheduled and unannounced audits.

## 1. Structural and Fire Safety (RSC/Accord/Alliance Standards)
*   **Building Integrity:** Ensure valid Structural Safety Certificates are displayed and current.
*   **Egress Routes:** Verify that all exit stairwells are clear of obstructions, doors open outward, and are never locked during working hours.
*   **Emergency Systems:** Inspect fire alarms, smoke detectors, and fire extinguishers; verify they have current inspection tags and are easily accessible.
*   **Fire Sprinklers/Hydrants:** Confirm water pressure levels and ensure no materials are stacked within 18 inches of sprinkler heads.
*   **Documentation:** Maintain up-to-date Fire Safety Inspection reports and electrical system test records (thermography scans).

## 2. Occupational Health and Safety (OHS)
*   **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Ensure workers are utilizing mandated PPE (gloves, masks, goggles, needle guards) for their specific stations.
*   **First Aid:** Verify that first aid boxes are fully stocked, non-expired, and easily accessible on every floor.
*   **Chemical Management:** Ensure all chemicals (dyes, solvents) are stored in a ventilated area with clear Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) in Bengali.
*   **Potable Water/Sanitation:** Confirm access to clean, unlimited drinking water and ensure toilets are gender-segregated and cleaned according to a posted schedule.

## 3. Human Resources and Labor Practices
*   **Age Verification:** Maintain original copies of national ID cards or birth certificates for all employees to prevent child labor.
*   **Working Hours:** Review timekeeping logs to ensure compliance with the Bangladesh Labour Act regarding maximum daily/weekly hours and mandatory overtime limits.
*   **Wage Records:** Verify that salary registers, bank transfer slips, and overtime payment records are accurate and signed by workers.
*   **Employment Contracts:** Ensure every worker has a signed appointment letter and a photo ID card.
*   **Policy Awareness:** Ensure the Code of Conduct and "Anti-Harassment/Anti-Discrimination" policies are posted prominently in the local language.

## 4. Environmental and Legal Compliance
*   **Environmental Clearance:** Ensure a valid Environmental Clearance Certificate (ECC) from the Department of Environment (DoE).
*   **Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** Confirm the ETP is operational, logs are maintained daily, and discharge testing results meet national standards.
*   **Trade License:** Verify the current Trade License, Factory License, and Fire License are renewed and on display.
*   **Waste Management:** Confirm proper segregation of hazardous and non-hazardous waste.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips:
*   **The "Mock Audit" Strategy:** Conduct internal audits once a quarter using the exact checklist of your major buyers.
*   **Digitize Records:** Move away from paper-based records to a digital attendance and payroll system to prevent "tampering" accusations during audits.
*   **Worker Training:** Ensure workers are interviewed during training sessions; they should know the emergency exit locations and their basic rights regarding the minimum wage.

### Common Pitfalls:
*   **"Invisible" Overtime:** Auditors often compare electricity consumption logs against production output to detect off-the-books overtime. Ensure your logs are mathematically consistent.
*   **Blocked Exits:** Floor managers often block exits with "temporary" fabric bins. This is an instant critical fail. Maintain a strict "no-storage-in-aisles" policy.
*   **Inconsistent IDs:** Mismatched names on ID cards versus payroll registers are a red flag for ghost workers. Keep all documentation perfectly synced.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the most common reason for a "Critical Fail" in a Bangladesh garment audit?**
A: Structural or fire safety violations—specifically blocked emergency exits, non-functioning fire alarms, or evidence of unauthorized sub-contracting—are the most frequent causes of immediate audit failure.

**Q: How do we handle discrepancies between factory records and worker verbal interviews?**
A: Always prioritize transparency. If a discrepancy is found during an interview, do not attempt to coach workers during the audit. Address the root cause of the error immediately and document it as a "Corrective Action Plan" (CAP) in progress.

**Q: Are there specific requirements for the Participant Committee (PC)?**
A: Yes. Under the Bangladesh Labour Act, the Participatory Committee must be elected by workers (not appointed by management). Auditors will verify the election minutes and meeting records to ensure the committee is functional and represents worker interests.

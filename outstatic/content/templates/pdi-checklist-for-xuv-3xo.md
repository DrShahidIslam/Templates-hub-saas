# Standard Operating Procedure: Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) for Mahindra XUV 3XO

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for conducting a professional Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) for the Mahindra XUV 3XO. The objective is to ensure the vehicle meets all quality, safety, and operational standards before it is transferred from the dealership to the customer. A thorough PDI minimizes post-delivery complaints, enhances customer satisfaction, and ensures that all technological and mechanical features of the XUV 3XO are functioning as intended.

## 1. Exterior & Paintwork Inspection
*   **Paint Uniformity:** Inspect all body panels under natural light for scratches, dents, paint drips, or mismatched color tones (re-painting indicators).
*   **Panel Gaps:** Check the consistency of gaps between the hood, doors, trunk, and fenders.
*   **Glass Inspection:** Verify that the windshield, windows, and ORVM mirrors are free from cracks, chips, or deep scratches.
*   **Rubber Seals:** Ensure all door beadings and window seals are seated correctly and not damaged.
*   **Wheels & Tires:** Inspect alloy wheels for curb rash. Check all five tires (including the spare) for manufacturing date codes (DOT) to ensure they are fresh, and check for signs of dry rot.

## 2. Under-the-Hood (Engine Bay)
*   **Fluid Levels:** Verify coolant, brake fluid, windshield washer fluid, and engine oil levels using the dipstick.
*   **Battery:** Ensure the battery terminals are clean and tightly secured. Check the manufacturing date sticker on the battery.
*   **Engine Sound:** Start the engine and listen for unusual rattling, hissing, or metallic knocking sounds.
*   **Leaks:** Inspect the floor under the engine bay for any fresh fluid leaks (oil, coolant, or condensation).
*   **Wiring:** Check that all engine bay wiring harnesses are properly clipped and free from exposed copper or signs of rodent damage.

## 3. Interior & Infotainment Systems
*   **Upholstery:** Inspect seats, headliner, and dashboard for stains, tears, or loose stitching.
*   **Adrenox/Infotainment:** Power on the dual-screen setup. Test touch responsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity, navigation accuracy, and the reversing camera feed quality.
*   **Climate Control:** Verify the performance of the HVAC system (AC cooling speed, heater functionality, and fan levels).
*   **Panoramic Sunroof (Skyroof):** Cycle the sunroof through its full open and close positions to ensure the motor operates smoothly without jamming or excessive noise.
*   **Controls:** Test every button on the steering wheel, window switches, door locks, and mirror adjustments.

## 4. Electrical & Lighting Check
*   **Exterior Lighting:** Confirm the "C-shaped" LED DRLs, headlights (high/low beam), fog lamps, turn indicators, and tail lamps are functional.
*   **Interior Lighting:** Check the dome lamps, boot lamp, and vanity mirror lights.
*   **Safety Features:** Ensure the instrument cluster does not display any "Check Engine," "Airbag," or "ABS" warning lights while the engine is running.

## 5. Road Test & Chassis Check
*   **Transmission:** If an automatic, ensure smooth shifts between P, R, N, and D. If a manual, check for clutch bite point and gear shift smoothness.
*   **Suspension:** Listen for abnormal thuds or squeaks when driving over minor bumps or speed breakers.
*   **Braking:** Test braking efficiency at low speeds to ensure there is no pedal "sponginess" or grinding noises.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The 'Daylight' Rule:** Never perform a PDI in a dimly lit basement or after sunset. Artificial lighting masks paint defects and minor scratches effectively.
*   **Check the Spare:** Many dealers forget to check the pressure or the condition of the spare tire. Ensure it is a full-size spare if applicable to your variant.
*   **Odometer Verification:** A new vehicle should typically have between 10km to 50km on the odometer. Anything significantly higher indicates the vehicle may have been used for test drives or transit mishandling.
*   **Software Version:** Ask the technician to check if the latest firmware update for the infotainment system is installed.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Should I sign the delivery papers before performing the PDI?**
A: Absolutely not. Do not sign the final delivery acceptance or registration forms until you are personally satisfied with the condition of the vehicle following your PDI.

**Q: What should I do if I find a defect during the PDI?**
A: Document the defect with clear photographs, bring it to the attention of the Sales Manager immediately, and request a written resolution or a different unit if the defect is major (e.g., body repair or engine issues).

**Q: Does the XUV 3XO require a specific PDI for the electronics?**
A: Yes. Because the XUV 3XO is highly digitized, ensure you spend at least 15 minutes testing the Adrenox system, phone pairing, and the 360-degree camera (if equipped) to avoid common software glitches.

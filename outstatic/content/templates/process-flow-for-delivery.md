# Standard Operating Procedure: Delivery Process Flow

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized workflow for the end-to-end delivery process. The objective of this document is to ensure operational efficiency, maintain inventory accuracy, and uphold the highest standards of customer satisfaction. By adhering to this protocol, delivery personnel and dispatch coordinators ensure that every shipment is tracked, handled securely, and successfully received by the end customer, thereby minimizing transit discrepancies and service failures.

## 1. Order Preparation and Quality Control
*   **Verification:** Confirm order details against the warehouse management system (WMS). Ensure all line items match the picking ticket.
*   **Inspection:** Perform a visual quality check on all products to ensure they are free from defects or damage prior to packaging.
*   **Packaging:** Utilize appropriate protective materials (bubble wrap, corner protectors, tamper-evident seals) based on the item’s fragility and dimensions.
*   **Documentation:** Generate and attach the shipping label and packing slip. Ensure the customer’s address and contact information are legible and accurate.

## 2. Dispatch and Loading
*   **Staging:** Place packaged orders in the designated "Ready for Dispatch" zone, organized by route or delivery priority.
*   **Vehicle Load-In:** Utilize the Last-In, First-Out (LIFO) method to load the delivery vehicle, ensuring the most efficient route flow.
*   **Security:** Verify that all high-value items are secured and that the vehicle load is balanced to prevent shifting during transit.
*   **Handover:** Obtain the driver’s signature on the manifest, confirming the transfer of responsibility from the warehouse team to the logistics team.

## 3. Transit and Delivery Execution
*   **Route Monitoring:** Utilize GPS tracking to monitor progress and adjust for real-time traffic updates.
*   **Communication:** Send a "Delivery En-Route" notification to the customer via SMS or email, including an estimated arrival window.
*   **On-Site Verification:** Upon arrival, verify the recipient’s identity or proof of delivery (POD) requirements.
*   **Documentation:** Capture proof of delivery through digital signature, photo evidence of the package at the doorstep, or physical receipt signature.
*   **Exception Handling:** If the customer is unavailable, follow the "Failed Delivery" protocol (leave a notice, photograph the location, and mark the status as "Attempted" in the system).

## 4. Post-Delivery Closure
*   **Status Update:** Update the internal tracking system to "Delivered" immediately upon completion.
*   **Returns Processing:** If a return is initiated at the point of delivery, ensure the return slip is signed and the item is correctly tagged for inventory reconciliation.
*   **Debrief:** Report any delivery incidents, vehicle malfunctions, or customer feedback to the operations manager for performance logging.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a "Photo POD" (Proof of Delivery) system. It significantly reduces "item not received" claims and increases customer trust.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use route optimization software to reduce fuel consumption and wear on vehicles.
*   **Pitfall:** Never bypass the verification step during loading. Misloaded items lead to high labor costs for re-delivery and customer dissatisfaction.
*   **Pitfall:** Failing to document damaged packaging upon delivery acceptance leads to liability disputes. Always inspect the exterior of the parcel with the customer present.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if the delivery address is incorrect or inaccessible?**
A: Do not attempt to deliver. Contact the dispatch office immediately to verify the address with the customer or to request an updated access code/instruction. 

**Q: How do we handle signature-required packages if the customer is not home?**
A: Follow the company’s re-delivery policy. Typically, leave a "Sorry we missed you" door hanger with instructions for the customer to reschedule or pick up the item at a local hub. Do not leave high-value items unattended.

**Q: If a customer claims the item is damaged upon delivery, what is the protocol?**
A: Document the damage with photos, have the customer sign a damage report, and immediately notify the returns department to initiate an expedited replacement process.

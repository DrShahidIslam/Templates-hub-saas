# Standard Operating Procedure: Obtaining Fire Safety NOC in Odisha

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for procuring a Fire Safety No Objection Certificate (NOC) within the state of Odisha, in accordance with the Odisha Fire Service (OFS) regulations. Adhering to these guidelines ensures compliance with the Odisha Fire Prevention and Fire Safety Rules, minimizing legal risks and ensuring the life safety of occupants. The process is primarily managed through the Odisha Fire Services online portal, promoting transparency and time-bound delivery.

## Phase 1: Pre-Application Documentation & Preparation

Before initiating the online portal submission, ensure all technical documentation is digitized and verified by a licensed professional.

*   **Building Blueprints:** Obtain approved architectural drawings from the local development authority (BDA, CDA, etc.).
*   **Occupancy Certificate:** Secure a copy of the provisional or final occupancy certificate.
*   **Fire Safety Plan:** Engage a fire safety consultant to prepare a layout indicating the location of extinguishers, hose reels, hydrants, smoke detectors, and fire exits.
*   **Technical Specifications:** Collect data sheets for installed fire pumps, alarm panels, and sprinkler systems.
*   **Self-Declaration:** Draft an affidavit on non-judicial stamp paper confirming adherence to the National Building Code (NBC) 2016.

## Phase 2: Online Application Submission

The Odisha Fire Services operates a centralized portal for NOC processing. Follow these steps for a successful submission:

*   **Portal Registration:** Create a user account on the [Odisha Fire Services Portal](https://odishafireservices.gov.in/).
*   **Form Filling:** Select "Apply for Fire Safety Certificate" and input accurate details regarding the building type (residential, commercial, industrial, or educational).
*   **Document Upload:** Attach the PDF copies of the building plans, structural stability certificate, and the Fire Safety Plan prepared in Phase 1.
*   **Payment of Fees:** Calculate the fee based on the building’s total built-up area and risk category; complete the payment via the integrated payment gateway.
*   **Acknowledgement:** Save the Application Reference Number (ARN) for tracking purposes.

## Phase 3: Inspection and Verification

Once the application is reviewed, the Fire Department will conduct a field audit.

*   **Scheduling:** Coordinate with the local Fire Station Officer (FSO) for the date of inspection.
*   **System Demonstration:** Ensure all fire safety equipment is functional. The inspecting officer will typically conduct a "mock test" of the hydrant system or alarm panel.
*   **Compliance Checklist:** Ensure fire exits are clearly marked, not obstructed, and corridors are free of storage.
*   **Rectification:** If the inspector identifies non-compliance, address these points immediately to receive a "Rectification Report" for follow-up.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Always engage a "competent person" or licensed fire engineer for the installation. Documentation signed by a certified professional carries more weight and reduces the likelihood of rejection.
*   **Pro Tip:** Conduct internal "pre-inspections" every six months to ensure that emergency lights and batteries are in working order before the formal renewal date.
*   **Pitfall - Misrepresentation:** Providing incorrect floor area details is the primary cause for immediate rejection and potential blacklisting of the entity.
*   **Pitfall - Obstructed Exits:** Do not store office supplies or waste in fire-rated stairwells. Inspectors have a zero-tolerance policy for blocked fire escape routes.
*   **Pitfall - Expired Equipment:** Ensure all fire extinguishers are serviced and tagged with a valid date. An extinguisher with an expired pressure gauge will fail the inspection.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is the validity period of a Fire Safety NOC in Odisha?**
Generally, the NOC for residential buildings is valid for 3 to 5 years (depending on the height and occupancy), while high-risk commercial or industrial facilities often require annual renewal or validation. Always check the expiry date printed on your specific certificate.

**2. Can I start my business before receiving the final NOC?**
No. Operating a facility without a valid Fire Safety NOC is a violation of the Odisha Fire Prevention and Fire Safety Rules and can lead to the sealing of the premises and legal action against the management.

**3. What should I do if my NOC application is rejected?**
Review the rejection letter provided by the Fire Department. It will explicitly state the deficiencies. Correct the specific safety gaps, prepare a compliance report, and re-apply through the portal under the "Resubmission" section.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Vehicle Inspection Protocol for Lyft Drivers

As an operations professional, maintaining vehicle integrity is not merely a requirement for platform compliance—it is a foundational pillar of passenger safety and service reliability. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory inspection process for Lyft vehicles. Drivers must perform these checks consistently to avoid account deactivation, ensure rider comfort, and mitigate liability. Adherence to this protocol ensures that every trip begins with a vehicle that meets or exceeds the regulatory standards of the ride-sharing platform.

## Pre-Shift Exterior Inspection
*   **Lights and Indicators:** Verify that headlights, high beams, turn signals, hazard lights, brake lights, and reverse lights are fully functional.
*   **Windshield and Glass:** Check for cracks, chips, or obstructions in the driver’s line of sight. Ensure all mirrors are secure and adjusted correctly.
*   **Tires and Wheels:** Inspect tire tread depth (must exceed 4/32 inch) and check for visible bulges or sidewall damage. Ensure all tires are inflated to the manufacturer’s recommended PSI.
*   **Body Integrity:** Confirm there is no significant structural damage, missing bumpers, or sharp exterior edges that could pose a safety risk.
*   **Wipers:** Test wiper blades to ensure they clear the windshield completely without streaking.

## Interior Cleanliness and Safety
*   **Seatbelts:** Verify that all seatbelts (driver and passenger) extend and retract smoothly and click securely into their buckles.
*   **Dashboard and Electronics:** Ensure no dashboard warning lights (Check Engine, ABS, Airbag) are illuminated. Confirm the smartphone mount is secure and does not obstruct the view.
*   **Sanitation:** Remove all trash, personal items, and debris from the floorboards, seats, and seat pockets. The vehicle should be free of lingering odors.
*   **Upholstery:** Inspect seats for excessive staining, tears, or sharp protrusions that could snag passenger clothing.
*   **Climate Control:** Confirm that both heating and air conditioning units are operational to ensure passenger climate comfort.

## Mechanical and Operational Checks
*   **Braking System:** Perform a stationary check to ensure the pedal does not feel "spongy" or sink to the floor.
*   **Fluid Levels:** Check engine oil, coolant, and brake fluid levels. Ensure there are no active leaks under the vehicle.
*   **Horn:** Test the horn to ensure it is audible and functional for defensive driving purposes.
*   **Emergency Kit:** Maintain an onboard kit containing a flashlight, basic first aid supplies, and a phone charging cable compatible with both iOS and Android devices.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Document Everything):** Keep a digital log of your inspection results. If you notice a minor defect, take a photo and note the date; this serves as proof of maintenance should a platform audit occur.
*   **Pro Tip (The "Blind Passenger" Test):** Periodically sit in your own backseat. Check for odors, reach under the seats for trash, and verify that the passenger-side door handle operates smoothly from the inside.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Warning Lights):** Never attempt to "drive through" a Check Engine light. Lyft’s safety algorithms can trigger automatic deactivations if sensors detect major engine issues. Address alerts immediately at a certified mechanic.
*   **Pitfall (Overloading):** Avoid cluttering your trunk with personal belongings. Passengers require full access to the cargo space; failure to provide this is a common reason for low star ratings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should I perform this inspection?**
You should perform a "Quick Walkaround" before every shift. A deep-dive inspection including fluid levels and tire pressure should be conducted at least once per week.

**What should I do if my vehicle fails a specific inspection point?**
If the issue affects safety (e.g., brakes, lights, seatbelts), you must go offline immediately. Do not accept rides until the vehicle has been repaired by a qualified professional.

**Will Lyft reimburse me for inspection-related repairs?**
Generally, no. Vehicle maintenance is the responsibility of the driver. However, keeping records of your repairs can be helpful for tax deductions as a business expense. Always consult with a tax professional regarding your vehicle-related write-offs.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Comprehensive Wedding Execution

Executing a successful wedding requires rigorous logistical oversight, timeline management, and proactive contingency planning. This SOP serves as the master blueprint for managing the wedding lifecycle, from pre-event coordination to post-event breakdown. The objective of this document is to ensure operational continuity, minimize friction between vendors, and guarantee that the couple’s vision is realized within the established constraints of budget and time.

## Phase 1: Pre-Event Logistics (T-Minus 3 Months)

*   [ ] Finalize Master Venue Layout: Map out guest flow, seating charts, and vendor load-in zones.
*   [ ] Vendor Confirmation: Re-verify arrival times, dietary restrictions, and power requirements for all contracted parties.
*   [ ] Emergency Kit Assembly: Prepare a kit containing safety pins, sewing supplies, stain remover, pain relievers, and extra charging cables.
*   [ ] Transportation Logistics: Confirm shuttle routes, pickup times, and parking arrangements for wedding party and VIP guests.
*   [ ] Script & Timeline Review: Finalize the Order of Events (OOE) and distribute a "Day-Of" contact list to all essential personnel.

## Phase 2: Execution & Load-In (Day-Of)

*   [ ] Venue Inspection: Conduct a walkthrough to verify site cleanliness, lighting levels, and HVAC functionality.
*   [ ] Vendor Load-In Coordination: Supervise vendor arrival according to the OOE; ensure decor and AV setup adheres to floor plans.
*   [ ] Lighting & Sound Check: Run a full technical rehearsal for speeches, music cues, and ceremony microphones.
*   [ ] Bridal Suite Management: Oversee the delivery of meals and hydration to the wedding party; confirm the availability of dress steamers/irons.
*   [ ] Guest Arrival Protocol: Manage the usher/greeter transition to ensure guests are seated before the processional begins.

## Phase 3: Event Flow & Breakdown

*   [ ] Transition Management: Execute the seamless transition from ceremony to cocktail hour and reception.
*   [ ] Vendor Meal Sync: Ensure catering staff serves vendors before the main dinner service begins.
*   [ ] Incident Response: Monitor the floor for potential hazards (spills, trip hazards) and manage requests from guests immediately.
*   [ ] Post-Reception Load-Out: Oversee vendor breakdown, ensuring no items are left behind and the venue is returned in its original condition to avoid penalty fees.
*   [ ] Final Reconciliation: Collect all personal items, gifts, and contracts for hand-off to the designated family contact.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The Buffer Zone):** Always build a 15-minute "buffer" into every transition in your timeline. Events rarely start exactly on time; this prevents a domino effect of delays.
*   **Pro Tip (Vendor Autonomy):** Provide vendors with a "Direct Line" sheet. By giving them each other's numbers, you reduce your role as the constant middleman, allowing you to focus on the couple.
*   **Pitfall (The Power Trap):** Never assume a venue has enough power. Always cross-reference the total amperage draw of the band/DJ/lighting rig against the venue’s circuit map. 
*   **Pitfall (Communication Silos):** Avoid relying solely on verbal instructions. If a change is made to the floor plan or timing, issue a brief, written update to all stakeholders immediately.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How should I handle an unexpected guest who does not have an RSVP?**
A: Have a pre-approved "contingency table" or a few extra seats held in reserve. Do not force an immediate decision; seat them politely, then discreetly consult with the couple after the dinner service starts to adjust if necessary.

**Q: What is the most critical document to have on hand during the event?**
A: The "Day-Of Contact Sheet." This should include the cell phone numbers for the lead venue manager, lead catering manager, lead photographer, DJ/bandleader, and a designated family member for emergencies.

**Q: How do I manage a vendor who is behind schedule?**
A: Move to the "Direct Intervention" protocol. Assess if their delay impacts guest experience. If so, reallocate resources or adjust the OOE immediately to hide the delay, then address the vendor’s failure to meet the contract terms post-event.

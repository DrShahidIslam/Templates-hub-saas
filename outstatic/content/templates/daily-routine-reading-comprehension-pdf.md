# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Routine Reading Comprehension

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized workflow for processing daily reading comprehension materials in PDF format. The objective of this procedure is to ensure systematic information extraction, retain key insights, and maintain a high level of cognitive engagement with professional documentation. By adhering to this routine, team members will improve information synthesis, minimize cognitive load, and ensure critical data is archived for future reference.

## Phase 1: Environment & Setup
*   **Clear Workspace:** Remove all physical and digital distractions to ensure deep work focus.
*   **PDF Access:** Open the assigned PDF in a dedicated document viewer (e.g., Adobe Acrobat, Preview, or LiquidText) that allows for annotation.
*   **Define Objective:** Identify the primary purpose of the reading (e.g., policy review, competitive analysis, or technical study) to prime your brain for relevant keywords.

## Phase 2: Active Reading Execution
*   **Initial Skim (The 10% Rule):** Review the Table of Contents, headings, bolded terms, and summary sections to grasp the document structure.
*   **Sequential Analysis:** Read one section at a time. Do not progress to the next section until you can summarize the previous one in a single sentence.
*   **Highlighting Discipline:** Limit highlights to 10% of the text. Use a specific color-coding system:
    *   *Yellow:* Key facts and definitions.
    *   *Blue:* Questions for further investigation.
    *   *Pink:* Action items or required tasks.
*   **Marginalia:** Utilize the comment function to jot down questions or immediate associations prompted by the text.

## Phase 3: Synthesis & Documentation
*   **The "One-Page" Summary:** Draft a brief summary (maximum 250 words) capturing the document’s core message, supporting arguments, and conclusions.
*   **Action Mapping:** Extract all action items from the "Pink" highlights and migrate them into your primary Task Management System (e.g., Asana, Trello, or Outlook).
*   **Archive & Tag:** Save the annotated PDF using a standardized naming convention (YYYYMMDD_Subject_Keywords) to facilitate retrieval.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use Text-to-Speech (TTS) software if your eyes are fatigued; listening to the text while following along can enhance comprehension speed.
*   **Pro Tip:** Apply the "Feynman Technique"—try to explain the document’s main concept to an imaginary colleague after finishing; if you stumble, revisit that section.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-highlighting. If you highlight an entire paragraph, you have effectively highlighted nothing. Stick to critical sentences only.
*   **Pitfall:** Passive reading. Avoid reading to "finish" the document. Read with the intent to apply or teach the material.

## FAQ
**Q: How long should I spend on a single PDF?**
A: This depends on complexity. However, if a document requires more than 60 minutes of intensive focus, it is often more efficient to break it into two sessions to maintain cognitive acuity.

**Q: Should I use AI tools to summarize the PDF for me?**
A: AI tools can assist in creating summaries, but for deep comprehension, you must perform the synthesis yourself. Use AI only as a tool to verify your understanding *after* you have completed your own notes.

**Q: What if the PDF is non-searchable/a scanned image?**
A: Use an OCR (Optical Character Recognition) tool before starting your session to ensure you can search for keywords and highlight text effectively.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Vehicle Inspection

As an operations professional, maintaining the safety, cleanliness, and operational readiness of your vehicle is paramount to ensuring high driver ratings, preventing platform deactivation, and ensuring passenger safety. This SOP outlines the rigorous inspection protocol required to maintain compliance with Uber’s vehicle standards and local regulatory requirements. Adhering to this routine daily not only mitigates mechanical risk but also professionalizes the ride-sharing experience.

## I. Exterior Inspection
*   **Body Integrity:** Walk the perimeter of the vehicle to check for new dents, deep scratches, or damaged panels.
*   **Lighting Systems:** Test headlights (high/low beams), turn signals, brake lights, and hazard lights. Ensure no bulbs are burnt out.
*   **Windshield & Glass:** Inspect for cracks or chips that exceed the size of a quarter. Ensure wiper blades are not streaking or torn.
*   **Tires:** Check tire pressure using a gauge (refer to the driver-side door jamb for PSI). Inspect tread depth—ensure it exceeds the minimum safety threshold (typically 4/32”). Check for visible bulges or punctures.
*   **License Plates:** Ensure both front and rear plates are securely fastened and visible.

## II. Interior & Passenger Experience
*   **Cleanliness:** Remove all personal trash, vacuum floor mats, and ensure seats are free of debris or stains.
*   **Odors:** Ensure the vehicle is free from lingering smoke, food, or pet odors. Use an odor neutralizer if necessary.
*   **Safety Features:** Verify all seatbelts function and retract correctly. Ensure the interior door handles and window controls are fully operational for all passenger doors.
*   **HVAC:** Test both the Air Conditioning and heating systems to ensure passenger comfort in varying climates.
*   **Tech Readiness:** Confirm your mobile device mount is secure, your charging cables (USB/Lightning/USB-C) are functional, and your dashcam (if equipped) is active.

## III. Engine & Mechanical Fluid Levels
*   **Engine Oil:** Check the dipstick level and color; ensure the vehicle is not overdue for an oil change.
*   **Coolant/Antifreeze:** Verify fluid level in the overflow reservoir when the engine is cold.
*   **Windshield Washer Fluid:** Top off the reservoir to ensure visibility during adverse weather.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Confirm fluid is at the "Max" line. If low, inspect for leaks immediately.
*   **Dashboard Warnings:** Start the engine and verify that no "Check Engine," "ABS," or "TPMS" (Tire Pressure) warning lights remain illuminated.

## IV. Documentation & Compliance
*   **Registration:** Ensure a current, valid vehicle registration is in the glovebox.
*   **Insurance:** Keep a digital or physical copy of your ride-share endorsed insurance policy.
*   **Uber Decals:** Ensure the Uber trade dress (emblem) is displayed clearly in the designated window locations per local mandates.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Create a "Driver Kit" kept in the trunk containing a small portable vacuum, extra charging cables, wet wipes, and a flashlight.
*   **Pro Tip:** Perform a "Dashboard Audit" every time you start the car. A ignored minor warning light can lead to a mechanical breakdown during a high-value trip.
*   **Pitfall (Tires):** Never ignore low tire pressure. It reduces fuel economy significantly and negatively impacts braking distance—a primary cause of preventable accidents.
*   **Pitfall (Cleanliness):** Do not rely on air fresheners to mask bad odors. Deep cleaning the upholstery is the only way to ensure a consistently professional environment.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should I perform this inspection?**
A high-level inspection should be part of your "pre-shift" routine every time you log onto the app. A comprehensive fluid and tire check should be performed at least once a week.

**2. What happens if I fail an official Uber vehicle inspection?**
If your vehicle fails an inspection at an Uber Greenlight Hub or partner location, you will typically be prohibited from going online until the necessary repairs are documented, fixed, and verified by a re-inspection.

**3. Do I need to keep a physical log of these inspections?**
While Uber does not always require a daily written log, keeping a simple digital log or maintenance notebook is an excellent practice. It serves as proof of vehicle upkeep should you ever need to dispute a deactivation or manage insurance claims.

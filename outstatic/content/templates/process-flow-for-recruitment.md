# Standard Operating Procedure: Recruitment Process Flow

The objective of this recruitment process is to ensure a standardized, equitable, and efficient approach to talent acquisition. By formalizing these steps, we minimize hiring bias, reduce time-to-fill metrics, and ensure that every new hire aligns with our organizational culture and technical requirements. This procedure applies to all departmental managers and Human Resources personnel involved in the end-to-end lifecycle of a new vacancy.

## Phase 1: Requisition and Role Definition
*   **Identify Need:** Department head completes a 'Personnel Requisition Form' detailing the business case, budget approval, and contract type (Full-time, Part-time, Contract).
*   **Define Competencies:** Draft the Job Description (JD) including core responsibilities, "must-have" vs. "nice-to-have" skills, and salary banding.
*   **Approval Workflow:** Route the JD for final sign-off from the Finance and Departmental lead to confirm headcount authorization.

## Phase 2: Sourcing and Screening
*   **Internal Posting:** Post the vacancy on the internal company portal for 5 business days to promote internal mobility.
*   **External Sourcing:** Distribute the JD across approved channels (LinkedIn, niche job boards, agency partners).
*   **Resume Screening:** HR to filter applications based on the predefined rubric; candidates must meet 70% of "must-have" criteria to proceed.
*   **Initial Phone Screen:** Conduct a 15–20 minute screening call to verify interest, salary expectations, and work authorization.

## Phase 3: Interviewing and Evaluation
*   **Technical Assessment:** Administer role-specific tests (e.g., coding challenge, writing sample, or case study) to ensure candidate output meets quality standards.
*   **Panel Interviews:** Conduct 2–3 rounds of interviews focused on behavioral and situational competencies using a standardized scorecard system.
*   **Reference Checks:** Perform at least two professional reference checks for the final candidate(s).

## Phase 4: Offer and Onboarding Preparation
*   **Selection:** Final hiring team meeting to review scorecards and reach a consensus on the preferred candidate.
*   **Offer Extension:** Generate a formal offer letter including compensation, benefits, and start date.
*   **Background Check:** Initiate standard background screening upon verbal acceptance.
*   **Onboarding Setup:** Trigger the IT and Office Operations request for hardware provisioning and systems access.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Standardize Scorecards.** Use a 1–5 rating scale for every candidate on the same set of questions to remove unconscious bias and facilitate objective post-interview discussions.
*   **Pro Tip: Keep the "Warm" Candidate Engaged.** In a competitive market, maintain weekly communication with the final candidate even if administrative steps (like background checks) are pending.
*   **Pitfall: Role Drift.** Often, hiring managers change the requirements halfway through the process. Ensure the JD is locked *before* the first interview to avoid wasting time interviewing unqualified talent.
*   **Pitfall: Delaying Feedback.** Lengthy response times signal to top-tier talent that your organization is disorganized. Aim to provide feedback within 48 hours of every interview stage.

## FAQ

**Q: How do we handle internal candidates who were not selected?**
A: It is mandatory to provide constructive, private feedback to internal applicants. Emphasize why the external or other internal candidate was a better fit for this specific role, and offer to discuss career development plans to prepare them for future openings.

**Q: What should we do if we cannot find a candidate who meets 100% of the JD?**
A: Evaluate the "nice-to-have" skills. If the candidate hits 80% of the core competencies and demonstrates high "coachability" or potential, it is often more cost-effective to hire and provide training than to hold the position open indefinitely.

**Q: Who makes the final decision on a hire?**
A: The Department Head holds the final decision-making power, provided the candidate has met the minimum bar set by the HR and technical panel. In cases of significant disagreement, the Hiring Manager should facilitate a final calibration meeting.

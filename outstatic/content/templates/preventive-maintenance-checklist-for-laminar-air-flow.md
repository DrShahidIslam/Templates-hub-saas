# Standard Operating Procedure: Laminar Air Flow (LAF) Preventive Maintenance

## Introduction
The Laminar Air Flow (LAF) cabinet is a critical environmental control unit designed to provide a sterile working zone for sensitive laboratory procedures. Maintaining the integrity of the unidirectional airflow and the efficiency of the High-Efficiency Particulate Air (HEPA) filters is mandatory to prevent cross-contamination and ensure operator safety. This SOP defines the standardized preventive maintenance (PM) protocol required to verify system performance, extend component lifespan, and ensure compliance with regulatory standards (e.g., ISO 14644). Strict adherence to this schedule is required for all laboratory personnel and facility engineers.

## Preventive Maintenance Checklist

## Section 1: Pre-Maintenance Safety & Preparation
*   **Decontamination:** Perform a complete wipe-down of the work surface, walls, and sash using an approved disinfectant (e.g., 70% IPA).
*   **PPE Compliance:** Ensure all technicians are wearing cleanroom-appropriate attire, including nitrile gloves, masks, and hairnets.
*   **Power Down:** Switch off the blower motor and ensure the unit is disconnected from the main power source for electrical safety.
*   **Lockout/Tagout:** Apply LOTO procedures if performing electrical component maintenance to prevent accidental activation.

## Section 2: Physical Inspection & Airflow System
*   **Structural Integrity:** Inspect the exterior housing for cracks, rust, or loose paneling.
*   **Sash Inspection:** Verify that the sliding or hinged sash moves smoothly and that the safety sensors/limit switches are fully functional.
*   **Blower/Motor Check:** Listen for abnormal vibrations, grinding noises, or heat buildup during the start-up cycle.
*   **Seal Integrity:** Inspect the gaskets surrounding the HEPA filter and the plenum for any signs of aging, cracking, or air leaks.

## Section 3: Filtration & Technical Performance
*   **Pre-Filter Replacement:** Remove and replace the pre-filter if dust accumulation exceeds 50% of the surface area or if specified by the maintenance schedule.
*   **HEPA Filter Integrity Test:** Conduct a DOP (Dispersed Oil Particulate) or PAO (Polyalphaolefin) challenge test to check for pinhole leaks in the filter media.
*   **Air Velocity Validation:** Measure the laminar air speed using a calibrated anemometer (standard range: 0.3 m/s to 0.5 m/s).
*   **Pressure Differential:** Check the Magnehelic gauge or digital sensor to ensure the pressure drop across the HEPA filter is within the manufacturer's specified operational range.

## Section 4: Electrical & Illumination
*   **Lighting Check:** Replace fluorescent or LED lamps if flickering or dimming occurs; ensure the UV-C germicidal lamp (if installed) is emitting within its effective wavelength.
*   **UV Intensity Verification:** Test the UV bulb output; replace the bulb every 6–12 months or if intensity falls below 40 µW/cm² at the work surface.
*   **Wiring Inspection:** Inspect power cords, internal cables, and terminal connections for signs of overheating, fraying, or oxidation.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always document the "Date of Last Calibration" on a sticker affixed to the front of the unit for easy audit verification.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a logbook of airflow velocity readings over time to detect gradual filter clogging before the system alarms trigger.
*   **Pitfall:** Never use harsh, corrosive cleaners or abrasives on the stainless steel surface or sash glass, as this can degrade the material and create crevices for microbial growth.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring the "Low Flow" alarm is a critical violation; if the alarm persists after a filter check, the blower may be failing, and the unit must be taken out of service immediately.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should I perform a full preventive maintenance check?**
Standard operations require a basic check monthly, a thorough inspection and filter validation every six months, and a full certification (re-validation) at least annually.

**2. What should I do if the HEPA filter shows a leak during the DOP test?**
Do not attempt to patch a HEPA filter with tape or sealant. A compromised filter must be replaced immediately by a certified technician to maintain sterile conditions.

**3. Why is it important to keep the sash at the correct working height?**
The sash height is calibrated to maintain the specific airflow velocity and laminar profile required to protect the product. Working outside this height disrupts the air curtain, risking contamination.

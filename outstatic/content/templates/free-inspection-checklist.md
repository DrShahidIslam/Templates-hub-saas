# Standard Operating Procedure: Free Inspection Protocol

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the rigorous protocol for performing complimentary site or product inspections. The objective of this procedure is to deliver high-value, professional assessments that establish trust with potential clients while minimizing operational overhead. By adhering to these standardized steps, technicians ensure consistent data collection, safety compliance, and the identification of upsell opportunities without compromising the integrity of the "free" service model.

## Phase 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Confirm Appointment:** Contact the client 24 hours prior to verify location, access instructions, and specific areas of concern.
*   **Equipment Audit:** Ensure all necessary diagnostic tools (e.g., thermal cameras, moisture meters, high-lumen flashlights, PPE) are packed and calibrated.
*   **Document Retrieval:** Review the client’s file or project history (if applicable) to identify recurring issues or past repairs.
*   **Insurance/Safety Check:** Verify that site-specific liability waivers are prepared for the client to sign upon arrival.

## Phase 2: Site Arrival and Briefing
*   **Professional Presentation:** Arrive in uniform with company-branded identification visible.
*   **Client Consultation:** Conduct a 5-minute "pain point" interview to understand why the client requested the inspection.
*   **Safety Perimeter:** Establish a safe workspace, clearing obstructions and notifying the client of any hazardous areas being inspected.
*   **Visual Documentation:** Before touching any systems, capture baseline photos of the area to document the pre-existing condition.

## Phase 3: Technical Inspection Protocol
*   **Structural/Surface Assessment:**
    *   Examine for visible cracks, water staining, or structural deformations.
    *   Verify integrity of seals, joints, and fasteners.
*   **Operational/Functional Testing:**
    *   Cycle all relevant mechanical components to observe noise, vibration, or latency.
    *   Use diagnostic tools to check electrical currents or thermal anomalies.
*   **System Integrity Check:**
    *   Inspect internal components for debris, corrosion, or wear-and-tear.
    *   Check for leaks, abnormal odors, or fluid discoloration.
*   **Compliance Verification:**
    *   Ensure all observed systems meet current industry safety standards.

## Phase 4: Closing and Reporting
*   **On-Site De-Brief:** Provide a concise summary of the inspection findings to the client immediately.
*   **Data Entry:** Input findings into the company CRM/Database while onsite to ensure accuracy.
*   **Recommendation Mapping:** Categorize findings into three tiers: Immediate Action Required, Preventive Maintenance, and Long-Term Replacement.
*   **Site Cleanup:** Leave the work area in a cleaner condition than it was found.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The "Educational Pivot":** Use your technical expertise to educate the client on *why* a system is failing rather than just stating that it is. Education builds authority and justifies future paid work.
*   **Pro Tip: Capture "The Evidence":** Always take a photo of a clean filter next to a dirty one, or a failed part next to a new one. Visual proof is the strongest sales tool available.
*   **Pitfall: Scope Creep:** Avoid performing unauthorized repairs or detailed troubleshooting that falls outside the scope of a "free inspection." Always clarify that "repairs require a separate work order."
*   **Pitfall: Over-Promising:** Do not provide verbal guarantees on repair timelines or costs without verifying inventory and labor scheduling back at the office.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Should I provide a written quote immediately after the free inspection?**
Yes, provide a preliminary estimate if possible. If the issue is complex, commit to sending a formal, detailed proposal within 24 business hours to maintain professionalism.

**2. What if the inspection reveals a major safety hazard?**
If a life-safety hazard is discovered, document it, inform the client immediately, and note the warning in the official report. Do not leave the premises until the client has acknowledged the danger in writing.

**3. How do I decline a request for free repairs during a free inspection?**
Maintain a professional script: "My role today is to provide a comprehensive diagnostic report. My tools and expertise are focused on identification. Once we review the report together, we can schedule a professional repair team to address these items for you."

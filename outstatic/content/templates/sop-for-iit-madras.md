# Standard Operating Procedure: Academic and Operational Engagement with IIT Madras

This document outlines the standardized workflow for engaging with the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras for research collaborations, industrial partnerships, or recruitment activities. As one of India’s premier technical institutes, IIT Madras operates under a structured ecosystem requiring strict adherence to administrative protocols, intellectual property guidelines, and campus engagement policies. Following these procedures ensures professional alignment with the institute’s Research Park (IITMRP) and central administrative offices.

## Phase 1: Initiation and Purpose Alignment
*   **Identify the Objective:** Define the specific nature of the engagement (e.g., Sponsored Research, Consultancy, Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) project, or Recruitment/Internship).
*   **Verify Institutional Alignment:** Cross-reference the project goals with IITM’s Center for Industrial Consultancy and Sponsored Research (IC&SR) guidelines.
*   **Initial Point of Contact:** Identify the relevant Department Faculty Coordinator or the Corporate Relations office at the IIT Madras Research Park.
*   **Documentation Check:** Prepare an Executive Summary (max 2 pages) outlining the project scope, technical requirements, and proposed budget.

## Phase 2: Administrative Submission and Formalization
*   **Drafting the Proposal:** Utilize the formal IC&SR templates for all technical and financial proposals.
*   **Financial Compliance:** Ensure the budget includes the necessary Institutional Overhead Charges (O&M) as mandated by IITM.
*   **Legal Review:** Submit the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) or Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to the Legal Cell of IITM via the Office of Industrial Consultancy.
*   **IP Assessment:** Clearly define Intellectual Property rights, foreground IP, and background IP, ensuring alignment with the institute's Technology Transfer Office (TTO) policies.

## Phase 3: Operational Execution and Campus Engagement
*   **Site Visit Authorization:** Apply for a visitor pass via the IITM Security portal at least 48 hours in advance for campus access.
*   **Project Kick-off:** Schedule an initial meeting with the Principal Investigator (PI) and the research team to establish project milestones and key performance indicators (KPIs).
*   **Resource Management:** If utilizing lab facilities, confirm laboratory safety training compliance for all external personnel.
*   **Reporting Cadence:** Establish a bi-weekly progress review cycle to ensure project alignment with the academic calendar.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Leverage the IIT Madras Research Park (IITMRP) ecosystem for commercialization; they offer unparalleled support for scaling academic innovations into industrial applications.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always factor in academic schedules (semester breaks and exam periods) when setting project milestones; faculty availability is significantly reduced during these windows.
*   **Pitfall:** Overlooking the Intellectual Property (IP) ownership clauses. IITM usually retains ownership of IP developed within their labs; always negotiate exclusivity or licensing terms early.
*   **Pitfall:** Failure to account for Institutional Overhead Costs. Never quote a "net" price without confirming the overhead percentage, as this is non-negotiable for the institute.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I approach an individual professor directly for a partnership?**
A: While direct contact with faculty is encouraged for initial discussions, all formal project proposals, financial transactions, and contracts must be routed through the Office of Industrial Consultancy and Sponsored Research (IC&SR).

**Q: What is the standard timeframe for processing an MoU?**
A: Depending on the complexity and scope of the legal clauses, the approval process typically takes between 4 to 8 weeks. It is advised to initiate discussions well ahead of the desired start date.

**Q: How do I recruit students from IIT Madras?**
A: All recruitment activities must be coordinated through the Training and Placement (T&P) Office. Direct outreach to individual students for internships or hiring without T&P notification is considered a violation of institute policy.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Audit Form UBC

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for completing, reviewing, and submitting the Audit Form UBC. Adherence to these guidelines ensures compliance, data integrity, and operational transparency across all departments. This document serves as the single source of truth for auditors and internal stakeholders tasked with validating unit-based compliance (UBC) data.

## Phase 1: Pre-Audit Preparation

*   **Review Documentation:** Access the latest version of the UBC policy manual and ensure familiarity with current performance benchmarks.
*   **System Access Verification:** Log into the secure portal using authorized credentials. Ensure all permissions are active for the specific unit being audited.
*   **Notification:** Provide at least 48 hours' notice to the department head of the unit scheduled for audit, ensuring they have the necessary files ready for review.
*   **Resource Gathering:** Collect all supporting documents (logs, timestamps, and previous audit reports) required to cross-reference entries.

## Phase 2: Execution of Audit Form UBC

*   **Header Accuracy:** Populate the audit header with the correct Unit ID, Auditor Name, and exact Date/Time of the assessment.
*   **Data Validation:** Cross-reference physical observations against system-generated digital reports. Discrepancies must be flagged immediately in the "Notes" column.
*   **Compliance Verification:** Step through each mandatory checklist item within the form. Ensure every item is marked as "Compliant," "Non-Compliant," or "Not Applicable."
*   **Evidence Attachment:** Upload digital copies of supporting evidence (e.g., photo logs or transaction summaries) to the designated file repository linked to the form.
*   **Attestation:** Review all entries for accuracy. By signing the form, you certify that the data represents an objective and thorough evaluation.

## Phase 3: Submission and Follow-Up

*   **Final Review:** Perform a final audit of the form for incomplete fields or missing signatures before clicking "Submit."
*   **Submission:** Submit the form via the secure portal. Ensure the confirmation screen is captured as a record of submission.
*   **Reporting:** Distribute a summary of findings to the Unit Manager and the Department Head within 24 hours of form submission.
*   **Archiving:** Save a localized PDF backup of the submitted form to the department’s shared secure drive.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Bulk Upload" feature for photo evidence to save time during site visits; ensure files are clearly labeled with timestamps.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always double-check the "N/A" justifications. If you mark an item as "Not Applicable," you must provide a brief reason why to prevent automated follow-up flags.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "batch auditing" where multiple units are audited at once. This significantly increases the risk of data cross-contamination and entry errors.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use placeholder data or "To Be Determined" in any text field. If information is missing, it is better to note the deficiency than to falsify an entry.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if the online Audit Form UBC portal is unresponsive?**
A: Do not attempt to use an offline document. Immediately contact the IT Support Desk and report the outage. Document the ticket number and resume the audit only once the system is fully restored.

**Q: Are digital signatures required for all stakeholders?**
A: Yes. All audit forms must be electronically signed by the Auditor and the Unit Lead to ensure accountability and to finalize the audit record.

**Q: How do I handle a "Non-Compliant" finding during an audit?**
A: Flag the issue in the comments section with a detailed description. You must also initiate a "Corrective Action Report" (CAR) and link the CAR reference number directly within the audit form submission.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Electrical Work Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory safety, procedural, and compliance requirements for performing electrical work within the facility. The objective of this document is to eliminate the risk of electric shock, arc flash incidents, and equipment damage while ensuring all work adheres to National Electrical Code (NEC) and Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) standards. This policy applies to all employees and contractors authorized to perform electrical maintenance, installation, or diagnostic tasks.

## Section 1: Pre-Work Preparation and Safety
*   **Permit Verification:** Ensure all necessary work permits are obtained and signed by the Facilities Manager.
*   **PPE Inspection:** Verify that all Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is rated for the specific voltage, is free of tears or defects, and has been tested within the current calendar year. This includes Class 0/00 rubber insulating gloves, leather protectors, arc-rated clothing, and eye protection.
*   **Documentation Review:** Review electrical schematics, one-line diagrams, and equipment manuals before touching any hardware.
*   **Scope Definition:** Clearly define the boundaries of the work to be performed to prevent "scope creep" which often leads to safety oversights.

## Section 2: Isolation and Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)
*   **Notification:** Inform affected personnel and area supervisors of the impending power interruption.
*   **De-energization:** Locate the primary disconnect or circuit breaker and transition it to the "OFF" position.
*   **LOTO Application:** Apply a standardized lock and "Danger: Do Not Operate" tag to the energy isolation device.
*   **Stored Energy Release:** Verify that capacitors are discharged and all secondary energy sources (e.g., batteries, backup generators) are isolated.
*   **Zero-Energy Verification:** Use a calibrated voltmeter to test the line/load side of the equipment to confirm the absence of voltage. **Always** test the meter on a known live source before and after the verification process (Test-Before-Touch protocol).

## Section 3: Execution of Electrical Tasks
*   **Environment Check:** Ensure the workspace is dry, well-lit, and clear of flammable materials or debris.
*   **Tooling:** Use only insulated tools rated for the voltage levels being encountered. Ensure tools are clean and free of conductive debris.
*   **Wiring Standards:** Ensure all wire colors follow local code (e.g., Black/Red for hot, White/Grey for neutral, Green/Bare for ground).
*   **Connections:** Verify all terminals are torqued to manufacturer specifications using a calibrated torque wrench. Loose connections are the leading cause of electrical fires.
*   **Grounding:** Confirm that the equipment grounding conductor is properly bonded to the system ground.

## Section 4: Testing, Restoration, and Closeout
*   **Visual Inspection:** Conduct a thorough visual audit for stray wire strands, debris, or improper insulation gaps before closing panels.
*   **De-commissioning:** Remove all tools, rags, and test equipment from the interior of the enclosure.
*   **Energization:** Remove LOTO devices only after the area is cleared of personnel and all guards/panels are secured.
*   **Operational Validation:** Test the equipment under load to ensure it performs within operational parameters.
*   **Documentation:** Update facility electrical logs, mark-up blueprints if changes were made, and close out the work order in the Maintenance Management System.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always treat every circuit as if it is live, regardless of what the breaker indicates. Never trust a circuit breaker to be a substitute for a physical LOTO procedure.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "clean-as-you-go" policy. Debris left inside a panel can create conductive paths for short circuits.
*   **Pitfall (Complacency):** The "I've done this a hundred times" mindset is the most common precursor to electrical accidents. Always follow the full SOP regardless of the task's simplicity.
*   **Pitfall (Incorrect Meter Usage):** Using a non-contact voltage tester as the primary method for verification is dangerous. Always use a contact-based, calibrated multimeter for true zero-energy verification.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if the lockout device doesn't fit the breaker or disconnect?**
A: Do not attempt to bypass the lockout. Use a lockout hasp or contact the Safety Department for a specialized device. If a safe lockout cannot be achieved, the circuit must remain energized and work must be deferred.

**Q: Am I required to wear arc-rated clothing even for low-voltage tasks?**
A: Yes. All electrical work requires a minimum level of arc-rated protection as defined by your facility's Arc Flash Hazard Analysis. Consult the equipment label for the required Cal/cm² rating.

**Q: What is the procedure if I find an undocumented "ghost" wire during my work?**
A: Stop work immediately. Trace the wire to its source before proceeding. Never cap a wire if you do not know its origin or purpose, as it could be a back-fed circuit from an auxiliary power source.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Quote Generation & Delivery

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional workflow for managing the daily quote cycle. Whether providing price estimates for clients or generating inspirational messaging for internal operations, the goal is to maintain consistency, accuracy, and professional tone. Adhering to this routine ensures that response times remain competitive while minimizing errors in data entry or communication.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Data Verification
*   **System Check:** Ensure the CRM (Customer Relationship Management) software or quote-generation platform is active and synced with current pricing indices.
*   **Review Pending Requests:** Audit the queue to identify urgent requests prioritized by timestamp or service level agreement (SLA) status.
*   **Gather Specifications:** Verify all necessary inputs—quantities, material costs, labor hours, and specific client requirements—are present before starting the calculation.
*   **Inventory/Resource Check:** Confirm that the items or services being quoted are currently available or within lead-time projections.

## Phase 2: Execution and Calculation
*   **Drafting the Quote:** Input data into the template, ensuring all fields (SKUs, descriptions, unit costs) are populated accurately.
*   **Dynamic Pricing Audit:** Apply standard markup percentages or volume discounts according to company fiscal policy.
*   **Proofreading:** Verify mathematical accuracy, unit precision, and client contact details. 
*   **Formatting:** Ensure the final document adheres to brand guidelines, including company logo placement, disclaimer language, and payment terms.

## Phase 3: Review and Dispatch
*   **Managerial Sign-off:** For quotes exceeding specific fiscal thresholds, route the document through the designated approval workflow.
*   **Transmission:** Dispatch the quote via the preferred professional channel (email, client portal, or formal document submission).
*   **Tracking Update:** Move the lead status in the CRM from "Quote Pending" to "Quote Sent" and schedule a follow-up date.
*   **Archiving:** Save a copy of the finalized quote to the shared drive or document management system for future audit trails.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Template Utilization:** Build standardized templates for common request types to reduce drafting time by up to 60%.
*   **Automated Triggers:** Utilize CRM automation to send "Quote Sent" confirmations automatically, providing immediate feedback to the client.
*   **The "Buffer" Strategy:** When estimating lead times, add a 10% administrative buffer to manage client expectations during unforeseen supply chain delays.

### Pitfalls
*   **The "Copy-Paste" Error:** Always review the specific nuances of a new quote; using a past quote as a baseline without clearing old dates or client-specific terms is a common source of liability.
*   **Ignoring Disclaimers:** Never omit legal disclaimers regarding price expiration dates; failing to set a "valid until" date exposes the firm to fluctuating market costs.
*   **Delayed Follow-up:** Allowing a sent quote to sit without a scheduled follow-up is the primary reason for lost opportunities.

## FAQ

**Q: How long should a quote remain valid?**
A: Industry standards typically suggest a validity period of 14 to 30 days. Anything longer risks exposure to inflation or material cost volatility.

**Q: What should I do if the client requests a price lower than my calculated floor?**
A: Do not bypass the floor manually. Refer the request to a department head or the sales manager to review potential volume-based exceptions.

**Q: How should I handle a high volume of requests?**
A: Implement a "batch processing" methodology where you verify all requests in the morning and perform all calculations in a concentrated block to maintain flow and reduce cognitive switching costs.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Vehicle Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory inspection and maintenance protocols required to ensure vehicle longevity, safety, and optimal performance. Regular adherence to this checklist mitigates the risk of mechanical failure, maintains manufacturer warranty compliance, and minimizes long-term repair costs. All inspections should be performed on a level surface with the engine cooled to ambient temperature.

## 1. Under-the-Hood Inspection
*   **Engine Oil:** Check the dipstick level and color. Oil should be amber/brown; milky or metallic-flecked oil indicates severe mechanical issues.
*   **Coolant Reservoir:** Ensure levels are between the "Min" and "Max" indicators. Never open a hot radiator cap.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Verify the reservoir is full. Inspect fluid clarity; dark fluid often indicates moisture contamination.
*   **Windshield Washer Fluid:** Top off with appropriate seasonal solvent.
*   **Battery Terminals:** Check for white or blue corrosion. Clean with a wire brush and baking soda solution if necessary.
*   **Belts and Hoses:** Visually inspect serpentine belts for fraying, cracks, or glazing. Squeeze radiator hoses to check for brittleness or soft spots.

## 2. Tire and Undercarriage Safety
*   **Tire Pressure:** Measure cold tire pressure (including the spare) using a digital gauge. Adjust to the manufacturer’s PSI found on the driver-side door jamb.
*   **Tread Depth:** Check for uneven wear patterns. Use a tread depth gauge or the "penny test" (minimum 2/32" depth required).
*   **Visual Damage:** Inspect sidewalls for bubbles, cuts, or embedded debris.
*   **Suspension/Steering:** Check for leaking struts or shock absorbers. Look for excessive play in CV boot joints.

## 3. Exterior and Visibility Systems
*   **Lighting Check:** Cycle through headlights (low/high), turn signals, hazard lights, brake lights, and reverse lights.
*   **Wiper Blades:** Inspect rubber for streaks or dry rot. Replace if blades skip or fail to clear the glass in a single pass.
*   **Side Mirrors/Glass:** Verify no cracks or chips are present that obstruct the driver’s field of vision.

## 4. Interior and Dashboard Diagnostics
*   **Warning Lights:** Turn ignition to the "On" position to ensure all dash warning lights (Check Engine, ABS, Airbag) illuminate momentarily and then turn off after ignition.
*   **Pedal Feel:** Ensure the brake pedal is firm and does not sink to the floor. Verify the parking brake holds the vehicle securely.
*   **Cabin Air Filter:** Remove and inspect for debris or heavy dust accumulation; replace every 12,000–15,000 miles.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a digital or physical "Vehicle Service Log." Documenting dates, mileage, and parts replaced significantly increases the resale value of your vehicle.
*   **Pro Tip:** If your vehicle is equipped with a Turbocharger, ensure you are using the specific oil viscosity recommended in your manual, as improper oil can destroy the turbo bearing.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not overfill the engine oil. Excess oil can cause foaming, which leads to poor lubrication and potential engine seal damage.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "minor" noises. A chirping belt or grinding brake pad is a precursor to a total system failure. Address these immediately.

## FAQ
**Q: How often should I perform this maintenance checklist?**
A: A comprehensive inspection should be performed at least every 5,000 miles or twice a year, whichever comes first.

**Q: Should I use generic parts for replacements?**
A: OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) parts are recommended for critical systems like brakes, sensors, and engine components to ensure perfect compatibility and performance.

**Q: What does the "Check Engine" light mean?**
A: It indicates that the vehicle’s On-Board Diagnostics (OBD-II) system has detected a malfunction in the engine or emissions system. Use an OBD-II scanner to read the specific fault code before driving long distances.

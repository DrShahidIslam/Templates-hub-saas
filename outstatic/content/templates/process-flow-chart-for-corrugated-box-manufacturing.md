# Standard Operating Procedure: Corrugated Box Manufacturing Process Flow

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized workflow for the manufacturing of corrugated fiberboard boxes. The objective is to maintain structural integrity, ensure dimensional accuracy, and maximize production efficiency while adhering to safety and quality control protocols. This document serves as a guide for floor supervisors and production teams to ensure consistency from raw material intake through to final palletization.

## Phase 1: Raw Material Preparation and Corrugation
*   **Paper Reel Inspection:** Verify GSM (grams per square meter) and moisture content of Kraft paper rolls (linerboard and medium).
*   **Splicing:** Ensure seamless transition between paper rolls using the auto-splicer to maintain continuous feed.
*   **Pre-Conditioning:** Apply steam and heat to the medium on the corrugator rollers to ensure optimal flute formation.
*   **Adhesive Application:** Monitor starch-based glue viscosity and temperature to ensure a strong bond between the medium and the liner.
*   **Drying and Slitting:** Pass the corrugated sheet through the hot plate section to set the glue, followed by precise width slitting and scoring (creasing).
*   **Cut-off:** Shear the continuous board into individual sheets based on the specified order dimensions.

## Phase 2: Converting and Finishing
*   **Flexographic Printing:** Align printing plates to ensure registration accuracy, checking ink viscosity and color consistency against the master proof.
*   **Slotting and Scoring:** Adjust slotting heads to ensure clean cut-outs for flaps and precision score lines for easy folding.
*   **Die-Cutting (If Applicable):** For custom or irregular boxes, mount the rotary or flatbed die precisely to ensure uniform edge quality.
*   **Gluing/Stitching:** Apply adhesive or mechanical stitches to the manufacturer’s joint (tab). Verify the "squaring" of the box to prevent trapezoidal folding.
*   **Folding and Compression:** Pass the joined boxes through the folding section and apply vertical pressure to ensure the adhesive cures under the correct tension.

## Phase 3: Quality Assurance and Shipping
*   **Bursting Strength Test:** Conduct periodic tests to ensure the board meets the required grade (e.g., 200# test).
*   **Dimensional Audit:** Measure internal length, width, and height against the technical specification sheet.
*   **Stacking and Palletizing:** Ensure boxes are stacked symmetrically; use edge protectors and stretch-wrap to prevent transit damage.
*   **Inventory Tagging:** Apply labels detailing SKU, quantity, customer PO, and production date.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Moisture Control):** Corrugated board is highly sensitive to humidity. Maintain the warehouse environment between 40-60% relative humidity to prevent "washboarding" or warping.
*   **Pro Tip (Anilox Maintenance):** Clean printing anilox rollers immediately after shifts to prevent dried ink buildup, which causes inconsistent print quality.
*   **Pitfall (Glue Failure):** "Delamination" often occurs when starch glue temperature is too low or application pressure is insufficient. Check glue lines on every roll change.
*   **Pitfall (Over-scoring):** Deep scores can weaken the structural integrity of the box, leading to shelf collapse. Periodically perform a "crush test" on score lines to ensure they provide flexibility without fracturing the paper fibers.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I calibrate the slitting and scoring knives?**
A: Knives should be inspected for sharpness at the beginning of every shift. Full calibration for dimensional accuracy should occur after any machine jam or during changeovers between vastly different board thicknesses.

**Q: What is the most common cause of "fish-tailing" during the folding process?**
A: Fish-tailing is usually caused by an off-center score or an imbalance in the folding belt tension. Verify the squareness of the blank and check that the folder-gluer belts are applying equal pressure on both sides.

**Q: How do I handle minor warping in the finished corrugated board?**
A: Warping is typically a result of moisture differential between the liner and the medium. Adjust the steam showers on the corrugator or increase the heat in the hot plate section to balance the moisture content across the board.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Housekeeping Quality Assurance Audit

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the rigorous criteria and methodology for conducting a comprehensive housekeeping audit. The objective of this protocol is to ensure consistent cleanliness, maintenance, and sanitation standards across all facility areas. By adhering to this checklist, management ensures guest/client satisfaction, upholds brand reputation, and maintains operational compliance with health and safety regulations. Auditors must approach each inspection with an objective, detail-oriented mindset, documenting findings transparently to facilitate continuous improvement.

## 1. Entryway, Foyer, and Public Areas
*   **Flooring:** Check for dust, debris, scuff marks, or wet spots. Ensure carpets are vacuumed in uniform lines.
*   **Glass & Mirrors:** Inspect for streaks, fingerprints, or smudges on entry glass and decorative mirrors.
*   **Furniture:** Confirm upholstery is free of stains, hair, and dust. Ensure cushions are straightened and aligned.
*   **Signage & Décor:** Dust all light fixtures, frames, and informational signage. Verify that all bulbs are functional.
*   **Aromatics:** Confirm the area is free of stale or unpleasant odors; verify air purification/scenting systems are active.

## 2. Guest/Individual Rooms
*   **Bedding:** Inspect linens for stains or tears. Verify hospital corners and tight mattress sheets. Ensure pillows are fluffed and positioned correctly.
*   **Surfaces:** Use the "White Glove Test" on high-touch surfaces (nightstands, headboards, remote controls, switches). 
*   **Bathroom Sanitation:** 
    *   Verify chrome fixtures are polished and free of water spots.
    *   Check for hair in drains, tubs, or sink basins.
    *   Ensure toilet seals are sanitized and the bowl is scrubbed under the rim.
    *   Confirm bathroom amenities (soap, shampoo, towels) are stocked at established par levels.
*   **HVAC & Air Quality:** Check vents for dust buildup; verify that the climate control system operates quietly and produces appropriate temperatures.

## 3. Storage, Utility, and Back-of-House
*   **Cart Organization:** Ensure housekeeping carts are clean, neatly organized, and free of overflowing trash.
*   **Chemical Safety:** Verify all chemical bottles are clearly labeled with secondary labeling and that Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are accessible nearby.
*   **Inventory Levels:** Audit cleaning supplies (liquids, cloths, mops) against the standard stock count to identify theft or over-ordering.
*   **Equipment Maintenance:** Inspect vacuum bags, floor buffers, and cart wheels for damage or excessive wear.

## 4. Safety and Compliance
*   **Fire Exits:** Confirm exits are unobstructed by carts, cleaning supplies, or temporary furniture.
*   **Electrical:** Ensure no frayed cords are in use and that outlets are not overloaded with cleaning equipment.
*   **Hazard Reporting:** Verify that any reported maintenance issues (leaks, broken tiles) have an associated work order submitted in the system.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: The "Guest Perspective" Walk:** Enter the room exactly as a guest would. Pause for five seconds upon entering. Often, your initial impression—the "first smell" or a stray piece of lint on the floor—is what the guest will notice.
*   **Pro Tip: Check the "Hidden" Spots:** Always look under the bed, behind the bathroom door, and inside the coffee maker. These are the most common areas where housekeeping staff may inadvertently cut corners.
*   **Pitfall: Relying on Routine:** Do not let the checklist become a "pencil-whipping" exercise. If you audit the same way every time, you become blind to gradual declines in quality. Rotate your focus areas periodically.
*   **Pitfall: Inconsistent Feedback:** Always provide the housekeeping supervisor with specific, actionable feedback immediately following the audit, rather than just delivering a score.

## FAQ

**Q: How often should a formal housekeeping audit be conducted?**
A: High-traffic facilities should conduct spot audits daily, with a full, deep-dive room audit performed on at least 10% of total inventory weekly to ensure consistent standards.

**Q: What should I do if I find a critical failure (e.g., pests or severe mold)?**
A: Immediately pull the room from inventory, notify the Facilities Manager and the General Manager, and escalate the issue to the professional cleaning/remediation vendor. Do not allow a guest to check in until the issue is resolved and re-inspected.

**Q: Should the housekeeping staff be present during the audit?**
A: It is generally recommended to conduct audits independently to ensure an unbiased assessment of the end result. However, post-audit reviews should include the housekeeping lead to discuss findings and provide necessary training.

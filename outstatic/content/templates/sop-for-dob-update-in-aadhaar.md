# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Date of Birth (DOB) Update in Aadhaar

This Standard Operating Procedure outlines the mandatory protocols and procedural steps for updating the Date of Birth (DOB) in an Aadhaar record. As the Date of Birth is a critical demographic field, the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) maintains strict governance over these updates. Adherence to this SOP ensures compliance with data integrity standards, prevents fraudulent identity claims, and minimizes the likelihood of request rejection due to documentation mismatch.

## Phase 1: Pre-Submission Preparation
*   **Verify Current Status:** Ensure the resident has access to the Aadhaar-linked mobile number, as OTP-based authentication is mandatory for online requests.
*   **Documentation Audit:** Confirm possession of an original, valid Proof of Date of Birth (PoDB) document. Ensure the document is scanned clearly in color or physically present for enrollment center verification.
*   **Self-Correction Limit:** Verify that the resident has not exhausted their allowed update limit (typically one-time for DOB). If the limit is exceeded, an in-person visit to an Aadhaar Enrollment Center is mandatory.

## Phase 2: Execution (Online or In-Person)
### For Online Updates (Self-Service Portal)
*   **Authentication:** Log in to the official UIDAI "MyAadhaar" portal using the Aadhaar number and the registered mobile OTP.
*   **Data Entry:** Navigate to 'Update Aadhaar Online' and select 'Date of Birth.'
*   **Document Upload:** Upload a high-resolution, color-scanned copy of the original PoDB document (e.g., Birth Certificate, Passport, SSLC/Mark sheet).
*   **Review & Payment:** Verify the accuracy of the new entry, accept the declaration, and proceed to the non-refundable payment gateway.

### For In-Person Updates (Enrollment Center)
*   **Appointment Scheduling:** Book an appointment via the UIDAI portal to reduce wait times.
*   **Biometric Verification:** Present the original PoDB document to the operator. The operator will scan the document and capture biometric authentication (fingerprints/iris) of the resident.
*   **Verification:** Review the printed preview form provided by the operator for typographical errors.
*   **Acknowledgment:** Collect the URN (Update Request Number) slip and ensure the operator provides a signed receipt.

## Phase 3: Post-Submission Tracking
*   **Monitoring:** Use the URN to track status on the official portal.
*   **Resolution:** Monitor the registered email/mobile for notifications regarding acceptance or rejection.
*   **Final Download:** Once the status shows "Completed," download the updated e-Aadhaar from the official website.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pitfall - The "Gap" Issue:** If the change in DOB involves a variation of more than 3 years from the recorded DOB, the system may flag it as an "Exception" case, requiring a visit to an Enrollment Center.
*   **Pro Tip - Quality Control:** Ensure the uploaded document is not a photocopy or a black-and-white scan. Use a professional scanning app or flatbed scanner to ensure the text, seal, and signature are legible.
*   **Pitfall - Incorrect Document:** Do not upload non-standard documents like "Affidavits" or "Self-Declarations" for DOB updates. UIDAI strictly mandates government-issued documents containing the full date of birth.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How many times can I change my Date of Birth in Aadhaar?**
A: As per current UIDAI policy, a resident is allowed to update their Date of Birth only once in their lifetime. For exceptional cases, one must visit the nearest Aadhaar Enrolment Center.

**Q: What documents are accepted as valid proof for DOB updates?**
A: Standard documents include a Birth Certificate, Passport, SSLC Certificate/Marksheet, PAN Card, or a Government-issued Photo ID card containing the DOB.

**Q: How long does the update process typically take?**
A: Once the request is submitted, the verification process usually takes between 7 to 30 working days. You can track the real-time status using your URN on the UIDAI website.

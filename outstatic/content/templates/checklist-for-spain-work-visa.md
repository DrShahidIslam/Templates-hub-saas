# Standard Operating Procedure: Spain Work Visa Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for foreign nationals seeking to obtain a work visa for Spain. Securing residency and work authorization in Spain requires meticulous documentation, adherence to specific consular requirements, and strict timelines. This document outlines the procedural flow to minimize the risk of application rejection and ensure a streamlined transition to employment within the Spanish territory.

## Phase 1: Pre-Application and Eligibility Assessment

*   [ ] **Identify Visa Type:** Confirm the appropriate category (e.g., Highly Qualified Professional, EU Blue Card, or General Work Visa via Labor Certification).
*   [ ] **Employer Authorization:** Ensure the employer has initiated the "Autorización de Residencia y Trabajo" (initial residency and work permit) with the Spanish Ministry of Labor.
*   [ ] **Passport Validity:** Verify passport is valid for at least 12 months beyond the intended arrival date and has at least two blank pages.
*   [ ] **Consular Jurisdiction:** Confirm your local Spanish Consulate or Embassy based on your current legal residence.

## Phase 2: Documentation Collection (Apostilled/Legalized)

*   [ ] **National Visa Application Form:** Complete and sign two copies of the official visa application form.
*   [ ] **Photographs:** Two recent, passport-sized color photos with a white background.
*   [ ] **Criminal Record Certificate:** Obtain an original, apostilled criminal background check from your country of residence (and any country resided in over the last five years).
*   [ ] **Medical Certificate:** A formal medical report signed by a registered physician stating you do not suffer from any diseases that could cause serious public health repercussions (per International Health Regulations 2005).
*   [ ] **Proof of Economic Means:** Provide evidence of sufficient funds (if applicable) or the employer’s formal contract.
*   [ ] **Proof of Residence:** Documents verifying your legal residence within the jurisdiction of the consulate where you are applying.

## Phase 3: Submission and Processing

*   [ ] **Visa Fee Payment:** Prepare the exact fee in the requested currency/method (typically money order or bank transfer as per consulate policy).
*   [ ] **Appointment Scheduling:** Book the consular appointment as soon as the initial labor authorization is granted, as slots fill quickly.
*   [ ] **Application Submission:** Attend the appointment in person to submit the file and provide biometric data if required.
*   [ ] **Visa Collection:** Monitor the status of your application via the provided tracking number and collect the visa label.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The "Apostille" Trap:** Many applicants fail to realize that foreign documents must be Apostilled (Hague Convention) or legalized by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs before submission. A standard notarization is insufficient.
*   **Translation Strictness:** All documents not in Spanish must be translated by a *Traductor Jurado* (sworn translator) officially recognized by the Spanish government. Translations performed by non-certified agencies will be rejected.
*   **The 90-Day Window:** Once the residency approval notification is received from Spain, you generally have a limited window (often 30–90 days) to apply for the actual visa. Do not delay.
*   **Timing of Medical Reports:** Medical certificates often have an expiration date (usually 3 months from the date of issuance). Do not request these too far in advance.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I apply for a work visa while visiting Spain on a tourist visa?**
A: No. Under standard regulations, you must apply for the work visa from your country of origin or legal residence. Applying while physically present in Spain as a tourist is generally not permitted unless you are changing from a specific student residency status.

**Q: How long does the visa processing take?**
A: Processing times vary significantly by consulate, typically ranging from 4 to 8 weeks after the appointment. Always factor in an additional 2-4 weeks for document procurement and translation.

**Q: What is the TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero)?**
A: The visa in your passport is usually a temporary entry document valid for 90 days. Upon arrival in Spain, you are required to register with the local authorities and apply for your TIE (Physical Residency Card) within 30 days of entry.

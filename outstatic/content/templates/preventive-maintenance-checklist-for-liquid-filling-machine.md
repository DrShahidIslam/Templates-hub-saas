# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance for Liquid Filling Machines

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory preventive maintenance (PM) protocols for liquid filling machines to ensure operational efficiency, hygienic compliance, and mechanical longevity. Regular adherence to this schedule minimizes unplanned downtime, prevents product contamination, and ensures consistent fill volumes. Technicians must follow these steps according to the prescribed frequency (Daily, Weekly, or Monthly) to maintain the integrity of the pneumatic, mechanical, and electronic components of the system.

## 1. Daily Inspection & Sanitation (Pre-Shift)
*   **Visual Integrity Check:** Inspect all seals, gaskets, and tubing for signs of wear, cracking, or leaks.
*   **Sanitization Cycle:** Perform a Clean-in-Place (CIP) or manual sanitization of all product contact parts (nozzles, hoses, and valves) to prevent microbial growth.
*   **Nozzle Calibration:** Check nozzle alignment relative to the conveyor line to prevent bottle neck collisions.
*   **Fluid Supply Lines:** Verify that hoses are free of kinks and that supply pressure is consistent with the machine’s operational manual.
*   **Emergency Stop Test:** Cycle the Emergency Stop (E-Stop) button to ensure it immediately terminates machine movement.

## 2. Weekly Mechanical & Pneumatic Maintenance
*   **Pneumatic System Purge:** Drain the water trap in the air filter-regulator unit to ensure dry, clean air enters the pneumatic cylinders.
*   **Lubrication:** Apply food-grade lubricant to all moving linear bearings, guide rods, and cam followers. Wipe away excess grease to prevent dust accumulation.
*   **Fastener Audit:** Check and tighten all critical bolts, mounting brackets, and nozzle adjustment screws that may have vibrated loose during production.
*   **Sensor Calibration:** Clean photoelectric sensors and proximity switches with a soft, lint-free cloth. Verify that they trigger accurately when a container is present.
*   **Conveyor Track Check:** Inspect the conveyor belt for tension and ensure the guide rails are adjusted correctly for current container dimensions.

## 3. Monthly Component & Electronic Overhaul
*   **Seal Replacement:** Replace O-rings and dynamic seals in the filling pistons/diaphragm pumps if there is any evidence of volume drift or product weeping.
*   **Electrical Cabinet Audit:** Inspect the control cabinet for loose wiring, signs of overheating, or dust buildup. Use non-conductive compressed air to clear debris (ensure power is locked out).
*   **Software/PLC Backup:** Verify the integrity of the PLC program and ensure operational parameters are saved as a backup.
*   **Pump & Motor Inspection:** Listen for abnormal bearing noise in the main drive motor or pump assemblies. Check for signs of grease leakage from motor seals.
*   **Precision Fill Audit:** Perform a gravimetric fill accuracy test. Compare the actual weight against the machine’s digital display to identify if calibration offsets are needed.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Documentation):** Always maintain a digital or physical "Maintenance Log." Recording the specific "before" and "after" fill weights during calibration provides invaluable data for troubleshooting long-term drift.
*   **Pro Tip (Organization):** Use color-coded sanitization brushes. One set for product-contact surfaces and a completely separate, distinct set for machine frame/conveyor cleaning to prevent cross-contamination.
*   **Pitfall (Over-lubrication):** Excessive grease is a common error. It attracts airborne contaminants and dust, which can act as an abrasive, accelerating wear on moving parts. Apply sparingly.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Air Quality):** Failing to drain the air-line water trap is the #1 cause of pneumatic solenoid failure and rust inside cylinders.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I replace the filling nozzles?**
A: There is no fixed interval, as it depends on the viscosity and abrasiveness of your product. Replace them if you notice burrs on the tips, irregular flow patterns, or if they fail the visual inspection for cleanliness/corrosion.

**Q: Why does the fill weight fluctuate throughout the shift?**
A: Fluctuations are typically caused by air bubbles in the feed line, temperature changes affecting product viscosity, or loose pneumatic seals. Check the supply tank level and verify that the pump intake is airtight.

**Q: Can I use standard industrial lubricants on my filling machine?**
A: Absolutely not. You must use only NSF-certified food-grade lubricants (e.g., H1-rated). Standard industrial oils can contaminate the product, posing a severe health risk and violating FDA/HACCP safety standards.

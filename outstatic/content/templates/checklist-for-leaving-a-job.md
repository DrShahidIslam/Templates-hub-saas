# Standard Operating Procedure: Professional Job Offboarding

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive framework for employees transitioning out of an organization. The objective is to ensure a seamless departure that maintains professional integrity, protects institutional knowledge, and preserves valuable professional relationships. Following these protocols minimizes administrative friction and ensures you leave your position on excellent terms with your former employer.

## Phase 1: Notice and Administrative Preparation

*   **Review your contract:** Confirm notice period requirements and any clauses regarding non-competes or intellectual property.
*   **Formally resign:** Draft a formal letter of resignation addressed to your direct manager. Keep it concise, professional, and positive.
*   **Coordinate the timeline:** Agree upon a final date with your manager and confirm the offboarding schedule (exit interview, IT handover, etc.).
*   **Review benefits:** Contact HR to understand the status of accrued PTO, 401(k) vesting, health insurance coverage, and stock options.

## Phase 2: Knowledge Transfer and Documentation

*   **Compile a handover document:** Create a comprehensive "Source of Truth" document that includes login credentials (if applicable), recurring task schedules, and key stakeholder contact lists.
*   **Archive work files:** Ensure all active project files are uploaded to the company cloud or shared drives. Organize folders by status (e.g., "Active," "Pending," "Archived").
*   **Map workflows:** Document non-obvious processes or unique team habits that a successor would need to know to perform your role effectively.
*   **Conduct a handover meeting:** Schedule a dedicated session with your replacement or the team lead to walk through the handover document and answer outstanding questions.

## Phase 3: Technical and Asset Offboarding

*   **Clean your digital footprint:** Delete personal files from company devices. Remove personal accounts (e.g., private email, social media) from browsers or applications.
*   **Asset inventory:** Collect all company property, including laptops, monitors, security badges, corporate cards, and uniforms.
*   **Coordinate IT handoff:** Follow company policy for wiping data or returning hardware to the IT department. Obtain a receipt for all returned equipment.
*   **Email management:** Setup an "Out of Office" auto-responder that directs inquiries to the appropriate internal contact for your specific projects.

## Phase 4: Professional Closure and Networking

*   **Clean your workspace:** Clear out your physical desk, cabinets, and common areas.
*   **Update LinkedIn:** Prepare to update your employment status on your end date.
*   **Send farewell communications:** Send brief, professional emails to close collaborators and mentors thanking them for their support.
*   **Request recommendations:** If appropriate, ask managers or long-term colleagues for a recommendation on LinkedIn while your work is still fresh in their minds.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Treat your last two weeks as if you are auditioning for a future reference. Your reputation is often cemented in the final days of your tenure.
*   **Pro Tip:** If you have high-level responsibilities, offer to be available for a brief "consultation period" via email for a few days post-departure to handle minor emergencies.
*   **Pitfall:** Burning bridges. Even if your departure is due to frustration, do not use the exit interview as a platform for venting or personal grievances. Remain objective and constructive.
*   **Pitfall:** Neglecting to save personal records. Once you lose access to your company email, you lose access to performance reviews, pay stubs, and historical tax documents. Ensure you have copies of these prior to your final day.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Should I reveal my new employer during the exit interview?**
You are generally not obligated to disclose where you are going. If you feel comfortable and the relationship is strong, you can share; otherwise, a simple "I’m pursuing an opportunity that aligns with my long-term career goals" is sufficient.

**2. Is it necessary to create a handover document even if I feel I wasn't supported?**
Yes. Your handover document is a reflection of your professionalism. By documenting your work thoroughly, you protect your own reputation and ensure your legacy at the company remains positive.

**3. What if I don't have a replacement to hand over to?**
If a successor has not been hired, address your handover documentation to your direct supervisor. Structure the file so it can be easily parsed by whoever eventually steps into the role.

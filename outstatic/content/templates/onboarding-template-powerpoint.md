# Standard Operating Procedure: Employee Onboarding PowerPoint Template Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized process for creating, maintaining, and deploying the corporate Onboarding PowerPoint template. The objective of this document is to ensure that all new hires receive a consistent, brand-aligned, and high-quality introduction to the organization, regardless of the department or facilitator. Adherence to this protocol minimizes instructional inconsistency, upholds corporate identity, and ensures that critical organizational information is disseminated effectively during the first week of employment.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Template Configuration
*   Verify that you are using the most current version of the "Master Onboarding Template" from the shared drive (never use a previous employee’s saved deck).
*   Ensure all embedded media (videos, audio clips) are hosted on a secure internal cloud server to prevent broken links.
*   Check that the company logo and color palette conform to the latest Brand Guidelines manual.
*   Update the "Welcome Date" and "Cohort Identifier" on the title slide to avoid confusion.

## Phase 2: Content Customization and Verification
*   Audit the "Company Mission, Vision, and Values" section to ensure zero deviations from the current strategic plan.
*   Review the "Organizational Structure" slide: Confirm all leadership names and titles are accurate as of the current date.
*   Update the "IT/Security Protocols" slide: Ensure password requirements, VPN instructions, and MFA setups reflect the most recent security policies.
*   Verify that all hyperlink destinations (e.g., HR portals, Employee Handbook, Benefit Enrollment sites) are functional.
*   Remove any placeholder text or "copy-paste" errors from department-specific sub-slides.

## Phase 3: Final Quality Assurance and Distribution
*   Perform a "Spell Check" and "Grammar Audit" across the entire presentation.
*   Run the "Accessibility Checker" (File > Info > Check for Issues) to ensure screen readers can interpret slides correctly.
*   Export the finalized presentation as a PDF and a PPTX file.
*   Upload the deck to the designated "Onboarding Resources" folder with the naming convention: `YYMMDD_Onboarding_Template_VersionX`.
*   Grant "View Only" permissions to the facilitator to prevent accidental modifications during live sessions.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Use Morph Transitions:** Utilize the "Morph" transition for smooth, professional navigation between organizational structure slides.
*   **The 10/20/30 Rule:** Keep text density low. Aim for no more than 10 lines of text per slide to ensure the facilitator remains the focus, not the screen.
*   **Placeholder Imagery:** Use high-resolution, internal photography of your office space to increase personal connection compared to generic stock photos.

### Pitfalls
*   **Version Control Failure:** Never edit the "Master Template" directly. Always save a copy as a new file before beginning modifications.
*   **Content Overload:** Avoid the "Wall of Text" syndrome. Onboarding is overwhelming; provide supplementary handouts rather than putting every HR policy detail on a slide.
*   **Technical Dependency:** Always have a hard-copy backup of your speaking notes and a local copy of the presentation saved on a USB drive in case of cloud synchronization failures.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should the Master Template be audited?**
A: The template should undergo a comprehensive audit quarterly to ensure alignment with HR policy shifts, leadership changes, and branding updates.

**Q: Should I include department-specific slides in the Master Template?**
A: No. The Master Template should contain core company information only. Department-specific content should be managed as a supplemental slide deck that is appended to the Master.

**Q: What is the recommended file size for the presentation?**
A: To prevent performance lag during screen sharing, keep the file size under 50MB. Compress all images (File > Compress Pictures) before final distribution.

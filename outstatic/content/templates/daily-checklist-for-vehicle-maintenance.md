# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Vehicle Maintenance Inspection

Effective vehicle maintenance is the cornerstone of operational efficiency, safety, and longevity for any fleet. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory daily inspection requirements for all company vehicles. By adhering to this checklist, drivers and maintenance staff ensure that all units remain compliant with safety regulations, minimize the risk of mechanical failure during transit, and uphold the company’s reputation for reliability. Each operator is required to complete this inspection at the start of every shift before the vehicle leaves the designated staging area.

## 1. Exterior & Safety Systems
*   **Lights:** Verify operation of headlights (high/low beam), turn signals, brake lights, reverse lights, and hazard flashers.
*   **Tires:** Inspect for visible debris, sidewall bulges, or excessive wear. Check tire pressure (visual inspection for "squat" or use a pressure gauge if required).
*   **Glass & Mirrors:** Ensure windshield and all mirrors are clean, free of cracks or chips that obstruct visibility, and properly adjusted.
*   **Body Integrity:** Check for new dents, scratches, or missing trim pieces. Ensure all doors and latches open and close securely.
*   **Emergency Equipment:** Confirm presence and condition of a fire extinguisher (proper gauge pressure), reflective triangles/flares, and first aid kit.

## 2. Under-the-Hood & Fluids
*   **Engine Oil:** Check the dipstick to ensure oil levels are within the safe operating range and verify that the oil is not milky (indicating coolant contamination).
*   **Coolant:** Inspect the overflow reservoir for appropriate levels. Never open a hot radiator cap.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Check levels against the reservoir markings.
*   **Washer Fluid:** Top off the reservoir to ensure clear visibility during inclement weather.
*   **Belts & Hoses:** Visually scan for fraying, cracking, or signs of leakage around connections.

## 3. Interior & Controls
*   **Dashboard Gauges:** Turn the ignition to the "On" position and check for warning lights (Check Engine, ABS, Oil Pressure). Ensure all gauges register normal readings upon startup.
*   **Braking System:** Ensure the pedal feels firm and does not sink to the floor. Test the parking brake engagement.
*   **Steering:** Check for excessive "play" or unusual noise when rotating the steering wheel.
*   **Cabin Cleanliness:** Remove trash and debris to maintain a professional environment and prevent pedal interference.
*   **Seatbelts:** Verify that the locking mechanism functions properly and webbing is not frayed.

## 4. Documentation & Compliance
*   **Registration & Insurance:** Ensure current documentation is present in the glove box or assigned folio.
*   **Fuel Level:** Verify the vehicle has sufficient fuel for the scheduled route; refuel if the level is below the corporate threshold (e.g., 1/4 tank).
*   **Logbook:** Record the mileage and sign off on the completion of the daily inspection in the vehicle logbook.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Perform your walk-around in a consistent pattern (e.g., clockwise) every single day. This helps you build muscle memory, ensuring you never skip a side of the vehicle.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use your smartphone to take photos of any pre-existing damage upon taking possession of the vehicle to protect yourself from liability.
*   **Pitfall (Complacency):** The "pencil-whipping" trap. Never sign off on the checklist without physically touching or visually verifying each point. Mechanical failures often hide in plain sight.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Sounds):** If a vehicle is making a new sound—even if the dashboard lights are clear—report it immediately. Small noises often precede catastrophic failures.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if I find a defect during the daily inspection?**
A: Do not operate the vehicle if the defect affects safety (e.g., faulty brakes, broken lights). Tag the vehicle "Out of Service," notify the Fleet Manager immediately, and document the specific defect in the vehicle logbook.

**Q: How long should this daily inspection take?**
A: A diligent, professional inspection should take approximately 5 to 7 minutes. Efficiency is important, but never at the expense of thoroughness.

**Q: Why is it necessary to check fluid levels when there are no leaks on the ground?**
A: Internal leaks (such as head gasket failures or burning oil) can deplete fluid levels without leaving a trace on the ground. Regular checks are the only way to catch internal mechanical degradation before it results in a total engine breakdown.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Operation and Maintenance of Hot Air Oven

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the safe and efficient operation of the Hot Air Oven. Designed for laboratory and industrial sterilization or drying processes, this document ensures consistency in thermal treatment while prioritizing user safety and equipment longevity. All personnel must review this document thoroughly before operating the unit to prevent thermal injury, sample degradation, or mechanical failure.

## 1. Pre-Operational Inspection
*   **Verify Cleanliness:** Ensure the interior chamber is free of debris, spills, or residual materials from previous runs.
*   **Inspect Power Source:** Confirm the power cord is undamaged and securely plugged into a grounded, dedicated electrical outlet.
*   **Check Ventilation:** Ensure the exhaust vents are unobstructed and the unit maintains at least 6 inches of clearance from surrounding equipment or walls.
*   **Review Parameters:** Confirm the required temperature and duration for the specific sample or material being processed.

## 2. Loading the Oven
*   **Sample Arrangement:** Place items centrally on the wire shelves. Do not overcrowd; ensure adequate space between items to allow for uniform convective airflow.
*   **Material Compatibility:** Verify that all items placed inside are heat-resistant. Never place plastics, volatile solvents, or flammable materials in the oven.
*   **Safety Constraints:** Use appropriate heat-resistant gloves when handling trays or heavy glassware.
*   **Seal Integrity:** Ensure the door gasket is free of obstructions and the door closes firmly to maintain a hermetic seal.

## 3. Operation Protocol
*   **Power On:** Switch the main power to "ON" and verify the temperature controller display illuminates.
*   **Temperature Setting:** Input the target temperature using the digital controller interface. Allow the oven to reach the set-point before inserting temperature-sensitive materials if necessary.
*   **Timer Setting:** Engage the timer (if equipped) for the required duration. Note that the timer should only commence once the internal temperature has stabilized at the set-point.
*   **Monitoring:** Periodically check the analog thermometer (if present) against the digital display to ensure calibration accuracy.

## 4. Post-Operational Procedure
*   **Cool Down:** Once the cycle completes, switch off the heat source. Allow the oven to cool to a safe handling temperature (typically below 50°C) before opening the door.
*   **Unloading:** Use thermal-resistant protective equipment to remove samples. 
*   **Final Inspection:** Ensure the unit is powered down and the interior is inspected for any glass breakage or residue.
*   **Log Entry:** Record the cycle details, including start/stop times, temperature, and user initials, in the Equipment Use Logbook.

## 5. Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Airflow):** Always keep the air vents partially open during the first few minutes of drying if the sample contains moisture; this prevents internal pressure buildup and helps remove evaporated vapor.
*   **Pro Tip (Calibration):** Schedule semi-annual temperature mapping using a calibrated external probe to account for "cold spots" within the oven chamber.
*   **Pitfall (Thermal Shock):** Avoid placing cold glass directly onto a hot metal shelf; the thermal shock can cause immediate shattering. 
*   **Pitfall (Overloading):** Never cover the bottom of the chamber with aluminum foil or trays. This disrupts the vertical air circulation and will lead to uneven heating or damage to the heating elements.

## 6. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I use the hot air oven to dry volatile or flammable liquids?**
A: Absolutely not. Hot air ovens are not explosion-proof. Heating volatile solvents poses a severe risk of fire or explosion. Use a vacuum oven or specialized solvent-rated equipment for such tasks.

**Q: Why does the oven display a different temperature than my samples?**
A: The digital display reflects the sensor's reading (usually near the air inlet). Depending on the load density, the center of your sample may take longer to reach the set temperature. Always allow for a "soak time" once the oven reaches the target temperature.

**Q: How often should the oven be cleaned?**
A: A light inspection and wipe-down of the interior should occur after every use. A deep clean (including shelf removal and vent inspection) should be performed monthly, or sooner if spills occur.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Precision Weighing Balance Operation

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the operation, maintenance, and verification of precision weighing balances in a controlled environment. Adherence to these procedures is critical to ensure measurement accuracy, minimize mechanical wear, and maintain compliance with Good Laboratory Practice (GLP) standards. All personnel must complete a documented training session on this specific instrument model before attempting operation.

## Pre-Operation Preparation and Environmental Checks

* Ensure the balance is situated on a vibration-dampened, stable, and level workbench, away from high-traffic zones.
* Verify the spirit level indicator; the air bubble must be perfectly centered within the ring.
* Confirm that the draft shield and weighing pan are clean, free of dust, debris, or chemical residues.
* Ensure the ambient temperature and humidity are within the manufacturer’s specified operating range.
* Verify that the instrument is connected to a dedicated power source and has undergone the required warm-up period (typically 30–60 minutes) as per the manual.

## Instrument Initialization and Calibration

* Power on the balance and allow the internal electronics to stabilize.
* Perform an internal calibration (if the instrument is equipped with an automated calibration function) to ensure accuracy against factory-set standards.
* If internal calibration is unavailable, use an external certified class-calibrated weight to perform a verification check.
* Record the "As-Found" weight value in the instrument logbook prior to any adjustments.
* Zero the balance (tare) to ensure the display reads exactly 0.0000g (or the specific resolution of the unit) before sample placement.

## Sample Weighing Procedure

* Use clean, dedicated weighing boats, glassine paper, or volumetric glassware appropriate for the sample state.
* Always handle samples with appropriate tools (e.g., spatulas or forceps); never touch the weighing pan or sample container with bare hands to prevent heat transfer and oil contamination.
* Place the sample container in the exact center of the weighing pan to minimize corner-load errors.
* Close all draft shield doors immediately after placement to stabilize internal airflow.
* Allow the weight reading to stabilize—indicated by the stability icon on the digital display—before recording the final value.
* Remove the sample and clean the weighing area immediately to prevent cross-contamination.

## Post-Operation Maintenance and Documentation

* Carefully clean the weighing pan and surrounding area using a lint-free cloth and an approved solvent (e.g., 70% isopropyl alcohol).
* Ensure no sample spillage has entered the balance mechanism or the draft shield tracks.
* Log the measurement data, including time, date, sample ID, and operator initials, in the formal balance usage log.
* Switch the balance to standby mode if it will not be used for an extended period, or leave powered on according to departmental "Always-On" policy.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

* **The Static Trap:** Static electricity is the most common cause of "drifting" weights. If the numbers fluctuate uncontrollably, use an anti-static ionizing blower or wipe the weighing container with an anti-static cloth.
* **Thermal Equilibrium:** Never weigh samples that are significantly hotter or colder than the ambient room temperature. This creates air currents that cause buoyancy errors, leading to false weight readings.
* **Draft Shield Discipline:** The most accurate readings are obtained by working in a draft-free environment. Even the slight movement of a person walking past the bench can disrupt a high-precision balance.
* **Overloading:** Never exceed the maximum capacity of the balance. Even a momentary overload can permanently damage the load cell and void the calibration.

## FAQ

**Q: How often should the balance be calibrated?**
A: Balances should be checked daily before the first use. A full calibration check should be performed after any major movement of the device, after a power outage, or according to the frequency defined in your specific Quality Management System (QMS).

**Q: Why is my reading drifting steadily in one direction?**
A: Drift is typically caused by temperature fluctuations, evaporation of a volatile sample, or static electricity. Check for open windows, air conditioning vents blowing directly on the unit, or ensure the weighing container is completely sealed.

**Q: Can I use a damp cloth to clean the weighing pan?**
A: Use only the manufacturer-recommended cleaning agents. Avoid dripping liquids directly onto the balance; instead, dampen a lint-free wipe away from the balance and then use it to clean the components to prevent internal electronic short-circuiting.

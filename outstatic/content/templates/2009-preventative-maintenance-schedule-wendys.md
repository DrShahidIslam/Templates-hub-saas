# Standard Operating Procedure: 2009 Wendy’s Preventative Maintenance (PM)

This document outlines the standardized Preventative Maintenance (PM) protocol for Wendy’s restaurant operations, designed to extend the lifecycle of kitchen equipment, ensure food safety compliance, and maintain peak operational efficiency. Adhering to this schedule minimizes emergency repair costs and prevents service bottlenecks during peak hours. All maintenance tasks must be performed by certified personnel or trained management staff utilizing approved safety gear and documented with verified logs.

## Daily Maintenance Checklist

*   **Grill & Griddle:** Scrape and clean griddle plates; verify heat zones; check grease trough clearance.
*   **Fryers:** Filter shortening (Boil-out if required by oil quality monitoring); wipe down exterior surfaces and drainboards.
*   **Sandwich Prep Unit:** Check gaskets for cracks; ensure internal temperature remains at or below 41°F (5°C); clean condenser intake.
*   **Soda Fountain/Dispenser:** Sanitize nozzles and diffusers; check CO2 levels and syrup bag connections.
*   **Floors & Drains:** Clean floor drains; scrub high-traffic areas; inspect for standing water.

## Weekly Maintenance Checklist

*   **Refrigeration/Freezer Units:** Vacuum condenser coils to remove dust/grease build-up; inspect door seals for air leaks.
*   **Ice Machine:** Clean and sanitize the ice bin and internal evaporator plate per manufacturer specs; replace water filter if flow rate has diminished.
*   **Exhaust Hoods:** Remove and degrease baffle filters; wipe down hood canopy surfaces to prevent fire hazards.
*   **Waste Management:** Deep clean exterior dumpster area; verify functionality of compactor or bin locking mechanisms.

## Monthly Maintenance Checklist

*   **HVAC Systems:** Replace intake air filters; check for unusual motor vibration; clear condensate lines.
*   **Plumbing:** Inspect all faucets and toilets for leaks; check water pressure; ensure grease traps are clear of excess buildup.
*   **Lighting:** Replace burnt-out bulbs in dining and kitchen areas; inspect illuminated menu boards for flickering or outages.
*   **Electrical:** Inspect all power cords for fraying or damage; check GFI outlets for proper tripping function.

## Quarterly/Semi-Annual Maintenance Checklist

*   **Fire Suppression System:** Coordinate with a licensed third-party contractor to inspect chemical suppression nozzles and pull stations.
*   **Equipment Calibration:** Calibrate all thermometers and thermostats to ensure food safety standards; verify conveyor oven timing and temperature.
*   **Structural Review:** Inspect exterior signage, parking lot lighting, and drive-thru communication speakers.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The Logbook):** Always maintain a physical or digital logbook. If an inspector or regional manager asks for proof of maintenance, a documented history is your strongest asset.
*   **Pro Tip (Temperature Logs):** Link PMs to temperature logging. If a freezer consistently fluctuates, perform a condenser coil inspection immediately, regardless of the schedule.
*   **Pitfall (Chemical Misuse):** Avoid using harsh abrasives on stainless steel, as they remove the protective chromium oxide layer, leading to permanent rust. Use only food-safe, non-corrosive degreasers.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Gaskets):** A torn door gasket is the #1 cause of compressor failure in refrigeration. If you see light through a closed seal, replace the gasket immediately.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is the most critical item to maintain for food safety?**
The refrigeration and freezer units are paramount. If temperatures fluctuate, product loss and foodborne illness risks increase significantly. Daily monitoring and weekly coil cleaning are non-negotiable.

**2. How do I know when a part needs professional repair versus basic maintenance?**
Basic maintenance involves cleaning, lubricating, and tightening. If a piece of equipment requires internal mechanical disassembly, produces burning smells, or shows signs of electrical arching, immediately pull the unit from service and call a certified technician.

**3. Who is responsible for documenting these checks?**
The General Manager is ultimately responsible, but the responsibility should be delegated to the Shift Lead or the "Maintenance Lead" of the store. Documentation must be signed, dated, and kept on-site for at least 12 months.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Audit Form UTSA (University of Texas at San Antonio)

This document outlines the standardized procedure for managing, completing, and filing the Audit Form UTSA. As a critical component of institutional compliance, this audit ensures that all administrative, financial, and departmental operations align with University of Texas System regulations and UTSA internal policy. Failure to maintain rigorous adherence to these protocols may result in audit findings, budget delays, or non-compliance penalties. Adherence to this SOP is mandatory for all personnel responsible for data entry, review, and submission.

## Phase 1: Pre-Audit Preparation and Documentation
*   Identify the specific audit scope (e.g., procurement, P-card, travel reimbursement, or internal departmental inventory).
*   Download the most recent version of the Audit Form UTSA directly from the official UTSA Financial Affairs or Compliance portal—do not use cached or local copies.
*   Gather all supporting documentation (receipts, approval emails, purchase orders, and justification memos) and organize them chronologically.
*   Verify that all transaction dates fall within the required fiscal period for the current audit cycle.

## Phase 2: Data Entry and Form Completion
*   Input departmental information including Cost Center, Fund, and Project ID numbers accurately.
*   Complete the "Auditor/Preparer Information" section, ensuring contact details are current for follow-up inquiries.
*   Input line-item details, ensuring the description matches the corresponding ledger entry in the UTSA financial system (e.g., DEFINE or FIS).
*   Cross-reference all dollar amounts against official bank statements or departmental expenditure reports to ensure zero variance.
*   Attach digital copies of all receipts and invoices to the submission portal as instructed by the specific department’s guidelines.

## Phase 3: Review and Quality Assurance
*   Conduct a self-audit of the completed form: verify that every field is filled and that no "N/A" fields are left blank if data is available.
*   Cross-verify signatures: ensure all required approvers (PIs, Department Heads, or Budget Managers) have provided electronic signatures via the approved UTSA authentication method.
*   Review for "red flags," such as split-purchasing patterns or unauthorized vendors, and include explanatory memos if necessary.
*   Submit the finalized document via the secure file transfer protocol designated by the Internal Audit office.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "Shadow File." Keep a digital folder containing all original source documents used for the audit form for at least three years, as state auditors may request verification long after the initial submission.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Comments" section liberally. If a transaction appears unusual, providing a brief, professional justification saves time by preventing follow-up questions from auditors.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "Version Control Errors." Many staff members use older templates stored on their desktops. Always navigate to the official UTSA website to ensure you are using the latest version of the form.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not redact sensitive information (like full credit card numbers) unless explicitly instructed by the UTSA Data Privacy Office, as this can invalidate the audit form.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Where can I find the latest version of the Audit Form UTSA?**
A: Always access the form via the UTSA Financial Affairs website under the "Forms and Resources" tab to ensure you are using the most current, compliant template.

**Q: What should I do if I discover a discrepancy during the audit process?**
A: Do not attempt to hide the discrepancy. Document the finding clearly in the "Notes" section of the audit form and attach a brief explanation detailing how the error occurred and what corrective steps are being taken to prevent recurrence.

**Q: Who should be the final signatory for my departmental audit form?**
A: The final signature must be the Departmental Budget Manager or the primary Principal Investigator (PI) responsible for the funding source being audited. Delegated signatures are generally not accepted unless documented via an official Delegation of Authority form on file with the university.

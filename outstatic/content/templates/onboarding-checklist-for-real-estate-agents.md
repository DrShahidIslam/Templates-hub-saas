# Standard Operating Procedure: Real Estate Agent Onboarding

This document outlines the standardized onboarding procedure for new real estate agents joining our brokerage. The objective of this process is to ensure that every agent—regardless of experience level—is fully equipped with the tools, knowledge, and compliance documentation required to represent our firm effectively from Day 1. Consistent onboarding reduces administrative friction, minimizes liability, and accelerates the time-to-first-closing for new hires.

## Phase 1: Administrative & Legal Compliance
*Complete these items prior to the agent's first day to ensure they are legally authorized to operate under the brokerage license.*

*   [ ] **Independent Contractor Agreement (ICA):** Obtain a signed copy of the broker-agent agreement.
*   [ ] **License Verification:** Confirm active status with the State Real Estate Commission.
*   [ ] **E&O Insurance:** Ensure the agent is added to the firm’s Errors & Omissions insurance policy.
*   [ ] **MLS Access:** Submit paperwork to the local Multiple Listing Service to transfer the agent’s license and secure login credentials.
*   [ ] **Association Membership:** Verify local/state/national REALTOR® association dues are paid and membership is transferred.
*   [ ] **W-9/Tax Forms:** Collect completed tax documentation for payroll/commission processing.

## Phase 2: Technical & Systems Integration
*Standardize the agent’s digital workflow to ensure they are connected to firm resources.*

*   [ ] **Email & Cloud Access:** Provision corporate email (G-Suite/Outlook) and cloud storage access (Dropbox/Google Drive).
*   [ ] **CRM Setup:** Create account in the company CRM and ensure lead routing rules are configured.
*   [ ] **Transaction Management Software:** Provision access to platforms like Dotloop, SkySlope, or DocuSign.
*   [ ] **Intranet/Portal Access:** Provide login for internal resources, marketing templates, and company policies.
*   [ ] **Company Directory:** Add agent to the team roster, internal Slack/Teams channels, and emergency contact list.

## Phase 3: Brand & Marketing Onboarding
*Ensure the agent is positioned to represent the brokerage brand professionally.*

*   [ ] **Professional Headshot:** Schedule a photography session or collect a high-resolution, branded headshot.
*   [ ] **Online Profiles:** Update the brokerage website’s "Meet the Team" page with the new agent’s bio.
*   [ ] **Marketing Collateral:** Provide the agent with templates for business cards, listing presentations, and "Just Listed/Sold" social media graphics.
*   [ ] **Social Media Guidelines:** Review the firm’s social media policy and provide branded covers/assets for their professional profiles.

## Phase 4: Operations & Training
*Facilitate a smooth transition into the company culture and operational cadence.*

*   [ ] **Broker/Manager Welcome Meeting:** Discuss production goals, expectations, and commission structures.
*   [ ] **Contract Training:** Conduct a mandatory walkthrough of the firm’s preferred purchase agreements and disclosure forms.
*   [ ] **Safety Protocol:** Review office safety procedures, physical security, and lone-agent safety protocols.
*   [ ] **Shadowing Program:** Assign a mentor or schedule three "desk duty" sessions for the agent to observe senior staff interactions.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Create a "Welcome Kit" that includes high-quality branded signage, a lockbox, a professional folder, and a company-branded notebook. This builds immediate loyalty.
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate the technical setup. Use a checklist template (like Trello or Asana) so the IT department and the Admin team aren't duplicating efforts.
*   **Pitfall:** Overloading the agent on Day 1. Split training into "Operations," "Contracts," and "Marketing" sessions over the first week rather than one long, exhausting orientation.
*   **Pitfall:** Neglecting culture. Real estate can be isolating. Ensure the new agent is invited to the first upcoming team meeting or lunch immediately upon arrival.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long should the official onboarding process take?**
A: A comprehensive onboarding process should span the agent's first two weeks, with the first three days focused on "getting to work" (tech and admin) and the remaining time focused on training and mentorship.

**Q: Should I treat experienced agents the same as newly licensed agents?**
A: While administrative tasks remain the same, you should skip the basic contract/procedure training for high-producing veterans and replace it with a "Brokerage Workflow Integration" session to show them how our specific tech stack differs from their previous brokerage.

**Q: Who is responsible for tracking the completion of these tasks?**
A: The Operations Manager or the Lead Office Administrator should serve as the "Onboarding Lead." They are responsible for checking off items and ensuring no liability-heavy steps (like MLS transfer) are skipped.

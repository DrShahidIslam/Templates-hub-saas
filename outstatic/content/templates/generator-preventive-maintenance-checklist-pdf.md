# Standard Operating Procedure: Generator Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for conducting routine preventive maintenance on industrial and commercial standby generator sets. Consistent maintenance is essential to ensure operational reliability, extend equipment lifespan, and guarantee compliance with safety regulations. This procedure should be performed by qualified personnel or certified technicians according to the manufacturer’s specific service intervals (typically monthly, quarterly, or annually).

## 1. Safety and Pre-Maintenance Protocols
*   Ensure the generator is in "OFF" mode or set to "Maintenance Mode" at the control panel.
*   Engage the emergency stop (E-Stop) button.
*   Disconnect the starting battery cables (negative first) to prevent accidental startup.
*   Verify that the engine and exhaust system have cooled to ambient temperature.
*   Wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including safety glasses, chemical-resistant gloves, and hearing protection.

## 2. Engine Systems Maintenance
*   **Lubrication System:** Check oil levels; inspect for leaks at the oil filter, drain plug, and gasket seals. Replace oil and filter based on manufacturer-recommended run hours.
*   **Cooling System:** Inspect coolant levels in the radiator/expansion tank. Check all radiator hoses for signs of cracking, bulging, or dry rot. Inspect the drive belt for tension and wear.
*   **Fuel System:** Inspect fuel lines and fittings for leaks. Drain moisture/sediment from the fuel-water separator. Ensure the fuel tank level is at full capacity.
*   **Air Intake:** Inspect the air filter element. Replace if dirty or if service interval is reached. Check air intake piping for obstructions or loose connections.

## 3. Electrical and Control Systems
*   **Battery Maintenance:** Clean battery terminals and cable ends with a wire brush. Check for corrosion; apply terminal protectant. Verify battery voltage and electrolyte levels (if applicable).
*   **Control Panel:** Inspect for any error codes or fault lights. Perform a "Lamp Test" to ensure all indicators are functional.
*   **Connections:** Visually inspect all wiring harnesses for signs of heat damage, chafing, or loose terminations. Ensure the battery charger is providing the correct float voltage.

## 4. Operational Testing (Post-Maintenance)
*   Reconnect battery cables (positive first).
*   Reset the control panel to "Auto" or "Manual" mode.
*   Perform a test run under load (if the transfer switch allows) to verify the generator reaches rated frequency and voltage.
*   Monitor exhaust color and engine temperature during the run; listen for abnormal vibrations or mechanical knocking.
*   Verify that the Automatic Transfer Switch (ATS) correctly transitions the load upon engine stabilization.

## 5. Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Wet Stacking" Trap:** Never run a diesel generator at no-load or light-load for extended periods. This causes unburned fuel to accumulate in the exhaust system. Ensure the unit is load-bank tested annually.
*   **Document Everything:** Maintain a digital or physical logbook. If an issue is noted during inspection but not repaired immediately, it must be flagged for the next service cycle.
*   **Fuel Quality:** Diesel fuel degrades over time. Use a fuel stabilizer if the generator is used infrequently, and schedule periodic fuel polishing to remove particulate matter and water.
*   **Don't Over-Tighten:** When replacing filters or drain plugs, use a torque wrench per manufacturer specifications to prevent stripped threads or seal failure.

## 6. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform a preventive maintenance inspection?**
A: Standard industrial practice dictates a monthly visual inspection, with full-service maintenance performed every 6 months or 250–500 operational hours, whichever comes first.

**Q: Why is my battery failing even though the generator is rarely used?**
A: Batteries suffer from "parasitic drain" while sitting. Ensure your battery charger is constantly active and performing a float charge; also, test the battery load capacity every 6 months.

**Q: What do I do if I find a leak during maintenance?**
A: Immediately tag the unit as "Out of Service." Determine the source of the leak, clean the area, and replace the faulty seal or hose. Do not attempt to operate the generator until the leak is fully repaired and the area is degreased.

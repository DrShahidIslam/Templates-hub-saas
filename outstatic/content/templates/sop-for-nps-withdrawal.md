# Standard Operating Procedure: National Pension System (NPS) Withdrawal

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols and administrative requirements for initiating and executing an NPS withdrawal request. As an Operations Manager, it is critical to ensure that all subscribers adhere to PFRDA guidelines to minimize processing delays and avoid tax liabilities. This document serves as the definitive guide for internal operations teams and subscribers to facilitate a compliant, error-free exit from the NPS scheme.

## Phase 1: Pre-Withdrawal Prerequisites
Before initiating the formal request, ensure all KYC and personal data are current to prevent rejection by the Central Recordkeeping Agency (CRA).

*   Verify that the NPS PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) is active and not currently suspended.
*   Ensure the registered mobile number and email ID are active for OTP-based authentication.
*   Confirm that the bank account linked to the PRAN is active and capable of receiving inward NEFT/RTGS credits.
*   Ensure that the current KYC details (Address/ID proof) match the records held by the Point of Presence (PoP) or CRA.

## Phase 2: Submission Process
Follow these steps to submit the withdrawal application via the CRA portal (eNPS).

*   **Log in:** Access the official CRA portal using your PRAN and password.
*   **Navigate:** Go to the ‘Transact Online’ tab and select ‘Withdrawal from NPS’.
*   **Select Exit Type:** Choose the appropriate withdrawal category (Superannuation, Premature Exit, or Deferment).
*   **Data Verification:** Cross-check the auto-populated personal details, including bank account particulars and nominee information.
*   **Annuity Selection:** Select an Annuity Service Provider (ASP) and an annuity scheme from the available options.
*   **Document Upload:** Upload the required self-attested documents (PAN card, bank statement/cancelled cheque, PRAN card copy).
*   **Authentication:** Complete the process using Aadhaar-based e-Sign or by printing and physically submitting the form to your associated Nodal Office.

## Phase 3: Post-Submission & Disbursement
Tracking the status post-submission is vital for ensuring the funds reach the designated accounts.

*   **Acknowledgement:** Save the 'Acknowledge Receipt' generated upon successful e-Sign.
*   **Status Monitoring:** Track the status of the request via the 'View Withdrawal Status' menu on the portal.
*   **Fund Settlement:** Monitor the bank account for the lump-sum credit, which usually occurs within T+3 to T+5 working days post-verification.
*   **Annuity Issuance:** Ensure the ASP receives the communication from the CRA to initiate the monthly pension disbursement.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Always initiate the withdrawal request at least 30 days before the intended date of superannuation to allow for administrative buffers.
*   **Pro Tip:** If you choose the e-Sign route, ensure the mobile number linked to your Aadhaar is the same as the one used for the NPS portal.
*   **Pitfall:** Entering incorrect bank IFSC codes is the leading cause of failed transactions. Always cross-verify the code on your passbook or cheque leaf.
*   **Pitfall:** Failing to update the nominee details before withdrawal can lead to significant legal hurdles if the subscriber passes away during the processing period.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use a joint bank account; PFRDA mandates that the bank account must be in the name of the subscriber only.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Is the lump-sum amount withdrawn from NPS taxable?**
As per current income tax laws, the lump-sum withdrawal of up to 60% of the total corpus is exempt from income tax for individual subscribers. However, the annuity amount received periodically is taxable as per the subscriber's applicable income tax slab.

**2. Can I defer my withdrawal if I don't need the money at 60?**
Yes, subscribers have the option to defer their lump-sum withdrawal and/or the annuity purchase up to the age of 75. You must submit the deferment request before your 60th birthday (or the date of superannuation).

**3. What happens if my withdrawal request is rejected?**
If a request is rejected due to data mismatch or document issues, the CRA will notify you via email/SMS. You must rectify the specific discrepancy (e.g., updating bank KYC) and resubmit the request on the portal. There is no penalty for rejection, but it causes significant administrative delays.

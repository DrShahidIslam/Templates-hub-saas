# Standard Operating Procedure: Monthly Billing Process Flow

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized workflow for the end-to-end billing cycle. The objective of this process is to ensure revenue accuracy, maintain compliance with contractual terms, optimize cash flow, and reduce Days Sales Outstanding (DSO). By adhering to this structured approach, the finance department ensures that all billable services are captured, validated against service level agreements (SLAs), and invoiced in a timely manner, thereby fostering transparency and maintaining client satisfaction.

## Phase 1: Data Gathering and Reconciliation
*   **Generate Service Usage Logs:** Pull raw data from CRM/ERP systems to identify billable services, hourly logs, or product shipments completed during the billing cycle.
*   **Validate Work Orders:** Cross-reference usage logs against approved work orders or service tickets to ensure all work is authorized.
*   **Apply Contractual Adjustments:** Verify client-specific discount structures, tiered pricing, or retainer balances to ensure the preliminary invoice reflects the correct commercial agreement.
*   **Internal Peer Review:** Perform a preliminary audit to identify any missing documentation or discrepancies between the service delivery logs and the client contract.

## Phase 2: Invoice Creation and QA
*   **Draft Invoice Generation:** Populate the draft invoices within the accounting software based on the reconciled usage data.
*   **Accuracy Check:** Compare the draft invoice total against the previous month’s invoice (unless contract terms dictate otherwise) to identify and explain any anomalous variances.
*   **Tax Compliance Verification:** Ensure correct sales tax, VAT, or withholding tax is applied based on the client’s billing address and local jurisdictional requirements.
*   **Final Management Approval:** Submit the invoice batch to the Finance Manager for final verification and sign-off prior to the distribution cutoff date.

## Phase 3: Distribution and Documentation
*   **Batch Delivery:** Dispatch invoices via the preferred client delivery method (e.g., secure portal upload, automated email, or physical mail).
*   **Verify Receipt:** Confirm that invoices have been received by the client’s Accounts Payable department, ideally through automated read-receipts or portal delivery logs.
*   **System Update:** Mark the status of the billable items as "Invoiced" in the accounting system to prevent revenue leakage or duplicate billing.
*   **Archiving:** Store a copy of the final invoice, supporting documentation, and approval logs in the central document management system for audit trail purposes.

## Phase 4: Follow-up and Dispute Resolution
*   **Aging Report Monitoring:** Run an Aged Receivables report 5 days prior to the net-due date to identify accounts nearing delinquency.
*   **Proactive Reminders:** Send polite follow-up emails to clients with invoices nearing the due date to ensure they have the necessary documentation for processing.
*   **Dispute Investigation:** If a client contests an invoice, initiate the "Dispute Log" immediately; pause the payment clock on the contested item until the root cause is identified and resolved via a credit memo or explanation.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The "Gap" Pitfall:** Avoid the "forgotten work order" trap by implementing a weekly (rather than monthly) review of service tickets. 
*   **Pro Tip – Automation:** Leverage automated billing triggers in your ERP to eliminate manual data entry. Human error accounts for the majority of billing disputes.
*   **Communication is Key:** If a complex project requires a manual adjustment, notify the client *before* the invoice arrives to prevent payment delays due to "sticker shock" or confusion.
*   **The "Stale Data" Trap:** Never bill based on estimated hours; always reconcile against actual system logs to maintain audit-ready transparency.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if a client refuses to pay due to a service disagreement?**
A: Immediately document the dispute in the ledger, flag the invoice as "Under Review," and escalate to the Account Manager. Do not let the invoice sit in an "Overdue" status without a corresponding note in the CRM.

**Q: How often should the billing SOP be updated?**
A: This SOP should be reviewed annually or whenever there is a significant change to the accounting software, tax regulations, or internal contract management workflows.

**Q: What is the recommended strategy for reducing Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)?**
A: Shift to electronic invoicing with "Pay Now" links, offer early-payment discounts, and maintain a rigorous follow-up cadence starting 48 hours before the invoice due date.

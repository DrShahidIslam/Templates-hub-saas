# Standard Operating Procedure: Comprehensive Vehicle Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory maintenance protocols for all company-operated vehicles. Consistent vehicle maintenance is critical to ensuring operational safety, extending the service life of fleet assets, and minimizing costly emergency repairs. All operators and fleet personnel are required to adhere to these intervals and procedures to maintain compliance with safety standards and manufacturer specifications.

## 1. Engine and Mechanical Systems
*   **Engine Oil:** Check oil levels using the dipstick; ensure the color is translucent/amber. Top off if necessary and log the date of the last oil change.
*   **Coolant System:** Inspect coolant levels in the reservoir. Ensure hoses are free of cracks, leaks, or bulging.
*   **Belts and Hoses:** Visually inspect serpentine belts for fraying, glazing, or missing teeth. Check all rubber hoses for signs of dry rot or seepage.
*   **Air Filter:** Inspect the engine air filter for debris. Replace every 12,000–15,000 miles or if excessive dust is present.

## 2. Tire and Brake Inspection
*   **Tire Pressure:** Measure PSI on all four tires (plus spare) using a calibrated gauge. Adjust to the manufacturer’s recommended pressure found on the driver-side door jamb.
*   **Tread Depth:** Check for uneven wear patterns. Use a tread depth gauge; replace tires if the depth falls below 4/32”.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Verify fluid levels in the master cylinder reservoir.
*   **Brake Pads/Rotors:** Listen for grinding or squealing during operation. Visually inspect pad thickness through wheel spokes; if less than 3mm, schedule immediate service.

## 3. Electrical and Fluid Systems
*   **Battery:** Inspect terminals for white or blue corrosion. Ensure connections are tight and secure.
*   **Lighting:** Test all external lights: headlights (high/low beam), turn signals, hazard lights, brake lights, and reverse lamps.
*   **Windshield Washer Fluid:** Ensure the reservoir is filled with seasonal-appropriate solvent.
*   **Wiper Blades:** Inspect for streaking or rubber separation. Replace every 6 months or if performance is compromised.

## 4. Interior and Safety Equipment
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Confirm no "Check Engine," "TPMS," or "ABS" warning lights are active.
*   **Safety Kit:** Verify the presence of a fire extinguisher (charged), first aid kit, reflective triangles, and a functioning jack with a lug wrench.
*   **Seatbelts:** Ensure all buckles latch securely and belts retract without binding.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "Maintenance Logbook" inside the glove compartment. Tracking dates and mileages religiously increases the resale value of the vehicle and ensures you never miss a service interval.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always check tire pressure while the tires are "cold" (driven less than one mile), as heat expands air and provides inaccurate readings.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "minor" noises. A squeal or a faint vibration is often a precursor to a catastrophic mechanical failure. Investigate odd sounds immediately.
*   **Pitfall:** Overfilling fluids. Adding too much oil or coolant can cause pressure buildup and blow seals, leading to more expensive repairs than the fluid shortage itself.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform a comprehensive maintenance inspection?**
A: A basic safety walk-around should occur before every use. A full mechanical inspection should be performed every 5,000 miles or every 6 months, whichever comes first.

**Q: What should I do if a warning light appears on the dashboard?**
A: If the light is amber, schedule an inspection within 48 hours. If the light is flashing or red, cease vehicle operation immediately and contact fleet management for a tow to avoid permanent engine damage.

**Q: Can I use aftermarket parts for repairs?**
A: All parts used must meet or exceed OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) specifications. Using sub-standard or aftermarket parts may void the manufacturer’s warranty and compromise vehicle safety.

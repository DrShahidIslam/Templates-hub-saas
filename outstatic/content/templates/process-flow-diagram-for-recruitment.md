# Standard Operating Procedure: Recruitment Process Flow Optimization

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized workflow for managing the end-to-end recruitment process within the organization. The objective is to ensure consistency, mitigate bias, and maintain a seamless candidate experience while ensuring that all departmental stakeholders remain aligned throughout the talent acquisition lifecycle. Adherence to this process is mandatory to maintain compliance with labor regulations and internal hiring standards.

## Phase 1: Requisition and Role Definition
*   **Identify Vacancy:** Hiring manager submits a formal "Job Requisition Form" detailing the business case for the role.
*   **Role Profiling:** Recruitment team and hiring manager define core competencies, technical requirements, and key performance indicators (KPIs).
*   **Compensation Benchmarking:** HR validates the salary range against internal equity and external market data.
*   **Approval Workflow:** Secure digital sign-off from Finance and Department Head.

## Phase 2: Sourcing and Attraction
*   **Job Description (JD) Creation:** Draft a gender-neutral, inclusive JD highlighting culture and benefits.
*   **Channel Strategy:** Determine posting strategy (Job boards, LinkedIn, niche industry forums, or internal referral programs).
*   **Sourcing Execution:** Launch outbound sourcing efforts (talent mapping and passive candidate outreach).
*   **Initial Filtering:** Recruiters conduct preliminary resume screening against the defined "Must-Haves."

## Phase 3: Evaluation and Assessment
*   **Initial Screening:** Conduct 30-minute phone or video calls to assess cultural fit and salary expectations.
*   **Functional Assessment:** Administer role-specific skills tests (coding challenges, case studies, or writing samples).
*   **Structured Interviewing:** Conduct deep-dive technical or behavioral interviews using standardized evaluation scorecards.
*   **Stakeholder Debrief:** Gather feedback from all interviewers to finalize a consensus candidate ranking.

## Phase 4: Selection and Onboarding
*   **Reference & Background Checks:** Verify employment history, educational credentials, and professional references.
*   **Offer Negotiation:** Extend the formal offer letter, ensuring all benefits and contingencies are clearly outlined.
*   **Closing:** Coordinate the candidate's acceptance and facilitate the resignation process if required.
*   **Onboarding Handoff:** Transfer all candidate data to the People Operations team to trigger the onboarding workflow.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) to automate status updates; candidate ghosting is the #1 cause of poor employer branding.
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement "blind screening" techniques (removing names/photos) during the initial resume review to reduce unconscious bias.
*   **Pitfall (Bottlenecking):** Failing to set a "Service Level Agreement" (SLA) for hiring manager feedback, which often leads to losing top-tier talent to competitors.
*   **Pitfall (Inconsistency):** Failing to use structured scorecards leads to subjective, "gut-feeling" hiring decisions rather than objective assessment of skills.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do we handle candidates who do not meet all criteria but show high potential?**
A: Utilize an "Emerging Talent" flag in the ATS. These candidates should be moved to a nurturing pipeline for future junior roles rather than immediate rejection.

**Q: What is the recommended turnaround time for interview feedback?**
A: Hiring managers are expected to provide feedback within 24 hours of an interview to maintain momentum and ensure the candidate feels prioritized.

**Q: How should we address salary expectations that exceed our budget?**
A: Focus the conversation on total rewards (equity, flexible work, professional development) and ensure the hiring manager is consulted before any offer extension to avoid scope creep.

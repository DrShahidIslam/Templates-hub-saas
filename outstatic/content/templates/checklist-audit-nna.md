# Standard Operating Procedure: NNA Compliance Audit

The Notary Public/Notarization Audit (NNA) is a critical quality control measure designed to ensure that all notarized documents adhere to state-specific regulations, internal company compliance standards, and industry best practices. This SOP provides a comprehensive framework for performing a thorough audit of Notarized Notary Acts (NNA), minimizing legal liability, and ensuring the integrity of the closing or signing process.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Documentation Review
*   Verify all notarized pages correspond exactly to the package index.
*   Confirm the presence of the signer's government-issued ID (check expiration dates and physical security features).
*   Ensure the Certificate of Acknowledgment or Jurat is fully completed with no blank spaces.
*   Verify that the document date matches the date of the notarial act.

## Phase 2: Notarial Seal and Signature Validation
*   Confirm the notary seal is clear, legible, and does not obstruct any text.
*   Ensure the notary signature matches the name registered on the seal.
*   Check that the notary commission expiration date is clearly stated and currently valid.
*   Verify that the "Venue" (State and County) matches the location where the signing occurred.

## Phase 3: Identity and Signer Verification
*   Confirm that the signer’s name on the document matches the identification provided.
*   Validate that the signer’s signature on the document is consistent with their identification signature.
*   Check for any signs of coercion or cognitive impairment observed/noted by the notary.
*   Verify the completeness of the Notary Journal entry (if applicable in the state of practice).

## Phase 4: Final Compliance and Submission
*   Cross-reference the notary’s stamp with the state-maintained database (if applicable).
*   Flag any "non-compliant" findings for immediate remediation with the signing agent.
*   Securely archive audit notes and digitized copies of the documents according to data retention policies.
*   Generate a final audit report for compliance oversight.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a magnifying glass for verifying the raised seal or embossed stamps on ink-signed documents to ensure they are not digital imprints.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always check the "Capacity" statement—ensure the signer is signing in the capacity stated (e.g., as Trustee, as Power of Attorney).
*   **Pitfall (Avoid):** Never assume a document is valid simply because it bears a seal. Many fraudulent or "notary-less" documents use fake stamps.
*   **Pitfall (Avoid):** Do not ignore "white-out" or heavy strike-throughs on certificates; these are red flags that the document may have been tampered with post-notarization.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is the most common error found during an NNA audit?**
The most frequent error is the "incomplete venue," where the state and county lines on the notarial certificate are left blank or incorrectly filled out by the notary.

**2. Should I fail an audit if the notary's seal is slightly smudged but legible?**
Not necessarily. If the information—such as the name, commission number, and expiration date—is legible and verifiable, it is generally considered acceptable, but it should be noted as a "Minor Observation" for quality improvement.

**3. What is the mandatory step if a document is found to be non-compliant?**
You must immediately halt the processing of that document, notify the signing agent to arrange for a re-notarization (if legally permissible), or escalate the issue to the Legal/Compliance Department if fraud is suspected.

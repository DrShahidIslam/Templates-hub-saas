# Standard Operating Procedure: Quality Assurance for Job Descriptions (JDs)

This SOP establishes a rigorous framework for the drafting, auditing, and finalization of Job Descriptions (JDs). The objective is to ensure that every JD accurately reflects organizational requirements, adheres to legal compliance standards, promotes inclusive language, and effectively attracts qualified talent. A high-quality JD serves as the foundational document for performance management, compensation benchmarking, and recruitment success; therefore, adherence to this protocol is mandatory for all HR and hiring stakeholders.

## Phase 1: Structural Audit and Alignment
*   [ ] **Verify Role Title:** Confirm the title is standard, industry-recognized, and devoid of gendered or exclusionary language (e.g., using "Server" instead of "Waitress").
*   [ ] **Validate Job Purpose:** Ensure a 2–3 sentence summary clearly defines why the position exists and its impact on the organization.
*   [ ] **Standardize Formatting:** Apply current corporate branding templates, including correct font, header hierarchy, and bulleted formatting.
*   [ ] **Check Compensation Banding:** Verify that the listed salary range aligns with current compensation studies and internal equity benchmarks.

## Phase 2: Content Accuracy and Compliance
*   [ ] **Essential Duties Audit:** Confirm that core responsibilities are actionable, measurable, and reflect current operational needs rather than legacy requirements.
*   [ ] **Qualifications Review:** Distinguish clearly between "Required" (non-negotiable) and "Preferred" (bonus) skills to prevent candidate pipeline attrition.
*   [ ] **Legal Disclaimer Check:** Ensure all current EEO (Equal Employment Opportunity) and ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) language is included and up-to-date.
*   [ ] **Physical/Environmental Requirements:** Explicitly document any physical demands (e.g., lifting weight, travel requirements) to ensure transparency and legal safety.

## Phase 3: Linguistic and Bias Assessment
*   [ ] **Tone Analysis:** Utilize bias-detection tools to scan for overly masculine or feminine-coded language.
*   [ ] **Plain Language Review:** Ensure technical jargon is defined or avoided to make the JD accessible to a broad, diverse applicant pool.
*   [ ] **Engagement Scan:** Assess the JD for "selling points"—does the description highlight the team culture, growth opportunities, and mission alignment?

## Phase 4: Final Approval Workflow
*   [ ] **Manager Review:** Obtain signed-off confirmation from the hiring manager on technical accuracy.
*   [ ] **Compensation/HR Sign-off:** Final review by the Compensation lead to ensure budgetary and grade alignment.
*   [ ] **Version Control:** Assign a unique document version number and save the final draft to the centralized repository.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **The "Rule of Five":** Try to limit "Required" qualifications to five key items. Overloading this section discourages highly qualified candidates—particularly women and underrepresented groups—from applying if they don't meet every single bullet point.
*   **Focus on Outcomes:** Instead of just listing tasks, describe the *desired outcome* (e.g., instead of "Manage social media," use "Increase organic social media engagement by 20% year-over-year").
*   **Use the "Day in the Life" lens:** Before finalizing, ask: "If I were the candidate, would I know exactly what success looks like in this role?"

### Pitfalls
*   **Copy-Paste Syndrome:** Avoid recycling old JDs. Roles evolve quickly; using a 2-year-old template often results in misaligned expectations and poor-quality hires.
*   **Unclear Language:** Using vague terms like "strong communicator" without context leads to ambiguity. Define what that means in your specific work environment.
*   **Ignoring Accessibility:** Failing to consider how the JD displays on mobile devices or screen readers for visually impaired candidates.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should we review existing JDs?**
We recommend an annual audit of all active JDs, or immediately upon the departure of an incumbent, to ensure the document evolves alongside business objectives.

**2. What should I do if a hiring manager demands "Required" qualifications that are unrealistic?**
Provide the manager with market data showing how such strict requirements significantly shrink the talent pool. Suggest moving the non-critical skills to the "Preferred" category to maximize candidate volume.

**3. Why is bias-correction in JDs a priority?**
Inclusive language is not just an ethical imperative; it is a business strategy. Studies consistently show that gender-neutral JDs increase the volume of qualified applications by 40–50%, leading to a more diverse and competitive workforce.

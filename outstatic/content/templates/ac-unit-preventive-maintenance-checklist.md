# Standard Operating Procedure: HVAC Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory preventive maintenance procedures for commercial and residential air conditioning units. The objective of this protocol is to ensure system longevity, optimize energy efficiency, maintain indoor air quality, and prevent costly emergency repairs. All procedures must be performed by qualified personnel using appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) and following local safety regulations.

## Phase 1: Pre-Maintenance Preparation and Safety
*   Verify the unit is powered down at the disconnect box before starting.
*   Ensure all necessary tools are staged: Multimeter, coil cleaner, fin comb, vacuum, leak detector, and replacement filters.
*   Perform a visual site inspection to identify signs of damage, unusual vibration, or excessive noise.
*   Check refrigerant levels and document initial suction/discharge pressures.

## Phase 2: Outdoor Condenser Unit
*   **Clear Debris:** Remove leaves, branches, and yard waste within a 2-foot radius of the unit.
*   **Clean Condenser Coils:** Use a specialized coil cleaner and low-pressure water to remove grime, dust, and pollen; ensure drainage paths are clear.
*   **Inspect Fins:** Carefully straighten bent cooling fins with a fin comb to ensure unrestricted airflow.
*   **Fan Motor & Blade:** Inspect fan blades for cracks or imbalances; verify motor mount tightness.
*   **Electrical Inspection:** Inspect contactors for pitting/arcing; tighten all high-voltage and low-voltage electrical connections.

## Phase 3: Indoor Air Handler and Evaporator
*   **Filter Replacement:** Replace disposable filters or deep-clean reusable filters; verify correct airflow orientation.
*   **Evaporator Coil Inspection:** Check for dust or mold buildup; clean if necessary (Note: If the system is sealed, use a visual scope or monitor pressure drops).
*   **Blower Assembly:** Clean blower blades and ensure the motor is properly lubricated (if required).
*   **Drainage System:** Flush condensate drain lines with an algaecide or nitrogen to prevent clogs and biological growth. Ensure the secondary drain pan is dry.
*   **Ductwork Check:** Perform a visual inspection of accessible ducting for signs of leaks, moisture, or disconnected joints.

## Phase 4: System Functionality and Testing
*   **Start-Up:** Restore power and monitor the start-up cycle.
*   **Electrical Check:** Measure amperage draw on the compressor and fan motors to ensure they are within the manufacturer’s RLA (Rated Load Amps) specifications.
*   **Performance Metrics:** Record supply and return air temperatures to calculate the "Delta T" (temperature split).
*   **Thermostat Calibration:** Verify thermostat settings correspond to real-time system output.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always take "Before and After" photos of coils and filters to include in the maintenance report for client transparency.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use an infrared thermometer to spot-check electrical connections for "hot spots" before touching them.
*   **Pitfall:** Never use a high-pressure power washer on condenser coils, as it will flatten the delicate aluminum fins and severely restrict airflow.
*   **Pitfall:** Overlooking the condensate drain is the #1 cause of water damage claims in HVAC maintenance; never skip the flush.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should preventive maintenance be performed?**
For residential systems, maintenance should be performed at least twice a year (spring for cooling, autumn for heating). Commercial units with high traffic should be serviced quarterly.

**2. Why is a "Delta T" reading important?**
The Delta T (the temperature difference between return air and supply air) acts as a primary indicator of system health. A reading too low or too high typically points to airflow issues, refrigerant leaks, or coil blockage.

**3. Should I lubricate the blower motor during every visit?**
Not necessarily. Many modern blower motors are "permanently lubricated." Check the unit’s service manual; applying oil to a sealed motor can lead to premature failure.

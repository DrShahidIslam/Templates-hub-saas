# Standard Operating Procedure: Quality Attribute Process Flow Visualization

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized methodology for designing, finalizing, and distributing high-quality Process Flow Charts (PFCs) focused on Quality Attributes (QAs). The objective is to translate complex technical requirements into clear, visual documentation suitable for both PowerPoint presentations and PDF distribution. This ensures that cross-functional teams, auditors, and stakeholders have a unified understanding of critical quality checkpoints within the operational lifecycle.

## Phase 1: Data Gathering and Scoping
*   **Identify Scope:** Define the specific process boundaries (start point to end point).
*   **List Quality Attributes:** Extract Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs) from the Product Specification or Quality Manual.
*   **Stakeholder Consultation:** Verify the process flow with Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) to ensure current state accuracy.
*   **Define Legend:** Standardize symbols (e.g., Rectangles for process steps, Diamonds for decision nodes, Ovals for start/end).

## Phase 2: Design and Visualization
*   **Tool Selection:** Utilize professional diagramming software (Lucidchart, Microsoft Visio, or PowerPoint/Draw.io).
*   **Flow Construction:** Map the sequence chronologically, integrating QA checkpoints directly into the relevant process steps.
*   **Visual Hierarchy:** Use color-coding to highlight high-risk quality attributes (e.g., Red for critical, Yellow for monitoring).
*   **Formatting:** Ensure consistent font sizes, alignment, and white space to prevent cognitive overload for the reader.

## Phase 3: Review and Distribution
*   **Technical Accuracy Audit:** Have a Quality Assurance lead verify that the flow chart aligns with current regulatory/internal requirements.
*   **Accessibility Check:** Ensure all text is legible when scaled for presentation slides.
*   **File Conversion:**
    *   **PPT:** Embed high-resolution images or vector graphics to ensure scalability.
    *   **PDF:** Export using "High-Quality Print" settings to maintain detail.
*   **Final Approval:** Obtain digital sign-off from the Department Head before formal release to the document control system.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use "Swimlanes" if the process involves multiple departments; it clarifies who is responsible for which Quality Attribute.
*   **Pro Tip:** Include a Revision History table on the first slide or page to ensure users are referencing the latest version.
*   **Pitfall (Complexity):** Avoid "spaghetti charts." If a process is too large, break it into a high-level summary flow with links to detailed sub-process maps.
*   **Pitfall (Static Data):** Never use hard-coded text if process variables are subject to change; use placeholders or linked Excel data if possible.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Should I put the flow chart in the PPT slide or just link to the PDF?**
A: It is best practice to include a summarized version on the PPT slide for presentation purposes and provide a link to the full-scale, high-resolution PDF for deep-dive technical reviews.

**Q: What is the ideal scale for these diagrams?**
A: If presenting live, prioritize readability over detail. If the diagram is for an SOP manual or audit documentation, prioritize technical precision, even if it requires a larger page size (e.g., 11x17).

**Q: How often should these process flows be updated?**
A: They should be reviewed whenever there is a formal change control process, a CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) related to the process, or at least annually to ensure alignment with current operations.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Comprehensive Garment Industry Audit

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory audit framework for evaluating manufacturing facilities within the garment industry. The objective is to ensure full compliance with international labor standards, quality assurance protocols, occupational health and safety (OHS) requirements, and environmental sustainability mandates. Adherence to this SOP mitigates supply chain risk, protects brand reputation, and guarantees product consistency.

## 1. Pre-Audit Preparation and Documentation Review
*   Verify all business licenses, export permits, and local operating certifications.
*   Review payroll records, time-attendance logs, and proof of age for all employees (to prevent child/forced labor).
*   Examine internal quality control (QC) logs and previous audit Corrective Action Plans (CAPs).
*   Confirm the presence of a valid grievance mechanism and whistleblower policy.

## 2. Occupational Health and Safety (OHS)
*   Ensure all sewing machines are equipped with needle guards and belt covers.
*   Verify that fire exits are clearly marked, unblocked, and equipped with panic bars.
*   Check that fire extinguishers are inspected, pressurized, and have clear signage.
*   Confirm that personal protective equipment (PPE) is available and being used (e.g., masks for cutting, gloves for chemical handling).
*   Inspect electrical panels for exposed wiring and ensure grounding is properly maintained.
*   Verify that chemical storage areas (dyes, solvents) are ventilated and labeled with Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS).

## 3. Production and Quality Assurance (QA)
*   Evaluate the raw material inspection process (fabric roll shading, defects).
*   Verify the existence of inline inspection tables and "traffic light" systems for defect monitoring.
*   Check the calibration records for sewing machines, heat presses, and needle detectors.
*   Review final inspection (AQL 2.5/4.0 standard) protocols and packing integrity.
*   Inspect storage conditions for finished goods to prevent moisture, mold, or pest contamination.

## 4. Labor Practices and Human Rights
*   Cross-reference clock-in/clock-out records with shift logs to ensure no unauthorized overtime.
*   Verify that the legal minimum wage is being paid and that overtime is voluntary and compensated at premium rates.
*   Interview a random sample of employees (in private) regarding harassment, verbal abuse, or freedom of association.
*   Ensure sanitation facilities (restrooms) are clean, gender-segregated, and accessible.

## 5. Environmental and Social Compliance
*   Inspect wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) functionality and effluent test reports.
*   Verify waste segregation practices (hazardous vs. non-hazardous).
*   Assess energy consumption logs and evidence of carbon footprint reduction initiatives.
*   Confirm local community engagement policies are documented.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always conduct "unannounced" audits. Scheduled audits allow management to "clean up" the floor, which hides systemic non-compliance.
*   **Pro Tip:** Focus on the "shadow production" risk. Ensure the facility isn't outsourcing work to undocumented, non-compliant subcontractors.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring the "soft" data. If employees are fearful or reluctant to speak during interviews, it is a high-probability indicator of deeper cultural management issues.
*   **Pitfall:** Neglecting fire safety in dormitory areas. Many facilities focus on the factory floor but fail to maintain fire safety in housing provided for migrant workers.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should a garment factory audit be conducted?**
A: High-risk suppliers should be audited bi-annually (every 6 months), while established, high-performing suppliers may move to an annual audit cycle with quarterly "spot checks."

**Q: What is the most common cause of audit failure?**
A: Inconsistent record-keeping. Even if a factory is doing the right thing, the inability to provide documentation (like overtime records or chemical disposal logs) results in an automatic audit failure.

**Q: What should be the immediate reaction to a major non-compliance finding?**
A: Categorize the finding as "Critical." Issue an immediate "Stop Work" order for the specific process involved, create a 24-hour Corrective Action Plan, and escalate to your procurement and sustainability leadership teams.

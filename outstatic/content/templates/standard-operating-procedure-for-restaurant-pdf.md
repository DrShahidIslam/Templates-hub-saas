# Standard Operating Procedure: Restaurant Operations Management

This document serves as the comprehensive framework for maintaining operational excellence within our restaurant. By standardizing our processes, we ensure consistency in food quality, guest service, and safety compliance. This SOP is designed to guide management and staff through the essential daily cycles, from the pre-shift preparation to the final closing procedures, ensuring a seamless experience for every guest while maintaining strict adherence to health and safety regulations.

## 1. Pre-Opening Preparation (Front-of-House)
*   **Facility Inspection:** Perform a perimeter walkthrough, checking for cleanliness, lighting functionality, and exterior signage integrity.
*   **Sanitation Setup:** Ensure all service stations, POS terminals, and dining tables are sanitized and organized according to the floor plan.
*   **Staff Briefing (Line-up):** Conduct a 10-minute briefing to review the daily special, 86’d items, VIP reservations, and performance goals.
*   **Supply Readiness:** Verify that silverware, linens, condiments, and menus are restocked to par levels.

## 2. Kitchen Operations & Food Safety
*   **Temperature Logging:** Record the temperatures of all refrigeration units and hot-holding equipment at the start of the shift.
*   **Prep Verification:** Inspect all food prep stations to ensure items are labeled, dated, and stored using the FIFO (First-In, First-Out) method.
*   **HACCP Compliance:** Monitor critical control points during cooking, cooling, and reheating processes to prevent foodborne illness.
*   **Station Reset:** Ensure all cutting boards are sanitized and non-essential prep items are properly stored between service rushes.

## 3. Service Standards & Guest Experience
*   **The Greeting:** Acknowledge every guest within 30 seconds of entry with a professional, welcoming tone.
*   **Order Accuracy:** Repeat back all orders to the guest to ensure accuracy, specifically checking for allergies or dietary restrictions.
*   **Table Maintenance:** Conduct "silent service"—proactively clearing finished plates and refilling beverages without interrupting guest conversation.
*   **Conflict Resolution:** Utilize the LATTE method (Listen, Acknowledge, Take action, Thank, Ensure satisfaction) for any guest complaints.

## 4. Closing & End-of-Day Reconciliation
*   **Cash Management:** Conduct a blind drop for all servers, verify the register drawer count, and secure all cash in the office safe.
*   **Cleaning Deep-Dive:** Execute the closing cleaning schedule, ensuring high-traffic areas, grease traps, and kitchen ventilation filters are addressed.
*   **Equipment Shutdown:** Properly power down all kitchen equipment and lock all exterior storage areas.
*   **Manager Log:** Complete the daily shift report detailing labor costs, waste, and any incidents that occurred during the shift.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a "Clean as You Go" policy. This reduces the end-of-shift workload by 30% and keeps the workspace significantly safer throughout service.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always designate a "Safety Lead" during shifts. This person is responsible for being the final set of eyes on equipment shut-offs and security locks.
*   **Pitfall:** Never ignore "small" maintenance issues (e.g., a dripping faucet or flickering light). These often lead to larger, costlier emergency repairs if left unaddressed.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-staffing during slow periods is a common profit killer. Adjust labor based on real-time forecasting, not just static scheduling.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should the SOP be updated?**
A: We recommend a formal review of this SOP every six months. However, specific operational adjustments—such as menu changes or new POS software—should trigger an immediate update to the relevant sections.

**Q: What is the most important metric to track daily?**
A: While labor and food costs are vital, "Table Turnover Time" and "Average Check Size" provide the most immediate insight into how efficiently your front-of-house team is maximizing revenue during a shift.

**Q: Where should the physical or digital copies of this SOP be stored?**
A: Digital versions should be accessible via a cloud drive available to all managers. Physical copies (laminated) should be kept in the Manager’s Office and the Kitchen Prep Station for quick reference.

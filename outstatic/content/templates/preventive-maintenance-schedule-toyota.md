# Standard Operating Procedure: Toyota Preventive Maintenance Schedule

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized protocol for performing routine preventive maintenance on Toyota vehicles. Adhering to this schedule is critical to ensuring vehicle longevity, maximizing fuel efficiency, maintaining safety standards, and preserving factory warranty compliance. This procedure is designed for fleet managers and automotive technicians to ensure consistency, accountability, and optimal mechanical performance across the entire Toyota fleet.

## Phase 1: Fluid & Filtration Systems
*   **Engine Oil and Filter:** Drain and replace engine oil using manufacturer-specified viscosity (e.g., 0W-20 or 5W-30). Replace oil filter with Genuine Toyota Parts to ensure bypass valve compatibility.
*   **Engine Air Filter:** Inspect for debris or blockage. Replace if saturated or if vehicle operates in dusty environments.
*   **Coolant System:** Check coolant levels and test freezing point/pH levels. Inspect hoses for dry rot, swelling, or leaks.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Check reservoir levels. Test for moisture content (copper strips or digital tester); flush system if fluid exceeds 2% water content.

## Phase 2: Drivetrain & Undercarriage Inspection
*   **Transmission/Transaxle:** Check for signs of weeping or active leaks. If equipped with a dipstick, inspect fluid color and smell (burnt odor indicates potential internal wear).
*   **Driveshaft and CV Boots:** Inspect CV axle boots for tears, grease leakage, or cracking. Ensure driveshaft support bearings are secure.
*   **Differential/Transfer Case:** Inspect gear oil levels and check for metal shavings or seal degradation.
*   **Exhaust System:** Examine hangers, clamps, and catalytic converter shields for rattles or corrosion.

## Phase 3: Braking, Suspension, and Steering
*   **Brake Pads/Rotors:** Measure pad thickness (mm). Inspect rotors for scoring, heat spotting, or uneven wear patterns.
*   **Suspension Components:** Check struts/shocks for oil weeping. Inspect control arm bushings for dry rot and tie-rod ends for excessive lateral play.
*   **Steering Rack:** Inspect rack and pinion boots for fluid leaks or tears.
*   **Tires:** Rotate tires to ensure even tread wear. Verify tread depth (minimum 4/32” recommended) and adjust air pressure to the specific door-jamb PSI placard.

## Phase 4: Electronic and Interior Diagnostics
*   **Battery Health:** Perform a load test on the 12V battery. Clean terminals and apply anti-corrosion spray.
*   **Lighting Check:** Verify high/low beams, turn signals, brake lights, and reverse lights.
*   **Cabin Air Filter:** Inspect and replace if air flow is restricted or if odors are present.
*   **Dashboard Warnings:** Use an OBD-II scanner to clear historical codes and confirm zero active Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always utilize the Toyota "Techstream" software for deep-dive diagnostics; generic OBD-II scanners may miss manufacturer-specific body control module (BCM) issues.
*   **Pro Tip:** Mark the date and mileage of the service clearly on the inside of the windshield with a sticker to improve driver accountability.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not over-tighten oil drain plugs. Toyota oil pans are prone to stripped threads; always use a new crush washer and a torque wrench set to factory specifications.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring the "Maintenance Required" light. This is not just an oil change indicator; it is a mileage-based trigger for comprehensive safety inspections.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does using non-Toyota branded parts void my warranty?**
A: Under the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, you are not required to use OEM parts. However, the parts must meet or exceed OEM specifications. Using subpar aftermarket filters or fluids that fail may lead to denied warranty claims if the failure is linked to those parts.

**Q: How often should I deviate from the 5,000-mile interval?**
A: You should move to a 3,000-mile interval if the vehicle is classified as "Severe Service." This includes frequent short-trip driving (under 5 miles), heavy idling, towing, or operation in extremely dusty or mountainous terrain.

**Q: Why does my TPMS light stay on after I adjust tire pressure?**
A: Many Toyota models require a manual reset of the Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) via a button located under the steering column or through the Multi-Information Display (MID) settings after pressure is corrected. Ensure the spare tire is also checked, as some models have sensors in all five tires.

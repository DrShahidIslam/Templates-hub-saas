# Standard Operating Procedure: Accounts Payable (AP) Process

## Introduction
The Accounts Payable (AP) process is a critical financial function designed to ensure that all company liabilities are recorded accurately, authorized appropriately, and settled in a timely manner. This SOP establishes a standardized framework for managing the end-to-end lifecycle of vendor invoices, from receipt to final payment. Adhering to these procedures minimizes the risk of fraudulent activity, eliminates duplicate payments, maintains positive vendor relationships, and ensures the integrity of the company’s general ledger.

## AP Process Step-by-Step Checklist

## Section 1: Invoice Receipt and Verification
*   **Centralized Intake:** Ensure all invoices are submitted to a dedicated, monitored email address (e.g., ap@company.com) or a centralized physical mail location.
*   **Initial Review:** Verify the invoice is addressed to the legal entity, includes a valid Purchase Order (PO) number, and clearly states the vendor’s contact information and payment terms.
*   **Duplicate Check:** Query the accounting system to ensure the invoice number has not been previously processed for the same vendor.
*   **Coding:** Assign appropriate General Ledger (GL) codes and department cost centers to the expense.

## Section 2: Approval and Authorization
*   **Three-Way Matching:** Perform a match between the Invoice, the Purchase Order, and the Receiving Report (Proof of Delivery/Service).
*   **Discrepancy Resolution:** If the invoice amount differs from the PO by more than the allowed variance threshold (e.g., 5%), escalate to the procurement team for clarification before proceeding.
*   **Workflow Routing:** Route the invoice to the designated budget owner for electronic approval via the ERP system.
*   **Delegation of Authority:** Ensure the approver has the authorization limit corresponding to the invoice dollar amount.

## Section 3: Data Entry and Processing
*   **System Input:** Enter the invoice details into the accounting software, ensuring accuracy in date, amount, vendor ID, and payment due date.
*   **Attachment:** Digitally attach the PDF invoice and supporting documentation to the system entry for future auditing purposes.
*   **Scheduling:** Set the payment date according to the vendor's agreed-upon terms to optimize cash flow management.

## Section 4: Payment Execution
*   **Payment Run:** Generate the weekly/bi-weekly payment batch report for review by the Controller or Finance Manager.
*   **Validation:** Verify that bank account details match the verified vendor master file.
*   **Authorization of Funds:** Obtain final electronic signatures for the payment batch.
*   **Remittance:** Process payments via the approved method (ACH, Wire, or Check) and send automated remittance advice to the vendor.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Leverage Automation.** Utilize OCR (Optical Character Recognition) software to reduce manual data entry and minimize human error.
*   **Pro Tip: Early Payment Discounts.** Flag vendors offering "2/10 Net 30" terms to prioritize these payments, effectively lowering your cost of goods sold.
*   **Pitfall: Vendor Master File Decay.** Failing to periodically audit the vendor master file is a major security risk. Regularly deactivate vendors who have not been used in over 12 months to prevent unauthorized payments.
*   **Pitfall: "Rubber Stamping."** Never allow invoice approvals without supporting documentation. Always require a valid PO or a written justification for non-PO expenses to maintain internal controls.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What should I do if an invoice is missing a PO number?**
Hold the invoice and contact the department head who requested the goods or services. Do not process the payment until a valid PO is created or an exception is formally approved by a manager.

**2. How often should we reconcile vendor statements?**
Vendor statements should be reconciled monthly, especially for high-volume suppliers, to catch missing invoices, unapplied credits, or billing discrepancies before they impact your credit standing.

**3. What is the standard procedure for handling disputed invoices?**
Immediately notify the vendor in writing regarding the specific discrepancy. Place the invoice on "Hold" status in the accounting system to prevent accidental payment, and do not remove the hold until the vendor submits a corrected invoice or a credit memo is issued.

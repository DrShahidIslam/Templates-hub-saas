# Standard Operating Procedure: Recruitment and Selection

## Introduction
The objective of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a consistent, equitable, and efficient framework for identifying, attracting, and hiring top-tier talent. This process ensures that all recruitment activities comply with legal requirements, uphold organizational culture, and deliver high-quality candidates who align with long-term business goals. By standardizing these steps, we mitigate bias, reduce time-to-hire, and ensure a seamless experience for both hiring managers and prospective employees.

## Recruitment & Selection Checklist

## Phase 1: Planning and Preparation
*   **Job Analysis:** Conduct a meeting with the hiring manager to define the specific technical requirements, soft skills, and key performance indicators (KPIs) for the role.
*   **Approval Workflow:** Obtain formal budget approval and Headcount Authorization from Finance and HR leadership before proceeding.
*   **Job Description Drafting:** Create a comprehensive job description outlining responsibilities, qualifications, and company benefits.
*   **Compensation Benchmarking:** Analyze market data to set a competitive salary range that aligns with internal equity.

## Phase 2: Sourcing and Attraction
*   **Internal Posting:** Post the vacancy on the internal company portal for a minimum of 3-5 days.
*   **External Posting:** Distribute the job description across relevant professional platforms (LinkedIn, Indeed, industry-specific boards).
*   **Sourcing Strategy:** Engage passive candidates via LinkedIn Recruiter or internal referrals if the role is niche or executive-level.
*   **Screening Criteria:** Define the "Must-Haves" vs. "Nice-to-Haves" to guide initial resume filtering.

## Phase 3: Screening and Interviewing
*   **Initial Resume Screen:** HR performs an initial review of all applicants against pre-defined criteria.
*   **Recruiter Phone Screen:** Conduct a 20-30 minute conversation to verify interest, salary expectations, and basic cultural fit.
*   **Hiring Manager Interview:** Conduct a deep-dive technical interview with the hiring manager.
*   **Structured Panel Interview:** Facilitate a panel interview with cross-functional team members to assess collaboration and cognitive problem-solving.
*   **Reference/Background Checks:** Verify employment history, credentials, and criminal background (as legally required) once a finalist is selected.

## Phase 4: Selection and Offer
*   **Final Candidate Debrief:** Review interview scorecards with the hiring team to make a data-driven hiring decision.
*   **Offer Formulation:** Draft an offer letter including compensation, benefits, equity, and start date.
*   **Verbal Offer:** Conduct a phone call to present the offer, address questions, and gauge acceptance probability.
*   **Contract Execution:** Secure a signed offer letter and initiate the onboarding documentation process.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Use Scorecards:** Always use a standardized scorecard for every interviewer to grade candidates on the same competencies. This reduces subjective bias.
*   **Pro Tip: Diversity Sourcing:** Do not rely solely on job boards. Build pipelines through networking events and diverse talent communities to ensure a wide range of perspectives.
*   **Pitfall: Ghosting Candidates:** Always send a personalized rejection email to candidates who were not selected. A poor candidate experience can damage your employer brand long-term.
*   **Pitfall: Delaying Feedback:** Lengthy gaps between interview stages often result in losing high-quality talent to competitors. Aim to move candidates to the next stage within 48 hours.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How do we ensure legal compliance during the interview process?**
All interviewers must be trained to avoid illegal questions regarding age, religion, marital status, or protected health information. Use a standardized script to ensure every candidate is asked the same core questions.

**2. What should we do if the Hiring Manager insists on hiring a candidate who failed the technical assessment?**
The recruiter should document the risk, hold a formal debrief meeting with the hiring manager, and escalate to HR leadership if the hire poses a significant risk to team performance or company standards.

**3. What is the standard timeline for a successful recruitment cycle?**
While this varies by role seniority, an efficient cycle typically takes 30 to 45 days from the initial job posting to the signed offer. Highly specialized roles may require a longer sourcing phase.

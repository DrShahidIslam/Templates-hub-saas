# Standard Operating Procedure: Biscuit Production & Quality Assurance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized production workflow for biscuit manufacturing. The objective is to ensure consistency in product texture, flavor, and safety while maintaining compliance with food hygiene regulations. All operators must adhere to these steps to minimize waste, prevent cross-contamination, and ensure that every batch meets our stringent quality specifications before moving to the packaging stage.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Ingredient Staging
*   **Sanitization:** Ensure all mixing equipment and contact surfaces have been sanitized according to the Master Cleaning Schedule.
*   **Verification:** Confirm that all raw materials (flour, sugar, fat, leavening agents) are within shelf-life and stored in climate-controlled areas.
*   **Scaling:** Weigh ingredients precisely according to the Master Formulation Sheet. Record batch numbers for full traceability.
*   **Sifting:** Sift dry ingredients to remove any foreign matter or large clumps that could affect dough homogeneity.

## Phase 2: Mixing and Dough Development
*   **Creaming:** Combine fat and sugar; mix until the desired aeration (specific gravity) is achieved.
*   **Hydration:** Gradually add liquid ingredients and leavening agents. Monitor mixer torque to ensure dough consistency is achieved without overworking the gluten.
*   **Temperature Check:** Measure dough temperature; it must remain between 18°C–22°C to ensure optimal rheology for the forming stage.
*   **Dough Resting:** Allow the dough to rest for the designated time to allow for moisture distribution and gluten relaxation.

## Phase 3: Forming and Baking
*   **Sheet/Molding:** Feed the dough into the forming machine. Ensure the thickness (gauge) is consistent across the entire conveyor belt.
*   **Weight Control:** Conduct a check-weighing on 5 raw units every 30 minutes to ensure target weight specifications are met.
*   **Oven Parameters:** Monitor oven zone temperatures (Top/Bottom/Heat Zones) and belt speed. 
*   **Moisture Monitoring:** Perform a mid-bake moisture check to ensure the center of the biscuit is baked through without burning the edges.

## Phase 4: Cooling and Final Quality Control
*   **Controlled Cooling:** Move biscuits through the cooling tunnel. Ensure they reach ambient temperature before packaging to prevent condensation (which leads to spoilage).
*   **Organoleptic Check:** Conduct sensory analysis (texture, snap, color, taste) against the Gold Standard reference sample.
*   **Metal Detection:** Pass all finished product through the calibrated metal detector. Log results in the Critical Control Point (CCP) register.
*   **Packaging:** Inspect seals and ensure date coding is accurate and legible.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a 1:1 ratio between the production schedule and the cleaning schedule. A clean line prevents flavor transfer from spiced biscuits to plain ones.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use an infrared thermometer to monitor oven belt temperature to detect inconsistencies before they affect the batch.
*   **Pitfall - Overmixing:** Excess friction during the mixing stage can heat the fat too much, leading to "oily" biscuits that lose their shape in the oven.
*   **Pitfall - Moisture Migration:** Do not rush the cooling process. If biscuits are packed while still warm, the residual moisture will cause the packaging to fog and the biscuit to lose its "snap."

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the most critical stage for preventing product failure?**
A: The dough temperature and consistency stage. If the dough is improperly mixed or temperature-controlled, no amount of oven adjustment can fix the final texture.

**Q: How often should the metal detector be tested?**
A: The metal detector must be calibrated and tested with standard test pieces (Ferrous, Non-Ferrous, and Stainless Steel) at the start of every shift and every two hours thereafter.

**Q: What should I do if the biscuits arrive at the packaging station too dark?**
A: Immediately decrease the belt speed or adjust the final oven zone temperature. Quarantine the affected batch for further QC inspection before releasing it for sale.

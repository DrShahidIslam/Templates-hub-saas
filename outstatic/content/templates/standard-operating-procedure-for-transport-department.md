# Standard Operating Procedure: Transport Department Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the Transport Department to ensure the safe, efficient, and cost-effective movement of goods and personnel. This document is designed to standardize fleet management, driver conduct, maintenance scheduling, and regulatory compliance. Adherence to these procedures is critical to minimizing downtime, reducing operational costs, and upholding the safety standards of the organization.

## 1. Fleet Maintenance and Readiness
*   **Pre-Trip Inspections:** Drivers must complete a daily digital "Walk-around Checklist" before departing the depot. Any defects (tires, lights, fluid leaks) must be reported via the fleet management software immediately.
*   **Preventative Maintenance (PM) Scheduling:** The Fleet Manager must track odometer readings and schedule servicing (oil changes, brake checks, tire rotations) every 5,000 miles or according to manufacturer guidelines.
*   **Breakdown Protocol:** In the event of a mechanical failure, drivers are to move the vehicle to a safe location, engage hazard lights, deploy warning triangles, and contact the Dispatch Office immediately.
*   **Documentation:** All maintenance logs, repair invoices, and inspection reports must be digitized and archived in the central asset management folder for audit readiness.

## 2. Dispatch and Route Optimization
*   **Load Verification:** Before departure, dispatchers must verify that the vehicle payload does not exceed the legal GVWR (Gross Vehicle Weight Rating) and that all cargo is secured according to safety regulations.
*   **Route Planning:** Utilize integrated GPS software to plan routes, accounting for traffic patterns, bridge clearances, and mandated rest periods.
*   **Communication:** Drivers must check in at pre-defined milestones (Departure, Mid-point, Arrival). Any delays exceeding 30 minutes must be reported to the dispatcher immediately.
*   **Fuel Management:** Vehicles should be refueled at company-approved fuel stations using authorized fuel cards; receipts must be uploaded to the portal within 24 hours.

## 3. Compliance and Safety
*   **Driver Credentialing:** The department must conduct quarterly checks of driver licenses, medical certificates, and insurance statuses to ensure 100% compliance.
*   **Accident Reporting:** In the event of an incident, the driver must secure the scene, contact emergency services if necessary, take photographs of the damage, and submit a formal "Incident Report Form" within 4 hours.
*   **Speed/Telematics Monitoring:** Fleet managers must review weekly telematics reports to identify harsh braking, rapid acceleration, and speeding infractions. Drivers exceeding safety thresholds are subject to mandatory re-training.
*   **Rest Periods:** Strictly enforce Hours of Service (HOS) regulations; dispatchers are prohibited from assigning tasks that require a driver to exceed legal driving time.

## 4. Administrative and Procurement
*   **Asset Lifecycle Management:** Evaluate vehicles every 36 months for replacement vs. repair cost analysis.
*   **Inventory Control:** Maintain a 15-day buffer of essential vehicle consumables (e.g., windshield wiper fluid, oil, basic tool kits).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate your maintenance alerts. Using telematics-integrated software to trigger service appointments based on engine hours or mileage reduces the risk of human error and catastrophic vehicle failure.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "minor" driver complaints. A recurring issue like a slight rattle or dim light is often a precursor to a major breakdown; addressing these early saves significant repair costs.
*   **Pro Tip:** Conduct surprise vehicle inspections once a month to ensure that "walk-around" checklists aren't being "pencil-whipped" (completed without actual inspection).
*   **Pitfall:** Underestimating "hidden" costs. When budgeting, always include a 15% contingency for unscheduled repairs, fluctuating fuel prices, and compliance-related training fees.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How do we handle vehicle repairs when the primary workshop is closed?**
A: We maintain a list of pre-approved 24/7 emergency repair vendors. Drivers are provided with an emergency contact card featuring these vendors; all after-hours repairs must be pre-authorized by the On-Call Dispatcher.

**Q: What is the procedure if a driver loses their license while employed?**
A: Immediate suspension of driving duties is mandatory. The employee must report the status change to HR and the Transport Manager within 24 hours. The employee may be reassigned to non-driving tasks pending license reinstatement, subject to company policy.

**Q: How should fuel card usage be audited?**
A: All fuel card transactions must be reconciled weekly against the vehicle’s fuel efficiency metrics. Any significant anomaly (e.g., fuel purchased for a vehicle not in the area) triggers an automatic internal investigation.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Janitorial and Custodial Services

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional requirements, safety protocols, and execution standards for janitorial services. The objective is to maintain a hygienic, safe, and professional environment through systematic cleaning practices. By following these guidelines, staff will ensure consistency, minimize the spread of pathogens, and extend the lifespan of facility assets. Adherence to these procedures is mandatory for all custodial personnel to ensure operational excellence and compliance with health and safety regulations.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Safety
*   **Don Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Wear gloves, goggles, and non-slip shoes before handling any chemicals.
*   **Chemical Review:** Read the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for all cleaning agents. Never mix chemicals, specifically ammonia and bleach.
*   **Signage:** Place "Wet Floor" caution signs at all entrances to the area being cleaned.
*   **Equipment Inspection:** Check vacuums, buffers, and mops for damage or frayed cords. Ensure all battery-operated devices are fully charged.
*   **Waste Management:** Prepare color-coded trash liners (e.g., clear for recycling, black for waste).

## Phase 2: General Office and Common Areas
*   **Surface Dusting:** Dust high-touch surfaces, including desks, windowsills, file cabinets, and light switches, using microfiber cloths.
*   **Trash Removal:** Empty all waste bins and replace liners. Wipe down the exterior of bins to remove sticky residue.
*   **Floor Maintenance:** Vacuum carpeted areas, ensuring edges and corners are cleaned. Sweep and wet-mop hard flooring using neutral floor cleaner.
*   **Glass and Mirrors:** Clean interior glass partitions and entrance doors to remove fingerprints and smudges.

## Phase 3: Restrooms and Sanitation
*   **Disinfection:** Spray toilets, urinals, and sinks with hospital-grade disinfectant. Allow for the required "dwell time" (as specified by the manufacturer) before wiping.
*   **Fixture Polishing:** Clean and polish faucets, handles, and dispensers.
*   **Replenishment:** Refill soap, paper towels, and toilet tissue dispensers. 
*   **Deep Clean:** Mop floors starting from the furthest corner and working toward the exit. Use a specialized floor disinfectant.

## Phase 4: Final Inspection and Closeout
*   **Quality Check:** Perform a visual sweep of the facility. Look for missed spots, streaks on mirrors, or lingering odors.
*   **Equipment Storage:** Return all chemicals and tools to the secured custodial closet. Ensure chemical bottles are labeled and stored upright.
*   **Reporting:** Log task completion in the daily activity report. Report any maintenance issues (e.g., leaking faucets, burnt-out lights) to facility management.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (The Dwell Time Rule):** Most disinfectants do not kill germs instantly. Always wait 3–10 minutes (per product instructions) before wiping surfaces to ensure microbial destruction.
*   **Pro Tip (Top-to-Bottom Method):** Always clean from the ceiling down to the floor. Dust falling from shelves onto a clean floor is a common rookie mistake.
*   **Pitfall (Cross-Contamination):** Never use the same microfiber cloth for a restroom and an office space. Implement a color-coded rag system to prevent the spread of bacteria.
*   **Pitfall (Excess Water):** Over-wetting floors can cause damage to flooring materials and creates a significant slip-and-fall hazard. Use a "damp-mop" technique.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What should I do if I encounter a chemical spill?**
Immediately refer to the SDS for that specific chemical. If the spill is small, contain it with absorbent materials provided in the spill kit. If the spill is large or poses a respiratory risk, evacuate the area and contact the facility manager immediately.

**2. How often should microfiber cloths be laundered?**
Microfiber cloths should be laundered after every shift. They should never be reused between different client sites or building zones without being sanitized in high-temperature water.

**3. What is the correct procedure for reporting a maintenance issue?**
Document the issue in the end-of-shift log, including the exact location, the nature of the problem, and a photo if possible. Notify the facility supervisor via the designated communication channel to ensure a work order is generated.

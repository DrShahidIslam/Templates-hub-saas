# Standard Operating Procedure: Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) for Mahindra XUV700

The Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) is a critical quality assurance process conducted before a vehicle is officially handed over to the customer. For a sophisticated vehicle like the Mahindra XUV700—which integrates advanced ADAS, dual-screen infotainment, and complex electrical architecture—a rigorous PDI is essential to identify manufacturing defects, transport damage, or software glitches. This SOP ensures that the vehicle meets all factory standards and that the customer receives a flawless product.

## 1. Documentation & Exterior Verification
*   **VIN Matching:** Verify the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) on the chassis plate, engine bay, and windshield against the provided invoice/insurance documents.
*   **Manufacture Date:** Decode the VIN to verify the month and year of manufacture.
*   **Paint Integrity:** Inspect the entire body in natural daylight for dents, scratches, uneven paint, or "orange peel" textures.
*   **Panel Gaps:** Check for uniform gaps between doors, hood, trunk, and fenders.
*   **Glass Inspection:** Ensure all windows and the panoramic sunroof are free from scratches or cracks.
*   **Tires & Alloys:** Check all four tires (plus spare) for uneven wear or cuts. Verify tire manufacturing date codes. Ensure alloy wheels are free from curb rash.

## 2. Interior & Comfort Features
*   **Upholstery:** Inspect leather/fabric for stains, tears, or stitching defects.
*   **Controls:** Test every button and knob, including window switches, ORVM adjustments, and seat adjustments (especially power seats with memory function).
*   **Dashboard & Trim:** Ensure there are no rattling sounds or loose plastic panels.
*   **HVAC:** Test the dual-zone climate control for rapid cooling/heating and airflow distribution.
*   **Infotainment:** Power on the Adrenox system. Check for touch responsiveness, speaker clarity, and reverse camera feed quality.
*   **Sunroof:** Operate the Skyroof fully to ensure smooth movement without jamming or abnormal noises.

## 3. Electrical & ADAS Systems
*   **Lighting:** Test all exterior lights (LED DRLs, Sequential indicators, High/Low beam, Fog lamps, Tail lights, and Reverse lights).
*   **Battery:** Use a multimeter to ensure the battery health is within the specified range (12.4V – 12.8V).
*   **ADAS Suite:** Ensure there are no "System Fault" warning lights on the instrument cluster. Verify Lane Keep Assist and Adaptive Cruise Control standby icons appear on the display.
*   **Connectivity:** Connect your smartphone via Apple CarPlay/Android Auto to ensure seamless integration.

## 4. Under-the-Hood & Mechanical
*   **Fluid Levels:** Verify engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, and windshield washer reservoir levels.
*   **Leaks:** Inspect the engine bay and the area beneath the car for any signs of oil or coolant leakage.
*   **Engine Noise:** Start the engine and listen for irregular knocking, whining, or metallic sounds.
*   **Mileage:** Check the odometer. A reading between 10km and 50km is standard for transit; anything significantly higher requires a valid explanation.

---

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always perform the PDI in an outdoor, well-lit area. Avoid checking the vehicle inside a dimly lit dealership workshop, as minor paint flaws remain hidden in low light.
*   **Pro Tip:** Bring a mobile power bank and a USB cable to test the charging ports and infotainment connectivity thoroughly.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not finalize the registration or insurance until you are personally satisfied with the PDI results. Once the vehicle is registered, the dealership is less likely to resolve minor aesthetic complaints.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring the "Software Update" status. Ask the technician to confirm the latest Adrenox firmware version is installed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I reject a vehicle during PDI?**
A: Yes. If you identify significant mechanical issues, structural paint damage, or extensive electrical faults, you are within your rights to ask for a different vehicle allotment before signing off on the delivery documents.

**Q: Is it normal to have a small amount of water in the exhaust?**
A: Yes, a small amount of condensation is normal, especially in cold weather, as it indicates a healthy combustion process. However, thick smoke or oil residue is a major red flag.

**Q: Should I test drive the car during PDI?**
A: Absolutely. While the yard inspection covers static issues, a test drive allows you to verify the suspension, steering alignment, transmission smoothness, and braking performance. Always ensure the vehicle is insured before taking it for a spin.

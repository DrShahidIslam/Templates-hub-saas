# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance for Rental Properties

## Introduction
The primary objective of a preventive maintenance program is to extend the lifespan of property assets, reduce emergency repair costs, and ensure tenant satisfaction and safety. By adopting a proactive rather than reactive stance, property managers can minimize downtime, maintain higher property valuations, and mitigate liability risks. This SOP serves as a mandatory protocol for conducting semi-annual inspections and routine maintenance cycles for all residential rental units under management.

## Preventive Maintenance Checklist

## 1. Exterior & Building Envelope
*   **Roofing:** Inspect for loose, damaged, or missing shingles/tiles; check flashing around chimneys and vents for signs of oxidation or gaps.
*   **Gutters & Downspouts:** Clear debris; ensure downspouts are securely attached and direct water at least six feet away from the foundation.
*   **Foundation:** Check for new cracks or shifting; ensure soil is graded away from the building to prevent water pooling.
*   **Exterior Siding/Paint:** Inspect for wood rot, peeling paint, or gaps in caulking around window frames and doors.
*   **Hardscaping:** Clear walkways of tripping hazards; check deck/balcony railings for stability and wood integrity.

## 2. HVAC & Energy Systems
*   **HVAC Units:** Change air filters; clean condenser coils of debris; verify thermostat calibration and drain line functionality.
*   **Water Heater:** Check the Temperature and Pressure (T&P) relief valve; flush the tank to remove sediment buildup; inspect for leaks.
*   **Electrical Panel:** Check for signs of burning or excessive heat; ensure all breakers are labeled clearly; test GFCI outlets in kitchens and baths.
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test all units; replace batteries regardless of current charge level; ensure expiration dates are current.

## 3. Plumbing & Interior Fixtures
*   **Faucets & Drains:** Check under all sinks for moisture/leaks; clear hair from shower drains; ensure aerators are free of mineral buildup.
*   **Toilets:** Perform a dye test to check for silent flapper leaks; ensure the base is secure and the wax ring seal is intact.
*   **Appliances:** Clean refrigerator coils; check dryer vents for lint blockages; verify dishwasher seals and garbage disposal functionality.
*   **Windows & Doors:** Check weather stripping for gaps; lubricate hinges and tracks; verify that all locks/deadbolts engage smoothly.

## 4. Safety & Security
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Ensure pressure gauge is in the "green" zone; check that the pin is intact and the unit is easily accessible.
*   **Lighting:** Replace all burnt-out interior and exterior bulbs; test motion-sensor security lighting.
*   **Mold/Mildew:** Inspect high-humidity areas (bathrooms/basements) for signs of moisture or fungal growth.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Digital Documentation:** Always photograph the condition of items before and after maintenance. Store these in a centralized property management software.
*   **Predictive Scheduling:** Use an automated calendar system to trigger maintenance reminders based on seasonal shifts rather than just calendar quarters.
*   **Tenant Cooperation:** Send a "Notice of Entry" at least 24-48 hours in advance (per local laws) and provide tenants with a checklist of items they can address to facilitate the process.

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Ignoring Small Leaks:** A "minor" drip under a sink can lead to subfloor rot, which is 10x more expensive to fix than the plumbing connection.
*   **Over-reliance on Tenants:** Never rely solely on tenants to report issues. Many minor maintenance needs go unnoticed by residents until they become major system failures.
*   **Skipping Documentation:** Failing to document maintenance can lead to liability issues during security deposit disputes or insurance claims.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should preventive maintenance be conducted?**
A: A comprehensive preventive maintenance inspection should occur at least semi-annually (every six months), with HVAC-specific checkups performed seasonally.

**Q: What is the most important item to check on this list?**
A: Water-related systems (HVAC drain lines, toilet seals, and sink plumbing) are the most critical. Water damage is the leading cause of insurance claims in rental properties and can escalate into structural mold issues rapidly.

**Q: Should I outsource these checks or do them in-house?**
A: Basic visual inspections can be handled by an experienced property manager. However, specialized systems like HVAC units and electrical panels should always be inspected by licensed, bonded professionals to ensure safety and code compliance.

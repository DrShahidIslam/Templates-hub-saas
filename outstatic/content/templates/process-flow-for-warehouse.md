# Standard Operating Procedure: Warehouse Process Flow

This document outlines the standardized operational workflow for warehouse management, encompassing the lifecycle of goods from procurement intake to final outbound distribution. Adherence to this SOP ensures optimal inventory accuracy, maximum space utilization, and the maintenance of safety standards across all functional areas. This workflow is designed to minimize bottlenecks, reduce human error, and provide a scalable framework for high-volume logistics operations.

## 1. Inbound Logistics & Receiving
*   **Pre-Arrival Verification:** Cross-reference incoming shipments against Advanced Shipping Notices (ASN) and Purchase Orders (PO).
*   **Offloading:** Direct shipments to the designated receiving dock; inspect packaging for external damage.
*   **Quantity Reconciliation:** Count items against the packing slip; report discrepancies immediately to the procurement department.
*   **Quality Inspection:** Conduct spot checks or full inspections based on SKU-specific quality control protocols.
*   **System Entry:** Update the Warehouse Management System (WMS) to reflect inventory receipt.
*   **Labeling:** Apply system-generated barcode/RFID labels if the manufacturer’s original labeling is insufficient.

## 2. Put-Away & Storage
*   **Staging:** Move accepted goods to the designated staging area before transport to permanent storage.
*   **Slotting:** Utilize the WMS to identify the optimal storage location based on velocity (A/B/C classification), SKU weight, and dimensions.
*   **Storage Execution:** Place inventory in assigned racking or bulk storage areas.
*   **Verification:** Scan the storage bin/rack location and the item barcode to confirm physical placement corresponds with system data.

## 3. Order Processing & Picking
*   **Order Release:** Batch orders in the WMS to prioritize shipping deadlines and geographic proximity.
*   **Pick List Generation:** Issue digital or physical pick lists sorted by efficient aisle navigation paths.
*   **Picking:** Execute picking using RF scanners or "pick-to-light" systems to ensure high accuracy.
*   **Staging for Packing:** Transport picked goods to the outbound packing station.

## 4. Packing & Shipping
*   **Verification:** Re-scan all items to ensure the picked goods match the specific customer order.
*   **Packing:** Use appropriate protective materials; select the smallest carton size to optimize dimensional weight costs.
*   **Labeling:** Apply carrier shipping labels and necessary hazard/handling signage.
*   **Final Manifesting:** Update WMS to "Shipped" status, triggering automated customer notifications.
*   **Load-Out:** Stage packages for carrier pickup or load directly onto outbound transport.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Implement Cycle Counting.** Avoid annual wall-to-wall shutdowns by performing daily rotational counts of specific SKUs to maintain 99%+ inventory accuracy.
*   **Pro Tip: Optimize Aisle Flow.** Ensure high-velocity "A-items" are stored nearest to packing stations to reduce forklift travel time.
*   **Pitfall: Poor Receiving Data.** Failing to capture accurate data at the dock is the "original sin" of warehouse management. If the entry is wrong, every subsequent step (picking, inventory levels, sales) will be compromised.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Ergonomics.** Inefficient workstation layouts lead to worker fatigue and higher injury rates. Invest in height-adjustable packing tables and anti-fatigue mats.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should we audit our warehouse storage locations?**
A: A formal cycle count program should be running daily, ensuring every SKU is counted at least once per quarter, depending on its velocity.

**Q: What is the most common cause of "lost" inventory?**
A: Mis-picks and "phantom inventory" caused by failing to scan items into or out of system locations during the physical move process. 

**Q: How do we handle damaged goods found during the inbound process?**
A: Quarantine damaged goods in a dedicated "Damaged/Hold" area immediately, document the damage with photos, and trigger a vendor return or write-off process in the WMS within 24 hours.

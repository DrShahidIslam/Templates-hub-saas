# Standard Operating Procedure: Labour Room Preparation and Readiness

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for preparing and maintaining a fully equipped, sterile, and responsive labour room. As an operations manager, the objective is to ensure that all critical equipment, medications, and sterile supplies are pre-verified prior to patient admission. Adherence to this checklist minimizes transition time during emergency scenarios and ensures the highest standards of maternal and neonatal safety.

## 1. Environmental Readiness & Sterilization
Before the arrival of a patient, the physical environment must meet stringent infection control standards.

*   Verify that the room has undergone terminal cleaning following the previous delivery.
*   Ensure all waste disposal bins (biohazardous, sharp containers, and general) are emptied and lined with appropriate color-coded bags.
*   Check that ambient room temperature is controlled and optimal for neonatal thermoregulation (approx. 24–26°C).
*   Test all lighting, including overhead surgical lamps and mobile examination lights.
*   Confirm that all surfaces, including delivery tables and side tables, have been wiped with approved hospital-grade disinfectant.

## 2. Maternal Monitoring & Resuscitation Equipment
Operational functionality of diagnostic and life-support equipment is non-negotiable.

*   **Fetal Monitoring:** Calibrate the Cardiotocograph (CTG) machine; ensure sufficient thermal paper is loaded.
*   **Vital Signs:** Test the blood pressure cuff, pulse oximeter, and thermometer for accuracy.
*   **Emergency Trolley (Crash Cart):** Confirm the seal is intact. Verify expiration dates on adrenaline, atropine, and calcium gluconate.
*   **Oxygen Supply:** Verify the vacuum/suction pressure and ensure the oxygen flowmeter is connected to a functional wall outlet.
*   **IV Access:** Stock a variety of IV cannulas (18G–22G) and confirmed IV fluid bags (Normal Saline/Lactated Ringer’s) are staged.

## 3. Delivery Kits & Surgical Supplies
Everything required for a routine or complicated vaginal delivery must be immediately accessible.

*   **Sterile Delivery Pack:** Confirm all packs are sealed, dated, and the sterilization indicators show the correct color change.
*   **Instrument Trays:** Ensure episiotomy scissors, cord clamps, needle holders, and forceps are ready for immediate use.
*   **Suturing Supplies:** Verify availability of absorbable sutures (e.g., Vicryl/Monocryl) and local anesthetics (e.g., Lidocaine).
*   **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Stock sterile gowns, gloves (assorted sizes), face shields, and fluid-resistant aprons at the scrub station.

## 4. Neonatal Resuscitation Station
The neonatal corner must be ready to receive the infant at a moment's notice.

*   **Radiant Warmer:** Switch on and ensure it is pre-heated to the "manual" or "servo" setting.
*   **Suction:** Test the bulb syringe and mechanical suction catheter (various sizes).
*   **Resuscitation Bag:** Ensure the Ambu bag is connected to the oxygen source and the mask seal is intact.
*   **Emergency Meds:** Confirm neonatal-specific dosages of medication are labeled and visible.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The 'Shadow' System):** Always maintain a "shadow" set of sterile gloves and essential tools in an open, non-sterile trolley during active labor. If the sterile pack is compromised, you do not lose time hunting for replacements.
*   **Pro Tip (Checklist Accountability):** Use physical, laminated checklists hung on the back of the door. A staff member must initial the time of check; this creates a culture of accountability.
*   **Pitfall (Expired Supplies):** The most common failure is failing to check expiry dates on specialized medications (e.g., Oxytocin or Magnesium Sulfate). Rotate stock using the First-In-First-Out (FIFO) method.
*   **Pitfall (Hidden Equipment):** Never store equipment under the delivery bed. Keep the floor space clear for rapid movement of emergency trolleys.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should the crash cart be inspected?**
A: The crash cart must be inspected at the beginning of every shift (every 8–12 hours) and immediately after any emergency use.

**Q: What is the most critical item to verify on the neonatal warmer?**
A: The suction functionality is the most critical; if a newborn has an obstructed airway, seconds matter, and a non-functioning suction unit is a primary failure point.

**Q: What should be done if a sterile indicator fails to change color?**
A: Immediately quarantine the entire pack. Do not attempt to use it under any circumstances, as the sterility of the instruments cannot be guaranteed. Replace it with a new, verified pack and report the batch number to the Central Sterile Supply Department (CSSD).

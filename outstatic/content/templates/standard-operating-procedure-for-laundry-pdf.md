# Standard Operating Procedure: Professional Laundry Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional protocols for the collection, sorting, laundering, and distribution of linens and garments. The objective of this procedure is to maintain operational efficiency, preserve textile longevity, and ensure the highest standards of hygiene and presentation. This document serves as the master reference for all staff members involved in laundry operations.

## 1. Collection and Sorting
*   **Safety Gear:** Don appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including gloves and an apron, before handling soiled linens.
*   **Initial Audit:** Inspect items for foreign objects (pens, jewelry, trash) and remove them immediately.
*   **Categorization:** Sort laundry into the following designated bins:
    *   **Whites:** Bed sheets, towels, and white table linens.
    *   **Colors/Darks:** Uniforms, colored fabrics, and non-colorfast items.
    *   **Delicates:** Wool, silk, or heavily embellished items.
    *   **Heavily Soiled:** Items requiring pre-treatment or specialized sanitization.
*   **Stain Assessment:** Identify items requiring pre-treatment and apply approved spotting agents according to manufacturer instructions.

## 2. Machine Loading and Chemical Application
*   **Capacity Management:** Ensure machines are not overloaded; leave at least 6 inches of headspace to allow for proper agitation.
*   **Detergent Dispensing:** Measure chemicals precisely according to the machine’s load capacity. Use only facility-approved, color-safe detergents.
*   **Temperature Selection:** 
    *   **Hot (140°F+):** For white linens and high-sanitization requirements.
    *   **Warm (100°F–120°F):** For standard colored items.
    *   **Cold (below 85°F):** For delicates and to prevent color bleeding.
*   **Cycle Initiation:** Select the cycle corresponding to the fabric type and confirm the door is securely latched.

## 3. Drying and Finishing
*   **Moisture Check:** Upon completion of the wash, shake out items to prevent tangling before transferring to the dryer.
*   **Drying Settings:** Select the appropriate temperature setting. Avoid over-drying, which causes fiber embrittlement.
*   **Prompt Unloading:** Remove items immediately after the cycle ends to prevent permanent wrinkling.
*   **Ironing/Pressing:** Use a professional-grade iron or steam press for linens and uniforms requiring a crisp finish.

## 4. Folding, Storage, and Inventory
*   **Inspection:** Fold only items that are completely dry and free of residual stains.
*   **Standardized Folding:** Utilize uniform folding dimensions to ensure stack stability and aesthetic consistency on storage shelves.
*   **Rotation:** Employ the "First-In, First-Out" (FIFO) inventory method to ensure equal wear across all linens.
*   **Storage:** Keep storage areas clean, dry, and well-ventilated to prevent mildew growth.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always unzip zippers and button buttons before washing to prevent snagging on other fabrics.
*   **Pro Tip:** Add a half-cup of white vinegar to the rinse cycle periodically to neutralize detergent buildup and soften fabrics naturally.
*   **Pitfall (Chemical Overload):** Using too much detergent does not clean better; it leaves residue that attracts dirt and can cause skin irritation.
*   **Pitfall (Neglecting Filters):** Failing to clean the dryer lint trap after every cycle is a significant fire hazard and reduces machine efficiency.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How do I handle items with unidentified stains?**
A: Do not attempt to heat-dry items with unidentified stains, as heat can "set" the stain permanently. Re-wash using a stain remover or send to a professional dry cleaner if the fabric is high-value.

**Q: What is the primary cause of yellowing in white linens?**
A: Yellowing is typically caused by residual detergent buildup, the use of chlorine bleach on synthetic fabrics, or hard water mineral deposits. Ensure proper rinsing and consider a fabric softener alternative.

**Q: Can I mix microfiber cloths with cotton towels?**
A: No. Microfiber attracts lint from cotton towels, which significantly reduces the efficacy and cleaning performance of the microfiber cloths. Always wash them separately.

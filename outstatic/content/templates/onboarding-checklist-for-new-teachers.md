# Standard Operating Procedure: New Teacher Onboarding

Effective onboarding is the cornerstone of staff retention and instructional excellence. This SOP is designed to transition new faculty members into our school community by providing them with the administrative, pedagogical, and cultural tools required to succeed from Day 1. The objective is to reduce operational anxiety and allow the teacher to focus on building student relationships and delivering high-quality instruction. This checklist should be initiated 30 days prior to the start date and completed within the first 60 days of employment.

## Phase 1: Pre-Arrival & Administrative Foundation
*Objective: Ensure all logistical and legal requirements are satisfied before the teacher enters the classroom.*

* [ ] **Contract & Documentation:** Verify completion of background checks, contracts, tax forms, and certifications.
* [ ] **IT Provisioning:** Create email credentials, Learning Management System (LMS) accounts, and student information system access.
* [ ] **Physical Space Preparation:** Ensure the classroom is cleaned, stocked with essential supplies, and equipped with functioning technology.
* [ ] **Welcome Communication:** Send a formal "Welcome Packet" containing the school calendar, staff handbook, and a point-of-contact for immediate questions.
* [ ] **Building Access:** Issue ID badges, keys, or electronic fob access to the building and specific wings.

## Phase 2: Cultural Integration & Compliance
*Objective: Align the new hire with the mission, vision, and core safety procedures of the institution.*

* [ ] **Compliance Training:** Complete mandatory training sessions (e.g., Title IX, child safety protocols, emergency/lockdown procedures).
* [ ] **Staff Orientation:** Conduct a walkthrough of the campus, identifying key locations: main office, staff lounge, supply closet, and IT help desk.
* [ ] **Mentorship Pairing:** Assign a "Buddy Teacher" from the same department to provide informal guidance during the first quarter.
* [ ] **Mission Alignment:** Host an executive briefing on the school’s pedagogical philosophy and community values.

## Phase 3: Instructional & Pedagogical Readiness
*Objective: Prepare the teacher to manage the classroom environment and deliver the established curriculum.*

* [ ] **Curriculum Review:** Provide access to pacing guides, core textbooks, and digital instructional resources.
* [ ] **Classroom Management Training:** Review school-wide disciplinary policies and restorative justice practices.
* [ ] **Attendance & Grading Policy:** Demonstrate how to input grades and attendance accurately in the school database.
* [ ] **Tech Integration Workshop:** Provide a hands-on tutorial for interactive displays, document cameras, and software required for daily instruction.

## Phase 4: The 30-60-90 Day Review
*Objective: Monitor progress and provide continuous support.*

* [ ] **30-Day Check-in:** Meeting with the Department Head to review initial challenges and identify resource gaps.
* [ ] **60-Day Observation:** Conduct a non-evaluative "supportive observation" followed by a feedback meeting.
* [ ] **90-Day Performance Review:** Formal check-in to discuss long-term professional development goals and institutional integration.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
* **Digital Repository:** Create a "New Teacher Dashboard" (a central folder in Google Drive/SharePoint) containing templates for lesson plans, parent emails, and newsletters so they don't have to reinvent the wheel.
* **The "Buddy" System:** Select a mentor who is not the teacher's direct supervisor. This encourages open, honest communication about small struggles that might otherwise go unreported.
* **Over-Communicate:** New teachers feel overwhelmed by acronyms and building-specific jargon. Provide a "Cheat Sheet" of common school abbreviations.

### Pitfalls
* **Information Overload:** Avoid dumping the entire Faculty Handbook on them on Day 1. Release information in "just-in-time" modules as they become relevant.
* **Assuming Prior Knowledge:** Even veteran teachers from other districts need to learn your specific nuances regarding data entry, parent communication protocols, and behavioral expectations.
* **Isolation:** The biggest cause of early burnout is feeling like they are working in a silo. Ensure your communication channels keep them connected to the wider staff.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should the mentor teacher be compensated for their role?**
A: Yes, wherever possible. Providing a stipend or professional development credits for mentorship validates the extra time and effort required to guide a new colleague.

**Q: What if the classroom setup is delayed due to maintenance?**
A: Maintain a "Flex Plan." This should include a dedicated space in the library or a spare office where the teacher can plan and organize materials until their classroom is ready.

**Q: How do we handle onboarding for mid-year hires?**
A: Accelerate the administrative and compliance phases to 1–2 days. Focus the remainder of the time on curriculum pacing and meeting key student stakeholders, as mid-year hires have less time to build rapport.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Illinois Uber Vehicle Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for Uber driver-partners operating in the state of Illinois to successfully complete the mandatory vehicle inspection. In accordance with Illinois state regulations and Uber’s platform requirements, all vehicles must undergo a multi-point safety assessment performed by a certified mechanic to ensure roadworthiness and passenger safety. Failure to provide a valid, signed inspection form will result in account deactivation.

## Step-by-Step Inspection Checklist

### Section 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Documentation:** Ensure you have your current Uber vehicle inspection form printed and ready for the technician.
*   **Cleanliness:** Thoroughly wash the exterior and clean the interior. A clean vehicle helps the inspector identify body damage or chassis issues more effectively.
*   **Maintenance Records:** Keep recent service receipts (oil changes, brake pads) accessible, as some inspectors may request proof of recent upkeep.

### Section 2: Exterior & Visibility
*   **Headlights/Taillights:** Verify that all low beams, high beams, turn signals (front/rear), brake lights, and hazard lights are fully functional.
*   **Windshield:** Inspect for cracks or chips larger than a dollar bill that obstruct the driver’s view.
*   **Wipers:** Test windshield wipers for streaking or failure to clear the glass.
*   **Mirrors:** Ensure all side-view and rearview mirrors are securely mounted and free of cracks.
*   **Body Integrity:** Verify that doors open and close securely from both the interior and exterior. Ensure the trunk latch is functional.

### Section 3: Tires, Brakes, & Suspension
*   **Tire Tread:** Confirm tread depth is at least 4/32 of an inch. Ensure no cords are showing or sidewall bulges exist.
*   **Brake Performance:** The inspector will test for sufficient pedal pressure and ensure there is no grinding or metallic noise during stops.
*   **Parking Brake:** The emergency/parking brake must hold the vehicle securely on an incline.
*   **Suspension:** The technician will check for excessive bounce or clunking sounds indicating worn struts or shocks.

### Section 4: Interior & Safety Systems
*   **Seat Belts:** All seat belts must latch and retract properly. This is a primary point of failure.
*   **Horn:** Must be clearly audible from at least 200 feet away.
*   **Dashboard Gauges:** Ensure the speedometer is functional and no "Check Engine" lights or persistent warning lights are illuminated.
*   **Interior Lighting:** Dome lights must function to ensure passenger safety at night.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Seek out "Uber-approved" inspection stations. Many local mechanic shops are familiar with the specific Illinois inspection form and can process it significantly faster than a general dealership.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always take a clear, high-resolution photo of the completed form immediately after the inspector signs it. This acts as a backup if the digital upload fails or if you are stopped by law enforcement.
*   **Pitfall - Missing Signatures:** A common reason for rejection is an incomplete form. Ensure the mechanic includes their shop’s stamp or license number and a clear, dated signature.
*   **Pitfall - Wait Times:** Do not wait until the day your current inspection expires. Many shops require appointments, and if your vehicle fails, you will need time to repair the issue and return for a re-inspection without going offline.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I perform the inspection myself if I am a certified mechanic?**
A: No. Uber requires that the inspection be performed by a licensed, third-party facility to maintain neutrality and liability standards. You cannot self-certify your own vehicle.

**Q: How often does an Uber vehicle in Illinois need to be inspected?**
A: In Illinois, vehicle inspections are typically required annually. However, you should check the "Documents" section of your Uber Driver app, as specific municipalities or changes in platform policy may require more frequent updates.

**Q: What happens if my vehicle fails the inspection?**
A: If your vehicle fails, you will receive a list of required repairs from the technician. You must complete these repairs and return to the facility (or a different one) for a re-inspection. You must remain offline until the vehicle passes and the updated document is approved by Uber.

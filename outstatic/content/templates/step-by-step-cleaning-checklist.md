# Standard Operating Procedure: Professional Facility Cleaning Standards

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for maintaining a high-level sanitary environment. By following these structured steps, cleaning personnel ensure consistency, efficiency, and compliance with hygiene regulations. This process relies on the "Top-to-Bottom, Dry-to-Wet" methodology, which prevents cross-contamination and ensures that debris knocked from higher surfaces is captured in subsequent steps. All staff must adhere to these guidelines to maintain organizational health and safety standards.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Safety
*   **Don Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Wear gloves, eye protection, and non-slip footwear as required by the chemical safety data sheets (SDS).
*   **Assemble Equipment:** Gather all necessary supplies, including microfiber cloths, HEPA-filter vacuums, mop buckets, and approved disinfectant solutions.
*   **Clear the Area:** Remove loose debris, personal items, and obstacles to ensure unobstructed access to all surfaces.
*   **Ventilation:** Open windows or activate HVAC exhaust systems to allow for proper airflow during chemical application.

## Phase 2: Dusting and High-Surface Cleaning
*   **Ceilings and Fixtures:** Dust ceiling fans, vents, and light fixtures using an extendable duster.
*   **Vertical Surfaces:** Dust wall hangings, window blinds, and door frames.
*   **Furniture Surfaces:** Wipe down tops of cabinets, desks, and shelves. Always work from the furthest corner of the room toward the exit.

## Phase 3: Sanitization and Wet Cleaning
*   **Apply Disinfectant:** Spray EPA-approved disinfectant onto surfaces (do not spray directly on electronics). Allow for the recommended "dwell time" (usually 3–10 minutes) to ensure efficacy.
*   **High-Touch Points:** Thoroughly wipe down light switches, doorknobs, handles, and shared technology interfaces.
*   **Countertops and Sinks:** Scrub surfaces using a non-abrasive sponge. Rinse and dry to prevent water spotting.
*   **Glass and Mirrors:** Use a dedicated glass cleaner and a lint-free cloth, utilizing an "S" pattern to avoid streaks.

## Phase 4: Floors and Final Inspection
*   **Vacuuming:** Perform a cross-hatch vacuuming pattern on all carpeted areas and rugs to ensure deep debris removal.
*   **Mopping:** Use a damp mop with the appropriate floor cleaner. Start from the corner furthest from the door and move backward to the exit.
*   **Waste Management:** Empty all trash receptacles and replace liners. Sanitize the interior of the bin if visible residue is present.
*   **Final Walkthrough:** Conduct a visual inspection to ensure no equipment has been left behind and that all surfaces are free of streaks or dust.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The Golden Rule:** Always clean from the top down. Gravity dictates that dust will settle; cleaning floors first is a wasted effort.
*   **Avoid Cross-Contamination:** Use color-coded cloths (e.g., blue for glass, red for restrooms, yellow for general surfaces) to ensure bacteria from one area is not transferred to another.
*   **Pitfall - Over-saturation:** Avoid soaking wood or electronic surfaces. Excessive moisture can lead to swelling, warping, or short-circuiting.
*   **Pitfall - Ignoring Dwell Time:** Wiping a disinfectant off immediately after applying it is a major error. It does not sanitize the surface unless it stays wet for the manufacturer's specified duration.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should microfiber cloths be changed?**
A: To prevent the spread of pathogens, cloths should be changed between rooms, or whenever the cloth becomes visibly soiled or loses its "tackiness."

**Q: Can I mix cleaning chemicals to make them more effective?**
A: Absolutely not. Mixing chemicals (such as bleach and ammonia) can create toxic, life-threatening gases. Always use chemicals strictly as labeled.

**Q: What is the most common area missed during a cleaning routine?**
A: Baseboards and the underside of door handles are the two most frequently overlooked areas. Incorporating these into the regular checklist is essential for a truly professional result.

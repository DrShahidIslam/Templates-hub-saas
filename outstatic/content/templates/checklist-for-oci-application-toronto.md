# Standard Operating Procedure: OCI Card Application (BLS Toronto)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols and documentation requirements for applicants submitting an Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) application through the BLS International center in Toronto. Adherence to these guidelines is critical to avoid application rejection or processing delays caused by missing documentation, incorrect formatting, or non-compliant photographs.

## Section 1: Pre-Submission Preparation
*   Confirm eligibility status: Ensure the applicant is of Indian origin and currently holds a valid foreign passport.
*   Verify jurisdiction: Confirm that the applicant resides within the jurisdiction of the Consulate General of India, Toronto.
*   Review current passport: Ensure the applicant’s foreign passport has a minimum of six months of validity remaining.
*   Renunciation/Surrender Certificate: Locate the original Surrender Certificate of the previous Indian passport. (If lost, obtain a notarized affidavit for the loss of Indian citizenship).

## Section 2: Documentation Checklist
*   OCI Application Form: Complete the online form on the official MHA portal, ensuring all data matches the foreign passport exactly. Print and sign the physical copies.
*   Photographs: Provide two (2) 2x2 inch photos with a white background. Ensure no shadows are present and ears/shoulders are clearly visible.
*   Foreign Passport Copy: Include a clear color copy of the information/data page.
*   Proof of Indian Origin: Provide a copy of the previous Indian Passport (first and last pages) or a copy of the Surrender Certificate.
*   Proof of Address (Toronto Jurisdiction): Submit two documents from the following: Utility bill (Hydro/Water/Gas), Lease Agreement, or Driver’s License.
*   Proof of Legal Status: Provide a copy of the Permanent Resident card or Work/Study permit.
*   Fees: Prepare the exact payment amount as specified by BLS Toronto (ensure payment methods align with current center policies, usually certified cheque or debit).

## Section 3: Physical Submission Process
*   Appointment Booking: Schedule an appointment via the BLS Toronto website. Print the appointment confirmation receipt.
*   Document Organization: Arrange documents in the exact order requested on the BLS checklist. Use paperclips; do not staple documents.
*   Declaration/Affidavit: Sign all required declaration forms in the presence of a BLS official or notary if required by the specific application type.
*   Receipt Collection: Upon successful submission at the BLS counter, collect the formal receipt with the reference number for tracking.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The Signature Trap:** Ensure your signature is within the designated box on the application form. If the signature touches the border, it will be rejected.
*   **Photo Compliance:** Do not use self-printed photos. Use a professional service that specializes in passport photos to ensure the 2x2 inch dimensions and lighting requirements are met.
*   **Renunciation Delay:** Many applicants fail to realize that the Renunciation process must be completed before or simultaneously with the OCI application. Do not skip this step.
*   **Consistency:** Ensure the name on your Indian passport, foreign passport, and current utility bills match exactly. Discrepancies often trigger a "Request for Clarification," adding weeks to the timeline.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Can I submit my application by mail?**
Currently, BLS Toronto requires in-person submission for OCI applications to ensure biometric verification and document integrity.

**2. How long does the OCI process usually take in Toronto?**
While processing times fluctuate based on the volume at the Consulate, applicants should expect a window of 8 to 12 weeks for final issuance.

**3. Do I need to surrender my Indian passport if I have already acquired foreign citizenship?**
Yes. You are legally required to renounce your Indian citizenship and obtain a Surrender Certificate before your OCI application can be processed.

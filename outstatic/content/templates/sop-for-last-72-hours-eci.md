# Standard Operating Procedure: Last 72 Hours (ECI Readiness)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the critical operational requirements, compliance mandates, and logistical benchmarks that must be met during the final 72-hour window preceding an Election Commission of India (ECI) designated event. As an operations lead, your primary objective during this period is to ensure absolute procedural integrity, seamless resource deployment, and strict adherence to the Model Code of Conduct (MCC). This document serves as the tactical roadmap to mitigate risks, ensure rapid incident response, and guarantee a zero-error execution phase.

## Phase 1: Resource Deployment & Infrastructure Audit (T-72 to T-48 Hours)

*   **Final Physical Verification:** Conduct a physical sweep of all polling stations or counting centers to ensure structural integrity and accessibility (ramps, lighting, and signage).
*   **Security Perimeter Setup:** Confirm the deployment of CAPF/State Police forces at designated perimeter points. Validate the placement of barricades and "No-Entry" zones.
*   **Communication Testing:** Conduct a "roll call" of all nodal officers. Test all satellite phones, radio sets, and dedicated helplines to ensure zero dead zones.
*   **Asset Distribution:** Execute the final handover of EVMs/VVPATs (if applicable) to the designated sectors, ensuring they are stored in the secure strongroom under 24/7 CCTV surveillance and dual-lock protocols.

## Phase 2: Compliance, MCC Enforcement & Logistics (T-48 to T-24 Hours)

*   **Quiet Period Enforcement:** Strictly implement the "Silence Period." Remove all political banners, posters, and hoardings within a 200-meter radius of the facility.
*   **Transport Coordination:** Finalize the deployment manifest for all polling personnel. Ensure all vehicles are tracked via GPS and assigned to specific routes with documented fueling stops.
*   **Material Verification:** Perform a "Double-Blind" count of statutory and non-statutory forms, indelible ink, and seals. Match physical stock against the official ECI inventory manifest.
*   **Emergency Contingency Plan:** Distribute the "Plan B" documentation to all sector officers, covering medical emergencies, power failures, or EVM malfunctions.

## Phase 3: Final Briefing & Operations Readiness (T-24 to T-0 Hours)

*   **Final Personnel Briefing:** Conduct a mandatory briefing session focusing on "Handling Disruptions" and "ECI Reporting Protocols." Verify that every staff member carries valid photo ID and authorization.
*   **IT/Technical Scrub:** Conduct a final stress test on the ECI webcasting portals and data entry systems. Ensure a secondary redundant power supply (UPS/Generator) is fueled and on-site.
*   **Last-Mile Logistics:** Confirm the delivery of food, potable water, and medical kits to all field personnel.
*   **Operational "Green Light":** Submit the final readiness report to the District Election Officer (DEO) confirming all boxes are checked and the site is mission-ready.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The "Shadow" Kit):** Always maintain a shadow set of essential forms and seals in a portable, waterproof case. Physical backups often save the day when digital systems lag.
*   **Pro Tip (Communication Loops):** Use a redundant WhatsApp or encrypted group specifically for "Immediate Alerts" between nodal officers to bypass standard bureaucratic delay chains.
*   **Pitfall (The Silence Period Trap):** Do not ignore digital footprints. Monitor social media and local messaging apps; political entities often switch to stealth campaigning during the final 48 hours.
*   **Pitfall (Underestimating the Fatigue Factor):** Operations staff often burn out at T-12 hours. Implement strict 4-hour rotation shifts to ensure alertness during the most critical pre-dawn hours.

## FAQ: Last 72 Hours

**Q: What is the priority if an EVM/VVPAT unit is suspected of tampering during the final 24 hours?**
A: Immediately seal the device, document the incident in the logbook, and contact the Returning Officer (RO) to replace the unit from the pre-verified reserve stock. Do not attempt a technical fix on-site.

**Q: How do we handle unauthorized political presence near the facility during the silence period?**
A: Do not engage directly. Notify the police nodal officer immediately to disperse the crowd and document the time and nature of the activity for the official observer’s report.

**Q: Is it permissible to deviate from the logistical route if a road is blocked?**
A: Only if authorized by the Sector Magistrate. All deviations must be logged with the new ETA and communicated to the District Control Room to ensure the tracking team is aware of the change.

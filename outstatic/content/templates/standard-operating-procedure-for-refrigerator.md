# Standard Operating Procedure: Refrigerator Maintenance and Operations

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the rigorous requirements for the operation, maintenance, and hygiene of refrigeration units. Adhering to these protocols is critical to maintaining food safety, extending the lifespan of mechanical components, and ensuring consistent temperature stability. This guide is designed for personnel responsible for facility management and kitchen operations to mitigate the risk of cross-contamination and energy inefficiency.

## Step-by-Step Operational Checklist

## Daily Operational Procedures
*   **Temperature Verification:** Inspect the external digital display and internal thermometer twice daily (start and end of shift). Ensure readings remain within the safe range (35°F to 38°F / 1.7°C to 3.3°C).
*   **Door Integrity Check:** Verify that door gaskets (seals) are intact and free of debris. Ensure the door latches securely to prevent ambient air infiltration.
*   **Visual Inspection:** Check for signs of internal icing or frost buildup on rear walls or cooling coils.
*   **Cleanliness Audit:** Remove any spills or leaks immediately using a food-safe disinfectant to prevent mold growth and bacterial cross-contamination.

## Weekly Maintenance and Sanitation
*   **Deep Cleaning:** Remove all contents. Wipe down interior walls, shelving, and drawers with a mild soap and water solution, followed by a food-grade sanitizer.
*   **Content Rotation:** Apply the First-In, First-Out (FIFO) method. Dispose of any expired items or spoiled inventory.
*   **Condenser Coil Check:** Inspect external coils for dust or lint accumulation. Use a soft-bristled brush or a vacuum with a hose attachment to gently remove buildup.
*   **Drain Line Clearance:** Inspect the condensate drain line to ensure it is not obstructed, preventing internal water pooling.

## Monthly Mechanical Inspections
*   **Gasket Inspection:** Test the seal by closing the door on a strip of paper; if it slides out easily, the gasket is compromised and requires replacement.
*   **Clearance Verification:** Ensure there is a minimum of 3-4 inches of clearance around the top and sides of the unit to allow for proper airflow and heat dissipation.
*   **Leveling Check:** Use a spirit level to ensure the refrigerator is perfectly level, preventing improper door swing and potential coolant leaks.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pitfall - Overcrowding:** Never overfill the refrigerator. Restricted airflow leads to "warm spots" and forces the compressor to run constantly, leading to premature motor failure.
*   **Pitfall - Hot Foods:** Do not place hot food directly into the refrigerator. Allow items to cool to room temperature first to avoid spiking the internal temperature and compromising other stored goods.
*   **Pro Tip - Temperature Logs:** Maintain a physical or digital logbook. Auditors and health inspectors prioritize consistent, historical temperature data over current readings.
*   **Pro Tip - Door Discipline:** Implement the "Open-Shut" rule. Minimize door opening frequency and duration to maintain thermal stability and reduce energy consumption.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if the refrigerator temperature rises above 40°F (4°C)?**
A: Immediately verify the door seal and check for blockages near the internal vents. If the temperature remains high for more than two hours, move all perishable inventory to a backup unit and contact a certified refrigeration technician.

**Q: How often should I perform a deep clean?**
A: A full interior deep clean should be performed at least weekly. However, spot cleaning should be conducted immediately upon the detection of any spills to prevent odor buildup and microbial growth.

**Q: Why is my refrigerator making a loud, humming noise?**
A: Constant, loud humming usually indicates a strained compressor caused by dirty condenser coils or inadequate ventilation. Clean the coils first; if the noise persists, contact maintenance as the compressor may be failing.

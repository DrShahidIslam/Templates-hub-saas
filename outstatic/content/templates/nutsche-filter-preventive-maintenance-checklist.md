# Standard Operating Procedure: Nutsche Filter Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory preventive maintenance (PM) protocols for Nutsche Filter-Dryers. Consistent maintenance is critical to ensure vacuum integrity, mechanical reliability, and filtration efficiency. Failure to adhere to these procedures may result in cross-contamination, product loss, mechanical failure, or hazardous leaks. All maintenance tasks must be performed by authorized personnel according to site-specific Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures.

## 1. Safety and Preparation
*   Ensure the vessel is fully decontaminated and purged of hazardous vapors.
*   Implement formal Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures on all power sources (agitator motor, hydraulic units) and pneumatic supplies.
*   Verify the availability of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) including chemical-resistant gloves, safety goggles, and respiratory protection if required.
*   Confirm that the vessel is at atmospheric pressure and ambient temperature.

## 2. Structural and Mechanical Integrity
*   Inspect the vessel shell and discharge housing for signs of corrosion, pitting, or cracks.
*   Check all structural support legs and foundation bolts for tightness and integrity.
*   Inspect the agitator shaft for run-out or vibration; ensure the shaft is plumb and centered.
*   Examine the mechanical seal assembly for leaks; check seal fluid levels and pressure regulators.
*   Lubricate agitator bearings and hydraulic system joints according to the manufacturer’s specification (MTS).

## 3. Filtration and Screen Assembly
*   Inspect the filter cloth or sintered metal screen for tears, plugging, or chemical degradation.
*   Check the O-ring seals on the filter plate periphery; ensure they are free of debris and have not lost elasticity.
*   Verify the integrity of the filter plate locking mechanism (e.g., bayonet or hydraulic bolts).
*   Clean all filtrate discharge ports to ensure no residual solids are blocking drainage.

## 4. Agitator and Discharge Systems
*   Inspect agitator blades (s-type or paddle) for erosion or buildup of hardened cake.
*   Verify the clearance between the agitator blades and the filter cloth (ensure no mechanical contact).
*   Test the agitator drive gearbox oil levels; check for metal shavings or water contamination in the oil.
*   Inspect the discharge valve (side or center) for smooth operation; ensure the valve seat is free of debris.

## 5. Vacuum and Pressure Systems
*   Perform a pressure decay test to verify vacuum integrity.
*   Inspect all sight glasses for crazing, cloudiness, or seal leaks.
*   Test the functionality of the rupture disk/pressure relief valve (ensure the tag date is current).
*   Inspect all vacuum hoses, gaskets, and connections for degradation or cracks.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always keep a spare O-ring kit specifically for the main flange and discharge valve on-site. These are the most common points of failure during a campaign.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a torque wrench for all flange bolts to ensure even pressure distribution, which prevents uneven gasket compression and vacuum leaks.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-tightening the mechanical seal gland can lead to premature seal failure or heat buildup. Always follow the manufacturer’s torque settings precisely.
*   **Pitfall:** Never use metal scrapers on the filter cloth or sintered media. Use appropriate solvent washes or plastic tools to prevent scratching the surface.

## FAQ
**Q: How often should the filter media be replaced?**
A: Filter media should be replaced based on the number of filtration cycles or evidence of pressure drop increase across the cake. Routine inspection during every PM interval is mandatory.

**Q: Why is a pressure decay test critical after maintenance?**
A: Nutsche filters operate under high vacuum/pressure. A pressure decay test confirms that gaskets were re-seated correctly and that the vessel remains hermetically sealed, preventing oxygen ingress or solvent vapor release.

**Q: What should I look for in the agitator gearbox oil?**
A: Look for milky discoloration (indicating water ingress from a seal failure) or metallic particulates (indicating gear wear). If detected, perform an immediate internal inspection and oil replacement.

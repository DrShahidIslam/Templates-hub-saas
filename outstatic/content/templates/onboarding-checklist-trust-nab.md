# Standard Operating Procedure: Trust Onboarding Process (NAB)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for onboarding a new Trust entity with National Australia Bank (NAB). Adhering to these steps ensures full compliance with Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) regulations, streamlines the application process, and minimizes delays caused by incomplete documentation. This document is intended for Relationship Managers, Operations Officers, and administrative support staff responsible for client intake.

## Phase 1: Pre-Submission Documentation & Verification
Before initiating the formal application, ensure all constituent documents are compiled and verified for accuracy.
*   **Obtain Trust Deed:** Secure a complete, signed, and stamped copy of the original Trust Deed and any subsequent Deeds of Variation.
*   **Identify Trustee Structure:** Determine if the Trustee is an individual or a Corporate Trustee.
    *   *If Corporate:* Obtain the ASIC Company Extract (dated within the last 30 days) and the Constitution.
*   **Identify Beneficial Owners:** Confirm the identity of all individuals who hold a 25% or greater interest in the trust, as well as the Settlor and any Appointors.
*   **Verify Tax Status:** Confirm the Trust’s Tax File Number (TFN) and ABN. Ensure the Trust is registered for GST if annual turnover exceeds the threshold.

## Phase 2: KYC & Compliance Requirements
NAB requires rigorous identification of all controlling parties associated with the Trust.
*   **Individual Identification:** Collect primary identification (Passport/Driver’s License) for all Trustees, Settlors, and significant beneficiaries.
*   **Residential Proof:** Obtain secondary proof of address for all key stakeholders (utility bills, council rate notices, or bank statements dated within 3 months).
*   **Trustee Authority:** Draft and sign a "Resolution of Trustees" document authorizing the opening of bank accounts and designating specific signatories.
*   **Purpose of Account:** Document the intended purpose of the account (e.g., investment, trading, SMSF operations) and provide an estimate of monthly transaction volume to assist with AML risk profiling.

## Phase 3: Submission & Account Activation
Once all physical and digital copies are verified, proceed with the formal submission.
*   **Application Submission:** Complete the NAB Business/Trust account application form via the online portal or branch representative.
*   **Signatures:** Ensure the "wet ink" or verified electronic signatures of all Trustees match the requirements stipulated in the Trust Deed.
*   **Verification of Identity (VOI):** Complete the mandatory VOI process for all signatories via a NAB-approved method (e.g., in-branch visit or digital identity verification service).
*   **Account Setup:** Monitor the account status via the NAB Connect platform; ensure internet banking access is granted to the authorized signatories.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The Deed Trap:** A common pitfall is providing an incomplete Trust Deed. Always include the "Execution Page" and the "Schedule," as these are frequently audited by compliance teams.
*   **Name Matching:** Ensure the name of the Trust on the application form is an exact match to the name appearing on the ASIC Register and the Trust Deed. Discrepancies here are the #1 cause of rejection.
*   **Beneficiary Updates:** If the Trust has a discretionary class of beneficiaries, ensure you have a clear definition of the "Primary Beneficiaries" as requested by NAB compliance, rather than just a blanket reference to the Deed.
*   **Digital Hygiene:** Always use encrypted channels for sending KYC documentation to your NAB representative; never send scans of passports or TFN documents via unencrypted email.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Do I need to provide the Trust Deed if the Trust is a Simple Family Trust?**
A: Yes. Regardless of the size or type of trust, the Trust Deed is the foundational legal document required for NAB to verify the powers of the Trustee and the nature of the Trust.

**Q: What happens if a Trustee is an overseas resident?**
A: Foreign Trustees are subject to additional KYC scrutiny. You will likely need to provide an international police check or a notarized identity verification from an approved authority in their country of residence.

**Q: How long does the onboarding process typically take?**
A: Once all documentation is submitted in "Good Order," the approval process generally takes 5–10 business days. Delays often occur due to missing signatures or illegible scans of identity documents.

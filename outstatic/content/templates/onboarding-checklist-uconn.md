# Standard Operating Procedure: University of Connecticut (UConn) New Employee Onboarding

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory onboarding workflow for new hires joining the University of Connecticut. The objective is to ensure institutional compliance, technical provisioning, and organizational integration. Adherence to this protocol minimizes administrative delays and ensures that all faculty and staff have the necessary tools to perform their duties on their first day. Hiring managers and departmental administrators must initiate these steps at least two weeks prior to the employee’s start date.

## Phase 1: Pre-Arrival & Administrative Foundation
*   **NetID Activation:** Ensure the employee has initiated their UConn NetID and established Duo Two-Factor Authentication.
*   **HR Paperwork Submission:** Verify the completion of the I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification (via UConn's designated portal) and tax withholding forms.
*   **UConn ID Card:** Coordinate the submission of a digital photo to the One Card Office to ensure the physical ID badge is available for pickup upon arrival.
*   **Background Check Clearance:** Confirm HR has finalized all necessary background screenings.
*   **Payroll Setup:** Verify that direct deposit information is entered into the Core-CT system.

## Phase 2: Technical Provisioning & Access
*   **Hardware Acquisition:** Request necessary laptops, peripherals, or specialized hardware through the department’s IT support liaison.
*   **Software Licensing:** Provision required access to University-licensed software (e.g., Office 365, Adobe Creative Cloud, specialized research tools).
*   **System Permissions:** Request access to departmental shared drives, SharePoint sites, or specific administrative systems (e.g., KFS, HuskyBuy, PeopleSoft) based on the employee's role.
*   **Email Configuration:** Ensure the UConn email account (@uconn.edu) is active and integrated with the relevant departmental listservs.

## Phase 3: Workspace & Environment Setup
*   **Office/Lab Preparation:** Clean the assigned workspace and ensure furniture (ergonomic chair, desk) is functional.
*   **Physical Security:** Request necessary building/lab access through the Facilities Operations or departmental key coordinator.
*   **Supplies:** Stock the workspace with essential office supplies (notebooks, pens, connectivity cables).
*   **Directory Updates:** Update the department’s internal contact list and the UConn phone/email directory.

## Phase 4: Orientation & Integration
*   **Welcome Communication:** Send a detailed email to the new hire including start time, office location, parking instructions, and the agenda for the first day.
*   **IT/Security Briefing:** Schedule a mandatory session regarding UConn’s cybersecurity policies and data privacy expectations.
*   **Departmental Culture:** Assign an "Onboarding Buddy" to assist the new hire with navigating campus resources, dining options, and departmental norms.
*   **Required Training:** Assign mandatory University training modules via the HuskyLearn portal (e.g., Title IX, Workplace Safety, FERPA compliance).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pitfall - The NetID Bottleneck:** The NetID is the key to every UConn system. If this is not set up immediately upon receipt of the offer letter, the employee will be unable to access email or training portals on Day 1.
*   **Pro Tip - Parking Permits:** Parking on the Storrs campus is permit-based. Direct the new employee to the UConn Parking Services website immediately to avoid ticketing and frustration during their first week.
*   **Pitfall - Software Lead Times:** Some specialized research or academic software requires multi-departmental approval. Do not wait until the start date to request these licenses.
*   **Pro Tip - The "Husky" Welcome:** Reach out to the employee a week before they start to introduce them to the team via email. This significantly reduces anxiety and boosts engagement.

## FAQ

**Q: How does a new hire obtain their physical Husky One Card?**
A: Once their ID photo has been approved and submitted via the One Card Office website, they must visit the One Card Office in the Wilbur Cross Building with a government-issued photo ID to receive their card.

**Q: Where can employees find information on mandatory University training?**
A: Mandatory compliance training is tracked via HuskyLearn. New employees should receive an automated notification, but they can log in with their NetID to see their assigned curriculum.

**Q: What should I do if a new hire cannot access their UConn email?**
A: First, ensure their NetID is active and password reset has been completed. If issues persist, contact the UConn University Information Technology Services (UITS) Help Center at 860-486-4357.

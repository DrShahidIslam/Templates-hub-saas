# Standard Operating Procedure: New Hire Onboarding Process

This document outlines the standardized workflow for onboarding a new hire at Amazon. The goal is to ensure a seamless integration, providing the necessary technical access, physical resources, and cultural orientation to ensure the new team member is productive and compliant from Day 1. By following this protocol, managers ensure that all logistical hurdles are cleared before the employee arrives, minimizing downtime and fostering a positive initial experience.

## Phase 1: Pre-Arrival & Systems Provisioning (T-minus 14 Days)

*   **System Access Request:** Submit a ticket through the internal IT portal to ensure the user’s credentials (LDAP) are created and assigned to the correct organizational unit.
*   **Hardware Procurement:** Request required equipment (Laptop, monitor, headset, security token) via the internal procurement tool, ensuring shipment to the employee’s home address or site location.
*   **Security Clearance:** Initiate background check completion and confirm all pre-employment screening status is "Clear" in the HR portal.
*   **Email & Permissions:** Verify that the hire has been added to relevant distribution lists, Slack/Chime channels, and has the appropriate folder access levels within the internal network.

## Phase 2: Orientation & Compliance (Day 1)

*   **Welcome Meeting:** Schedule a 30-minute introductory sync to cover the agenda for the week, expectations, and immediate questions.
*   **Compliance Training:** Ensure the employee completes mandatory "Day 1" modules, including Information Security, Code of Conduct, and Workplace Safety.
*   **Badge & Physical Access:** If working on-site, escort the employee to Badging Services to ensure building, floor, and sensitive area access.
*   **Manager Introduction:** Review the team structure, the org chart, and the specific "Leadership Principles" that apply to the hire’s functional role.

## Phase 3: Integration & Knowledge Transfer (Week 1)

*   **Project Roadmap:** Define clear 30-60-90 day goals, including initial "quick wins" to build momentum.
*   **Mentor/Buddy Assignment:** Introduce the hire to their designated "onboarding buddy" who will assist with cultural navigation and tactical questions.
*   **Tool Training:** Walkthrough of role-specific software, internal dashboards, and communication protocols (e.g., how to use the internal ticketing system or wiki).
*   **Weekly Check-in:** Establish a recurring 1-on-1 meeting to address blockers and provide feedback on initial tasks.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Send a "Welcome" email 48 hours before the start date with clear instructions on where to log in and who to contact for technical issues. This reduces anxiety.
*   **Pro Tip:** Leverage the internal Wiki/Knowledge Base for training materials. Encourage the new hire to "self-serve" documentation early to build autonomy.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-scheduling. Do not fill their entire first week with back-to-back meetings. Allow time for them to read through internal documentation and set up their workspace.
*   **Pitfall:** Assuming familiarity. Do not assume the hire understands Amazon-specific acronyms. Provide a "Glossary of Terms" document early on to avoid confusion.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if the new hire’s hardware does not arrive on Day 1?**
A: Immediately submit an urgent ticket to IT Support and, if possible, coordinate with your team to provide a loaner device to ensure the employee can access essential training modules.

**Q: How do I handle access requests that require manager approval for sensitive databases?**
A: Identify these permissions during the "Pre-Arrival" phase. Submit these requests as early as possible, as some security protocols may require secondary approvals that take 48–72 hours.

**Q: Is it mandatory for every new hire to have an assigned buddy?**
A: While highly recommended to facilitate cultural integration, the "buddy" program is a best practice. It significantly improves retention and reduces the burden of tactical questions on the direct manager.

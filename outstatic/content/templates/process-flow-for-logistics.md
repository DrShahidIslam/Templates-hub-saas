# Standard Operating Procedure: Logistics Process Flow

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the end-to-end logistics process, ensuring the seamless movement of goods from procurement through to final delivery. This document serves as a standardized guide to maintain inventory accuracy, optimize transportation routes, and maximize customer satisfaction. All logistics personnel, warehouse staff, and procurement officers are required to adhere to these protocols to minimize bottlenecks and operational expenditure.

## Phase 1: Procurement and Inbound Logistics
*   **Order Verification:** Cross-reference Purchase Orders (PO) against supplier confirmations to ensure quantity and specifications match.
*   **Inbound Scheduling:** Coordinate with vendors to schedule delivery slots, ensuring warehouse labor availability.
*   **Receipt Inspection:** Conduct a physical count and quality check (QC) of all incoming goods against the packing slip.
*   **Documentation:** Log all received items into the Warehouse Management System (WMS) and attach corresponding digital documentation.
*   **Put-away:** Categorize items by SKU and move inventory to designated racking locations using the First-In-First-Out (FIFO) methodology.

## Phase 2: Inventory Management and Order Fulfillment
*   **Order Processing:** Validate customer orders for stock availability and prioritize based on delivery SLAs.
*   **Picking:** Generate digital pick lists; organize paths by warehouse layout to minimize travel time.
*   **Packaging:** Select appropriate dunnage and shipping containers to protect goods while optimizing dimensional weight.
*   **Labeling:** Apply correct shipping labels, including carrier-specific barcodes, hazard symbols, and "fragile" warnings where necessary.
*   **Final Manifest:** Generate the Bill of Lading (BOL) and stage packages in the outbound docking area.

## Phase 3: Outbound Logistics and Distribution
*   **Carrier Dispatch:** Verify that carrier pickup times are aligned with warehouse outbound windows.
*   **Loading:** Supervise the loading process to ensure weight distribution stability within the transport vehicle.
*   **Tracking Updates:** Sync tracking numbers with the customer notification system to provide real-time updates.
*   **Proof of Delivery (POD):** Monitor shipments via carrier portals and ensure digital PODs are archived upon receipt.

## Phase 4: Reverse Logistics (Returns)
*   **RMA Authorization:** Validate Return Merchandise Authorizations (RMA) before accepting returns.
*   **Return Inspection:** Assess the condition of returned items; designate as "Restockable," "Refurbished," or "Disposal."
*   **Credit Processing:** Trigger financial adjustments in the ERP once the return is logged into the system.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Data Integrity:** Perform cycle counts at least weekly to ensure WMS data mirrors actual physical stock.
*   **Route Optimization:** Utilize logistics software to analyze traffic patterns and fuel costs, shifting to automated routing when possible.
*   **Cross-Docking:** Identify high-velocity items that can bypass the put-away phase and move directly to outbound transit.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **Lack of Redundancy:** Relying on a single carrier leads to severe disruption during capacity crunches. Always maintain a backup carrier list.
*   **Poor Communication:** Silos between the warehouse team and the customer support team often lead to "Where Is My Order" (WISMO) complaints.
*   **Inaccurate Weight/Dims:** Failing to measure actual dimensions before shipping results in unexpected carrier adjustment fees.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most effective way to handle inventory discrepancies?**
A: Immediate reconciliation. If a count is off, initiate a formal "Stock Discrepancy Report" to investigate the root cause, whether it be a picking error, shipping damage, or systemic glitch.

**Q: How often should we audit our carrier performance?**
A: Conduct quarterly business reviews (QBRs) with your primary carriers to assess on-time delivery rates, damage percentages, and cost-per-mile.

**Q: What should be the priority if a shipment is delayed in transit?**
A: Proactive communication. Notify the customer immediately via automated email or SMS, providing a revised delivery estimate and a token of goodwill (e.g., a shipping credit) to mitigate dissatisfaction.

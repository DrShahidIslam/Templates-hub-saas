# Standard Operating Procedure: UK Standard Visitor Visa Application (India)

This document serves as the formal operational procedure for Indian nationals applying for a UK Standard Visitor Visa. Ensuring strict adherence to documentation standards and evidence-based reporting is critical to mitigating the risk of refusal. This process requires meticulous attention to detail, financial transparency, and chronological organization of supporting evidence.

## Phase 1: Pre-Application Documentation & Preparation

Before initiating the online application, ensure the following evidence is compiled. Digital copies must be clear, legible, and saved in PDF format.

*   **Valid Passport:** Must be valid for the duration of your stay with at least one blank page. Provide copies of previous passports if they contain travel history (US, UK, Schengen, Canada, Australia).
*   **Proof of Funds:** 
    *   Bank statements (last 6 months) showing consistent income and closing balance.
    *   Salary slips (last 6 months).
    *   Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 3 years.
*   **Proof of Employment/Business:**
    *   **Salaried:** No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the employer, appointment letter, and ID card.
    *   **Business Owners:** Business registration documents, ITRs of the company, and bank statements of the company.
*   **Travel Itinerary:** A clear, realistic breakdown of the trip, including hotel bookings (or invitation letters from friends/family) and flight reservations (do not purchase non-refundable tickets until a visa is granted).

## Phase 2: Online Application and Payment

*   **Create UKVI Account:** Access the official GOV.UK portal. Ensure all data matches your passport exactly.
*   **Select Visa Type:** Standard Visitor Visa (up to 6 months).
*   **Complete Declaration:** Provide accurate information regarding past travel, criminal records, and immigration history.
*   **Visa Fee Payment:** Pay the fee online via credit/debit card (GBP).
*   **Biometric Appointment:** Once the form is submitted, redirect to the VFS Global portal to select an appointment slot at your nearest Visa Application Centre (VAC) in India (e.g., Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai).

## Phase 3: Biometrics and Submission

*   **Document Upload:** Upload all documents to the VFS portal at least 48 hours before the appointment.
*   **Appointment Day:** 
    *   Carry your printed appointment confirmation and the checklist generated after online submission.
    *   Bring the original passport.
    *   Submit fingerprints and digital photograph at the center.
    *   Choose a collection method (courier or self-pickup).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The "Genuine Visitor" Test:** The most common reason for refusal is "lack of strong ties to home country." Ensure you provide evidence of family, property, or long-term employment in India to prove you intend to return.
*   **Consistency is Key:** Ensure the details on your bank statements (names, account numbers) match your cover letter. Discrepancies in financial data are frequent triggers for rejection.
*   **Avoid "Over-Documenting":** Do not include unnecessary items like property deeds or school certificates unless specifically relevant to your case. Too much irrelevant documentation clutters the file and irritates the case officer.
*   **The Cover Letter:** Always include a professional cover letter summarizing your visit purpose, who is funding the trip, and an explanation of any unusual financial transactions (e.g., a sudden large deposit).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Do I need to book flight tickets before applying?**
A: No. The Home Office explicitly advises against purchasing non-refundable travel tickets before a visa is granted. Provide a "flight reservation" or a clear itinerary instead.

**Q: How far in advance should I apply?**
A: You can apply up to 3 months before your date of travel. It is recommended to apply at least 6–8 weeks in advance to account for seasonal processing backlogs.

**Q: Can I use a savings account with a sudden large deposit?**
A: Large, unexplained deposits are a major red flag. If you have deposited a large sum recently, you must provide proof of the source of funds (e.g., sale of property, inheritance, bonus payout) to avoid suspicion of "funds parking."

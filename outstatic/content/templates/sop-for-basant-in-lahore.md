# Standard Operating Procedure: Basant Festival Coordination (Lahore)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the primary operational framework for managing logistics, safety protocols, and event execution during the Basant festival in Lahore. Given the high-density nature of this event, this SOP prioritizes public safety, infrastructure integrity, and regulatory compliance. All team members must adhere to these guidelines to ensure a seamless experience while mitigating the inherent risks associated with high-altitude kite flying, rooftop gatherings, and large-scale crowd movement.

## Phase 1: Pre-Event Compliance & Risk Mitigation

*   **Legal Review:** Verify current provincial government directives regarding kite-flying regulations, permitted materials (strictly banning metallic/glass-coated strings), and designated zones.
*   **Structural Audit:** Conduct a comprehensive safety inspection of all hosting rooftops. Ensure guardrails are at a minimum height of 42 inches and verify the weight-bearing capacity of the structures.
*   **Emergency Access:** Clear all emergency exit routes. Remove any clutter or decorative obstructions from stairwells and roof access points.
*   **Permit Acquisition:** Secure all required NOCs (No Objection Certificates) from local law enforcement and civil administration at least 72 hours prior to the event.

## Phase 2: Logistics and On-Site Setup

*   **Crowd Control Zones:** Establish distinct areas for kite flying and spectator seating. Maintain a "No-Fly Zone" near electrical transformers and power lines.
*   **Communication Infrastructure:** Set up a dedicated radio or communication loop for the event safety team. Distribute contact lists for the nearest emergency trauma centers and local police stations.
*   **Supplies Procurement:** Stock medical kits (focusing on laceration care), fire extinguishers (Class A/B/C), and industrial-grade hydration stations.
*   **Power Management:** Ensure all high-voltage decorative lighting is professionally installed and grounded to prevent electrical hazards during peak flying hours.

## Phase 3: Execution and Operational Management

*   **Crowd Flow Monitoring:** Assign floor managers to monitor rooftop density. Implement a "One-In-One-Out" policy if density thresholds are exceeded.
*   **Safety Spotting:** Deploy trained safety officers to watch for low-hanging kite strings (dore) to prevent accidental neck or eye injuries among attendees.
*   **Material Inspection:** Enforce a strict "No Metallic String" policy at the entry point. Conduct random bag checks for prohibited materials.
*   **Incident Response:** In the event of an injury or structural breach, the lead safety officer has the authority to order an immediate cessation of all kite-flying activities.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

**Pro Tips:**
*   **Sun Protection:** Provide ample shaded areas and high-SPF sunscreen; the reflection from rooftops intensifies solar exposure.
*   **Digital Briefing:** Send a "Safety WhatsApp Blast" to all attendees the morning of the event highlighting the Do’s and Don'ts.
*   **Strategic Staffing:** Use veteran staff who have managed past Basant events; experience in spotting dangerous "dore" patterns is invaluable.

**Pitfalls to Avoid:**
*   **Overloading:** Never allow speakers, catering tables, or large groups to cluster near the edge of a roof.
*   **Ignoring Wind Conditions:** If high-speed gusts exceed 30 km/h, pause the activity; strong winds lead to unpredictable kite behavior and increased collision risks.
*   **Neglecting Neighbors:** Inadequate communication with adjacent properties often leads to noise complaints or disputes; maintain a buffer zone.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the primary safety risk during Basant?**
A: The most significant risks are falls from unprotected rooftops and lacerations from illicit glass-coated kite strings. Prioritizing secure guardrails and enforcing material bans are the top countermeasures.

**Q: How do we handle unauthorized kite strings falling onto our roof?**
A: Use a designated "Retrieval Tool" (a long, blunt-ended pole) to move the string safely away from guests. Never attempt to untangle strings with bare hands, as they may be razor-sharp.

**Q: Should we shut down operations if we see police approaching?**
A: Yes. If local authorities arrive for an inspection or enforcement activity, immediately halt all kite-flying and present your NOCs calmly. Cooperate fully with law enforcement to avoid unnecessary escalation.

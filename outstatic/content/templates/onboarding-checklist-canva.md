# Standard Operating Procedure: Canva Enterprise Onboarding

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for onboarding new team members into the organization’s Canva Enterprise ecosystem. Effective onboarding ensures that all users have immediate access to brand-compliant assets, collaboration tools, and appropriate permission levels, thereby maintaining design integrity and operational efficiency across the organization.

## Phase 1: Account Provisioning and Security
*   **Verify Email Domain:** Confirm the user has a valid corporate email address matching the organization’s SSO (Single Sign-On) domain.
*   **Send Invitation:** Navigate to the Canva Enterprise dashboard under *People* and send the invitation link via the official domain.
*   **Assign SSO/MFA:** Ensure the user has linked their Canva account to the corporate Identity Provider (IdP) for secure, seamless access.
*   **Select License Type:** Assign the user as either a 'Member' (standard user) or 'Administrator' (if they require folder management or billing access).

## Phase 2: Workspace Setup and Configuration
*   **Add to Team:** Ensure the user is added to the correct 'Team' group within the enterprise workspace to grant access to shared projects.
*   **Define Brand Kit Access:** Map the user to the specific Brand Kit associated with their department (e.g., Marketing, Sales, Product) to ensure access to approved logos, fonts, and palettes.
*   **Grant Folder Permissions:** Assign appropriate read/write access to specific departmental folders as outlined in the team directory.
*   **Set Default Preferences:** Instruct the user to configure their 'Design' settings, including default measurement units and notification preferences.

## Phase 3: Orientation and Resource Training
*   **Internal Brand Guidelines:** Share the internal 'Canva Style Guide' document which outlines usage rules for logos and font hierarchy.
*   **Canva Design School Introduction:** Direct the user to complete the *Canva Design School* foundational courses relevant to their specific role.
*   **Workflow Tutorial:** Demonstrate how to use the 'Comment' and 'Version History' features to facilitate asynchronous design feedback.
*   **Resource Library Familiarization:** Show the user where the 'Templates' folder is located and how to utilize custom branded templates versus generic stock assets.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Create a 'New Hire' project folder titled *[User Name] - Sandbox*. This allows the new team member to practice design workflows without cluttering master brand assets.
*   **Pro Tip:** Encourage the use of the 'Brand Folder' naming convention to prevent the accumulation of unorganized, duplicated files.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not grant 'Administrator' access to junior team members; this increases the risk of accidental deletion of global Brand Kits or team-wide assets.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid sharing external templates without first vetting them for corporate font compliance; always ensure templates are moved into the official 'Brand Kit' architecture.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How do I request access to a folder I cannot see?**
Contact the department's Canva Administrator. They can adjust your folder permissions within the 'People' settings tab without requiring an account reset.

**2. What should I do if a brand asset (logo or color) is missing?**
Report the discrepancy to the Design Lead or Admin. They will upload the missing asset directly to the Brand Kit, ensuring it becomes immediately available to all team members.

**3. Can I work on designs offline?**
Canva is a web-based, cloud-native platform. You must have an active internet connection to save changes and collaborate with your team; therefore, offline work is not supported.

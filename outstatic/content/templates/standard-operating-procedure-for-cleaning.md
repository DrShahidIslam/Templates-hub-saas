# Standard Operating Procedure: General Facility Cleaning & Sanitation

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for maintaining high standards of cleanliness, hygiene, and organization within the facility. Adherence to these procedures ensures a safe, professional, and welcoming environment for all staff and visitors while mitigating health risks and maintaining asset longevity. All personnel are expected to follow these guidelines systematically to ensure consistency and compliance with safety regulations.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Safety
*   **Don Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Wear appropriate gloves, eye protection, and non-slip footwear as required by chemical Safety Data Sheets (SDS).
*   **Gather Supplies:** Collect all necessary equipment (microfiber cloths, vacuum, mop, multi-purpose cleaner, glass cleaner, disinfectant) on a mobile cart to prevent cross-contamination.
*   **Secure the Area:** Place "Wet Floor" signage in areas currently being cleaned to alert staff and prevent slip-and-fall incidents.
*   **Ventilation:** Ensure the area is well-ventilated, especially when using heavy-duty chemical cleaning agents.

## Phase 2: Surface Cleaning and Dusting
*   **High-to-Low Dusting:** Begin by dusting high surfaces (vents, light fixtures, tops of cabinets) so that falling debris is captured during floor cleaning.
*   **Clear Surfaces:** Remove loose items from desks and tables. Clean and disinfect all hard surfaces using an approved multi-purpose cleaner.
*   **Electronics Maintenance:** Use a dry, lint-free microfiber cloth for screens; use canned air for keyboards. Never spray liquid directly onto electronic devices.
*   **Spot Check:** Inspect walls and baseboards for scuff marks and wipe down as necessary.

## Phase 3: Sanitation and Restrooms
*   **Apply Chemicals:** Spray disinfectant on toilets, sinks, and counters. Allow for the required "dwell time" (refer to product label, usually 3–10 minutes) to ensure microbial kill.
*   **Deep Scrub:** Scrub fixtures using designated abrasive pads or brushes, ensuring high-touch points like faucets and flush handles are prioritized.
*   **Mirror Cleaning:** Wipe mirrors with glass cleaner using a clean cloth, moving in a top-to-bottom pattern to avoid streaks.
*   **Restock:** Refill paper towels, toilet paper, and hand soap dispensers to 100% capacity.

## Phase 4: Flooring and Finishing
*   **Trash Removal:** Empty all waste bins and replace liners. Wipe down the interior of the bins if evidence of leakage or odors exists.
*   **Vacuuming/Sweeping:** Vacuum all carpeted areas (including edges) and sweep hard-surface floors to remove debris.
*   **Mopping:** Mop hard floors using a clean mop head and the correct dilution of floor cleaner. Use a "figure-eight" motion to ensure uniform coverage.
*   **Final Inspection:** Perform a walkthrough to ensure no streaks remain, trash was collected, and all signage is removed once surfaces are dry.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The Color-Coded Cloth System:** Utilize color-coded microfiber cloths (e.g., Blue for glass, Red for restrooms, Yellow for general surfaces) to prevent the spread of bacteria from high-risk areas to low-risk areas.
*   **Pro Tip: The Dwell Time Rule:** The most common mistake in commercial cleaning is wiping away disinfectant immediately after spraying. Always adhere to the manufacturer’s dwell time to ensure true sanitization.
*   **Pitfall: Mixing Chemicals:** Never mix cleaning products, specifically ammonia and bleach. This can create toxic gases. Always refer to the SDS.
*   **Pitfall: Using Dirty Mops:** Using a graying or dirty mop water is ineffective. Change the mop bucket water frequently—at least once every two rooms or if the water becomes visibly murky.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should the cleaning cart be sanitized?**
A: The cleaning cart and all storage containers should be wiped down with disinfectant at the end of every shift to prevent the cart from becoming a source of cross-contamination.

**Q: What should I do if I encounter a chemical spill?**
A: Consult the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) immediately for spill response protocols. If it is a large spill, evacuate the area, notify your supervisor, and restrict access until the Hazmat response team arrives.

**Q: Can I use the same cloth for the bathroom and the desk area?**
A: Absolutely not. To maintain sanitation standards, cloths must be color-coded and disposed of or sent to professional laundering immediately after use in a high-risk area.

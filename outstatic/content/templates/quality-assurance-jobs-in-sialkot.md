# Standard Operating Procedure: Quality Assurance Protocol for Sialkot Export Manufacturing

As a global hub for sports goods, surgical instruments, and leather apparel, Sialkot requires a rigorous approach to Quality Assurance (QA) to maintain international compliance and brand reputation. This SOP outlines the professional standard for conducting on-site QA inspections to ensure that products meet stringent ISO, ASTM, or client-specific specifications before they are cleared for international export.

## Phase 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Documentation Review:** Obtain and verify the approved Purchase Order (PO), Product Specification Sheet, and the client’s "Golden Sample" (approved reference unit).
*   **Scheduling & Logistics:** Confirm the production completion percentage with the factory manager. Inspections should only occur when at least 80% of goods are packed.
*   **Equipment Calibration:** Ensure all inspection tools (calipers, digital scales, color matching cabinets, and light meters) are calibrated according to local Sialkot certification standards.
*   **Communication:** Confirm the factory’s facility address and access permissions for the inspection team.

## Phase 2: On-Site Inspection Execution
*   **Random Sampling (AQL):** Implement the ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 sampling plan. Randomly select cartons from the warehouse floor—do not allow factory staff to select the samples for you.
*   **Visual/Aesthetic Inspection:**
    *   Inspect for stitching defects, leather blemishes, or surgical instrument finish irregularities (burrs/oxidation).
    *   Check for color consistency using a standardized light box.
*   **Functional Testing:**
    *   **Sports Goods:** Perform durability tests (impact testing, tensile strength).
    *   **Surgical Instruments:** Conduct stress tests and ensure hinge tension meets medical standards.
    *   **Packaging:** Verify that barcodes are scannable and labeling meets destination country regulations (e.g., CE/FDA markings).
*   **Measurement Verification:** Utilize precision tools to verify that dimensions match the technical drawings provided in the specs.

## Phase 3: Reporting and Disposition
*   **Defect Categorization:** Classify findings as Critical (safety risk), Major (functional failure), or Minor (cosmetic imperfection).
*   **Report Generation:** Compile the inspection report including high-resolution photos of both pass/fail criteria and findings.
*   **Final Decision:** Issue a status of "Pass," "Pending," or "Fail." If the batch fails, document the corrective action plan (CAP) required by the factory.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain an objective relationship with factory staff. While professional, avoid being overly casual to ensure you retain authority during the inspection.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always carry your own independent light source and color swatch books; do not rely on the factory’s internal lighting, which may be manipulated to hide imperfections.
*   **Pitfall - The "Pre-selection" Trap:** Never accept a pre-arranged stack of "perfect" units from the factory manager. Always insist on choosing from the total inventory yourself to ensure true random sampling.
*   **Pitfall - Overlooking Packaging:** Many Sialkot exporters lose shipments due to poor moisture control (silica gel) or weak palletization; always inspect the packing environment for dampness.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the most critical factor when inspecting surgical instruments in Sialkot?**
A: Material traceability and surface passivation. Ensure the stainless steel grade (e.g., AISI 410/420) is verified through laboratory testing records, as raw material substitution is a common risk.

**Q: How do I handle a factory that refuses access to the full inventory?**
A: Immediately document the refusal in your preliminary report and notify your client. A lack of transparency during the inspection phase is a red flag for quality issues and should be escalated as a breach of the production contract.

**Q: Are there specific local regulations I should be aware of?**
A: Yes. Ensure all goods are compliant with the Sialkot Chamber of Commerce and Industry (SCCI) export standards, particularly regarding environmental compliance and ethical labor practices, which are increasingly scrutinized by EU and North American buyers.

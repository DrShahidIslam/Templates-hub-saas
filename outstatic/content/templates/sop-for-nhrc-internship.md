# Standard Operating Procedure: NHRC Internship Application & Execution

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for students and researchers applying for and participating in the internship programs at the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) of India. The NHRC internship is a highly competitive, merit-based program aimed at sensitizing students to human rights issues. This document outlines the professional requirements, administrative expectations, and procedural milestones necessary to successfully secure and complete an internship tenure.

## Section 1: Pre-Application & Documentation
*   **Verify Eligibility:** Ensure current enrollment in a recognized university for a degree in Law, Human Rights, Political Science, Sociology, Social Work, or Criminology.
*   **Monitor Notifications:** Regularly visit the official [NHRC India website](https://nhrc.nic.in/) under the "Internship" section for official circulars.
*   **Prepare Digital Dossier:**
    *   Scanned copy of the University/College ID card.
    *   No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the Head of the Department or Dean.
    *   Updated Curriculum Vitae (CV) highlighting human rights interest.
    *   Statement of Purpose (SOP) (500 words) articulating your interest in human rights.
*   **Submission:** Complete the online application form within the stipulated deadline. Do not wait for the final date to avoid server congestion.

## Section 2: Selection & Post-Selection Protocols
*   **Merit Review:** Acknowledge that the selection is purely based on academic performance and the quality of the submitted SOP.
*   **Formal Acceptance:** If selected, download the call letter from the website/email. Confirm acceptance via the specified portal or email within 48 hours.
*   **Logistics Planning:** If the internship is physical, arrange for accommodation in New Delhi well in advance. If hybrid/virtual, ensure a stable internet connection and a distraction-free workspace.
*   **Security Clearance:** Ensure you have government-issued ID proofs ready for entry into the NHRC office premises.

## Section 3: Internship Execution & Reporting
*   **Orientation Attendance:** Mandatory participation in the induction program; take detailed notes on the organizational hierarchy and mandate.
*   **Research Projects:** Begin literature reviews on the assigned topic immediately. Utilize the NHRC library or digital archives.
*   **Field Visits:** If assigned a field visit (e.g., to a prison or shelter home), maintain professional decorum, follow all safety instructions, and keep an incident log.
*   **Daily Reporting:** Maintain a daily log of tasks performed to facilitate the final report submission.
*   **Final Project Submission:** Draft a comprehensive report on the assigned research project; ensure all citations are in Bluebook or APA format.

## Section 4: Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip 1 (The SOP):** When writing your Statement of Purpose, focus on specific human rights issues (e.g., rights of the elderly, manual scavenging, or prisoner rights) rather than generic statements.
*   **Pro Tip 2 (Professionalism):** During the internship, treat every staff interaction as a networking opportunity. Be proactive in asking for feedback on your research drafts.
*   **Pitfall 1 (Plagiarism):** NHRC maintains a zero-tolerance policy for plagiarism. Ensure all academic work is original.
*   **Pitfall 2 (Attendance):** Maintain 100% attendance. Unexplained absences will result in the withholding of the internship certificate and stipend (if applicable).

## Section 5: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Is the NHRC internship paid?**
Yes, the NHRC provides a stipend to interns for the duration of the internship, provided they complete the program successfully and meet all attendance requirements.

**2. Can I apply if I am in my first year of college?**
Generally, the internship is reserved for students in the final years of their undergraduate or postgraduate programs. Please check the specific circular for the current cycle to confirm eligibility.

**3. What is the dress code for a physical internship?**
The dress code is formal business attire. As it is a government institution, participants are expected to dress modestly and professionally.

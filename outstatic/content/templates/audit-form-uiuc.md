# Standard Operating Procedure: Audit Form Management (UIUC)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for managing, reviewing, and submitting audit forms within the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) framework. This document ensures compliance with institutional policies, maintains data integrity for internal audits, and minimizes processing delays for administrative approvals. Personnel responsible for departmental audit documentation must adhere to these steps to ensure all records meet the university's fiscal and operational standards.

## Phase 1: Pre-Submission Preparation
*   Verify the specific audit form version required for the current fiscal cycle via the UIUC Business and Financial Services (OBFS) website.
*   Gather all necessary supporting documentation, including original receipts, transaction logs, and internal approval memos.
*   Ensure all data entry points align with the University’s Chart of Accounts (COA) coding requirements.
*   Confirm that all signatories possess the delegated authority to approve the specific fund or project code involved.

## Phase 2: Data Entry and Documentation
*   Input the correct Account Number, Fund Code, and Organization Code (CFOP) into the appropriate form fields.
*   Attach scanned copies of supporting invoices in PDF/A format, ensuring clear legibility of dates and amounts.
*   Validate that the "Business Purpose" field provides a clear, concise justification for the audit item in alignment with UIUC fiscal policy.
*   Perform a secondary cross-check between the physical or digital source documents and the values entered into the form.

## Phase 3: Review and Authorization
*   Conduct a "self-audit" of the form to check for arithmetic discrepancies or missing mandatory fields.
*   Submit the draft to the department head or Principal Investigator (PI) for digital signature via the university's approved workflow platform (e.g., DocuSign or WorkflowGen).
*   Monitor the workflow status to ensure the form does not stall at the departmental approval stage.
*   Once authorized, save a copy of the final, signed PDF to the secure departmental repository for future reference.

## Phase 4: Final Submission and Retention
*   Submit the completed form to the relevant unit (e.g., Grants and Contracts or Division of Financial Operations) using the designated submission portal.
*   Document the submission date and the assigned tracking number for follow-up inquiries.
*   Retain copies of all audit-related documents according to the university’s Records Retention Schedule (typically 3–7 years, depending on the funding source).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Search" function in the UIUC portal to find the latest form templates; avoid using saved forms from previous semesters as policy updates occur frequently.
*   **Pro Tip:** If a form is complex, perform a "dry run" with your internal accounting officer before initiating the formal routing process to save time.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid using generic language like "Meeting" or "Supplies" in the business purpose field; use descriptive terms like "Lab supplies for research project X" to prevent audit flags.
*   **Pitfall:** Never use digital signatures that are not verified through the university’s single sign-on (SSO) credentials, as these will be rejected during a formal compliance audit.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Where can I find the most recent version of the UIUC Audit Form?**
A: All official, up-to-date audit forms are hosted on the Office of Business and Financial Services (OBFS) website or the specific departmental intranet portals under the "Forms" section.

**Q: What should I do if the audit form status remains "Pending" for more than 5 business days?**
A: First, check the workflow status to identify the current signatory. Send a polite, professional follow-up email to the person holding the form, noting the submission date and the impact of the delay on department operations.

**Q: Are there specific formatting requirements for supporting documentation?**
A: Yes. Documents should generally be submitted as black-and-white PDFs at 300 DPI. Ensure no sensitive PII (Personally Identifiable Information) or full credit card numbers are visible; redact them if necessary following university data privacy protocols.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Transportation & Logistics Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the systematic approach for managing transportation operations to ensure the safe, timely, and cost-effective movement of goods. This document applies to all logistics staff, dispatchers, and external freight partners, serving as the baseline for operational excellence, regulatory compliance, and consistent service delivery. By following these protocols, the organization mitigates transit risks, optimizes fuel efficiency, and maintains high standards of customer satisfaction.

## Phase 1: Pre-Departure Planning & Documentation
*   **Order Verification:** Confirm shipment details, including weight, dimensions, hazardous material classifications, and special handling requirements (e.g., temperature control).
*   **Route Planning:** Utilize logistics software to identify the most efficient route, accounting for road closures, construction, and traffic patterns.
*   **Documentation Audit:** Ensure the Bill of Lading (BOL), commercial invoice, insurance certificates, and proof of delivery (POD) forms are digitized and ready for the driver.
*   **Carrier Selection:** Select a pre-vetted carrier based on equipment availability, proximity to origin, and historical performance metrics.

## Phase 2: Loading & Safety Compliance
*   **Vehicle Inspection:** Complete a visual inspection of the transport unit (tire pressure, lighting, brakes, cargo securement equipment) using the standard DVIR (Driver Vehicle Inspection Report).
*   **Loading Protocol:** Supervise the loading process to ensure weight distribution adheres to legal bridge-law standards and that cargo is braced to prevent shifting.
*   **Seal Verification:** Apply a tamper-evident security seal to the trailer and record the serial number on the shipping documents.
*   **Safety Briefing:** Verify the driver has completed a pre-trip safety check and is in possession of all necessary hazardous material placards, if applicable.

## Phase 3: Transit Monitoring & Communication
*   **Real-Time Tracking:** Utilize GPS telematics to monitor the vehicle’s progress against the established ETA.
*   **Exception Management:** Immediately notify the Operations Manager of any unforeseen delays (accidents, weather, mechanical failure) and execute the contingency rerouting plan.
*   **Driver Check-ins:** Establish mandatory communication windows (e.g., every 4 hours or at major junctions) for status updates.

## Phase 4: Arrival & Post-Trip Reconciliation
*   **Proof of Delivery (POD) Validation:** Upon arrival, ensure the consignee inspects the freight for damage before signing the BOL. Any discrepancies must be documented with photos immediately.
*   **Offloading Supervision:** Ensure offloading occurs according to site-specific safety protocols.
*   **Document Closure:** Finalize all digital paperwork and upload the signed POD into the Transportation Management System (TMS) within 24 hours.
*   **Performance Review:** Debrief the driver or carrier on any issues encountered to update the carrier scorecard.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always maintain a "buffer zone" in your ETA calculations. Accounting for a 10% contingency time window prevents missed appointment fees.
*   **Pro Tip:** Digitizing your PODs via a mobile app reduces billing cycles by an average of 4-6 days.
*   **Pitfall:** Overloading trailers. Always cross-reference the scale ticket against the manifest to avoid heavy fines and safety violations.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "deadhead" miles. Failing to coordinate return loads significantly erodes your operating margin.

## FAQ
**Q: What should I do if a shipment is damaged in transit?**
A: Immediately document the damage with high-resolution photos, have the driver note the damage on the BOL before the consignee signs, and file a formal claim with the carrier’s insurance department within the contractually mandated timeframe (usually 48–72 hours).

**Q: How do we handle unexpected road closures during transit?**
A: The dispatch team must utilize real-time traffic data (such as Waze for Business or PC*Miler) to suggest an alternate route. If the detour adds significant mileage, calculate the fuel surcharge impact before authorizing the change.

**Q: What constitutes a "High-Priority" shipment?**
A: Any shipment that involves JIT (Just-in-Time) manufacturing, medical supplies, or high-value electronics requires active GPS tracking and direct communication with the driver every 60 minutes.

# Standard Operating Procedure: MBA Admissions Lifecycle

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized framework for managing the end-to-end MBA admission process. It is designed to ensure operational excellence, maintain high data integrity, and provide a seamless experience for prospective candidates. By following this protocol, the admissions team will consistently evaluate applicants against institutional criteria while optimizing conversion rates and maintaining compliance with internal governance standards.

## Phase 1: Lead Management and Inquiry Handling
*   **Initial Engagement:** Acknowledge all incoming inquiries via the CRM within 24 business hours.
*   **Qualification:** Assess prospective candidates based on eligibility criteria (e.g., minimum years of work experience, undergraduate GPA, test score potential).
*   **Information Distribution:** Provide the "Admissions Handbook" and link to the application portal.
*   **Lead Nurturing:** Schedule follow-up outreach for candidates who have engaged with marketing materials but have not yet initiated an application.

## Phase 2: Application Processing and Documentation
*   **Verification:** Confirm receipt of official transcripts, test scores (GMAT/GRE/TOEFL/IELTS), and letters of recommendation (LORs).
*   **Quality Control:** Audit uploaded documents for authenticity and readability. Flag incomplete files for candidate notification.
*   **Application Fee Reconciliation:** Verify that the non-refundable application fee has been processed through the secure payment gateway.
*   **Status Update:** Move the applicant status to "Under Review" only once all mandatory documents are verified as complete.

## Phase 3: Evaluation and Interview Coordination
*   **Preliminary Screening:** Conduct a holistic review of the Statement of Purpose (SOP), resume, and academic history.
*   **Shortlisting:** Generate a shortlist of candidates based on predefined scorecards and institutional DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) targets.
*   **Interview Scheduling:** Utilize an automated scheduling tool to coordinate interviews between candidates and the admissions committee or alumni interviewers.
*   **Feedback Integration:** Standardize interview evaluation rubrics to ensure bias mitigation and consistent reporting across all interviewers.

## Phase 4: Final Decision and Enrollment
*   **Committee Review:** Compile all data points (Test scores, GPA, Essays, Interview feedback) for a final deliberation.
*   **Communication:** Distribute official decision letters (Admit, Waitlist, Deny) via the secure student portal.
*   **Enrollment Deposit:** Monitor the portal for incoming enrollment deposits within the specified deadline.
*   **Onboarding:** Transition admitted students to the "Admitted Student" workflow, including housing assistance, visa processing (for international students), and course registration.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Personalization is Key.** Use custom tokens in your email automation. Mentioning specific program highlights or faculty research that aligns with the applicant's goals drastically increases yield rates.
*   **Pro Tip: Audit the Pipeline.** Monthly data audits are essential to identify "stuck" applications. Identify at which stage candidates drop off to improve messaging.
*   **Pitfall: Manual Errors.** Never rely on spreadsheets for tracking. Use a dedicated CRM to ensure automated triggers and avoid human errors in deadline tracking.
*   **Pitfall: Communication Silos.** Lack of transparency between the Admissions and Financial Aid departments can lead to confusion. Ensure both teams operate on the same data set.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How do we handle discrepancies in unofficial versus official transcripts?**
Conditional admission may be granted based on unofficial documents; however, enrollment must be contingent upon receiving official, sealed transcripts by the start of the first term.

**2. What is the protocol if an applicant requests a deadline extension?**
Exceptions should only be granted in documented cases of technical failure or extreme personal hardship. All extensions must be approved by the Director of Admissions.

**3. How do we ensure fairness during the committee review process?**
Implement a "Blind Review" protocol for the first round of evaluation, where reviewers do not see the names or demographic data of candidates until the final committee discussion.

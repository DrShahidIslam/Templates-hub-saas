# Standard Operating Procedure: FSC Chain of Custody Audit Preparation

The Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) Chain of Custody (CoC) audit is a rigorous verification process designed to ensure that certified forest products are tracked from the forest to the end consumer. As an operations manager, maintaining compliance requires consistent documentation, clear product segregation, and staff training. This SOP and accompanying checklist serve as the primary framework for internal audit readiness, ensuring that your organization meets FSC-STD-40-004 requirements and maintains its certification status without major non-conformities.

## 1. Documentation & Recordkeeping
*   [ ] **FSC Certificate Validity:** Confirm the current FSC certificate is displayed in the office and available on the FSC Public Search portal.
*   [ ] **Product Group Schedule:** Review the latest scope of products; ensure all materials currently processed or sold as "FSC Certified" are explicitly listed on your scope.
*   [ ] **Supplier Verification:** Verify that all suppliers of FSC-certified input materials provide valid FSC certificates (check the FSC Info website for expiry dates).
*   [ ] **Invoice Integrity:** Audit a sample of purchase invoices to ensure the FSC claim (e.g., "FSC 100%", "FSC Mix Credit") is clearly stated for each certified input.
*   [ ] **Training Records:** Ensure all staff involved in handling or selling FSC-certified products have signed training logs confirming their understanding of FSC procedures.

## 2. Inventory Management & Segregation
*   [ ] **Physical Labeling:** Confirm that raw materials and finished goods are clearly marked with FSC status in the warehouse (e.g., signs, tags, or pallet stickers).
*   [ ] **Segregation Protocol:** Verify that non-certified materials are physically separated or temporally separated from FSC-certified materials to prevent cross-contamination.
*   [ ] **Conversion Factors:** Review established conversion rates (e.g., volume of logs to board feet) and ensure calculations are consistent and documented.
*   [ ] **Credit Account Audits:** If using a "Credit System," ensure the credit account balance is accurate and that credits have not been overdrawn.

## 3. Sales & Outbound Logistics
*   [ ] **Sales Documentation:** Review outgoing invoices/delivery notes to ensure they display the organization’s FSC certificate code and the correct FSC claim for the products sold.
*   [ ] **Trademark Compliance:** Verify that the use of the FSC logo on invoices, marketing materials, or product labels strictly follows the FSC Trademark Standards (FSC-STD-50-001).
*   [ ] **Non-Certified Sales:** Ensure that products not meeting FSC requirements are never sold as FSC-certified.

## 4. Internal Review & Corrective Actions
*   [ ] **Internal Audit Completion:** Ensure an annual internal audit has been performed and documented, including a summary of findings.
*   [ ] **Non-Conformity Tracking:** Review the log of any non-conformities identified during the previous audit and verify that "Corrective Action Reports" (CARs) have been closed.
*   [ ] **Management Review:** Conduct a documented management meeting reviewing the FSC system performance and signature of approval.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "Certification Binder" (digital or physical) that contains all certificates, training logs, and purchase records. Auditors prefer organized systems over searching through disparate folders.
*   **Pro Tip:** Perform "Traceability Exercises" regularly. Pick a finished product from the warehouse and trace it backward to the original raw material purchase invoice and supplier certificate.
*   **Pitfall:** Expiration dates. Many organizations lose their certification status simply because a supplier's certificate expired, and the organization failed to update their supplier list. 
*   **Pitfall:** Trademark misuse. Do not use the FSC logo on marketing materials without submitting a trademark approval request to your certification body.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**What is the most common reason for a "Major Non-Conformity" during an audit?**
Failure to maintain accurate volume records (mass balance) is the most frequent cause. If your input volume does not correlate logically with your output volume, the auditor will flag this as a critical failure.

**Can we ship FSC products if our certificate is expired but under renewal?**
No. Once your certificate expires, you must immediately stop selling, labeling, or invoicing any products as FSC-certified. You may only resume once the new certificate is officially issued by your certification body.

**How far back should our records go for an audit?**
Generally, auditors will request to see records spanning from the date of the previous audit to the present. It is industry best practice to keep all FSC-related documentation for a minimum of five years.

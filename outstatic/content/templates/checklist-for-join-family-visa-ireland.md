# Standard Operating Procedure: Join Family Visa (Ireland)

This document serves as a comprehensive guide for individuals applying for an Irish "Join Family" visa. Following these procedures is critical to ensuring your application meets the stringent requirements set by the Irish Naturalisation and Immigration Service (INIS). Failure to provide complete documentation or clear evidence of relationship and financial sustainability often leads to summary refusal. This SOP outlines the necessary steps to prepare, verify, and submit your application with maximum efficiency and compliance.

## 1. Documentation Preparation (Evidence of Relationship)
Before commencing the online application, ensure all primary documentation is compiled, verified, and translated (where applicable).

*   **Proof of Identity:** Valid passport for both the sponsor and applicant (copy of all pages).
*   **Marriage/Birth Certificates:** Original legal documents proving the relationship (certified translations required if not in English).
*   **Evidence of Relationship:** Photos, social media history, flight receipts, and correspondence logs to prove a subsisting relationship.
*   **Sponsor’s Status:** Copy of the sponsor’s Irish Passport, GNIB card, or IRP card.

## 2. Financial Sustainability & Accommodation
The Department of Justice requires proof that the sponsor can support the applicant without recourse to public funds.

*   **Employment Details:** Sponsor’s employment contract and an original letter from the employer confirming current employment status.
*   **Proof of Earnings:** P60/P21 balancing statements for the previous three years and the most recent six months of payslips.
*   **Bank Statements:** Six months of original bank statements (stamped and signed by the bank) for the sponsor.
*   **Accommodation Proof:** Evidence of housing adequacy (e.g., lease agreement, utility bills, or a letter from the landlord/homeowner).

## 3. Online Application & Submission
Once documentation is audit-ready, proceed to the formal application portal.

*   **AVATS System:** Complete the online application at the [AVATS portal](https://www.visas.inis.gov.ie/). Ensure all data matches your supporting documents exactly.
*   **Summary Sheet:** Print and sign the Summary Application Sheet generated after submission.
*   **Fee Payment:** Pay the required visa fee (note: some nationalities are exempt; check the current fee schedule).
*   **Submission of Documents:** Follow the specific instructions provided on the summary sheet regarding where to send physical documents (e.g., the local embassy or the Dublin Visa Office).

## 4. Post-Submission Logistics
*   **Document Tracking:** Always use a tracked courier service when sending original documents to ensure proof of delivery.
*   **Monitor Status:** Keep the AVATS reference number secure to check for updates on the official website.
*   **Biometrics:** If required, ensure you attend the biometric appointment at the designated center promptly.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The "Genuine Relationship" Hurdle:** Do not simply submit a marriage certificate. Include a narrative document (a "cover letter") detailing the history of your relationship. A high-quality cover letter is often the deciding factor in borderline cases.
*   **Missing Pages:** A common pitfall is failing to photocopy every page of the sponsor's passport, including blank pages. Always submit a complete copy.
*   **Originals vs. Copies:** While some documents can be copies, the Immigration Office often requests originals for key documents like bank statements and marriage certificates. Ensure these are identifiable and organized.
*   **Translation Fraud:** Ensure all translations are performed by a professional, certified translator. Do not use informal or self-translations, as these will lead to automatic rejection.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How long does the visa processing take?**
A: Processing times vary significantly based on the volume of applications and the complexity of the case. It typically ranges from 6 to 12 months, but you should consult the latest processing updates on the Irish Immigration Service website for current timelines.

**Q: Do I need to be in Ireland while my family member applies?**
A: Generally, the sponsor must already have established residency in Ireland and have the appropriate immigration permission before the family member applies for the Join Family visa.

**Q: Can I travel to Ireland while the visa application is pending?**
A: It is strongly discouraged. If the applicant holds a passport that allows for visa-free travel (e.g., US, Canada), they may technically travel, but it can complicate the application. If the applicant requires a visa to enter Ireland, they must wait for the Join Family visa to be granted before traveling.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance for Biosafety Cabinets (BSC)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory preventive maintenance (PM) protocols for Class II Biosafety Cabinets. Regular maintenance is critical to ensure personnel protection, product sterility, and environmental safety. All procedures must be performed by qualified personnel or certified third-party technicians in accordance with NSF/ANSI 49 standards. Failure to adhere to these intervals may compromise containment integrity and invalidate regulatory compliance.

## Pre-Inspection Safety Protocols
*   Ensure the cabinet is empty of all biohazardous agents and chemical reagents.
*   Perform a thorough surface decontamination using an appropriate disinfectant (e.g., 70% ethanol or specialized sporicidal agent).
*   Verify that the sash is in the correct operating position as specified by the manufacturer.
*   Confirm the presence of all required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including gloves, lab coat, and safety goggles.

## Step 1: Physical and Structural Inspection
*   Inspect the sash for smooth operation, checking for frayed cables, damaged tracks, or uneven movement.
*   Examine the work surface and side walls for signs of corrosion, pitting, or structural damage.
*   Check all exterior panels and the support stand for stability and signs of rust or mechanical fatigue.
*   Verify the integrity of the cabinet seals, ensuring no gaps exist at joints or utility ports.

## Step 2: Airflow and Mechanical System Performance
*   Measure inflow velocity using a calibrated anemometer to ensure it meets manufacturer specifications (typically 100 fpm for Class II, Type A2).
*   Perform a smoke pattern test to verify unidirectional downward airflow and proper containment at the sash opening.
*   Inspect the blower motor for unusual vibration or excessive noise, which may indicate bearing wear.
*   Check the HEPA filter pressure gauges (Magnehelic or digital) and document readings against the baseline provided during the previous certification.
*   Examine the plenum seals for integrity to prevent bypass of unfiltered air.

## Step 3: Electrical and Lighting Systems
*   Test the internal GFCI outlets for proper tripping and reset functionality.
*   Verify that the fluorescent lighting is providing adequate intensity across the entire work surface.
*   Ensure the Germicidal Ultraviolet (UV) lamp is operational and that the safety interlock (which prevents UV activation while the sash is open) is functioning correctly.
*   Examine the power cord and plug for fraying or heat damage.

## Step 4: Final Certification and Documentation
*   Apply the updated "Certified" sticker to the front panel with the date of inspection and the technician’s identification.
*   Document all airflow readings, filter integrity test results (DOP/PAO test), and mechanical observations in the equipment logbook.
*   Submit the formal Field Service Report to the Facilities or Lab Management department for archival.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always schedule your annual certification 30 days before the current sticker expires to account for any necessary repairs that might take the unit out of service.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a secondary manual log of the Magnehelic gauge readings every time you start the cabinet; this helps identify filter loading patterns before a total failure occurs.
*   **Pitfall:** Never block the front air intake grill with items such as paper towels or pipette tip boxes. This is the #1 cause of airflow turbulence and containment breach.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid over-cleaning the HEPA filter face; these are extremely delicate and can be punctured by simple contact with a cloth or cleaning tool.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often must a BSC undergo formal certification?**
A: A BSC must be certified at the time of installation and at least annually thereafter. Recertification is also required if the cabinet is moved, or if major repairs (like motor or filter replacement) are performed.

**Q: Can we perform the annual certification in-house?**
A: While routine visual inspections can be performed in-house, the actual airflow testing, HEPA filter integrity testing (leak testing), and site installation assessment must be performed by an accredited professional using calibrated instruments.

**Q: What should I do if the alarm sounds during operation?**
A: Stop all work immediately, cover your materials, gently lower the sash, and notify your supervisor. Do not continue to use the cabinet until the alarm condition has been investigated and cleared by a technician.

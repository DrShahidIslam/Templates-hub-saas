# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Vehicle Inspection Process

This document establishes the standardized protocol for conducting a comprehensive vehicle safety and compliance inspection for Uber partner-drivers. Regular inspections are critical to ensuring driver-partner eligibility, maintaining platform safety standards, and preventing vehicle downtime. This SOP should be used in conjunction with the official Uber-provided Vehicle Inspection Form (VIF) to ensure all regulatory and brand requirements are satisfied.

## Section 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   Ensure the vehicle is parked on flat, level ground in a well-lit area.
*   Verify that the engine is cool (for fluid checks) and the parking brake is engaged.
*   Have the vehicle registration, proof of insurance, and the printed VIF (PDF) ready for documentation.
*   Gather necessary tools: flashlight, tire pressure gauge, and a cloth for cleaning mirrors/cameras.

## Section 2: Exterior & Structural Integrity
*   **Body Condition:** Inspect for significant dents, deep scratches, or damaged panels that impact the vehicle’s professional appearance.
*   **Lighting Systems:** Verify operation of headlights (low/high beam), taillights, brake lights, turn signals (front/rear), and hazard lights.
*   **Glass & Visibility:** Check the windshield for cracks or chips obstructing the driver’s view. Verify that all side mirrors and the rearview mirror are intact and adjustable.
*   **Tires:** Inspect tire tread depth (must exceed 2/32 inch). Check for bulges, uneven wear patterns, or sidewall damage. Confirm tire pressure matches manufacturer recommendations.

## Section 3: Interior & Safety Features
*   **Seat Belts:** Test the locking mechanism and retraction of all seat belts (front and back). Ensure the belts are free of fraying or cuts.
*   **Interior Controls:** Verify that the horn, windshield wipers, and HVAC (heating and cooling) systems are fully operational.
*   **Doors & Locks:** Confirm that all passenger doors open and close securely from both the inside and outside. Test the child-safety locks.
*   **Warning Indicators:** Start the engine and confirm that no "Check Engine," "Airbag," or "ABS" warning lights remain illuminated on the dashboard.

## Section 4: Documentation & Final Review
*   **VIN Verification:** Cross-reference the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) on the dashboard with the VIF PDF document.
*   **Certification:** Ensure the inspecting mechanic or authorized agent has signed, dated, and stamped the form where required by local law.
*   **Digital Storage:** Scan the completed, signed physical form and upload it to the Uber Partner Dashboard immediately to avoid account deactivation.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Conduct a "pre-inspection" 48 hours before your official appointment. This allows time to replace burnt-out bulbs or address minor issues that would cause a failure.
*   **Pro Tip:** Keep a clean vehicle; inspectors are more likely to approve vehicles that are well-maintained inside and out, as it suggests diligent ownership.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "Illegal" window tint. Many jurisdictions have strict VLT (Visible Light Transmission) percentages. If your tint is too dark, you will fail the inspection regardless of mechanical health.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring the "Check Engine" light. Even if the car drives fine, an active engine light is an automatic fail for Uber compliance.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Does my vehicle inspection form expire?**
A: Yes. Most jurisdictions require an annual inspection. Check your Uber Driver App under the "Documents" tab to see your specific expiration date.

**Q: Can I use any mechanic for the inspection?**
A: Uber typically requires inspections to be performed at a certified repair facility or an Uber Greenlight Hub. Always check the official list of approved inspection locations in your specific city before scheduling.

**Q: What happens if I fail the inspection?**
A: If your vehicle fails, you will receive a report detailing the necessary repairs. Once repairs are completed, you must return to an inspection facility to have the vehicle re-inspected and cleared before you can resume accepting trips.

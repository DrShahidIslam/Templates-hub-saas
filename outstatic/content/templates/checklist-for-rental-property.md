# Standard Operating Procedure: Rental Property Inspection & Turnover

Maintaining rental properties to a high standard is critical for asset protection, tenant retention, and legal compliance. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides a rigorous framework for conducting property inspections and managing the turnover process. By adhering to these protocols, property managers can minimize downtime, prevent disputes over security deposits, and ensure the habitability of the premises for incoming residents.

## Phase 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Documentation Review:** Retrieve the original move-in inspection report and photographs for comparison.
*   **Notice Delivery:** Ensure proper legal notice (as per state/local law) has been provided to the outgoing tenant.
*   **Tool Kit Assembly:** Gather a camera/smartphone, cleaning checklist, keys, master lockbox, and testing tools (outlet tester, moisture meter).
*   **Utility Coordination:** Verify utility transfer dates to ensure electricity and water remain active for inspections and cleaning.

## Phase 2: Interior Assessment (Room-by-Room)
*   **Walls & Ceilings:** Check for holes, scuffs, water stains, or peeling paint.
*   **Flooring:** Inspect for carpet stains, tears, hardwood scratches, or cracked tiles.
*   **Doors & Windows:** Test all locks, handles, latches, and window screens for smooth operation.
*   **Electrical:** Use an outlet tester to verify all sockets work; check all light fixtures and wall switches.
*   **Plumbing:** Run taps to check water pressure and drainage; inspect under sinks for leaks or moisture.

## Phase 3: Kitchen & Bathroom Utilities
*   **Appliances:** Run the dishwasher, test the stove/oven, and ensure the refrigerator is cooling properly.
*   **Ventilation:** Confirm bathroom exhaust fans and stove hood vents are drawing air effectively.
*   **Toilets:** Flush to check for proper cycling, handle tension, and base leaks.
*   **Cabinets:** Check hinges and drawer glides for alignment and damage.

## Phase 4: Exterior & Safety Systems
*   **Safety Devices:** Test all smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms; replace batteries as required by law.
*   **HVAC:** Change air filters and confirm the thermostat controls heating/cooling stages accurately.
*   **Exterior Grounds:** Inspect siding, gutters, fences, and ensure entryways are clear of debris.
*   **Landscaping:** Confirm that irrigation systems are functional and greenery is trimmed away from the structure.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Photo Evidence" Rule:** Never rely on memory. Take high-resolution photos of any damage that will be deducted from a security deposit to protect against litigation.
*   **Pitfall - The "Hidden" Leak:** Always check the interior of kitchen cabinets under the sink. Tenants often ignore slow drips that cause significant mold and water damage over time.
*   **Pro Tip - Pre-Move-In Walkthrough:** Conduct a "pre-move-in" inspection with the new tenant present. This sets expectations for cleanliness and documents the condition before their belongings arrive.
*   **Pitfall - Ignoring Codes:** Don’t assume the previous maintenance was up to code. Check local ordinances for smoke alarm placement requirements, as these change frequently.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long should I keep inspection records?**
A: You should retain digital and physical copies of inspection reports and photos for at least three to seven years after a tenant moves out, depending on your local statute of limitations for contract disputes.

**Q: What is the best way to handle minor scuffs on walls?**
A: Maintain a supply of the specific paint brand and color code used in the property. Touching up high-traffic areas during turnover is significantly cheaper than a full repainting cycle.

**Q: Should I allow the tenant to perform their own move-out cleaning?**
A: Yes, but provide them with a "Move-Out Cleaning Expectations" document. This prevents ambiguity and reduces the time your professional cleaners need to spend on-site, saving you money on the turnover.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Building Preventive Maintenance

## Purpose and Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory protocol for conducting routine preventive maintenance (PM) within our facility. The objective of this program is to ensure operational continuity, extend the lifespan of critical assets, maintain occupant safety, and mitigate the risk of catastrophic system failure. This document applies to all maintenance staff and third-party contractors responsible for the building’s physical infrastructure and mechanical systems. Strict adherence to this schedule is required to maintain compliance with safety codes and organizational standards.

---

## Preventive Maintenance Checklist

## 1. HVAC and Air Quality Systems
*   **Filter Inspection:** Inspect all air handling unit (AHU) and fan coil unit (FCU) filters; replace if dirty or according to quarterly schedule.
*   **Coil Cleaning:** Clean evaporator and condenser coils to ensure maximum thermal efficiency.
*   **Belt Tension:** Check drive belts for wear, tension, and alignment; replace frayed belts immediately.
*   **Drain Pans:** Ensure condensate pans are free of debris and drainage lines are clear to prevent water damage or microbial growth.
*   **Calibration:** Verify thermostat and sensor calibration against a calibrated handheld thermometer.

## 2. Electrical and Lighting Systems
*   **Panel Inspections:** Visually inspect electrical distribution panels for signs of overheating, loose connections, or buzzing sounds.
*   **Emergency Lighting:** Conduct monthly discharge tests on all emergency exit signs and battery-backed lighting fixtures.
*   **Outdoor Lighting:** Inspect parking lot and exterior perimeter lighting; replace non-functional photocells or LEDs.
*   **Grounding:** Verify that all equipment grounding connections are secure and free of corrosion.

## 3. Plumbing and Water Systems
*   **Leak Detection:** Inspect all exposed piping, valves, and joints in mechanical rooms and restrooms for signs of seepage or corrosion.
*   **Backflow Prevention:** Review certification status for backflow preventers to ensure annual testing is up to date.
*   **Drain Maintenance:** Treat floor drains with biological cleaners to prevent odors and sludge buildup.
*   **Water Heaters:** Inspect pressure relief valves and flush tanks to remove sediment buildup (semi-annual).

## 4. Life Safety and Security
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Perform monthly visual inspections, ensuring gauges are in the "green" zone and pins/seals are intact.
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test device functionality and clear sensors of dust.
*   **Door Hardware:** Check fire-rated door closers, panic bars, and latching mechanisms for smooth operation and compliance.
*   **Exit Paths:** Ensure no obstructions are present in hallways, stairwells, or near exterior exits.

## 5. Building Envelope and Grounds
*   **Roofing:** Clear debris from roof drains, scuppers, and gutters; inspect flashing for cracks or peeling.
*   **Windows/Seals:** Inspect caulking around exterior window frames for gaps; address as needed to maintain thermal envelope.
*   **Exterior Surfaces:** Evaluate masonry, siding, and parking surfaces for cracks, trip hazards, or water pooling issues.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Predictive Maintenance:** Use infrared thermography on electrical panels during peak loads to detect hotspots before a failure occurs.
*   **Standardization:** Use a Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) to digitize these checklists and automate recurring work orders.
*   **Documentation:** Always log the "as-found" and "as-left" condition of equipment. This data is invaluable for warranty claims and insurance audits.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **The "Check-the-Box" Mentality:** Simply marking an item as complete without physical verification leads to systemic failure. Technicians must provide specific notes for any discrepancies.
*   **Ignoring Unusual Sounds:** Never overlook a strange noise or vibration; in 90% of cases, it is the primary indicator of an imminent mechanical failure.
*   **Bypassing Safety:** Never attempt maintenance on electrical or high-pressure systems without performing proper Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should this full checklist be completed?**
While specific items have monthly or semi-annual intervals, a comprehensive walk-through should occur at least once per month to ensure no system is overlooked.

**2. What should I do if I find a defect that requires parts not currently in stock?**
Document the defect, take photos, and immediately initiate an emergency work order. Secure the area if the defect poses a safety risk and inform the Facility Manager for expedited procurement.

**3. Is it necessary to hire outside contractors for these tasks?**
Routine inspections can often be handled in-house. However, specialized tasks—such as elevator servicing, fire suppression system testing, or backflow certification—must be performed by certified, licensed professionals to remain compliant with local building codes.

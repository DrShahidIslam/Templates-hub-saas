# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance (PM) Schedule Adherence

The objective of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to ensure that all critical assets, machinery, and facilities equipment undergo preventive maintenance (PM) in accordance with the established operational schedule. Strict adherence to these protocols minimizes unplanned downtime, extends the lifecycle of capital equipment, reduces emergency repair costs, and ensures compliance with safety and regulatory standards. This document provides a structured framework for scheduling, executing, and documenting all maintenance activities to maintain operational continuity.

## Phase 1: Planning and Scheduling

*   **Audit Asset Inventory:** Confirm that all assets are tracked in the Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) with unique identifiers and maintenance requirements.
*   **Generate Schedule:** Utilize the CMMS to auto-generate work orders based on time-based intervals (e.g., quarterly) or usage-based triggers (e.g., engine hours).
*   **Resource Allocation:** Verify the availability of required spare parts, specialized tools, and certified personnel at least 72 hours prior to the scheduled maintenance date.
*   **Coordinate Downtime:** Liaise with department heads to schedule maintenance windows that minimize impact on production workflows.

## Phase 2: Execution and Compliance

*   **Work Order Issuance:** Distribute digital or physical work orders to the assigned technician, ensuring all safety protocols (Lock-Out/Tag-Out) are included.
*   **Site Preparation:** Ensure the area is cleared of debris and that all safety barriers are installed before work begins.
*   **Execution:** Perform tasks strictly according to the manufacturer’s service manual and internal standard operating procedures.
*   **Inspection and Testing:** Conduct a post-maintenance functionality test to verify that the equipment operates within performance benchmarks before returning it to service.

## Phase 3: Documentation and Audit

*   **Completion Recording:** Log the completion date, technician name, parts used, and any findings (e.g., unusual wear, vibration) in the CMMS immediately upon finish.
*   **Data Verification:** Review the completed work order for accuracy to ensure the PM cycle is correctly reset for the next interval.
*   **Performance Reporting:** Generate a monthly "PM Adherence Report" to identify gaps between planned tasks and completed tasks.
*   **Root Cause Analysis (RCA):** Conduct an RCA for any missed or delayed PM activities to identify systemic failures (e.g., part shortages or staffing gaps).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a "Buffer Period" for non-critical assets (e.g., +/- 3 days) to account for production surges, while maintaining a strict 0-day tolerance for safety-critical systems.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use visual management (e.g., color-coded tags on machines) to show at a glance if a machine is current or overdue for maintenance.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-reliance on reactive maintenance. Avoid the "if it isn't broken, don't fix it" mindset, as this typically leads to higher long-term costs and safety hazards.
*   **Pitfall:** Poor documentation. If a maintenance task is completed but not recorded, it is considered "not done" for audit and compliance purposes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is an acceptable benchmark for PM adherence?**
A: Industry best practice is an adherence rate of 90% or higher. Anything below 85% suggests a systemic issue with scheduling, resource allocation, or labor availability.

**Q: How should we handle emergency breakdowns that interfere with scheduled PM?**
A: Emergency repairs must take priority for safety and production. However, the missed PM must be rescheduled within 48 hours to prevent the accumulation of maintenance backlog.

**Q: Who is responsible for verifying that a PM was done correctly?**
A: The assigned technician performs the work, but a supervisor or lead technician must conduct a "QA Spot Check" on at least 10% of all PM work orders to ensure standard compliance.

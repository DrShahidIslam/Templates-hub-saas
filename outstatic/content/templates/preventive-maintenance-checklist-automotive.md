# Standard Operating Procedure: Automotive Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for performing routine preventive maintenance on fleet or personal vehicles. The primary objective is to maximize vehicle longevity, ensure operator safety, and minimize the risk of costly mechanical failures. Adherence to this checklist facilitates consistent service quality and provides an auditable trail of vehicle health documentation.

## Phase 1: Under-Hood Inspection
*   **Engine Oil:** Check dipstick for level and clarity. Replace oil and filter according to manufacturer intervals (typically every 3,000–7,500 miles).
*   **Coolant System:** Inspect coolant levels in the reservoir. Check hoses for cracks, bulges, or signs of leaks. Ensure the radiator cap seal is intact.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Verify fluid level in the master cylinder. Test for contamination (dark color or moisture content).
*   **Power Steering Fluid:** Inspect reservoir level and check the pump for unusual noises or leaks.
*   **Battery:** Inspect terminals for corrosion. Ensure cable connections are tight. Perform a load test if the battery is over three years old.
*   **Air Filter:** Inspect the engine air filter for debris or clogs. Replace if light does not pass through the element.
*   **Belts:** Check the serpentine/drive belt for fraying, glazing, or missing teeth.

## Phase 2: Under-Vehicle & Suspension Inspection
*   **Exhaust System:** Inspect the muffler, catalytic converter, and pipes for rust holes, hanging brackets, or excessive soot buildup.
*   **Suspension Components:** Examine struts, shocks, and bushings for leakage or physical damage.
*   **Drivetrain:** Check CV boots and drive shaft seals for grease spray or tears.
*   **Tires:** Measure tread depth (minimum 2/32"). Check sidewalls for dry rot, bulges, or puncture hazards. Rotate tires if necessary.
*   **Brakes (Physical):** Check brake pad thickness (minimum 3mm required). Inspect rotors for scoring or warping.

## Phase 3: Exterior & Lighting
*   **Headlights/Taillights:** Test high/low beams, turn signals, brake lights, and reverse lights.
*   **Wipers:** Inspect blades for streaking or rubber degradation. Ensure washer fluid spray is aimed correctly.
*   **Mirrors:** Verify stability and functionality of adjustment motors.

## Phase 4: Interior & Safety Systems
*   **Dashboard Warnings:** Confirm no active check engine, ABS, or airbag lights are present.
*   **Safety Belts:** Ensure retraction mechanism functions correctly and buckles latch securely.
*   **Horn:** Test for clear audible output.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Oil Samples):** For high-mileage or commercial vehicles, perform an oil analysis every 10,000 miles to detect microscopic internal wear before it results in a breakdown.
*   **Pro Tip (Documentation):** Always reset the vehicle's maintenance light and log the date, mileage, and parts used in a digital maintenance management system (CMMS).
*   **Pitfall (Over-tightening):** Never over-torque drain plugs or wheel lugs. Always use a calibrated torque wrench to prevent stripped threads or warped rotors.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring "Minor" Leaks):** A small coolant seep is often the precursor to a major hose failure. Address small leaks immediately to prevent mid-trip engine overheating.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform this comprehensive checklist?**
A: This full inspection should be conducted every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, or at least every six months, whichever comes first.

**Q: Should I use synthetic or conventional oil?**
A: Always refer to your vehicle’s Owner’s Manual. Synthetic oil generally offers better thermal stability and longevity, but only if it meets the specific viscosity and manufacturer certification requirements for your engine.

**Q: What is the most common cause of roadside breakdowns?**
A: Electrical/battery failures and tire-related issues are statistically the most common causes. Prioritizing battery terminal health and tire pressure/condition significantly reduces your risk of being stranded.

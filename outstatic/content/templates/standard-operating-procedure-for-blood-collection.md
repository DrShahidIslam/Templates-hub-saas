# Standard Operating Procedure: Venipuncture and Blood Collection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the systematic process for the safe and efficient collection of venous blood samples. Adherence to these protocols is mandatory to ensure patient safety, minimize the risk of pre-analytical errors, maintain sample integrity, and uphold universal precautions against bloodborne pathogens. All personnel performing venipuncture must be trained, certified, and compliant with current institutional safety guidelines.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Patient Identification

*   **Review Requisition:** Verify the physician’s order, confirm patient details, and identify any specific collection requirements (e.g., fasting, medication timing).
*   **Assemble Supplies:** Gather a phlebotomy tray containing the appropriate needle gauge, evacuated tubes (in order of draw), tourniquet, 70% isopropyl alcohol swabs, gauze, hypoallergenic tape, and a puncture-resistant sharps container.
*   **Hand Hygiene:** Perform thorough hand hygiene using soap and water or an alcohol-based hand rub.
*   **Don PPE:** Put on clean, non-sterile examination gloves.
*   **Patient Verification:** Utilize two patient identifiers (e.g., full name and date of birth). Compare these against the laboratory requisition form and the patient's wristband.
*   **Patient Education:** Explain the procedure clearly, address patient anxiety, and inquire about previous history of fainting or complications with blood draws.

## Phase 2: Site Selection and Preparation

*   **Positioning:** Ensure the patient is seated in a phlebotomy chair with arm support or lying supine.
*   **Site Inspection:** Inspect the antecubital fossa for a suitable vein. Palpate the area to determine the vein’s depth, direction, and bounce.
*   **Tourniquet Application:** Apply the tourniquet 3–4 inches above the chosen site. Note: Do not leave the tourniquet on for more than 60 seconds to prevent hemoconcentration.
*   **Disinfection:** Cleanse the site with a 70% isopropyl alcohol swab using a concentric circular motion, moving outward. Allow the site to air-dry completely; do not blow or fan the area.

## Phase 3: Venipuncture Execution

*   **Anchoring:** Use the thumb of your non-dominant hand to pull the skin taut below the insertion site to anchor the vein.
*   **Needle Insertion:** With the bevel facing up, insert the needle into the vein at a 15–30 degree angle.
*   **Tube Engagement:** Push the evacuated tube into the holder. Ensure the blood flow begins. 
*   **Order of Draw:** Follow the standardized order: Blood Culture bottles, Citrate (light blue), Serum (red/gold), Heparin (green), EDTA (lavender), Fluoride (gray).
*   **Mixing:** Immediately and gently invert each tube 5–10 times (do not shake) to ensure proper additive mixing.
*   **Tourniquet Removal:** Release the tourniquet as soon as blood flow is established or once the final tube begins to fill.

## Phase 4: Completion and Post-Collection Care

*   **Needle Removal:** Place folded gauze over the site and withdraw the needle in a smooth, continuous motion. Apply immediate pressure.
*   **Safety Activation:** Engage the needle safety mechanism immediately and dispose of the needle into a sharps container.
*   **Site Management:** Instruct the patient to keep their arm straight and apply firm pressure for 1–2 minutes. Apply a bandage once bleeding has stopped.
*   **Labeling:** Label all tubes at the bedside in the presence of the patient, including the date, time, and collector’s initials.
*   **Disposal/Hygiene:** Dispose of all waste, remove PPE, and perform hand hygiene again.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Avoid Hemolysis:** Never use a needle gauge smaller than 23g, and ensure the alcohol has completely dried before insertion to prevent lysing red blood cells.
*   **Avoid Hematoma:** If a hematoma begins to form during the draw, release the tourniquet and remove the needle immediately, applying firm pressure.
*   **The "Anchor" is Key:** Many "missed" veins are simply rolling veins. Ensure your non-dominant hand provides firm traction to stabilize the vein throughout the entire insertion process.
*   **Fainting (Syncope):** If a patient reports feeling dizzy, remove the needle, lay the patient flat, elevate their legs, and apply cold compresses to the forehead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What should I do if blood stops flowing mid-collection?**
First, check if the needle has moved; gently advance or pull back slightly. Ensure the tube is properly seated. If these fail, do not probe; remove the needle and attempt a new draw at a different site.

**2. Can I draw blood from an arm with an IV line?**
Avoid drawing from the same arm as an active IV infusion. If absolutely necessary, draw from a site *distal* (below) the IV site after the nurse has paused the infusion for at least 5 minutes.

**3. Why is the "Order of Draw" so important?**
The order of draw is essential to prevent cross-contamination of additives (e.g., EDTA from a lavender tube contaminating a chemistry sample), which can lead to erroneous test results.

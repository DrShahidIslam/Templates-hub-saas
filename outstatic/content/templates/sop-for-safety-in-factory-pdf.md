# Standard Operating Procedure: Factory Safety Management and Compliance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety protocols required to maintain a secure, hazard-free working environment within the production facility. The objective of this document is to minimize workplace injuries, ensure compliance with OSHA (or equivalent local) safety regulations, and foster a culture of proactive risk mitigation. All employees, contractors, and visitors are required to adhere to these procedures without exception.

## 1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Compliance
*   **Verification:** Ensure all personnel have been issued the correct PPE for their specific station (e.g., steel-toed boots, high-visibility vests, eye protection, earplugs, or respirators).
*   **Inspection:** Before the start of every shift, employees must inspect their PPE for cracks, tears, or degradation.
*   **Storage:** Ensure that PPE is cleaned and stored in designated lockers, not left on the factory floor where it could become a tripping hazard or contaminated.

## 2. Machinery and Equipment Safety
*   **Lockout/Tagout (LOTO):** Strictly enforce LOTO procedures before performing any maintenance, clearing jams, or conducting repairs. Ensure the power source is locked and a clearly labeled tag is applied.
*   **Guardrails and Shields:** Inspect all machine guarding systems daily. Never operate machinery if a safety shield is missing, bypassed, or damaged.
*   **Emergency Stops:** Test all emergency stop (E-stop) buttons at the beginning of each week to confirm they are functioning correctly and are unobstructed.

## 3. Floor Management and Housekeeping
*   **Clear Aisles:** Maintain clear passage for foot traffic and forklifts. No materials, tools, or waste should be stored in marked walkways or emergency egress routes.
*   **Spill Mitigation:** Any liquid spill must be addressed immediately using spill kits. Utilize "Wet Floor" signage until the area is completely dry.
*   **Waste Disposal:** All hazardous waste, scrap metal, and oily rags must be disposed of in designated, fire-rated, color-coded containers.

## 4. Emergency Preparedness and Response
*   **Egress Routes:** Ensure all fire exits are clearly marked, illuminated, and unlocked from the inside at all times.
*   **Assembly Points:** Maintain clear signage for emergency assembly areas. Conduct quarterly fire and evacuation drills.
*   **First Aid Access:** Verify that all first aid kits are fully stocked, accessible, and within their expiration dates. Ensure an eyewash station is reachable within 10 seconds of any chemical handling area.

## 5. Reporting and Incident Documentation
*   **Near-Miss Reporting:** Any situation that "almost" resulted in an injury must be reported to the Floor Supervisor immediately to prevent future recurrence.
*   **Incident Log:** In the event of an injury, secure the scene, provide medical assistance, and complete a formal Incident Report Form within 24 hours.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The "Buddy System"):** Implement a buddy system for high-risk operations (e.g., confined space entry or heavy lifting) to ensure immediate assistance is available.
*   **Pro Tip (Visual Cues):** Use floor marking tape and shadow boards for tools. If a tool has a dedicated shadow, it is easier to spot if it goes missing or is left in a dangerous location.
*   **Pitfall (Complacency):** The greatest risk in a factory is "normalization of deviance"—where safety steps are skipped because "nothing happened last time." Management must conduct random spot checks to ensure protocols are not being ignored due to production speed pressures.
*   **Pitfall (Poor Communication):** Relying solely on verbal instructions is dangerous. Ensure all safety updates are posted on physical bulletin boards and discussed during daily "toolbox talks."

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should we conduct a full facility safety audit?**
A: A comprehensive formal audit should be conducted quarterly, though departmental safety walkthroughs should occur daily.

**Q: What is the first action to take if an E-stop button fails a test?**
A: Immediately lock out the machine, tag it as "Out of Service," notify the maintenance department, and inform the shift supervisor. Do not allow the machine to be used until the repair is verified.

**Q: Who is responsible for updating the safety SOP?**
A: The Operations Manager, in conjunction with the Safety Officer, is responsible for reviewing and updating the SOP annually or following any significant workplace incident or change in facility machinery.

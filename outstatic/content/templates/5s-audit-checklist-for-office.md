# SOP: 5S Office Audit Protocol

## Introduction
The 5S methodology is a foundational Lean tool designed to create a structured, efficient, and safe office environment. By maintaining discipline in our physical and digital workspace, we minimize waste, reduce search times, and foster a culture of continuous improvement. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides a rigorous framework for conducting monthly 5S audits. The objective is not merely to "clean," but to optimize workflows and ensure that every item has a dedicated purpose and place. Auditors should conduct these inspections with a focus on objectivity, providing constructive feedback to improve team productivity.

## 5S Audit Checklist

## 1. Sort (Seiri)
*Goal: Eliminate unnecessary items.*
*   Are all desktop surfaces free of non-essential paperwork or equipment?
*   Are filing cabinets, drawers, and supply closets free of obsolete documents and expired supplies?
*   Have "red-tagged" items (items awaiting disposal/relocation) been processed within 48 hours?
*   Is there a clear distinction between active projects and archived materials?
*   Are shared spaces (e.g., mailrooms, supply cabinets) free of personal clutter?

## 2. Set in Order (Seiton)
*Goal: Organize the workspace for efficiency.*
*   Are frequently used items stored within easy reach?
*   Are storage areas, shelves, and cabinets clearly labeled for quick identification?
*   Are office supplies (staplers, pens, tape) returned to designated spots after use?
*   Is there a "home" for every item, and does that location follow a logical workflow pattern?
*   Are cable management systems utilized to eliminate trip hazards and tangles?

## 3. Shine (Seiso)
*Goal: Maintain a clean and high-functioning environment.*
*   Are desks, monitors, and keyboards free of dust and debris?
*   Are shared communal areas (printers, coffee stations) wiped down daily?
*   Is there a documented schedule for deep-cleaning or maintenance of office hardware?
*   Are light fixtures, vents, and office corners free of dust accumulation?
*   Is the physical condition of the office conducive to a professional and focused work environment?

## 4. Standardize (Seiketsu)
*Goal: Create consistent visual cues and protocols.*
*   Are 5S guidelines and cleaning schedules clearly posted?
*   Are labels uniform, readable, and placed consistently?
*   Do employees understand their specific responsibilities regarding the 5S maintenance schedule?
*   Is there a visual system in place to indicate when supplies (paper, toner, etc.) need reordering?
*   Are digital folders/naming conventions standardized across team shared drives?

## 5. Sustain (Shitsuke)
*Goal: Build habits to ensure long-term adherence.*
*   Has the monthly 5S audit been completed and documented this cycle?
*   Is there evidence of follow-up on corrective actions from the previous month’s audit?
*   Are employees actively participating in the improvement of their immediate work areas?
*   Is 5S performance discussed during team meetings or monthly operations reviews?
*   Does the team feel empowered to suggest better organization methods for their tasks?

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Take "Before" and "After" photos during the audit. Visual evidence is the most powerful tool for demonstrating the impact of 5S on team morale and productivity.
*   **Pro Tip:** Digitally audit the "Digital Desktop." A cluttered desktop with hundreds of unsorted files is a primary cause of office inefficiency.
*   **Pitfall - The "Spring Cleaning" Syndrome:** Avoid viewing 5S as a quarterly event. 5S must be a continuous, small-habit activity. If you are "cleaning" for hours, you aren't practicing 5S; you are behind on maintenance.
*   **Pitfall - Lack of Ownership:** Avoid assigning 5S tasks to only one person (e.g., the office manager). For 5S to succeed, every individual must take ownership of their personal space.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How long should a 5S office audit take?**
A: A routine walk-through audit for a standard office department should take no more than 15–20 minutes. If it takes longer, the environment likely lacks standardized maintenance.

**Q: What if an employee refuses to organize their desk?**
A: Frame 5S as an efficiency initiative rather than a personal critique. Emphasize that a clear workspace reduces cognitive load and stress, and focus on the "Sort" and "Set in Order" phases as tools to make their specific job easier.

**Q: How do we handle "sentimental" but useless clutter?**
A: Use the "12-month rule." If an item has not been used, referenced, or interacted with in the last 12 months, it should be archived off-site or discarded. If it is high-sentimental value but low-utility, it should be kept in a designated, out-of-the-way space, not in the primary workspace.

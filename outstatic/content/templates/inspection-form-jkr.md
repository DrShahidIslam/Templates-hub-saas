# Standard Operating Procedure: JKR Site Inspection Documentation

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for completing the Jabatan Kerja Raya (JKR) inspection form. Adherence to these procedures ensures compliance with public works standards, minimizes project delays, and maintains a rigorous audit trail for structural integrity and safety. All site engineers and project managers are required to utilize this documentation process to validate construction milestones prior to subsequent work phases.

## 1. Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Documentation Review:** Ensure the latest "Approved for Construction" drawings (AFC) and the JKR Specification Book are accessible.
*   **Safety Compliance:** Verify that all team members are equipped with appropriate PPE (Hard hat, safety vest, steel-toed boots) before entering the site.
*   **Notify Stakeholders:** Issue a formal Request for Inspection (RFI) to the JKR supervising officer (SO) at least 24–48 hours in advance, as per contract requirements.
*   **Material Certification:** Have all Mill Certificates, test reports (Cube tests, steel tensile tests), and delivery dockets organized in a folder for immediate inspection.

## 2. Site Execution & Form Completion
*   **Verification of Dimensions:** Cross-check the as-built dimensions against the grid lines and levels indicated in the architectural and structural drawings.
*   **Quality Assessment:** Inspect workmanship against JKR quality standards (e.g., concrete finish, reinforcement cover, verticality).
*   **Photographic Evidence:** Capture high-resolution photos of the area being inspected, including close-ups of reinforcement bars, drainage falls, or structural connections.
*   **Data Entry:** Fill out the JKR form headers (Project Title, Contract No., Date/Time, Location) with precision. Ensure the "Remarks" section is descriptive and refers to specific non-conformance reports (NCR) if applicable.

## 3. Review and Sign-Off Protocol
*   **Joint Inspection:** Walk through the site alongside the JKR representative, pointing out critical features.
*   **Corrective Action Logging:** If defects are identified, document them clearly in the inspection form. Do not close the form until the SO has verified the rectification.
*   **Signature Acquisition:** Obtain the signature of the JKR SO and the Contractor’s Representative immediately upon the conclusion of the inspection.
*   **Digital Archiving:** Scan the signed document and upload it to the Project Document Management System (PDMS) within 24 hours of completion.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always carry a digital laser distance measurer and a spirit level/plumb bob. Discrepancies identified on-site should be corrected before the JKR officer arrives to maintain a "First-Time Right" reputation.
*   **Pro Tip:** Keep a "Snag List" specifically for your own internal check before requesting the official JKR inspection.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "vague" descriptions in the remarks section. Phrases like "General check performed" are insufficient; use specific metrics such as "Concrete slump 100mm, verified as per Clause 3.2.1."
*   **Pitfall:** Never proceed with the next phase of work (e.g., concrete pouring) if the JKR form is not signed. This is a common cause of payment rejection and structural audit failures.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if the JKR supervising officer refuses to sign the form?**
A: Do not force the signature. Request a written explanation for the refusal, document the specific points of contention in your site diary, and immediately escalate the matter to your Project Manager for a formal meeting with the JKR SO.

**Q: Are digital signatures accepted on JKR forms?**
A: This depends on the specific project contract. If the project is registered under JKR’s digital portal (e.g., e-submission systems), digital signatures are mandatory. For traditional projects, wet-ink signatures remain the standard.

**Q: How long should inspection forms be retained?**
A: According to standard JKR contract conditions, all quality documentation must be kept for the duration of the Defects Liability Period (DLP) and usually for a minimum of 7 years post-completion for archival and audit purposes.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Washing Machine Maintenance and Operation

This document outlines the professional procedure for operating a commercial or residential washing machine to ensure optimal garment longevity, machine performance, and energy efficiency. Adherence to these protocols prevents mechanical strain, minimizes utility waste, and maintains a sanitary laundry environment.

## 1. Pre-Wash Preparation
*   **Garment Inspection:** Check all pockets for foreign objects (coins, keys, tissues) that may damage the drum or clog the drainage pump.
*   **Sorting:** Separate laundry by fabric weight, color (lights/darks), and required water temperature.
*   **Pre-Treatment:** Apply specialized stain removers to high-traffic areas (collars, cuffs) or stubborn stains at least 10 minutes prior to loading.
*   **Fastening:** Close all zippers, buttons, and hooks to prevent snagging on other fabrics during the agitation cycle.
*   **Delicates Protection:** Place lingerie, hosiery, or delicate knitwear inside mesh laundry bags.

## 2. Machine Loading and Detergent Dosing
*   **Weight Distribution:** Load the machine loosely. Do not compress clothes; as a rule of thumb, ensure you can fit a vertical hand between the top of the laundry and the drum ceiling.
*   **Detergent Selection:** Use only High-Efficiency (HE) detergent for front-loading machines to prevent excessive sudsing.
*   **Precise Dosing:** Measure detergent according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Overloading detergent leads to residue buildup on clothing and internal machine components.
*   **Additive Placement:** Ensure bleach, fabric softener, and detergent are placed in their designated, labeled compartments rather than directly on the fabric.

## 3. Cycle Selection and Execution
*   **Temperature Setting:** Select the lowest temperature setting suitable for the fabric type to save energy and reduce fiber degradation.
*   **Cycle Type:** Choose the cycle that matches the soil level (e.g., "Quick Wash" for lightly soiled items, "Heavy Duty" for towels/bedding).
*   **Final Inspection:** Verify that the door or lid is securely latched before initiating the start sequence.
*   **Monitoring:** Ensure the machine reaches the spin cycle without excessive vibration or "walking," which indicates an unbalanced load.

## 4. Post-Wash Maintenance
*   **Immediate Removal:** Unload garments promptly after the cycle terminates to prevent mildew growth and deep-set wrinkling.
*   **Moisture Management:** Leave the washer door or lid slightly ajar after each use to allow the drum to air-dry, preventing mold and "musty" odors.
*   **Gasket Maintenance:** Wipe down the rubber door seal (bellows) in front-load machines to remove trapped hair, lint, and standing water.
*   **Filter Cleaning:** Check the drain pump filter monthly for debris accumulation to ensure proper water evacuation.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Run a "Tub Clean" cycle or a hot cycle with white vinegar once a month to descale the internal heater and sanitize the drum.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always shake out heavily soiled items (mud, sand) outside before loading to prevent abrasives from damaging the drum finish.
*   **Pitfall - Overloading:** Filling the machine beyond its rated capacity prevents proper agitation, meaning clothes will not be cleaned effectively.
*   **Pitfall - Softener Abuse:** Excessive fabric softener usage creates a "waxy" buildup in the machine, which can eventually lead to mechanical sensor failure.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Why does my machine smell after a cycle?**
A: Residual moisture and detergent buildup (often from non-HE soap or overuse of softener) promote mold growth. Leave the door open between loads and perform a monthly maintenance wash with a dedicated cleaner.

**Q: Can I wash shoes in the machine?**
A: It is generally advised against, as the weight causes an unbalanced load and the physical impact can damage both the shoes and the machine’s drum. If necessary, use a mesh bag and a low-spin, low-heat cycle.

**Q: Why are my clothes still wet after the spin cycle?**
A: This usually indicates an unbalanced load or a clogged drain pump filter. Try rearranging the load and re-running the spin cycle, or inspect the drainage hose for kinks or blockages.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Residential Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides a comprehensive framework for the routine maintenance of a residential property. Implementing a structured maintenance schedule is essential for preserving asset value, ensuring occupant safety, and avoiding costly emergency repairs. This document serves as a standardized guide for homeowners and property managers to systematically inspect and service critical systems and structural components of a home.

## 1. Exterior and Envelope Maintenance
*   **Roof Inspection:** Inspect shingles for curling, cracking, or missing sections. Check flashing around chimneys and vents for signs of degradation.
*   **Gutters and Downspouts:** Clear debris biannually. Ensure downspouts direct water at least six feet away from the foundation.
*   **Foundation and Siding:** Walk the perimeter to check for cracks in the foundation or gaps in siding/trim. Seal any penetrations to prevent pest entry.
*   **Hardscaping:** Inspect walkways and driveways for cracks or trip hazards. Clean oil stains and pressure wash as needed.
*   **Landscaping:** Trim tree branches that overhang the roof or touch the siding. Ensure bushes are pruned at least 18 inches away from exterior walls.

## 2. HVAC and Mechanical Systems
*   **Air Filters:** Replace HVAC filters every 30–90 days, depending on usage, pet ownership, and air quality.
*   **HVAC Servicing:** Schedule professional semi-annual tune-ups (spring for cooling, autumn for heating).
*   **Water Heater:** Drain a few gallons from the tank annually to flush out sediment; inspect the anode rod every 3 years.
*   **Dryer Vent:** Clean the dryer exhaust duct annually to mitigate fire hazards and improve drying efficiency.

## 3. Plumbing and Water Safety
*   **Leak Detection:** Inspect under all sinks and around toilets for moisture. Check the water meter when no water is running to identify hidden leaks.
*   **Faucets and Drains:** Clean aerators to remove mineral buildup. Ensure drains flow freely; use enzymatic cleaners instead of harsh chemicals.
*   **Sump Pump:** Test the sump pump annually by pouring a bucket of water into the basin to ensure the float switch activates.
*   **Shut-off Valves:** Exercise main water shut-off valves and toilet supply valves quarterly to ensure they do not seize.

## 4. Interior and Life Safety
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test monthly. Replace batteries annually (or when chirping). Replace units every 10 years.
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Check pressure gauge monthly. Ensure the unit is accessible and has not passed its expiration date.
*   **GFCI/AFCI Outlets:** Test all Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter outlets monthly using the "Test" and "Reset" buttons.
*   **Caulking and Grout:** Inspect tile areas in bathrooms and kitchens. Recaulk areas showing signs of shrinkage or mold to prevent water intrusion.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Digital Logs:** Maintain a digital maintenance log, including dates of service, receipts, and photos of repairs. This adds significant value during a future property sale.
*   **Seasonal Sync:** Tie your maintenance schedule to seasonal changes (e.g., "Spring Forward/Fall Back" time changes) to remind yourself to change smoke detector batteries.
*   **Vendor Relationships:** Keep a list of vetted local contractors. Do not wait for a pipe to burst to look for an emergency plumber.

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Deferred Maintenance:** The "if it isn't broken, don't fix it" mindset is the fastest way to incur high long-term costs. Minor leaks ignored today become structural rot tomorrow.
*   **Ignoring Attic/Crawlspace:** Most homeowners forget to check these areas. These are the most common breeding grounds for mold and pest infestations.
*   **Over-Cleaning:** Avoid pressure-washing delicate siding or using harsh chemical drain cleaners, as these can cause permanent damage to your home’s systems.

## FAQ

**Q: How often should I have a professional inspect my home?**
A: A general home health inspection by a licensed contractor is recommended once every three years, while HVAC systems and chimney flues should be serviced annually.

**Q: What is the most critical item to maintain to prevent major damage?**
A: Moisture management. Keeping water away from your foundation (via gutters) and preventing indoor leaks (via plumbing checks) will prevent 90% of structural and mold issues.

**Q: Should I perform these tasks myself or hire a pro?**
A: Simple tasks like filter changes, gutter cleaning, and smoke detector testing are DIY-friendly. Complex tasks involving electrical wiring, gas lines, or roof structural repairs should always be handled by licensed, insured professionals.

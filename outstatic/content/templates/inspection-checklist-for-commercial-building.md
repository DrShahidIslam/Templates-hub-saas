# Standard Operating Procedure: Commercial Building Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional requirements for conducting a comprehensive inspection of a commercial property. The objective is to identify maintenance requirements, safety hazards, and structural deficiencies that could impact operational continuity, insurance compliance, or asset value. This document serves as the primary protocol for facility managers, property inspectors, and maintenance supervisors to ensure systematic and thorough assessment of all physical assets.

## 1. Exterior and Site Grounds
*   **Parking Lots/Pavement:** Inspect for cracks, potholes, oil stains, and proper line striping visibility.
*   **Signage:** Ensure all exterior signage is secure, illuminated, and compliant with local zoning codes.
*   **Landscaping:** Confirm trimming of vegetation away from the building envelope; verify irrigation systems are operational.
*   **Drainage:** Check storm drains for debris; verify gutters and downspouts are clear and diverting water away from the foundation.
*   **Lighting:** Verify all parking lot and exterior security lights are functional; replace burnt-out LEDs.

## 2. Structural and Building Envelope
*   **Roofing:** Inspect for ponding water, loose flashing, damaged vents, or missing membrane sections.
*   **Walls/Facade:** Look for cracks in masonry, spalling concrete, or evidence of water infiltration at window seals.
*   **Windows/Doors:** Check for proper alignment, smooth operation of hinges, and integrity of weather stripping.
*   **Foundation:** Scan for signs of settling, major foundation cracks, or efflorescence.

## 3. Interior Common Areas and Systems
*   **HVAC:** Check air filters for cleanliness; verify thermostat programming and listen for unusual mechanical noises.
*   **Electrical:** Inspect electrical panels for labeling, signs of heat damage/arcing, and clear clearance access.
*   **Plumbing:** Test all common area sinks and toilets; check for leaks under vanities and water pressure consistency.
*   **Life Safety:** Test smoke detectors, ensure fire extinguishers are tagged/charged, and verify emergency exit lighting functionality.
*   **Flooring/Walls:** Inspect for trip hazards (carpet fraying, loose tiles) and peeling paint or water stains on walls/ceilings.

## 4. Mechanical and Utility Rooms
*   **Boilers/Water Heaters:** Check for leaks, corrosion, or pressure gauge irregularities.
*   **Fire Suppression:** Ensure sprinkler heads are unobstructed and the pressure gauge on the riser is within the green zone.
*   **Security Systems:** Verify operation of card readers, security cameras, and motion sensors.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always perform inspections using a digital mobile app that allows for photo tagging. Visual evidence is critical for insurance claims and contractor quotes.
*   **Pro Tip:** Perform inspections during or immediately after a heavy rainstorm to identify "hidden" water leaks that are invisible during dry weather.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not overlook "nuisance" issues. Small cracks or minor leaks often turn into capital expenditure (CapEx) emergencies if left unaddressed.
*   **Pitfall:** Never enter restricted mechanical or electrical areas without proper PPE and the required technician certifications.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should a commercial building inspection be performed?**
A: A comprehensive "deep dive" inspection should be performed quarterly, while a "walk-through" visual inspection should be conducted at least monthly.

**Q: What is the most common area where commercial buildings fail inspections?**
A: Fire and life safety compliance—specifically, blocked exit routes, expired fire extinguishers, and faulty emergency exit signage—are the most common violations.

**Q: Should tenants be involved in the inspection process?**
A: While you should provide notice to tenants, inspections are generally best performed by management/maintenance staff to ensure an objective, standardized assessment of the building's physical health.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Academic and Administrative Excellence at XIMB Bhubaneswar

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the systemic framework for students, faculty, and administrative staff at Xavier Institute of Management, Bhubaneswar (XIMB). The objective is to maintain operational efficiency, uphold the institution’s Jesuit values, and ensure a seamless academic experience. This guide covers daily conduct, institutional protocols, and resource management to optimize performance within the XIMB ecosystem.

## Section 1: Academic Rigor and Classroom Protocol

*   **Attendance Compliance:** Maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in all core and elective courses; monitor individual portals daily to ensure no discrepancy in records.
*   **Assignment Submission:** Adhere strictly to the Learning Management System (LMS) deadlines. Upload files in the specified format (usually PDF) at least one hour before the cut-off to avoid technical glitches.
*   **Case Study Preparation:** Conduct pre-class readings for case-based sessions. Utilize the library’s database subscriptions for industry-specific data and historical context.
*   **Group Dynamics:** Establish a team charter early in the trimester to define roles, responsibilities, and conflict resolution mechanisms for collaborative projects.

## Section 2: Administrative and IT Infrastructure Usage

*   **Campus Network Access:** Connect only via official institute credentials. Ensure VPN settings are configured for remote access to journals and library archives.
*   **Facility Booking:** Reserve seminar halls or discussion rooms through the central administration portal at least 48 hours in advance.
*   **Student ID Maintenance:** Keep the RFID-enabled ID card active; report any loss to the Security Office immediately to prevent unauthorized access.
*   **Office of Student Affairs (OSA) Communication:** Direct all leave applications, medical records, and logistical grievances through the OSA portal to maintain an official audit trail.

## Section 3: Placement and Corporate Relations

*   **Resume Standardization:** Strictly follow the placement committee (PlaceCom) approved template. Update and audit resumes quarterly with verified project details.
*   **Mock Interview Preparation:** Participate in the peer-to-peer interview review cycle facilitated by the seniors/mentors to refine industry-specific jargon and behavioral responses.
*   **Placement Policy Compliance:** Review the annual placement brochure and guidelines to understand eligibility criteria, the "one student, one offer" rule, and opt-out procedures.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Leverage the XIMB alumni network on LinkedIn early in your first year. Reach out for informational interviews rather than direct job requests.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the library’s bloomberg terminal training sessions; proficiency in this tool is a massive value-add during summer internships.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-committing to extracurricular clubs and committees without prioritizing core academic deliverables. This often leads to burnout and a dip in CGPA.
*   **Pitfall:** Neglecting the "soft" deadlines communicated via WhatsApp groups. Even if not on the LMS, peer communication at XIMB is essential for coordination.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How do I resolve a discrepancy in my attendance record?**
A: Submit a formal request through the student portal, attaching supporting documentation (e.g., medical certificates or authorized leave approvals) and CC the respective course faculty.

**Q: Where can I access the library’s digital resources from off-campus?**
A: You must log into the institutional remote access portal using your XIMB email credentials. Ensure your browser cache is cleared if you encounter persistent access errors.

**Q: What is the protocol for seeking financial support for academic competitions?**
A: All funding requests for national/international competitions must be routed through the Dean’s office with a detailed proposal, budget breakdown, and a recommendation letter from your Faculty Advisor.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Vehicle Safety and Compliance Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety, cleanliness, and mechanical inspection requirements for vehicles operating on the Lyft platform. Maintaining a high vehicle standard is critical for driver safety, passenger satisfaction, and continued account eligibility. This checklist is designed to meet and exceed Lyft’s state-specific vehicle inspection requirements (where applicable) and general platform maintenance standards. Drivers should perform a "pre-trip" version of this inspection daily and a "comprehensive" version monthly to ensure all systems are functioning optimally.

## Section 1: Exterior & Structural Integrity
*   **Bodywork:** Inspect for significant dents, deep scratches, or rusted body panels that may compromise vehicle safety or professional appearance.
*   **Glass & Visibility:** Ensure the windshield is free of cracks or chips that obstruct the driver’s view or compromise structural integrity. 
*   **Lighting System:** Test all exterior lights, including low/high beams, turn signals, brake lights, reverse lights, and hazard lights.
*   **Tires:** Verify tire tread depth meets legal requirements (minimum 2/32"). Check for sidewall bulges, embedded objects, or uneven wear patterns.
*   **License Plate:** Ensure plates are clean, legible, and properly illuminated as per local regulations.

## Section 2: Interior & Passenger Comfort
*   **Safety Restraints:** Pull each seatbelt firmly to ensure locking mechanisms engage. Verify buckles latch securely and belts are not frayed.
*   **Door Functionality:** Test all four passenger door handles (interior and exterior) and child safety locks to ensure they operate smoothly.
*   **Upholstery & Floors:** Ensure seats are free of tears or stains. Clean floor mats and remove any loose items or debris under seats.
*   **Climate Control:** Verify that both heating and air conditioning units function correctly to maintain a comfortable cabin environment.
*   **Technology & Charging:** Check that the phone mount is secure and that your USB/charging cables are functional for passenger use.

## Section 3: Mechanical & Fluid Systems
*   **Fluids:** Check oil, coolant, brake fluid, and windshield washer fluid levels. Top off as necessary to prevent engine overheating or brake failure.
*   **Braking Performance:** Ensure the brake pedal provides firm resistance and does not travel too close to the floor. Verify there is no grinding noise during stops.
*   **Steering:** Confirm that the steering is responsive and does not "pull" to one side while driving on flat pavement.
*   **Dashboard Warnings:** Ensure no "Check Engine," "ABS," or "Tire Pressure" warning lights are illuminated on the instrument cluster.

## Section 4: Documentation & Legal Compliance
*   **Registration:** Confirm a current, valid vehicle registration document is present in the glove box.
*   **Insurance:** Keep a physical or digital copy of the Lyft-specific insurance policy readily available.
*   **State Inspection:** If your jurisdiction requires an annual safety/emissions sticker, confirm it is current and displayed in the required location.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Carry a small "emergency kit" in your trunk containing a tire pressure gauge, a portable jump starter, and basic cleaning supplies. This prevents minor issues from turning into missed shifts.
*   **Pro Tip:** Document your inspection. Taking a quick photo of your odometer and vehicle condition before starting your shift provides a timestamped record in case of customer disputes.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid ignoring "minor" rattles or squeaks. In high-mileage rideshare vehicles, small noises are often the first indicator of suspension or brake component failure.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use heavy air fresheners. While you want the car to smell clean, many passengers are sensitive to artificial scents; keep the interior neutral and well-ventilated.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often does Lyft require an official vehicle inspection?**
A: Requirements vary significantly by city and state. Some regions require an inspection upon onboarding and annually thereafter, while others may not require a formal mechanical inspection form. Always check your local Lyft dashboard for specific regional requirements.

**Q: What should I do if a passenger notices a mechanical issue?**
A: If a passenger identifies a safety concern, apologize, end the trip immediately if it is unsafe to continue, and contact Lyft Support. Do not continue to drive the vehicle until a certified mechanic has cleared the issue.

**Q: Am I required to have a specific car model to pass inspection?**
A: Yes, Lyft maintains a list of eligible vehicle years and models. Generally, vehicles must be newer (usually 15 years or newer, depending on the market) and must have four doors and seatbelts for at least five passengers. Always consult the "Vehicle Requirements" page in your driver portal.

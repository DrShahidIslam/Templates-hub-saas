# Standard Operating Procedure: New Zealand Work Visa Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for foreign nationals intending to secure a work visa for New Zealand. Navigating the immigration landscape requires meticulous attention to detail, as application requirements fluctuate based on the specific visa category—such as the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV), Working Holiday Scheme, or specialized occupational visas. Following this structured checklist will ensure all documentation is compliant with Immigration New Zealand (INZ) standards, thereby minimizing processing delays and the risk of application declinature.

## Phase 1: Eligibility and Pathway Identification
Before initiating the application, you must confirm your legal pathway.
*   **Identify the correct visa category:** Determine if you qualify for the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV), a specific Purpose visa, or a partnership-based work visa.
*   **Review INZ occupation lists:** Verify if your role is on the Green List or requires a Job Check (employer-led).
*   **Assess Health Requirements:** Determine if you need a chest X-ray or a full medical certificate based on your intended length of stay and country of origin.
*   **Assess Character Requirements:** Obtain police certificates from your home country and any country you have lived in for 12+ months in the last 10 years (if over age 17).

## Phase 2: Documentation Preparation
Gather all primary source documents and ensure they are digitized to INZ specifications.
*   **Passport:** Must be valid for at least three months beyond your intended departure date from New Zealand.
*   **Employment Offer:** Ensure a signed employment agreement is present, which meets New Zealand minimum wage and employment standards.
*   **Employer Accreditation:** Confirm your employer holds current accreditation with INZ.
*   **Evidence of Qualifications:** Provide original or certified copies of degree certificates or relevant trade certifications.
*   **Evidence of Experience:** Prepare updated CVs and, where necessary, written references from previous employers to substantiate work history.
*   **Identity Photos:** Digital files meeting INZ specifications (usually 3:4 ratio, plain background).

## Phase 3: Application Submission and Monitoring
*   **Create a RealMe Account:** This is the mandatory portal for all online visa submissions.
*   **Data Entry:** Complete the online form, ensuring 100% accuracy in personal details; discrepancies here are the primary cause of rejection.
*   **Upload Supporting Documents:** Organize files into clear categories (e.g., "Financials," "Qualifications," "Identity"). Ensure all files are in PDF format.
*   **Payment of Fees:** Process the visa application fee and the International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy (IVL) if applicable.
*   **Submit and Acknowledge:** Save the receipt and the application reference number for tracking via the RealMe portal.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Gap" Trap:** Do not leave unexplained gaps in your employment history or residential history on your application form. Even short gaps should be addressed with a brief explanatory note.
*   **Translation Compliance:** Any document not in English must be accompanied by an official, certified translation. Do not use generic software translators.
*   **Employer Communication:** Maintain a proactive line of communication with your employer. If you are applying for an AEWV, the employer must complete the "Job Check" process before you can submit your visa application.
*   **Over-Documentation:** While being thorough is good, do not upload irrelevant documents. Only provide what is requested to avoid clouding the visa officer’s assessment.

## FAQ

**Q: How long does the visa processing take?**
A: Processing times vary significantly based on the visa type and current volumes. Check the Immigration New Zealand "Current Processing Times" tool on their official website, as it is updated weekly.

**Q: Do I need a medical examination for a short-term visa?**
A: Generally, if you are staying for less than 6 months, you may not need a full medical. However, if you are from a country with a higher incidence of tuberculosis or intend to stay longer, a chest X-ray or full certificate is usually mandatory.

**Q: Can I change employers once my work visa is approved?**
A: If you are on an Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV), your visa is tied to your employer. If you wish to change jobs, you must typically apply for a variation of conditions or a new work visa before starting the new role.

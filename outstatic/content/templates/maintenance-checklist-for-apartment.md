# Standard Operating Procedure: Apartment Preventative Maintenance

Maintaining an apartment unit is essential for preserving asset value, ensuring tenant safety, and minimizing long-term capital expenditure. This SOP serves as a comprehensive guide for property managers and maintenance technicians to execute a systematic inspection and service routine. By adhering to this checklist on a semi-annual or turn-over basis, you can preemptively identify mechanical failures, structural degradation, and safety hazards, thereby reducing emergency repair costs and maintaining high tenant satisfaction levels.

## HVAC and Air Quality
*   **Filter Replacement:** Inspect and replace HVAC air filters; ensure the MERV rating meets manufacturer specifications.
*   **Condensate Line:** Inspect the condensate drain line for blockages; clear with a wet/dry vac or chemical treatment if necessary.
*   **Thermostat Calibration:** Verify the thermostat is accurately reading ambient temperature and cycling the unit correctly.
*   **Vents and Grilles:** Dust and vacuum supply and return vents to ensure unrestricted airflow.

## Plumbing and Water Systems
*   **Leak Inspection:** Check under all bathroom and kitchen sinks for signs of moisture, mold, or active drips.
*   **Toilet Functionality:** Inspect toilets for running water, stable mounting (no rocking), and flush efficiency. Replace flappers or fill valves if symptoms of wear appear.
*   **Fixture Aerators:** Remove and clean aerators on all faucets to remove mineral deposits and improve water pressure.
*   **Drain Flow:** Test all drains for speed; clear hair or debris from bathroom drains using a snake or non-corrosive enzymatic cleaner.
*   **Water Heater:** Check the water heater temperature settings (recommended 120°F) and inspect the base for rust or signs of leaking.

## Electrical and Safety Systems
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test the alarm function; replace batteries regardless of current charge level (if not hardwired). 
*   **GFCI Outlets:** Test all Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter outlets in kitchens and bathrooms to ensure they trip and reset correctly.
*   **Lighting:** Test all permanent light fixtures; replace flickering or burnt-out bulbs with energy-efficient LED equivalents.
*   **Electrical Panel:** Check the breaker box for loose wires or charred labels; ensure all breakers are clearly marked.

## Structural and Cosmetic Inspection
*   **Windows and Doors:** Lubricate hinges and tracks; check weather stripping for integrity and ensure locks function smoothly.
*   **Walls and Ceilings:** Inspect for hairline cracks, water stains, or peeling paint which may indicate underlying roof or plumbing leaks.
*   **Flooring:** Inspect for loose tiles, lifted carpet seams, or hardwood scratches that may require specialized repair.
*   **Cabinetry:** Tighten loose hinges on cabinet doors and ensure drawers track evenly without binding.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Preventive Documentation:** Always photograph the unit after the inspection. This serves as vital evidence in the event of a security deposit dispute or insurance claim.
*   **Pro Tip: The "Sniff Test":** If you smell mustiness, do not ignore it. It is often the first indicator of a slow leak behind a wall, long before visible mold or water staining appears.
*   **Pitfall: Over-tightening:** When working on plumbing connections, avoid over-tightening. It is a common cause of cracked PVC threading and future, more severe leaks.
*   **Pitfall: Neglecting Exterior Factors:** Maintenance often stops at the front door. Ensure that window weep holes are clear of debris; if they are clogged, rainwater will be forced inside, damaging drywall and flooring.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How often should a full apartment inspection be performed?**
It is recommended to conduct a comprehensive maintenance inspection at least twice a year, or immediately upon a tenant turnover.

**2. What should I do if I find a persistent water leak?**
Immediately turn off the local water shut-off valve. If the leak is behind a wall or the source is unclear, shut off the main water supply to the unit and contact a licensed plumber to prevent structural water damage.

**3. Are there specific legal requirements for tenant notice?**
Yes. In most jurisdictions, you must provide the tenant with formal written notice (usually 24–48 hours) before entering the unit for non-emergency maintenance, unless the lease agreement or local laws state otherwise. Always check your local landlord-tenant regulations.

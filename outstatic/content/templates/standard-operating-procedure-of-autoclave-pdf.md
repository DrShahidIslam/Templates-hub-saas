# Standard Operating Procedure: Autoclave Sterilization

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for the safe and effective operation of laboratory autoclaves. Proper sterilization is critical for eliminating biological contaminants, ensuring experimental integrity, and maintaining personnel safety. All users must be trained on this equipment and adhere strictly to these guidelines to prevent mechanical failure, thermal injuries, or biological hazards. Failure to follow these steps may result in compromised sterilization cycles or catastrophic equipment damage.

## 1. Pre-Cycle Inspection and Loading

*   **Inspect the Chamber:** Ensure the interior chamber is free of debris, broken glass, or biological spills. Check that the drain strainer is clear of blockages.
*   **Water Level Check:** Verify that the water reservoir is filled to the designated fill line with distilled or deionized water. Never use tap water, as mineral buildup will damage heating elements.
*   **Container Suitability:** Confirm that all materials are autoclavable (e.g., borosilicate glass, polypropylene plastics, stainless steel). Never autoclave sealed containers or materials containing solvents or radioactive isotopes.
*   **Venting:** Ensure all caps are loosened (one-quarter turn) to allow steam penetration and prevent pressure-induced explosions.
*   **Organization:** Arrange items in a manner that allows for adequate steam circulation. Do not overcrowd the chamber, as this hinders heat transfer.

## 2. Cycle Operation and Monitoring

*   **Door Securing:** Close the door firmly and ensure the locking mechanism is fully engaged.
*   **Cycle Selection:** Select the appropriate cycle based on the load type:
    *   **Gravity Cycle:** For dry goods, glassware, and instruments.
    *   **Liquid Cycle:** For media, buffers, and water (includes a slow exhaust to prevent boiling over).
*   **Parameter Verification:** Input the required time and temperature settings (standard: 121°C at 15 psi for 15–30 minutes).
*   **Cycle Initiation:** Start the cycle and verify that the cycle light or digital interface shows active heating.
*   **Documentation:** Record the start time, user name, load type, and cycle parameters in the autoclave logbook for regulatory compliance.

## 3. Unloading and Post-Cycle Procedures

*   **Cooling Phase:** Allow the autoclave to reach a safe pressure (near zero) and temperature before attempting to open the door.
*   **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Don heat-resistant gloves, a lab coat, and safety goggles before opening the door.
*   **Door Opening:** Stand to the side of the door—not directly in front—to allow residual steam to escape safely. Crack the door slightly (approx. 2 inches) and wait 5–10 minutes to allow steam to vent before fully opening.
*   **Removing Loads:** Use appropriate trays or carts to transfer hot items. Handle liquids with extreme caution as they may remain superheated and susceptible to "bumping" (sudden boiling).
*   **Final Check:** Return the autoclave to "Standby" or "Off" mode and ensure the door is securely closed.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Use an autoclave-safe indicator tape on every item. The appearance of dark stripes confirms the item has been exposed to the required sterilization temperatures.
*   **Pitfall:** Never autoclave materials containing bleach (sodium hypochlorite). The chloride ions will cause severe, irreversible corrosion of the stainless-steel chamber.
*   **Pro Tip:** For large liquid volumes, increase the cycle time significantly, as the heat requires more time to reach the center of the liquid.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not mix waste (biohazardous) and clean goods in the same cycle, as this increases the risk of cross-contamination.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Why is my autoclave showing a "Low Water" alarm even though the reservoir looks full?**
This is often caused by a dirty water level sensor or mineral buildup on the heating element. Perform a chamber cleaning cycle using an approved descaling agent to resolve mineral interference.

**2. How do I know if my autoclave is actually sterilizing the items?**
You should conduct a regular "Spore Strip" biological indicator test. These tests contain *Geobacillus stearothermophilus* spores; if they do not grow after incubation post-cycle, your autoclave is effectively sterilizing.

**3. What should I do if the door will not open after the cycle completes?**
Never force the door. First, verify the pressure gauge reads zero. If the pressure is zero and the door is still jammed, the safety solenoid may be locked. Turn the unit off, wait for it to cool completely, and contact facilities maintenance or your autoclave vendor.

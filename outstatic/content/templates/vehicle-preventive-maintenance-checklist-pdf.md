# Standard Operating Procedure: Vehicle Preventive Maintenance (VPM)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory protocol for conducting vehicle preventive maintenance. The primary objective is to maximize fleet reliability, ensure operator safety, and minimize long-term capital expenditure by identifying mechanical degradation before it results in catastrophic failure. All personnel responsible for maintenance must utilize the following checklist to ensure consistent, documented compliance with manufacturer specifications and safety standards.

## 1. Fluids and Lubrication
*   **Engine Oil:** Check oil level using the dipstick; verify color and consistency (milky appearance indicates coolant contamination).
*   **Engine Coolant:** Inspect reservoir levels when the engine is cold; ensure concentration meets freeze-point requirements.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Verify fluid level and check for moisture contamination or discoloration.
*   **Transmission Fluid:** Check level and smell for a "burnt" odor, which indicates internal clutch wear.
*   **Power Steering Fluid:** Inspect for leaks at the pump and rack; ensure fluid is at the "Cold Fill" or "Hot Fill" line.
*   **Windshield Washer Fluid:** Top off with appropriate seasonal solvent.

## 2. Tires and Suspension
*   **Tire Pressure:** Measure all tires (including spare) against the manufacturer’s door-jam placard PSI.
*   **Tread Depth:** Measure at multiple points across the tire face; replace if tread is below 3/32".
*   **Tire Condition:** Check for sidewall cracks, bulges, embedded debris, or uneven wear patterns indicating alignment issues.
*   **Suspension:** Inspect shocks and struts for hydraulic fluid weeping.
*   **Steering Linkage:** Verify tie rods and ball joints for excessive play or torn dust boots.

## 3. Electrical and Lighting Systems
*   **Battery:** Inspect terminals for corrosion; verify cable security and battery hold-down bracket integrity.
*   **Exterior Lighting:** Cycle through headlights (high/low), turn signals, hazard lights, brake lights, and reverse lamps.
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Confirm no "Check Engine" or warning lights persist after engine startup.
*   **Auxiliary Systems:** Test interior climate control, defrost, and infotainment screen functionality.

## 4. Safety and Exterior Hardware
*   **Braking System:** Inspect rotors for deep scoring and brake pads for remaining friction material thickness.
*   **Belts and Hoses:** Inspect serpentine belt for fraying/cracks and coolant hoses for softening or swelling.
*   **Wipers:** Inspect blades for streaking or rubber separation; replace every six months.
*   **Glass:** Check windshield for safety-compromising cracks or chips within the driver’s line of sight.
*   **Safety Equipment:** Confirm presence and expiration dates of fire extinguishers, road flares, and first-aid kits.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

**Pro Tips:**
*   **Documentation:** Always timestamp your digital or paper checklist. In the event of a warranty claim or insurance investigation, a chronological service history is your strongest asset.
*   **Temperature Matters:** Always check transmission fluid at the manufacturer-specified operating temperature, as fluid expands when hot.
*   **The "Clean-First" Rule:** Wipe off filler caps and dipstick ports before opening them to prevent debris from entering the engine or transmission.

**Pitfalls:**
*   **Ignoring "Small" Leaks:** A minor weep is a future failure. Document it on the checklist and monitor it for progression rather than ignoring it.
*   **Overtightening:** Using impact wrenches on oil drain plugs or lug nuts often leads to stripped threads or warped rotors. Always use a calibrated torque wrench.
*   **Over-reliance on Sensors:** Do not assume a lack of dashboard warning lights means the vehicle is in perfect condition; mechanical failures (e.g., worn suspension bushings) are not always tracked by the ECU.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How frequently should the Preventive Maintenance checklist be executed?**
A: This depends on the manufacturer's recommendation and vehicle usage intensity. Generally, a "Light" inspection should occur monthly, with a comprehensive "Deep-Dive" inspection every 5,000 miles or six months.

**Q: What should I do if I discover a critical failure item during the inspection?**
A: Immediately mark the vehicle as "Out of Service" (OOS). Remove the keys, place an OOS sign on the steering wheel, and report the specific defect to the Fleet Manager before any further vehicle operation.

**Q: Can I use generic fluids for all my vehicles?**
A: No. Always check the owner's manual. Using the wrong specification of brake fluid or transmission fluid can void warranties and cause irreparable damage to sensitive electronic components.

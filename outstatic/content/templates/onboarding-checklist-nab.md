# Standard Operating Procedure: New Account/Associate Business (NAB) Onboarding

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for the onboarding of a New Account or Business (NAB) entity. As an Operations Manager, the objective is to ensure that all administrative, compliance, and operational requirements are met with precision. A seamless onboarding experience is critical to mitigating risk, ensuring regulatory adherence, and establishing a professional baseline for long-term account management. This SOP is designed to be executed by the Operations Team in coordination with Sales and Legal departments.

## Phase 1: Pre-Onboarding & Due Diligence
Before the account is officially activated, the following verification steps must be completed to ensure the entity meets organizational requirements.

*   **Validate Entity Status:** Verify the business registration (e.g., Articles of Incorporation, LLC filings) with the relevant Secretary of State or governing registry.
*   **KYC/AML Screening:** Perform comprehensive Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) checks, including UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) identification.
*   **Credit Verification:** Review the entity’s credit report or financial statements to determine payment terms and credit limits.
*   **Conflict of Interest Check:** Run the business name against internal databases to ensure no conflicts exist with current clients or competitors.

## Phase 2: Administrative Setup
Once due diligence is approved, the account must be configured within the company’s internal ecosystem.

*   **CRM Data Entry:** Input primary contact details, billing addresses, and tax identification numbers into the CRM.
*   **Contract Execution:** Secure a fully executed Master Service Agreement (MSA) or Statement of Work (SOW) via electronic signature platforms.
*   **Financial Configuration:** Set up the account in the ERP/Accounting system, defining tax nexus, payment terms, and invoicing preferences.
*   **Access Provisioning:** If applicable, create client portal credentials or grant specific software permissions as outlined in the contract.

## Phase 3: Operational Integration
This phase bridges the gap between administrative setup and the actual commencement of services.

*   **Internal Kickoff Meeting:** Conduct a brief synchronization meeting with the account management and technical delivery teams to review contract scope.
*   **Client Welcome Packet:** Disseminate the formal Welcome Packet, including key contacts, escalation paths, and service level agreements (SLAs).
*   **Technical Implementation:** Verify that any required hardware, software, or API integrations are successfully deployed and tested.
*   **Project Timeline Confirmation:** Provide the client with a finalized onboarding schedule, including key milestones and review dates.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate your KYC checks through API integration with third-party verification services to save hours of manual data entry and minimize human error.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a 'Golden Record' for the client in your CRM; ensuring that the Accounting and Sales departments reference the exact same data prevents invoicing errors.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not allow the commencement of services until the MSA is fully signed. "Verbal agreements" lead to significant legal exposure if a dispute arises.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "Over-Onboarding." Do not inundate the client with unnecessary documentation; focus on the essentials required to provide immediate value.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How long should the standard NAB onboarding process take?**
Under normal circumstances, the entire onboarding cycle—from due diligence to service commencement—should be completed within 5 to 10 business days, depending on the complexity of the contract.

**2. What happens if a KYC check returns a 'high risk' status?**
If a screening returns a high-risk flag, immediately pause the onboarding process and escalate the findings to the Compliance Officer or Legal department for a secondary manual review.

**3. Is it possible to bypass the formal onboarding SOP for "priority" clients?**
No. Expediting a process should never equate to skipping a step. If a client is high-priority, allocate more resources to complete the steps faster, but maintain all compliance and administrative checkpoints to protect the firm.

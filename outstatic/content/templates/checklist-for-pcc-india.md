# Standard Operating Procedure: Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) Application for India

This document outlines the systematic procedure for obtaining a Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) from Indian authorities. A PCC is a mandatory document for Indian citizens residing in India or abroad who require proof of their criminal record status for immigration, employment, or long-term visa purposes. This SOP is designed to ensure accuracy, minimize processing delays, and ensure compliance with the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) and Passport Seva guidelines.

## Phase 1: Pre-Application Preparation
*   **Verify Eligibility:** Ensure you hold a valid Indian Passport.
*   **Documentation Audit:** Collate original documents and self-attested photocopies.
*   **Address Verification:** Confirm that your current address matches the address on your passport or that you have valid proof of residence if currently residing elsewhere.
*   **Digital Access:** Ensure you have an active account on the [Passport Seva Portal](https://portal2.passportindia.gov.in/).

## Phase 2: Application Submission
*   **Log in/Register:** Access the Passport Seva Portal using existing credentials or register as a new user.
*   **Application Selection:** Select "Apply for Police Clearance Certificate."
*   **Data Entry:** Carefully input personal details. Ensure the spelling matches your passport exactly.
*   **Payment & Scheduling:** Pay the requisite fee online via Credit/Debit card, Net Banking, or SBI Challan. 
*   **Slot Booking:** Select the nearest Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK) for your appointment.
*   **Print Application Receipt:** Save and print the Application Reference Number (ARN) receipt.

## Phase 3: Appointment Day Execution
*   **Document Submission:** Carry the original passport, the ARN receipt, and proof of address (if applicable).
*   **Biometrics:** Participate in the biometric data collection process at the PSK.
*   **Verification:** Undergo the document verification process by the Passport Granting Officer.
*   **Police Verification:** Await the physical/digital police verification visit to your registered residential address. Ensure a family member is present to confirm your residency.

## Phase 4: Final Collection
*   **Status Tracking:** Monitor the status via the Passport Seva website using your ARN.
*   **Dispatch Notification:** Once the status changes to "PCC printed and dispatched," track the shipment via the provided Speed Post tracking number.
*   **Receipt:** Collect the physical document upon delivery.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pitfall - Address Mismatches:** The most common cause for delay is an address discrepancy. If you have moved, ensure you apply for a 'Re-issue of Passport' with a change of address before or alongside the PCC request.
*   **Pro Tip - Verification:** Keep two neighbors’ contact information handy during the police verification visit; this often expedites the police report filing.
*   **Pitfall - Photocopy Quality:** Always carry clear, legible copies. Blurred or incomplete photocopies often lead to immediate rejection at the PSK counter.
*   **Pro Tip - SMS Alerts:** Enable SMS notifications on the portal to receive real-time updates on your application status.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Is it mandatory to visit the Passport Seva Kendra in person for a PCC?**
Yes, personal appearance is mandatory for biometrics and original document verification.

**2. How long does the entire PCC process usually take?**
Typically, it takes between 7 to 14 business days, depending on the speed of the local police station's verification report (PVR).

**3. Can I get a PCC if I am currently residing outside India?**
Yes, Indian citizens abroad can apply for a PCC through the Indian Embassy or Consulate in their country of residence. The procedure may vary slightly based on the specific jurisdiction.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Botanical Care and Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory guidelines for the care, maintenance, and health monitoring of indoor and greenhouse plant collections. The objective of this protocol is to ensure optimal growth, aesthetic presentation, and long-term viability of all plant assets. Adherence to these procedures is required to mitigate risks of pest infestation, root decay, and environmental stress.

## 1. Routine Inspection and Assessment
*   **Visual Foliage Check:** Inspect leaves for discoloration, wilting, or signs of pests (e.g., webbing, sticky residue, or spots).
*   **Soil Moisture Analysis:** Utilize the "finger-test" method or a soil moisture meter to ensure substrate hydration aligns with specific species requirements.
*   **Environmental Monitoring:** Verify that ambient light levels, temperature, and humidity remain within the prescribed range for the specific plant variety.
*   **Debris Removal:** Clear fallen leaves or debris from the soil surface to prevent fungal growth and pest nesting.

## 2. Irrigation and Fertilization Protocol
*   **Watering Technique:** Apply water directly to the soil base, avoiding foliage saturation, which can lead to bacterial rot.
*   **Drainage Verification:** Ensure all pots have functioning drainage holes. Remove excess water from cachepots or saucers within 30 minutes of irrigation.
*   **Fertilization Schedule:** Apply water-soluble fertilizer only during the active growing season (Spring/Summer). Follow the manufacturer's dilution ratio strictly; do not exceed recommended concentrations.
*   **Water Quality:** Use room-temperature water. If utilizing tap water, allow it to sit for 24 hours to dissipate chlorine where applicable.

## 3. Pruning and Sanitation
*   **Tool Sterilization:** Clean pruning shears with 70% isopropyl alcohol before and after each plant to prevent the cross-contamination of pathogens.
*   **Deadheading:** Remove spent blooms and yellowing or necrotic leaves to redirect energy toward new growth.
*   **Dust Mitigation:** Wipe leaves gently with a damp, lint-free cloth monthly to maximize photosynthetic efficiency.
*   **Repotting:** Transfer plants to larger vessels only when root-bound, ensuring the use of species-appropriate soil media (e.g., succulent mix vs. tropical peat-based mix).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The "Drip Tray" Rule):** Never leave a plant sitting in standing water for more than one hour; "wet feet" is the leading cause of root rot.
*   **Pro Tip (Acclimation):** When moving a plant to a new location, allow a 14-day transition period before assessing its performance, as plants undergo "transplant shock."
*   **Pitfall (Over-Care):** Most plant fatalities are caused by over-watering rather than under-watering. When in doubt, let the soil dry out.
*   **Pitfall (Pest Introduction):** Always quarantine new plant acquisitions for a minimum of two weeks away from the main collection to ensure they are pest-free.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How do I know if I am watering too much?**
A: Common signs of over-watering include yellowing leaves, a mushy stem base, or a foul, swampy odor emitting from the soil.

**Q: Why are the tips of my leaves turning brown and crispy?**
A: Brown, crispy tips usually indicate low humidity or the accumulation of mineral salts from tap water. Consider using filtered water and introducing a humidifier.

**Q: Should I mist my plants daily?**
A: Misting provides very little long-term humidity benefit and can sometimes promote fungal growth on leaves. It is more effective to use a dedicated room humidifier or a pebble tray for plants requiring high moisture.

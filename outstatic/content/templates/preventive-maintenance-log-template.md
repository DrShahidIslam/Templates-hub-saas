# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance (PM) Log Management

This SOP defines the standardized process for documenting, tracking, and auditing preventive maintenance activities across all operational assets. The purpose of this log is to ensure compliance, extend asset lifecycles, minimize unplanned downtime, and maintain a historical audit trail for regulatory and safety requirements. Adherence to this procedure is mandatory for all maintenance personnel and operations supervisors to ensure data integrity and organizational accountability.

## 1. Preparation and Initialization
*   **Identify Assets:** Ensure every piece of equipment is labeled with a unique Asset ID corresponding to the Master Asset Register.
*   **Define PM Frequency:** Confirm the required interval for the asset (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly, or quarterly) based on manufacturer recommendations or reliability-centered maintenance (RCM) protocols.
*   **Assemble Documentation:** Gather the required PM templates, physical service manuals, and necessary calibration tools before approaching the asset.
*   **Safety Protocols:** Verify that Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures are understood and the required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is available for the specific task.

## 2. Execution and Data Entry
*   **Verify Asset ID:** Cross-reference the physical asset tag with the log template header to prevent misattribution of maintenance data.
*   **Perform Inspection:** Follow the step-by-step technical checklist prescribed in the service manual (e.g., lubrication, bolt tensioning, filter replacement, software updates).
*   **Record Real-Time Data:** Enter metrics immediately (e.g., temperature, pressure, vibration levels, or hours run) into the log rather than relying on memory.
*   **Status Classification:** Clearly mark the asset status as "Operational," "Conditional" (requires monitoring), or "Down/Needs Repair."
*   **Signature/Timestamp:** Every entry must include the technician’s name, the exact date, and the time the PM was completed.

## 3. Review and Verification
*   **Managerial Sign-off:** Submit the completed log to the shift supervisor or maintenance lead for verification and signature within 24 hours of completion.
*   **Exception Reporting:** If a task could not be completed, document the specific reason (e.g., parts shortage, safety hazard) and flag it for immediate rescheduling.
*   **Digital Archiving:** Scan or input the physical log data into the Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) to ensure historical trends can be analyzed.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

**Pro Tips:**
*   **Trend Analysis:** Use the "Notes" section of your template to track subtle changes (e.g., "Slight increase in motor noise compared to last month"). This allows for predictive maintenance before a failure occurs.
*   **Standardize Language:** Use a consistent set of status codes (e.g., OK, N/A, FAIL, RPR) to make data easier to filter in digital dashboards.
*   **Photographic Evidence:** For complex repairs or critical wear points, attach a date-stamped photo to the log entry to provide visual context for future audits.

**Common Pitfalls:**
*   **"Dry-labbing":** Never pre-sign or estimate metrics. Falsifying maintenance logs creates massive safety liabilities and voided manufacturer warranties.
*   **Vague Descriptions:** Avoid writing "Fixed" or "Checked." Always state what was done (e.g., "Replaced 5-micron intake filter" or "Lubricated drive chain with Lithium Grease #2").
*   **Failure to Escalate:** Do not ignore "Conditional" readings. If a machine shows abnormal wear, log it as an issue immediately rather than waiting for the next PM cycle.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if the specific PM template doesn't cover a new issue I found?**
A: Use the "Comments/Observations" section at the bottom of the log to document the anomaly in detail. Then, file a separate "Unscheduled Maintenance Request" (UMR) to ensure the issue is addressed.

**Q: How long must we retain these physical logs?**
A: Per operational standards, all PM logs must be retained for at least three years, or for the duration of the asset's active life, whichever is longer. Always check with your local safety/compliance lead for industry-specific retention laws.

**Q: Can I use a digital tablet to fill out the log?**
A: Yes, digital entry is encouraged as it reduces data loss and improves searchability. However, ensure that the software automatically timestamps the entry and prevents unauthorized editing of past records.

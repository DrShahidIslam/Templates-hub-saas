# Standard Operating Procedure: Computer Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for performing routine preventive maintenance on company computer hardware and software. The objective is to maximize system uptime, extend the operational lifespan of hardware, and mitigate security risks associated with unpatched software or physical hardware failure. Adherence to this checklist ensures a standardized, high-performance computing environment across the organization.

## Phase 1: Physical Maintenance (Hardware)

*   **Shut Down and Disconnect:** Perform a full system shutdown and disconnect the power cable and all peripherals to prevent electrical discharge.
*   **External Cleaning:** Use a microfiber cloth slightly dampened with 70% isopropyl alcohol to wipe down the chassis, monitor screen, and external surfaces. Avoid spraying liquids directly onto any component.
*   **Peripheral Sanitization:** Clean keyboards and mice using compressed air to dislodge debris. Wipe surfaces with disinfectant wipes approved for electronics.
*   **Internal Dust Removal (Desktop Units):** Open the chassis (if warranty allows) and use canned compressed air to gently remove dust from fans, heat sinks, and motherboard components. Maintain a distance of at least 6 inches to avoid static buildup or component damage.
*   **Cabling Inspection:** Inspect power cords and data cables for fraying, exposed wiring, or loose connections. Replace any damaged cables immediately.

## Phase 2: Software & System Optimization

*   **Operating System Updates:** Run the official OS update tool (e.g., Windows Update or macOS Software Update) and install all critical security patches.
*   **Disk Cleanup:** Run system-native disk cleanup utilities to remove temporary files, cached web data, and empty the recycle bin.
*   **Storage Health Check:** Monitor drive health using S.M.A.R.T. diagnostic tools to identify potential hardware failure before data loss occurs.
*   **Software Inventory:** Audit installed applications. Uninstall unused or unauthorized software that may pose a security risk or consume system resources.
*   **Startup Management:** Review and disable non-essential applications that launch automatically at startup to improve system boot times and overall responsiveness.

## Phase 3: Security & Backup Verification

*   **Antivirus/Endpoint Protection:** Ensure the security software is active, updated to the latest definition database, and has completed a full system scan.
*   **Backup Integrity Check:** Verify that automated off-site or cloud backups have been successful within the last 24 hours. Perform a "test restore" of a small sample file to ensure data recoverability.
*   **Password/Access Audit:** Remind users to verify that their password meets complexity requirements and verify multi-factor authentication (MFA) settings where applicable.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Always wear an anti-static wrist strap when opening a computer case to prevent ESD (Electrostatic Discharge) from frying sensitive circuitry.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a log of the maintenance performed (including the date and technician name) to track hardware reliability over time.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use household glass cleaners or ammonia-based products on monitor screens, as they strip anti-glare coatings.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid over-tightening screws when reassembling the computer chassis; this can crack plastic threading or damage internal mounts.

## FAQ

**Q: How often should preventive maintenance be performed?**
A: For a standard office environment, physical cleaning should occur every 3–6 months, while software/security maintenance should be performed monthly.

**Q: Is compressed air safe for all internal components?**
A: Yes, provided you hold the can upright and do not spray liquid propellant. Always hold fans in place with a finger while blowing air to prevent them from spinning at excessive RPMs, which can damage bearings.

**Q: What if I identify a failing hard drive during the scan?**
A: If a S.M.A.R.T. error or unusual drive noise is detected, stop all operations immediately, prioritize an emergency backup of critical data, and initiate the hardware replacement protocol.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Apartment Preventive Maintenance

This document outlines the standard procedure for conducting scheduled preventive maintenance (PM) in residential apartment units. The objective of this program is to extend the lifecycle of major building systems, minimize emergency work orders, ensure resident safety, and maintain high property standards. All maintenance personnel must adhere to this checklist to ensure consistency and mitigate liability.

## H2: Electrical and Lighting Systems
*   **Light Fixtures:** Check all interior lights; replace burnt-out bulbs with energy-efficient LED equivalents.
*   **Outlets and Switches:** Test GFCI outlets in kitchens and bathrooms using the "Test/Reset" buttons. Inspect for loose plates or signs of scorching.
*   **Smoke/CO Detectors:** Test all units. Replace batteries every 6 months (or per local code) and verify the expiration date on the device casing.
*   **Breaker Panel:** Ensure the door closes securely. Visually inspect for signs of arcing or burning smells.

## H2: HVAC and Air Quality
*   **Filter Replacement:** Replace HVAC air filters. Ensure the correct Merv rating is used and the directional arrow is facing the airflow.
*   **Drain Lines:** Flush AC condensate drain lines with an algaecide or vinegar solution to prevent clogs and water damage.
*   **Thermostat:** Test operation in both heating and cooling modes. Ensure the display is clear and calibration is accurate.
*   **Ventilation:** Check bathroom exhaust fans for debris buildup and suction strength.

## H2: Plumbing and Water Management
*   **Leaks:** Inspect under all sinks, toilets, and water heaters for moisture, staining, or active drips.
*   **Toilets:** Check for silent leaks (dye test if necessary) and ensure the flapper is sealing correctly. Confirm the handle mechanism does not stick.
*   **Drains:** Check sink and tub stoppers for proper operation and remove any visible hair or debris from strainers.
*   **Water Heater:** Visually inspect for rust at the base or signs of corrosion on the pressure relief valve.

## H2: Appliances
*   **Refrigerator:** Clean condenser coils to ensure efficiency. Verify the door seal (gasket) is tight by performing a paper-slip test.
*   **Dishwasher:** Run a test cycle to ensure water drains completely and no leaks occur at the door seal.
*   **Range/Stove:** Test all burners for consistent ignition and check oven temperature calibration.
*   **Washer/Dryer:** Clean dryer lint traps and inspect the exhaust hose for blockages. Ensure washing machine hoses are free of cracks or bulges.

## H2: Structural and Safety
*   **Doors and Windows:** Lubricate hinges to prevent squeaking. Ensure locks, deadbolts, and window latches operate smoothly and latch securely.
*   **Caulking:** Inspect tubs, showers, and backsplashes for cracked or missing caulk; re-seal as necessary to prevent water intrusion.
*   **Flooring/Walls:** Note any tripping hazards, such as loose transition strips or carpet tears. Check for signs of unauthorized modifications or pest activity.

## H2: Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always carry a "Maintenance Kit" containing extra batteries, a voltage tester, a multi-bit screwdriver, and a tube of clear silicone caulk. This allows for immediate minor repairs, saving a return trip.
*   **Pro Tip:** Document everything. Take a photo of the completed filter change or the repaired faucet. This creates an audit trail that protects the company during disputes.
*   **Pitfall:** Never enter a unit without proper notice. Always follow the lease agreement's "Right of Entry" requirements (typically 24–48 hours notice).
*   **Pitfall:** Do not attempt repairs that require a licensed master electrician or plumber if not certified. Mismanaged repairs lead to code violations and increased liability.

## H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should we conduct full preventive maintenance?**
A: Industry standard is at least once per year (usually during the annual lease renewal period), though bi-annual checks are recommended for properties with older plumbing or HVAC systems.

**Q: What should I do if I find evidence of lease violations (e.g., unauthorized pets or smoking)?**
A: Do not confront the resident. Document the findings with photos and immediately report them to the Property Manager so they can handle the communication through the proper legal channels.

**Q: Is it necessary to replace smoke detector batteries if they are hardwired?**
A: Yes. Hardwired smoke detectors still contain a battery backup to ensure operation during power outages. These must be replaced annually regardless of the hardwired connection.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Quality Control (QC) Protocols

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the formal framework for maintaining consistent product excellence across all stages of production. The objective of this QC protocol is to minimize variance, ensure compliance with internal benchmarks and external regulatory requirements, and foster a culture of continuous improvement. By adhering to these standardized steps, the operations team will mitigate the risk of defect escape, optimize resource allocation, and preserve brand integrity.

## Phase 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Documentation Review:** Ensure the latest version of the Technical Specification Sheet (TSS) and Bill of Materials (BOM) is accessible.
*   **Calibration Check:** Verify that all measurement tools (calipers, scales, digital sensors) have valid calibration certificates.
*   **Environmental Baseline:** Confirm the inspection area meets required hygiene, lighting, and humidity standards.
*   **Sampling Plan Determination:** Define the Acceptable Quality Limit (AQL) level based on the current batch size and risk profile.

## Phase 2: Execution of QC Inspection
*   **Visual Inspection:** Conduct a sensory assessment for surface defects, color accuracy, and alignment against approved "Golden Samples."
*   **Dimensional Verification:** Perform physical measurements at designated intervals; record data points in the Quality Management System (QMS).
*   **Functional Testing:** Subject a representative sample of units to simulated operational stress tests to verify performance specifications.
*   **Compliance Verification:** Cross-reference physical products with mandatory safety labels, SKU markings, and packaging requirements.

## Phase 3: Disposition and Documentation
*   **Pass/Fail Determination:** Classify the batch based on the pre-defined AQL thresholds.
*   **Non-Conformance Reporting (NCR):** If defects are identified, document the specific anomaly, quantity, and potential root cause in an NCR form.
*   **Segregation:** Ensure "Rejected" items are physically quarantined in a marked "Hold" area to prevent accidental shipment.
*   **Data Finalization:** Digitally sign off on the inspection report and notify the Production Manager of the status (Approved, Rejected, or Rework Required).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Adopt "First-Piece Inspection":** Inspecting the very first unit off the production line prevents entire runs from being ruined by a setup error.
*   **Trend Analysis:** Log recurring issues in a Pareto chart to identify the 20% of causes responsible for 80% of your quality defects.
*   **Cross-Training:** Rotate QC inspectors to prevent "observer bias," where the same inspector overlooks the same recurring subtle defects over time.

### Pitfalls
*   **Confirmation Bias:** Never assume a process is "always good" just because previous batches were perfect. Always inspect with a blank slate.
*   **Inadequate Lighting:** Working in dim environments is a leading cause of missed aesthetic defects. Ensure luxury-standard illumination (min. 1000 lux).
*   **Ignoring Rework:** Re-inspected items must be held to the same standard as new items; do not "fast-track" reworked goods without full validation.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is an Acceptable Quality Limit (AQL)?**
AQL represents the worst tolerable process average when a continuing series of lots is submitted for acceptance sampling. It is the percentage of defects allowed in a batch before the entire lot is rejected.

**2. What should I do if the calibration of a tool expires mid-inspection?**
Stop all testing immediately. Quarantine any products inspected since the last successful calibration check. Re-calibrate or replace the tool before resuming operations.

**3. How do we define "Minor" vs. "Major" defects?**
A "Minor" defect is a discrepancy that does not affect the functionality of the product (e.g., a slight label misalignment). A "Major" defect is a failure that renders the product unusable or unsafe (e.g., electrical short, broken components).

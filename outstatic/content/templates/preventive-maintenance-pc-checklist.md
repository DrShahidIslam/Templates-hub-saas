# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance for Desktop PCs

### Purpose and Overview
This document outlines the systematic procedure for performing preventive maintenance on desktop computer systems. The objective of this SOP is to minimize hardware failure, optimize system performance, ensure data integrity, and extend the operational lifespan of computing assets. Adherence to this checklist ensures a standardized approach to maintaining a healthy IT environment, reducing unscheduled downtime and costly emergency repairs.

## 1. Physical Maintenance and Environmental Hygiene
*   **Safety Protocol:** Power down the PC completely and disconnect the power cord from the wall outlet.
*   **External Cleaning:** Use a lint-free microfiber cloth with a small amount of isopropyl alcohol (70% or higher) to wipe down the chassis, monitor, and peripherals. Do not spray cleaners directly onto the equipment.
*   **Dust Removal:** Utilize a can of compressed air or an anti-static vacuum to remove accumulated dust from intake/exhaust vents, fan blades, and internal heat sinks.
*   **Cable Integrity Check:** Inspect all cables (power, data, peripherals) for fraying, exposed wiring, or loose connections. Replace damaged cables immediately.
*   **Cable Management:** Ensure all cables are neatly routed and organized to prevent strain on ports and to maintain proper airflow.

## 2. Hardware Diagnostic Inspection
*   **Fan Operation:** Power on the system briefly to ensure all case, CPU, and GPU fans are spinning smoothly and are not generating excessive noise or grinding sounds.
*   **Drive Health (SMART):** Use disk utility software to check the health status of Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) or Solid State Drives (SSDs). Look for "Predictive Failure" warnings.
*   **Peripheral Connectivity:** Verify that all ports (USB, HDMI, Ethernet) are clean and functional. Test the responsiveness of mice, keyboards, and other input devices.
*   **Battery Backup/UPS:** If the PC is connected to an Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS), perform a self-test to ensure the battery can hold a charge.

## 3. Software Optimization and Security
*   **Operating System Updates:** Run the OS update tool to ensure all critical security patches and driver updates are installed.
*   **Disk Space Management:** Analyze drive usage; remove temporary files, empty the Recycle Bin, and uninstall unused or redundant applications.
*   **Startup Program Audit:** Disable unnecessary background applications that launch at startup to improve system boot times and resource availability.
*   **Security Suite Verification:** Ensure the antivirus/endpoint protection software is active, up-to-date, and that the most recent full system scan was completed successfully.
*   **Data Backup Validation:** Confirm that the primary backup solution is running correctly and that at least one recovery point has been created within the last 24 hours.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a digital log for each machine. Tracking performance data over time allows you to identify hardware degradation before a catastrophic failure occurs.
*   **Pro Tip:** When cleaning inside the case, hold fan blades steady with a finger or zip tie while spraying compressed air to prevent the fan from spinning too fast and damaging the bearing or back-feeding voltage into the motherboard.
*   **Pitfall:** Never use a household vacuum cleaner inside a PC chassis; it can generate a massive static discharge, which will permanently destroy sensitive electronic components.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "over-cleaning" software. Using third-party "registry cleaners" often causes more system instability than the performance gains they promise.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform physical cleaning on an office PC?**
A: In a standard office environment, a deep clean should be performed every 6 months. If the PC is in a dusty environment, such as a warehouse or workshop, this interval should be reduced to every 3 months.

**Q: If I find a warning in the SMART diagnostic tool, what should I do?**
A: A SMART warning is a precursor to drive failure. Immediately perform a full data backup to a secondary location, order a replacement drive, and schedule the replacement as soon as possible. Do not wait for the drive to fail completely.

**Q: Is it necessary to reapply thermal paste to the CPU during maintenance?**
A: Generally, no. Thermal paste degradation usually takes several years. Unless you observe abnormally high CPU temperatures during normal operation, leave the thermal compound undisturbed to avoid the risk of damaging the CPU pins or motherboard socket.

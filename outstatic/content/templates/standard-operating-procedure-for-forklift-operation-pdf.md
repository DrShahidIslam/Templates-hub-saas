# Standard Operating Procedure: Forklift Operation and Safety

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory safety requirements and operational protocols for the utilization of industrial forklift equipment. Compliance with these procedures is essential to ensure a zero-harm environment, protect inventory integrity, and maintain full adherence to OSHA (or local regulatory) standards. All operators must hold a valid, site-specific certification before engaging with this equipment.

## Phase 1: Pre-Operational Inspection
Before the ignition is engaged, the operator must verify that the equipment is mechanically sound. Any identified defects must be reported immediately to the maintenance department and the unit must be tagged out of service.

*   **Tires and Chassis:** Inspect for debris, significant cuts, or loss of tread. Ensure the chassis is free of loose debris.
*   **Fluid Levels:** Check hydraulic oil, engine oil, coolant, and battery water/charge levels.
*   **Mast and Carriage:** Inspect lift chains for tension and signs of wear. Check hoses for leaks.
*   **Safety Devices:** Test the horn, backup alarm, seat belt functionality, and flashing beacon.
*   **Forks:** Ensure forks are straight, locked into position, and free of cracks or stress fractures.

## Phase 2: Operational Protocols
Safe handling of the forklift requires constant situational awareness and adherence to load-bearing limitations.

*   **Mounting and Starting:** Use the "three-point contact" method when entering the cabin. Fasten the seatbelt before turning the ignition key.
*   **Load Engagement:** Center the load on the forks and ensure the load is tilted back slightly to stabilize the weight. Never exceed the rated load capacity as indicated on the data plate.
*   **Movement:** Always travel with the load trailing if it obstructs vision. Keep forks 4–6 inches off the ground during transit.
*   **Pedestrian Awareness:** Maintain a strict "Right-of-Way" policy for pedestrians. Use the horn at blind intersections and doorways.
*   **Turning and Grades:** Never turn on an incline. Always drive up a ramp with the load upgrade and down a ramp with the load downgrade.

## Phase 3: Parking and Shutdown
Proper storage of the forklift prevents unauthorized usage and potential accidents during non-operational hours.

*   **Location:** Park only in designated staging areas. Do not block emergency exits, fire extinguishers, or walkways.
*   **Neutralization:** Bring the machine to a complete stop and place the transmission in Neutral.
*   **Lowering:** Lower the forks completely to the floor and tilt them forward until they are flat against the ground.
*   **Securing:** Apply the parking brake. Turn off the engine (or switch off power for electric units). Remove the key to prevent unauthorized access.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Treat the forklift like a vehicle, not a toy. Develop a "scanning habit"—constantly move your eyes to check the floor, the path ahead, and the area above (overhead racking) for potential hazards.
*   **Pitfall - The "Free-Fall" Mistake:** Never operate the controls while standing outside the forklift. Even if you are just reaching for a pallet, ensure you are fully seated in the operator’s position.
*   **Pitfall - Speeding:** Excessive speed is the leading cause of forklift tip-overs. Always take corners slowly and decelerate before turning.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain the "Safety Triangle." Your center of gravity is safest when the load is kept low and close to the mast.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if the forklift starts to tip over?**
A: Do not attempt to jump. Stay in the seat, grip the steering wheel firmly, brace your feet, and lean in the opposite direction of the fall. Jumping increases the risk of being crushed by the overhead guard.

**Q: Can I carry a passenger on the forklift?**
A: Absolutely not. Unless the forklift is explicitly designed with a secondary seat and seatbelt for an additional passenger, no one is permitted to ride on the forks or anywhere on the machine.

**Q: How often should I complete the pre-operational checklist?**
A: A pre-operational inspection must be completed at the start of every shift, or every time you switch to a different forklift, even if someone else has already inspected it that day.

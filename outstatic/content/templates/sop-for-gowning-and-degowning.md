# Standard Operating Procedure: Gowning and Degowning Protocol

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory requirements for gowning and degowning within controlled environments. The objective of this protocol is to minimize human-borne contamination, ensure personnel safety, and maintain the integrity of the work zone. Compliance with these procedures is critical to operational success and must be adhered to by all staff, contractors, and visitors entering the facility. Failure to follow these steps may result in cross-contamination or a breach of environmental standards.

## Gowning Procedure Checklist

### Pre-Gowning Requirements
*   Remove all personal jewelry (watches, rings, earrings, necklaces).
*   Ensure hair is clean and facial hair is neatly trimmed.
*   Perform a thorough hand-washing protocol (minimum 30 seconds using approved antiseptic soap).
*   Dry hands completely with lint-free paper towels.

### Donning Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
*   **Hair Cover:** Place the hair net or bouffant cap on, ensuring all hair, including bangs and sideburns, is completely covered.
*   **Shoe Covers:** Apply shoe covers, ensuring they are pulled taut over the heels and sides of the shoes.
*   **Facemask:** Secure the mask over the nose and mouth. Ensure a snug fit by pinching the bridge wire.
*   **Coverall/Gown:** Put on the gown without allowing it to touch the floor or surrounding surfaces. Zip/snap the garment completely.
*   **Gloves:** Don clean, powder-free gloves. Ensure the cuffs of the gloves are pulled over the sleeves of the gown to create a seamless seal.

## Degowning Procedure Checklist

### Exit Protocol
*   **Gloves:** Remove gloves using a "glove-to-glove" and "skin-to-skin" technique, ensuring the exterior of the glove does not touch the skin. Dispose of immediately in designated waste containers.
*   **Gown:** Unfasten the gown carefully. Avoid touching the exterior of the garment. Roll the gown inside-out while removing it to trap contaminants.
*   **Facemask:** Remove the mask by the ear loops or ties. Do not touch the front of the mask. Dispose of immediately.
*   **Hair Cover:** Remove the hair cover and discard.
*   **Shoe Covers:** Remove shoe covers one at a time, ensuring the hands do not contact the exterior surface of the covers.
*   **Final Sanitization:** Perform a final hand-sanitizing step using approved alcohol-based hand rub upon exiting the gowning area.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Always gown from head to toe (top-down) and degown in reverse order (bottom-up) to prevent debris from falling onto sterile surfaces.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a full-length mirror during training to verify that no skin or hair is exposed before entering the clean zone.
*   **Pitfall:** Touching the outside of the gown during removal is the most common cause of self-contamination. Always roll the gown inward.
*   **Pitfall:** Skipping the hand-washing step under the assumption that gloves provide sufficient protection. Gloves can develop microscopic punctures; clean hands are your last line of defense.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I wear perfume or cologne while working in the controlled environment?**
A: No. Scented products can introduce volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and may pose a risk to the air quality standards of the facility.

**Q: What should I do if my gown tears during my shift?**
A: If a breach occurs, you must immediately exit the clean zone, dispose of the compromised gown, perform a full hand-cleaning, and don a new gown before re-entering.

**Q: Why must I wear gloves over the gown cuffs?**
A: Placing the gloves over the cuffs prevents exposed skin or clothing fibers at the wrist from shedding into the work area and creates a secure barrier between the environment and the operator.

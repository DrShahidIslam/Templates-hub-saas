# Standard Operating Procedure: Yara Fertilizer Application and Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized protocols for the handling, storage, and precise application of Yara fertilizer products. As a global leader in crop nutrition, Yara requires adherence to strict application parameters to ensure optimal nutrient uptake, environmental stewardship, and regulatory compliance. This document serves as the operational guide for farm managers and field technicians to maximize yield potential while minimizing nutrient runoff and resource waste.

## Phase 1: Pre-Application Preparation and Calibration
*   **Soil Testing:** Ensure a comprehensive soil analysis has been conducted within the last 6 months to determine specific nutrient deficiencies.
*   **Product Verification:** Confirm the Yara product (e.g., YaraMila, YaraBela, or YaraTera) matches the specific crop growth stage and nutrient requirements defined in the fertilization plan.
*   **Equipment Calibration:** Inspect all spreaders or fertigation systems. Calibrate flow rates and spread patterns to match the calculated application rate (kg/hectare or units/acre).
*   **PPE Compliance:** Ensure all operators are equipped with mandated Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including gloves, goggles, and long-sleeved clothing, as specified in the Yara Safety Data Sheet (SDS).

## Phase 2: Handling and Storage Protocols
*   **Climate Control:** Store fertilizer in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area to prevent hygroscopic absorption and caking.
*   **Inventory Rotation:** Adhere to the First-In, First-Out (FIFO) principle to maintain product integrity and potency.
*   **Contamination Prevention:** Keep fertilizers isolated from herbicides, pesticides, or other chemical agents to prevent cross-contamination or hazardous reactions.
*   **Seal Integrity:** Ensure all bags or bulk containers are tightly sealed immediately after use.

## Phase 3: Field Application Execution
*   **Weather Monitoring:** Do not apply fertilizer if wind speeds exceed 15 km/h (to prevent drift) or if heavy rainfall is forecasted within 24 hours (to prevent leaching/runoff).
*   **Uniformity Check:** Conduct a "tray test" in the field to verify the distribution pattern of the granular spreader.
*   **Speed Control:** Maintain a constant, pre-calculated tractor speed to ensure a uniform application rate across the entire plot.
*   **Buffer Zones:** Maintain a minimum 10-meter buffer zone from all water bodies, drainage ditches, and sensitive ecological zones.

## Phase 4: Post-Application and Documentation
*   **Equipment Cleaning:** Clean spreaders thoroughly with water to prevent corrosion from nitrogen-based salts.
*   **Documentation:** Log the exact quantity applied, the specific field location, the date/time of application, and the weather conditions at the time of deployment.
*   **System Flush:** For fertigation systems, flush irrigation lines with fresh water post-application to prevent nutrient crystallization and clogging.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use Yara’s "CheckIT" app or the "Megalab" analysis tool to match real-time leaf tissue findings with the appropriate corrective Yara fertilizer blend.
*   **Pro Tip:** If using YaraTera (soluble fertilizers), always perform a compatibility test if mixing with other crop protection products to check for precipitation.
*   **Pitfall (Leaching):** Applying too much nitrogen early in the season before the root system is established leads to high leaching risks; split your applications based on crop uptake curves.
*   **Pitfall (Over-application):** Do not ignore the "Burn" risk. Always calibrate accurately to avoid crop toxicity from salt accumulation in the root zone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Can Yara fertilizers be mixed with herbicides?**
Generally, it is not recommended to tank-mix fertilizers with pesticides unless explicitly stated on the product label, as it can interfere with the efficacy of the pesticide or cause the fertilizer to precipitate. Always perform a jar test first.

**2. How do I determine if my Yara fertilizer has degraded?**
If the granules appear sticky, discolored, or show signs of heavy caking (clumping that does not break under light pressure), the product has likely absorbed moisture. Consult your local Yara representative before use, as application accuracy will be compromised.

**3. What should I do if there is a spill?**
In the event of a spill, sweep up the material immediately and place it into a labeled recovery container. If the spill reaches a water source, contact local environmental authorities immediately and follow the protocols listed on the specific Yara SDS for that product.

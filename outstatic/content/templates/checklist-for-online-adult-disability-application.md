# Standard Operating Procedure: Online Adult Disability Application (SSDI/SSI)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides a structured framework for preparing and submitting an online application for Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) or Supplemental Security Income (SSI). Navigating the Social Security Administration (SSA) portal requires meticulous organization and precision, as incomplete or inconsistent information is the leading cause of application processing delays. This guide is designed to ensure all documentation is ready, verifiable, and presented in a format that maximizes the clarity of your claim.

## Phase 1: Pre-Application Preparation
Before accessing the SSA portal, gather all supporting documentation to ensure you can complete the online workflow without timing out.

*   **Proof of Identity:** Locate your Social Security card, birth certificate, and if applicable, proof of U.S. citizenship or legal alien status.
*   **Medical Evidence:** Compile a comprehensive list of all healthcare providers, including names, addresses, phone numbers, and patient ID numbers.
*   **Medical Records:** Gather dates of hospitalizations, emergency room visits, and specific names of all medications, dosages, and prescribing physicians.
*   **Employment History:** Prepare a summary of your work history for the last 15 years, including job titles, dates of employment, and gross earnings.
*   **Financial Records:** Obtain bank statements, investment account balances, and information regarding other sources of income (e.g., workers' compensation, pensions).

## Phase 2: The SSA Online Portal Submission
Follow these steps systematically to ensure the electronic application is captured correctly by the SSA database.

*   **Create or Access 'my Social Security' Account:** Register at ssa.gov. Ensure your profile information matches your legal identification documents exactly.
*   **Initiate the Application:** Select "Apply for Disability" from the dashboard. Save your "Re-entry Number" immediately; this allows you to return to the application if the session expires.
*   **Input Personal Information:** Enter your personal identifiers, checking for typos in SSN or Date of Birth.
*   **Detailed Medical Disclosure:** When listing impairments, use the medical terminology provided by your physician. Describe how these conditions restrict your ability to perform basic work functions (sitting, standing, lifting, concentrating).
*   **Review for Consistency:** Cross-reference your self-reported limitations with the medical records you have gathered. Ensure no dates overlap or contradict each other.

## Phase 3: Post-Submission Workflow
Submission is only the beginning of the administrative process. Maintain rigorous follow-up to ensure your file remains active.

*   **Request Confirmation:** Print or save the electronic confirmation receipt provided at the end of the submission.
*   **Submit Form SSA-3368:** This is the Adult Disability Report. Ensure it is fully completed and matches the online submission.
*   **Authorization for Release:** Sign and submit Form SSA-827 (Authorization to Disclose Information to the SSA). Without this, the SSA cannot legally request your records from your doctors.
*   **Monitor Correspondence:** Check your "my Social Security" portal weekly for status updates or requests for "Consultative Examinations" (CE).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: The "Functional Capacity" Narrative.** The SSA cares less about the diagnosis name and more about the functional limitations. Focus your descriptions on what you *cannot* do (e.g., "I cannot stand for more than 10 minutes" rather than "I have back pain").
*   **Pro Tip: Chronological Organization.** Before the SSA requests records, have a chronological summary of your medical timeline ready. This makes it easier for the claims examiner to digest your case.
*   **Pitfall: Discrepancies.** If your application says you stopped working in 2022, but your W-2s show you worked until 2023, the SSA will flag your application for a fraud review. Always verify dates against official tax documents.
*   **Pitfall: Missing Follow-ups.** Many applicants lose their claims because they fail to respond to an SSA request for an exam within the allotted 10-day window. Set reminders for all incoming mail.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Does having a lawyer help with the initial online application?**
A: While not required, an experienced disability attorney can help ensure your "Functional Capacity" report is phrased in a way that aligns with SSA standards, which may increase the likelihood of approval at the initial stage.

**Q: How long does the online application process usually take to complete?**
A: If you have all your documents gathered, the initial application takes approximately 1–2 hours to complete. If you are gathering documents as you go, it may take several days.

**Q: Will the SSA reach out to my doctor directly?**
A: Yes, once you provide the SSA-827 release form, the SSA will contact your listed medical providers. However, it is your responsibility to ensure the doctors’ offices have your current contact information and are prepared to release your records promptly.

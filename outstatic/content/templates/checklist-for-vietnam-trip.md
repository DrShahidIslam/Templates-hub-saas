# Standard Operating Procedure: Vietnam Expedition Readiness

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory logistical, administrative, and physical preparation required for a successful trip to Vietnam. Designed to minimize friction and maximize operational efficiency, this checklist covers document compliance, health protocols, financial readiness, and situational awareness. Adherence to these protocols ensures a seamless transition upon arrival and mitigates common risks associated with international travel in Southeast Asia.

## Phase 1: Administrative and Legal Compliance

*   **Visa Procurement:** Verify visa requirements based on citizenship. If eligible for an E-visa, apply via the official government portal at least 15 business days prior to departure. Print two hard copies of the E-visa approval.
*   **Passport Validity:** Ensure passport has at least six months of validity remaining beyond the intended date of departure from Vietnam.
*   **Travel Insurance:** Secure a comprehensive travel insurance policy that explicitly includes medical evacuation and coverage for motorbike-related incidents (if applicable).
*   **Passport Photos:** Carry 2–4 extra passport-sized photos for potential visa-on-arrival amendments or local SIM card registration.

## Phase 2: Financial and Connectivity Logistics

*   **Currency Strategy:** Notify your home bank of international travel dates to prevent card freezes. Carry a primary debit card (ATM-friendly) and a backup credit card with no foreign transaction fees.
*   **Local Currency:** Carry $200–$300 USD in crisp, unmarked bills as an emergency buffer. Exchange for Vietnamese Dong (VND) at licensed gold shops or banks upon arrival.
*   **Connectivity:** Pre-order an eSIM (e.g., Airalo or Holafly) or plan to purchase a local Viettel SIM card at the airport arrival terminal for the most reliable coverage.
*   **Offline Mapping:** Download the entire Vietnam region on Google Maps to ensure navigation access in remote areas with unstable cellular service.

## Phase 3: Health and Tactical Gear

*   **Medical Kit:** Pack a specialized kit containing: Imodium (loperamide), electrolyte sachets, broad-spectrum antibiotics (consult your GP), and high-DEET mosquito repellent.
*   **Clothing:** Prioritize breathable, moisture-wicking fabrics. Include one set of "temple-appropriate" attire that covers shoulders and knees.
*   **Footwear:** Bring a reliable pair of broken-in walking sandals or sneakers. Avoid brand-new footwear to prevent blisters during high-mobility days.
*   **Power Management:** Bring a universal power adapter (Vietnam uses Type A, C, and F). Ensure all power banks are stored in carry-on luggage, not checked bags.

## Phase 4: Arrival and In-Country Operations

*   **Transport App:** Install the "Grab" application. It is the gold standard for reliable, price-transparent ride-hailing in Vietnam.
*   **Document Digitalization:** Scan your passport, visa, and insurance policy. Save these to a secure cloud folder (e.g., Google Drive) and keep an offline copy on your mobile device.
*   **Address Cards:** Keep a printed card or saved photo of your hotel’s address in Vietnamese, as many taxi drivers may not speak fluent English.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pitfall - The "Street Food" Gamble:** Avoid ice in drinks in extremely remote areas and prioritize stalls with high turnover. High-traffic stalls ensure ingredients are fresh, minimizing the risk of foodborne illness.
*   **Pro Tip - Domestic Flights:** If traveling between North and South Vietnam, use domestic carriers like Bamboo Airways or Vietnam Airlines. Budget carriers often have significant delays.
*   **Pro Tip - Traffic Situations:** When crossing streets in cities like Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City, maintain a steady pace. Do not stop or retreat; the motorbike flow will navigate around your predictable path.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Is it necessary to tip in Vietnam?**
Tipping is not mandatory or expected in local shops, but it is highly appreciated in the service industry (tour guides, upscale restaurants). Rounding up the bill or leaving 5–10% for exceptional service is considered polite.

**2. What is the most reliable way to get from the airport to the city?**
Always use the "Grab" app or book an official airport taxi at the designated kiosk inside the terminal. Avoid drivers soliciting business in the arrival hall, as they often overcharge or take non-standard routes.

**3. Do I need vaccinations before traveling to Vietnam?**
While no specific vaccines are legally mandated for entry, the CDC recommends being up-to-date on Hepatitis A and Typhoid, as these are common risks associated with contaminated food and water. Consult a travel clinic 6 weeks prior to departure.

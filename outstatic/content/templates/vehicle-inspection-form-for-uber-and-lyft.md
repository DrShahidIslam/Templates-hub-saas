# Standard Operating Procedure: Ride-Share Vehicle Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory inspection protocols for drivers operating on platforms such as Uber and Lyft. To ensure passenger safety, platform compliance, and optimal vehicle performance, drivers must conduct a systematic inspection before each shift. This checklist serves as the primary defense against vehicle failures and regulatory non-compliance, ensuring your vehicle remains eligible for operation at all times.

## 1. Exterior & Safety Systems
*   **Lights & Signals:** Test low beams, high beams, turn signals (front/rear), brake lights, and hazard lights. Ensure all bulbs are functional and lenses are free of cracks.
*   **Windshield & Wipers:** Inspect the windshield for cracks or star chips that obstruct the driver’s view. Verify wiper blades clear the glass without streaking.
*   **Tires:** Check for even tread wear and ensure the pressure matches the manufacturer’s recommended PSI (found on the driver-side door jamb).
*   **Body Integrity:** Ensure there are no sharp edges or loose panels that could pose a risk to passengers or pedestrians. Doors and trunk must open/close securely.
*   **License Plates:** Confirm plates are clearly visible, securely attached, and not obstructed by frames.

## 2. Interior Cleanliness & Passenger Comfort
*   **Sanitation:** Remove all trash, personal items, and debris. Use an interior-safe disinfectant to wipe down high-touch surfaces (door handles, seatbelts, window switches).
*   **Upholstery:** Ensure seats are free of stains, tears, or foul odors. 
*   **Floor Mats:** Confirm mats are properly secured and not interfering with the pedal box.
*   **Climate Control:** Verify that the A/C and heating systems function correctly, as passenger comfort is a primary driver of ratings.
*   **Seatbelts:** Ensure all seatbelts extend and retract smoothly and click securely into the buckle.

## 3. Mandatory Documentation & Supplies
*   **Documentation:** Ensure your current registration, proof of insurance, and the platform-specific vehicle inspection form (if required by your municipality) are stored in the glove compartment.
*   **Ride-Share Trade Dress:** Verify that your Uber/Lyft decals are displayed correctly (typically lower right corner of the windshield or as specified by local law).
*   **Emergency Kit:** Maintain a basic kit including a flashlight, first-aid supplies, and a tire pressure gauge.
*   **Charging Cables:** Ensure at least two charging cables (Type-C and Lightning) are available and working for passenger use.

## 4. Mechanical Fluid & Performance
*   **Fluid Levels:** Check engine oil, brake fluid, coolant, and windshield washer fluid levels. Top off as necessary.
*   **Dashboard Alerts:** Ensure no "Check Engine" lights or warning indicators are illuminated. If a light appears, document it immediately and seek professional service before driving.
*   **Braking Performance:** During the first few minutes of driving, confirm that the brakes feel responsive with no grinding or squealing sounds.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "Go-Bag" in the trunk with extra cleaning supplies, a portable jump starter, and a spare phone charger. This prevents small issues from cutting your shift short.
*   **Pro Tip:** Keep a digital log of all maintenance performed. Most ride-share platforms require proof of service if your vehicle reaches a high mileage threshold.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not ignore minor warning lights. Ignoring an intermittent sensor issue often leads to a major, costly breakdown during peak hours.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid over-scenting the vehicle. While you want the car to smell clean, heavy air fresheners can trigger allergies or discomfort in passengers, leading to lower ratings.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform this inspection?**
A: A full inspection should be performed before every shift. A quick "walk-around" should be conducted whenever you stop for gas or a break.

**Q: What if I find a minor cosmetic issue, like a small scratch?**
A: Minor scratches are generally acceptable as long as they do not affect the structural integrity or safety of the vehicle. However, keep the area clean and consider minor touch-up paint to maintain a professional appearance.

**Q: Am I required to have a professional mechanic sign off on my inspection?**
A: This depends on your local jurisdiction. Some cities require an annual inspection by a certified mechanic, while others allow self-certification via the app. Always check your local DMV or platform "Vehicle Requirements" page to ensure compliance.

# Standard Operating Procedure: UK Student Visa Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for prospective students applying for a UK Student Visa (formerly Tier 4). Navigating the UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) requirements requires extreme attention to detail, as minor documentation errors are the most common cause of visa refusals. Adherence to this protocol ensures that all eligibility criteria, financial requirements, and evidence standards are met to maximize the probability of a successful application.

## 1. Pre-Application Phase: Eligibility & CAS
Before initiating the formal application, ensure you have secured the foundational documents required to prove your academic intent.

*   **Secure Unconditional Offer:** Ensure you hold an unconditional offer from a licensed Student Sponsor (University/College).
*   **Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS):** Obtain your CAS statement from your institution. Verify that all personal details (name, passport number, course title) match your official documents exactly.
*   **Check CAS Expiry:** Ensure your CAS is dated no more than six months prior to your visa application date.
*   **English Language Proficiency:** Verify if your CAS confirms your English level. If not, secure a Secure English Language Test (SELT) certificate (e.g., IELTS for UKVI).

## 2. Financial Evidence & Maintenance
UKVI is strictly regulated regarding financial capability. You must prove you can support yourself without recourse to public funds.

*   **Calculate Funds:** Determine the required amount based on course length and location (Inner London vs. Outer London).
*   **28-Day Rule:** Ensure your bank statement shows the required funds have been held for a minimum of 28 consecutive days.
*   **Closing Date:** The closing date on your bank statement must be no more than 31 days old at the time you submit your application.
*   **Currency Conversion:** Use the OANDA exchange rate calculator to verify your funds meet the GBP requirement if held in a foreign currency.
*   **Parental Consent:** If using parental bank accounts, provide the original birth certificate and a signed letter of consent from parents/legal guardians.

## 3. Documentation Preparation
All documents not in English must be accompanied by a professional translation that includes the date, translator's credentials, and confirmation of accuracy.

*   **Valid Passport:** Must have at least one blank page.
*   **TB Test Certificate:** If you are a resident of a country where a TB test is required, obtain this from a UKVI-approved clinic.
*   **Academic Certificates:** Originals of all qualification certificates listed on your CAS.
*   **ATAS Certificate:** If your course requires an Academic Technology Approval Scheme (ATAS) certificate, ensure this is printed and included.

## 4. Submission & Biometrics
This phase transitions the process from document collection to official legal filing.

*   **Complete Online Form:** Fill out the Student Visa application on the GOV.UK portal.
*   **Pay Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS):** Complete the payment for the healthcare surcharge to access the National Health Service (NHS).
*   **Pay Visa Fee:** Finalize the application fee payment.
*   **Biometric Appointment:** Schedule your appointment at a Visa Application Centre (VAC) to provide fingerprints and a digital photograph.
*   **Document Upload:** Upload all scanned documents to the designated portal prior to the biometric appointment.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The 28-Day Trap:** The most frequent reason for refusal is the "28-day rule." If your balance dips even one penny below the required threshold for a single day during the 28-day period, your application will be refused.
*   **Formatting Matters:** When scanning documents, use high-resolution color scans. Poor-quality or black-and-white copies can cause processing delays.
*   **Consistency:** Ensure your name appears exactly the same on your CAS, passport, and bank statements. If your name format differs (e.g., different middle name order), provide a formal affidavit.
*   **Do Not Book Travel:** Do not finalize non-refundable travel arrangements until you have physical possession of your visa, as processing times can vary significantly based on peak demand.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I use a digital bank statement for my application?**
A: Yes, but it must be stamped by the bank on every page, or accompanied by a formal covering letter from the bank on their official letterhead verifying the authenticity of the documents.

**Q: What happens if I miss my biometric appointment?**
A: Your application will likely be treated as withdrawn. You must cancel or reschedule through the portal at least 48 hours in advance to avoid penalties.

**Q: Does the Student Visa allow me to bring dependents?**
A: Generally, only postgraduate research students or students on government-sponsored courses lasting at least six months can bring dependents. Always check the current policy on the GOV.UK website as this is subject to frequent change.

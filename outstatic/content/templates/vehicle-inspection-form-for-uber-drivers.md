# Standard Operating Procedure: Vehicle Inspection Protocol

As an Uber driver, the safety and reliability of your vehicle are the foundation of your professional reputation and passenger rating. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for conducting a thorough pre-shift inspection. By following this protocol, you ensure compliance with platform safety requirements, minimize the risk of mechanical failure during service, and maintain the aesthetic standards expected by passengers. Consistent inspections not only protect your passengers but also safeguard your vehicle investment and long-term earnings potential.

## Exterior Inspection
The exterior is the first point of contact for the passenger. A well-maintained exterior signals professionalism and safety.

*   **Tires:** Check all four tires (and the spare) for adequate tread depth and ensure they are inflated to the manufacturer’s recommended PSI. Inspect sidewalls for bulges, cracks, or embedded debris.
*   **Lights:** Verify that all headlights, high beams, tail lights, turn signals, brake lights, and hazard lights are fully functional. 
*   **Body Integrity:** Scan for significant dents, deep scratches, or loose trim. Ensure windows and mirrors are clean and free of cracks or chips that obstruct vision.
*   **Wipers:** Inspect wiper blades for wear or streaking and ensure the windshield washer fluid reservoir is topped off.
*   **License Plates:** Confirm plates are clean, legible, and properly secured in the designated mounting brackets.

## Interior & Passenger Comfort
The interior must be pristine to ensure high ratings and a comfortable experience.

*   **Cleanliness:** Remove all personal clutter, trash, or debris. Vacuum floors and seats, and ensure floor mats are properly positioned.
*   **Upholstery:** Inspect seats for stains, rips, or odors. Use an odor-neutralizer if necessary (avoid heavy synthetic fragrances).
*   **Safety Belts:** Ensure all passenger seat belts extend, retract, and latch smoothly without obstruction.
*   **Climate Control:** Confirm the A/C and heating systems are operating correctly and the cabin air filter is clean to ensure optimal airflow.
*   **Tech & Connectivity:** Ensure the Uber app device mount is secure, your phone is charging, and your auxiliary charging cables for passengers are functional.

## Mechanical & Fluid Systems
Mechanical reliability prevents downtime and mid-trip emergencies.

*   **Fluid Levels:** Check the engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, and power steering fluid levels. Top off as required.
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Verify that no warning lights (Check Engine, TPMS, Oil Pressure) are illuminated upon startup.
*   **Brakes:** Perform a low-speed "brake test" once in motion to ensure the pedal feels firm and the vehicle stops without pulling to either side.
*   **Horn:** Test the horn to ensure it is audible and functional for defensive driving purposes.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **The "Five-Minute Walkaround":** Create a muscle-memory routine where you walk in a full circle around the vehicle before every shift. It takes less than 120 seconds but identifies issues before they become road-side emergencies.
*   **Emergency Kit:** Maintain a small kit in your trunk containing a flashlight, a basic toolset, an umbrella, and a first-aid kit.
*   **Lighting Audit:** Use your smartphone camera to record yourself testing turn signals and brake lights; the reflection in a storefront window or your garage wall makes it easy to spot a blown bulb alone.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **The "Out of Sight" Trap:** Do not ignore minor fluid drips under the vehicle. A small puddle today is a major engine failure tomorrow.
*   **Aesthetic Neglect:** Never underestimate the impact of a clean vehicle. Dirt or grime is the fastest way to drop your star rating, regardless of your driving skill.
*   **Over-Reliance on Sensors:** While modern cars have warning sensors, they can malfunction. Never rely solely on dashboard lights; perform physical checks of tires and fluids regularly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How often should I perform this inspection?**
Ideally, a brief walkaround should occur before every time you log onto the app. A comprehensive inspection involving fluid levels and tire pressure should be conducted at least once per week.

**2. What should I do if I find a mechanical issue during my inspection?**
Do not go online if the vehicle has a safety-critical issue (e.g., brake failure, bald tires, or engine warning lights). Contact a professional mechanic immediately to document and repair the issue to ensure passenger safety.

**3. Does Uber provide a specific inspection form?**
Yes, Uber requires a formal vehicle inspection by a certified mechanic for new accounts and annual renewals. Check your local Uber Hub or the "Vehicle Documents" section of your driver app to download the specific form required for your city's regulations.

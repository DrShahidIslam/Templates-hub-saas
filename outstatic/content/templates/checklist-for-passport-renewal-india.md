# Standard Operating Procedure: Passport Renewal Process (India)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols and administrative requirements for the renewal of an Indian passport through the Passport Seva system. Adherence to these guidelines ensures a seamless application process, minimizes the risk of rejection, and reduces turnaround time at the Passport Seva Kendra (PSK). All applicants must ensure that the information provided matches their official government documentation exactly.

## Section 1: Pre-Application Preparation
*   **Check Passport Validity:** Ensure your passport is within one year of expiry or that you have exhausted all visa pages.
*   **Register on Passport Seva:** Visit [passportindia.gov.in](https://www.passportindia.gov.in/) and create a user account.
*   **Collate Identity Proofs:** Gather originals and self-attested photocopies of:
    *   Current Passport (Front and Back pages).
    *   Proof of Address (Aadhaar card, utility bill, or bank passbook).
    *   Proof of Date of Birth (Birth certificate or school leaving certificate).
    *   Non-ECR (Emigration Check Not Required) proof, if applicable (usually Matriculation certificate).
*   **Review Documentation:** Verify that names, addresses, and parent/spouse details are consistent across all documents.

## Section 2: Online Application Submission
*   **Complete the Form:** Select "Re-issue of Passport" and choose the appropriate reason (e.g., expiry, exhaustion of pages, change in personal particulars).
*   **Application Type:** Select between "Normal" and "Tatkaal" based on urgency and eligibility.
*   **Payment & Scheduling:** Pay the application fee online using Credit/Debit card, Net Banking, or SBI Challan.
*   **Appointment Booking:** Select a PSK or Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK) near you based on real-time availability.
*   **Print ARN:** Download and print the Application Reference Number (ARN) receipt.

## Section 3: PSK Appointment Day
*   **Document Checklist:** Organize documents in a clear folder (Originals + Self-attested copies).
*   **Arrival Time:** Arrive at the PSK 15 minutes before your scheduled appointment time.
*   **Process Flow:**
    *   **Counter A (TCS Staff):** Initial verification of original documents and scanning.
    *   **Counter B (Verifying Officer):** Detailed document scrutiny and data verification.
    *   **Counter C (Granting Officer):** Final approval and police verification determination.
*   **Exit:** Ensure you receive the acknowledgement slip before leaving the premises.

## Section 4: Post-Appointment Procedures
*   **Track Status:** Monitor the status of your application via the Passport Seva portal or SMS notifications.
*   **Police Verification (PV):** Ensure you are available at your registered address for the police visit. Keep two references ready with their address proofs and contact details.
*   **Delivery:** Upon successful verification, the passport will be dispatched via Speed Post to the registered address.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "DigiLocker" integration during the online form submission to minimize the number of physical documents required at the PSK.
*   **Pro Tip:** If your address has changed, ensure you have proof of the new address for the past one year to avoid complications during police verification.
*   **Pitfall (Name Mismatch):** Any discrepancy between your Aadhaar name and your current passport name will cause an immediate hold. Get your Aadhaar updated before applying.
*   **Pitfall (Invalid Photos):** Do not attempt to take your own photos for the application. The PSK will capture your biometric photo on-site; professional studio photos are generally not required for the submission packet.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Can I change my address during the renewal process?**
Yes. You can update your address in the application form. You will need to provide proof of the new address, which will then be verified during the police verification stage.

**2. Is police verification mandatory for renewal?**
In most cases, yes, especially if there has been a change in address or if the passport has expired for more than three years. If you are renewing a valid passport with no changes, it may occasionally be skipped, but the system decides this automatically.

**3. What happens if I miss my scheduled appointment at the PSK?**
You are permitted to reschedule your appointment up to three times within one year of your initial payment. If you miss all three, your fee will be forfeited, and you must start a new application.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Driver Vehicle Inspection

## Introduction
Maintaining vehicle safety and cleanliness is paramount to ensuring passenger comfort, regulatory compliance, and high driver ratings. This SOP outlines the mandatory inspection protocol that every driver must perform to ensure their vehicle meets Uber’s safety standards and professional requirements. Consistent adherence to these checks minimizes the risk of mechanical failure, prevents platform deactivation due to vehicle condition, and maximizes passenger trust.

## Step-by-Step Inspection Checklist

## Exterior Inspection
*   **Body Integrity:** Check for significant dents, deep scratches, or damaged panels that could compromise the vehicle’s professional appearance.
*   **Lighting Systems:** Test headlights (low and high beam), taillights, brake lights, turn signals (front and rear), and hazard lights.
*   **Tires:** Inspect tire pressure and tread depth. Ensure tires show no signs of sidewall bulging, uneven wear, or embedded objects.
*   **Windshield & Glass:** Check for cracks, chips, or obstructions that impair driver visibility. Ensure all mirrors are intact and securely adjusted.
*   **Wipers:** Test windshield wipers for streak-free performance and ensure washer fluid reservoirs are topped off.

## Interior Inspection
*   **Cleanliness:** Ensure carpets are vacuumed, upholstery is free of stains or odors, and door panels are wiped down.
*   **Seat Belts:** Verify all seat belts are fully functional, extend/retract smoothly, and click securely into place.
*   **Climate Control:** Ensure the HVAC system is operational and the cabin temperature can be adjusted for passenger comfort.
*   **Door Mechanics:** Test all four passenger doors (and child safety locks) to ensure they open, close, and lock smoothly from both the inside and outside.
*   **Floor Mats:** Confirm mats are properly secured and do not obstruct the driver’s pedal area.

## Mechanical & Safety Essentials
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Confirm there are no active “Check Engine,” “ABS,” or “Airbag” warning lights illuminated.
*   **Braking System:** Ensure the brake pedal feels firm and the vehicle stops without pulling to one side or producing grinding noises.
*   **Fluids:** Verify engine oil, coolant, and brake fluid levels are within the manufacturer’s recommended range.
*   **Emergency Kit:** Maintain an up-to-date kit including a flashlight, reflective triangles, and a first-aid kit.
*   **Documents:** Ensure valid proof of registration, insurance, and the required local vehicle inspection certificate are in the glovebox.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (The "Passenger Lens"):** Perform a "smell test" every morning. Use a neutralizer rather than a heavy fragrance, as many passengers are sensitive to strong odors.
*   **Pro Tip (The Daily Routine):** Integrate this inspection into your pre-shift ritual (e.g., while waiting for the engine to warm up) to save time.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Small Noises):** Never ignore a new rattle or squeak. Small mechanical sounds are often precursors to larger, more expensive failures that could leave you stranded during a shift.
*   **Pitfall (Over-Customization):** Avoid excessive air fresheners, dangling charms, or dashboard clutter. Keep the environment professional, minimalist, and neutral.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should this full inspection be conducted?**
A: A high-level visual "walk-around" should be performed before every shift. A comprehensive mechanical and safety inspection should be conducted at least once a month, or every 3,000 miles, whichever comes first.

**Q: What should I do if my "Check Engine" light turns on while I am online?**
A: Finish your current trip safely, then log off immediately. Do not accept new requests until the vehicle has been diagnosed by a professional mechanic, as driving with active warnings is a safety liability and violates platform policies.

**Q: Am I required to keep physical copies of my inspection forms?**
A: While Uber may only require digital submission for annual compliance, it is highly recommended to keep a digital or physical log of your maintenance history. This protects you in the event of a dispute regarding the vehicle's condition.

# Standard Operating Procedure: New Hire Onboarding Template Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for creating, maintaining, and deploying Google Docs onboarding templates. By utilizing a centralized, version-controlled template system, your organization ensures that every new hire receives a consistent, professional, and comprehensive welcome experience, regardless of their department or role. Following these steps minimizes administrative friction and guarantees that all critical compliance, culture, and technical information is disseminated effectively on Day 1.

## Phase 1: Template Preparation & Standardization
*   **Establish a Master Directory:** Create a dedicated "Onboarding Templates" folder in your Shared Drive with restricted editing access to prevent unauthorized changes.
*   **Define Core Modules:** Standardize the document structure to include:
    *   Company Mission, Vision, and Values.
    *   The "First Week" Roadmap (Day 1, 3, 5).
    *   Key Personnel & Org Chart links.
    *   Tools & Software Access Requirements.
    *   HR Policy highlights (Remote work, benefits, attendance).
*   **Standardize Branding:** Apply company header/footer, typography, and color palette to maintain a professional brand identity.
*   **Create Placeholders:** Use bold, bracketed text (e.g., **[INSERT NAME HERE]**) for information that must be customized per individual.

## Phase 2: Execution & Deployment
*   **Generate Employee Copy:** Right-click the Master Template and select "Make a copy." Never edit the master file for an individual hire.
*   **Naming Convention:** Rename the new file using a strict format: `YYMMDD_Onboarding_FirstNameLastName`.
*   **Permission Settings:** Share the document with the new hire using "Commenter" access rather than "Editor" access to prevent accidental deletion of onboarding steps.
*   **Folder Organization:** Move the completed copy into a folder titled `[Department] > Onboarding > [Year] > [EmployeeName]`.

## Phase 3: Monitoring & Lifecycle Management
*   **Progress Tracking:** Instruct the new hire to use the "Checklist" feature in Google Docs to mark off completed items.
*   **Manager Review:** Schedule a 15-minute sync on Day 3 and Day 10 to review the document progress and address any blockers.
*   **Archive Process:** Upon completion of the 30-day onboarding period, move the document to an "Archived Onboarding" folder for future audits.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Table of Contents" feature (Insert > Table of Contents) at the start of your document. It automatically updates as you add headers, making long onboarding documents much easier to navigate.
*   **Pro Tip:** Embed video snippets (e.g., a welcome message from the CEO) using screenshots linked to external hosting services to make the document feel more interactive.
*   **Pitfall (Link Rot):** Regularly audit the internal links within your template. Broken links to company policies are the #1 source of frustration for new hires.
*   **Pitfall (Overloading):** Avoid "Information Dumping." If the document exceeds 15 pages, break it down into modular "Week 1," "Week 2," and "Resource" documents.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Should I use Google Docs or a dedicated HRIS software for onboarding?**
A: Google Docs are superior for flexibility, ease of collaboration, and cost-effectiveness in small to mid-sized teams. However, if you require legal signature tracking (e.g., NDAs), you should link your Google Doc to a dedicated e-signature platform.

**Q: How often should the Master Template be updated?**
A: Conduct a quarterly audit. Review the template during your recurring operations meeting to ensure department heads have updated their specific contact information and technical requirements.

**Q: How do I handle sensitive information like temporary passwords in these docs?**
A: Never include raw passwords in an onboarding doc. Instead, link to a secure password management tool (e.g., 1Password or LastPass) where the credentials have already been securely shared with the employee.

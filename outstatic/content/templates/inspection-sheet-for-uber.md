# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Vehicle Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory inspection requirements for vehicles operating on the Uber platform. Maintaining a high standard of vehicle hygiene, safety, and mechanical integrity is critical for securing top-tier driver ratings, ensuring passenger comfort, and adhering to local regulatory compliance. Drivers are expected to complete this inspection prior to the start of every shift to mitigate operational risks and ensure a seamless experience for every rider.

## 1. Exterior Inspection
*   **Bodywork Integrity:** Check for significant dents, deep scratches, or detached panels that may violate Uber’s cosmetic standards.
*   **Lighting Systems:** Test all headlights, high beams, turn signals (front and rear), brake lights, and hazard lights. Ensure lenses are clean and free of cracks.
*   **Tires and Wheels:** Inspect all four tires for adequate tread depth (minimum 4/32 inch recommended) and verify proper inflation. Ensure there are no sidewall bulges or exposed wires.
*   **Windshield and Windows:** Check for large cracks or chips in the windshield that obstruct the driver’s view or passenger visibility. Ensure all windows operate smoothly.
*   **Cleanliness:** Ensure the exterior is free of excessive road grime, mud, or bird droppings.

## 2. Interior Inspection
*   **Sanitation and Odor:** Remove all trash, personal items, and lingering odors. Use a neutralizer if necessary; avoid overpowering air fresheners.
*   **Upholstery and Floor Mats:** Inspect seats for stains, rips, or tears. Ensure floor mats are secured properly and are free of debris.
*   **Climate Control:** Verify that the HVAC system is functional, providing both cooling and heating effectively.
*   **Passenger Access:** Ensure all doors open and close easily from the inside and outside, and that all seat belts latch securely.
*   **Dashboard/Electronics:** Ensure the driver phone mount is secure and that all charging cables provided for passengers are functional and tangle-free.

## 3. Mechanical & Safety Systems
*   **Fluid Levels:** Check oil levels, brake fluid, coolant, and windshield washer fluid. Top off if necessary.
*   **Braking System:** Ensure the pedal feels firm and that the vehicle does not pull to one side during braking. 
*   **Emergency Equipment:** Verify the presence of a functional spare tire, jack, and lug wrench. Ensure a first-aid kit and a flashlight are accessible.
*   **Dashboard Warnings:** Confirm there are no active "Check Engine," "ABS," or tire pressure warning lights illuminated.

## 4. Documentation & Compliance
*   **Uber Placards:** Ensure the Uber trade dress (emblem) is clearly displayed in the front and rear windshields as required by local law.
*   **Registration and Insurance:** Verify that current registration and proof of ride-share insurance are in the glove compartment or accessible digitally.
*   **Driver Identification:** Ensure your driver profile is accessible and the phone battery is charged to at least 80% to start the shift.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The "Five-Minute Buffer":** Always perform this inspection 10 minutes before your first trip. Rushing your morning check leads to missed mechanical issues that could ruin your day's earnings.
*   **Pro Tip: Passenger Perspective:** Sit in your own back seat once a week. This allows you to spot stains, smells, or broken features that you might become "nose-blind" or "eye-blind" to as the driver.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Small Sounds:** Never ignore a new rattle or squeak. Small sounds often indicate loose components that can become major mechanical failures on the road.
*   **Pitfall: Over-scenting:** A common mistake is using strong air fresheners to mask bad smells. Most riders prefer a neutral-smelling vehicle; heavy scents can cause motion sickness.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform this full inspection?**
A: You should perform a "quick look" inspection before every shift and a more thorough, itemized inspection on a weekly basis to ensure long-term vehicle health.

**Q: What should I do if my vehicle fails a specific part of the inspection?**
A: If the issue relates to safety (brakes, tires, lights), the vehicle should be removed from service immediately until repairs are completed. Cosmetic issues should be rectified before your next trip to prevent negative feedback.

**Q: Am I required to keep an inspection log?**
A: While Uber may not require a physical log for every single check, maintaining one is excellent evidence for your records if you are ever audited by local transportation authorities or if you need to provide proof of maintenance during a claims process.

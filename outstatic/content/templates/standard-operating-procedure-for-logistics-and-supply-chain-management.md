# Standard Operating Procedure: Logistics and Supply Chain Management

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the foundational framework for maintaining an efficient, transparent, and resilient supply chain. The objective is to standardize processes from procurement and inventory management to distribution and last-mile delivery. By adhering to these protocols, the organization aims to minimize operational costs, mitigate supply chain disruptions, reduce lead times, and enhance overall customer satisfaction through consistent, data-driven logistical excellence.

## Phase 1: Procurement and Inbound Logistics
*   **Supplier Qualification:** Audit new suppliers based on ISO certifications, financial stability, and historical lead-time performance.
*   **Purchase Order (PO) Issuance:** Generate POs via the ERP system, ensuring clear specifications, pricing, and mutually agreed-upon delivery windows (INCOTERMS).
*   **Receiving Protocols:** Inspect incoming shipments for damage, verify quantities against the packing list, and log data into the Warehouse Management System (WMS) within 4 hours of arrival.
*   **Quality Control (QC):** Segregate non-conforming items to a quarantine area and initiate the Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) process immediately.

## Phase 2: Inventory Management
*   **Cycle Counting:** Perform daily rolling inventory counts of high-value SKUs to ensure system accuracy remains above 99%.
*   **Stock Rotation:** Strictly enforce First-In-First-Out (FIFO) or First-Expired-First-Out (FEFO) methodology for all stock movements.
*   **Safety Stock Levels:** Review reorder points quarterly based on consumption volatility and lead-time variability.
*   **Warehouse Optimization:** Slot fast-moving products closer to shipping bays to reduce picking travel time.

## Phase 3: Outbound Logistics and Fulfillment
*   **Order Processing:** Validate orders for financial clearance and stock availability before transmitting to the floor.
*   **Picking & Packing:** Utilize wave picking strategies; ensure all items are scanned for verification during the packing stage to prevent shipping errors.
*   **Carrier Selection:** Select the optimal carrier based on real-time transit cost, reliability scores, and the specific delivery service level requested by the customer.
*   **Documentation:** Generate automated BOL (Bill of Lading), commercial invoices, and tracking links for customer visibility.

## Phase 4: Performance Monitoring and Reporting
*   **KPI Tracking:** Monitor On-Time In-Full (OTIF) rates, cost per unit, and inventory turnover ratios on a weekly basis.
*   **Carrier Reviews:** Conduct monthly performance reviews with logistics partners to address service failures and identify cost-saving lanes.
*   **Incident Reporting:** Log all shipment delays or stockouts in the Central Issues Database for root cause analysis (RCA).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a "Control Tower" approach. Use real-time data dashboards to gain end-to-end visibility, allowing you to pivot before a minor disruption becomes a major bottleneck.
*   **Pro Tip:** Build "Elastic Capacity." Maintain pre-approved secondary suppliers for critical components to avoid single-point-of-failure risks.
*   **Pitfall - Ignoring Data Hygiene:** Inputting incorrect weight/dimension data into the ERP will lead to systemic inaccuracies in shipping costs and warehouse space planning.
*   **Pitfall - Over-reliance on Lean:** While "Just-in-Time" minimizes carrying costs, it lacks resilience. Ensure a buffer of critical safety stock to account for global supply chain unpredictability.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How do we determine the appropriate level of safety stock?**
Safety stock should be calculated using the formula: (Max Daily Usage × Max Lead Time) - (Avg Daily Usage × Avg Lead Time). Adjust this based on your service level targets (e.g., aiming for 98% vs 99% availability).

**2. What should be done if a shipment arrives with discrepancies?**
Immediately document the issue with photographs, note the discrepancy on the carrier's Proof of Delivery (POD), and notify the procurement department to file a claim against the supplier or carrier within the contractually mandated timeframe.

**3. How often should this SOP be reviewed?**
This SOP should undergo a formal review annually, or immediately following any significant shift in business operations, technology integration, or major changes to global logistics regulations.

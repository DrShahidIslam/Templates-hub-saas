# Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance for Industrial Weighing Scales

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory preventive maintenance requirements for industrial weighing scales to ensure measurement accuracy, regulatory compliance, and equipment longevity. Regular maintenance minimizes the risk of drift, sensor fatigue, and mechanical failure, which can lead to costly inventory errors and non-compliance with quality standards (such as ISO/IEC 17025 or local Weights and Measures regulations). This procedure must be performed by authorized personnel according to the manufacturer’s specifications.

## Preventive Maintenance Checklist

## 1. Pre-Inspection & Physical Assessment
*   **De-energize Equipment:** Power down the scale and disconnect the power source (or lock-out/tag-out if connected to hard-wired systems).
*   **Structural Integrity Check:** Inspect the weigh bridge, platform, and frame for signs of rust, cracks, or structural deformation.
*   **Clear Debris:** Inspect the area beneath and around the platform. Remove all dust, spills, debris, or foreign objects that may interfere with the free movement of the load receptors.
*   **Load Cell Inspection:** Check load cell cables for fraying, rodent damage, or moisture ingress. Ensure all connections to the junction box are tight and free of corrosion.

## 2. Mechanical Calibration & Leveling
*   **Verification of Level:** Use a spirit level to ensure the scale is perfectly level. Adjust the leveling feet if necessary, ensuring all feet are in firm contact with the surface.
*   **Corner Load Test:** Place a test weight (equivalent to 1/3 of the scale’s capacity) on each corner of the platform. Ensure the reading is within the tolerance range specified in the manufacturer's manual.
*   **Repeatability Test:** Perform a minimum of three weighings using the same weight at the center of the platform. Results must be identical or within the allowed margin of error.

## 3. Electronic & Software Diagnostics
*   **Indicator/Controller Check:** Inspect the display for dead pixels, screen flickering, or unresponsive keys.
*   **Battery/Power Supply:** If the unit is battery-operated, test the charge retention. If mains-powered, ensure the surge protector is functional and the plug is grounded.
*   **Software Updates:** Verify that the firmware is up to date and that no error codes are stored in the system history.
*   **Communication Ports:** Inspect RS-232, USB, or Ethernet ports for bent pins or signs of oxidation.

## 4. Final Documentation & Certification
*   **Calibration Log:** Record the final calibration values in the master equipment logbook, including the date, technician name, and serial number of the test weights used.
*   **Tagging:** Affix a "Service Performed" sticker onto the scale, noting the date of the next scheduled maintenance.
*   **Sign-off:** Obtain the signature of the department supervisor confirming the maintenance was completed according to protocol.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always allow the scale to "warm up" for at least 15–30 minutes before performing final calibration, as internal electronic components need to reach thermal equilibrium for maximum accuracy.
*   **Pitfall - Overloading:** Never exceed the stated maximum capacity of the scale, as this can permanently stretch the load cell and invalidate the calibration.
*   **Pitfall - Harsh Cleaning:** Never use high-pressure water jets or harsh chemical solvents on a scale unless it is explicitly rated for "Washdown/IP69K" environments.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use certified traceable weights only. Using unverified "dead weight" (like random metal blocks) will lead to significant measurement inaccuracy.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform preventive maintenance?**
A: Ideally, a basic visual and physical inspection should be performed monthly. A full professional calibration check should occur at least every 6 to 12 months, or more frequently if the scale is used in high-traffic or harsh environments.

**Q: What do I do if my scale fails the corner load test?**
A: First, check for debris or mechanical interference underneath the platform. If the issue persists, the load cell may be damaged or the summing board requires adjustment. If you cannot rectify the issue, contact an authorized service technician immediately.

**Q: Do I need to re-calibrate after moving the scale?**
A: Yes. Moving a precision weighing instrument, even a few feet, can alter the leveling and subject the load cells to vibration. Always re-level and perform at least a quick span calibration check after relocating the unit.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Residential Property Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides a comprehensive framework for conducting a systematic inspection of a residential property. Whether for pre-purchase due diligence, routine maintenance, or tenant move-in/out assessments, this guide ensures that no critical structural, mechanical, or cosmetic defect is overlooked. The objective is to establish an objective, evidence-based record of the property’s current condition to mitigate risk and prioritize future capital expenditures.

## Exterior & Structural Integrity
*   **Foundation:** Scan for horizontal or jagged vertical cracks, bowing walls, or signs of settling.
*   **Roofing:** Inspect shingles for curling, missing sections, or excessive granule loss; check flashing around chimneys and vents for rust or gaps.
*   **Siding/Cladding:** Look for rot, water stains, insect damage, or loose panels.
*   **Grading/Drainage:** Ensure the soil slopes away from the foundation; check that downspouts extend at least 4–6 feet from the house.
*   **Windows/Doors:** Verify all frames are square and seals are intact to prevent moisture intrusion.

## Interior & Infrastructure
*   **Walls/Ceilings:** Check for water stains (indicating past/present leaks), cracks, or signs of settling.
*   **Flooring:** Walk the perimeter and center of rooms to feel for soft spots (subfloor rot) or uneven leveling.
*   **Electrical:** Test all GFCI outlets (kitchen/bath/exterior), check for flickering lights, and ensure the breaker panel is organized and unobstructed.
*   **HVAC:** Check the age of the unit, verify the filter cleanliness, and confirm consistent airflow from vents.
*   **Plumbing:** Inspect beneath all sinks for leaks; flush toilets to check for instability or tank issues; turn on faucets to check water pressure and drainage speed.

## Kitchen & Wet Areas
*   **Appliances:** Run cycles on the dishwasher and washing machine; test oven heating elements.
*   **Countertops/Cabinetry:** Check for delamination, water damage near sink cutouts, and ensure cabinet hinges/drawers operate smoothly.
*   **Ventilation:** Confirm bathroom exhaust fans effectively remove steam and moisture to prevent mold growth.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always bring a high-lumen flashlight and a marble. Use the flashlight to inspect dark corners/crawlspaces and use the marble on floors to quickly identify significant leveling issues.
*   **Pro Tip:** Take photos of every major system’s data plate (HVAC serial numbers, water heater manufacture date) to reference later.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not rush the "hidden" areas. Many inspectors focus on cosmetics (paint/flooring) and ignore the attic or crawlspace, where 80% of major structural defects are found.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "settling" cracks. While some are normal, persistent gaps in door frames often indicate active structural movement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should I hire a professional if I use this checklist?**
A: This checklist is excellent for routine maintenance or preliminary assessments. However, for a real estate transaction, you should always hire a licensed, insured home inspector who possesses specialized diagnostic equipment like moisture meters and thermal imaging cameras.

**Q: How often should I perform a full house inspection?**
A: A comprehensive property check should be performed at least twice a year—typically during the seasonal transitions of spring and autumn—to identify issues caused by extreme weather conditions.

**Q: What is the most common "hidden" issue homeowners miss?**
A: Improper attic ventilation. Without adequate airflow, moisture from the home condenses in the attic, leading to premature roof decay, mold growth, and decreased insulation efficiency, none of which are visible from the living space.

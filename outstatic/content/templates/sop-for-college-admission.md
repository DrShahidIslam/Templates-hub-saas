# Standard Operating Procedure: College Admission Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized framework for managing the college admission cycle, from initial inquiry to final enrollment. The objective is to ensure institutional compliance, maintain accurate data integrity, and provide a seamless, transparent experience for prospective students. By following these protocols, the admissions department will maximize conversion rates while upholding the academic standards and values of the institution.

## Phase 1: Pre-Admission and Inquiry Management
*   **Centralize Inquiry Tracking:** Log all inquiries into the CRM within 24 hours of receipt.
*   **Information Distribution:** Send automated yet personalized digital prospectuses to inquiries.
*   **Eligibility Audit:** Pre-screen applicants for baseline academic eligibility requirements (e.g., GPA, prerequisite coursework).
*   **Virtual/On-Campus Tours:** Schedule and conduct personalized tours, ensuring faculty and student ambassadors are briefed.

## Phase 2: Application Processing and Verification
*   **Documentation Intake:** Verify receipt of high school transcripts, standardized test scores (if applicable), letters of recommendation, and personal statements.
*   **Document Authentication:** Validate the authenticity of foreign transcripts through authorized credential evaluation services (e.g., WES, ECE).
*   **Application Fee Reconciliation:** Confirm payment of application fees before moving the file to the review queue.
*   **Internal Routing:** Distribute completed files to the appropriate academic department heads for qualitative review.

## Phase 3: Evaluation and Decisioning
*   **Holistic Review:** Assess candidates based on academic merit, extracurricular leadership, and alignment with institutional mission.
*   **Admissions Committee Meeting:** Conduct weekly consensus meetings for borderline or highly competitive applicants.
*   **Decision Issuance:** Update the candidate portal status and trigger official notification letters (Acceptance, Waitlist, or Denial).
*   **Compliance Review:** Ensure all data entries meet federal/state privacy regulations (FERPA/GDPR).

## Phase 4: Enrollment and Onboarding
*   **Deposit Management:** Track enrollment deposits and update seat capacity dashboards in real-time.
*   **Financial Aid Integration:** Liaise with the Financial Aid office to ensure scholarship packages are attached to admission offers.
*   **Welcome Communication:** Send "Next Steps" packets, including housing forms, course registration guides, and orientation dates.
*   **Final Transcript Verification:** Perform a mandatory audit of final high school transcripts post-graduation to confirm continued eligibility.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use automated email drip campaigns to nurture applicants who have started but not submitted their applications. A gentle nudge can increase completion rates by up to 20%.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain an "Admissions Knowledge Base" FAQ document for counselors to ensure all representatives provide consistent information.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-promising financial aid or scholarships during the recruitment phase. Always phrase aid as "pending official review" to avoid legal complications.
*   **Pitfall:** Failure to archive communication logs. If a dispute arises regarding a promised waiver or deadline extension, lack of documentation will favor the applicant.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How long should we keep application files for rejected candidates?**
In accordance with standard record retention policies and FERPA guidelines, non-enrolled application files should typically be retained for one to three years, depending on your state’s specific privacy laws.

**2. What is the standard protocol for waitlisted applicants?**
Waitlisted students should be updated on their status at set intervals (e.g., 30 days after the enrollment deposit deadline). Always encourage them to submit a "letter of continued interest" to show commitment.

**3. How do we handle discrepancies in transcript data?**
Immediately place the application on "Administrative Hold" and request an official, sealed transcript directly from the issuing institution. Do not accept unofficial copies for final verification purposes.

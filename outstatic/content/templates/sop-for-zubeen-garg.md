# Standard Operating Procedure: Event Management & Artist Liaison for Zubeen Garg

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional protocols, technical requirements, and logistical coordination necessary to facilitate a high-profile performance by Zubeen Garg. As an artist of immense cultural impact and technical complexity, Zubeen Garg requires a streamlined, organized approach to ensure both the safety of the artist and the delivery of a world-class production. This document serves as the definitive guide for tour managers, venue coordinators, and event production teams.

## Phase 1: Pre-Production & Technical Rider Compliance

*   Verify all equipment against the latest technical rider (check for specific microphone preferences and monitor mixes).
*   Confirm the stage layout, including riser dimensions for the band and specific placement for the artist’s center-stage performance area.
*   Coordinate with the sound engineer regarding monitor preferences, specifically Zubeen Garg’s requirement for high-fidelity stage monitoring.
*   Finalize the setlist with the management team and confirm any special guest requirements or collaborative performances.
*   Ensure the venue’s electrical power supply meets the specific voltage and grounding requirements for the band’s gear.

## Phase 2: Logistics, Security, and Hospitality

*   Confirm "Green Room" requirements, ensuring the space is private, climate-controlled, and stocked according to the hospitality rider.
*   Coordinate secure point-to-point ground transportation with a driver experienced in high-profile artist handling.
*   Establish a multi-layered security protocol: define the "inner circle" access (crew/management) and crowd control barriers to prevent unauthorized stage ingress.
*   Assign a dedicated stage manager to act as the primary point of contact between the artist’s tour manager and venue staff.
*   Conduct a site walk-through to ensure clear, unobstructed pathways from the vehicle arrival point to the Green Room and onto the stage.

## Phase 3: Soundcheck and Performance Execution

*   Commence technical soundcheck at least three hours prior to the gate opening; ensure the full band is present.
*   Perform a dedicated "line check" for the artist’s microphone and in-ear monitor systems.
*   Execute a full rehearsal of transition moments, including lighting cues, video content playback, and stage-to-FOH (Front of House) communication.
*   Implement a "lock-down" procedure during the final 30 minutes before showtime to ensure the artist is undisturbed.
*   Maintain a real-time communication channel (radio/comms) between the stage manager, lighting director, and sound engineer throughout the performance.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Always maintain a backup sound system or redundant microphone setup. Spontaneity is a hallmark of Zubeen Garg’s live performances; be prepared for extended sets or improvised interactions with the audience.
*   **Pro Tip:** Ensure the stage floor is non-slip and clear of cables, as the artist is highly active during performances.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not underestimate the demand for "stage access" by VIPs or organizers. Strict adherence to the credentialing system is essential to avoid crowd surges or stage congestion.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring the local cultural context or the specific intensity of Zubeen Garg’s fan base can lead to security lapses. Always staff the front-of-stage barricades with experienced event security personnel.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**What should be the priority if the artist requests a last-minute change to the setlist?**
Immediate communication with the FOH sound engineer and lighting designer is critical. Ensure the team has the flexibility to adapt lighting cues and monitor settings on the fly without compromising safety.

**How is the security perimeter managed during the performance?**
Security should be divided into three tiers: inner stage security, barricade security (front of stage), and venue perimeter security. Access to the stage must be strictly limited to those with verified tour credentials.

**What is the standard procedure for press or media access?**
All media must be vetted by the artist’s management team at least 48 hours in advance. Photos/videos are generally restricted to the first three songs unless specified otherwise in the media contract.

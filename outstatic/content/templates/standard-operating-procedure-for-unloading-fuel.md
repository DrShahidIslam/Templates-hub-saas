# Standard Operating Procedure: Bulk Fuel Unloading

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety protocols and operational steps for the receipt and unloading of bulk fuel into storage tanks. Compliance with these procedures is critical to prevent environmental contamination, fire hazards, and equipment damage. All personnel involved in the unloading process must be trained, authorized, and equipped with appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) at all times.

## 1. Pre-Unloading Inspection and Preparation
*   **Site Security:** Ensure the unloading area is cordoned off with safety cones or caution tape to prevent unauthorized vehicle or pedestrian traffic.
*   **Static Grounding:** Verify the transport vehicle is properly bonded/grounded to the facility’s grounding point before making any hose connections.
*   **Fire Suppression:** Confirm that a fully charged, verified-date fire extinguisher is positioned at the unloading station and easily accessible.
*   **Tank Capacity Verification:** Check storage tank levels via the gauging system or manual dip to confirm there is sufficient ullage (empty space) to accommodate the full delivery volume.
*   **PPE Check:** Ensure the operator is wearing flame-resistant clothing (FRC), nitrile/chemical-resistant gloves, safety goggles, and steel-toed boots.

## 2. Hose Connection and System Integrity
*   **Spill Containment:** Deploy a spill containment kit (absorbent pads and drain covers) under the hose connection manifold.
*   **Hose Inspection:** Physically inspect all delivery hoses for cracks, bulges, or compromised couplings. Do not use damaged equipment.
*   **Verify Product Grade:** Cross-reference the fuel type on the delivery manifest with the storage tank identification label to prevent cross-contamination.
*   **Coupling:** Connect the delivery hose to the storage tank inlet. Ensure the cam-lock or threaded connection is fully locked and secure.
*   **Vapor Recovery:** If applicable, connect the vapor recovery hose to the transport tanker to mitigate volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions.

## 3. Execution of Unloading
*   **Authorization:** Open the storage tank fill valve slowly. Verify that the product is flowing into the intended tank.
*   **Monitoring:** The operator must remain at the connection point throughout the entire offloading process. Never leave the site unattended while fuel is transferring.
*   **Leak Detection:** Observe all connections, valves, and the hose length for signs of seepage or structural failure.
*   **Emergency Shutdown (ESD):** In the event of a leak, spill, or fire, immediately activate the emergency stop button and close the vehicle’s internal safety valve.

## 4. Post-Unloading Procedures
*   **Drainage:** Once the tanker is empty, ensure the delivery hose is fully drained by gravity before disconnecting.
*   **Disconnection:** Close the storage tank valve first, then disconnect the delivery hose. Immediately cap the storage tank fill port to prevent debris ingress.
*   **Documentation:** Sign the delivery manifest, noting the exact time of completion, the final gauge readings, and any temperature-compensated volumes.
*   **Cleanup:** Remove all spill containment equipment and dispose of any used absorbent materials in accordance with hazardous waste regulations.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pitfall - Cross-Contamination:** The most common error is delivering diesel into a gasoline tank. Always utilize a "blind-check" system where the driver and the facility operator independently verify the tank label before opening the valve.
*   **Pro Tip - Static Mitigation:** Always connect the ground cable *before* removing the tank fill cap. Static electricity is at its highest potential during the initial vapor release.
*   **Pro Tip - Weather Awareness:** Avoid unloading during active lightning storms or extreme wind, as these conditions significantly increase the risk of static discharge and spill trajectory control.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if a minor spill occurs during the connection process?**
A: Immediately cease operations. Contain the spill using absorbent pads from the spill kit and notify the site manager. Do not attempt to resume unloading until the spill is cleaned, documented, and the source of the leak is repaired.

**Q: Is it acceptable to leave the unloading area for a few minutes to check tank levels?**
A: No. The operator must maintain "eyes-on" supervision of the transfer process at all times to react instantly to hose failures or tank overflows.

**Q: How often should the storage tank overfill protection be tested?**
A: Overfill prevention systems (e.g., high-level alarms or mechanical shut-off valves) should be tested annually by a certified technician to ensure they remain functional and compliant with local environmental regulations.

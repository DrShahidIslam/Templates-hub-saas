# Standard Operating Procedure: Bank Account Freeze Protocol

## Overview
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory steps to be taken when a corporate bank account is placed under a freeze by a financial institution or regulatory authority. The primary objective of this process is to mitigate financial risk, ensure legal compliance, preserve operational liquidity, and maintain transparent communication with internal and external stakeholders. Execution of this SOP requires immediate coordination between the Finance, Legal, and Executive departments.

---

## Phase 1: Initial Assessment and Containment
*   [ ] **Verify Authenticity:** Confirm the freeze is legitimate by contacting the bank’s relationship manager directly via verified, secure channels. Do not rely on emails or phone numbers provided in the notice itself.
*   [ ] **Identify the Cause:** Determine if the freeze is triggered by a regulatory inquiry (e.g., AML/KYC non-compliance), a court order, a tax lien, or an internal security flagging.
*   [ ] **Secure Access:** Immediately lock down digital banking credentials to prevent further unauthorized attempts to access or transfer funds while the account is frozen.
*   [ ] **Assess Financial Exposure:** Calculate the total balance currently held in the frozen account and list all pending transactions, automated payments (ACH), and payroll obligations that will fail.

## Phase 2: Internal Mobilization
*   [ ] **Notify Executive Leadership:** Provide an urgent briefing to the CFO and General Counsel regarding the freeze, its scope, and potential impact on business continuity.
*   [ ] **Engage Legal Counsel:** Retain or brief external legal counsel specializing in banking/commercial law to prepare a formal response to the freezing entity.
*   [ ] **Pause Outbound Payments:** Direct the Accounts Payable team to immediately halt all scheduled outgoing payments from the affected account to avoid overdraft fees or administrative penalties.
*   [ ] **Redirect Incoming Cash Flow:** Notify clients or vendors who pay into this account to temporarily redirect funds to an alternative, unencumbered operating account.

## Phase 3: Documentation and Resolution
*   [ ] **Gather Evidence:** Compile all requested documentation (e.g., tax filings, board resolutions, transaction justifications) mandated by the bank’s notice.
*   [ ] **Formal Communication:** Submit a written request to the bank (and relevant legal authorities) formally acknowledging the freeze and requesting a specific timeline for resolution.
*   [ ] **Monitor and Escalate:** Maintain a log of all communications with bank representatives. If the freeze persists beyond the initial 48-hour window without a clear explanation, escalate the matter to the bank’s Ombudsman or regulatory oversight board.
*   [ ] **Financial Re-routing:** Execute a temporary liquidity plan to ensure payroll and critical vendor commitments are met through secondary financing or contingency cash reserves.

---

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always maintain a "Backup Banking" strategy. Keep a separate, active operating account at a different financial institution to ensure that a freeze at one bank does not result in a total business shutdown.
*   **Pro Tip:** Keep digital copies of your KYC (Know Your Customer) documents and Articles of Incorporation readily available in a secure vault; these are the most common documents requested to lift a freeze.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not attempt to "bypass" the bank by moving funds out through high-risk channels once a freeze notice is received; this is often interpreted as money laundering and can trigger criminal investigations.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring the "Notice of Freeze" letter. Even if the freeze appears to be a mistake, formal, written communication is required to trigger the bank’s internal investigation process.

## FAQ
**1. How long does a typical bank account freeze last?**
A standard administrative freeze for KYC issues may be resolved in 3-5 business days. However, freezes related to court orders or criminal investigations can persist for months or until a court mandate releases them.

**2. Can I still receive incoming wires while the account is frozen?**
Usually, no. Most freezes disable all inbound and outbound transaction capabilities. Always assume the account is non-functional until explicitly told otherwise by your relationship manager.

**3. Am I entitled to know why my account was frozen?**
Banks are often restricted by "anti-tipping-off" laws (especially in cases of suspected money laundering) from disclosing the specific reason for a freeze. You may only receive a generic notification referencing "internal policy" or "regulatory requirement."

# Standard Operating Procedure: Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) Plant Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety protocols, operational guidelines, and maintenance requirements for the daily operation of an LPG bottling and storage facility. Strict adherence to this procedure is essential to mitigate the risk of fire, explosion, and gas leaks, ensuring the safety of all personnel and the integrity of the facility. All operators must be trained, certified, and equipped with appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) before engaging in any plant operations.

## 1. Pre-Operational Safety Inspection
*   Perform a visual inspection of the plant perimeter for unauthorized personnel or hazards.
*   Verify that all fire suppression systems (hydrants, extinguishers, and water curtains) are pressurized and functional.
*   Conduct a gas leak detection test using calibrated portable sniffers near valve clusters, pump seals, and filling carousels.
*   Ensure all static grounding leads are securely connected to tankers and filling equipment.
*   Confirm that all PPE (anti-static footwear, flame-retardant coveralls, and safety goggles) is worn correctly.

## 2. Product Receipt and Unloading
*   Confirm the tanker is correctly positioned and the engine is shut off.
*   Apply wheel chocks to the tanker to prevent accidental movement.
*   Connect the dedicated grounding cable to the tanker chassis.
*   Perform a pressure check of the receiving storage tank to ensure it is within safe operating limits.
*   Connect the liquid and vapor hoses, ensuring all "break-away" couplings are locked.
*   Open valves slowly to monitor for pressure spikes; observe gauges continuously throughout the transfer.
*   Upon completion, purge hoses of liquid LPG into the flare system or recovery vessel before disconnecting.

## 3. Bottling and Cylinder Processing
*   Perform a pre-fill inspection on all cylinders (check for structural damage, corrosion, and expired hydrostatic test dates).
*   Purge cylinders to ensure no air remains, as air can create an explosive mixture.
*   Place cylinders on the filling carousel; ensure scale calibration is verified at the start of every shift.
*   Monitor filling speed to prevent static accumulation; do not exceed the maximum allowable filling density (85% of volume).
*   Conduct a post-fill leak check using a soap solution or gas detection sensor on the valve and bung area.
*   Attach the safety seal and quality control tag immediately after verifying the fill weight.

## 4. Emergency Shutdown Procedure
*   Immediately trigger the Emergency Shutdown (ESD) button if a major leak or fire is detected.
*   Evacuate all personnel to the designated assembly point upwind of the facility.
*   Activate the remote water spray system to cool storage tanks and prevent BLEVE (Boiling Liquid Expanding Vapor Explosion).
*   Notify local emergency services and the plant safety officer immediately.
*   Do not attempt to re-enter the facility until the area has been declared "gas-free" by safety authorities.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a "buddy system" for all high-risk tasks. A second set of eyes significantly reduces human error during valve operation and leak checks.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always keep the facility "housekeeping" pristine. Oil spills or loose debris can hide minor leaks and provide fuel for a potential fire.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "minor" hissing sounds. Often, what sounds like a small leak is the beginning of a seal failure. Investigate every noise immediately.
*   **Pitfall:** Overfilling cylinders. This is a common and dangerous practice that leaves no room for liquid expansion as temperatures rise, leading to cylinder rupture.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should the plant's pressure relief valves (PRVs) be tested?**
A: PRVs must be tested and calibrated according to local regulatory standards, typically annually or every 24 months, by a certified third-party testing facility.

**Q: What is the most critical step when receiving an LPG tanker?**
A: Ensuring the grounding connection is secure is the most critical step to prevent static electricity discharge, which is the primary ignition source for vapor clouds during transfer.

**Q: Can I use standard electrical tools for maintenance in the filling area?**
A: Absolutely not. Only "Intrinsically Safe" or "Explosion-Proof" tools specifically rated for hazardous, gas-rich environments are permitted within the operational zone of an LPG plant.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Warehouse Operational Excellence

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the foundational framework for maintaining safety, inventory accuracy, and workflow efficiency within our warehouse facility. The primary objective of this document is to standardize daily operations, minimize shrinkage, optimize throughput, and ensure full compliance with Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) regulations. All personnel are required to adhere to these procedures to ensure a seamless supply chain lifecycle, from inbound receiving to final outbound dispatch.

## 1. Receiving and Inbound Logistics
*   **Pre-Arrival Verification:** Confirm scheduled deliveries against the Appointment Log.
*   **Inspection:** Conduct a visual inspection of freight for external damage (e.g., crushed corners, broken seals) before signing the Bill of Lading (BOL).
*   **Unloading:** Safely transfer goods from the carrier vehicle to the staging area using appropriate material handling equipment (MHE).
*   **Verification:** Count physical inventory and compare against the Purchase Order (PO). Note any discrepancies, overages, or shortages immediately.
*   **System Entry:** Update the Warehouse Management System (WMS) to record receipt of goods; tag items with internal barcodes if necessary.
*   **Put-Away:** Move verified stock to assigned bin/rack locations within 4 hours of receipt.

## 2. Inventory Management and Storage
*   **Slotting Optimization:** Place high-velocity items (fast movers) in the "Golden Zone" (waist-to-shoulder height near packing stations).
*   **Cycle Counting:** Perform daily rolling cycle counts to ensure 99.9% inventory accuracy.
*   **Organization:** Maintain clear signage on all aisles and racks. Ensure no items are left on the floor (pallet overhang or floor overflow is prohibited).
*   **Environmental Control:** Monitor temperature-sensitive zones daily; log readings in the climate control register.

## 3. Order Fulfillment and Picking
*   **Pick List Generation:** Review the wave/pick list generated by the WMS.
*   **Picking Protocol:** Follow the system-directed route to minimize travel time. Utilize "Pick-to-Light" or "RF Scanning" methods to ensure SKU accuracy.
*   **Verification:** Perform a secondary scan or visual confirmation at the pack station to ensure the picked item matches the order requirement.
*   **Quality Control:** Inspect items for damage or expiration dates before final packaging.

## 4. Packing and Outbound Dispatch
*   **Packing Standards:** Use appropriately sized shipping cartons; apply correct dunnage to prevent movement and damage during transit.
*   **Labeling:** Apply carrier-compliant shipping labels, hazardous material placards (if applicable), and packing slips.
*   **Staging:** Move completed pallets/parcels to the outbound dock staging area sorted by carrier and service level (e.g., Next Day Air, Ground).
*   **Loading:** Verify BOL and ensure outbound loads are properly braced and blocked to prevent shifting during transport.

## 5. Facility Maintenance and Safety
*   **Daily MHE Check:** Operators must complete a pre-shift safety inspection (brakes, hydraulics, battery) for all forklifts and pallet jacks.
*   **Housekeeping:** Maintain "Clean-as-you-go" policies. Keep aisles free of debris, packing materials, and spills.
*   **Safety Gear:** Ensure all personnel are wearing required PPE (steel-toed boots, high-visibility vests) while on the warehouse floor.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Batching is Key.** Group orders by zone or carrier to reduce transit time for pickers; never pick single orders if batching is possible.
*   **Pro Tip: Cross-Docking.** Identify items that can go directly from receiving to shipping without hitting the storage racks to reduce labor costs.
*   **Pitfall: Poor Slotting.** Keeping slow-moving items in prime floor space will inevitably throttle your throughput. Re-slot seasonally.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring BOL Discrepancies.** If you sign a BOL without noting damage, the insurance claim process for the carrier becomes impossible. Always note exceptions in writing.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should we conduct full physical inventory counts?**
A: While daily cycle counting is mandatory to maintain accuracy, a full wall-to-wall physical inventory count should be performed at least once per fiscal year to reconcile accounting books and identify long-term shrinkage.

**Q: What is the procedure if a picker notices an inventory mismatch?**
A: The picker must "flag" the item in the WMS immediately. Do not guess the count. Move to the next pick and notify the Inventory Lead to conduct a spot-check before the system allows further picks from that location.

**Q: Are unauthorized personnel allowed on the warehouse floor?**
A: No. All visitors must be logged in the visitor registry, wear a high-visibility vest, and be accompanied by a warehouse supervisor or manager at all times to ensure site safety and security.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Vehicle Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides a comprehensive framework for executing vehicle preventive maintenance. Regular inspections are critical to ensuring operational reliability, extending vehicle lifespan, and minimizing the risk of catastrophic failure. This guide is designed to standardize the maintenance process, ensuring that all safety-critical components are evaluated systematically. By adhering to this SOP, maintenance personnel can document vehicle health effectively and maintain high safety standards.

## 1. Engine & Fluids Inspection
*   **Engine Oil:** Check oil level using the dipstick; ensure it is within the crosshatch markings. Inspect for discoloration or metallic debris.
*   **Engine Coolant:** Check reservoir levels when the engine is cool. Ensure the mixture is free of oil contamination or sediment.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Verify fluid is at the "MAX" line. Inspect for dark color, which indicates moisture absorption.
*   **Transmission Fluid:** Check level and color (should be translucent red or amber). Note any burnt odor.
*   **Power Steering Fluid:** Verify reservoir levels and inspect lines for wet spots or leaks.
*   **Windshield Washer Fluid:** Top off the reservoir to ensure visibility during all weather conditions.

## 2. Battery & Electrical System
*   **Battery Terminals:** Check for corrosion (white/blue powder). Clean with a wire brush and baking soda solution if necessary.
*   **Battery Connections:** Ensure clamps are tight and not movable by hand.
*   **Exterior Lighting:** Cycle through headlights (high/low), turn signals, hazard lights, brake lights, and reverse lights.
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Confirm no "Check Engine" or warning lights remain illuminated upon engine start.

## 3. Tires & Suspension
*   **Tire Pressure:** Measure all four tires (plus the spare) using a digital gauge. Adjust to the manufacturer’s PSI found on the driver-side door jamb.
*   **Tread Depth:** Use a depth gauge or "penny test" to ensure tread is above the legal minimum (typically 2/32").
*   **Sidewall Inspection:** Check for cracks, bulges, or dry rot.
*   **Suspension:** Visually inspect struts/shocks for oil leakage and verify that CV boots are not torn.

## 4. Braking System & Visibility
*   **Brake Pads/Rotors:** Look through wheel spokes to estimate remaining pad thickness. Listen for squealing or grinding during a test drive.
*   **Wiper Blades:** Inspect rubber for streaks, cracks, or hardening. Test spray functionality.
*   **Glass Inspection:** Check the windshield and mirrors for cracks or pitting that could obstruct vision.

## 5. Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Record Keeping:** Maintain a physical or digital logbook for every vehicle. Documentation is the most valuable asset when selling a vehicle or proving warranty compliance.
*   **Pro Tip: The "Cold" Rule:** Always check tire pressure when the tires are "cold" (driven less than one mile) to ensure accurate readings.
*   **Pitfall - Overfilling:** Never overfill engine oil or coolant reservoirs; this can lead to pressure build-up and seal failures.
*   **Pitfall - Ignoring Minor Sounds:** Never ignore a new rattle or squeak. Preventive maintenance is about addressing minor issues before they necessitate expensive repairs.

## 6. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I perform this comprehensive inspection?**
A: A preventive maintenance check should be performed every 5,000 miles or every six months, whichever comes first, regardless of the vehicle's age.

**Q: Can I perform these checks if I have no mechanical experience?**
A: Most checks, such as fluid levels, tire pressure, and visual inspections, are non-technical and can be done safely by anyone. If you identify a leak or a complex mechanical failure, consult a certified technician immediately.

**Q: Why is it important to check the spare tire?**
A: Spare tires often lose pressure over time due to slow leaks or temperature fluctuations. Checking it ensures you are not stranded during an emergency when you need it most.

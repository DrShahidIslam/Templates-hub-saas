# Standard Operating Procedure: Electrical Panel Maintenance and Inspection

This document establishes the mandatory protocol for the inspection, maintenance, and documentation of electrical distribution panels within the facility. Adherence to this SOP ensures compliance with NFPA 70E (Standard for Electrical Safety in the Workplace), minimizes the risk of arc flash incidents, and prevents unplanned downtime due to equipment failure. All personnel performing these tasks must be qualified, authorized, and equipped with the appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as dictated by the site’s current arc flash hazard analysis.

## Phase 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
* Verify that the specific panel’s Arc Flash label is visible and legible.
* Review the facility’s single-line diagram to confirm the upstream supply source.
* Perform a "Lockout/Tagout" (LOTO) procedure if physical internal maintenance is required.
* Ensure all required PPE (arc-rated clothing, voltage-rated gloves, face shield, and safety glasses) is inspected and within its testing cycle.
* Gather necessary equipment: Non-contact voltage tester, insulated hand tools, lint-free cloths, and a calibrated digital multimeter.

## Phase 2: Visual and Physical Inspection
* Inspect the panel enclosure for signs of corrosion, water ingress, or physical damage.
* Check that all door latches, hinges, and locking mechanisms are functional.
* Confirm that all circuit breakers are properly labeled and that the panel directory is accurate and up-to-date.
* Open the panel cover (using required arc-rated PPE) and inspect for signs of thermal distress, such as discolored insulation, charred wire, or evidence of arcing.
* Check for "hot spots" using an infrared thermal imaging camera (if available) while the panel is under load.
* Verify that all wiring connections are secure; check for loose lugs or oxidized terminals.

## Phase 3: Cleaning and Documentation
* Use a vacuum cleaner with a non-conductive hose to remove dust and debris from the panel interior; do not use compressed air, as this can force conductive dust into sensitive components.
* Clean the panel exterior with a dry, non-abrasive cloth.
* Document findings in the Electrical Maintenance Log. If any anomaly is detected, trigger an immediate Corrective Maintenance Work Order.
* Reinstall the dead-front cover, ensuring all fasteners are tightened to the manufacturer's specified torque.
* Remove LOTO devices only after the panel is fully secured and the area is cleared of tools.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
* **Pro Tip:** Always maintain a copy of the electrical panel directory in a waterproof sleeve attached to the inside of the panel door for quick reference during emergencies.
* **Pitfall:** Never store combustible materials, chemicals, or storage boxes within the 36-inch clearance zone in front of the electrical panel, as this violates NEC (National Electrical Code) and creates a significant fire hazard.
* **Pro Tip:** Use torque-indicating paint markers on connections once they have been torqued to specification; this allows for a quick visual inspection in the future to see if a connection has vibrated loose.
* **Pitfall:** Do not use aerosol cleaning agents inside the panel unless they are specifically rated for electrical switchgear; common solvents can degrade plastic insulation or leave conductive residues.

## FAQ
**Q: How often should electrical panels be inspected?**
A: At a minimum, panels should undergo a visual inspection every six months. A comprehensive maintenance check, including thermal imaging and torque verification, should be conducted annually or as dictated by the facility’s risk assessment.

**Q: Can I perform maintenance on a panel while it is energized?**
A: Work on energized equipment is strictly prohibited unless it can be demonstrated that de-energizing introduces additional or increased hazards, or is unfeasible due to equipment design. Always follow your site-specific Electrical Safety Program (ESP) and obtain an Energized Electrical Work Permit if required.

**Q: What should I do if I find a loose connection?**
A: Immediately isolate power to that specific circuit or the entire panel (following LOTO protocols). Once confirmed de-energized, re-torque the connection according to the manufacturer’s specifications. If the lug or wire shows heat damage, the component must be replaced rather than simply tightened.

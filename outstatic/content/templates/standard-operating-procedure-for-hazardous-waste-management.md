# Standard Operating Procedure: Hazardous Waste Management

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory protocol for the identification, collection, storage, and disposal of hazardous waste within this facility. Adherence to these procedures is critical to ensure compliance with federal, state, and local environmental regulations (such as EPA/RCRA standards) and to maintain a safe working environment for all personnel. Failure to comply with these guidelines may result in severe legal penalties, environmental contamination, and significant health risks to staff.

## 1. Waste Identification and Characterization
*   **Classification:** Evaluate all waste streams to determine if they meet the criteria for hazardous waste (ignitability, corrosivity, reactivity, or toxicity).
*   **Labeling:** Immediately upon generation, containers must be labeled with the words "Hazardous Waste" and a description of the contents.
*   **Safety Data Sheets (SDS):** Consult the SDS for every chemical byproduct to understand specific handling requirements and disposal restrictions.
*   **Waste Streams:** Separate incompatible chemicals (e.g., strong acids from strong bases) into different accumulation zones to prevent dangerous reactions.

## 2. On-Site Collection and Handling
*   **Container Integrity:** Use only containers that are chemically compatible with the waste being stored. Ensure containers are leak-proof and have secure, airtight lids.
*   **Filling Limits:** Never fill containers to the brim. Leave at least 2–3 inches of "headspace" (ullage) to allow for thermal expansion of liquids.
*   **Secondary Containment:** Place all liquid hazardous waste containers within a secondary containment tray or basin capable of holding 110% of the volume of the largest container.
*   **Spill Kits:** Ensure a fully stocked hazardous material spill kit is located within 25 feet of the accumulation area.

## 3. Storage and Accumulation
*   **Accumulation Area Management:** Maintain designated areas with restricted access. Ensure signs reading "Danger: Hazardous Waste" are clearly visible.
*   **Inspection Log:** Perform and document weekly inspections of all storage areas. Check for leaks, bulging, corroded labels, or open bungs.
*   **Time Limits:** Adhere strictly to accumulation time limits (typically 90 days for Large Quantity Generators). Mark the "Start Date" clearly on every container.
*   **Housekeeping:** Keep aisles clear and ensure that emergency exits remain unobstructed at all times.

## 4. Disposal and Manifesting
*   **Authorized Vendors:** Utilize only third-party contractors licensed for hazardous waste transportation and disposal.
*   **Manifesting:** Ensure all Uniform Hazardous Waste Manifests are accurately completed, signed by the generator, and filed for a minimum of three years.
*   **Waste Profile:** Coordinate with the disposal facility to confirm that the chemical profile of the waste matches their acceptance criteria before scheduling a pickup.
*   **Records Retention:** Keep a digital and physical archive of all manifests, waste analysis reports, and training records.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use color-coded labels or tape to identify different types of waste quickly (e.g., red for flammables, yellow for corrosives).
*   **Pro Tip:** Digitizing your inspection logs via an operations management software can alert managers instantly if a weekly inspection is missed.
*   **Pitfall (The "Empty" Container Trap):** Do not assume a container is non-hazardous just because it is "empty." Residual chemicals can still trigger RCRA requirements. Ensure containers are "RCRA-empty" (less than 1 inch of residue) before disposal.
*   **Pitfall (Incompatible Storage):** The most common safety violation is storing acids and bases in the same secondary containment. Even small drips mixing can create toxic gases or heat.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if a container is found leaking?**
A: Immediately cordon off the area. If you are trained, use the spill kit to contain the leak. If the spill is larger than what you are equipped to handle, evacuate the area and contact the designated Safety Officer or Emergency Response Team immediately.

**Q: Can I consolidate multiple smaller waste containers into one large drum?**
A: Only if the chemicals are chemically compatible and you are specifically trained to perform waste consolidation. Always wear required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) during this process.

**Q: How often must employees receive hazardous waste training?**
A: Personnel handling hazardous waste must receive initial training upon hire and annual refresher training to maintain compliance with regulatory standards.

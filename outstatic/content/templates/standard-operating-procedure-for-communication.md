# Standard Operating Procedure: Organizational Communication

Effective communication is the cornerstone of operational excellence. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the frameworks, channels, and expectations for all internal and external correspondence. By standardizing our communication protocols, we minimize ambiguity, ensure timely decision-making, and uphold the professional integrity of the organization. This document applies to all employees and stakeholders, providing a roadmap for choosing the right medium for the right message.

## 1. Choosing the Right Communication Channel
Before initiating a message, assess the urgency and complexity of the information to select the appropriate platform.

*   **Urgent/Critical (Immediate Action Required):** Use phone calls or in-person/video conferencing.
*   **Routine/Collaborative:** Use project management software (e.g., Asana, Jira) or shared document comments.
*   **Formal/Documentation:** Use official email for records that require a paper trail.
*   **Informal/Quick Sync:** Use instant messaging platforms (e.g., Slack, Teams). 
*   **Company-wide/Strategic:** Use official announcements via the centralized company dashboard or designated leadership channels.

## 2. Best Practices for Professional Email
Email is our primary formal communication tool; ensure every sent message adds value and clarity.

*   **Drafting:** Use clear, descriptive subject lines (e.g., "ACTION REQUIRED: Q3 Budget Approval").
*   **Structure:** Follow the BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front) method—state the objective or request in the first two sentences.
*   **Brevity:** Limit body text to three paragraphs or fewer; use bullet points for lists or data sets.
*   **Attachments:** Ensure files are properly labeled and hosted on cloud drives rather than sent as heavy attachments.
*   **Review:** Proofread for tone, clarity, and grammatical accuracy before hitting "Send."

## 3. Standards for Instant Messaging
Messaging platforms should facilitate speed without compromising organizational focus.

*   **Status Indicators:** Update your status (e.g., "In a meeting," "Deep work mode") to manage response expectations.
*   **Threaded Replies:** Always use reply threads to keep channel feeds clean and context-rich.
*   **Professionalism:** Maintain a business-appropriate tone, even when using emojis or gifs.
*   **Escalation:** If a text conversation lasts more than 10 minutes without resolution, switch to a call or meeting.

## 4. Meeting Hygiene
Meetings are a communication tool, not a default workspace.

*   **Agenda Requirement:** No agenda, no meeting. Every calendar invite must contain a clear objective and a list of required outcomes.
*   **Punctuality:** Start and end exactly on time to respect all participants' schedules.
*   **Documentation:** Assign a note-taker for every meeting. Distribute "Action Items" (Task, Owner, Due Date) within 60 minutes of conclusion.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The "Email-Call" Rule:** If you have exchanged more than three emails regarding a complex issue without reaching a resolution, stop typing and schedule a 5-minute sync call.
*   **The Pitfall of Tone:** Text-based communication lacks inflection. If a topic is sensitive or high-conflict, default to video or in-person communication to avoid misunderstandings.
*   **"Reply All" Discipline:** Only use "Reply All" when every recipient genuinely requires the information contained in the reply.
*   **Notification Management:** Use "Do Not Disturb" functions during deep work blocks to maintain productivity; responding to every notification in real-time is a common productivity killer.

## FAQ

**Q: How quickly should I respond to non-urgent emails?**
A: Standard operating expectation is a 24-hour response window for internal emails, and a 4-business-hour response window for external client inquiries.

**Q: What if I receive a message that is meant for someone else?**
A: Do not simply forward it. Respond to the sender acknowledging receipt, inform them that you are routing the message to the appropriate owner, and loop in the correct stakeholder.

**Q: Are there specific hours for communication?**
A: All communication should occur within core business hours (e.g., 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM). If you are working outside these hours, use the "Schedule Send" feature to deliver the message at the start of the next business day.

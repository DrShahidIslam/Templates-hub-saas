# Standard Operating Procedure: Roof Preventative Maintenance

## Introduction
Proactive roof maintenance is the most effective strategy to extend the service life of a building’s primary structural shield and minimize long-term capital expenditure. This SOP outlines a systematic approach to identifying early-stage defects, such as membrane degradation, drainage obstructions, and flashing failures. By conducting bi-annual inspections—ideally in the spring and fall—facility managers can mitigate the risk of water infiltration, prevent premature material failure, and ensure compliance with warranty requirements.

## Inspection Checklist

## Phase 1: Perimeter and Edge Systems
*   **Parapet Walls:** Inspect masonry and coping caps for loose mortar, cracks, or displaced metal segments.
*   **Edge Metal/Flashing:** Check for signs of wind uplift, loose fasteners, or buckling at the roof edge.
*   **Expansion Joints:** Ensure rubber bellows are free of punctures and that mechanical coverings are securely attached.
*   **Signage/Equipment Mounts:** Inspect any roof-mounted signage or railing anchors for compromised seals or loose bolts.

## Phase 2: Drainage and Water Management
*   **Roof Drains:** Remove debris (leaves, sediment, trash) from drain domes and strainers.
*   **Scuppers and Downspouts:** Verify that water flow is unimpeded and that splash blocks or downspout extensions are correctly positioned.
*   **Standing Water:** Document areas of "ponding" (water remaining 48+ hours after precipitation), as these are high-risk zones for membrane degradation.
*   **Gutters:** Check for sagging gutters, rusted seams, or improper pitch.

## Phase 3: Membrane and Surface Integrity
*   **Punctures and Tears:** Scan the field membrane for physical damage caused by foot traffic or dropped tools.
*   **Seam Integrity:** Probe seams for "fishmouths" or unbonded areas, especially at T-joints in TPO/PVC systems.
*   **Blistering:** Identify air or moisture pockets beneath the membrane surface which may indicate insulation issues.
*   **Vegetation:** Remove all moss, lichen, or plants, as root systems can penetrate membranes and retain moisture.

## Phase 4: Penetrations and HVAC
*   **Pipe Boots:** Check rubber or lead pipe boots for cracking, sun-rotting, or loose clamping bands.
*   **HVAC Curbs:** Inspect the flashing around HVAC units; ensure that service panels are closed and units are not vibrating against the roof surface.
*   **Pitch Pockets:** Ensure sealant in pitch pockets is pliable and not pulling away from the pipe or the pan edges.
*   **Equipment Maintenance:** Look for oil or coolant leaks from HVAC units, as chemical exposure can dissolve certain membrane types (e.g., EPDM).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Photo Documentation:** Always take "Before" and "After" photos of every defect found. This builds a history that is invaluable for insurance claims and proving warranty compliance.
*   **Pro Tip: Safety First:** Never inspect a roof alone. Ensure all personnel are trained in fall protection and OSHA-compliant tethering if working within 6 feet of an edge.
*   **Pitfall: Over-tightening:** Avoid overtightening fasteners during minor repairs; stripping a screw hole creates a permanent leak point that is difficult to seal.
*   **Pitfall: Mixing Materials:** Never use incompatible sealants. For example, applying asphalt-based cement to an EPDM membrane will cause the membrane to swell and fail. Use only manufacturer-recommended adhesives.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How often should preventative maintenance inspections be conducted?**
Standard practice is bi-annually (spring and fall). However, an additional inspection is required immediately following any major weather event, such as high-velocity wind storms, hail, or heavy snow accumulation.

**2. Can facility staff perform these inspections, or do I need a professional?**
Facility staff can perform basic visual inspections and debris removal. However, actual repairs to membranes or complex flashing should be performed by a certified roofing contractor to ensure the work does not void the manufacturer’s warranty.

**3. What is the most common cause of roof failure?**
Neglected drainage systems. When drains are clogged, the resulting ponding water adds immense weight to the structure and keeps the membrane in a constant state of saturation, which significantly accelerates material aging and leakage.

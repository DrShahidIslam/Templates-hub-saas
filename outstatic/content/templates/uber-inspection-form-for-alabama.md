# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Vehicle Inspection (Alabama Market)

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for completing an Uber vehicle inspection in the state of Alabama. To maintain active status on the platform, all partner-drivers must ensure their vehicle meets both Uber’s global safety standards and the specific regional regulatory requirements. Failure to provide a properly completed and signed inspection form—verified by a licensed mechanic—will result in an immediate suspension of your driver account.

## Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Locate Official Form:** Download the official Alabama Uber Vehicle Inspection Form directly from the Uber Driver Dashboard or the Help section of the Driver app.
*   **Select a Certified Facility:** Ensure the inspection is performed by a licensed mechanic or at an authorized automotive service center. Note: Uber does not provide the mechanics; you must arrange this independently.
*   **Document Readiness:** Have your vehicle registration and current insurance document readily available for the inspector.

## H2: Exterior Vehicle Checklist
*   **Headlights:** Ensure both low and high beams are functional.
*   **Taillights & Brake Lights:** Verify all rear illumination, including the third brake light, is operational.
*   **Turn Signals:** Test front and rear blinkers on both the left and right sides.
*   **Windshield:** Inspect for cracks or large chips that obstruct the driver’s view or compromise structural integrity.
*   **Wipers:** Confirm wiper blades effectively clear the windshield without streaking.
*   **Mirrors:** Verify the rearview mirror and both side-view mirrors are intact and adjustable.
*   **Body Condition:** Ensure there is no significant body damage that would reflect poorly on the platform (e.g., jagged edges or detached panels).
*   **Tires:** Check for sufficient tread depth (minimum 4/32") and ensure there are no sidewall bulges or exposed cords.

## H2: Interior Vehicle Checklist
*   **Seat Belts:** Test all seat belts for proper latching, retraction, and locking functionality.
*   **Doors:** Confirm all doors open and close securely from both the inside and outside.
*   **Windows:** Ensure all power windows operate smoothly without jamming.
*   **Horn:** Verify the horn is audible and operational.
*   **Speedometer:** Ensure the gauge is fully functional and accurately displays vehicle speed.
*   **Dashboard Lights:** Confirm no "Check Engine" or other critical warning lights are illuminated on the instrument cluster.
*   **Brakes:** The foot brake must hold the vehicle firmly, and the emergency/parking brake must be fully operational.
*   **HVAC:** Verify the heating and air conditioning systems are functional for rider comfort.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pitfall: Illegible Forms:** The most common reason for inspection rejection is poor handwriting or missing signatures. Ensure the mechanic’s stamp/license number is clearly visible.
*   **Pro Tip: Combined Maintenance:** Schedule your Uber inspection to coincide with a routine oil change or tire rotation. Many shops will offer a "bundle" rate if you are already paying for service.
*   **Pitfall: Expired Documentation:** Ensure your vehicle registration is current. An inspection form will be rejected if the registration on file is expired, regardless of the vehicle's mechanical condition.
*   **Pro Tip: High-Quality Uploads:** When uploading your form to the app, use a scanning app (like Adobe Scan) rather than a photo. Clear, high-contrast digital copies are processed significantly faster by Uber’s automated systems.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use an inspection form from another state?**
A: No. Alabama has specific regional requirements. You must use the Alabama-specific form provided in your Uber dashboard to ensure compliance with local regulations.

**Q: Does the inspection form expire?**
A: Yes. Uber inspections are generally valid for one year, but they may need to be renewed earlier if you receive a notification in your app or if state requirements change.

**Q: What happens if my vehicle fails the inspection?**
A: If your vehicle fails, the inspector will note the issues. You must have these repairs completed by a qualified professional, return to the mechanic to re-verify the repair, and then submit the corrected, signed form to the Uber app.

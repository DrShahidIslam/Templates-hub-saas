# Standard Operating Procedure: Construction Site Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes a rigorous framework for conducting comprehensive construction site inspections. The primary objective is to ensure compliance with architectural specifications, local building codes, and health and safety regulations while identifying potential defects or hazards early in the project lifecycle. This SOP serves as a mandatory guide for project managers, site superintendents, and quality control officers to maintain consistent standards of workmanship and operational excellence across all project phases.

## 1. Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Documentation Review:** Confirm the current set of Approved for Construction (AFC) drawings, change orders, and material specifications are accessible.
*   **Safety Compliance:** Ensure the inspection team is equipped with necessary Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including hard hats, high-visibility vests, steel-toed boots, and safety glasses.
*   **Tools Verification:** Calibrate and verify the functionality of measurement tools (laser levels, tape measures, moisture meters, thermal cameras).
*   **Schedule Coordination:** Notify relevant subcontractors of the inspection timeline to ensure areas of work are accessible and cleared of debris.

## 2. Structural & Foundation Integrity
*   **Excavation & Footing:** Verify soil compaction levels and depth against geotechnical reports. Ensure reinforcement steel (rebar) size, spacing, and clearance meet structural engineering specs.
*   **Concrete Pour:** Check formwork stability, cleanliness, and vibration methods. Verify slump tests and air-entrainment percentages match the mix design.
*   **Framing:** Inspect load-bearing walls, joist hangers, and truss connections. Confirm structural timber is free from rot, warping, or insect damage.
*   **Fasteners:** Ensure the correct gauge and frequency of nails, screws, and anchor bolts are installed as per the structural engineer’s schedule.

## 3. MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, & Plumbing) Systems
*   **Plumbing:** Perform pressure testing on all supply lines and leak testing on drain/waste/vent systems. Verify pipe slope and support spacing.
*   **Electrical:** Confirm correct wire gauges, conduit routing, and grounding/bonding. Ensure all junction boxes are accessible and properly secured.
*   **HVAC:** Check ductwork for airtight seals and proper insulation. Verify condensate lines have the correct pitch to prevent water backup.

## 4. Interior & Exterior Finishing
*   **Building Envelope:** Inspect flashing, weather barriers, and sealants around windows and doors to ensure a watertight installation.
*   **Insulation:** Ensure continuous coverage without gaps, compression, or voids. Verify that vapor barriers are on the correct side of the wall assembly.
*   **Drywall & Paint:** Check for surface smoothness, corner bead alignment, and proper primer application. Ensure finish coats match the approved color schedule.

## 5. Site Safety & Environmental Controls
*   **Hazard Mitigation:** Verify that all fall hazards (open edges, pits) are guarded with approved railing systems.
*   **Erosion Control:** Inspect silt fences, storm drain protection, and tracking pads for integrity.
*   **Housekeeping:** Confirm that primary egress routes remain clear and waste materials are being staged in designated disposal containers.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always utilize digital inspection software to geotag photos of non-compliant items. This creates an unalterable audit trail for insurance and liability protection.
*   **Pro Tip:** Schedule inspections during "critical path" milestones rather than arbitrary dates to ensure maximum efficiency.
*   **Pitfall:** Overlooking "hidden" work. Once walls are closed, defects become exponentially more expensive to fix. Ensure the sign-off happens *before* concealment.
*   **Pitfall:** Failing to document verbal instructions. Any changes requested on-site must be followed up with a written Field Observation Report (FOR).

## FAQ

**Q: How often should formal inspections occur?**
A: Inspections should be conducted at every major milestone (e.g., foundation, framing, rough-in, pre-drywall, and final punch list) and weekly for general safety compliance.

**Q: What should I do if a subcontractor disputes a failure notice?**
A: Refer immediately to the project specifications and the engineer of record. Maintain a neutral, data-driven stance and require corrective action based on documented code requirements rather than opinions.

**Q: Is it necessary to inspect materials upon delivery?**
A: Yes. Incoming materials should be inspected for damage and specification compliance immediately upon arrival to prevent the installation of non-conforming or defective products.

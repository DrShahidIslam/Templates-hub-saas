# Standard Operating Procedure: Generator Preventive Maintenance Documentation

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for executing, recording, and auditing preventive maintenance (PM) for standby and prime power generators. Utilizing a centralized Excel-based tracking system ensures compliance with manufacturer specifications, extends equipment lifespan, and minimizes the risk of critical power failure. This document serves as the instructional foundation for maintenance personnel to ensure all site-specific data is captured consistently within your designated Excel maintenance log.

## Phase 1: Pre-Maintenance Inspection & Documentation
*   Ensure the Excel maintenance file is open or the mobile tablet/logbook is ready for real-time entry.
*   Verify the current "Run Hours" on the generator control panel and cross-reference with the previous entry in the Excel sheet to identify discrepancies.
*   Conduct a visual walk-around inspection, noting any obvious oil leaks, coolant drips, or loose connections.
*   Document the "General Condition" status in the Excel file before initiating any mechanical work.

## Phase 2: Lubrication and Cooling Systems
*   **Engine Oil:** Check oil levels; inspect for signs of contamination (milky appearance). Drain and replace oil/filters according to the scheduled interval in the Excel tracker.
*   **Coolant System:** Check coolant levels and freeze point protection. Inspect hoses for cracks or brittleness. Record the concentration levels in the "Fluids/Chemistry" column of the spreadsheet.
*   **Radiator:** Check for debris buildup in the fins; clear obstructions with compressed air or low-pressure water.
*   **Filter Replacements:** Log the date and "Next Due" service date for air, oil, and fuel filters within the maintenance spreadsheet to trigger future alerts.

## Phase 3: Electrical and Battery Systems
*   **Battery Voltage:** Measure the float voltage of the starting batteries. Record findings in the "Electrical" tab of the Excel sheet.
*   **Terminals:** Inspect battery terminals for corrosion. Clean as necessary and apply dielectric grease.
*   **Battery Charger:** Verify the charger is functioning and maintaining proper float voltage.
*   **Control Panel:** Check for error codes or active alarms. Clear historical logs after confirming no critical issues are present.

## Phase 4: Fuel and Air Intake Systems
*   **Fuel Levels:** Measure and record the current fuel volume. Check the fuel tank for water accumulation; drain if necessary.
*   **Fuel Lines:** Inspect for leaks or signs of dry rot in the fuel supply and return lines.
*   **Air Intake:** Check the air cleaner service indicator; replace the filter element if the indicator is in the "Service Required" zone. Record the replacement event.

## Phase 5: Functional Testing and Final Log Update
*   **Load Bank Test:** Initiate a test run under load (if required by site policy). Monitor voltage and frequency stability.
*   **Post-Run Check:** After a 10-minute cool-down period, perform a final visual sweep to ensure no leaks have developed during operation.
*   **Digital Sign-off:** Input the "Final Status" as "Pass/Fail" in the Excel master sheet and save the file with the date-stamped filename (e.g., GEN_PM_2023-10-27.xlsx).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Automate Alerts:** Use Excel’s "Conditional Formatting" to highlight cells in red when a maintenance due date is within 30 days.
*   **Pro Tip: Version Control:** Always save a backup copy of your Excel tracker in a cloud-based folder (OneDrive/SharePoint) to prevent data loss.
*   **Pitfall: Data Lag:** Never perform maintenance and update the Excel log at the end of the week. Input data immediately upon completion to avoid forgetting critical sensor readings.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Warnings:** Do not clear fault codes in the control panel without investigating the root cause and documenting the resolution in the Excel log’s "Notes" section.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I perform a comprehensive generator PM?**
A: Typically, generators require a light inspection monthly and a full, fluid-service maintenance every 6–12 months, or every 250–500 operating hours, whichever comes first. Refer to your specific manufacturer manual.

**Q: Should I store physical logs or only use the Excel sheet?**
A: It is recommended to keep a physical copy or a photo-scanned copy of the "Maintenance Sign-off" sheet in the generator enclosure, but the Excel sheet should be the primary, searchable "Single Source of Truth."

**Q: What do I do if my Excel log shows a trend of increasing oil consumption?**
A: Any downward trend in performance metrics recorded in your Excel sheet should trigger a "Proactive Investigation" report. Do not wait for a failure; schedule a technician to perform a cylinder leak-down test or internal inspection.

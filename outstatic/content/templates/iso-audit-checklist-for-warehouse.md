# Standard Operating Procedure: ISO Warehouse Audit Protocol

This document establishes the standardized procedure for conducting an internal or external ISO quality management audit within warehouse operations. The objective of this audit is to ensure alignment with ISO 9001:2015 standards, focusing on risk-based thinking, process control, document integrity, and continuous improvement. Adherence to this protocol ensures operational excellence, minimizes non-conformance risks, and maintains organizational compliance.

## 1. Documentation and Record Control
*   Verify that the Quality Manual is accessible and current.
*   Ensure all Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) reflect actual warehouse practices.
*   Check for the presence of document control stamps or digital versioning metadata on all printed forms.
*   Confirm that outdated documents have been removed from the operational floor.
*   Review training records to ensure all staff have signed off on current SOPs.

## 2. Infrastructure and Maintenance
*   Verify that preventive maintenance logs for material handling equipment (forklifts, conveyors, etc.) are up to date.
*   Check for valid inspection certificates for lifting gear and racking systems.
*   Confirm that calibration records for scales, scanners, and temperature monitoring devices are current and traceable.
*   Inspect the facility for adequate environmental controls (lighting, climate control, and cleanliness).

## 3. Inventory Management and Traceability
*   Conduct a spot check on stock accuracy; compare physical counts against the Warehouse Management System (WMS) data.
*   Verify that all materials are clearly labeled with part numbers, batch/lot numbers, and expiration dates (if applicable).
*   Review the segregation process for quarantined, damaged, or non-conforming items.
*   Confirm that FIFO (First-In, First-Out) or FEFO (First-Expired, First-Out) protocols are strictly followed.

## 4. Handling, Storage, and Preservation
*   Inspect storage racking for signs of structural damage or overloading.
*   Check that hazardous materials are stored in designated areas with proper Safety Data Sheets (SDS) displayed.
*   Verify that aisles remain clear and floor markings (pedestrian lanes vs. forklift zones) are visible.
*   Ensure storage conditions (temperature/humidity) are being logged per requirements.

## 5. Non-Conformance and Corrective Action
*   Examine the log of non-conformances (e.g., shipping errors, damaged goods).
*   Verify that Root Cause Analysis (RCA) has been performed for identified non-conformances.
*   Confirm that Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA) have been closed within the designated timeframe.
*   Assess whether past audit findings have been effectively addressed and verified for long-term resolution.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: "Show, Don't Just Tell":** Auditors prefer evidence over verbal confirmation. Keep digital snapshots or physical logs ready for every process mentioned.
*   **Pro Tip: The Walkthrough Test:** Pick a random item in the warehouse and ask the lead to trace its journey from receiving to its current location. This is the ultimate test of traceability.
*   **Pitfall: The "Dusty Manual" Trap:** Creating a high-quality manual that stays on a shelf is a major audit failure. Ensure the documents on the floor match the documents in the manual exactly.
*   **Pitfall: Neglecting External Providers:** ISO audits often flag issues with third-party logistics (3PL) or maintenance contractors. Ensure their certifications and service agreements are part of your audit file.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How far back should audit records be kept?**
A: Generally, ISO standards require keeping records for the retention period defined in your organization's document control policy, but a minimum of three years is the industry standard for audit evidence.

**Q: What happens if I find a discrepancy during the audit?**
A: Do not hide it. Document it immediately as an internal non-conformance. Proactively identifying and correcting your own gaps shows the auditor that your QMS (Quality Management System) is functional and self-correcting.

**Q: Does every staff member need to be interviewed?**
A: No, but the auditor will likely select a sample. Ensure that even temporary staff understand the basic safety and quality protocols relevant to their specific tasks.

# Standard Operating Procedure: New Hire Onboarding via ADP Workforce Now

This SOP outlines the standardized process for onboarding a new employee within the ADP Workforce Now platform. Adhering to this procedure ensures data integrity, compliance with federal and state labor laws, and a seamless transition for the new hire. As an Operations Manager, your goal is to minimize manual data entry errors and ensure all payroll, benefits, and tax configurations are finalized prior to the employee’s first day.

## Phase 1: Pre-Hire Data Entry
*   **Initialize the Hire Record:** Log into ADP and navigate to the "People" tab. Select "Hire Employee" and enter the candidate’s legal name, primary contact information, and personal email address.
*   **Define Employment Details:** Assign the correct "Job Profile," "Department," "Cost Center," and "Manager." Ensure the "Hire Date" matches the offer letter exactly.
*   **Configure Compensation:** Input base salary or hourly rate, pay frequency (weekly, bi-weekly, etc.), and FLSA status (Exempt/Non-Exempt).
*   **Assign Security Access:** Grant the appropriate system permissions based on the employee’s role to ensure they can access their employee portal for self-service enrollment.

## Phase 2: Onboarding Workflow Activation
*   **Trigger Onboarding Tasks:** Navigate to the "Onboarding" module and select the appropriate "Onboarding Template" (e.g., Full-Time, Part-Time, or Contractor).
*   **Review Task List:** Ensure the following items are included in the workflow:
    *   Direct Deposit authorization.
    *   Emergency contact information.
    *   Company policy handbook acknowledgment.
    *   Benefit election timeline notifications.
*   **Send Welcome Invitation:** Dispatch the ADP registration email to the new hire. Verify that the "Start Date" on the invitation allows for timely completion of tasks.

## Phase 3: Compliance and Tax Setup
*   **Form I-9 Verification:** Ensure the new hire has provided Section 1 data. Designate an authorized representative to complete Section 2 within three business days of the hire date.
*   **Federal/State Tax Setup:** Verify that the "Tax" tab is populated with the correct W-4 information provided by the employee during the onboarding process.
*   **E-Verify (If applicable):** If your organization utilizes E-Verify, ensure the data flows from the I-9 section to the E-Verify portal.

## Phase 4: Final Review and Activation
*   **Audit Payroll Profile:** Perform a final review of the "Payroll" tab to ensure all deductions, taxes, and direct deposit details are active and error-free.
*   **Manager Notification:** Send a confirmation email to the hiring manager once the employee has completed their ADP self-onboarding.
*   **Archive Paperwork:** Ensure all digital signatures are captured and stored within the employee’s electronic document file in ADP for audit readiness.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Bulk Hire" feature if you are onboarding five or more employees simultaneously to save time on data entry.
*   **Pro Tip:** Set up "Automatic Notifications" in the ADP settings to remind employees to finish their tasks 48 hours before their deadline.
*   **Pitfall:** Waiting until the last minute to trigger the onboarding email. Always send the invite at least one week prior to the start date to avoid payroll delays.
*   **Pitfall:** Misconfiguring the "Pay Group." This is the most common cause of missing payroll runs. Always double-check the Pay Group against the department schedule.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if the new hire doesn’t receive the ADP registration email?**
A: First, check the "Person Profile" to verify that the email address was entered correctly. If correct, navigate to the employee’s record, select "Account Actions," and click "Resend Activation Email."

**Q: Can I change a new hire's information after I have submitted the hire record?**
A: Yes. As long as the first payroll has not yet been processed, you can edit most fields via the "People" tab. If payroll has already closed, you may need to submit a "Retro-pay" adjustment or a data change request.

**Q: How do I handle missing I-9 documentation?**
A: You must contact the employee immediately. If documentation is not provided by the third business day, follow your internal policy regarding non-compliant new hires, which may include suspension of onboarding until proof of eligibility is provided.

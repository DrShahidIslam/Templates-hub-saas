# Standard Operating Procedure: Office Workplace Inspection

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the requirements for conducting comprehensive workplace inspections within an office environment. The objective is to identify potential hazards, ensure compliance with occupational health and safety (OHS) regulations, and maintain an environment that promotes employee well-being and operational efficiency. Regular inspections are mandatory to mitigate risks such as trip-and-fall incidents, fire hazards, and ergonomic strains. All designated safety officers or facility managers are expected to complete these inspections on a quarterly basis, or following any significant office reconfiguration.

---

## Workplace Inspection Checklist

## 1. Fire Safety and Emergency Egress
* [ ] **Exit Routes:** Ensure all corridors, stairwells, and doorways are clear of boxes, furniture, or equipment.
* [ ] **Signage:** Verify that "EXIT" signs are illuminated and clearly visible.
* [ ] **Fire Extinguishers:** Ensure units are mounted correctly, have current inspection tags, and are not obstructed.
* [ ] **Alarm Pull Stations:** Confirm access is not blocked by desks or storage units.
* [ ] **Emergency Lighting:** Conduct a brief test (where applicable) to ensure backup power illuminates egress paths.

## 2. Electrical Safety and Infrastructure
* [ ] **Cabling:** Inspect all power cords for fraying, cracks, or exposed wiring. Ensure cables are secured via cord covers to prevent trip hazards.
* [ ] **Power Strips:** Verify that power strips are plugged directly into wall outlets (daisy-chaining is strictly prohibited).
* [ ] **Outlets:** Ensure cover plates are intact and that no outlets show signs of heat discoloration or buzzing.
* [ ] **Equipment:** Verify that all office appliances (microwaves, coffee makers, heaters) have been PAT tested (Portable Appliance Testing) as per local regulations.

## 3. Ergonomics and Workspace Configuration
* [ ] **Desks/Chairs:** Ensure chairs are in good working order (adjustable height, back support, wheels).
* [ ] **Monitors:** Check that screens are at eye level to prevent neck strain.
* [ ] **Lighting:** Assess if lighting is sufficient for tasks; ensure no flickering ballasts are present.
* [ ] **Storage:** Ensure heavy items are stored on lower shelves and that overhead storage bins are not overloaded or unstable.

## 4. Environmental and General Sanitation
* [ ] **Flooring:** Check carpets and hard floors for loose tiles, ripples, or slippery patches.
* [ ] **Air Quality:** Ensure ventilation vents are clean and not blocked by files or partitions.
* [ ] **Sanitation:** Verify that waste bins are not overflowing and that shared surfaces are regularly disinfected.
* [ ] **Spill Kits:** If hazardous materials are stored (e.g., cleaning supplies), ensure they are labeled and spill kits are nearby.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips:
* **The "Fresh Eyes" Rule:** Rotate the inspection team every six months. Familiarity often leads to "inattentional blindness," where inspectors stop noticing hazards they pass every day.
* **Digital Documentation:** Use a mobile-based form (e.g., Microsoft Forms or specialized EHS software) to capture timestamped photos of hazards. This creates an immediate, objective record for remediation.
* **Employee Feedback:** Conduct a 2-minute "pulse check" with employees during the inspection. They often know about minor hazards (e.g., a loose floorboard) long before management spots them.

### Pitfalls to Avoid:
* **"Checking the Box":** Never mark an item as compliant without physically testing or inspecting it. Skipping steps renders the safety record legally invalid.
* **Ignoring Near-Misses:** If you see someone stumble but not fall, do not ignore it. Investigate that area immediately for floor level changes or low-visibility obstacles.
* **Ignoring the "Hidden" Areas:** Inspectors often focus on main walkways. Don't forget to check server closets, storage rooms, and breakrooms, where clutter often accumulates.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should these inspections be conducted?**
For standard office environments, a comprehensive inspection should be performed quarterly. However, high-traffic areas or areas with frequent equipment turnover may require monthly spot checks.

**2. What should I do if I find a critical safety hazard?**
If a hazard poses an immediate risk (e.g., exposed live wires or a major trip hazard in an exit path), you must cordone off the area immediately, report it to the Facilities Manager, and log a formal maintenance request before leaving the site.

**3. Is it necessary to involve employees in the inspection process?**
While the safety officer is responsible for the formal report, it is highly recommended to include at least one non-managerial employee. This promotes a "safety culture" and ensures that the inspection covers areas that employees interact with most frequently.

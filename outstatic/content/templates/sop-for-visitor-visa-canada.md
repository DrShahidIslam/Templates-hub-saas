# Standard Operating Procedure: Canada Visitor Visa (TRV) Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory administrative process for preparing, submitting, and managing a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) application for Canada. Adherence to this protocol ensures that all documentation meets Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) standards, minimizing the risk of application refusal due to technical errors or incomplete evidentiary support.

## Phase 1: Document Gathering and Eligibility Verification
*   **Verify Travel Purpose:** Determine if the visit is for tourism, business, or family visitation.
*   **Passport Validity:** Ensure the passport is valid for at least six months beyond the intended date of departure from Canada.
*   **Digital Scan Requirements:** All documents must be scanned in high resolution (min 300 DPI), saved in PDF format, and remain under 4MB per file.
*   **Proof of Financial Support:** Compile the last 4–6 months of personal bank statements, pay stubs, and employment verification letters.
*   **Travel Itinerary:** Prepare a formal cover letter outlining the purpose of the trip, duration of stay, and proof of onward travel arrangements.

## Phase 2: Application Portal and Forms
*   **GCKey Registration:** Create or log into a secure GCKey account via the official IRCC portal.
*   **Eligibility Questionnaire:** Answer the IRCC online portal questions accurately to generate a personalized document checklist.
*   **Form IMM 5257:** Complete the Application for Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa) made outside of Canada. Ensure no gaps exist in the "Background Information" section.
*   **Form IMM 5645:** Complete the Family Information form; ensure every field is populated or marked "N/A."
*   **Schedule 1 (If Applicable):** Complete if you have a history of military service, government roles, or membership in certain organizations.

## Phase 3: Final Review and Submission
*   **Biometrics Check:** Verify if biometrics (fingerprints and photo) are required for your country of residence.
*   **Letter of Invitation:** If visiting family/friends, ensure the host has provided a signed letter including their Canadian status and contact information.
*   **Final Quality Assurance:** Double-check that all uploaded documents match the specific file requirements listed in the portal.
*   **Payment:** Process the application fee and biometrics fee via credit or debit card through the secure portal.
*   **Submission Tracking:** Save the submission confirmation receipt and note the Application Number for future tracking.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Ties to Home" Factor:** The most common reason for refusal is a lack of evidence demonstrating strong ties to your home country (e.g., property ownership, full-time employment, family responsibilities). Do not overlook this section.
*   **Travel History:** Be meticulously accurate regarding previous visa refusals from any country. Concealment of past rejections is grounds for a permanent ban due to misrepresentation.
*   **Bank Balance Spikes:** Avoid large, unexplained deposits into your bank account immediately before applying, as this raises red flags regarding the source of funds.
*   **Drafting the Cover Letter:** Write a professional, concise cover letter (1 page maximum) that acts as an "executive summary" of your application.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to book my flight before I apply for the visa?**
A: No. IRCC explicitly advises against purchasing non-refundable tickets before receiving your visa, as there is no guarantee of approval. A proposed itinerary or flight reservation is sufficient.

**Q: What is the purpose of biometrics?**
A: Biometrics are used to confirm your identity. Once submitted, your biometrics are generally valid for 10 years, which simplifies the process for future visa applications.

**Q: How long does the processing take?**
A: Processing times fluctuate based on the volume of applications and your country of residence. Always check the official IRCC "Check processing times" tool to get an estimate for your specific region.

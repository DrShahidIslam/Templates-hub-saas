# Standard Operating Procedure: Health and Safety Audit

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for conducting a comprehensive Health and Safety (H&S) audit. The objective of this audit is to identify workplace hazards, verify compliance with local regulatory requirements (e.g., OSHA, HSE), and ensure that all preventative measures are functioning as intended. This document serves as a baseline to mitigate risk, protect organizational assets, and foster a culture of safety.

## 1. Documentation and Administrative Review
*   **Safety Policy:** Verify that the current Health and Safety Policy is signed, dated, and displayed in a prominent location.
*   **Training Records:** Audit personnel files to ensure all employees have completed mandatory safety training, including refresher courses.
*   **Incident Logs:** Review the accident/near-miss register from the last 12 months to identify recurring themes or patterns.
*   **Risk Assessments:** Confirm that written risk assessments exist for all core operations and have been reviewed within the last 12 months.
*   **Permit-to-Work:** Examine logs for high-risk activities (e.g., hot work, working at heights) to ensure proper authorization.

## 2. Facility and Infrastructure Inspection
*   **Emergency Exits:** Verify all exit paths are clearly marked, illuminated, and entirely free of obstructions.
*   **Fire Safety:** Confirm fire extinguishers are inspected, tagged, and accessible; check that smoke detectors and fire alarms are functional.
*   **Housekeeping:** Ensure that work areas are clean, floors are free of slip/trip hazards, and waste is disposed of in appropriate containers.
*   **Electrical Safety:** Inspect visible wiring for fraying; verify that all power strips/extension cords are not daisy-chained.
*   **Signage:** Ensure safety signage (e.g., PPE requirements, restricted areas, hazardous chemical warnings) is legible and accurately placed.

## 3. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) & Hazardous Materials
*   **PPE Inventory:** Verify that the correct PPE for the specific environment is available, in good condition, and used by staff.
*   **Chemical Storage:** Ensure all chemicals are clearly labeled and stored according to their Safety Data Sheets (SDS).
*   **SDS Accessibility:** Confirm that a physical or digital binder of current SDS for all chemicals on-site is easily accessible to all staff.
*   **Spill Kits:** Audit the presence and contents of spill response kits to ensure supplies are not expired or depleted.

## 4. Emergency Preparedness and First Aid
*   **First Aid Kits:** Check that kits are fully stocked, items are within their expiration dates, and the kit is clearly visible.
*   **Emergency Contact List:** Verify that current emergency contact numbers are posted and updated.
*   **Evacuation Plan:** Confirm that evacuation maps are posted in high-traffic areas and that all staff are aware of the designated assembly point.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: The "Shadow" Audit:** Perform an unannounced walkthrough audit alongside a scheduled one. Scheduled audits often lead to "polishing the stage," whereas unannounced audits provide a true reflection of daily safety culture.
*   **Pro Tip: Photographic Evidence:** Always attach timestamped photos to your findings. This provides undeniable proof for corrective action follow-ups.
*   **Pitfall: The Paper-Pushing Trap:** Don't get so focused on checking boxes on your clipboard that you forget to observe behavior. A perfect facility with employees who ignore safety protocols is still a high-risk environment.
*   **Pitfall: Documentation Lag:** Failing to document a finding immediately often leads to "memory drift." If you see it, write it down instantly.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should a formal H&S audit be conducted?**
A: High-risk environments should undergo formal audits quarterly, while low-risk office environments can be audited annually. However, informal "spot checks" should be conducted monthly.

**Q: What is the most common audit failure?**
A: Blocked emergency exits and expired fire extinguisher inspections are the most frequent violations across all industries.

**Q: What should I do if I find a critical safety violation during an audit?**
A: Stop work immediately if the risk poses an imminent threat to life or limb. Notify the site manager, implement an immediate temporary fix, and document the incident for a formal root cause analysis.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Alberta Regulatory Inspection Compliance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory requirements and procedural workflow for completing inspection forms in accordance with Alberta provincial regulations. Whether operating under the Alberta Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) Act, Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act (EPEA), or specific site-based safety standards, accurate documentation is the cornerstone of corporate due diligence. This SOP ensures that all inspections are conducted systematically, consistently, and in full compliance with provincial legislative mandates.

## Section 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Verify Documentation:** Confirm the specific inspection form version matches the current Alberta regulatory requirements (e.g., OHS code updates).
*   **Equipment Calibration:** Ensure all diagnostic tools, sensors, or measurement devices are calibrated and within their current certification cycle.
*   **PPE Audit:** Confirm that the inspector is equipped with all site-required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as dictated by the site-specific Hazard Assessment.
*   **Personnel Notification:** Notify relevant supervisors or department heads that an inspection is in progress to ensure operational awareness.

## Section 2: On-Site Execution Checklist
*   **Site Identification:** Clearly record the project name, location (GPS coordinates or legal land description), and date/time of the inspection.
*   **Hazard Identification:** Document all active and potential hazards observed during the walkthrough. Compare these against the existing site Hazard Assessment.
*   **Compliance Verification:** 
    *   Verify valid certificates for operators and specialized equipment.
    *   Check for clear, legible signage regarding emergency exits and muster points.
    *   Confirm integrity of guardrails, fire suppression systems, and lockout/tagout (LOTO) points.
*   **Photographic Evidence:** Capture time-stamped, high-resolution photos of both compliant areas and identified non-conformance issues.
*   **Participant Engagement:** Interview at least one frontline worker to verify their understanding of emergency procedures and hazard reporting protocols.

## Section 3: Documentation and Reporting
*   **Detailing Non-Conformances:** For every item marked "Fail" or "Deficient," provide a clear, objective description of the issue.
*   **Corrective Action Plan (CAP):** Assign a specific corrective action, a responsible party, and a hard deadline for resolution for every identified deficiency.
*   **Stakeholder Sign-off:** Ensure both the inspector and the site supervisor sign and date the form.
*   **Data Archival:** Upload the completed form to the centralized document management system within 24 hours to ensure an audit-ready digital trail.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a mobile-first digital inspection app. This ensures GPS data is automatically embedded and prevents "pencil-whipping" by requiring photos for every check-box.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always document the "Why." If a safety feature is bypassed, record the specific justification or lack thereof to prevent ambiguity during an external audit.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid generic comments like "OK" or "Good." Use descriptive, measurable observations (e.g., "Fire extinguisher pressure gauge is in the green zone, expiry date 12/2025").
*   **Pitfall:** Do not delay filing. In Alberta, OHS inspectors may request site records with little notice; a backlog of paperwork is a red flag for regulators.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How long must I keep completed inspection forms?**
Under Alberta OHS guidelines, it is recommended to maintain safety records for a minimum of three years, though specific industry standards may require longer retention periods.

**2. What happens if I identify an immediate danger during an inspection?**
You are legally obligated under the Alberta OHS Act to issue a "Stop Work" directive for that specific task or area immediately and notify the site supervisor before completing the inspection form.

**3. Are digital signatures legally binding for Alberta inspections?**
Yes, provided the software used creates a secure audit trail that identifies the signatory and prevents unauthorized alteration of the document post-signature.

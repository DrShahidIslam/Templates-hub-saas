# Standard Operating Procedure: Asset and Facility Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for managing, executing, and documenting maintenance activities within the organization. The objective of this protocol is to ensure operational continuity, extend the lifecycle of physical assets, maintain safety standards, and minimize unplanned downtime. By adhering to these guidelines, maintenance teams will foster a culture of proactive care rather than reactive repair, ensuring all facility systems remain in optimal working condition.

## Phase 1: Request and Scheduling
*   **Log the Request:** All maintenance needs must be submitted via the designated Maintenance Management System (MMS). Include asset ID, location, and priority level.
*   **Assessment:** Review the request for urgency (Emergency, Urgent, or Routine).
*   **Resource Allocation:** Assign a qualified technician based on the skill set required for the specific task.
*   **Scheduling:** Coordinate with department heads to minimize operational disruption. Notify relevant stakeholders of potential downtime.

## Phase 2: Preparation and Safety
*   **Safety Audit:** Conduct a pre-task Job Hazard Analysis (JHA). Ensure all Lock-Out/Tag-Out (LOTO) procedures are executed if working on electrical or mechanical systems.
*   **PPE Verification:** Confirm all technicians are equipped with the required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as dictated by the maintenance task.
*   **Staging:** Gather necessary tools, replacement parts, and manuals before arriving at the site.
*   **Permit Check:** If the work involves "Hot Work," confined spaces, or heights, verify that all necessary permits are signed and valid.

## Phase 3: Execution and Documentation
*   **Primary Repair:** Execute the work according to the manufacturer’s technical manual or approved internal best practices.
*   **Quality Inspection:** After completion, perform a functional test to ensure the asset is operating within manufacturer specifications.
*   **Cleaning:** Remove all waste, debris, and tools from the work area. Restore the environment to its original state.
*   **Documentation:** Update the maintenance log in the MMS. Include the work performed, hours logged, parts used, and any recommendations for future upgrades.

## Phase 4: Sign-off and Review
*   **User Verification:** Obtain a sign-off from the department lead or the primary user of the asset to confirm satisfactory resolution.
*   **Inventory Reconciliation:** Update inventory levels if parts were pulled from stock; trigger a reorder request if supplies are low.
*   **Final Filing:** Close the maintenance ticket in the system to trigger the analytics dashboard update.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a Preventive Maintenance (PM) calendar. 80% of maintenance should be planned (PM); only 20% should be reactive (Break-fix).
*   **Pro Tip:** Take "Before and After" photos. This provides a visual audit trail and is invaluable for insurance claims or vendor disputes.
*   **Pitfall:** Never skip LOTO. Even for "quick" fixes, the risk of accidental power-up is the leading cause of industrial injury.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "nuisance" alarms. Small, intermittent issues are often precursors to total equipment failure. Address them early.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if a spare part is not in stock during a critical repair?**
A: Immediately escalate to the Operations Manager to evaluate "cannibalizing" parts from redundant systems or sourcing from an emergency local vendor. Update the ticket status to "Waiting for Parts" to pause the clock on turnaround time metrics.

**Q: How do we categorize maintenance priority?**
A: Use the "Impact vs. Urgency" matrix. Emergencies are defined by immediate safety risks or total loss of production. Routine tasks are preventative or aesthetic in nature.

**Q: Who is authorized to sign off on a completed maintenance task?**
A: Sign-off must be performed by the Department Lead or the primary system operator. If the asset is a shared facility component, the Facilities Manager must conduct the final inspection.

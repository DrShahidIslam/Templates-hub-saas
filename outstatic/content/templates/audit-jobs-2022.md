# Standard Operating Procedure: Audit Jobs 2022

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols and workflow requirements for the comprehensive review, verification, and reconciliation of the 2022 job portfolio. The objective of this audit is to ensure data integrity, verify client billing accuracy, confirm the completion of project deliverables, and maintain compliance with fiscal reporting standards. All audit personnel must adhere to these guidelines to ensure consistency across departmental records and financial statements.

## Phase 1: Pre-Audit Preparation and Data Extraction

*   **System Access Verification:** Ensure all team members have read/write access to the "2022_Closed_Projects" database and the centralized billing portal.
*   **Data Export:** Extract the master list of all jobs finalized between January 1, 2022, and December 31, 2022.
*   **Scope Definition:** Identify any "Carry-over" jobs that began in 2021 or concluded in 2023 to ensure correct cross-fiscal categorization.
*   **Tool Setup:** Initialize the "2022 Audit Tracking Spreadsheet," cross-referencing against the CRM exports for discrepancies in job status labels.

## Phase 2: Documentation Review and Validation

*   **Deliverable Verification:** Confirm that every job folder contains a signed "Certificate of Completion" or final client sign-off email.
*   **Time-Entry Audit:** Review time-tracking logs to ensure hours billed align with original project estimates. Identify and flag any unbilled time logs.
*   **Expense Reconciliation:** Cross-reference all third-party invoices (vendors/subcontractors) against project expense reports. Ensure a 1:1 match between project costs and invoice receipts.
*   **Contract Compliance:** Validate that the final project output aligns with the initial Statement of Work (SOW) terms, including agreed-upon deliverables and timeline constraints.

## Phase 3: Financial Reconciliation and Reporting

*   **Revenue Verification:** Compare final billed totals in the accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks/NetSuite) against the initial project revenue projections.
*   **Margin Analysis:** Calculate the final profit margin for each job; flag any projects falling below the 15% threshold for manual management review.
*   **Tax Compliance Check:** Ensure all tax documentation (W-9s for contractors, tax exemptions for clients) is present and correctly tagged within the fiscal file structure.
*   **Final Archiving:** Upon validation, move the audited job folder to the "2022_Audited_Final" server directory and set to "Read Only" status.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The Batch Method):** Audit jobs in batches of 20. Attempting to audit the entire year's portfolio at once leads to "review fatigue" and increases the likelihood of missing small discrepancies.
*   **Pro Tip (Color Coding):** Use conditional formatting in your tracker: Green for "Clean," Yellow for "Pending Explanation," and Red for "Significant Discrepancy."
*   **Pitfall (Scope Creep):** Avoid the trap of re-opening 2022 projects for minor administrative tweaks. Unless the financial impact is >$500, document the variance as "immaterial" rather than spending billable hours correcting it.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring Anomalies):** Never dismiss a "Zero-Cost" job without verifying it wasn't a clerical error in the billing system.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if I find a discrepancy that cannot be resolved?**
A: If documentation is missing or a financial gap remains unexplained after 48 hours, mark the entry as "Escalated" in the audit tracker and flag it for the Department Head for final determination.

**Q: Are internal, non-billable jobs included in this audit?**
A: Yes. All jobs, including administrative and internal R&D projects, must be audited to ensure internal resource allocation data remains accurate for 2023 planning.

**Q: What is the deadline for the final report submission?**
A: The final consolidated audit report must be exported to PDF and uploaded to the Executive SharePoint site no later than the close of business on the last Friday of the current month.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Client Questionnaire Onboarding

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the standardized process for administering, managing, and integrating client onboarding questionnaires. The objective of this workflow is to ensure high-quality data collection, minimize friction for the client, and guarantee that project teams receive all necessary documentation to initiate service delivery without delay. By adhering to this protocol, we reduce administrative bottlenecks and ensure a professional, cohesive brand experience from the first interaction.

## Phase 1: Preparation & Configuration
*   **Select Questionnaire Template:** Identify the appropriate template based on the client service tier or project type.
*   **Customization:** Review the template for specific client needs. Remove irrelevant fields and add custom queries if the initial discovery call identified unique constraints.
*   **Security & Compliance:** Ensure the questionnaire platform (e.g., Typeform, Jotform, Google Forms) is set to collect data in compliance with internal privacy policies and GDPR/CCPA standards.
*   **Automated Triggers:** Ensure the CRM (e.g., HubSpot, Salesforce) or project management tool (e.g., Asana, Monday.com) is integrated to trigger a notification once the form is submitted.

## Phase 2: Deployment & Client Communication
*   **Personalized Invitation:** Draft a formal email invitation. The email must include the direct link, an estimated time to complete, and a clear deadline for submission.
*   **Access Verification:** Test the link on both desktop and mobile devices to ensure form accessibility.
*   **Calendar Integration:** If applicable, include a link to the project kick-off call scheduler in the "Thank You" page of the questionnaire.
*   **Tracking:** Update the client’s status in the CRM to "Onboarding: Questionnaire Sent" and log the send date.

## Phase 3: Monitoring & Follow-up
*   **Automated Reminders:** Schedule a follow-up email at the 48-hour mark for incomplete responses.
*   **Manual Outreach:** If the deadline is missed, place a personalized follow-up call or email to address potential confusion or technical roadblocks.
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Review:** Upon submission, verify that all mandatory fields are complete and that the information provided is coherent and actionable.
*   **Data Migration:** Extract data to the designated project folder. Notify the relevant account manager/stakeholders that the onboarding phase is complete and the project is ready for kick-off.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use "Conditional Logic." Only show questions that are relevant to the client’s specific path to keep the form length manageable and increase completion rates.
*   **Pro Tip:** Include a "Save and Resume" feature if the questionnaire exceeds 15 questions to prevent abandonment.
*   **Pitfall (Too Many Questions):** Never include "nice to have" data in an onboarding questionnaire. Only ask for information essential to project execution.
*   **Pitfall (Ambiguous Language):** Avoid industry jargon. If a question can be misinterpreted, provide a brief tooltip or "info icon" explanation next to the field.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if a client skips a "mandatory" field?**
A: Do not force submission if the data is genuinely not available. Instead, reach out to the client to discuss why the information is needed and determine if a placeholder or secondary source can be used.

**Q: How long should the onboarding questionnaire be?**
A: Aim for a 10–15 minute completion time. If your project requirements are extensive, consider splitting the onboarding into two stages: an initial "Foundational Info" form and a secondary "Deep-Dive" document to be completed during the kick-off meeting.

**Q: Should the questionnaire be branded?**
A: Yes. Use your company’s color palette, logo, and tone of voice. A professionally branded form reinforces trust and signifies that you are an organized, detail-oriented partner.

# Standard Operating Procedure: KAUST Research & Operational Integration

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory operational protocols for personnel, researchers, and contractors engaging with the King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) ecosystem. KAUST operates under a unique framework integrating world-class research facilities, specialized administrative zones, and campus-wide safety compliance. Adherence to this SOP ensures seamless project execution, alignment with institutional safety standards, and optimal utilization of campus resources.

## 1. Pre-Arrival and Access Authorization
*   **Submit Access Request:** Complete the online KAUST Campus Access Request form at least 10 business days prior to arrival.
*   **Identity Verification:** Submit clear copies of government-issued identification (Passport/Iqama) to the Security Office via the secure portal.
*   **Digital Access Credentials:** Register your mobile device for the campus digital pass system to access secured laboratories and faculty zones.
*   **Health Compliance:** Ensure all vaccination and health clearance certificates are uploaded to the university’s internal health management system.

## 2. Laboratory and Facility Integration
*   **Environmental Health & Safety (EHS) Training:** Complete the mandatory EHS orientation module specifically tailored to your designated lab zone.
*   **Equipment Reservation:** Utilize the Core Labs booking system (iLab) to schedule time for specialized instrumentation.
*   **Chemical/Hazard Inventory:** Register all hazardous materials with the EHS department before transporting them into any campus facility.
*   **Waste Management:** Review the color-coded waste disposal manual and ensure all laboratory waste is segregated as per KAUST sustainability standards.

## 3. Communication and Reporting
*   **Emergency Contact Sync:** Save the KAUST Emergency Operations Center (EOC) number (911 internal) in your mobile contact list.
*   **Incident Logging:** Report any laboratory mishaps or operational delays via the "Service Desk" portal within 2 hours of discovery.
*   **Data Security:** Ensure all research data is stored on university-approved secure servers; do not use external cloud storage for sensitive intellectual property.

## 4. Operational Exit and Handover
*   **Asset Decommissioning:** Notify the Facility Manager to schedule a site inspection 72 hours before vacating any workstation.
*   **Data Export Compliance:** Verify that all research data transfers comply with institutional IP protection policies before offboarding.
*   **Key/Badge Return:** Surrender all physical access badges and equipment keys to the Security Department to receive clearance.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **Networking:** Engage with the Core Labs staff early in your project; they possess proprietary knowledge on equipment optimization that is not documented in standard manuals.
*   **Communication:** Utilize the "KAUST Intranet" for real-time updates on campus service interruptions or power maintenance schedules.
*   **Culture:** Remember that KAUST is a residential campus; maintain high professional decorum in communal areas, as residence and research zones are closely linked.

### Pitfalls
*   **Procrastination on Access:** Access requests submitted fewer than 5 days in advance are frequently flagged for manual review, significantly delaying project start times.
*   **Ignoring EHS Alerts:** Non-compliance with EHS guidelines will result in immediate suspension of lab privileges. Do not bypass safety protocols for the sake of speed.
*   **Data Siloing:** Attempting to use private external drives instead of the university network for research storage will result in access violations and potential data loss.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I bring external guests into the lab space?**
A: No. All guests must be pre-authorized via the visitor management system. Unauthorized guests in laboratory zones constitute a major security breach.

**Q: How do I handle a chemical spill if I am unsure of the protocol?**
A: Evacuate the area immediately, restrict access by closing the lab door, and call the KAUST Emergency Operations Center (911) instantly. Do not attempt to clean up hazardous materials without EHS supervision.

**Q: Is there an expedited process for time-sensitive research equipment repairs?**
A: Yes. Use the iLab "Urgent Request" flag when booking maintenance; however, ensure your Principal Investigator (PI) follows up via email to the Core Lab Manager to validate the urgency.

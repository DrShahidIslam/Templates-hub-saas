# Standard Operating Procedure: Gold Loan Audit Protocol

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory audit requirements for gold loan portfolios to ensure regulatory compliance, mitigate credit risk, and verify the physical existence and purity of collateral. As an operations manager, the objective of this audit is to confirm that every gold loan disbursed is backed by sufficient, accurately appraised, and safely stored collateral, while ensuring that all documentation aligns with internal lending policies and statutory guidelines.

## 1. Documentation and KYC Verification
*   **KYC Completeness:** Verify that the borrower’s identity and address proofs (Aadhaar, PAN, etc.) are valid, verified against original documents, and stored securely.
*   **Loan Agreement:** Confirm the presence of a signed and stamped Loan-Cum-Hypothecation agreement.
*   **Appraisal Report:** Ensure the appraisal sheet is signed by both the customer and the certified appraiser, detailing the gross weight, net weight, and caratage of the gold.
*   **Disbursement Records:** Match the disbursed amount against the valuation report and the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio mandated by central bank guidelines.

## 2. Physical Verification and Storage
*   **Vault Security:** Conduct a dual-custody check for the vault keys. Ensure that the combination/access log is maintained daily.
*   **Packet Integrity:** Verify that every gold packet in the vault is sealed, tamper-proof, and clearly labeled with a unique Loan Account Number (LAN).
*   **Physical Reconciliation:** Perform a 100% physical count of gold packets against the core banking system (CBS) report.
*   **Purity Verification:** Select a random sample of 5–10% of the portfolio for re-appraisal by an independent third-party appraiser to ensure the initial purity grading was accurate.

## 3. Compliance and Operational Controls
*   **LTV Monitoring:** Check that the current LTV ratio has not exceeded the regulatory ceiling due to fluctuations in market gold prices. 
*   **Dormant/NPA Accounts:** Identify accounts that are overdue or classified as Non-Performing Assets (NPA) and verify if the physical gold is still held in the vault.
*   **Insurance Coverage:** Confirm that the gold stored in the branch vault is covered under a valid Cash-in-Safe/Gold-in-Vault insurance policy for the total aggregate value.
*   **Staff Rotation:** Ensure that the gold appraiser is rotated periodically as per internal policy to prevent collusion.

## 4. Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use UV lights to inspect the authenticity of the seals on gold packets; these are harder to forge than standard stickers or tape.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always document the "weight of the stone" separately. A common pitfall is including the weight of studded stones as pure gold weight, which artificially inflates the collateral value.
*   **Pitfall - The "Fake" Appraisal:** Never allow the branch appraiser to work in isolation. If a customer has a close relationship with the appraiser, request a secondary verification.
*   **Pitfall - Documentation Gaps:** A loan is legally unenforceable if the signature on the physical ledger does not match the digitized signature stored in the system.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the recommended frequency for a full physical audit?**
A: A 100% physical audit should be performed at least once every quarter. However, "surprise" audits of high-value loan packets should occur monthly to maintain operational integrity.

**Q: How should we handle a discrepancy in weight discovered during an audit?**
A: Immediately quarantine the packet, notify the Audit Head, and suspend the appraiser responsible for that specific loan pending an investigation. The customer must be contacted only after the internal findings are finalized.

**Q: If gold prices drop significantly, what is the protocol?**
A: If the LTV ratio exceeds the permissible limit due to a market crash, the branch must issue a "Top-up Notice" to the customer, requesting either a partial loan repayment or additional gold collateral within a specified timeframe.

# Standard Operating Procedure: University Admissions & Academic Administration

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the comprehensive lifecycle management for university operations, ranging from student recruitment and admissions to final enrollment. It is designed to ensure compliance with institutional policies, maintain regulatory standards (such as FERPA and SEVP), and provide a seamless experience for prospective students. Adherence to these protocols is mandatory for all administrative staff to ensure operational excellence and data integrity.

## Phase 1: Prospective Student Outreach & Inquiry Management

*   **Inquiry Categorization:** Log all incoming inquiries into the CRM within 24 hours. Classify by academic program, residency status (Domestic/International), and entry term.
*   **Initial Response Protocol:** Deploy automated acknowledgment emails followed by personalized outreach from an admissions counselor within 48 business hours.
*   **Virtual/Physical Tours:** Schedule campus visits through the centralized booking portal. Ensure tour guides are briefed on current university accolades and campus safety protocols.
*   **Material Distribution:** Provide digital brochures and financial aid resource guides relevant to the specific program of interest.

## Phase 2: Application Processing & Review

*   **Document Verification:** Audit incoming application files for transcripts, letters of recommendation, and standardized test scores. Ensure all international transcripts are verified via recognized credential evaluation services (e.g., WES, ECE).
*   **Application Gatekeeping:** Flag incomplete applications in the Student Information System (SIS). Send automated "Missing Documents" reminders on a 7-day cadence.
*   **Faculty Review Committee:** Route completed files to the appropriate department chair or admissions committee for qualitative assessment.
*   **Decision Issuance:** Once a committee decision is reached, update the SIS status and trigger the formal "Offer" or "Denial" letter via the applicant portal.

## Phase 3: Enrollment, Onboarding, & Compliance

*   **Intent to Enroll:** Collect enrollment deposits and monitor for "Accepted/Declined" status.
*   **Visa/Immigration Processing:** For international students, issue Form I-20 or DS-2019 only after confirming proof of financial solvency. Coordinate with the International Student Office (ISO) for SEVIS reporting.
*   **Academic Advising Assignment:** Match incoming students with department advisors based on declared majors.
*   **Registration Clearing:** Conduct a final pre-semester audit to ensure all health records (immunizations), proof of residency, and final official transcripts are on file.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (Data Hygiene):** Always utilize the unique Student ID number as the primary key in your database rather than names to avoid duplication errors.
*   **Pro Tip (Cross-Departmental Synergy):** Hold weekly "Sync Meetings" between Admissions and Financial Aid to prevent bottlenecks in student scholarship processing.
*   **Pitfall (Compliance Risk):** Failure to properly document international visa eligibility (I-20 issuance) is a major audit risk. Never bypass manual review for international financial documentation.
*   **Pitfall (Communication Lag):** The "Yield Period" (time between offer and enrollment) is sensitive. High-touch communication during this window is the strongest predictor of high enrollment rates.

## FAQ

**Q: How do we handle applicants who do not meet the minimum GPA requirement?**
A: All applicants failing to meet minimum academic standards must be reviewed by the Graduate or Undergraduate Academic Appeals Committee. Direct communication should outline the specific deficiency and the path for potential re-application.

**Q: What is the procedure for updating an application record after a document has been submitted?**
A: Staff must never overwrite existing files. Instead, upload the new document as an "addendum" and timestamp it to ensure a complete audit trail of the application history.

**Q: Who is authorized to override a rejection decision?**
A: Only the Dean of Admissions or the Department Chair possesses the authority to overturn a decision. Any such override must be accompanied by a written justification uploaded to the student’s internal file.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Building Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory protocol for conducting routine preventive maintenance (PM) across all facility assets. The objective is to maximize equipment lifespan, ensure regulatory compliance, optimize energy consumption, and minimize unplanned downtime. All maintenance personnel must adhere to these guidelines to maintain a safe, functional, and efficient building environment.

## Phase 1: Electrical and Lighting Systems
*   **Distribution Panels:** Inspect for signs of overheating, loose connections, or corrosion. Use infrared thermography to identify hotspots.
*   **Emergency Lighting:** Test all exit signs and battery-backed emergency lights to ensure 90-minute operation.
*   **Exterior Lighting:** Replace burnt-out LED modules, check photocell sensors for correct dusk-to-dawn activation, and clear debris from light housing.
*   **Grounding:** Verify the integrity of grounding wires and bonding connections in main switchgear rooms.

## Phase 2: HVAC and Air Quality
*   **Air Handling Units (AHUs):** Replace filters based on pressure differential readings. Inspect belts for wear and tension.
*   **Condenser Coils:** Clean exterior coils of dust, pollen, and debris to ensure efficient heat exchange.
*   **Drain Pans:** Flush condensate lines and treat pans with biocide tablets to prevent algae growth and blockages.
*   **Thermostat Calibration:** Verify that sensor readings align with calibrated handheld instruments and recalibrate where drift is detected.

## Phase 3: Plumbing and Hydraulic Systems
*   **Backflow Preventers:** Confirm annual certification is current and visually inspect for leaks at valve unions.
*   **Sump Pumps:** Test float switches and discharge lines to ensure immediate activation during heavy rain events.
*   **Restroom Fixtures:** Check flush valves for leaking seals and inspect sink aerators for mineral buildup (descaling).
*   **Water Heaters:** Inspect relief valves and drain the tank base to remove sediment buildup.

## Phase 4: Structural and Envelope Integrity
*   **Roofing:** Inspect flashings, drains, and membrane seals. Remove debris that could cause water ponding.
*   **Doors and Hardware:** Lubricate hinges, check door closers for proper latching speed, and verify functionality of panic bars.
*   **Windows:** Inspect weather stripping and seals for cracks or gaps; ensure all operable windows lock securely.
*   **Building Facade:** Check for cracks, spalling, or loose masonry that could pose a safety hazard.

## Phase 5: Life Safety and Security
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Confirm the gauge pressure is in the green zone and the tamper seal is intact.
*   **Fire Alarm System:** Perform a visual inspection of smoke detectors and manual pull stations; verify the control panel shows no active fault codes.
*   **Access Control:** Test badge readers and request-to-exit (REX) sensors for response time and latching reliability.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Utilize a CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System) to automate recurring work orders. Digitizing your logs creates a clear audit trail for insurance and resale value.
*   **Pro Tip:** When performing infrared scans of electrical panels, ensure the load is at least 40% of capacity to get accurate heat signatures.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not "pencil whip" logs. Always document the actual date and the specific technician who performed the work; falsified records are a significant legal and insurance liability.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring small leaks. A minor drip in a mechanical room today often evolves into a catastrophic equipment failure or mold issue tomorrow.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How often should I review the Preventive Maintenance schedule?**
A: The schedule should be audited annually. As equipment ages, the frequency of inspections may need to increase, or conversely, if newer equipment is installed, tasks may be optimized.

**Q: What should I do if I find a critical issue during a routine check?**
A: Immediately escalate the finding to the Facilities Director, document the issue with photographs, and initiate a "Corrective Maintenance" work order to prioritize the repair.

**Q: Are there specific PPE requirements for these tasks?**
A: Yes. All technicians must adhere to OSHA standards, including arc-flash gear for electrical work, safety glasses for cleaning tasks, and gloves when handling chemicals or plumbing components. Refer to your site-specific Safety Data Sheets (SDS).

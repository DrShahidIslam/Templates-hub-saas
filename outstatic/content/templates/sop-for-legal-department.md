# Standard Operating Procedure: Legal Department Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as the primary framework for the Legal Department to ensure regulatory compliance, mitigate organizational risk, and provide efficient counsel to internal stakeholders. By adhering to these standardized workflows, the department will maintain consistency in document management, contract lifecycles, and cross-departmental communications, thereby safeguarding the company’s legal and financial interests.

## 1. Intake and Matter Management
*   **Request Submission:** All legal requests must be submitted via the centralized Legal Request Portal. Email requests are discouraged to prevent loss of tracking.
*   **Triage and Classification:** Upon receipt, counsel must categorize the request by urgency (P1-Urgent, P2-Standard, P3-Administrative) and practice area (e.g., Intellectual Property, Employment, Corporate Governance).
*   **Conflict Check:** Verify the request against the Conflict of Interest Register to ensure no ethical breaches exist in representing the company on the specific matter.
*   **Assignment:** The Legal Operations Lead will assign the matter to the appropriate subject matter expert based on capacity and expertise.

## 2. Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM)
*   **Drafting and Review:** Utilize approved department templates for all Standard Operating Agreements. Any deviation from the template requires a redlined justification note.
*   **Version Control:** All drafts must be saved in the secure Document Management System (DMS) using the naming convention: [YYYYMMDD]_[ProjectName]_[VersionNumber].
*   **Internal Approval Workflow:** Route draft contracts through the internal approval matrix (e.g., Finance for payment terms, IT for security requirements, Department Head for operational scope).
*   **Execution:** Finalized contracts must be routed through the designated e-signature platform. No physical signatures are permitted without express approval from the General Counsel.

## 3. Compliance and Record Keeping
*   **Regulatory Monitoring:** Legal counsel must review relevant legislative updates quarterly and document potential impacts on company policy in the Compliance Dashboard.
*   **Data Retention:** Ensure all legal documentation is maintained in alignment with the corporate retention policy (minimum of 7 years for active contracts).
*   **Privilege Logging:** Maintain a real-time privilege log for all active litigation or sensitive investigations to ensure attorney-client privilege is protected during discovery.

## 4. Pro Tips & Pitfalls

**Pro Tips:**
*   **Early Involvement:** Encourage business units to involve Legal at the inception of a project, not at the execution phase; this reduces emergency rework.
*   **Knowledge Base:** Maintain a "Playbook" of common negotiation points for standard contracts to empower non-legal staff to handle routine renewals without constant oversight.
*   **Automated Reminders:** Use automated calendar alerts for contract renewals 90, 60, and 30 days out to avoid auto-renewals that are no longer favorable.

**Pitfalls to Avoid:**
*   **The "Shadow Legal" Trap:** Avoid providing legal advice via informal channels (e.g., Slack/IM). Always move substantive discussions to formal email threads or recorded meetings.
*   **Vague Instructions:** Never accept a request without a clearly defined "Desired Outcome." Vague requests lead to scope creep and excessive billable hours (for outside counsel).
*   **Ignoring Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs):** Treat every NDA with the same rigor as a multi-million dollar master service agreement; bad NDAs are a primary source of data leakage.

## 5. FAQ

**Q: What is the turnaround time for a standard contract review?**
A: Under normal operating conditions, the initial review turnaround is 5-7 business days. Requests marked as "Urgent" are assessed within 24 hours.

**Q: How do I request outside counsel for a specialized matter?**
A: Requests for outside counsel must be submitted to the General Counsel with a written justification outlining why internal resources are insufficient and an estimated budget.

**Q: Where should I store drafts of sensitive litigation files?**
A: All sensitive or privileged materials must be stored in the "Restricted Access" folder within the Legal DMS, which is limited to Legal Department personnel and relevant executive stakeholders only.

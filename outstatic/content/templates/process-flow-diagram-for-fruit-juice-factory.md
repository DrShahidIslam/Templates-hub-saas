# Standard Operating Procedure: Fruit Juice Production Process Flow

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process flow for a commercial fruit juice production facility. The objective is to ensure consistent product quality, maintain food safety compliance (HACCP/FSMA standards), and optimize operational efficiency from raw material intake to final packaging. Adherence to these protocols is mandatory for all production floor staff to minimize contamination risks and maximize yield.

## 1. Raw Material Receiving and Preparation
*   **Inspection:** Verify the quality of incoming fruit (Brix levels, acidity, and sensory attributes).
*   **Sorting:** Remove foreign objects, damaged produce, and debris using an automated sorting conveyor.
*   **Washing:** Execute a multi-stage wash process, utilizing potable water and food-grade sanitizers to remove pesticide residues and soil.
*   **Grading:** Segregate fruits by size and maturity to ensure consistent extraction efficiency.

## 2. Extraction and Refining
*   **Pre-heating:** Briefly heat fruit (if required by process specifications) to deactivate enzymes and facilitate juice release.
*   **Extraction:** Utilize mechanical extractors (screw press or centrifugal) to separate juice from pomace.
*   **Finishing:** Pass juice through centrifugal decanters or fine-mesh vibrating screens to remove excess pulp and fibrous material.
*   **Filtration (Optional):** Perform ultra-filtration or membrane filtration if clear juice (e.g., apple or grape) is required.

## 3. Standardization and Pasteurization
*   **Formulation:** Adjust Brix levels, acidity, and vitamin fortifications per the batch master recipe.
*   **Degassing:** Remove trapped air through a vacuum deaerator to prevent oxidation and extend shelf life.
*   **Pasteurization:** Subject the juice to a Heat Exchanger (HTST - High Temperature Short Time) at prescribed temperature parameters (e.g., 95°C for 30 seconds) to ensure microbial safety.
*   **Homogenization:** Ensure uniform suspension of particles (if producing cloudy juice) to prevent separation in the final package.

## 4. Filling and Packaging
*   **Filling:** Perform aseptic filling in a controlled environment to maintain sterility.
*   **Capping/Sealing:** Immediately seal containers using induction heat sealing or vacuum crimping.
*   **Coding:** Apply batch numbers, production dates, and expiration codes using industrial inkjet or laser printers.
*   **Secondary Packaging:** Shrink-wrap individual units into cases and palletize according to shipping specifications.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Yield):** Regularly calibrate extraction equipment. Even a 1% improvement in extraction efficiency can result in significant annual cost savings.
*   **Pro Tip (Energy):** Implement a heat recovery system on the pasteurizer to pre-heat incoming raw juice, drastically reducing utility costs.
*   **Pitfall (Oxidation):** Failing to properly deaerate will lead to color degradation and "off" flavors within weeks of production.
*   **Pitfall (CIP):** Inadequate Clean-in-Place (CIP) cycles between batches of different fruit types can lead to cross-allergen contamination and flavor blending.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How often should the pasteurization equipment be calibrated?**
All temperature sensors and flow meters must be calibrated at a minimum of every six months or immediately following any significant maintenance activity to ensure kill-step validity.

**2. What is the most common cause of bottle contamination?**
The most frequent cause is ineffective container sterilization prior to the filling stage. Ensure all bottles/cartons pass through an ozone or UV-C sterilization tunnel immediately before entering the filling zone.

**3. How do we manage juice pulp waste?**
Pulp and pomace should be handled as organic waste. We recommend partnering with local agricultural operations to convert this byproduct into livestock feed or organic compost to minimize waste management costs.

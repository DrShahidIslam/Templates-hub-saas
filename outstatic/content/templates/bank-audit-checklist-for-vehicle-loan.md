# Standard Operating Procedure: Vehicle Loan Audit Protocol

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive framework for internal and external auditors to evaluate the integrity, compliance, and risk profile of a bank’s vehicle loan portfolio. The objective of this audit is to ensure that all credit disbursements strictly adhere to the institution’s lending policy, regulatory requirements (such as KYC/AML norms), and internal risk management frameworks. By following this systematic approach, auditors can identify potential non-performing assets (NPAs), verify collateral security, and ensure the enforceability of legal documentation.

## 1. Pre-Disbursement Documentation Review
Before assessing the asset's status, the foundation of the loan must be validated through the following document checks:
* **KYC/AML Verification:** Confirm the existence and validity of Aadhar, PAN, or Passport documentation. Verify that the customer is not on any "Denied Party" or "Sanctions" lists.
* **Credit Assessment:** Review the CIBIL/Credit Bureau score and verify that the Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio falls within the bank’s approved tolerance levels.
* **Income Proof:** Validate salary slips, ITR filings, or bank statements for the preceding 6–12 months to ensure repayment capacity.
* **Application Integrity:** Ensure the loan application form is complete, signed by the applicant(s), and stamped in accordance with local regulations.

## 2. Asset Verification & Security Documentation
This section focuses on the tangible collateral and the legal security interest held by the bank.
* **Pro-forma Invoice:** Verify that the vehicle purchase price matches the invoice issued by the authorized dealership.
* **Hypothecation Verification:** Confirm that the bank’s name is recorded in the RC (Registration Certificate) book as the "Hypothecary."
* **Insurance Coverage:** Check for valid Comprehensive Insurance coverage with the Bank’s "Hypothecation Clause" endorsed on the policy.
* **Physical Inspection Report:** Verify that a pre-disbursement inspection report exists, detailing the vehicle’s VIN (Vehicle Identification Number), Chassis Number, and Engine Number.

## 3. Disbursement & Accounting Accuracy
Audit the financial trail to ensure the funds were released as per the bank’s internal authorization protocols.
* **Payment Trail:** Confirm funds were disbursed directly to the dealer/vendor rather than the borrower’s personal account.
* **Processing Fees:** Audit the ledger to ensure that all processing fees, documentation charges, and upfront taxes were collected and accounted for.
* **Sanction Letter Consistency:** Verify that the disbursed amount, interest rate, and tenure match the terms explicitly stated in the approved Sanction Letter.
* **NACH/ECS Mandate:** Ensure the Electronic Clearing Service (ECS) or NACH mandate is registered and verified for monthly EMI debits.

## 4. Post-Disbursement Monitoring
Ensure the account remains healthy and that monitoring triggers are active.
* **RC Submission Tracking:** Verify if the original Registration Certificate was received from the RTO and stored in the bank's secure vault.
* **EMI Payment History:** Review the repayment schedule to ensure no arrears exist. Check for "bounce" patterns in the early stages of the loan.
* **Portfolio Health:** Categorize the loan as Standard, SMA-0, SMA-1, or SMA-2 based on the number of days past due (DPD).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
* **Pro Tip (The "Blind Spot"):** Always cross-reference the Chassis/Engine number on the physical document against the bank’s digital Loan Management System (LMS). Typos here are a frequent source of "unsecured" lending in an audit.
* **Pro Tip (Insurance Lapse):** Set up a tickler system for insurance renewal. A vehicle loan with an expired insurance policy is a major audit observation, as the bank’s primary collateral is left unprotected.
* **Pitfall (The "Round Tripping"):** Watch for loans where the down payment is funded by a related party of the dealer. This can indicate inflated vehicle pricing (over-invoicing) to obtain a larger loan than the vehicle's market value.
* **Pitfall (Incomplete RC):** Failing to ensure the "Hypothecation" stamp is present on the physical RC is a fatal flaw in the event of a vehicle recovery/repossession legal battle.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the most critical document for a vehicle loan audit?**
A: The Registration Certificate (RC) showing the bank as the hypothecary is the most critical document. Without this, the bank has no legal priority over the vehicle in the event of default.

**Q: How should a missing "Pre-Disbursement Inspection" report be treated?**
A: This must be flagged as a high-risk finding. Without this, there is no proof that the asset actually existed at the time of funding, potentially exposing the bank to fraud.

**Q: What happens if the dealer’s invoice and the loan amount differ?**
A: This is an immediate red flag. The loan amount should only cover the invoice value plus permissible add-ons. If the loan amount is higher, it indicates "top-up" or "personal loan" behavior disguised as a vehicle loan, which violates lending policy.

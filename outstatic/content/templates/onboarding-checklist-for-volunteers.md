# Standard Operating Procedure: Volunteer Onboarding

## Introduction
The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to provide a structured, consistent, and welcoming onboarding process for all new volunteers. Effective onboarding is essential to ensure that volunteers are properly vetted, aligned with the organization’s mission, and equipped with the necessary tools to perform their duties safely and efficiently. By following these steps, we minimize administrative friction, enhance volunteer retention, and ensure that our operational standards are met from day one.

## Phase 1: Application and Pre-Screening
*   **Initial Inquiry Review:** Verify the volunteer’s application form for completeness and interest alignment.
*   **Background Check (If Applicable):** Initiate necessary criminal background or reference checks based on the specific role requirements.
*   **Initial Discovery Call/Interview:** Conduct a 15–20 minute video or phone call to assess soft skills, availability, and motivation.
*   **Role Matching:** Formally offer the specific role and confirm the volunteer’s commitment to the required schedule.

## Phase 2: Documentation and Compliance
*   **Volunteer Agreement:** Secure a signed copy of the Volunteer Handbook and Code of Conduct.
*   **Liability Waiver:** Ensure the volunteer has signed the organization’s general liability release form.
*   **Data Privacy:** Have the volunteer sign a Confidentiality Agreement regarding sensitive organizational or client data.
*   **Emergency Contact Setup:** Collect and store emergency contact information in the secure volunteer database.

## Phase 3: Orientation and Training
*   **Organizational Overview:** Provide an introduction to the mission, core values, and current operational goals.
*   **Tool Access:** Provision access to necessary internal platforms (e.g., Slack, scheduling apps, CRM) and provide login credentials.
*   **Safety Briefing:** Conduct a mandatory walkthrough of facility safety protocols, emergency exits, and reporting procedures.
*   **Task-Specific Training:** Perform a guided demonstration of the specific tasks the volunteer will undertake.
*   **Shadowing Session:** Pair the new volunteer with an experienced mentor or staff member for at least one full shift.

## Phase 4: Integration and Feedback
*   **Introduction to Team:** Send a welcome email to the staff or existing team introducing the new volunteer.
*   **Workspace Setup:** Ensure the volunteer has a designated area or equipment if working on-site.
*   **First-Week Check-in:** Schedule a follow-up call at the end of the first week to address questions and gather initial feedback.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Create a "Welcome Packet" (PDF or physical binder) that acts as a quick-reference guide. This prevents volunteers from feeling overwhelmed by verbal instructions.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use an automated scheduling tool for interviews to minimize back-and-forth emails.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-training. Do not attempt to teach every aspect of the organization in one session. Focus only on what is required for them to perform their role successfully on day one.
*   **Pitfall:** Neglecting the "human" element. A strict focus on paperwork without an introduction to the team culture often leads to early volunteer attrition.

## FAQ
**Q: How long should the onboarding process take?**
A: Depending on the complexity of the role, the entire process should be completed within 7–14 days. Aim for a balance between thoroughness and speed to keep momentum high.

**Q: Should I treat student volunteers differently?**
A: The core steps remain the same, but you may need to add a "Documentation for Credit" step if the student needs verification of hours for their educational institution.

**Q: What if a volunteer fails the background check?**
A: You should have a pre-written, compassionate, but firm letter template ready to inform the individual that they cannot be placed in the requested role due to internal policy. Always consult with your HR or legal department before issuing such notice.

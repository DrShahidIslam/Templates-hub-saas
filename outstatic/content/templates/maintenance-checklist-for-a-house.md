# Standard Operating Procedure: Residential Preventive Maintenance

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory preventive maintenance tasks required to preserve the structural integrity, safety, and operational efficiency of a residential property. By adhering to this structured schedule, homeowners can mitigate the risk of catastrophic failure, lower long-term repair costs, and maintain property value. This document serves as a comprehensive baseline for seasonal inspections and routine upkeep.

## I. Exterior Maintenance (Quarterly)
*   **Roof Inspection:** Visually inspect shingles/tiles for cracks, curling, or missing segments. Clear debris from valleys to prevent water pooling.
*   **Gutters & Downspouts:** Remove leaves and organic matter. Ensure downspouts direct water at least 5 feet away from the foundation to prevent basement seepage.
*   **Foundation & Siding:** Check for fresh cracks in concrete or gaps in exterior siding. Seal gaps with high-grade exterior caulk to prevent pest infiltration.
*   **Hardscape:** Inspect driveways and walkways for trip hazards. Reseal asphalt or pressure wash pavers to prevent vegetative growth.

## II. HVAC & Climate Control (Bi-Annually)
*   **Air Filters:** Replace HVAC filters every 90 days (or 30 days if pets are present). Mark the installation date on the filter frame.
*   **Condenser Units:** Clear vegetation, weeds, and debris from a 2-foot radius around exterior AC units to ensure proper airflow.
*   **Vents and Registers:** Vacuum dust from supply and return registers. Ensure no furniture or heavy drapes are obstructing airflow.
*   **Thermostat Calibration:** Verify that the thermostat readings match ambient room temperature. Test the "Emergency Heat" setting during fall inspection.

## III. Plumbing & Water Systems (Biannually)
*   **Water Heater:** Flush the tank to remove mineral sediment buildup. Test the Temperature & Pressure (T&P) relief valve by lifting the lever briefly to ensure it releases water.
*   **Leak Detection:** Inspect under all sinks, near toilet bases, and around the water heater for signs of moisture, mold, or mineral staining.
*   **Drains:** Check for slow-draining fixtures. Use non-corrosive enzymatic cleaners to maintain pipe flow; avoid harsh caustic chemicals.
*   **Main Shut-off Valve:** Locate the main water shut-off valve and ensure it is accessible and functional. Label it clearly.

## IV. Interior Safety & Appliances (Annually)
*   **Smoke & CO Detectors:** Press the "Test" button monthly. Replace batteries annually (or when the low-battery chirp initiates). Replace units entirely every 10 years.
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Check pressure gauge needles to ensure they are in the "Green" zone. Shake dry-chemical extinguishers to prevent powder settling.
*   **Dryer Vent:** Disconnect the vent hose from the rear of the dryer and vacuum out lint buildup. This is a critical fire prevention step.
*   **Appliance Seals:** Inspect rubber gaskets on refrigerators and dishwashers. Clean with a damp cloth and mild soap to maintain an airtight seal.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Create a "Maintenance Binder." Keep receipts, warranty documents, and a log of dates when services (like furnace cleanings) were performed. This adds significant value during a future home sale.
*   **Pro Tip:** Perform a "Low-Light Inspection." Use a high-lumen flashlight to inspect crawlspaces and attics; uneven lighting reveals cracks and moisture spots that are invisible in daylight.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use "Universal" cleaners on delicate stone or hardwood surfaces. Always check manufacturer guidelines before applying chemical solutions.
*   **Pitfall:** Never ignore a "minor" drip. A leak of one drop per second can waste over 3,000 gallons of water per year and often indicates a failing washer or valve.

## FAQ
**Q: How often should I hire a professional for home maintenance?**
A: You should schedule a professional inspection for your HVAC system and electrical panel at least once a year. These systems require specialized diagnostic tools that exceed the scope of standard homeowner maintenance.

**Q: Can I perform maintenance myself, or will it void my warranties?**
A: Most manufacturers encourage routine cleaning and filter changes. However, always document your work with photos and receipts. If you attempt to repair a complex appliance or electrical component yourself, you risk voiding the warranty. When in doubt, consult the manufacturer’s manual.

**Q: What is the most overlooked maintenance item?**
A: Dryer vent cleaning. Lint accumulation is a leading cause of residential house fires and significantly reduces the efficiency of your appliance, leading to higher energy bills.

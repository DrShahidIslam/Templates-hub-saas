# SOP: Internal Audit Protocol for Transitional Facilities

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory internal audit framework for transitional facilities to ensure compliance with safety, operational, and regulatory standards. The objective is to identify potential risks, verify the efficacy of rehabilitative programming, and ensure the physical environment remains conducive to resident stability. This audit is to be conducted quarterly by the facility manager or an appointed compliance officer to maintain institutional integrity and resident welfare.

## 1. Documentation and Regulatory Compliance
* Verify current operating licenses and permits are displayed and up to date.
* Confirm that resident admission and discharge logs are complete, accurate, and time-stamped.
* Ensure all resident files contain signed Informed Consent forms and updated Individualized Service Plans (ISPs).
* Check the Incident Report log for completeness; verify that all entries include staff signatures and corrective action plans.
* Confirm that HIPAA/privacy compliance protocols are being strictly followed regarding digital and physical file storage.

## 2. Facility Safety and Infrastructure
* Inspect all fire suppression systems (extinguishers, smoke detectors, sprinklers) for current inspection tags.
* Audit emergency evacuation routes for obstructions and ensure signage is visible and illuminated.
* Verify the functionality of security hardware, including keyed access points, CCTV recording systems, and perimeter lighting.
* Conduct a walk-through of common areas and resident rooms to identify structural hazards (e.g., mold, loose flooring, exposed wiring).
* Inspect hygiene stations, checking for adequate supplies of soap, sanitizer, and PPE.

## 3. Health, Wellness, and Medication Management
* Audit medication administration records (MARs) for gaps, unauthorized staff signatures, or transcription errors.
* Verify that all controlled substances are stored in a double-locked, secure cabinet with a verified inventory log.
* Confirm that resident health profiles are updated and that emergency contact information is current.
* Ensure that the facility’s food storage and kitchen area meet local health department sanitation standards (check temperature logs for refrigerators/freezers).

## 4. Operational Staffing and Training
* Cross-reference staff shift rosters with time-clock records to ensure compliance with minimum staffing ratios.
* Verify that all staff have current certifications (CPR, First Aid, Crisis Intervention) on file.
* Review minutes from the last two monthly staff meetings to confirm the dissemination of policy updates.
* Interview two staff members regarding their knowledge of emergency protocols (fire, medical crisis, or security breach).

## 5. Resident Programming and Environment
* Review attendance logs for mandatory life-skills workshops or counseling sessions.
* Conduct a brief, anonymous survey (or physical inspection) to ensure basic amenities (linens, hygiene kits) are provided.
* Audit the "Suggestion Box" or resident grievance log to verify that all complaints were addressed within 48–72 hours.
* Observe house rules enforcement: ensure policies regarding curfews and contraband are applied consistently across the resident population.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
* **Pro Tip:** Don’t announce the audit date too far in advance. Unannounced "spot checks" yield a more accurate reflection of daily operational habits.
* **Pro Tip:** Use digital audit software (e.g., GoCanvas or Formstack) to automatically flag recurring issues for management review.
* **Pitfall: "Siloing" Records.** Failing to cross-reference incident reports with staffing levels often hides the root cause of facility disturbances. 
* **Pitfall: Neglecting the "Culture."** Audits are not just about paperwork; if staff morale is low, procedural adherence usually drops simultaneously. Note the "vibe" of the facility during your walkthrough.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How long should we retain the internal audit reports?**
A: Records should be retained for a minimum of 3–5 years, depending on your state’s specific regulatory requirements for transitional housing.

**Q: What should I do if I find a critical non-compliance issue during the audit?**
A: Immediate remediation is required. Document the finding, implement an interim corrective action plan, and report the issue to your direct supervisor or the board of directors within 24 hours.

**Q: Should residents be involved in the audit process?**
A: While they should not be involved in the administrative audit, resident interviews are highly encouraged to provide qualitative data on the effectiveness of the facility's programming and the quality of care.

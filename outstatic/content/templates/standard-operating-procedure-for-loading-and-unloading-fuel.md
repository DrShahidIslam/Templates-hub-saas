# Standard Operating Procedure: Loading and Unloading Fuel

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory safety protocols and operational steps for the loading and unloading of liquid fuels. Adherence to these procedures is critical to prevent static discharge, spills, environmental contamination, and fire hazards. All personnel involved in these operations must possess the required certifications and wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) at all times.

## 1. Pre-Operation Preparation
*   **Verify Documentation:** Confirm that the bill of lading, hazardous materials shipping papers, and destination instructions are accurate and present.
*   **PPE Check:** Ensure all personnel are wearing anti-static footwear, flame-resistant clothing (FRC), chemical-resistant gloves, and safety goggles.
*   **Site Security:** Place warning cones and "No Smoking / Engine Off" signage at least 25 feet from the loading/unloading point.
*   **Grounding and Bonding:** Connect the grounding cable to the vehicle chassis and the facility grounding point *before* touching any product hoses to prevent static discharge.
*   **Fire Suppression:** Verify that a fully charged, inspected fire extinguisher is placed in a designated, accessible location near the work area.

## 2. Loading Procedure
*   **Positioning:** Spot the tanker precisely at the loading rack, engage the parking brake, and place wheel chocks.
*   **Vapor Recovery:** Connect the vapor recovery hose to the vehicle’s vapor header to capture displaced vapors and prevent emissions.
*   **Connection:** Secure the loading arm or hose to the vehicle’s API adapter. Ensure a "dry-break" connection is locked and leak-free.
*   **Bottom Loading:** Open the internal valves and initiate the loading process at a low initial flow rate to minimize static generation. 
*   **Monitoring:** Remain at the controls for the duration of the transfer. Monitor the overfill protection system/sensors.
*   **Completion:** Once the desired volume is reached, close internal valves, drain the hose into the storage unit, and disconnect couplings.

## 3. Unloading Procedure
*   **Verification:** Confirm the receiving tank has sufficient ullage (capacity) to accommodate the full volume of the fuel being transferred.
*   **Hose Inspection:** Check for cracks, soft spots, or loose fittings on all hoses before attachment.
*   **Connection:** Attach the discharge hose to the vehicle outlet and the storage tank intake using cam-lock fittings. Ensure dust caps are replaced on fittings after use.
*   **Gravity or Pump Transfer:** Open valves sequentially—starting from the receiving tank valve, moving toward the tanker outlet—to prevent "hammer" effects.
*   **Emergency Stop:** Keep the Emergency Shut-Off (ESO) button within immediate reach throughout the entire transfer.

## 4. Post-Operation and Cleanup
*   **Drain and Stow:** Ensure all hoses are completely drained of residual product before disconnecting. Store hoses on racks to prevent kinking.
*   **Final Inspection:** Verify that all tank valves are closed, vents are secure, and no leaks are present.
*   **Equipment Removal:** Disconnect grounding/bonding cables only after all hoses have been stowed.
*   **Record Keeping:** Sign off on the delivery manifest and log the transfer in the facility shift report.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always use the "touch-and-go" method for grounding; touch the grounding clamp to the vehicle before securing it to ensure a complete circuit.
*   **Pitfall - Rushing:** The most common cause of spills is rushing the "breakdown" phase. Never disconnect a hose until you have visually confirmed the valve is closed and the pressure has been bled off.
*   **Pitfall - Static:** Never splash-fill a tank. Always ensure the fill pipe extends to the bottom of the tank to prevent static buildup caused by free-falling liquid.

## FAQ
**Q: What should I do if a small leak occurs during transfer?**
A: Immediately hit the Emergency Shut-Off (ESO) button, isolate the leak if safe to do so, and notify the site supervisor or environmental safety officer. Do not attempt to fix a pressurized leak while the system is running.

**Q: Can I use a cellular phone during the loading process?**
A: No. Electronic devices that are not intrinsically safe pose an ignition risk. Cell phones must be stored away from the loading/unloading area.

**Q: Why is the grounding cable the first thing connected and the last thing removed?**
A: The grounding cable creates a path for stray electrical charges to dissipate into the earth. Keeping it connected throughout the entire process ensures that any static charge built up during product flow is neutralized immediately.

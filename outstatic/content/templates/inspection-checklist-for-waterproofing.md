# Standard Operating Procedure: Waterproofing Inspection Protocol

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory inspection requirements for waterproofing applications to ensure structural integrity and compliance with building codes. Proper waterproofing is the primary defense against moisture infiltration, which can lead to structural decay, mold growth, and premature failure of finishes. This document provides a rigorous framework for site supervisors and quality control inspectors to verify substrate preparation, membrane application, and drainage systems prior to final closure or backfilling.

## 1. Pre-Application Substrate Inspection
Before any waterproofing material is applied, the substrate must be verified as structurally sound and properly prepared to prevent adhesion failure.

*   Verify substrate moisture content levels are within the manufacturer’s specified tolerance (use a moisture meter).
*   Ensure all surfaces are clean, free of dust, oil, grease, curing compounds, or laitance.
*   Inspect for sharp edges, protrusions, or honeycombing in concrete; grind smooth or fill with an approved mortar.
*   Confirm proper slopes/falls (minimum 1:80 for balconies/decks) toward drainage outlets.
*   Check that all internal and external corners have been addressed with proper fillets or bond-breaker tapes.

## 2. Membrane Application Inspection
This phase focuses on ensuring the continuity of the waterproofing layer, which is the most common point of failure.

*   Verify the membrane is compatible with the substrate and any overlapping materials.
*   Check dry film thickness (DFT) at random intervals using a wet film gauge or physical cut-out testing (if permitted).
*   Confirm that all penetrations (pipes, vents, drains) are sealed with appropriate collars or flashing reinforcement.
*   Ensure proper overlap widths are maintained according to the technical data sheet (TDS).
*   Inspect for "holidays," pinholes, blisters, or air bubbles during the curing process.
*   Verify that the membrane has been carried up vertical surfaces (upstands) to the required minimum height (typically 150mm above finished floor level).

## 3. Flood Testing and Quality Verification
The flood test is the critical performance benchmark for horizontal waterproofing applications.

*   Seal all drains and outlets using appropriate mechanical plugs.
*   Fill the area with water to a minimum depth of 25mm–50mm or as specified by local codes.
*   Maintain the standing water for a minimum of 24 to 48 hours.
*   Inspect the underside of the slab/deck for signs of dampness or active dripping throughout the duration of the test.
*   Verify no loss of water level, accounting for potential evaporation.
*   Document the completion of the flood test with photos and signed verification forms.

## 4. Protection and Drainage Inspection
Once the waterproofing is complete, it must be protected from mechanical damage during subsequent construction phases.

*   Verify that drainage mats or protection boards are installed correctly without puncturing the membrane.
*   Ensure that backfilling materials are free of sharp debris or large rocks that could damage the membrane.
*   Confirm that filter fabrics are properly secured and overlap according to project specifications.
*   Check that all drainage pipes are clear of construction debris.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip:** Always record the batch numbers of the waterproofing products used. In the event of a warranty claim, this allows you to trace materials back to the manufacturing date.
*   **Pitfall - Rushing the Cure:** Many failures occur because the next trade started work before the membrane fully cured. Always adhere to the "re-coat" and "traffic" time windows listed on the TDS.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use high-visibility tape to mark any minor repairs made to the membrane during inspection; this makes it easy for the QC manager to re-verify those specific areas.
*   **Pitfall - Neglecting Upstands:** The most common failure point is the junction where the horizontal membrane meets the wall. Ensure the wall surface is primed and the membrane is adequately bonded to the vertical substrate.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if a pinhole is discovered after the membrane has cured?**
A: Do not simply apply more material over the top. The area must be abraded, cleaned with the manufacturer-approved solvent, and a patch of reinforcing fabric and membrane must be applied, extending at least 50mm beyond the repair area.

**Q: Is a flood test mandatory for all waterproofing projects?**
A: While highly recommended for all horizontal installations, it is mandatory for high-risk areas like balconies, podium decks, and wet rooms. If a flood test is not feasible due to site conditions, an electronic leak detection (ELD) test should be performed.

**Q: How do I handle weather interruptions during the curing process?**
A: If rain is imminent, stop application immediately. If a surface has been rained on during the initial set, it must be inspected for surface damage or dilution of the product; in most cases, the affected area will require complete removal and re-application.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Safety Step Protocol

This document outlines the mandatory procedures for identifying, assessing, and mitigating operational hazards during facility tasks. Adherence to this protocol is critical for maintaining an injury-free work environment, ensuring regulatory compliance, and upholding organizational standards. This procedure applies to all employees, contractors, and visitors operating within high-risk work zones.

## Phase 1: Pre-Operational Hazard Identification
Before initiating any task, personnel must conduct a thorough environmental assessment to identify potential safety gaps.

*   **Area Inspection:** Conduct a visual sweep of the workspace to identify tripping hazards, spills, or obstructed emergency exits.
*   **Equipment Audit:** Verify that all machinery and safety guards are in good working condition and free of damage.
*   **PPE Verification:** Confirm that all required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)—including eye protection, high-visibility vests, and footwear—is worn correctly and is in compliance with safety standards.
*   **Signage Check:** Ensure that adequate warning signs and floor markings are visible and current.

## Phase 2: Active Risk Mitigation
Once hazards are identified, utilize the following steps to neutralize risks before proceeding with operations.

*   **Hazard Containment:** Isolate the work area using stanchions, caution tape, or physical barriers to prevent unauthorized entry.
*   **LOTO (Lockout/Tagout):** If equipment requires maintenance or adjustment, ensure the power source is locked and tagged to prevent accidental reactivation.
*   **Ergonomic Alignment:** Adjust workstations to minimize strain; ensure heavy lifting utilizes mechanical aids or proper team-lifting techniques.
*   **Communication Protocol:** Verbally announce intended movements or equipment start-ups to all nearby personnel to ensure situational awareness.

## Phase 3: Post-Operational Verification
Safety does not end when the task is complete. Use this phase to restore the environment to a "Safe State."

*   **Site Restoration:** Clean the workspace of all debris, scrap materials, or liquids used during the process.
*   **Equipment Storage:** Return tools and safety equipment to their designated storage locations.
*   **Debrief:** Report any "near-miss" incidents to the floor supervisor immediately to prevent future recurrence.
*   **Sign-off:** Complete the daily safety log to confirm the area has been cleared and remains compliant with facility standards.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **The "Two-Minute Rule":** If a task feels rushed or disorganized, stop for two minutes to regroup. Most accidents occur when personnel try to "make up time" by skipping pre-operational checks.
*   **Peer Accountability:** Encourage a "See Something, Say Something" culture where employees feel empowered to stop a colleague's unsafe action regardless of their rank.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **Complacency:** Do not assume that because a task was performed safely yesterday, it is inherently safe today. Environmental variables change constantly.
*   **Normalization of Deviance:** Never accept a "workaround" that bypasses safety guards or standard procedure just because it is more convenient. 

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if I identify a safety hazard I cannot fix myself?**
A: Immediately stop all work in the vicinity, secure the area with physical barriers if possible, and escalate the issue to your immediate supervisor or the Facility Safety Officer.

**Q: Are there exceptions to the PPE requirements for quick, minor tasks?**
A: No. Exceptions create a culture of inconsistency. All safety protocols must be followed regardless of the duration of the task.

**Q: Where should I document a near-miss incident?**
A: All near-misses must be logged in the electronic Safety Management System (SMS) within 24 hours. These records are vital for proactive safety training and procedural improvements.

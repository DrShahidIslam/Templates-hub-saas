# Standard Operating Procedure: New User Onboarding for [App Name]

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for effectively onboarding new users to the [App Name] platform. The objective of this procedure is to reduce time-to-value, ensure high feature adoption rates, and mitigate early-stage churn. By following this systematic approach, the Operations and Customer Success teams will ensure that every user receives a consistent, high-quality experience from the moment of registration through to their first "Aha!" moment.

## Phase 1: Pre-Onboarding & Account Provisioning
*   **Verify Credentials:** Confirm that the user’s email address is validated and the account is active within the CRM/Admin portal.
*   **Segment User:** Categorize the user based on their specific plan (e.g., Free, Pro, Enterprise) and primary use case identified during signup.
*   **Trigger Welcome Email:** Ensure the automated "Welcome" email containing the login link and initial resource links has been dispatched.
*   **System Permissions:** Grant appropriate access levels based on the user's role (Admin, Editor, or Viewer) to prevent configuration errors.

## Phase 2: Guided Setup & Feature Activation
*   **The "First Login" Tour:** Initiate the interactive product walkthrough (e.g., Pendo or WalkMe overlay) to highlight the primary dashboard navigation.
*   **Data Integration:** Assist the user in connecting essential third-party integrations (e.g., Slack, CRM, or Google Drive) to ensure immediate utility.
*   **Configuration Review:** Verify that global settings, such as time zones, notification preferences, and workspace branding, are correctly configured.
*   **First Action Milestone:** Guide the user to perform the "Key Success Metric" (e.g., creating their first project or uploading their first data set).

## Phase 3: Education & Resource Hand-off
*   **Knowledge Base Access:** Provide the user with direct links to the Help Center, FAQ section, and video tutorial library.
*   **Support Channel Introduction:** Explicitly state the method for requesting support (e.g., Zendesk ticketing, Intercom chat, or dedicated account manager).
*   **Best Practices Overview:** Share the "Getting Started" PDF or "Top 5 Pro Tips" email to encourage advanced feature exploration.
*   **Milestone Check-in:** Schedule an automated follow-up email or message 48 hours after sign-up to assess the user’s progress.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pitfall: Overwhelming the User.** Avoid "Feature Bloat" in the initial tour. Stick to the 3-5 core features required to get the job done.
*   **Pro Tip: Leverage Behavioral Triggers.** Use tools like Amplitude or Mixpanel to trigger "Help" prompts specifically when a user stops moving their cursor or lingers on a complex configuration page.
*   **Pitfall: Manual Bottlenecks.** Avoid manual account provisioning for B2B Enterprise clients; ensure API automation handles user creation to prevent friction.
*   **Pro Tip: Personalization.** Always address the user by name and reference their specific industry or role in automated communication to increase open rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if a user fails to complete the onboarding tour?**
A: Do not force a restart. Allow the user to trigger the tour manually via the "Help/Get Started" widget on the dashboard footer.

**Q: How do we measure the success of our onboarding process?**
A: Track the "Time-to-Activation" (TTA) metric—the time it takes for a user to perform the first core action after registration. A successful onboarding process will show a downward trend in TTA over time.

**Q: Should I reach out personally to every new user?**
A: For high-touch Enterprise users, yes. For self-service/SMB users, scale your outreach through segmented email automation and in-app messaging to maintain efficiency.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Workplace Safety Protocols

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory safety requirements and behavioral expectations for all personnel operating within the facility. The objective of this document is to mitigate operational risks, ensure compliance with Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) regulations, and foster a proactive culture of incident prevention. Adherence to these protocols is non-negotiable and applies to all employees, contractors, and visitors.

## Section 1: Pre-Operational Hazard Assessment
*   Conduct a site walk-through to identify potential trip hazards, spills, or obstructed emergency exits.
*   Verify that all machinery guards, warning signage, and protective barriers are securely in place and functional.
*   Confirm that all required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is available, clean, and in good condition.
*   Check that current safety data sheets (SDS) are accessible for any hazardous materials being utilized during the shift.

## Section 2: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Requirements
*   Don mandatory base-level PPE (e.g., steel-toed boots, high-visibility vest) upon entry to the designated floor.
*   Don task-specific PPE (e.g., safety goggles, ear protection, chemical-resistant gloves) prior to commencing specialized operations.
*   Perform a "fit check" on all respirators or tight-fitting gear to ensure an adequate seal.
*   Inspect gear for wear and tear; replace damaged items immediately through the operations manager before beginning work.

## Section 3: Emergency Preparedness & Response
*   Locate the two nearest emergency exits and the designated assembly point outside the facility.
*   Verify the proximity and accessibility of the nearest fire extinguisher and eye-wash station.
*   Review the facility-wide emergency signal protocols (e.g., alarm tones for fire vs. lockdown).
*   Ensure personal communication devices are charged and that the internal emergency contact list is saved.

## Section 4: Incident Reporting and Escalation
*   Cease operations immediately upon witnessing a "near miss" or an actual safety incident.
*   Secure the area to prevent further harm or contamination to other personnel.
*   Notify the immediate supervisor or safety officer verbally within 15 minutes of the occurrence.
*   Complete the formal digital Incident Report Form within 24 hours, including photos and witness statements.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **The "Two-Minute Drill":** Before starting any task, spend two minutes visualizing the work sequence to identify where a potential accident could occur.
*   **Active Peer Monitoring:** Encourage a culture where employees feel comfortable calling out a peer’s unsafe behavior; safety is a collective responsibility.
*   **Ergonomic Awareness:** Even in non-industrial roles, practice proper lifting techniques (legs, not back) to prevent chronic injury.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **Complacency:** The most common cause of accidents is "task familiarity," where experienced workers take shortcuts. Never bypass a safety guard, even if it feels tedious.
*   **Ignoring "Small" Hazards:** Walking past a small puddle or a loose cable is a failure of safety leadership. Own the workspace—if you see it, fix it or report it immediately.
*   **Documentation Neglect:** Failing to report a near-miss is a missed opportunity to prevent a future injury. If it happened once, it can happen again.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if the PPE provided to me is damaged?**
A: Do not use the equipment. Tag it as "Out of Service" and report it to your supervisor immediately to receive a replacement. Operating with damaged PPE is a direct violation of safety policy.

**Q: Does the "Report a Near-Miss" policy lead to disciplinary action?**
A: No. We follow a "Just Culture" model. Reporting a near-miss is encouraged and incentivized as a way to learn and improve processes. We only take disciplinary action for willful, repeated, or malicious disregard for safety rules.

**Q: If I see a contractor violating safety protocols, what is my responsibility?**
A: You are authorized to issue a "Stop Work" order if you perceive an immediate threat to life or property. Afterward, notify the site safety manager or operations supervisor immediately so the contractor can be briefed or removed as necessary.

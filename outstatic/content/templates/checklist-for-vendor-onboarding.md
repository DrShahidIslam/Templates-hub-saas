# Standard Operating Procedure: Vendor Onboarding Process

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for vetting, registering, and integrating new vendors into the company’s supply chain. Adhering to this process ensures that all partners meet our regulatory, financial, and ethical standards, thereby minimizing operational risk and ensuring seamless procurement integration. This process must be completed in its entirety before any purchase orders are issued or services are rendered.

## Phase 1: Vetting and Due Diligence
*   **Initial Request:** Obtain a formal request from the department head explaining the business need for the vendor.
*   **Conflict of Interest Check:** Require the vendor to sign a Conflict of Interest Disclosure statement.
*   **Background Verification:** Conduct a web-based search for public records, news, and credit history to ensure financial stability.
*   **Regulatory Screening:** Run the entity name against global watchlists (e.g., OFAC, sanctions lists, and debarment lists).
*   **Performance References:** Obtain at least two verifiable client references to confirm reliability and service quality.

## Phase 2: Documentation and Compliance
*   **Vendor Information Form (VIF):** Ensure the vendor provides a completed VIF, including legal name, tax ID (W-9 or equivalent), and primary contact details.
*   **Certificate of Insurance (COI):** Verify that the vendor holds active insurance policies (General Liability, Workers’ Comp, etc.) that meet our minimum coverage requirements.
*   **Contract Execution:** Secure a signed Master Services Agreement (MSA) or standard Purchase Agreement reviewed by the Legal department.
*   **Banking Verification:** Secure a pre-printed voided check or official letter from the bank confirming ACH/Wire transfer details to prevent payment fraud.

## Phase 3: Systems Integration and Final Approval
*   **ERP/Accounting Setup:** Create a unique Vendor ID in the company’s accounting software.
*   **Internal Access Control:** Provision any necessary software licenses or building security badges required for the vendor to perform their work.
*   **Stakeholder Briefing:** Notify the Accounts Payable (AP) and Procurement teams that the vendor is active and ready for transaction processing.
*   **Welcome Communication:** Send an onboarding email to the vendor providing the primary point of contact, invoicing guidelines, and payment terms.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Automate the document collection process using a secure vendor portal or cloud-based document management system to avoid handling sensitive files via email.
*   **Pro Tip:** Standardize payment terms across all vendors (e.g., Net 30) to simplify cash flow forecasting.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not allow "emergency" onboarding bypasses. Bypassing due diligence often leads to significant financial loss and compliance violations.
*   **Pitfall:** Ensure that the person authorizing the vendor is not the same person responsible for approving their invoices, as this creates a lack of internal control.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if a vendor refuses to provide their tax identification number?**
A: Under no circumstances should you proceed with onboarding. A valid tax ID is mandatory for regulatory compliance and tax reporting purposes.

**Q: How often should we re-verify a vendor’s credentials?**
A: It is industry best practice to re-verify insurance certificates annually and conduct a full financial due diligence review every two to three years.

**Q: Can a department head bypass the background check for an "urgent" vendor?**
A: No. All vendors, regardless of the size or urgency of the engagement, must clear the full onboarding process to protect the company from liability and fraud.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Statutory Audit Preparation for Listed Companies

As a listed entity, the statutory audit process is a critical governance exercise that extends beyond simple financial verification to include rigorous compliance with securities regulations, accounting standards (Ind AS/IFRS/GAAP), and internal control mandates (e.g., SOX or IFC). This SOP serves as a comprehensive roadmap for Finance and Compliance teams to streamline audit workflows, ensure data integrity, and minimize audit findings. The objective is to facilitate a seamless audit lifecycle while maintaining the high standards of transparency required by market regulators and stakeholders.

## 1. Pre-Audit Planning and Documentation
*   **Engagement Letter Review:** Confirm the audit scope, timeline, and auditor independence requirements with the Audit Committee.
*   **Audit Calendar:** Finalize key dates for interim and final audit phases, including sign-off deadlines for the Board.
*   **Internal Control Review:** Ensure the Internal Financial Controls (IFC) framework is updated and documented for the current reporting period.
*   **Communication Channel:** Establish a secure, centralized digital portal (e.g., a Virtual Data Room) for all audit evidence to prevent version control issues.

## 2. Revenue and Receivables
*   **Revenue Recognition:** Verify compliance with performance obligations under applicable revenue recognition standards.
*   **Cut-off Testing:** Review sales invoices and shipping documents issued in the days surrounding the balance sheet date to ensure proper period-end allocation.
*   **Accounts Receivable Aging:** Audit the provision for Expected Credit Losses (ECL) and review correspondence for disputed balances.
*   **Contract Review:** Select a sample of high-value customer contracts to cross-reference with recognized revenue and deferred income.

## 3. Financial Statements and Disclosure Compliance
*   **Related Party Transactions (RPTs):** Cross-check the RPT register against Board/Audit Committee approvals and market disclosures.
*   **Share Capital Reconciliation:** Reconcile shareholding patterns, ESOPs, and treasury stock with the Registrar of Companies (ROC) and stock exchange filings.
*   **Earnings Per Share (EPS):** Validate the calculation of basic and diluted EPS against weighted average share data.
*   **Subsequent Events:** Perform a review of material events occurring between the balance sheet date and the audit sign-off date (e.g., litigation outcomes, significant asset impairments).

## 4. Operational and Corporate Governance
*   **Board Minutes:** Ensure all significant decisions made in board and committee meetings are appropriately reflected in the financial statements.
*   **Regulatory Filings:** Audit the consistency between financial statements and quarterly/annual filings submitted to the Stock Exchange (e.g., 10-K, 10-Q, or local equivalent).
*   **Tax Audit Alignment:** Verify that tax provisions and deferred tax assets/liabilities reconcile with the statutory tax audit filing.
*   **Inventory and Assets:** Perform physical verification reports review and ensure valuation methodologies are consistently applied.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The "Early Warning" System.** Conduct "Pre-Audit" dry runs with internal audit teams two months before the year-end to identify accounting gaps before the external auditors arrive.
*   **Pro Tip: Audit Trail Hygiene.** Ensure every adjustment journal entry has a clear, written rationale and an authorized signature trail to satisfy "walk-through" tests.
*   **Pitfall: Disorganized PBC List.** Failure to provide "Provided by Client" (PBC) items in the requested format or sequence is the primary cause of audit delays. Assign a dedicated audit coordinator to track PBC status daily.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Qualitative Disclosures.** Do not focus solely on numbers; auditors are increasingly scrutinizing the Management Discussion and Analysis (MD&A) section for consistency with audited figures.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How do we handle discrepancies found by auditors during the audit?**
A: Log all findings in an "Audit Issues Tracker." Discuss significant adjustments with the Audit Committee immediately and ensure all proposed adjustments are reconciled against the trial balance before final sign-off.

**Q: What is the primary focus of the auditor regarding "Going Concern"?**
A: Auditors will review your cash flow forecasts, liquidity ratios, and access to credit lines. Prepare a robust, evidence-backed narrative on the entity's ability to operate for the next 12 months, particularly if the company has recently faced operational losses.

**Q: How should we manage communication with the Audit Committee?**
A: The Audit Committee should be briefed periodically (at least quarterly). Provide a "Status of Audit" report highlighting any management disagreements or high-risk items identified by the auditors during the process.

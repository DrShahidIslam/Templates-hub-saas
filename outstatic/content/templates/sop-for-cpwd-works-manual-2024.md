# Standard Operating Procedure: Execution of Works under CPWD Manual 2024

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive operational framework for the planning, execution, and monitoring of civil and electrical works as per the CPWD Works Manual 2024. Adherence to this SOP ensures transparency, financial propriety, and technical compliance, mitigating risks associated with procurement, contract management, and project delivery. This document acts as a functional bridge between the administrative directives of the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs and the field-level execution team.

## 1. Project Initiation and Administrative Approval
*   **Need Assessment:** Define the scope of work based on user requirements and site inspections.
*   **Preliminary Estimates (PE):** Draft the PE including detailed justification, site plans, and architectural sketches.
*   **Administrative Approval (AA) & Expenditure Sanction (ES):** Obtain formal approval from the competent financial authority.
*   **Budget Allocation:** Verify the availability of funds under the relevant head of account before proceeding.

## 2. Technical Sanction (TS) and Detailed Planning
*   **Detailed Estimate Preparation:** Draft comprehensive estimates based on the current Delhi Schedule of Rates (DSR) or Market Rates (MR) where applicable.
*   **Technical Sanction:** Secure TS from the designated authority, ensuring all structural designs and load calculations are certified by qualified engineers.
*   **Drawings:** Finalize working drawings, structural layouts, and MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) services layout.
*   **Soil Investigation:** Ensure mandatory site investigations are completed and documented before finalizing foundation designs.

## 3. Tendering and Procurement Process
*   **NIT (Notice Inviting Tender):** Prepare the NIT ensuring inclusion of mandatory clauses as per the 2024 manual.
*   **e-Tendering:** Conduct the entire bidding process through the NIC/CPPP portal to maintain transparency.
*   **Technical Evaluation:** Evaluate bids based on eligibility criteria (work experience, financial turnover, and technical capacity).
*   **Financial Evaluation:** Open financial bids for technically qualified tenderers and prepare a comparative statement.
*   **Letter of Award (LOA):** Issue the LOA to the lowest responsive bidder (L1) and ensure performance security is submitted within the prescribed timeline.

## 4. Execution and Contract Management
*   **Site Handover:** Formally document the handing over of the site to the contractor.
*   **Quality Control:** Establish a site testing laboratory or ensure third-party testing for concrete, steel, and other critical materials.
*   **Daily Progress Report (DPR):** Maintain a digitized DPR tracking manpower, machinery, and daily activities.
*   **Site Order Book:** Maintain a physical and digital Site Order Book signed by both the Junior Engineer (JE) and the contractor’s representative.
*   **Measurement Book (MB):** Record measurements in the MB promptly after completion of each stage of work.

## 5. Completion and Handover
*   **Final Bill Preparation:** Cross-verify the final measurements against the sanctioned estimates.
*   **Quality Audit:** Conduct a final inspection to identify snags or defects.
*   **Completion Certificate:** Issue the formal completion certificate once all contractual obligations are met.
*   **Defects Liability Period (DLP):** Monitor the structure during the DLP and ensure the contractor performs necessary repairs.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Utilize the 2024 digital dashboard features for real-time tracking of MB entries to prevent end-of-project delays.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain an updated "Register of Disputes" to flag potential litigation risks early.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-reliance on "Market Rate" (MR) items. Always prioritize items available in the DSR to avoid audit objections.
*   **Pitfall:** Failure to update the "Extension of Time" (EOT) register during rainy seasons or labor shortages. This often leads to unnecessary liquidated damages disputes.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is the primary difference in the 2024 manual regarding green building mandates?**
The 2024 manual explicitly mandates the integration of GRIHA or IGBC certification standards for all projects above a specific cost threshold, requiring dedicated sub-headings in the Detailed Estimate for sustainable materials.

**2. How should price adjustment claims be handled under the 2024 guidelines?**
Price adjustments must strictly follow the formula-based approach provided in the current manual; claims must be supported by indices published by the Economic Advisor, Ministry of Commerce and Industry.

**3. Is there a change in the procedure for handling Extra Items (EI)?**
Yes, the 2024 manual emphasizes the "Pro-rata" method for extra items that are similar to existing contract items, requiring explicit approval from the next higher authority if the cumulative value exceeds 5% of the tendered amount.

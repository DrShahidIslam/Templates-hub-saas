# Standard Operating Procedure: Tenant Move-Out Process

## Introduction
The tenant move-out process is a critical phase of the property management lifecycle. A standardized approach ensures the protection of the property asset, minimizes vacancy turnover time, and maintains strict compliance with local landlord-tenant laws. This SOP provides a comprehensive framework for landlords to execute a professional, transparent, and efficient transition between tenancies, ensuring that security deposit dispositions are handled legally and property conditions are documented thoroughly.

## Step 1: Pre-Departure Coordination
*   **Notice Review:** Confirm receipt of written notice to vacate and verify the date against the lease agreement.
*   **Tenant Communication:** Send a "Move-Out Instructions" email to the tenant detailing cleaning expectations, key return procedures, and utility transfer requirements.
*   **Forwarding Address:** Collect the tenant’s forwarding address in writing to ensure legal compliance for security deposit statements.
*   **Schedule Inspection:** Coordinate a specific date and time for the move-out inspection, ideally while the tenant is present to avoid future disputes.

## Step 2: The Move-Out Inspection
*   **Comparison:** Bring a copy of the original Move-In Inspection checklist and photos for direct comparison.
*   **Property Walkthrough:**
    *   **Walls/Floors:** Inspect for damage beyond "normal wear and tear" (e.g., unauthorized paint, large holes, carpet stains).
    *   **Fixtures:** Check all light bulbs, door handles, cabinet hardware, and window locks.
    *   **Appliances:** Verify all appliances are operational and clean, including the interior of the oven and refrigerator.
    *   **Plumbing:** Test faucets, toilets, and drains to ensure no leaks or blockages.
*   **Documentation:** Take timestamped, high-resolution photos and videos of the entire property, specifically documenting any damages.

## Step 3: Offboarding and Security
*   **Key Collection:** Retrieve all sets of keys, garage remotes, gate clickers, and mail keys.
*   **Utility Transition:** Verify that the tenant has scheduled utility shut-offs or transfers to the landlord’s name effective the day after move-out.
*   **Security Assessment:** Immediately change or re-key the locks to ensure the property is secure before beginning turnover work.

## Step 4: Final Settlement
*   **Cleaning/Repairs:** Hire professional cleaners and contractors immediately to minimize downtime. 
*   **Invoice Management:** Collect and file all receipts for cleaning, repairs, or unpaid rent to be deducted from the security deposit.
*   **Security Deposit Accounting:** Draft a formal Security Deposit Disposition statement, outlining all charges, and mail it with the remaining balance to the forwarding address within the time frame mandated by your local state laws.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: The Pre-Move-Out Walkthrough:** Offer a "pre-inspection" 7–10 days before the lease ends. This gives tenants the chance to fix minor issues themselves, which often saves you labor costs and reduces disputes over damages.
*   **Pitfall: Normal Wear and Tear:** Do not attempt to charge tenants for reasonable aging of the property, such as minor scuffs on walls or carpet matting. Charging for these items is often considered illegal and can lead to small claims litigation.
*   **Pro Tip: Date-Stamping:** Use apps like Timestamp Camera to ensure your move-out photos are irrefutable evidence in case of a legal dispute.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Statutory Deadlines:** Failing to send the security deposit statement within the state-mandated timeframe (e.g., 21 or 30 days) can result in the landlord losing the right to withhold any portion of the deposit, regardless of property damage.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Can I charge the tenant for professional carpet cleaning if it wasn't in the lease?**
A: Generally, you can only charge for professional cleaning if the carpet is left in a condition worse than "normal wear and tear." Always check your local statutes regarding "non-refundable" cleaning fees, as they are prohibited in some jurisdictions.

**Q: What if the tenant leaves behind personal property?**
A: Do not discard personal belongings immediately. Follow your specific state "abandoned property" laws, which usually require formal notification and a waiting period before the items can be disposed of or sold.

**Q: Am I required to let the tenant attend the final inspection?**
A: In many states, you are required to provide the tenant with the opportunity to be present for the final inspection. If they decline, ensure you document that you provided the option in writing.

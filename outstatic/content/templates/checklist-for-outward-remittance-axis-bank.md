# Standard Operating Procedure: Outward Remittance via Axis Bank

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for processing outward remittances through Axis Bank. Designed for operational efficiency and regulatory compliance (FEMA guidelines), this document ensures that all international fund transfers are executed accurately, minimizing the risk of rejections, delays, or AML (Anti-Money Laundering) flaggings. Adherence to these steps is required for all finance personnel managing cross-border transactions.

## Phase 1: Pre-Transaction Verification & Compliance
*   **Beneficiary Validation:** Confirm the beneficiary name, bank name, and physical address are exact matches to the invoice/proforma.
*   **SWIFT/BIC Code Check:** Validate the SWIFT code using the official Axis Bank portal or a verified database to ensure the correct intermediary bank routing.
*   **Purpose Code Selection:** Select the correct RBI Purpose Code (e.g., S0001 for advance payment against imports, S0301 for professional services). Mismatched codes lead to immediate rejection by the Reserve Bank of India.
*   **Limit Check:** Verify the transaction amount against the annual Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) limits or corporate outward remittance authorizations.

## Phase 2: Documentation Preparation
*   **A2 Form:** Ensure the A2 Form is filled digitally or manually, signed by an authorized signatory with a stamp.
*   **Invoice/Contract:** Obtain a copy of the proforma invoice or commercial contract justifying the payment.
*   **Tax Compliance (Form 15CA/15CB):**
    *   Generate Form 15CA via the Income Tax portal.
    *   Secure Form 15CB from a Chartered Accountant if the payment is subject to Tax Deducted at Source (TDS).
*   **Letter of Indemnity:** Prepare the letter on company letterhead if required by the bank for specific high-value transactions.

## Phase 3: Axis Bank Portal Execution
*   **Initiation:** Log into the Axis Bank Corporate/Retail portal and select 'Outward Remittance'.
*   **Data Entry:** Input beneficiary details exactly as registered. Cross-reference account numbers to prevent digits transposition.
*   **Charges Selection:** Choose the appropriate charge structure:
    *   **OUR:** Remitter bears all bank charges.
    *   **SHA:** Remitter and beneficiary share charges.
    *   **BEN:** Beneficiary bears all charges.
*   **Verification:** Review the draft transaction screen. Verify the exchange rate and the total debit amount before submitting.

## Phase 4: Post-Remittance Reconciliation
*   **Transaction Reference:** Note the Transaction Reference Number (TRN) and the SWIFT MT103 copy once generated.
*   **Accounting Entry:** Record the transaction in the internal ERP, ensuring the exchange rate gain/loss is accounted for.
*   **FIRC Request:** Request the Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate (FIRC) or remittance advice to share with the beneficiary as proof of payment.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always double-check the 'IBAN' for European countries. Many transactions are stalled because the IBAN is omitted or incorrect.
*   **Pro Tip:** Schedule remittances at least 48 hours before the actual deadline to account for potential intermediary bank inquiries.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid "Round Numbers." Payments of exactly $10,000 or $50,000 often trigger additional AML scrutiny. Add small cents to make the amount non-standard.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not use abbreviations in the beneficiary name field; use the full legal name to avoid mismatch errors during the bank’s screening process.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Why was my remittance rejected even though the details were correct?**
Most rejections stem from an incorrect RBI Purpose Code or a mismatch between the beneficiary name on the invoice and the name registered in the bank’s beneficiary master. Ensure these fields are perfectly aligned.

**2. How long does it usually take for the funds to reflect in the beneficiary account?**
Typically, transfers take 2 to 5 working days. This depends on the number of intermediary banks involved and the currency of the transaction.

**3. What should I do if I provided the wrong account number?**
Immediately contact your Axis Bank Relationship Manager and submit a 'Swift Amendment' request. Note that this incurs a service fee and is only possible if the funds have not yet been credited to the beneficiary's account.

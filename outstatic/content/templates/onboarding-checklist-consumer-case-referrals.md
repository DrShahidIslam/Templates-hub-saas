# Standard Operating Procedure: Consumer Case Referral Onboarding

## Introduction
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for onboarding consumer case referrals to ensure high-quality service delivery, regulatory compliance, and seamless client integration. Efficient onboarding is critical to building trust, setting realistic expectations, and gathering the necessary documentation to initiate case resolution. By following this structured approach, Operations Managers and Case Coordinators ensure that no essential data is omitted and that every referral is transitioned from "Lead" to "Active Case" with maximum operational efficiency.

## Section 1: Initial Intake & Validation
*   **Verify Referral Source:** Confirm the origin of the referral (e.g., website, partner agency, direct outreach) and ensure tracking codes are updated in the CRM.
*   **Conflict of Interest Check:** Perform a mandatory search against the client database to ensure no ethical or legal conflicts exist.
*   **Documentation Audit:** Review initial referral documents (e.g., incident report, correspondence, intake forms) for missing information or illegible data.
*   **Qualification Assessment:** Verify that the consumer meets the eligibility criteria for the specific program or case type.
*   **Initial Outreach:** Contact the consumer within 2 hours of receipt to acknowledge the referral and express a professional tone.

## Section 2: Administrative Setup
*   **Profile Creation:** Establish a unique client record in the Case Management System (CMS).
*   **Document Digitization:** Securely upload all hard-copy referrals into the encrypted document management system.
*   **Data Accuracy Check:** Ensure contact details (phone, email, secondary contact) are verified through a "double-check" protocol.
*   **Assign Case Manager:** Allocate the case to the appropriate team member based on current bandwidth and area of specialization.
*   **Compliance Verification:** Ensure the consumer has signed the Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and any necessary HIPAA/legal releases.

## Section 3: Client Engagement & Expectations
*   **Welcome Communication:** Send the official Welcome Packet (email) containing the Case Manager’s contact info, office hours, and the case lifecycle roadmap.
*   **Needs Assessment Interview:** Conduct a deep-dive interview to identify primary pain points, goals, and desired outcomes.
*   **Establish Communication Cadence:** Mutually agree on how and when the client will receive status updates (e.g., weekly phone call, bi-weekly email).
*   **Setting Milestones:** Define the first three actionable milestones and communicate the anticipated timeline to the client to manage expectations.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use an automated email template for the "Welcome Packet" to ensure consistency, but always add a personalized introductory sentence to build rapport.
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a 24-hour "cooling off" check-in if the case involves sensitive or emotionally charged matters; sometimes clients need extra reassurance post-intake.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-promising timelines. Always provide a conservative estimate for case progress to avoid future dissatisfaction.
*   **Pitfall:** Failing to verify the identity of the referral source. Ensure all referrals come through verified channels to prevent fraud or data breaches.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What should I do if the initial referral information is incomplete?**
A: Do not proceed to the Administrative Setup. Flag the case as "Pending Information" and send an immediate request for the missing documentation to the referral source or the client.

**Q: How do we handle clients who are non-responsive during onboarding?**
A: Follow the "Three-Attempt Rule": attempt contact via phone, email, and SMS over a period of 5 business days. If no response is received, mark the case as "Inactive/Pending" and notify your supervisor.

**Q: Can we customize the onboarding process for high-priority referrals?**
A: Yes. High-priority cases may bypass standard queue times; however, the core compliance and documentation steps (Section 1 and 2) must never be skipped for any client.

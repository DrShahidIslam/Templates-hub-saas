# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Routine for Primary School Operations

This document outlines the standardized daily operational routine for primary school staff, designed to ensure a consistent, safe, and high-quality educational environment. By adhering to these procedures, faculty and administration can maximize instructional time, maintain student welfare, and ensure a seamless flow of daily activities. Adherence to this SOP is mandatory for all staff to maintain institutional excellence and safety compliance.

## Morning Arrival and Soft Start
*   **Facility Readiness:** Ensure all classrooms are unlocked, ventilation systems are active, and necessary supplies are pre-distributed by 07:45.
*   **Staff Presence:** Duty staff must be at their assigned posts (gates, hallways, playground) by 08:00.
*   **Greeting Protocols:** Staff should greet students at the classroom door to establish emotional regulation and check for signs of illness or distress.
*   **Soft Start Activity:** Provide low-stakes, independent work (e.g., morning journals or math warm-ups) to facilitate a calm transition as students arrive.

## Instructional Blocks and Transitions
*   **Morning Meeting:** Conduct a 15-minute community-building session to set daily objectives and review expectations.
*   **Transition Management:** Use auditory signals (chimes or bells) to indicate transitions. Maintain a "quiet transit" policy in corridors to minimize disruption to other classes.
*   **Active Supervision:** During instruction, teachers must maintain a roaming presence to monitor student engagement and provide 1:1 support.
*   **Break/Recess Coordination:** Ensure playground duty staff are positioned for maximum visibility. Verify that First Aid kits are accessible at all times.

## Nutrition and Hygiene Protocols
*   **Pre-Lunch Handwashing:** Enforce a strict handwashing or sanitization protocol before all meals.
*   **Monitoring:** Staff must supervise the lunchroom to ensure dietary requirements are met and to monitor for potential allergic reactions.
*   **Cleanup:** Ensure students clear their own trays, promoting personal responsibility and facility cleanliness.

## Dismissal Procedures
*   **Departure Prep:** Initiate pack-up 10 minutes prior to dismissal. Ensure all students have necessary homework or notices.
*   **Identification:** Staff must verify the identity of anyone collecting a student against the authorized pick-up list.
*   **Handover:** Teachers must maintain visual contact until the student is successfully handed over to a guardian or directed to the bus/after-school club queue.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

**Pro Tips:**
*   **Visual Schedules:** Keep a physical, high-visibility schedule on the whiteboard to reduce anxiety and increase student autonomy.
*   **The "Wait Time" Rule:** After giving an instruction, wait 5–7 seconds before expecting movement to ensure all students have processed the directive.
*   **Positive Reinforcement:** Keep a "caught being good" ledger to acknowledge positive behavior shifts immediately.

**Pitfalls:**
*   **Inconsistent Enforcement:** The biggest operational failure is inconsistent rule enforcement. If a rule is set, it must be applied to every student, every day.
*   **Ignoring Transitions:** Failing to plan for transition times leads to "unstructured chaos." Always define the entry and exit expectation for every activity.
*   **Lack of Communication:** Neglecting to update parents on daily shift changes or event logistics creates unnecessary friction at dismissal.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What is the protocol for a student arriving late?**
Students arriving after the designated start time must report to the front office for a "late pass" before entering the classroom to ensure accurate attendance records and minimize disruption to the ongoing lesson.

**2. How should teachers handle behavioral escalations during group work?**
Teachers should utilize a pre-defined "cool-down" zone within the classroom. If the student does not self-regulate within five minutes, follow the tiered behavioral support protocol by contacting the office or school counselor.

**3. Are staff allowed to deviate from the daily schedule?**
Minor timing adjustments for instructional needs are permitted; however, major changes to start, lunch, or dismissal times must be cleared by the administrative office 24 hours in advance to ensure safety coverage is adjusted accordingly.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Gold Loan Audit Protocol

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive framework for conducting internal audits of gold loan portfolios. The objective is to ensure institutional compliance, mitigate risk associated with asset valuation, verify the physical security of collateral, and maintain regulatory adherence. By following this systematic approach, auditors can ensure that all gold loan disbursements are backed by legitimate collateral, appraised accurately, and stored with maximum security.

## 1. Documentation and KYC Verification
*   **Customer Identification:** Verify that KYC documents (PAN, Aadhaar, Proof of Address) are active, legible, and matched against original documents.
*   **Application Integrity:** Ensure the loan application form is signed and dated by the borrower and the branch official.
*   **KYC Compliance:** Confirm the loan amount complies with the entity’s internal KYC-based tier limits and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) policy.
*   **Loan Agreement:** Verify that the "Terms and Conditions" document is stamped as per local state laws and signed by the borrower.

## 2. Appraisal and Valuation Audit
*   **Gold Purity Check:** Cross-verify the purity certificate (caratage) against the system-entered data.
*   **Net Weight Validation:** Ensure the net weight (excluding stones/wax/dirt) is accurately recorded in the appraisal sheet.
*   **LTV Ratio Compliance:** Confirm the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio is within the regulatory ceiling (e.g., current RBI guidelines or internal risk appetite).
*   **Market Price Mapping:** Verify that the valuation is based on the approved daily gold rate provided by the head office, not an arbitrary branch-level rate.

## 3. Physical Inventory and Security Audit
*   **Vault Security:** Inspect the dual-custody register; confirm that the vault keys are held by two authorized officials.
*   **Packet Verification:** Select a random sample (e.g., 5-10% of total packets) and verify that the weight and description on the external packet label match the system logs.
*   **Sealing Integrity:** Check that every gold packet is heat-sealed, intact, and bears the borrower’s signature/thumbprint across the seal.
*   **Fire/Theft Insurance:** Verify that the insurance policy for the vault is active, covers the current vault value, and that premiums are paid up to date.

## 4. Operational and Disbursement Audit
*   **Disbursement Mode:** Ensure funds were transferred via bank transfer/cheque and not in cash (where prohibited by law).
*   **Authorization Levels:** Confirm that the appraisal and the loan disbursement were authorized by two different personnel (Maker-Checker principle).
*   **Interest Accrual:** Verify that interest calculation logic is consistent with the product scheme chosen by the customer.
*   **Asset Classification:** Ensure that any loan overdue by >90 days is correctly classified as a Non-Performing Asset (NPA) in the system.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (The Blind Spot Check):** Always perform a "surprise audit" of the gold packets. If the branch staff knows you are coming, they can manipulate logs.
*   **Pro Tip (Stones/Embedded Items):** Be wary of "imitation gold" or heavy stones. Ensure the appraiser consistently deducts the weight of non-gold elements.
*   **Pitfall (The 'Old Loan' Trap):** Do not assume existing loans are compliant. Auditors often focus only on new disbursements, missing long-standing issues like expired insurance or invalid documentation.
*   **Pitfall (Neglecting Custodian Logs):** Failing to check the movement register (the log of when the vault is opened) is a major security loophole that invites internal fraud.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the most common reason for audit failure in gold loans?**
A: Inaccurate appraisal (overvaluation) and failure to adhere to the LTV ratio are the most frequent causes of audit failure.

**Q: How often should a physical vault audit be conducted?**
A: A 100% physical verification of all gold packets should be conducted at least once annually, while "random sampling" audits should be performed monthly by regional managers.

**Q: What should I do if I find a discrepancy in the weight of a gold packet?**
A: Immediately freeze the loan account in the system, escalate the matter to the Compliance and Security Departments, and secure the vault until a secondary investigation is completed.

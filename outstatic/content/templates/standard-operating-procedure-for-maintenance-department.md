# Standard Operating Procedure: Maintenance Department Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the foundational protocols for the Maintenance Department to ensure the operational integrity, safety, and longevity of facility assets. By standardizing maintenance workflows—ranging from routine preventative inspections to urgent emergency repairs—this document provides a roadmap for minimizing equipment downtime, reducing overhead costs, and ensuring full compliance with occupational health and safety regulations. All personnel are required to adhere to these procedures to maintain a culture of accountability and professional excellence.

## 1. Preventive Maintenance (PM) Scheduling
*   **Asset Categorization:** Update the asset registry to categorize equipment by criticality (High/Medium/Low priority).
*   **Calendar Generation:** Use the Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) to auto-generate PM work orders based on manufacturer specifications or run-hour intervals.
*   **Resource Allocation:** Verify that parts, specialized tools, and personnel hours are available prior to the scheduled PM date.
*   **Execution:** Technicians perform inspections, lubrication, and testing according to the specific PM checklist for each asset.
*   **Documentation:** Log all findings, part replacements, and technician hours into the CMMS immediately upon completion.

## 2. Emergency Repair Workflow
*   **Triage:** Upon receiving an emergency request, the Maintenance Manager or Lead Tech must assess the impact on safety and production.
*   **Isolation:** Implement Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures immediately if the equipment poses a safety hazard.
*   **Response:** Deploy the on-call technician with the necessary diagnostic equipment and critical spare parts.
*   **Root Cause Analysis (RCA):** Post-repair, document the cause of failure to determine if the emergency could have been prevented through improved PM scheduling.
*   **Reporting:** Notify department heads of downtime duration and the final operational status of the equipment.

## 3. Inventory and Parts Management
*   **Cycle Counting:** Perform weekly cycle counts on high-turnover consumables to prevent stockouts.
*   **Reorder Points:** Maintain automated alerts in the inventory management system when stock hits the "minimum threshold."
*   **Asset Tagging:** Ensure every spare part is tagged with a unique SKU and shelf location code for easy retrieval.
*   **Vendor Management:** Review vendor performance quarterly based on lead times, quality of parts, and cost-competitiveness.

## 4. Safety and Compliance (HSE)
*   **LOTO Protocol:** Enforce mandatory LOTO procedures for all repairs involving energy sources (electrical, mechanical, pneumatic).
*   **PPE Verification:** Require all technicians to wear the appropriate PPE (e.g., safety glasses, steel-toed boots, high-vis vests) before entering the floor.
*   **Waste Disposal:** Follow strict environmental protocols for the disposal of hazardous waste, such as oils, chemicals, and batteries.
*   **Safety Audit:** Conduct monthly facility walk-throughs to identify and rectify physical safety hazards (e.g., frayed cables, blocked fire exits).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Predictive Maintenance:** Transition from "Time-Based" to "Condition-Based" maintenance using vibration analysis and infrared thermography to catch issues before they manifest as breakdowns.
*   **Pro Tip: Technician Feedback Loop:** Hold a brief 10-minute "huddle" each morning to review the previous day’s challenges and upcoming priority tasks.
*   **Pitfall: Paper Records:** Relying on paper logs is a major pitfall. It leads to data loss and lack of trend analysis. Always digitize your maintenance history.
*   **Pitfall: Over-Maintenance:** Performing maintenance too frequently can sometimes lead to infant mortality failures in parts. Stick strictly to manufacturer-recommended intervals.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I do if a part is not in stock during an emergency repair?**
A: Consult the "Approved Vendor List" for local suppliers, prioritize the repair via a temporary workaround if safe, and update the inventory management system to adjust reorder points to prevent recurrence.

**Q: How do we determine if a repair should be outsourced?**
A: If the repair requires specialized certification (e.g., high-voltage electrical, specialized HVAC, or elevator maintenance) or if the internal time-to-repair exceeds the cost of a service contract, outsource the task.

**Q: What is the priority for a non-urgent maintenance request?**
A: Non-urgent requests are to be entered into the CMMS queue, reviewed during the weekly production planning meeting, and scheduled in accordance with current production cycles to minimize impact.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Laboratory Centrifuge Operations

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) provides mandatory guidelines for the safe and efficient operation of laboratory centrifuges. Proper usage is critical to prevent mechanical failure, ensure sample integrity, and protect personnel from exposure to hazardous materials. All operators must be trained on the specific model in use before attempting to operate the equipment. Adherence to these protocols is essential for maintaining equipment longevity and workplace safety.

## 1. Pre-Operation Inspection and Preparation
*   **Inspect the Rotor:** Ensure the rotor is clean, free of cracks, and securely seated on the drive spindle.
*   **Check Chamber Conditions:** Verify the centrifuge chamber is dry and free of debris or broken glass from previous runs.
*   **Inspect Tubes:** Check all centrifuge tubes for stress cracks, chips, or deformations. Do not use damaged tubes.
*   **Verify Compatibility:** Confirm that the tubes are rated for the intended speed (RPM/RCF) and chemical compatibility.
*   **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Don appropriate PPE, including lab coat, gloves, and safety glasses.

## 2. Sample Loading and Balancing
*   **Determine Weight:** Use a laboratory balance to ensure opposing tubes are equal in mass. 
*   **Achieve Balance:** Balance samples within 0.1 grams or according to the manufacturer’s specific tolerance.
*   **Symmetrical Loading:** Place tubes in the rotor in a symmetrical pattern. If using an odd number of samples, use a water-filled "blank" tube of identical weight and type to achieve balance.
*   **Secure Lids:** Ensure all tube caps/lids are snapped shut or screwed on tightly to prevent aerosolization or leakage.
*   **Rotor Locking:** Ensure the rotor lid is properly seated and locked (if applicable) before closing the main centrifuge door.

## 3. Centrifuge Operation
*   **Parameter Setup:** Input the desired speed (RPM or RCF), time, and temperature (if refrigerated) on the digital interface.
*   **Close and Lock:** Ensure the centrifuge door is fully latched. Many modern units will not start unless the door sensor is fully engaged.
*   **Initial Observation:** Start the cycle and monitor the machine during the acceleration phase. If unusual noise, vibration, or wobbling occurs, press the "STOP" button immediately.
*   **Monitoring:** Stay within the immediate vicinity until the rotor reaches the set speed to ensure the system is stable.

## 4. Post-Operation and Cleanup
*   **Wait for Stop:** Never attempt to open the door until the rotor has come to a complete stop and the "door lock" indicator light has deactivated.
*   **Aerosol Check:** If the samples contain hazardous biological agents, allow the centrifuge to sit for 10 minutes post-run to allow aerosols to settle before opening.
*   **Unloading:** Remove samples carefully. Inspect the rotor for any signs of spillage.
*   **Sanitization:** Wipe down the rotor and the interior chamber with 70% ethanol or the manufacturer-recommended disinfectant.
*   **Log Entry:** Record the usage in the equipment logbook, noting the time, speed, and any irregularities observed.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always use the RCF (Relative Centrifugal Force) setting rather than RPM whenever possible, as RCF is independent of the rotor size and provides more reproducible results.
*   **Pro Tip:** Maintain a "Balance Log" near the centrifuge to help users quickly find matching tube sets.
*   **Pitfall (The "Imbalance" Error):** The most common cause of centrifuge failure is poor balancing. Never "eyeball" the volume; always use a scale.
*   **Pitfall (Corrosion):** Leaving salt-based buffers or corrosive chemicals in the rotor chamber will cause pitting and mechanical fatigue, eventually leading to a catastrophic rotor failure. Always wipe dry after every use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if the centrifuge begins to vibrate heavily during the run?**
A: Immediately press the emergency stop button. Do not attempt to open the lid until the rotor has completely stopped spinning. Once stopped, check for a broken tube or an improperly balanced load.

**Q: Can I use different types of tubes in the same run if they weigh the same?**
A: Generally, no. Different materials (e.g., glass vs. plastic) or different shapes (e.g., conical vs. round-bottom) may have different stress points. Only use identical tubes for a balanced load.

**Q: How often should the centrifuge be professionally calibrated?**
A: Centrifuges should be professionally serviced and calibrated at least once every 12 months, or sooner if the unit is used in a high-throughput environment.

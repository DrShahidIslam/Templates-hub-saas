# Standard Operating Procedure: Uber Vehicle Inspection Documentation

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory protocols for performing and documenting a vehicle inspection using the official Uber-compliant PDF inspection form. Adherence to these procedures ensures that the vehicle meets local regulatory safety standards, maintains platform eligibility, and minimizes the risk of account deactivation due to non-compliance. This document is intended for drivers and fleet managers to standardize the inspection process and ensure accuracy in reporting.

## Section 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Verification of Documentation:** Ensure you have the current version of the Uber-approved inspection form downloaded and printed.
*   **Environmental Requirements:** Conduct the inspection in a well-lit area, preferably during daylight hours, to ensure all body panels and glass are clearly visible.
*   **Safety Protocol:** Engage the parking brake and ensure the engine is off before beginning the exterior walkthrough.
*   **Tool Preparation:** Have a flashlight, a tire tread depth gauge, and a camera-ready smartphone for supplemental evidence collection.

## Section 2: Exterior Vehicle Assessment
*   **Body Integrity:** Inspect for significant dents, deep scratches, or body panel misalignment that may affect the vehicle's structural integrity or professional appearance.
*   **Glass and Visibility:** Check the windshield for cracks or stone chips larger than a quarter. Ensure all side mirrors are securely mounted and offer a full, unobstructed range of motion.
*   **Lighting Systems:** Verify operation of low beams, high beams, turn signals (front/rear), brake lights, and hazard lights.
*   **Tires and Wheels:** Measure tread depth on all four tires (must be at least 4/32” or as per local Uber requirements). Check for uneven wear or sidewall damage.
*   **Doors and Latches:** Confirm that all four passenger doors open and close securely from both the interior and exterior handles.

## Section 3: Interior and Functional Assessment
*   **Safety Restraints:** Pull each seatbelt firmly to ensure the locking mechanism engages properly. Inspect webbing for fraying or cuts.
*   **HVAC Systems:** Test both air conditioning and heating functions. Ensure air vents are unobstructed and directional controls are functional.
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Verify that no warning lights (Check Engine, ABS, TPMS, Airbag) remain illuminated while the engine is running.
*   **Upholstery:** Inspect seats and floor mats for excessive staining, tears, or foul odors.
*   **Audio/Horn:** Sound the horn to ensure it is audible and functional.

## Section 4: Documentation and Final Submission
*   **Mechanic Certification:** If required by your region, ensure the form is signed and stamped by a licensed mechanic.
*   **Form Accuracy:** Double-check that the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN), license plate number, and odometer reading match the vehicle exactly.
*   **Digital Capture:** Scan or photograph the completed form clearly, ensuring all text is legible before uploading to the Uber Partner portal.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Take high-quality photos of the vehicle from all four angles simultaneously with the inspection; these serve as a secondary record if the document is rejected due to clerical errors.
*   **Pro Tip:** Always carry a digital copy of your signed inspection form on your phone, as local authorities or airport officials may request proof of inspection during operations.
*   **Pitfall:** Using an expired or generic inspection form template. Always download the most recent version directly from your Uber Partner Dashboard to avoid immediate rejection.
*   **Pitfall:** Neglecting to check the spare tire or the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS); these are common failure points in mandatory inspections.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How often do I need to complete this inspection?**
Inspection requirements vary by city and vehicle age. Generally, most jurisdictions require an annual inspection, while some regions may require it more frequently based on mileage. Check your local dashboard for specific renewal dates.

**2. Can I perform the inspection myself?**
In most markets, Uber requires the inspection to be performed by a certified mechanic or at an approved inspection center. Self-inspections are rarely accepted unless specifically permitted by local law.

**3. What should I do if my inspection is rejected?**
Review the rejection notice in the Uber app carefully; it usually highlights the specific field or component that was flagged. Correct the issue (e.g., replace a burnt-out bulb or get the mechanic to fix a clerical error on the form), then resubmit the updated PDF immediately.

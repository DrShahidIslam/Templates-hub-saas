# Standard Operating Procedure: Residential Preventive Maintenance

As an operations manager, I define preventive maintenance as the proactive management of home assets to prevent degradation, costly emergency repairs, and safety hazards. By following this structured SOP, homeowners can extend the lifespan of their building systems, improve energy efficiency, and protect their property value. This document serves as a master checklist for seasonal and recurring tasks necessary to maintain a high-functioning home environment.

## 1. Exterior & Structural Integrity
*   **Roof Inspection:** Inspect shingles for curling, cracking, or missing components. Check flashing around chimneys and vents for signs of oxidation or gaps.
*   **Gutter Maintenance:** Clear debris from gutters and downspouts. Ensure downspouts direct water at least six feet away from the foundation.
*   **Foundation Assessment:** Walk the perimeter to check for new cracks, settling, or pooling water near the structure.
*   **Siding and Trim:** Clean surfaces and inspect for rot, peeling paint, or gaps where pests may enter. Repair or caulk any breaches.
*   **Driveway and Walkways:** Inspect for cracks and reseal as necessary to prevent water infiltration and freeze-thaw cycle damage.

## 2. HVAC & Energy Efficiency
*   **Air Filters:** Replace HVAC furnace filters every 60–90 days, or monthly if pets are present.
*   **Condenser Units:** Clear debris, leaves, and vegetation away from the outdoor AC unit to allow proper airflow.
*   **Ductwork:** Inspect registers for dust buildup and schedule professional duct cleaning every 3–5 years.
*   **Thermostat:** Test for responsiveness and ensure programmed schedules are optimized for seasonal changes.
*   **Ventilation:** Confirm that attic and bathroom exhaust fans are venting externally and not into the attic space.

## 3. Plumbing & Water Systems
*   **Water Heater:** Flush the tank annually to remove sediment buildup. Inspect the temperature/pressure relief valve for leaks.
*   **Leak Detection:** Check under all sinks, toilets, and near the washing machine for signs of moisture or corrosion.
*   **Sump Pump:** Test the pump by pouring a bucket of water into the pit to ensure the float switch triggers the motor.
*   **Shut-off Valves:** Exercise main water shut-off valves and fixture valves to ensure they do not seize over time.
*   **Drain Maintenance:** Clear hair from shower traps and treat drains with a mild enzyme cleaner to prevent slow buildup.

## 4. Safety & Interior Systems
*   **Life Safety Devices:** Test smoke and carbon monoxide detectors monthly. Replace batteries annually, or as indicated by the device.
*   **Fire Extinguishers:** Check gauges to ensure pressure is in the "green" zone; ensure the pin is intact and the unit is accessible.
*   **Appliance Maintenance:** Clean the refrigerator condenser coils, check the clothes dryer vent for lint accumulation, and inspect dishwasher gaskets.
*   **Electrical Panels:** Ensure breakers are clearly labeled and that there is at least 36 inches of clearance in front of the panel.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip (Documentation):** Create a digital "Property Maintenance Log." Upload photos of serial numbers, receipts for work performed, and dates of service. This is invaluable for resale and insurance claims.
*   **Pro Tip (Seasonal Alignment):** Tie your maintenance schedule to major holidays or seasonal time changes (Spring/Fall) to make the tasks routine.
*   **Pitfall (Neglecting the Dryer Vent):** A clogged dryer vent is a leading cause of residential fires. Do not rely solely on the lint trap; inspect the exterior exhaust port every six months.
*   **Pitfall (Over-Tightening):** When performing minor repairs (like plumbing connections), avoid over-tightening, as this can strip threads or crack seals, creating a new leak.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I have a professional perform a full home inspection?**
A: It is recommended to have a professional HVAC tune-up twice a year (before cooling and heating seasons) and a general home inspection by a licensed professional every 3–5 years to identify "silent" issues.

**Q: What is the most ignored maintenance task that causes the most damage?**
A: Managing water drainage. Improperly directed downspouts or clogged gutters leading to water pooling against the foundation are the primary causes of basement flooding and structural foundation damage.

**Q: Should I perform electrical or gas-related maintenance myself?**
A: No. Unless you are licensed or highly trained, stick to visual inspections. Any task involving the removal of panels, handling of live wires, or manipulation of gas lines should be handled exclusively by a certified professional.

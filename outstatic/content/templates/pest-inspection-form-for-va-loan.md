# Standard Operating Procedure: VA Loan Pest Inspection Compliance

## Introduction
The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) maintains stringent property condition requirements to ensure the health, safety, and structural integrity of a home before finalizing a loan. A critical component of this process is the Wood Destroying Insect (WDI) Inspection. This SOP provides a comprehensive framework for operations managers to ensure that pest inspections for VA loans are conducted, documented, and reported in strict accordance with VA Lenders Handbook (VA Pamphlet 26-7) guidelines, preventing unnecessary delays in the underwriting process.

## Step-by-Step Inspection Checklist

## Section 1: Pre-Inspection Preparation
*   **Verify Regional Requirements:** Confirm if the property is located in a state or county where a WDI inspection is mandated by the local VA Regional Loan Center (RLC).
*   **Contractor Vetting:** Ensure the inspection company is licensed, bonded, and carries professional liability insurance in the state where the property is located.
*   **Scheduling Coordination:** Contact the listing agent or property manager to ensure all areas—including crawl spaces, attics, and basements—are accessible.
*   **Documentation Check:** Confirm the inspector is prepared to use the standardized NPMA-33 (National Pest Management Association) form, which is the industry standard accepted by the VA.

## Section 2: On-Site Inspection Execution
*   **Visual Assessment:** Inspect the foundation, sills, joists, sub-flooring, and wooden structural components for visible evidence of infestation or damage.
*   **Evidence Collection:** Document any presence of termites, powderpost beetles, carpenter ants, or other wood-destroying organisms.
*   **Moisture Evaluation:** Identify signs of excessive moisture, standing water in crawl spaces, or wood-to-ground contact, as these are primary attractants for pests.
*   **Clearance Determination:** Confirm whether the property is currently infested or if there is visible evidence of prior treatment/damage that has not been repaired.

## Section 3: Post-Inspection Reporting and Submission
*   **Form Accuracy:** Review the NPMA-33 for completeness, ensuring all boxes are checked and the inspector’s license information is legible.
*   **Addendum Review:** Ensure any required treatment warranties or repair certifications are attached if active infestation is discovered.
*   **Underwriting Delivery:** Upload the final report to the loan file within 24 hours of receipt to avoid expiration.
*   **Certification Validity:** Confirm the report is dated within 90 days of the anticipated closing date; reports older than this will require re-inspection.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: The "Wood-to-Ground" Rule:** Inspectors often flag wood siding or trim touching the soil. Advise your sellers to trim back landscaping and install flashing prior to the inspection to avoid "subject to" repair conditions.
*   **Pitfall: Incomplete Access:** If the inspector cannot access the crawl space due to debris or locked hatches, the VA will flag the file as "Incomplete." This creates a bottleneck that requires a re-inspection fee and additional days of processing.
*   **Pro Tip: Verification of Local Amendments:** Some states have specific "State-Approved" WDI forms that override the standard NPMA-33. Always check the specific requirements for the state of the property to prevent document rejection.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Subterranean Pathways:** Don’t just look for bugs; look for the "mud tubes" that termites use to travel. Even if no bugs are found, evidence of past activity often necessitates a clearance letter.

## FAQ

**Q: Is a pest inspection mandatory for every VA loan?**
A: Not necessarily. While the VA strongly recommends it, mandatory inspections are dictated by the specific VA Regional Loan Center (RLC) covering the property’s jurisdiction. However, it is standard industry practice to obtain one to satisfy lender overlays and minimize risk.

**Q: Who is responsible for paying for the WDI inspection?**
A: Under VA rules, the veteran (buyer) is generally prohibited from paying for a "pest inspection" if it is mandated by the VA or the lender. Typically, the seller pays this fee; however, if the buyer requests a home inspection that happens to include pest detection, the buyer may pay for the broader inspection.

**Q: What happens if the inspection reveals an active infestation?**
A: The loan cannot proceed until a licensed professional treats the property and provides a guarantee/warranty that the infestation is eradicated. Once treated, a re-inspection is required to confirm the work was performed to industry standards.

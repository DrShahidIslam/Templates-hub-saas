# Standard Operating Procedure: Basant Festival Execution

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the operational requirements, safety protocols, and logistical coordination necessary for the successful execution of Basant (the Spring Festival). Given the inherent risks associated with high-altitude kite flying and the density of participants, this document serves as the primary mandate for site management, supply chain coordination, and public safety enforcement to ensure a compliant and enjoyable event.

## 1. Pre-Event Planning & Procurement
*   [ ] **Vendor Selection:** Vet kite manufacturers to ensure compliance with non-metallic, non-conductive string materials (ban on glass-coated ‘manjha’).
*   [ ] **Permit Acquisition:** Obtain local administrative approvals, fire department clearances, and municipal noise permits.
*   [ ] **Site Assessment:** Survey rooftops, terraces, and open grounds for structural integrity and proximity to high-voltage power lines.
*   [ ] **Inventory Audit:** Ensure all logistical supplies (kites, spools, refreshments, safety gear) are stocked at least 72 hours prior to the event.

## 2. Infrastructure & Safety Setup
*   [ ] **Clearance Zones:** Designate "No-Fly Zones" within 50 meters of electrical grids and public thoroughfares.
*   [ ] **Barricading:** Install temporary fencing around rooftops and high-elevation perimeters to prevent falls.
*   [ ] **Emergency Stations:** Establish an on-site first-aid tent equipped with basic trauma kits and fire extinguishers.
*   [ ] **Access Control:** Assign security personnel to verify attendee lists and restrict access to non-load-bearing areas.

## 3. Operational Execution (Day-Of)
*   [ ] **Safety Briefing:** Conduct a mandatory pre-flight briefing for all participants regarding string safety and wind conditions.
*   [ ] **Crowd Monitoring:** Maintain active surveillance on rooftops to ensure that the density of attendees does not exceed the weight-bearing capacity of the structures.
*   [ ] **Weather Tracking:** Monitor wind velocity and weather patterns; suspend operations immediately if storm cells or dangerous gusts are detected.
*   [ ] **Waste Management:** Deploy mobile sanitation teams to ensure that shredded kite paper and debris do not obstruct pedestrian paths.

## 4. Post-Event Demobilization
*   [ ] **Site Sweep:** Conduct a thorough sweep of the premises to remove all discarded string (a critical safety hazard for wildlife and public transport).
*   [ ] **Structural Inspection:** Re-evaluate rooftop integrity following the event to ensure no damage occurred.
*   [ ] **Debriefing:** Hold a closing meeting with stakeholders to document any safety incidents and inventory remaining assets.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use brightly colored kites to improve visibility for emergency responders or ground-level personnel in the event of a tangle or emergency.
*   **Pro Tip:** Implement a hydration schedule; Basant often involves prolonged exposure to sun and physical exertion.
*   **Pitfall:** Never ignore the "metallic string" prohibition. Using metallic or glass-coated thread is a severe legal liability and a direct threat to the regional power grid.
*   **Pitfall:** Over-reliance on verbal instructions. Always post physical safety signs (e.g., "Do not approach ledge") in high-traffic areas.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What is the most important safety rule regarding kite strings?**
The use of metallic or glass-coated (manjha) strings is strictly forbidden. Only cotton or synthetic non-conductive strings are permitted to prevent electrocution and lacerations.

**2. How should we handle accidental power line entanglement?**
Never attempt to retrieve a kite that has snagged on a power line. Cordon off the area immediately, notify the local utility provider, and ensure no one approaches the grounded string.

**3. What is the protocol if a participant exceeds the rooftop capacity?**
Security must immediately implement a "one-in, one-out" policy, or close off access to the affected roof level until the density is reduced to safe, pre-calculated limits.

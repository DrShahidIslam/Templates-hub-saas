# Standard Operating Procedure: Presiding Officer Duties

The role of the Presiding Officer is pivotal to the integrity, efficiency, and legality of any formal meeting or election process. This SOP outlines the comprehensive responsibilities required to maintain order, ensure adherence to governing bylaws (such as Robert’s Rules of Order), and facilitate equitable participation among all stakeholders. The following checklist serves as the definitive guide for executing these duties with professionalism and impartiality.

## Phase 1: Pre-Meeting Preparation
*   **Agenda Verification:** Confirm the meeting agenda aligns with the organization’s objectives and verify that all necessary supporting documents are distributed.
*   **Quorum Check:** Review the membership list to calculate the exact number required for a legal quorum.
*   **Procedural Review:** Refresh knowledge on the specific governing bylaws or standing rules applicable to the meeting.
*   **Logistics Audit:** Confirm that the venue (physical or virtual) is equipped with necessary recording devices, ballots, microphones, and signage.
*   **Stakeholder Briefing:** Meet briefly with the Secretary and Parliamentarian to ensure roles are clearly defined and contingencies are understood.

## Phase 2: Opening and Call to Order
*   **Formal Call:** Strike the gavel (if applicable) and announce the official start time to the assembly.
*   **Attendance Logging:** Direct the Secretary to record those present and confirm the existence of a quorum.
*   **Approval of Minutes:** Lead the assembly in the review and formal approval of the minutes from the previous meeting.
*   **Agenda Adoption:** Formally propose the current agenda for adoption, allowing for necessary amendments or additions.

## Phase 3: Facilitating Deliberations
*   **Recognition of Speakers:** Maintain a neutral stance; recognize members who have been granted the floor while ensuring equal speaking time.
*   **Maintaining Decorum:** Enforce rules of decorum; immediately address personal attacks, irrelevant digressions, or violations of standing rules.
*   **Motion Management:** Clearly restate motions as they are proposed, call for seconds, open the floor for debate, and facilitate the voting process.
*   **Conflict De-escalation:** Intervene proactively when debates become heated, focusing the assembly back on the motion at hand.

## Phase 4: Closing and Reporting
*   **Action Item Recap:** Briefly summarize the motions passed and assign owners to post-meeting tasks.
*   **Scheduling:** Confirm the date, time, and location of the next meeting.
*   **Formal Adjournment:** Declare the meeting officially closed after a motion to adjourn has been passed or by unilateral decision if the agenda is complete.
*   **Record Certification:** Sign the official minutes and any passed resolutions in conjunction with the Secretary.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Tip: Remain Impartial.** As the Presiding Officer, your role is to facilitate the will of the body, not to push your personal agenda. If you must debate a topic, vacate the chair to a deputy until the motion is resolved.
*   **Tip: Use Clear Language.** Avoid legal jargon where simple, descriptive language suffices. If a motion is confusing, ask the mover to clarify their intent before bringing it to a vote.
*   **Pitfall: The "Runaway Train."** Failure to control the clock can lead to meeting fatigue and poor decision-making. Use a timekeeper and strictly adhere to allotted time slots per agenda item.
*   **Pitfall: Ignoring Bylaws.** Never override the organization’s governing documents to "save time." Procedural shortcuts are the primary cause of overturned decisions and legal challenges.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if a member continually interrupts or causes a disturbance?**
A: Use the "Call to Order." Politely but firmly remind the member that they are violating the rules of decorum. If the behavior persists, you have the authority to request they leave the assembly or, in extreme cases, have them removed by security.

**Q: How do I handle a tie vote?**
A: Consult your specific bylaws first. Generally, a tie vote results in the motion failing. In some organizations, the Presiding Officer may cast a deciding vote, but this should only be done if clearly stipulated in your governing documents.

**Q: What is the most important document to have on hand during the meeting?**
A: Beyond the agenda, you must always have a copy of your governing bylaws and a reference copy of your chosen parliamentary authority (e.g., Robert’s Rules of Order Newly Revised). Having these accessible prevents disputes over procedure.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Employee Onboarding via Google Sheets

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for utilizing a Google Sheets-based onboarding template to ensure a seamless transition for new hires. By centralizing documentation, task tracking, and resource allocation in a collaborative environment, this process minimizes administrative bottlenecks, ensures compliance, and enhances the new hire experience. This template serves as the single source of truth for both the People Operations team and the incoming employee during their first 90 days.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Template Initialization
*   **Duplicate the Master Template:** Open the designated "Onboarding Master Template" in Google Sheets and select 'File' > 'Make a copy'. Rename the file using the convention: `[YYYY-MM-DD]_[NewHireName]_Onboarding`.
*   **Permissions Management:** Share the sheet with the new hire via their personal or corporate email address with 'Editor' permissions.
*   **Customize Role-Specific Tasks:** Tailor the "Tasks" tab to reflect specific project milestones, software access requirements, and departmental training sessions.
*   **Set Conditional Formatting:** Ensure the status column (Not Started, In Progress, Complete) uses Data Validation (dropdown menus) to maintain data integrity.

## Phase 2: Execution and Tracking
*   **Pre-boarding Sync:** Send the link to the employee one week prior to their start date with an invitation to review the "Welcome" and "First Week" tabs.
*   **Real-time Progress Monitoring:** Managers must review the sheet every Tuesday and Thursday to identify blockers or pending tasks that require leadership intervention.
*   **Resource Mapping:** Hyperlink all necessary documentation, company handbooks, and video tutorials directly into the "Resources" column.
*   **Final Review:** During the 30-day performance check-in, review the "Onboarding Checklist" with the employee to confirm all administrative and cultural orientation goals have been achieved.

## Phase 3: Archiving and Analytics
*   **Status Verification:** Once all items are marked as 'Complete', archive the sheet to a restricted 'Completed Onboarding' folder.
*   **Feedback Integration:** Add a row for 'Onboarding Feedback' where the new hire can rate the clarity and usefulness of the template.
*   **Data Export:** Periodically export the sheet data to a Master Dashboard (or CSV) to track trends in onboarding completion times and identify departmental delays.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Protected Ranges" feature for sensitive columns (like salary or personal identification info) to ensure only authorized HR personnel can edit specific cells.
*   **Pro Tip:** Integrate your Google Sheet with Slack or Microsoft Teams via Zapier to receive notifications when a new hire marks a milestone as 'Complete'.
*   **Pitfall (Complexity Overload):** Avoid "tab bloat." Keep the sheet under five tabs to ensure the mobile experience remains functional for the new hire.
*   **Pitfall (Stale Links):** Always verify that links to internal folders are set to "Anyone with the link can view." Broken permissions are the #1 cause of new hire frustration.

## FAQ
**1. Why use Google Sheets instead of dedicated Onboarding Software?**
Google Sheets offers unparalleled flexibility and zero barrier to entry. It allows for rapid iteration of processes without the need for technical configuration or high licensing costs.

**2. How do I handle tasks that are dependent on multiple departments?**
Use the 'Assignee' column in your sheet and use the '@' mention feature within Google Sheets to notify specific department heads when their specific sub-task is ready for action.

**3. What should I do if a new hire accidentally deletes a row?**
Use the 'Version History' feature (File > Version History > See version history) to restore the sheet to a previous state before the data loss occurred.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Recruitment and Selection Process

The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to standardize the recruitment and selection process to ensure the acquisition of high-quality talent, maintain legal compliance, and provide a consistent, professional experience for all candidates. This process is designed to minimize bias, improve time-to-hire metrics, and ensure that every new hire aligns with the organizational culture and operational requirements.

## 1. Needs Analysis and Requisition
*   **Role Identification:** Department head submits a formal request outlining the need for the position (new headcount vs. backfill).
*   **Job Analysis:** HR and the Hiring Manager collaborate to define key responsibilities, essential competencies, and KPIs for the role.
*   **Budget Approval:** Confirm the salary band and budget allocation with Finance.
*   **Job Description Drafting:** Finalize the official job description, including non-negotiable requirements and "nice-to-have" skills.

## 2. Sourcing and Attraction
*   **Internal Posting:** Post the position on the internal company intranet/portal for a minimum of 3–5 business days.
*   **External Posting:** Distribute the job description across designated platforms (LinkedIn, industry-specific boards, careers page).
*   **Candidate Sourcing:** Actively reach out to passive candidates via professional networks.
*   **Applicant Tracking:** Ensure all incoming applications are logged directly into the Applicant Tracking System (ATS).

## 3. Screening and Selection
*   **Resume Screening:** HR reviews applications against the defined selection criteria.
*   **Initial Phone/Video Screen:** Conduct a 20-30 minute screening to verify candidate interest, salary expectations, and basic qualifications.
*   **Technical/Skills Assessment:** Administer standardized testing or practical exercises relevant to the role.
*   **Structured Interviews:** Conduct in-person or virtual panel interviews using a standardized rubric to ensure objective evaluation.

## 4. Closing and Onboarding
*   **Reference Checks:** Perform at least two professional reference checks for the preferred candidate.
*   **Offer Presentation:** Issue a formal offer letter detailing compensation, benefits, and start date.
*   **Negotiation:** Manage counter-offers or salary adjustments within the authorized range.
*   **Onboarding Setup:** Notify IT, Facilities, and the department head to prepare for the candidate's first day.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Structured Interviewing.** Use a "scoring matrix" for every candidate. Comparing scores rather than "gut feelings" significantly reduces cognitive bias and improves hiring accuracy.
*   **Pro Tip: Candidate Ghosting.** Always communicate the status of the application within 48 hours of a decision. A negative candidate experience can damage your employer brand.
*   **Pitfall: Scope Creep.** Avoid adding "nice-to-have" requirements halfway through the hiring process. This leads to an unrealistic candidate profile and prolonged time-to-hire.
*   **Pitfall: Overlooking Soft Skills.** Technical expertise is rarely enough. Use behavioral interview questions (e.g., "Tell me about a time you failed...") to gauge emotional intelligence and cultural fit.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. How do we ensure legal compliance during the interview process?**
To maintain compliance, use a standardized set of interview questions for every candidate applying for the same role. Avoid questions regarding personal life, religion, family status, or age, as these may lead to claims of discrimination.

**2. What should be done if no suitable candidates are found after 30 days?**
If the talent pipeline is empty, pause the process to audit the job description. Often, the salary is below market value, or the requirements are too restrictive. Adjust the strategy and re-launch the search.

**3. What is the standard duration for a reference check?**
A professional reference check should take between 10–15 minutes. Focus on verifying dates of employment, job responsibilities, and specific questions regarding performance and reliability.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Routine for Oily Skin

## Overview
Effective management of oily skin requires a balanced approach focused on controlling excess sebum production, maintaining pore clarity, and ensuring adequate hydration. This SOP outlines a standardized, professional-grade protocol designed to stabilize oil levels, prevent congestion, and protect the skin barrier. Adherence to this routine minimizes the risk of breakouts and promotes a refined, matte-finish complexion.

## Morning Routine (Pre-Activity Protocol)
The goal of the morning session is to remove nighttime debris and prep the skin for environmental exposure.

*   **Cleansing:** Utilize a gel or foam-based cleanser containing salicylic acid or tea tree oil. Massage for 60 seconds to emulsify trapped oils.
*   **Toning:** Apply an alcohol-free, astringent toner containing witch hazel or niacinamide to balance pH levels and tighten pore appearance.
*   **Targeted Treatment:** Apply a lightweight niacinamide or zinc serum to regulate sebum production throughout the day.
*   **Hydration:** Use a non-comedogenic, oil-free water-based moisturizer. Do not skip this step; dehydrated skin will overproduce oil to compensate.
*   **UV Protection:** Finish with a broad-spectrum, mattifying sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30. Opt for "dry-touch" or gel-based formulas to prevent heavy residue.

## Evening Routine (Restorative Protocol)
The goal of the evening session is to remove environmental pollutants, makeup, and accumulated sebum to facilitate overnight cellular repair.

*   **Double Cleansing (Step 1):** Use a micellar water or a light cleansing oil to break down SPF and makeup. This prevents pore blockage.
*   **Double Cleansing (Step 2):** Follow with your primary foaming cleanser to deep-clean the pores and remove remaining residue.
*   **Exfoliation (2–3 times per week):** Use a BHA (Beta Hydroxy Acid) exfoliant, such as salicylic acid, to penetrate deep into pores and dissolve oil buildup.
*   **Treatment:** Apply a retinoid or an oil-regulating serum to encourage cell turnover and prevent follicular congestion.
*   **Hydration:** Apply a lightweight, barrier-repairing moisturizer. Ensure the product is labeled "oil-free."

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Avoid Over-Cleansing:** Stripping the skin of all moisture triggers "rebound oil production." If your skin feels tight or "squeaky" after washing, your cleanser is too harsh.
*   **Manage Texture:** Use blotting papers throughout the day to remove excess oil without disturbing your makeup or UV protection layer.
*   **The "Toweling" Rule:** Always use a clean towel daily. Oily skin traps bacteria easily; reusing towels can lead to secondary breakouts.
*   **Pitfall - Skipping Moisturizer:** A common misconception is that oily skin does not need moisture. This will lead to transepidermal water loss (TEWL) and increased oil gland activity.
*   **Pitfall - Comedogenic Products:** Always verify labels for "non-comedogenic" or "won't clog pores" to ensure the formulation is optimized for oil-prone skin.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I wash my face more than twice a day if I get oily?**
A: No. Washing more than twice a day can strip the skin barrier, leading to increased irritation and further oil production. If you feel oily mid-day, use a blotting paper rather than washing.

**Q: Can I use face oils if I have oily skin?**
A: It is generally recommended to avoid heavy, occlusive oils. However, specific non-comedogenic oils like jojoba or squalane can sometimes help regulate skin by mimicking natural sebum, but proceed with caution and monitor your skin’s reaction.

**Q: Why is my skin still oily even though I use strong acne products?**
A: Often, harsh acne products (like high-strength benzoyl peroxide) cause skin dehydration. When the skin is dehydrated, it produces more oil to protect itself. Switch to a gentler cleanser and focus on hydration.

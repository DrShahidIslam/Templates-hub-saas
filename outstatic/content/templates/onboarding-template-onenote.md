# Standard Operating Procedure: New Hire OneNote Onboarding Template

## Introduction
The OneNote Onboarding Template serves as the single source of truth for new employees, designed to reduce time-to-productivity and foster organizational alignment. This document outlines the process for provisioning, customizing, and maintaining the OneNote notebook for incoming team members. By standardizing this process, we ensure every new hire receives consistent information regarding company culture, technical access, and departmental workflows, thereby minimizing onboarding friction and operational ambiguity.

## Step-by-Step Checklist

## Phase 1: Provisioning and Setup
*   [ ] **Create Instance:** Duplicate the "Master Onboarding Template" from the SharePoint/OneDrive repository. 
*   [ ] **Rename Notebook:** Re-save the notebook using the standard naming convention: `[Year]_[Department]_[Last Name]_[First Name]_Onboarding`.
*   [ ] **Set Permissions:** Grant "Edit" access exclusively to the new hire and their direct manager. Remove all legacy personnel from the access list.
*   [ ] **Validate Sync:** Open the notebook in the OneNote Desktop app to ensure all sections, tabs, and embedded files are synced and accessible.

## Phase 2: Content Customization
*   [ ] **Insert Personal Data:** Update the "Welcome" section with the employee's name, start date, and assigned mentor/buddy contact info.
*   [ ] **Tailor Tech Stack:** Review the "IT Resources" section and remove software/access instructions that are not relevant to the employee’s specific role.
*   [ ] **Calendar Integration:** Insert the first-week agenda directly into the "Schedule" tab, including links to recurring team meetings.
*   [ ] **Knowledge Base Links:** Ensure all hyperlinks to the company Intranet or Document Management System (DMS) are active and permissions are set to "Everyone in Organization."

## Phase 3: Launch and Review
*   [ ] **Final Audit:** Perform a "read-through" from the perspective of the new hire to ensure tone is professional and instructions are unambiguous.
*   [ ] **Dispatch:** Send an email invitation to the new hire with the OneNote link, instructions on how to sync the notebook to their desktop, and a brief explanation of how they are expected to use it.
*   [ ] **Progress Tracking:** Schedule a 15-minute check-in for the end of Day 3 to confirm the employee has successfully navigated the notebook.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip: Use Tags for Action Items:** Encourage the new hire to use OneNote’s "To-Do" tags (Ctrl+1) within the notebook so their manager can easily track completed tasks during 1-on-1s.
*   **Pro Tip: Embed, Don’t Attach:** When sharing policy documents, embed the file or use a hyperlink rather than attaching the file. This ensures the employee always accesses the most current version.
*   **Pitfall - Information Overload:** Avoid dumping the entire company’s historical knowledge into the template. Keep it focused on the "First 30 Days."
*   **Pitfall - Ignoring Mobile Sync:** Remind the employee to sign in to the OneNote mobile app; onboarding is often interrupted by desk moves or off-site training where mobile access is vital.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I use a Shared Notebook or individual files?**
A: Always use a shared cloud-based notebook (SharePoint/OneDrive). This allows for real-time collaboration and ensures that if the employee’s laptop fails, their notes remain secure in the cloud.

**Q: Can I password-protect sections?**
A: Avoid password-protecting individual sections of the onboarding notebook. It creates unnecessary friction and can lead to lost credentials; rely on standard SharePoint folder permissions instead.

**Q: How often should the "Master Template" be updated?**
A: The master template should undergo a formal audit every quarter to ensure that links, software instructions, and departmental contacts remain current.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Island Operations (ACNH)

As an Operations Manager, maintaining optimal island efficiency requires a structured, repeatable daily routine. This SOP outlines the essential tasks required to maximize resource harvesting, maintain aesthetic standards, and build villager rapport within Animal Crossing: New Horizons. By following this standardized protocol, the Island Representative ensures consistent progress in infrastructure development, economic growth, and island aesthetic maintenance.

## Phase 1: Morning Administration and Resource Gathering
*   **Mail Review:** Check the mailbox at the front of the residence for letters, gifts, and ordered goods from Nook Shopping.
*   **Resource Extraction:** Hit all six rocks on the island using the "two-hole" method to maximize iron nuggets, clay, stone, and gold nuggets.
*   **Fossil Survey:** Locate and dig up the four buried fossils scattered across the island; assess them at the Museum immediately.
*   **Money Tree Execution:** Locate the glowing golden crack in the ground, dig up the 1,000 Bells, and bury a bag of 10,000 Bells in the same hole to yield a high-value money tree.
*   **Special Character Check:** Scan the island for daily visitors (e.g., Celeste, Wisp, Gulliver, or Label) and engage to secure daily rewards.

## Phase 2: Commercial and Infrastructure Optimization
*   **Nook’s Cranny:** Sell all gathered materials, fossils, and seashells. Check the "Hot Item" of the day to determine if crafting it will yield a higher profit margin. 
*   **Nook Stop Terminal:** Access the Resident Services kiosk to collect daily Nook Miles bonuses, which increase incrementally based on consecutive login streaks.
*   **Tailor Services:** Check Able Sisters for seasonal fashion rotations and unique items to diversify inventory and island aesthetic.
*   **Infrastructure Maintenance:** Check for any new weeds, trash, or dropped items that negatively impact the island’s star rating.

## Phase 3: Social Capital and Relationship Management
*   **Villager Engagement:** Speak to every villager once per day to maintain friendship levels. Deliver wrapped gifts if a villager is "best friend" status to maximize the chance of receiving a framed photo.
*   **NPC Interaction:** Speak to Tom Nook or Isabelle regarding daily island news and potential public works project updates.
*   **Gift Distribution:** Distribute daily gifts to residents; ensure wrapping paper is used to boost friendship points.

## Phase 4: Evening Closing and Planning
*   **Storage Management:** Clear inventory of all gathered resources and organize them into home storage to prevent clutter.
*   **Market Analysis:** Review the Stalk Market (Turnip prices) if applicable. Check prices both morning (AM) and afternoon (PM) to ensure optimal ROI.
*   **Future Planning:** Verify that all planned construction or house expansions are scheduled to avoid workflow bottlenecks.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always carry a vaulting pole and ladder—or keep the Ladder/Vaulting Pole setup kit in your inventory—to ensure total map mobility.
*   **Pro Tip:** Use the "Empty Pockets" strategy before harvesting resources to minimize the need for mid-task inventory management.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid shaking trees with a net in hand if you are not prepared for wasps; always stand directly in front of the tree to ensure a successful catch.
*   **Pitfall:** Do not time-travel backward if you have turnips in your inventory, as this will cause them to rot instantly and result in total capital loss.

## FAQ: Operations Management

**Q: Does it matter what time of day I perform these tasks?**
A: Yes. Some NPCs and shop inventories are time-sensitive. Stalk market prices change at noon, and specific characters (like Wisp or Celeste) only appear after dark.

**Q: How do I maximize my Island Rating?**
A: Focus on "Island Evaluations" provided by Isabelle. She will highlight specific pain points, such as needing more fencing, more flowers, or fewer dropped items on the ground.

**Q: Is it necessary to hit every rock every day?**
A: Yes. Resources like Iron Nuggets and Gold Nuggets are essential for late-game crafting and island beautification projects; missing a day results in a permanent loss of potential revenue and inventory growth.

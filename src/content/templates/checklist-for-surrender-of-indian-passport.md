# Standard Operating Procedure: Surrender of Indian Passport

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory process for Indian citizens who have acquired foreign citizenship and are required to surrender their Indian passport. Under the Indian Citizenship Act, 1955, it is illegal for an Indian citizen to hold an Indian passport after acquiring the nationality of another country. This document provides a structured workflow to ensure compliance with the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) regulations, preventing legal complications such as penalties or travel restrictions.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Documentation
Before initiating the application, ensure all physical and digital documentation is compiled. Incomplete applications will lead to processing delays or rejection.

*   **Original Indian Passport:** The current, valid, or expired Indian passport to be surrendered.
*   **Proof of Foreign Citizenship:** Original and photocopy of the Foreign Passport and the Naturalization Certificate (or equivalent document).
*   **Application Form:** Complete the online application via the official VFS Global/MEA portal.
*   **Photographs:** Two recent 2x2 inch color photographs with a white background.
*   **Self-Declaration:** A notarized affidavit if there are discrepancies in name or date of birth between the Indian and foreign documents.
*   **Marriage Certificate (If applicable):** Required if a name change occurred post-acquisition of foreign citizenship.

## Phase 2: Online Submission and Fee Payment
The administrative workflow requires digital verification before physical submission.

*   **Online Registration:** Create an account on the official portal and fill out the "Surrender Certificate" application form.
*   **Review Data:** Cross-verify all details against your Naturalization Certificate to avoid data mismatches.
*   **Print Application:** Once submitted, print the physical copy of the application form.
*   **Fee Payment:** Pay the applicable surrender fees (varies by region/country) via the portal. Ensure you print the payment receipt.
*   **Appointment Booking:** Schedule a slot at the nearest Indian Embassy, Consulate, or VFS service center.

## Phase 3: Physical Submission and Verification
Attendance in person or via an authorized representative (where permitted) is required for final verification.

*   **Document Assembly:** Arrange documents in the order specified by the local consulate (usually: Application form, Fees receipt, Indian Passport, Foreign Passport copies).
*   **Biometrics:** Ensure you are prepared for biometric capture if the service center mandates it.
*   **Collection Receipt:** Collect the acknowledgement receipt provided at the counter. This serves as proof that your application is under process.
*   **Tracking:** Use the reference number on your receipt to track the status of your Surrender Certificate issuance.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Penalty" Trap:** Failure to surrender your passport within three years of acquiring foreign citizenship or after traveling on an Indian passport post-naturalization may incur a "Renunciation/Surrender Penalty." Always declare your travel history accurately.
*   **Do Not Cancel Yourself:** Never manually cancel your Indian passport (e.g., cutting corners). The authorities must perform the cancellation stamp officially.
*   **Name Discrepancies:** If your name on the foreign passport differs from the Indian passport, file a "Deed Poll" or "Affidavit of Name Change" immediately. This is the #1 cause of application rejection.
*   **Passport Validity:** Even if your Indian passport is expired, you are still legally required to surrender it and obtain a Surrender Certificate.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Do I need a Surrender Certificate if I have already obtained an OCI card?**
A: Yes. The Surrender Certificate is a mandatory prerequisite for applying for an Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) card. You cannot obtain an OCI card without first surrendering your Indian passport.

**Q: Can I travel to India using my Indian passport after becoming a foreign citizen?**
A: No. Traveling on an Indian passport after acquiring foreign citizenship is a violation of the Foreigners Act and can lead to heavy fines and blacklisting. You must surrender the passport and apply for an appropriate visa or OCI.

**Q: How long does the process take?**
A: Processing times vary significantly based on the jurisdiction and the complexity of the case (e.g., lost passports require more time). On average, allow for 15–30 business days from the date of physical submission.

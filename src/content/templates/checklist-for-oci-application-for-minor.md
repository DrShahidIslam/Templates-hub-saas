# Standard Operating Procedure: OCI Application for Minors

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) serves as a comprehensive guide for parents and legal guardians applying for an Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) card on behalf of a minor. The OCI process requires meticulous attention to detail regarding document apostilling, notarization, and specific formatting requirements. Failure to adhere to these standards often results in application rejection or significant processing delays. This document outlines the mandatory procedural steps to ensure a successful submission.

## Section 1: Pre-Application Preparation
*   **Verify Eligibility:** Ensure the minor is a citizen of a foreign country and was eligible to become a citizen of India on or after January 26, 1950.
*   **Verify Jurisdiction:** Confirm your current residential address falls under the jurisdiction of the specific Indian Consulate or Embassy to which you are applying.
*   **Check Passport Validity:** Ensure the minor’s foreign passport has a minimum of six months of validity remaining from the date of application.
*   **Gather Proof of Indian Origin:** Collect the parents' or grandparents' Indian Passports, OCI cards, or birth certificates proving their previous Indian citizenship.

## Section 2: Documentation Checklist
*   **Online Application Form:** Complete the registration form on the official OCI portal, print, and sign. (Note: The minor must sign or provide a thumbprint if unable to sign).
*   **Photograph Requirements:** Two 2x2 inch color photos with a white background, matching the uploaded digital photo specifications.
*   **Birth Certificate:** A copy of the minor’s birth certificate showing the names of both parents.
*   **Proof of Legal Status:** A copy of the minor’s foreign passport and, if applicable, proof of citizenship (e.g., Naturalization Certificate).
*   **Proof of Address:** Two documents showing proof of residence (e.g., utility bill, lease agreement, or driver's license of the parent).
*   **Parental Authorization:** A notarized "Parental Authorization Form" signed by both parents.
*   **Status of Parents:** Copies of both parents' passports and, if applicable, their OCI cards or valid visas.
*   **Renunciation Certificate:** If the minor previously held an Indian passport, a copy of the Renunciation Certificate is mandatory.

## Section 3: Submission and Verification
*   **Document Sequencing:** Arrange documents exactly in the order requested by the service provider (e.g., VFS Global).
*   **Fee Payment:** Pay the non-refundable application fee via the portal and print the receipt.
*   **Self-Attestation:** Ensure all copies of supporting documents are self-attested by the parent (signed as "Certified true copy").
*   **Submission:** Submit the physical application package via the designated courier service or in-person appointment.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The "Signature" Trap:** Ensure the minor’s signature on the application form perfectly matches the signature on their passport. If the child is too young to sign, use a clear thumbprint.
*   **Notarization Precision:** Ensure that any document requiring notarization is stamped by a Notary Public. Some consulates require specific wording on the parental consent form—check your local consulate’s website for the current template.
*   **Name Discrepancies:** Ensure the minor’s name and parent names are identical across all documents (Birth Certificate, Passport, and OCI application). If names differ due to marriage or legal name changes, include a legal name-change affidavit.
*   **Avoid Paper Clips:** Do not use staples or paper clips. Use binder clips to organize sections to prevent document damage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can both parents sign the OCI application?**
A: Yes, both parents must sign the Parental Authorization Form. If one parent is unavailable due to legal separation or other reasons, provide supporting court documentation or an affidavit explaining the situation.

**Q: Does the minor need to be present for the submission?**
A: Generally, no. However, if you are submitting via a physical appointment at a VFS center, check the local branch policy as some locations require the minor to be present for biometric verification.

**Q: How long does the OCI process usually take?**
A: Processing times vary significantly based on jurisdiction and background verification. Expect a minimum of 8 to 12 weeks. Always track your application status via the official government portal using your registration number.

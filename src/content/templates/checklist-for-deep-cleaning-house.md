# Standard Operating Procedure: Residential Deep Cleaning Protocol

This document outlines the professional standards and procedural requirements for executing a comprehensive deep cleaning of a residential property. Unlike routine maintenance, a deep clean addresses the accumulation of grime, dust, and debris in neglected areas, ensuring a high level of sanitation and aesthetic restoration. Adherence to this SOP ensures maximum efficiency, cross-contamination prevention, and superior results for both operational teams and homeowners.

## Phase 1: Preparation and Essential Supplies
Before commencing, ensure the environment is cleared of personal clutter. Standard supplies must include:
*   Microfiber cloths (color-coded by area)
*   HEPA-filter vacuum cleaner with attachments
*   Multi-purpose degreaser, glass cleaner, and disinfectant
*   Extension poles for high-reach dusting
*   Scrub brushes (stiff and soft bristle)

## Phase 2: Systematic Cleaning Checklist

## H2: General Living Areas and Bedrooms
*   **High-Level Dusting:** Remove cobwebs from ceiling corners, crown molding, and light fixtures.
*   **Vertical Surfaces:** Dust and wipe down baseboards, door frames, window sills, and light switches.
*   **Furniture Care:** Vacuum upholstery, including underneath cushions. Dust all shelves, frames, and electronic components using static-free cloths.
*   **Flooring:** Move lightweight furniture to vacuum and mop floor edges. Spot clean carpets if necessary.

## H2: Kitchen Sanitation
*   **Appliance Exterior/Interior:** Clean inside and behind the refrigerator, microwave, dishwasher, and oven. Descale the coffee maker and toaster crumb trays.
*   **Cabinetry:** Degrease cabinet fronts and handles. Wipe out the interior of drawers to remove loose debris.
*   **Fixtures:** Scrub the sink basin and polish the faucet. Clean the backsplash and grout lines behind the stove.
*   **Waste Management:** Sanitize the trash bin interior and exterior.

## H2: Bathroom Hygiene
*   **Descaling:** Apply a specialized descaling agent to showerheads and faucets.
*   **Surface Scrubbing:** Deep scrub tile grout, bathtub basin, and shower enclosure glass to remove soap scum.
*   **Sanitization:** Disinfect the entire toilet unit, including the base, handle, and hinges.
*   **Mirrors/Vanity:** Clean mirrors with streak-free glass cleaner; wipe out vanity drawers.

## H2: Entryways and Utility Spaces
*   **Floors:** Deep mop hard flooring; use an enzyme cleaner if pet odors are present.
*   **Air Quality:** Replace or clean HVAC filters and dust floor-level return vents.
*   **Windows:** Clean tracks, frames, and glass panes (interior).

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Top-to-Bottom Rule:** Always clean from the ceiling down to the floor. Dusting a surface after you have vacuumed the floor creates rework.
*   **Dwell Time:** Do not wipe chemicals off immediately. Let disinfectant sit for the time specified on the bottle (usually 3–10 minutes) to ensure proper bacteria kill rates.
*   **Pitfall - Cross-Contamination:** Using the same cloth for the bathroom and the kitchen is a critical failure. Use distinct sets of microfiber cloths to maintain hygiene standards.
*   **Pitfall - Oversaturation:** Never spray cleaning agents directly onto electrical outlets or light switches; spray the cloth first to prevent short circuits.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should a deep clean be performed?**
A: For most households, a professional-level deep clean is recommended every 6 months. High-traffic homes or those with pets may require quarterly service.

**Q: Should I clean the windows from the outside?**
A: This SOP focuses on interior surfaces. Exterior window cleaning is a specialized task requiring different safety equipment and should be scheduled separately if necessary.

**Q: Is it necessary to empty the refrigerator completely?**
A: Yes. A deep clean includes removing expired items and wiping down shelves and drawers to remove bacteria and spills, which is not possible if the fridge is full.

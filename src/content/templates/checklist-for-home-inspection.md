# Standard Operating Procedure: Residential Home Inspection

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the professional requirements for conducting a comprehensive residential home inspection. The objective is to identify material defects, safety hazards, and maintenance requirements to provide the client with an objective assessment of the property’s condition. This SOP assumes the inspector utilizes a systematic room-by-room and system-by-system approach to ensure no critical components are overlooked, maintaining professional standards and mitigating liability.

## Phase 1: Exterior and Structural Components

*   **Foundation:** Examine for major cracks, settling, or water intrusion evidence.
*   **Grading and Drainage:** Ensure soil slopes away from the foundation; check for standing water or improper gutter discharge.
*   **Roofing:** Inspect shingle condition, flashings, chimneys, vents, and valleys for signs of wear, loose materials, or water ponding.
*   **Siding and Trim:** Check for rot, cracks, pest damage, or improper clearance from the ground.
*   **Exterior Features:** Evaluate decks, porches, balconies, and railings for structural stability and code-compliant guard heights.

## Phase 2: Mechanical and Utility Systems

*   **HVAC System:** Operate and test the heating and cooling systems; check for abnormal noises, filter condition, and airflow efficiency.
*   **Electrical Panel:** Inspect the main service panel for proper labeling, breaker types, signs of overheating (arcing), and adequate capacity.
*   **Plumbing:** Test water pressure at fixtures, inspect exposed pipes for leaks, and evaluate the water heater’s age, pressure relief valve, and fuel source.
*   **Ventilation:** Confirm that attic, kitchen, and bathroom fans vent to the exterior rather than into enclosed wall cavities.

## Phase 3: Interior and Living Spaces

*   **Windows and Doors:** Operate every accessible window and door to ensure proper latching, seals, and ease of operation.
*   **Walls and Ceilings:** Scan for signs of past or present moisture damage, cracks, or sagging.
*   **Kitchen:** Test all built-in appliances (oven, dishwasher, disposal), inspect under-sink cabinetry for leaks, and verify GFCIs near water sources.
*   **Bathrooms:** Evaluate toilet stability, shower/tub drainage, and tile/grout condition for potential water penetration.
*   **Attic and Crawl Space:** Assess insulation levels, vapor barriers, and look for signs of pest infestation, mold, or structural modification.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Document with Scale.** Always include a reference object (like a coin or screwdriver) in photos of cracks or damage to provide a sense of scale for the client.
*   **Pro Tip: Trace the Source.** If you find a stain on a ceiling, always investigate the area directly above it (roof or plumbing) rather than just noting the stain itself.
*   **Pitfall: The "Everything is Fine" Bias.** Avoid rushing. Even if the house looks "perfect," never skip the attic or crawl space; these are the most common areas for hidden, high-cost defects.
*   **Pitfall: Overstepping.** Do not offer repair estimates or tell the client whether or not they should buy the house. Your role is strictly to report the physical condition of the property.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Is a home inspection a pass/fail test?**
No. A home inspection is a neutral, diagnostic evaluation of a property’s condition. There is no such thing as a "failing" house; the report simply provides the data required for the client to make an informed financial decision.

**2. Should the buyer attend the inspection?**
Yes. Attending the inspection allows the client to see firsthand where issues are located, ask questions in real-time, and better understand how to maintain the home’s systems in the future.

**3. What happens if I find an inaccessible area?**
If a component is physically blocked by furniture, boxes, or locked doors, you must document it as "inaccessible" in the report. Do not attempt to dismantle systems or move heavy items that could result in damage to the seller's property.

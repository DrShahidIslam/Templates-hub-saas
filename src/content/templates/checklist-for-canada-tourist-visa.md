# Standard Operating Procedure: Canada Visitor Visa Application

This document outlines the systematic procedure for preparing, organizing, and submitting a Canadian Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) application. Success in securing a visa depends heavily on the meticulous preparation of documentation to prove that you meet the requirements of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA), specifically regarding your intent to leave Canada at the end of your authorized stay and your financial capacity to support your visit.

## Phase 1: Eligibility and Document Gathering

*   [ ] **Determine Travel Purpose:** Clearly define your reason for travel (tourism, family visit, or business).
*   [ ] **Passport Verification:** Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your intended date of departure from Canada. Ensure it has at least one blank page.
*   [ ] **Identity Documents:** Prepare clear, color scans of the bio-data page of your passport and any previous visas/entry stamps.
*   [ ] **Digital Photo:** Obtain a professional digital photo that meets IRCC specifications (35mm x 45mm, white background, taken within the last 6 months).

## Phase 2: Financial Documentation

*   [ ] **Proof of Funds:** Collect bank statements for the last 4–6 months. These must show your name, account history, and sufficient funds to cover your stay.
*   [ ] **Employment Proof:** Obtain a formal letter from your employer on company letterhead stating your job title, salary, duration of employment, and an approved leave of absence.
*   [ ] **Income Tax Returns:** Include the last 2 years of Income Tax Return (ITR) filings to demonstrate fiscal responsibility.
*   [ ] **Asset Evidence:** If applicable, include property deeds, vehicle registrations, or investment portfolios to establish strong ties to your home country.

## Phase 3: Intent and Ties to Home Country

*   [ ] **Travel Itinerary:** Create a clear, day-by-day itinerary of your intended activities in Canada.
*   [ ] **Cover Letter:** Draft a personal statement explaining the purpose of your visit, your specific plans, and a firm commitment to return home based on your current personal, professional, or social obligations.
*   [ ] **Family Ties:** Provide evidence of immediate family members remaining in your home country (e.g., birth certificates, marriage certificates).

## Phase 4: Application Submission

*   [ ] **IRCC Account Creation:** Create a secure account via the official IRCC portal.
*   [ ] **Form Completion:** Accurately complete the IMM 5257 (Application for Visitor Visa) and IMM 5645 (Family Information) forms.
*   [ ] **Payment:** Pay the processing fee and biometrics fee via the secure online portal.
*   [ ] **Biometrics Appointment:** Once you receive the Biometrics Instruction Letter (BIL), schedule an appointment at the nearest Visa Application Centre (VAC) within the mandated timeframe.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip: Be Transparent.** If you have previously been refused a visa for Canada or any other country, disclose it clearly. IRCC systems are interconnected; non-disclosure is an automatic ground for a 5-year ban for misrepresentation.
*   **Pro Tip: Provide Context.** A bank statement with a massive, unexplained deposit right before application is a red flag. Always provide a brief explanation or source of funds for large, irregular transactions.
*   **Pitfall: Generic Letters.** Avoid using templates found online. The visa officer wants to see your specific travel plan and your genuine ties to your home country.
*   **Pitfall: Incomplete Documents.** Ensure all documents are in English or French. If the original is in another language, you must include a certified translation.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to book my flights before applying for the visa?**
A: No. IRCC specifically advises against purchasing non-refundable tickets before receiving your visa approval. A clear itinerary is sufficient.

**Q: How long does the visa processing take?**
A: Processing times vary by country and season. Check the current "Check processing times" tool on the official IRCC website for the most accurate estimate based on your residence.

**Q: What happens if my visa application is refused?**
A: You will receive a letter from IRCC detailing the reasons for the refusal. You are entitled to re-apply at any time, provided you have addressed the specific reasons for the previous denial with new, stronger evidence.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Indian Passport Surrender

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the mandatory requirements and procedural steps for Indian citizens who have acquired foreign citizenship and are required to surrender their Indian passport. Under the Indian Citizenship Act, 1955, an Indian passport holder must surrender their travel document upon acquisition of a foreign nationality. Failure to do so may result in penalties, legal complications, and potential issues when applying for future Indian visas or OCI cards.

---

## Phase 1: Documentation Preparation

Before initiating the surrender application, ensure all documents are original and accompanied by clear photocopies.

*   **Surrender Certificate Application Form:** Download and complete the form via the official government portal (e.g., VFS Global or the relevant Indian Embassy/Consulate website).
*   **Foreign Passport:** The original foreign passport along with a self-attested copy of the identification/data page.
*   **Indian Passport:** The original Indian passport being surrendered.
*   **Naturalization Certificate:** Original naturalization certificate or the official document stating the date of acquisition of foreign citizenship.
*   **Proof of Address:** A document reflecting your current residency in the foreign country (e.g., Utility bill, driver’s license).
*   **Photographs:** Two recent color photographs (2x2 inches) meeting the specific specifications defined by the mission.
*   **Fees:** Printed payment receipt for the surrender fee and any applicable administrative/courier charges.

## Phase 2: Application Submission

*   **Online Registration:** Complete the online registration form accurately, ensuring the data matches your foreign passport exactly.
*   **Print and Sign:** Print the completed application form and sign in the designated boxes.
*   **Courier or In-Person Appointment:** Verify if the jurisdiction requires a physical appearance or accepts mail-in applications. If mailing, use a trackable service.
*   **Verification:** Ensure all photocopies are self-attested (signed by the applicant).

## Phase 3: Post-Submission Procedures

*   **Track Application:** Use the reference number provided to monitor the status on the portal.
*   **Cancellation Stamp:** Once processed, your Indian passport will be returned with a "Cancelled" stamp and the corners clipped. 
*   **Surrender Certificate:** Retain the original Surrender Certificate provided by the Embassy/Consulate. This is a vital document for future OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) applications.

---

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The "Penalty" Pitfall:** If you traveled on an Indian passport *after* acquiring foreign citizenship, you are liable to pay a penalty. Declare this truthfully in your application to avoid legal complications during future immigration checks.
*   **Data Consistency:** Ensure your name, date of birth, and place of birth are consistent across your Indian passport, foreign passport, and naturalization certificate. If there are discrepancies, provide a notarized affidavit.
*   **Do Not Discard:** Never destroy or lose your Indian passport once you become a foreign citizen. Even if expired, it must be surrendered.
*   **Validity Check:** If your Indian passport expired years ago, you may need to provide a notarized "Affidavit of Non-Use" for the period between the passport's expiry and your acquisition of foreign citizenship.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Is it mandatory to surrender the Indian passport if it has already expired?**
Yes. You are legally required to surrender an Indian passport regardless of whether it is currently valid or has expired, provided you have acquired citizenship of another country.

**2. How long does the surrender process usually take?**
Processing times vary by jurisdiction, typically ranging from 10 to 20 business days. It is advised to account for additional time if you are mailing your documents.

**3. Do I need the Surrender Certificate to apply for an OCI card?**
Yes. The Surrender Certificate is a mandatory document for the OCI application process. Without proof of surrender, your OCI application will be rejected.

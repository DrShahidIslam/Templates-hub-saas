# Standard Operating Procedure: Obtaining an Illinois REAL ID

This document outlines the formal procedure for successfully transitioning from a standard Illinois driver’s license or ID card to a federally compliant REAL ID. In accordance with the REAL ID Act, a REAL ID is required for domestic air travel and entry into secure federal facilities. To ensure a seamless visit to the Illinois Secretary of State (SOS) Driver Services facility, applicants must prepare and organize specific documentation ahead of time to satisfy federal verification requirements.

## Phase 1: Document Gathering Checklist

Before scheduling your visit, collect the following original documents (photocopies are not accepted). Ensure all documents are current and show your full legal name.

### Proof of Identity (Choose One)
*   Valid, unexpired U.S. passport or passport card.
*   Original or certified copy of a birth certificate.
*   Certificate of Naturalization or Citizenship.
*   Consular Report of Birth Abroad.

### Proof of Social Security Number (Choose One)
*   Social Security card (must show full nine-digit number).
*   W-2 form.
*   Paystub with your name and full SSN.
*   1099 form.

### Proof of Residency (Two Documents Required)
*   Utility bill (gas, electric, water, or cable) issued within the last 90 days.
*   Bank statement or credit card statement dated within the last 90 days.
*   Lease agreement, mortgage statement, or deed.
*   Official mail from a state or federal government agency (e.g., IRS, Social Security Administration).

### Proof of Signature (Choose One)
*   Credit or debit card with a pre-printed signature on the back.
*   Canceled check.
*   Court order or judgment.
*   Current Illinois driver’s license or ID card.

## Phase 2: Facility Visit Workflow

Follow these steps on the day of your appointment to ensure efficient processing.

*   **Pre-Visit Verification:** Use the [Illinois Secretary of State REAL ID Interactive Checklist](https://realid.ilsos.gov/) to confirm your specific document requirements.
*   **Arrival:** Arrive 15 minutes before your scheduled appointment time.
*   **Initial Review:** Present your organized packet of documents to the attendant for a preliminary audit.
*   **Application:** Complete the application form provided at the facility or online if available for your location.
*   **Vision & Written Test:** Be prepared to take a vision screening; a written test may be required if your license has expired beyond the grace period.
*   **Payment:** Pay the applicable fee (same as a standard renewal/duplicate fee) via credit card, debit card, or check.
*   **Temporary ID:** You will receive a temporary paper receipt/ID while your permanent REAL ID is mailed to your address on file.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Name Discrepancies:** If your current legal name differs from the name on your birth certificate (e.g., due to marriage or divorce), you must bring certified court-issued documents (marriage certificate, divorce decree) to create a legal "paper trail."
*   **The "Original" Rule:** Photocopies are strictly rejected. If you have lost your birth certificate or Social Security card, request certified replacements well in advance of your visit.
*   **Avoid the Rush:** Mid-week, mid-morning appointments generally experience shorter wait times than Monday mornings or Friday afternoons.
*   **Signature Match:** Ensure the signature on your identification documents matches your intent for your new license, as this will be captured digitally.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a REAL ID if I already have a valid U.S. Passport?**
A: No. A valid U.S. Passport is already REAL ID-compliant for domestic air travel. However, obtaining a REAL ID is recommended for those who prefer to keep their passport safely stored at home while traveling domestically.

**Q: Can I apply for a REAL ID online?**
A: No. Due to the requirement for document verification and digital identity capture (signature and photo), a personal visit to a Driver Services facility is mandatory for the initial issuance.

**Q: Does it cost more to get a REAL ID compared to a standard license?**
A: No. In Illinois, the cost for a REAL ID is identical to the cost of a standard driver's license or ID card renewal.

# Standard Operating Procedure: Canada Visitor Visa Application

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines the standardized process for preparing and submitting an application for a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) to Canada. As an operations manager, I emphasize that the success of a visa application relies heavily on document integrity, proof of financial stability, and demonstrating clear intent to return to one's home country. Adherence to this checklist will minimize the risk of procedural delays or administrative refusals.

## Phase 1: Eligibility and Documentation Gathering

*   **Determine Visa Type:** Confirm if you require a TRV or an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) based on your citizenship.
*   **Valid Passport:** Ensure your passport has at least six months of validity beyond your intended date of departure from Canada and contains at least one blank page.
*   **Proof of Identity:** Prepare high-quality color scans of the passport bio-data page and all pages containing stamps, visas, or markings.
*   **Digital Photos:** Secure a digital photograph that meets IRCC specifications (35mm x 45mm, white background, taken within the last 6 months).

## Phase 2: Financial and Intent Evidence

*   **Proof of Financial Support:** Collect the last 4–6 months of personal bank statements, pay stubs, and proof of assets (e.g., property deeds, investments).
*   **Letter of Intent:** Draft a formal letter explaining the purpose of the trip, the duration of stay, and a specific itinerary.
*   **Proof of Ties to Home Country:** Compile evidence of permanent employment (leave approval letter), family commitments, property ownership, or enrollment in academic institutions to demonstrate intent to return.
*   **Invitation Letter (If applicable):** If visiting a Canadian resident, obtain a signed letter of invitation including their contact details, proof of status in Canada, and the nature of the relationship.

## Phase 3: Application Assembly and Submission

*   **GCKey Account Creation:** Register or log in to your IRCC secure portal account.
*   **Questionnaire Completion:** Answer the online assessment questions accurately to generate your personalized document checklist.
*   **Form Completion:** Complete the *Application for Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa) Made Outside of Canada (IMM 5257)* and *Family Information Form (IMM 5645)*.
*   **Review and Upload:** Verify that all uploaded files are in PDF format and clear enough to read. Do not exceed file size limits.
*   **Fee Payment:** Pay the application processing fee and the biometric fee via the portal.

## Phase 4: Biometrics and Processing

*   **Biometrics Appointment:** Once the Biometric Instruction Letter (BIL) is received, schedule an appointment at the nearest Visa Application Centre (VAC) promptly.
*   **Status Monitoring:** Monitor your GCKey portal for updates regarding your passport request or decision letter.
*   **Passport Submission:** If approved, follow instructions provided in the Passport Request Letter to submit your physical passport for visa stamping.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pitfall - Vague Intent:** A common reason for refusal is a "lack of purpose of visit." Be as specific as possible regarding where you are going, who you are meeting, and what you will be doing each day.
*   **Pro Tip - Financial Consistency:** Avoid sudden, large deposits into your bank account just before applying. IRCC officers prefer to see a steady, established financial history.
*   **Pitfall - Incomplete History:** Do not omit past visa refusals from any country. IRCC shares information with international partners; misrepresentation will lead to a 5-year ban.
*   **Pro Tip - The "Why":** Always highlight your employment or family ties in your home country. You must convince the officer that you have a compelling reason to leave Canada when your visa expires.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Does a valid visitor visa guarantee entry into Canada?**
No. A visa is a document that allows you to travel to a Canadian port of entry. The final decision on your entry remains with the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) officer at the border.

**2. Should I purchase non-refundable flight tickets before my visa is approved?**
No. IRCC explicitly advises applicants not to purchase non-refundable travel tickets until their visa has been approved and the passport has been returned.

**3. What is the difference between a Visitor Visa and a Super Visa?**
A standard Visitor Visa is for general tourism or business and is typically valid for up to 6 months per entry. A Super Visa is a multi-year visa specifically designed for parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens or permanent residents, allowing for stays of up to 5 years at a time.

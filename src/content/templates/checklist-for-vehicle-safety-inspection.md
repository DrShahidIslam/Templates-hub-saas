# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Vehicle Safety Inspection

## Introduction
The objective of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to ensure that all company vehicles are maintained in a safe, operational condition. Regular inspections are critical to minimizing mechanical failure, reducing liability, ensuring driver safety, and extending the lifecycle of company assets. This procedure must be performed by the designated driver prior to the start of every shift. Any defects identified must be reported to the Fleet Management Department immediately; if a vehicle is deemed "Unsafe for Operation," it must be grounded until repairs are completed.

---

## Pre-Departure Inspection Checklist

## Section 1: Exterior & Body Condition
*   **Walk-around Assessment:** Inspect the body for new dents, scratches, or missing panels. Document any damage that was not present during the last inspection.
*   **Tire Condition:** Check tire pressure (including the spare) and inspect treads for uneven wear, nails, or embedded debris. Ensure all lug nuts are present and tight.
*   **Lights and Signals:** Test low beams, high beams, turn signals (front/rear), brake lights, and hazard lights. Ensure all lenses are clean and free of cracks.
*   **Windshield & Wipers:** Inspect the windshield for chips or cracks that obstruct the driver's view. Verify wiper blades are not frayed and provide a clear sweep.
*   **Fluid Leaks:** Check the ground beneath the vehicle for any signs of leaking oil, coolant, transmission fluid, or brake fluid.

## Section 2: Under-the-Hood Components
*   **Oil Levels:** Pull the dipstick, wipe it clean, reinsert, and check that the oil level is within the manufacturer's safe operating range.
*   **Coolant & Brake Fluid:** Verify fluid levels in the reservoirs. Ensure caps are securely fastened.
*   **Belts and Hoses:** Visually inspect engine belts for fraying or cracking and check hoses for signs of bulging or leaking.
*   **Battery:** Ensure terminals are clean, free of corrosion, and tightly secured.

## Section 3: Interior & Safety Equipment
*   **Dashboard Gauges:** Turn the ignition to the "On" position and verify that all warning lights (Check Engine, ABS, Airbag, Oil Pressure) function briefly and clear once the engine is running.
*   **Brake Test:** Test the firmness of the brake pedal while the vehicle is stationary. Ensure the parking brake engages and holds the vehicle securely.
*   **Steering:** Ensure there is no excessive "play" or grinding noises when turning the wheel while stationary.
*   **Safety Gear:** Confirm the presence and expiration dates of the fire extinguisher, first aid kit, and reflective emergency triangles.
*   **Seatbelts:** Pull fully on all seatbelts to ensure the locking mechanism engages and that the webbing is not frayed.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

### Pro Tips
*   **The "Gold Coin" Test:** Use a coin to check tire tread depth. If the edge of the coin is easily visible above the tread, the tires likely require replacement.
*   **Photos are Proof:** Always take timestamped photos of existing damage to avoid being held accountable for incidents you did not cause.
*   **Climate Adjustments:** In extreme heat or cold, pay extra attention to tire pressure, as temperature fluctuations cause significant psi changes.

### Pitfalls to Avoid
*   **"Rushing the Walk-around":** Never perform a walk-around while distracted. Many safety issues are caught by sound or smell before they are caught by sight.
*   **Ignoring "Minor" Noises:** A squealing belt or a rhythmic clicking is usually a precursor to a total failure. Report these early to prevent roadside breakdowns.
*   **Skipping the Log:** Failing to document the inspection creates a liability gap. If it isn't written down, it didn't happen.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. What should I do if I find a mechanical issue during my inspection?**
Immediately notify your supervisor or the Fleet Department. Do not operate the vehicle if the issue involves brakes, steering, lights, or engine cooling.

**2. How often should I check the fluids if the vehicle is in constant use?**
For high-mileage or heavy-use vehicles, perform a fluid check at the start of every shift. If the vehicle is used intermittently, a weekly check is the minimum requirement.

**3. Does this checklist replace professional maintenance?**
No. This checklist is a preventative measure. Professional maintenance should still follow the manufacturer's recommended service intervals (e.g., oil changes, tire rotations, and brake pad replacements).

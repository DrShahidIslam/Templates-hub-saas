# Standard Operating Procedure: Wedding Reception Execution

The objective of this SOP is to ensure the seamless execution of a wedding reception from initial vendor arrival to the final guest departure. As an operations manager, the goal is to maintain a high-level oversight of logistics, timeline synchronization, and guest experience. This document serves as the master checklist to mitigate risks, manage vendor workflows, and ensure that the event adheres to the pre-planned schedule, allowing the wedding party and guests to focus entirely on the celebration.

## Phase 1: Pre-Reception Setup and Vendor Coordination
*   Confirm arrival times for all vendors (caterers, florists, DJ/Band, lighting).
*   Conduct a site walk-through to ensure floor plans match the approved layout.
*   Verify audio/visual equipment functionality (microphone check, lighting cues, projector).
*   Ensure all tables are set according to the seating chart (place cards, menus, chargers).
*   Confirm the bar is fully stocked and the service team is briefed on the drink menu.
*   Designate a "drop-off" area for gifts and secure them in a locked room or monitored space.
*   Complete a temperature check of the venue (HVAC settings) to ensure guest comfort.

## Phase 2: Guest Arrival and Cocktail Hour
*   Verify signage is visible for parking, entrance, and gift tables.
*   Monitor drink service to ensure timely replenishment of glassware and ice.
*   Coordinate with catering staff to ensure hors d'oeuvres are circulating at the scheduled frequency.
*   Ensure the background music volume is appropriate for social conversation.
*   Maintain a clear path to the guestbook station and ensure pens are functional.
*   Perform a final scan of the reception area for any safety hazards (loose cables, spilled liquids).

## Phase 3: The Formal Program Execution
*   Coordinate with the Emcee/DJ regarding the timing for the Grand Entrance.
*   Direct the wedding party to their staging positions prior to introductions.
*   Signal the kitchen/catering lead to prep for dinner service immediately following speeches.
*   Manage the timeline for formal dances, cake cutting, and speeches to keep the program on track.
*   Liaise with the photographer/videographer to ensure they are in position for key moments.
*   Adjust lighting levels according to the transition from dinner service to open dancing.

## Phase 4: Post-Reception and Load-Out
*   Ensure the cake-top or personal items (flutes, cake cutters) are handed to a designated family member.
*   Oversee the breakdown of tables and chairs to prevent property damage.
*   Conduct a final sweep of the venue to recover any misplaced personal belongings.
*   Confirm that all rental equipment is accounted for and ready for pick-up.
*   Perform a final walkthrough with the venue manager to confirm no damages occurred.
*   Verify that final vendor payments/gratuities have been distributed as per instructions.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Always keep a "Wedding Emergency Kit" on-site containing safety pins, stain remover, double-sided tape, and a portable phone charger.
*   **Pro Tip:** Assign a specific point person for the couple so they don't have to deal with operational issues during their reception.
*   **Pitfall:** Avoid over-scheduling the program. Leave 15–20 minutes of "buffer time" in the timeline to account for unexpected delays in transitions.
*   **Pitfall:** Never underestimate the importance of the restroom check. Assign a staff member to check cleanliness and supply levels every hour.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How should I handle an unexpected delay in the wedding timeline?**
A: Communicate the delay immediately to the DJ and catering lead. Pivot to open floor or cocktail extensions if necessary, and avoid rushing guests through dinner, as this often leads to a poor guest experience.

**Q: What is the most common issue during the dinner service?**
A: The most common bottleneck is the pace of service. Ensure the catering manager knows exactly how many meals are served per table and maintain constant communication with the kitchen to sync service with the program schedule.

**Q: How do we manage guests who leave early or arrive uninvited?**
A: Have a plan with the door staff or ushers. For early leavers, ensure they have access to their vehicles; for unexpected guests, have a pre-designated "buffer" seating plan or a flexible catering count to accommodate without disrupting the flow.

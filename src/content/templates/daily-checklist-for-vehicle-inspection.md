# Standard Operating Procedure: Daily Vehicle Inspection

Effective fleet management relies on proactive maintenance and risk mitigation. This Daily Vehicle Inspection SOP is designed to ensure all vehicles are safe, roadworthy, and compliant with operational standards before they leave the depot. By conducting a systematic walk-around, drivers identify mechanical issues, safety hazards, and exterior damage early, preventing costly downtime and ensuring the safety of all personnel. This procedure must be completed by every operator at the start of each shift.

## Exterior & Body Inspection
*   **Body Damage:** Inspect the entire perimeter for new dents, deep scratches, cracked glass, or body panel misalignment.
*   **Tires:** Check for proper inflation, visible tread depth (ensure no bald spots), and ensure there are no embedded objects like nails or glass.
*   **Lights & Signals:** Test headlights (high/low beam), turn signals (front/rear), brake lights, and hazard lights. Verify that all lenses are clean and free of cracks.
*   **Mirrors & Windows:** Clean all mirrors and windshields. Adjust mirrors to the correct viewing angle and ensure windows operate smoothly.
*   **Fluid Leaks:** Observe the ground beneath the vehicle for evidence of engine oil, coolant, or transmission fluid leaks.

## Under the Hood (Engine Bay)
*   **Engine Oil:** Check the dipstick to ensure oil levels are within the manufacturer’s recommended range and color is appropriate.
*   **Coolant:** Verify the coolant reservoir level while the engine is cold. Do not open a hot radiator cap.
*   **Brake Fluid:** Check the brake fluid reservoir to ensure levels meet the "Full" mark.
*   **Belts & Hoses:** Visually inspect engine belts for fraying or cracking, and hoses for signs of bulging or leaks.
*   **Battery:** Ensure battery terminals are clean, tight, and free of heavy corrosion.

## Interior & Safety Equipment
*   **Dashboard Indicators:** Start the engine and verify that no "Check Engine," "ABS," or "Airbag" warning lights remain illuminated.
*   **Fuel Levels:** Verify that fuel levels are sufficient for the scheduled route; refuel immediately if below the 1/4 tank threshold.
*   **Safety Kit:** Ensure the vehicle is equipped with a functional fire extinguisher, road flares/triangles, and a first-aid kit.
*   **Seatbelts:** Test the locking mechanism and ensure the webbing is not frayed or torn.
*   **Documentation:** Confirm that the current insurance card, vehicle registration, and fleet fuel card are present in the glove compartment.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **Pro Tip:** Use a consistent "S" pattern (starting at the front driver’s side and moving clockwise around the vehicle) to ensure no section is overlooked.
*   **Pro Tip:** Take photos of any pre-existing damage found during your shift start; this protects you from being held liable for issues you did not cause.
*   **Pitfall:** Never "dry run" the inspection. Checking off boxes without physically verifying the items leads to complacency and potential roadside failures.
*   **Pitfall:** Ignoring "minor" warning lights. A flickering sensor today often indicates a component failure tomorrow. Always report alerts to the maintenance department immediately.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if I find a mechanical issue during the inspection?**
A: Do not operate the vehicle. Immediately log the issue in the fleet maintenance software or report it to your supervisor so the vehicle can be red-tagged and repaired.

**Q: How long should a proper daily inspection take?**
A: A thorough inspection should take between 5 to 10 minutes. If it is taking longer, you may be performing repairs instead of inspections; focus on identification, not fixing.

**Q: Am I responsible for checking tire pressure with a gauge every single day?**
A: While a visual inspection is required daily, manual pressure checks with a gauge should be performed according to the fleet’s weekly maintenance schedule, unless the vehicle is equipped with a Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) showing a deviation.

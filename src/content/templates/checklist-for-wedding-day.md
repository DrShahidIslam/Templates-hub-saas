# Standard Operating Procedure: Wedding Day Execution

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is designed to ensure the seamless execution of a wedding day, minimizing logistical friction and maximizing the experience for the couple and guests. As an operations manager, the goal is to shift from reactive firefighting to proactive coordination. This checklist serves as the master control document for the Wedding Coordinator or Lead Planner to manage timelines, personnel, and inventory effectively.

## Phase 1: Pre-Ceremony Logistics (06:00 – 11:00)
*   [ ] **Final Inventory Audit:** Verify all personal items (vows, rings, marriage license, guestbook) are accounted for.
*   [ ] **Vendor Check-in:** Confirm arrival times and direct setup crews (florists, rentals, lighting).
*   [ ] **Stationary Setup:** Place welcome signs, guestbook, and gift table signage.
*   [ ] **Personal Kits:** Ensure the "Emergency Kit" (sewing supplies, stain remover, tape, snacks, hydration) is fully stocked and accessible.
*   [ ] **Bridal Suite Prep:** Ensure food/beverages are delivered for the bridal party; clear space for hair/makeup artists.

## Phase 2: Ceremony Execution (11:00 – 15:00)
*   [ ] **Ceremony Sound Check:** Test microphones, processional music, and lapel mics for the officiant.
*   [ ] **Seating Protocol:** Confirm usher briefing and monitor seating progress.
*   [ ] **Flow Management:** Direct the bridal party line-up and cue the music transitions.
*   [ ] **Officiant Liaison:** Confirm the marriage license is signed and witnessed immediately following the recessional.

## Phase 3: Transition & Cocktail Hour (15:00 – 17:00)
*   [ ] **Room Flip/Conversion:** Coordinate with catering and venue staff to transition ceremony space to reception layout, if required.
*   [ ] **Guest Logistics:** Facilitate the transition of guests from ceremony to cocktail area.
*   [ ] **Photography Coordination:** Execute the formal family portrait list efficiently to keep the couple on schedule.
*   [ ] **Vendor Meal Distribution:** Ensure catering team has prioritized feeding the photography and video crew.

## Phase 4: Reception & Program Management (17:00 – 23:00)
*   [ ] **Grand Entrance Coordination:** Cue the DJ/Band and announce the couple’s entrance.
*   [ ] **Microphone Management:** Confirm the AV setup for toasts; alert speakers 10 minutes prior to their slot.
*   [ ] **Timeline Oversight:** Monitor service pace to ensure dinner service aligns with speech timing.
*   [ ] **Floor Logistics:** Manage the flow for dancing, cake cutting, and bouquet tosses.

## Phase 5: Breakdown & Load-Out (23:00 – End)
*   [ ] **Gift/Card Security:** Secure all gifts and personal items; designate a trusted family member or secure locker for transport.
*   [ ] **Final Walkthrough:** Conduct a sweep of the venue with site management to ensure no personal property is left behind.
*   [ ] **Vendor Sign-off:** Verify that all rentals are accounted for and cleared for pick-up.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls
*   **The 15-Minute Buffer:** Always bake a 15-minute "buffer" into every transition on the timeline. If you are on time, you are early; if you are running late, the buffer absorbs the delay.
*   **Communication Silos:** Avoid relying on one channel. Use a mix of visual cues (hand signals) and radio communication with key leads.
*   **Pitfall - The "Missing" Ring:** Never allow the rings to be passed between multiple people. The Ring Bearer carries a prop; the Best Man or Maid of Honor holds the actual bands until the moment of the exchange.
*   **Pitfall - Underestimating Transport:** Always add 20% to estimated transit times for shuttles, as wedding parties almost always take longer than expected to board.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the most critical item in the Emergency Kit?**
A: A steamer and high-strength fabric tape. Garments often arrive wrinkled, and minor snags are the most common cause of stress for the bridal party.

**Q: How should I handle an aggressive or difficult vendor?**
A: Remain neutral and focused on the contract. If a vendor is not performing, provide a clear, one-time redirection based on the pre-agreed scope of work. If issues persist, document them for post-wedding follow-up; do not engage in an emotional argument during the event.

**Q: What is the best way to handle a delay in the schedule?**
A: Communicate with the catering captain and the DJ/Band immediately. These are the two parties who control the room's pace. If the ceremony runs late, shorten the cocktail hour slightly rather than cutting time from the dancing or dinner service.

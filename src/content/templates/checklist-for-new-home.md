# Standard Operating Procedure: New Home Onboarding and Move-In

This document serves as the formal operational framework for managing the transition into a new residential property. Executing a structured move-in process is critical to ensuring legal compliance, home safety, physical security, and logistical efficiency. Following these procedures will mitigate common risks such as service interruptions, property damage, and administrative oversight.

## Phase 1: Pre-Move Logistics (T-Minus 14 Days)

*   **Utility Transfer:** Schedule electricity, water, gas, internet, and waste management services to activate effective at 8:00 AM on the day of arrival.
*   **Key Inventory:** Request a master list of all keys (entry, mail, shed, garage) and verify that all codes for smart locks or garage openers have been reset to default or factory settings.
*   **Professional Cleaning:** Schedule a move-in deep clean while the property is vacant to ensure sanitation of ducts, carpets, and appliances.
*   **Address Update:** File change-of-address forms with the postal service and update billing addresses for banking, insurance, and employer records.

## Phase 2: Immediate Post-Arrival Inspection (Day 0)

*   **Property Walkthrough:** Conduct a room-by-room inspection to identify pre-existing damage, wall scuffs, or leaks; photograph all findings and timestamp them for your records.
*   **Safety Audit:** Locate and test all smoke detectors, carbon monoxide monitors, and fire extinguishers.
*   **Emergency Shut-offs:** Clearly identify and tag the main water shut-off valve, the electrical circuit breaker panel, and the gas main valve.
*   **Lock Replacement:** Re-key all exterior doors and update security system codes immediately to ensure exclusive access.

## Phase 3: Setup and Habitability (Days 1–7)

*   **Appliance Validation:** Run cycles on the dishwasher, dryer, and HVAC system to ensure optimal functionality under load.
*   **Filter Replacement:** Replace HVAC filters, refrigerator water filters, and any secondary water treatment cartridges.
*   **Pest Control:** Install preventative bait or traps in high-risk areas (kitchen, crawl spaces, garage) before unpacking furniture.
*   **Inventory Documentation:** Catalog high-value items and furniture condition for potential insurance claims.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **Pro Tip (The "First Night" Kit):** Pack a transparent bin containing essentials for the first 24 hours: chargers, toiletries, linens, basic tools, a flashlight, and medications. You should not have to dig through boxes on night one.
*   **Pro Tip (Utility Mapping):** Create a digital file containing all account numbers, service dates, and emergency contact numbers for your utility providers for easy reference.
*   **Pitfall (Ignoring the Breaker Box):** Many homeowners fail to check the breaker panel until a power surge occurs. Test that every switch is labeled correctly and not tripped.
*   **Pitfall (The "Empty Trash" Trap):** Do not discard original manuals or appliance warranties. Keep these in a dedicated "House Binder" or a cloud-based folder.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Should I change the locks even if the previous owner seemed reliable?**
Yes. From an operational security standpoint, you never know who has received a duplicate key over the lifetime of the property (contractors, house sitters, previous tenants). Re-keying is the most cost-effective way to secure your new asset.

**2. How soon should I perform a deep clean?**
Always schedule the professional deep clean while the house is empty. It is significantly more efficient for cleaners to scrub baseboards, cabinets, and appliances without the obstruction of moving boxes or furniture.

**3. What is the most critical item to locate during the first walk-through?**
The main water shut-off valve. In the event of a burst pipe, every second counts. Knowing exactly where this valve is and ensuring it functions properly can save you from catastrophic water damage.

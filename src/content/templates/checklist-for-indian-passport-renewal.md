# Standard Operating Procedure: Indian Passport Renewal

This document serves as the definitive guide for Indian citizens residing in India or abroad who require a passport renewal. An efficiently executed renewal process prevents processing delays, minimizes the risk of application rejection, and ensures your travel documentation remains valid without gaps. Please follow the steps below in sequential order to ensure full compliance with the Passport Seva system requirements.

## 1. Documentation Preparation (Pre-Application)

Before logging into the portal, gather the following physical documents to ensure data accuracy:

*   **Existing Passport:** Original old passport with self-attested photocopies of the first and last pages (including ECR/Non-ECR page and any observation pages).
*   **Proof of Current Address:** Self-attested copy of any one of the following: Aadhaar card, utility bill (water/electric), rental agreement, income tax assessment order, or a bank passbook with a photo.
*   **Proof of Date of Birth (If applicable):** Birth certificate or school leaving certificate (only required if changing specific details).
*   **Annexures:** If you have changed your name or require specific marital status updates, ensure you have the appropriate Annexures (e.g., Annexure 'E' for self-declaration) signed and ready.

## 2. Online Application Process

*   **Registration:** Log in to the [Passport Seva Portal](https://www.passportindia.gov.in/). Create a new user account if you do not have one.
*   **Form Submission:** Select 'Apply for Fresh Passport/Re-issue of Passport.' Choose 'Re-issue' and select the specific reason (e.g., Expiry, Exhaustion of pages, or Change in particulars).
*   **Accuracy Check:** Cross-reference all names and dates against your existing government-issued ID proofs. Even a minor spelling discrepancy can lead to a 'Show Cause' notice.
*   **Payment:** Pay the requisite fees online (Credit/Debit card, Internet Banking, or SBI Challan). Note: Fees are non-refundable once the appointment is scheduled.
*   **Appointment Scheduling:** Choose your nearest Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK). Print the 'Application Receipt' containing your Application Reference Number (ARN).

## 3. The Appointment Day Checklist

*   **Arrival:** Arrive at the PSK 15 minutes prior to your scheduled time. Do not arrive too early, as they may not admit you until your slot.
*   **Physical Folder:** Organize your originals and one set of self-attested photocopies in a transparent folder.
*   **Verification:** Carry your appointment printout (or the SMS confirmation on your phone).
*   **Mobile Policy:** Ensure your mobile phone is fully charged. You may be asked to show digital proofs during the verification process.
*   **Biometrics:** Be prepared for live photograph capture and fingerprint scanning at the center.

## Pro Tips & Pitfalls

*   **The "Tatkaal" Trap:** If you choose Tatkaal, ensure your address proof is impeccable. Any ambiguity in documentation under the Tatkaal scheme leads to an immediate rejection or long verification delays.
*   **Document Consistency:** Ensure the name on your Aadhaar card perfectly matches the name on your old passport. If they differ, update your Aadhaar card *before* initiating the passport renewal.
*   **E-Passports:** If applying at major centers, check if the e-Passport facility is available; it is often worth the upgrade for expedited immigration clearance abroad.
*   **Common Pitfall:** Forgetting to carry the *original* old passport. Photocopies alone are insufficient; you must surrender the physical old passport to the officer.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to provide a police verification again for renewal?**
A: It depends on your case. If the address has changed or if your passport expired more than a year ago, police verification is usually mandatory. If renewing before expiry at the same address, it is often skipped.

**Q: Can I change my address during the renewal process?**
A: Yes. During the online application, simply select the option for "Change in existing details" and provide the new address proof. You will be required to upload the new address document.

**Q: How long does the renewal take?**
A: Under normal circumstances, it takes 15–20 working days. Under the Tatkaal scheme, you can receive the passport within 1–3 working days, subject to police verification clearance.
